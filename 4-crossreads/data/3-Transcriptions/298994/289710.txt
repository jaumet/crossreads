<p>We were not destined to stay long here. On Sunday Oct 7th 1917, a big regiment of Indians passed through this small village. They looked particularly well, did these Indians, they seemed to have Indian leaders too!</p>
<p>One appreciated these farm houses, even if it was just a French farm house with pigs living next door (a few inches from your head).</p>
<p>On Oct 10th 1917 we were on the move again. We were busy getting our gear ready for the line again. We left our billets for the line in a drenching shower: We were fairly well soaked by the time we reached Abeele, a fairly big French town.</p>
<p>A transport train conveyed us to Ypres, by the way, Dame Rumour said this was the first time a troop train had run for three years.</p>
