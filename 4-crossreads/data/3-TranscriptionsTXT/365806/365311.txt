 Since being in France I have seen only four broken down lorries and  they  two of them were due to the side of the road falling away &amp; letting them down.  They really do wonderful work. 
 This is a splendid Canteen (British) in this town.  There is a room set apart  from  for officers where one can get a very nice meal &amp; sit around a real coal fire.  It is very enjoyable to get in there &amp; have a yarn.   My present billet is a vile place but is at least dry &amp; fairly warm. 
 It is very hard to make a letter anything like interesting. I seem to have exhausted all the topics which do not offend censorship Regs. 
 Alroy Cohen is in hospital with influenza. 
 Pitt is quite well &amp; is now my 2nd-in-command.  I think I told you he got a Military Cross &amp; that I got a Mention in Dispatches for a Raiding job. 
 Best of love to you all, Yours affectionately Caesar
