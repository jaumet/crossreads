<p>Thu. 28th Nov.<br/>Our anchorage is between two of the huge booms stretching across the Firth off Leith.   The destroyer C11 circled around with cheers, and amidst the wailing of sirens &amp; the blowing of whistles from every ship in port, the cheers of the crews &amp; , last but not least, the cheers of a big bevy of trousered &amp; chic munition girls at the dock entrance, we entered the docks, the "Ficaria" leading.</p>
<p>Approaching the dock past the rows of camouflaged ships (the huge "Roseleaf" was noticeable) the ships drew in with a bagpipe band piping weird strains of welcome &amp; a huge crowd gathered outside the wharf.</p>
<p>The wharf itself was filled with brass hats &ndash; Colonel Dunlop shrieking the King's welcome through a megaphone before the mooring lines were fast.</p>
