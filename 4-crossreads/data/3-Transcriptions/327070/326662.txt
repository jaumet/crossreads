<p>Tuesday 14 March 1916</p>
<p>A fairly hot day &amp; I felt the desert work during the morning a good deal.</p>
<p>In the afternoon just as I had started on the morrow's mail a wire came from "Oakley" stating that Leo was being brought out that evening. This upset correspondence especially as there was a big bundle of letters to be censored.</p>
<p>After tea waited &amp; waited &amp; abt 8.30 Ol &amp; Leo came out: latter was looking well tho' a moustache considerably altered his appearance. Went back into Cairo with them, had supper, &amp; the Leo &amp; I came back, Leo sleeping here with me in a make-up bunk on the floor.</p>
<p>Wednesday 15 March 1916</p>
<p>Again a warm day. Leo left abt 7.30 to report in Cairo.</p>
<p>Parades as usual, &amp; as soon as the afternoon's work was over changed &amp; went in to Pension Wales. Had tea with Ol &amp; Leo &amp; then went to Pictures starting at 6 o'clock: all explanations were in French but the show was a very clean one. Took Ol back to her pension &amp; the Leo &amp; I came home, getting here abt 9.30.</p>
<p>Thursday 16 March 1916</p>
<p>A hot day. On duty as orderly officer &amp; kept going during the day.</p>
<p>Leo left abt 6.30 &amp; in the evening turned up again. There was no parade in the afternoon, a night route march taking its place, but of course I had to stay in camp on duty.</p>
