<p>Jany 1917<br/>14/22 &ndash; over Wadi El Arish likely to take a few weeks in building. Kid it won't be a target for hostile bombs. Tiny Edwards made T/Cpl &amp; Grant T/Cpl vice Garbutt evacuated sick. Got a Bengal Razor.<br/>22nd/1st &ndash; Major Fowler of 1st L.H.F.A. recently returned from England arrived &amp; took command of unit Hard to say as to who will be C.O. Rumours say that the 4th LHB being reformed &amp; a 2nd M.D. comprising 2 Y &amp; 3rd &amp; 4th to be 2nd balance 1st <indecipherable> Rumoured 4th L.H.F.A. to be commanded by Maj. F.<br/>2nd/6th - All preparations for shift made &amp; on 2nd at 6.30 or 7 am we moved off &amp; camped at Moseify about 4.30 pm &amp; at 8.30 am on 3rd we moved off &amp; arrived at Mazar about 2.30 pm. Met Arty &amp; about 60 Sd. Carts [sand carts] returning. Few tents pitched &amp; all settled by night only to again shift by next mng.<br/>Jany 1917 &amp; Feby 1917<br/>2nd/6th - &amp; tents struck. As usual camels delayed &amp; altho only shifting about a mile distant did not get there till 3.30. Again pitched tents &amp; all set when the C.O. wanted an officers mess &amp; altho only staying there for two days had &amp; E.P. tent pitched &amp; all panniers etc had to be put in out of the <u>sun</u> likewise fantasse's. Altho 2 yrs rough handling panniers never before so treated &amp; fantasses's [suitcases] never yet put under cover. On 5th <strike>rumour</strike> report that 7th had sighted Turkish patrol. 5th <strike>stood</strike> got ready but nothing doing. Diplomatic relations severed 'tween America &amp; Germany owing to Germ. subm. policy of sinking all neutral shipping on sight. Opinions differ as to effect on Britain. British civil ration 4 lbs bread 2 &frac12; meat &frac34; oz/lbs sugar per week.</p>
