<p>Cemeteries we pass none. All seems peaceful &amp; deserted on these fields which but lately teemed with armies &amp; <strike>heard the</strike> resounded shriek of shell &amp; cries of dying men. So pass away man &amp; his little works to get place in the silent majesty of the Great Architect &amp; his works. Thus they pass these men children of the sword &amp; in their place the grass grows green the greener where the blood has run thickest &ndash; the ruined village alone remaining as silent testimony to mans destructive power &ndash; &amp; in a few years even these will have passed away.</p>
<p>Already in Belgium one can see the peasants cutting up the wire entanglements &amp; putting them in little heaps so that the land may once more come under the share.</p>
<p>That night we arrive at Souslesne or some name like it (Solesmes) We arrive here at 7.30 pm &amp; do not move till 10.30 when we go on till 1.30 &amp; stand still for the rest of the night.
