<p>The billet fairly shakes with the concussion.</p>
<p>28 April 1916 Fri<br/>Bricklaying to-day. One does all sorts of jobs in this game. There is a big gun of ours not far away from our billet &amp; for the last couple of hours the Germans having been trying to get it by continuous shelling. If one gets out of its course in this direction Good-night to us. Vic Smith comes in with shrapnel wound.</p>
<p>29 April 1916 Sat<br/>Big mail arrives &amp; news that Roy has fallen in &amp; will shortly be over here to take part in the great Adventure. I only hope that I am spared to greet him. Vic Smith Evacuated. Several wets at the estaminet on the farm. Received tin of biscuits &amp; book from Roy. Jacko our mascot sipps freely. Bill Fordyce gets locked up overnight.</p>
