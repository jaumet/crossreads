<p>proper thing before.<br/>I was sorry to hear that poor old Nigger had died; he has been a great old horse has not he &ndash; suppose Father will have to buy another draught horse for the harvesting.<br/>It was very nice for you having Uncle Fred and Aunt Katie both up home &ndash; the car must be the attraction all right which is rather a nasty one for you and Father is it not.<br/>I suppose the Sulky and Taffy feel like a bullock dray after being used to tearing along in the car.<br/>Those supine tablets you sent me are great things; I got a slight cold the other day and took some of them and it was gone the next day; though I don't think they are very nice to eat for a' that.<br/>I have written down Oliver's address but am afraid it wont be much use to me and I may not be able to see Mr Trelawny  <strike>for</strike> as leave has been stopped for a bit I think.<br/>I was supprised that Oliver is a Captain: the old hands here think an awful lot of him and often talk about what a good officer he was &ndash; I wish he was back her now with us but he is lucky having such a good job in England.<br/>Dalgety's  &amp; the S.S. Journal are good papers and I read every word of them; Gossip does write well though I don't always agree quite with what he says which sounds very priggish I know but he is a marvellous man &ndash; Must stop now! Goodbye Mother dear and don't exceed the speed limit in the old puff puff because if you do you will end up in jail &amp; you would have found it <strike>better</strike> quicker to go slow in the sulky.<br/>I am<br/>Your loving son<br/>Robert C. Wilson.</p>
