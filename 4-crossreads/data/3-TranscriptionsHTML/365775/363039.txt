<p>through pretty little villages &amp; now through green fields and waving crops, many of them falling to the reaper &amp; binder. one thing most conspicuous was the absence of man labour, all the harvesting is done by the women &amp; children, they seem to me to be doing all the work in the fields &amp; ably doing it too. however shall tell you more of the work done by the French women later. shall endeavour just now to stick to my trip across.</p>
<p>Our next stop is "Macon" where we have lunch &amp; water our horses, late Sunday night or rather Monday morning finds us at "Juvisy" a suburb some 15 miles from Paris, the closest we got to that gay city much to our chagrin. Here</p>
