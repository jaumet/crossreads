We arrived here at about 1.30 A.M. and I was that tired &amp; footsore I could hardly crawl, ( In fact I have been so on every march I have been on yet, so am thinking of throwing in the stripes &amp; getting a job in the cookhouse or something.) I had a real good sleep &amp; breakfasted in my dugout at 10 A.M. on rations I had carried, while Fritz shelled the ground in front of them, and I had never seen shells burst so close before, but when one burst about 20 yards directly in front of mine I lost interest in them &amp; retired in to the dugout &amp; shut the door. Later. We have been informed of why we are here, &amp; what is going to happen tonight, or what is <u>hoped</u></p>
