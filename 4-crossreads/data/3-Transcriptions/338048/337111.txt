<p>A hospital train in which the nurses could be seen moving about. We went to Waterloo Stn &amp; then were dismissed. A motor bus took Pynor &amp; I to the Tottenham Court Rd Y.M.C.A. where we booked beds at 1/6 including breakfast left our overcoat &amp; haversack and set out for a look about.</p>
<p>We strayed about, and tried a tube just for the experience You put a penny in a slot &amp; there's your ticket. After walking along to the stair you stand on the top step &amp; in a short time you are on the bottom one. If you are in a hurry you can walk down these continuously moving stairways &amp; of course we were in a hurry. We got out of the tube at Westminster Bridge Road because the name sounded good, had to pay a penny because we had come too far for a penny ticket, strayed about and found ourselves in front of the Houses of Parliament.</p>
<p>A policeman let us in &amp; after signing books etc got into [indecipherable] Stranger's Gallery where questions were being answered. In reply to a question as to why munitions were being imported the ministry stated they were producing at home as much as they possibly could. For example sulphuric acid. There is now a plant in England which produces twice the pre-war product of all English plants, <strike>the</strike> at a cost equal to &frac14; of the cost of imported product fixed a few months ago. This plant will pay for itself in 9 mos. We heard other things discussed, had a little supper &amp; went to bed.</p>
