Being dark it was almost impossible to form any opinion of the surroundings. Teddy's first concern was to crawl into his bunk &amp; go to sleep &amp; I used to chaff him about missing Hobart's beautiful scenery. He had not been to Hobart, but even then bed was his "home". I soon followed him &amp; awoke at six am 26th September hearing a lot of bustling outside the door. "Where are the two operators" called out Captain Jackson. "In bed sir." "Pull them out quickly." Hearing this talk I jumped up &amp; began to dress but had only donned a shirt when the second officer came &amp; turned us out for "Muster". I saw everyone lined up &amp; wondered what "muster" was. Teddy in his "panjamas" as the carpenter called them &amp; myself in my shirt found that Hobart air was rather cool. The Port Doctor then examined each of us by looking at our faces &amp; smiling. All being well on board he gave the signal for the quarantine flag to be lowered &amp; passed the A13 for a berth.
