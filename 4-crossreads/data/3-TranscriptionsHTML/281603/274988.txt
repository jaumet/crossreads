On the night of the 7th we were relieved from supports by the 1st Battalion &amp; we moved back to reserve trench in Railway dug outs which were built like a big tunnel with two rows of wire bunks on each side under the railway line.  On the afternoon of the 8th I was detailed off a corporal of Brigade Headquarters guard also in the Railway dug outs &amp; it was for twenty four hours.  On the night of the 10th I went with fatigue party to supports where we had to carry engineers &amp; miners' gear from Jackson dump to communication trench &amp; winter quarters &amp; on the night of the 11th I was again detailed off with Engineers fatigue in communication trenches on revetting work &amp; worked till 3am next morning.</p>
<p>On the morning of the 12th I had to go with an officer &amp; four other men to Ypres to look up route so as to guide the platoon out.  At dusk of the same day I had to go to the railway at Ypres &amp; meet the Tenth Battalion &amp; guide a platoon of them to our trench &amp; when they had taken over I had to lead our platoon to the train.  Our ride was short &amp; disentrained near Poperinghe &amp; marched to Dominion camp &amp; when we turned in it was 2.30 am next morning.</p>
<p>We were allowed to sleep till dinner time &amp; in the afternoon I was detailed off as Company Orderly Corporal which I had for four days.
