<p>Sunday 30 July 1916<br/>Had charge parties revetting Bay Avenue.  Wiring Duck Walks, etc.  Things quiet.</p>
<p>Monday 31 July 1916<br/>All kinds of work in hand.  News of big Russian successes.  Things quiet here.</p>
<p>Tuesday 1 August 1916<br/>Things as usual.  Heavy bombardment in places along our line, also of Enemy positions.</p>
<p>Wednesday 2 August 1916<br/>Some heavy Artillery actions on both sides.  Aereal activity also.  Things going well.</p>
<p>Thursday 3 August 1916<br/>In charge parties revetting Avenues, etc.  Also setting out new dugouts.  Heavy shelling on Jay Post.  Things going well.</p>
<p>Friday 4 August 1916<br/>Three Saprs. wounded today, Brice, Alta &amp; Brown.  Revetting trenches &ndash; putting in Dugouts, etc.  Things going well.</p>
<p>Saturday 5 August 1916<br/>Things rather quiet.  A fair amount of shelling on both side.  Enemy brought down one of our aeroplanes, no serious damage.</p>
<p>Sunday 6 August 1916<br/>Shells lobbed in the village this afternoon killing Sap. McDonald, &amp; two Infantrymen also wounding some.  Usual work revetting &amp; constructing &amp; generally [indecipherable].</p>
<p>Monday 7 August 1916<br/>Charge parties Revetting trenches, cleaning out, building concrete shelters.  Things going well.  One or two casualties each day.</p>
<p>Tuesday 8 August 1916<br/>Same work as yesterday.  Sap. Smith wounded last night by M. Gun bullets supposed mortally.  Things as usual.</p>
<p>Wednesday 9 August 1916<br/>Heavy shelling by enemy of positions at Fleur Baix.  Several casualties reported.  Progressive work as usual.  Nothing importance reported.</p>
<p>Thursday 10 August 1916<br/>Work going on as usual, everything O.K.  A good deal of Artillery fire from both side.</p>
<p>Friday 11 August 1916<br/>Cpl. Griggs wounded slightly, Sap. Gustephson more seriously being badly wounded in both legs, right shoulder &amp; hand.</p>
<p>Saturday 12 August 1916<br/>Usual work, revetting, Dugouts &amp; general improvements.  Things going well.</p>
