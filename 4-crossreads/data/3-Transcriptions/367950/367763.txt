<p>Method of putting down horse lines in sandy desert country: pegs are useless. Two grain sacks are buried at each end with the line rope passed round leaving a hitch at one end for straining and 2 small sand bags</p>
<p>(Drawing showing the rope and the cornsacks and sand bags. Under the rope is written GROUNDS LEVEL whilst at each end of the rope is written CORN SACK and towards the left and the right is written SAND BAG ANCHOR)</p>
<p>are buried midway with a short piece of rope, wire etc as additional anchors. Five minutes work with a shovel will soon dig the sacks out again. The sand is emptied out and these sack are carried on the saddle to the next camp.</p>
