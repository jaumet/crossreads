Our advance party left here to-day for our destination, a village called Estaires about three miles behind the firing line. They travelled up by motor transport.

April 23rd. Sun. April 24th. Mon.
In Ward 13 Surgical.

Off duty this afternoon. Although there was a rumour that we are leaving for up the "Line" to-day I managed to get a pass in to town.

This being Easter Monday there was a holiday in town. The people were going about in Sunday attire, Blacks being predominant. Intended going up to Bonsecours again, but the trams were so overcrowded, that I changed my mind and wandered around the streets.

Saw a splendid monument but for what it had been put up for I cannot say, as there were no inscription on it.

On the left hand side of this monument was a fine statue of a horse and a youth, the right hand side had a fine representation of a cow while in the centre was a women and young boy in a boat.

Went to the fine old museum, which was very interesting indeed, the anatomical studies being specially instructive.

The building, to look at from the outside, was not impressive, but the inside of it was splendid.

Went into the Cathedral and had a good look round this magnificent old place. As usual, candles were lit and people, mostly women were at their devotion, at the numerous shrines which are to be found in these places.
