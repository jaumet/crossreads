
<p>328</p>
<p>1917 APRIL</p>
<p>4 WEDNESDAY</p>
<p>Fine but dull &amp; we had a</p>
<p>storm of sleet in afternoon</p>
<p>Cleaning things round stables</p>
<p>ect all morning &amp; we went</p>
<p>up the river (Lys) &amp; grazed</p>
<p>the horses for a couple of hours</p>
<p>in afternoon. Pay parade</p>
<p>in eveninv. We are paid</p>
<p>at the rate of 1 franc = 8&frac12;<sup>d</sup>&nbsp;&amp;</p>
<p>all the civilian people reckon</p>
<p>1 franc = 10<sup>d</sup>&nbsp;so we make 1&frac12;<sup>d</sup></p>
<p>on each franc. I got two</p>
<p>letters from father 26<sup>th</sup>&nbsp;Mar &amp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>6<sup>th</sup>&nbsp;Feb. &amp; a letter 26 Jan &amp; a</p>
<p>&quot;Bulletin&quot; from Linda.</p>
