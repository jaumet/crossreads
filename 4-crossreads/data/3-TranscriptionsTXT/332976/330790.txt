 Febuary 1917 2nd   The weather has been very cold lately but to-day the sun came out &amp; warmed the place a little, although the snow is still on the ground.  To-night is very bright, &amp; two Taubes came over Albert &amp; dropped about 8 bombs in &amp; around the town.  His machine guns played very freely &amp; the hut next to ours was hit several times, wounding two.  A couple of our own planes went up after them &amp; they cleared off to their own lines. 3rd   At about 10.30 p.m. our artillery opened a heavy bombardment towards the Poziers Front where the 1st &amp; 2nd Divisions are, which lasted until about 12 midnight.
