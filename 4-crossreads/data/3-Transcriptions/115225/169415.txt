
<p>Jan 15<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Still standing out in the bay with five other transports. latest latreen wire is that we will sail to-morrow. we played crib &amp; 500 - I slept below on account of a cold on my chest.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 16<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; We weighed anchor at 1.30 PM. with the five other transports. &quot;Berimar&quot; &quot;Orsova&quot; &quot;Kenilworth Castle&quot; &quot;Port Littleton&quot; &amp; one other. escorted by the cruiser &quot;Cornwall&quot; - The sea was smooth as a mill pond, the weather beautiful &amp; we watched the flashing of mirrors from all along the shore they looked like diamonds flashing in the sun, the Cape&#39;s farewell to the convoy -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 17<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Sea still smooth &amp; weather lovely, we played cards in the afternoon &amp; evening - The port holes are closed daily at sunset also the dead-lights - I slept on deck under the awning.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan. 18<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; The &quot;Orontes&quot; caught us up to-day &amp; stayed with the convoy - the weather is still fine &amp; the sea smooth - we played, draughts &amp; cards -</p>
