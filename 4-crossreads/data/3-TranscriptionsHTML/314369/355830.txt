<p>19 May 1915<br/>Wednesday</p>
<p>13th Bat sent to Quinns Hill for 24 hours support   &amp;  then 24 hours in firing line.<br/>Sgt Leans of No 12 platoon wounded in hip so leaves me in charge of the platoon.<br/>The Turks make a general attack all along the line but only lose heavily<br/>Australian casualties about 500<br/>Turkish casualties about 7000.</p>
<p>1915 May 20<br/>Thursday</p>
<p>Go into trenches during the morning   &amp;  do some heavy firing<br/>During the afternoon a red crescent flag appears in the Turkish Trench on the extreme right of the of our position followed by others at intervals right along the whole line.<br/>It appears the Turks want an armistice to bury the dead which is becoming a nuisance both to the eyesight   &amp;  the intolerable stench of bodies lying in the open for nearly 3 weeks. Somehow no arrangement can be come to and</p>
