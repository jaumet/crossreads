1917Dec. 9Communion.  Have lost my voice.
Dec. 10 &amp; 11Off parade &ndash; light duty.
Dec. 12Getting better but quality of rations nauseating.
Dec. 13Left Tingry with Lt. Mackenzie as advance party to Courset.  H.Q. at Courset, Companies at Sacriquier.  Not feeling too well so hired room &ndash; clean sheets &amp; plenty of eiderdown quilts.  Oh for peace!
Dec. 14Bn. arrd. at 11.30.  We had provided billets for everyone but the guard &amp; I soon fixed 'em up.  The Colonel was billetted at a Chateau owned by the Baron de Givenchy &amp; has to take his boots off before going upstairs to his room!  I have lived on the best for two days but the issue rations were awful.
Dec. 15Mde. Fournier provided a fine breakfast this morning &amp; I then adjusted the billetting claims.  Started off to catch the battalion which had left earlier &amp; was lucky enough to get a lift in a car to Blequin via Senleques.
