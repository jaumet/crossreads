<p>About midnight we pulled into a small railway siding and by the dim light of a few oil lamps we made out the name on the white notice boards Tel-El-Kebir Camp. As dreary as it looked we were that way we were very glad to be at the end of that Journey &amp; when we were told to "Get out at the Hick", we just grabbed our equipment &amp; kit bags &amp; fell out.</p>
<p>From there we were marched about 4 miles through the camp, carrying all our possessions &amp; about 2 am we just staggered into what was known as the Quarantine camp. Here, after a snack of something to eat, we were told we could turn in &amp; have a sleep &amp; I just fell down on the sand where I was with my mate Nugget Lyons &amp; in a few seconds we were fast asleep.</p>
