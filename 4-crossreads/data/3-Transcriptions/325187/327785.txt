<p>[Wednesday 9 February 1916]</p>
<p>A draft of 40 men arrived today to fill the places of sick men<br/> [Following paragraphs are crossed out]<br/>since we left the Aerodrome Camp.<br/>The leakage &amp; waste even here is tremendous &amp; a man is lucky to keep fit &amp; well.<br/>One man of our field Ambulance died lately of Cerebro Spinal Meningitis I suppose brought from Australia.<br/>Mumps &amp; Pneumonia &amp; Measles claim most at present, but as a whole we are a healthy crowd &amp; plenty of good Tucker for we Officers cooked on a Primus stove &ndash; as no wood on the Desert &amp; the issue is all used by the men</p>
