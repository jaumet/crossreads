 November, 1915 Saturday 20 Mail day outwards &amp; I hear one in.  you are very good little Mother writing to me so regularly &amp; I do hope you take that little trip to the Murrumbidgee it would be like old times One had happy days on M'gong  Poor "Thirley" that packed our Mail up has just been shot, they are grand animals &amp; I have learnt to love both Donks &amp; Mules. They feed &amp; water us in return for us feeding &amp; watering them: everything is  packed  - A few Turks give themselves up each day, they are unkept rough looking soldiers, with black coal bags for Blankets in many cases.
