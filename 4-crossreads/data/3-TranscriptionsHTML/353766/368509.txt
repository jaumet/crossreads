<p>German officer hauled into British cutter. Suppose he'd gone "dotty". Pulled out his pistol &amp; shot at <u>his own</u>  men still struggling in the water. Flattened out with an oar. The wonder is that they weren't all mad after passing thro' all that hell. Now there's a funny side; I believe there's quite a lot of jealousy between the Inflex &amp; the Invinc. over that action. They say ________ X<br/>Abrolhos Rocks. I had certainly never heard of this place before, &amp; it has no beauty that I shd. desire  to see it again. 30 miles fr. Brazilian coast. A few rocks &amp; lighthouse. Grim reminder of war on starboard beam. Two masts sticking up out of the water. German collier sunk herr by gunfire month ago. Canopus here &amp; two other colliers besides ours. Talk about command of the sea! This is the way to find it out. Poor old Canopus, with her leaky boilers &amp; (very) Reserve crew. Their officers come aboard &amp; we exchange comments on things in general &amp; the war in particular. Fill up with coal, prepare to leave. Everybody's getting fed up, for the strain is terrible. No ship ever built even came up to our record &amp; the strain is beginning to tell. No letters for weeks, &amp; nothing but hard unceasing, monotonous grind! Something is going to snap before long.
