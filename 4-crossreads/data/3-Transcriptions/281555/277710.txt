<p>2 hrs march, through very open country roads. several rests. no opposing fire. Relieved Northumberland Fusilers about 9.30Pm. Weather dull. Business quiet.</p>
<p>11th. Tuesday.<br/>Position very fair 450 range quiet. raining. very rough trenches. real bogs &amp; flooded saps. no comforts or shelter from weather whatever, soon got to &amp; made some &amp; fires going.</p>
<p>12th. <strike>Saturday. </strike> Wed.<br/>Weather very nice during day. night moonlight &amp; quiet.</p>
<p>13th Thursday<br/>Raining again &amp; changable quiet morn opposing artillery gave us a few light shells no casualties in B. Coy otherwise very quiet.</p>
<p>14th. Friday.  English parcel. Film. Weather very dirty, rain, hail &amp; snow with light wind. Teasing enemy machine guns. enemy fire very reckless &amp; poor seen bailing their trenches out early morning.</p>
<p>15th. Saturday.<br/>Weather as Friday more sunshine</p>
