<p>Mon Aug 11th:-<br/>Back again to Warminister &amp; Sutton Veny camp.</p>
<p>Fri 22nd Aug:-<br/>London again. This time on marriage leave. So I'm going to do the trick at last &amp; to a girl whom I've known since my first furlough in 1916 so I've had plenty of time for reflection. "Here's Luck".</p>
<p>Tues Sept 2nd 1919:-<br/>"Hitched up" well &amp; truly today at St Barnabas' Church East Dulwich, London. "The deed is done".</p>
<p>Sun Sept 21st:-<br/>Left by the 2.40 pm from Paddington station to Warminister.<br/>After my kits &amp;  mail as from now I'm finished with camps &amp; will be on indefinite leave till "me &amp; the missus" are put on to a boat.</p>
<p>Mon Sept 22nd:-<br/>Returned to London. Domiciled at 3 Daneville Road Camberwell S.E.</p>
<p>Sept 25th Thurs:-<br/>Railway strike in England. Things are getting more</p>
