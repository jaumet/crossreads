<p>2nd hand trousseau of an Egyptian princess whose marriage was broken off.  Of course whatever you buy you have to bargain for as they always ask about 10 times what they expect to get for their goods.  There are plenty of trams in Cairo also motors (private &amp; taxis) &amp; victories (called Arabeiahs) drawn by a pair of horses will take one anywhere in Cairo itself for 3 piastres (7&frac12;).  We often saw these vehicles full of our soldiers &ndash; sometimes with a soldier driving &amp; every day one could see lots of our "boys" riding the little Egyptian donkeys &amp; racing each other thro' the streets <strike>(Clothes &amp; boots seemed cheap &amp; there are some fine big stores.)</strike> singing Tipperary, at first funny later pathetic, because they were the trench regiments or reinforcements.</p>
