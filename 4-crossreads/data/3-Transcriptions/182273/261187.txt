
<p>1918&nbsp;&nbsp; December</p>
<p>5 THUR&nbsp; Arr. Harfleur 6-30 am.</p>
<p>Marched out to</p>
<p>Australian base Camp. all</p>
<p>hands &#39;molo&#39;. Saw M<sup>r.</sup></p>
<p>Johnson &amp; many 101<sup><u>st </u></sup>boys. Wash</p>
<p>shave etc. Parade 1.30. Issue</p>
<p>of clothes by Q.M. Went to</p>
<p>Cinema. &amp; lecture after by M<sup>r.</sup> Norwood</p>
<p>6 FRI&nbsp; Parade 9.30 Medical</p>
<p>Inspection after. Packed up &amp; moved</p>
<p>off to wharf at 4.PM. Kits taken</p>
<p>by motors. 10 Kilo march. went</p>
<p>straight on board <sup>S</sup>/<sub>S</sub> S/Western Miller</p>
<p>Sailed about 11 P.M</p>
<p>7 SAT&nbsp; Fine trip, Calm. Arr.</p>
<p>Southampton 8. am. Embarked</p>
<p>10 am. &amp; entrained for Codford</p>
<p>arriving there 2.30.PM. Band</p>
<p>marched us into Camp. R.B.A.A.</p>
<p>at Heytesbury. good dinner</p>
<p>waiting. Met quite a number</p>
<p>of the old boys. Down the</p>
<p>village at night. Had a</p>
<p>good time. Posted letters to</p>
<p>Lindsay Palmes &amp; Amy.</p>
