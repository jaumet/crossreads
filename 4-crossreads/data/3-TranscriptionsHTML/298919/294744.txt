<p>carrying the mail which has been sent by soldiers &amp; sailors we have a Colonel for a passenger.  Immediately after dinner the anchor was heaved up, and we proceeded down the bay.  At the entrance two boats were engaged in laying a defence net.  Passing between two buoys we were interrogated by the Captain of one of the vessels if we had damaged the net, but as we had not noticed any net the answer was "No".  Alexandria is two days run from here and as the Shipper does not want to arrive there till day light on Friday the engines are only going "Slow". At 2.30 we were abreast of Cape Hellis where the English and French troops are engaged</p>
