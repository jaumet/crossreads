<p>Two Taubes came over here about 3.30 p.m. and dropped several bombs no damage done. The anti-air-craft guns pelted them with shells and we in our present positions got all those shells, pellets and nose-caps which rained down after bursting in the air. I wish they would shift those guns they are beginning to be something of a nuisance we have to duck for cover from the descending shells the bombs which Fritz drops are nothing compared to it.</p>
<p>13th January 1917 El Arish Saturday<br/>Cloudy and some little rain. The Regiment paraded in full strength this morning for Major-General Chamal the Commander of the Anzac Mounted Division and he told us a lot of flim-flam about being good fellows: - proud to command such men &ndash; good work dome &ndash; devotion to duty &ndash; etc etc ad nauseum.
