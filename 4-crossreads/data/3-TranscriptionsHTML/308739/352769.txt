<p>Sunday 27 August 1916<br/>Col. Fuller &amp;amp; Col. Onslow out this afternoon motoring.<br/><strike>Aus.</strike> Anzac Hostel handed over to Tommies &amp;amp; Y.M.C.A. today.<br/>Went to Church to-night.<br/>Wrote home.</p>
<p>Monday 28 August 1916<br/>Out to see Col. Fuller.<br/>Got Saddle &amp;amp; leather equipment from Heath &amp;amp; Sons, 115 Pt. also Quart-Pots from Radcliffe, 40 Pt.<br/>Am collecting everything together as Colonel Fuller will shortly be leaving for the Canal.  He refuses to take a holiday to England which has been offered by the Medical Authorities.  Officers can easily get away for a trip after being wounded.</p>
<p>Tuesday 29 August 1916<br/>Banked Cheque for Col. Fuller.  Out to see Him progressing well.<br/>[The following sentence crossed through.]<br/>Both Col. Fuller &amp;amp; Onslow out to Town by Motor Car, met Major Bruxner &amp;amp; other 6th Officers who were up for Holidays.<br/>I <strike>left</strike> met Officers in Town &amp;amp; arranged to leave for Kantara tomorrow by 7 a.m. Train to get Kit Bags.</p>
<p>Wednesday 30 August 1916<br/>I left by 7 a.m. Train arrived El Maler 5 p.m., left with Kit Bags by 9 p.m. Train &amp;amp; stayed Kantara the night.<br/>Wired through to Transport Depot, Kantara for G.S. Waggon to meet Train.</p>
