<p>&amp; then a month after at Anzac made Sergeant &amp; dated back one month, have acted as Company Sergeant Major &amp; Coy QuarterMaster Sergeant &amp; now a sort of a "Bucksheesh" Lieutenant. So you see I have been through the mill in nearly every capacity &amp; I hope my career &amp; promotion as an officer is more rapid. I know both you &amp; Ralph will be pleased to hear of this, also Dad &amp; the Mater etc etc.<br/>There is only one time since being in the Battn. have I felt prouder than I do now, that was during the evacuation from Anzac when I in company with an old comrade called Sergt Kelly were selected to be the last two men in our Company to leave the trenches &amp; get off the Peninsula. I havn't mentioned this previously on account of the censorship re the said evacuation. Will give you a detailed account "in fact wrote you a good account &amp; had to destroy it" when the opportunity occurs. Well dear will finish up tomorrow.<br/>13.3.16<br/>I am not allowed to write about our doings or where we are, but the spring is</p>
