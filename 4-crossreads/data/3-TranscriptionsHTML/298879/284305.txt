<p>The mess orderlies do all the work so far. Routine on board as follows.   6AM Rise &amp; stow away hammocks  8 AM breakfast. 11 AM beer issued. Price 3d. 12 PM dinner 5.30 PM tea, 9.30 everyone in bed &amp; lights out.</p>
<p>Monday 19th  Fine weather, calm sea. Many dolphins seen. Speed of ship about 9 knots per hour.</p>
<p>Tues 20   Passed Wilson's Prom. Many rocks &amp; islands passed</p>
<p>Wed 21st  Entered the Bight whales on Starboard side. Been sweeping &amp; scrubbing decks. Syrup jam &amp; butter served out for dinner. Boxing singing and band at nighttime Large shark seen</p>
<p>Thurs. 22  ship rolling. Blowing from West. 2 big sharks seen. Passed Adelaide whales about 30' long sharks 12'</p>
<p>Friday 23rd Expect to arrive at Albany on Sund.</p>
<p>Sunday 25th Arrived at Albany. Troopships assembling here. Cold wind &amp; and rain</p>
<p>Monday 26th  Raining cold. HMAS Melbourne in sight. Castor oil given me for a cold.</p>
<p>Nov 1st  Whole fleet of about 40 transports weighed anchor &amp; left Albany. Great sight. Cruiser on both sides of us.<br/>No 3 strong headwind &amp; big seas, fine</p>
<p>Nov 9th German Cruiser Emden sunk by HMAS Sydney off Cocos Islands</p>
<p>No 10 1230 miles off Columbo.</p>
<p>Nov 13 Father Neptune arrived on board &amp; everyone ducked and shaved. I escaped</p>
<p>Nov 15 arrived in Colombo port, Fine weather</p>
<p>Nov 17th all transports left Colombo. Russian warship with us &amp; British Cruiser Hampshire<br/>Natives very dark skinned</p>
<p>Nov 25th Arrived in Gulf of Aden. Reached Aden</p>
<p>Nov 26th Left Aden. Fine weather</p>
<p>Dec 2nd arrived at entrance of Suez Canal.<br/>Searchlights taken on board. Egypt on left or Port side &amp; Arabia on Starboard</p>
<p>Mon 3rd reached Port Said about 60,000 Indian troops on banks of Canal</p>
