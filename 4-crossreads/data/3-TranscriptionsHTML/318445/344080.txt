<p><u>Notice</u><br/>of the many successful actions conducted in the course of the Great War of 1914-19, none was more extraordinary than the capture of Mount St Quentin. The troops of the 2nd Division of the Australian Imperial Force were responsible for this conspicuous achievement, but the position actually was won and held by the 5th Brigade on the 31st August 1918. In the following report of the battle, written to commemorate the glory of the 5th Brigade, which certainly was most active and inesistible of the forces engaged, I have recorded as many consistent details as I could gather.
