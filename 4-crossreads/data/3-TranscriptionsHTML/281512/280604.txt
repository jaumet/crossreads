<p>23rd August<br/>So much passes, so much seen, &amp; such unprecedented happenings that to enter them at length would take hours. Here it is 11 Pm. &amp; I have just returned from the Artillery on hill 60, &amp; as weary &amp; tired after 60 miles jolting that I feel like dropping off to sleep.  In the morning I went in my service Car, which by the way is a Ford, accompanied by Wilkins, to the large centre St. Omer, to buy a camp bed. This I have found essential as the great amount of condensation, even on the grass under the tents, makes ground sleeping decidedly unhygienic. The Twenty mile ride out was delightful;  the grain has all been harvested &amp; now stands in sheaves ready for "ricking". The beautiful Avenued roadways &amp; quaint villages, with the troops billeted &amp; drilling therein offer pictures at every turn. At Ballieul I saw great numbers of our infantry who are now recuperating,</p>
