<p>Old Dezzy, (our battalion hard case) acted in the capacity of cook and he deserves the M.M. for his services</p>
<p>I received a good bundle of letters from home while here.</p>
<p>We left Foilliz on the 22nd April 1918 and proceeded to Dours. Dours was a biggish town, alas deserted by the people!</p>
<p>No one knew our movements now, not even the company "dag".</p>
<p>The main road in Dours is crossed by the Somme canal. Some "Tommy" Engineers were busy mending up a bridge, Fritz had seen fit to knock about.</p>
<p>On the 24th/4/18 we were awakened from sleep by a loud bursting of shells Fritz had opened up a barrage on Dours There were a number of batteries just in front of Dours. The drew the crabs! We got to a field at the "toot" Shells were landing all around our billets by</p>
