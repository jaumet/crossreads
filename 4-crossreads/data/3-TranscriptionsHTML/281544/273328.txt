<p>to be able to go somewhere for an evening's enjoyment if only to forget this terrible war. The Show was over at 7.30 &amp; it is now dark at that time. The Town is practically deserted, except in the Estaminet's. We had a couple rounds of Drinks &amp; then jogged along slowly home, arriving there about 8.30 when I boiled the Kettle &amp; had Toast &amp; Cocoa for Supper &amp; as the Moon is now rising late &amp; the weather cloudy, Fritz did not trouble us with his bombs although during the night I heard several shells coming over &amp; exploding somewhere in the bush.</p>
<p>4th Thursday.<br/>I was booked on the Shunter at 4 Pm &amp; was kept very busy all the shift &amp; signed off at Midnight &amp; came on again Friday 5/10/17. <strike>at 3.30 on an Emergency job, we stood by until 6 Pm when we had to take a train load of Ammunition go Light Engine up to B1 Siding on the Old Belgian Line &amp; pick up a Truck of 12" Shells</strike></p>
