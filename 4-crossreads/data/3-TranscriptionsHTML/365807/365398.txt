<p>In France<br/>5-5-17</p>
<p>Dear Judge,</p>
<p>I was awfully glad to get your letter of 14th Feb. It must seem I am afraid lax on my part not to have answered before, but I only got your letter on May 3.</p>
<p>I have not written you personally for some time but I asked those, whom I did write to, to forward my letters on to you.</p>
<p>I know you will be glad of the good news that I have got my Commission. You will or should have heard of my experiences at Lark Hill, Salisbury Plains, amusing and otherwise &ndash; mostly otherwise. I badgered those in authority so much and made myself such a nuisance to everyone that they were glad to get rid of me, and sent me over with the first Draft to La Belle France &ndash;</p>
<p>My experiences of such trip till arrival at the Base Depot I wrote Mr Langree &ndash; and I hope you saw the letter-</p>
