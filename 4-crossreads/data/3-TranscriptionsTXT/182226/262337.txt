 Thursday&nbsp;11 November, 1915 
 Are making hay while the sun shines. 
 Sid Moss, a chap from the 13  th  &nbsp;M.G. have chummed up. We left the Sailors home at 7.45 a.m. had a breakfast &amp; went &amp; played billiards &amp; drank beer till lunch time. We then went &amp; sent a wire to Sids people at Gibraltar for&nbsp;&pound;5. 
 After that we took a ride on hired bikes about 3 miles out, at a wayside pub we patronised with the Maltese. 
 I took photos of the mob &amp; gave out &quot;picture cards&quot; to a lot of school kiddies. On the way back all went well till we were nearly home, then Sid ran into a donkey &amp; almost immediately after, into am[?] 
 There was a terrible mix up. The police took a whole host of names. It cost us a stollar[?] between us to square matters. 
 Slept at &quot;Hotel De France&quot; Valletta.
