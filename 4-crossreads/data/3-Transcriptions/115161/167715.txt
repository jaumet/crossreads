what a time he must have had, and what pluck too.&nbsp; I thoroughly enjoy working in this pleasant atmosphere, although at times it is a bit &quot;thick&quot; owing to the great variety of perfumes and powder which float around the room from many of the fair sex employed therein, who in their ignorance consider the former essential, and in their foolishness imagine their faces and necks are improved by a camouflage of the latter.&nbsp; It has always seem to me a crying shame that so many pretty faces should be covered with a thick layer of powder, I can quite understand a little being applied at times but the quantity used by some girls now-a-days is beyond all reason and if for one moment they imagine that their beauty is enhanced, or their chances of early matrimony advanced, then they make a grievous error.&nbsp; Men like to see the real face of the girl they intend to marry, and also they like to be quite sure that the figure is all their own and no artificiality exists there.</p>
<p>Woman, - what a topic, what a study, - and here all round me were hundreds, all different.&nbsp; Amongst the whole lot I noticed very few with high necks and long skirts, very very few.&nbsp; Oh! it was a great study.</p>
