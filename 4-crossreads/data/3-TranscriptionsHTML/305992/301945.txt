<p>15  [Mar]<br/>Arrived at Taranto. Met by a Italian destroyer &amp; trawlers 6 miles out at<br/>swept channel.<br/>10 am Anchored at outer harbor.<br/>3 pm hove up &amp; proceeded through the narrow entrance to the Inner Harbor opposite the British Military base &amp; moored to a bouy.<br/>5 pm Troops all disembarked into lighters.<br/>7 pm All troops &amp; baggage away. Whilst on the way into inner harbor we passed our Escort Sloop Wallflower. Semephored that she had engine trouble &amp; wish us to report same to H.M.S. Queen, the flagship battleship, that she could not come inside this day. Conveyed signals to H.M.S. Queen re same.</p>
<p>16th<br/>All European mails discharged into lighters 2000 bags. Received a letter this day from a officer which we brought over with us from Australia, they having been sent from Suez via Alexandria &amp; Taranto to Salisbury Plain England. During this trip we Zig Zag all the way night &amp; day. On arrival we passed the Camberra at [sketch of anchor]. Also the Lismore Castle Tagus &amp; Kaiser I Hind troopships. The latter having 6 inch gun &amp; 2 Howtizers capable of firing a depth charge 2000 yards &amp; exceedingly effective against submarines.</p>
