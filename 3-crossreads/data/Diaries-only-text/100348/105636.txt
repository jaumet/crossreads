2nd November 1916.
This job will do me. Four hours on, and eight off. We get shelled here otherwise an uneventful life. I rescued a packhorse from a bog hole in the road today. It is good to get a chance to do these poor beggars a good turn, they serve us well. There is a battery of howitzers in position near here. It is great to watch them firing at night. No light is seen in the gun pits, except a pencil of light from a electric torch that flashes over the sights, an even voice calls the fire orders from a dugout nearby; "Range! Four, five, eight, O.; Charge! No. 5.
