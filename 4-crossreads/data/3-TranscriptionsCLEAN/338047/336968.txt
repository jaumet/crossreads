  
 1919 April 3 Achi Barber turned up the boys informed him that they refused to go into the barn to get chatty again.  He seemed rather nonplussed & went away.  While he was absent, a number of the fellows went down the road to a row of cottages & soon came back for their equipment.  They had been taken in by the very people who refused to take in officers.  The sergeant said that there was a billet along the road that would take in 10 men, so nine others & myself went to it.  It belonged to M.
