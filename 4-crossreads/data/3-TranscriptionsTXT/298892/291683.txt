 Now and again, too, an inquisitive enemy plane ventures over our camp, at a great altitude, to have a pry round and the quietude is broken by the sharp bark of anti-aircraft guns which spring into sudden life and announce themselves by bursts of flame in all sorts of unexpected places. They are all wonderfully concealed. They generally succeed in frightening the maurauder off but seldom damage him. At night is the time when things really liven up on the front.
