 I went one day to have a good look over St. Pauls Cathedral, went in the main entrance, had a look round, listened to the service and came out by another door, and had to enquire my way back to my starting point. 
 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ---------------------------------------- 
 You can get some lovely trips on the busses into the country, to Reigate or Dorking, and many other places, so one Sunday morning having nothing to do, and feeling that I should like a blow in the country got up a bit earlier than usual, as I had heard that the busses were always &quot;packed&quot; on these country trips.&nbsp; I saw a bus marked &quot;Shoreditch&quot; and was lucky enough to secure a top seat.&nbsp; Paid my fare and settled myself down for a good blow of fresh air.&nbsp; After travelling for about an hour, through nothing but dusty and dirty streets of the &quot;slummiest&quot;part of London, the bus &quot;pulled up&quot; in the vicinity of London Bridge, I being the only passenger left.&nbsp; The conductor told me that this was the terminus.&nbsp; I told him that I thought he was wrong and that as his bus was &quot;labelled&quot; Shoreditch, I expected him to convey me there, especially as I also had a ticket for that place.&nbsp; He simply said &quot;This is Shoreditch!&quot;.&nbsp; I got off that bus and had to wait about half an hour in the heat and dust for another to take me back.
