<p>1917<br/>February<br/>soon after we started, and was very thick by the time we arrived.<br/>[Diagram of "Ground Plan of Camp shewing general arrangement of Huts."]<br/>The town looked picturesque with its white mantle, very like a picture on a Xmas Card.   We had several snowball fights with the local lads and sent one crowd of boys that attacked us, flying up the street.   Afternoon tea was obtainable at the School of Arts for the moderate sum of 4d, - tea bread and butter and two cakes.  I thought the pubs were very interesting with their hanging signs, "The Six Bells", "The Ship Inn"</p>
