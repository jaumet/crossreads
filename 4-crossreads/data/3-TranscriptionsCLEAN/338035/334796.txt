Monday 17thNight.  Trip to Amesbury - very interesting village.
Tuesday 18thReceived Referee from Harry Baldock.
Wednesday 19thPay day.Lecture from Capt. Meehan.  Each man 51/2d. day is allowed while in Camp for extras by Imperial authorities.  In addition we derive 10% of all profits money received for articles purchased in our Canteen including liquor.  Regarding refuse marrow bones for which we receive 7 ton are used for making tooth brushes for Army.Cooked bones (5/10/- ton)for extracting glycerine for munitions.  Dripping 2/2/- cwt. and other bread 3/-/- ton.ThursdayAfternoon.  Route march to Durrington & Figheldean.  Easily our most pleasant route march to date.Whilst halted on the road some school children passed and the boys threw them pennies.  The first little girl who superceded the main body went away with 4/- in pennies.Night.  To Amesbury for a walk.
Thursday 20thRoute march to Amesbury mile post.Afternoon.  Kit inspection.
