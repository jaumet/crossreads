<p>transports packed with Ghurkhas and Sikhs anchored near us.  They were bound for France.  We eventually disembarked at Suez, and continued our journey overland.  It was a very interesting trip on account of its historical associations, and took 7 hours to accomplish before reaching our destination Cairo.  Periodical and picturesque views were had of the Canal &amp; Bitter Lakes interspersed by ships and sand storms.  One would travel for miles with nothing but a sea of barren sand visible, but at other times would enter a native village surrounded by a veritable bower of green.  When the train pulled  up at any of these places, we were besieged by natives selling</p>
