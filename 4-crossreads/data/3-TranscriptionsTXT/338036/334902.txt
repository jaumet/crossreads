
[17th May contd]He sent a good deal back during afternoon.  We had a late tea, Alf & I did cooking.  Immediately afterwards a terrific artillery duel opened. our shanty was rocking about rather nastily.  It continued for an hour.  Not many casualties.  Fritz raided but was cut up & he did not succeed in penetrating our trenches.
Friday 18th [May]Was surprised & pleased to get the news from Major Honman that Sam had won the Military Medal during the heavy bombardment of 5/6 May.  The major was highly elated.  The order awarding the medal read as follows:-
No 1181 Sgt Crook S. R. 9th Aust Field Amb was at Charing Cross Dressing Station during the heavy bombardment of night of 5/6th May 1917.  He lost no opportunity in seeing to the safety & comfort of patients & personnel under heavy shell fire.  H.E. & shrapnel (20 shell holes are within a radius of 50 yds of dressing station)  he assisted at & directed the removal of patients to safety.
