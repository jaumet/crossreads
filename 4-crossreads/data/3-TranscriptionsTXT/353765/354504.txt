 May 1915 [Note in margin:] Friday 14th &ndash; of   appointed as C.O. All pleased to hear news. Especially the officers.  All  Nearly all packed. 2nd L to F.A. leaves Maadi Mid at 15th, Cairo 5.30am 16th expect arrive Alexandria noon &amp; embark straight away SS Menominee. Major Croll in charge not Major Mac. 15th &ndash; Completing packing &amp; taking "Bungalow" stuff to camp in morning. In afternoon Brigadier announced to L.H.F.A. that transports were not to go. Great disappointment for them. Bearers wgn orderlies &amp; tent division &amp; 5 officers going. Also time of leaving altered to 8pm. Cheers etc before leaving all happy cheering &amp; singing "Tipperary" &amp; other songs on way to stn. 5th Marched from Bab-el-Louk stn to Cairo stn also singing &amp; cheering any white girls we saw. Wishart, Guthrie &amp; another dropped out on a/c of pace. [Note in margin:] Left Maadi 15th &ndash; 5th Regt first leave Cairo stn. 6th &amp; L.H.F a next, then 7th L.H. Some of L.H.F.A. including self stood on our dig &amp; wouldn't nor be imposed upon &amp; pushed anywhere so missed our train &amp; travelled by goods in cattle wgns. Fun with officers at Cairo stn over stretchers &amp; embarking. Eventually we hunted some off the platform. Stretched out on paper later &amp; got about 1 &frac12; hrs sleep. Wilkie, Geo Goodchap &amp;   Morgan singing till about 3am. Up about 5am on Sunday. Arrived at Alexandria [Note in margin:] Alexandria Sunday 16th &ndash; about 6.15am, unloaded &amp; reloaded equipment on boat. Breakfast about 9am. Cooked our own (Rob did). Had snack at J.W.C. Assn on wharf. English people there. Enjoyed it. Duffy &amp; I wandered around looking at boats &amp; Territorials etc. Dinner 7pm. No leave granted. 5th 6th &amp; 7th L.H., 2nd L.H.F.A., some of 3rd L.H. Bde &amp; NZ Red Cross on board. Boat packed like sardines. Standring &amp; I camped
