Last night the big guns were silent, but the machine guns were all out, a perfect din and rattle all night.<br/>Trenches Saturday 15th. 16 0455  Last night was the quietest that we have had. Early in the evening, we were entertained by a big German gun or guns which I think was trying to fix up our battery.<br/>It fired about 60 shells which must have been 9 inch at least. We could trace the course of the shells which went over our place &amp; landed some distance a head, &amp; would burst with an awful explosion<br/>Although it was some distance away, we could feel the rush of wind after each explosion. After that, we had very little big gun firing. I think there must be a German sniper in our lines, there is a lot of sniping going on that we cannot account for, otherwise.<br/>Received PC from Tom, who is now in Egypt. Will's battalion I think is now out of the trenches.<br/>Bae St Maur. We are now out of the trenches for a while. We are billeted in a filthy place that was one time a large factory.
