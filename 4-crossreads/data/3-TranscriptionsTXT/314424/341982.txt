This evening several of our planes surprised an enemy plane that had just emerged from a cloud &amp; forced it to land behind our lines.
Tuesday April 30. 1918. 1366.Showery most of the day.
This is the first village that I have been in, in France where there was any difficulty in getting water. There is one well in the village &amp; it is 250 ft to the water. Most of the water is carried 4 kilos &amp; when the tanks are empty we have to fall back on the well.

Wednesday May 1. 1918. 1367.May Day! What a contrast to May Day in Australia &amp; especially in Melbourne. Here all is war &amp; preparations for war. There war is unknown &amp; its horrors unrealized except by the few
Cold &amp; cloudy this morning.
[one line of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my father today at dinner time]
This evening &amp; tonight our artillery severely straffed the enemy.
Thursday May 2. 1918. 1368.A fine day.
I was up early this morning &amp; got the breakfast ready. The artillery continues to bombard the enemy this morning. Yesterday 4 tins of cocoa &amp; milk were issued to the Transports for 40 men, &amp; today we had it for dinner. It was rather wishey washey.
Friday May 3. 1918. 1369.A fine day but rather cloudy.
