<p>When we got into our huts we layed down and went straight to sleep, but that was only for a few hours, as most of us were very hungry and hunger will keep you from doing almost anything.  After having some dinner we felt much better, but "oh" how sore our poor bones were from being cramped up in the train, but we soon got over that, as we sent into Cairo that same night.  As I have said I went into Cairo that same night, but what I saw is not worth mentioning, as it was dark and you could not see much.  The Aerodrome Camp is situated about four miles this side of Cairo and on the edge of a sandy plain, in fact it is a desert.
