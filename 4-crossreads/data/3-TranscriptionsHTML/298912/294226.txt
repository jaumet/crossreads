<p>Then some of the school was sent away &amp; in consequence our tents were changed. We got a very poor one this time &amp; one night after we had been out all the evening &amp; returned to camp at 2.30am it began to rain &amp; rain it did. Soon we were washed out. Balfour got into my bed &amp; Hop was soaked to the skin. Owing to the steep slope it washed down in torrents &amp; next morning we were soaked to the skin &amp; cold. For a week our blankets were wet through  &amp; in consequence of which I spent 48 hrs in the hospital [what for I don't know] Camp at Casula then assumed anything but a military aspect &amp; we simply did as we pleased with only a slight fear of punishment.</p>
