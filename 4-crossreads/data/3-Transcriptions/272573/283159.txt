<p>I had often noticed a peculiar sign where three roads met and a small square was fenced off. I learned to-day that eleven of the villagers had been made to dig their own graves at this spot two years ago and then they were shot by the Germans.</p>
<p>March 29th. Wed.<br/>Cycled to Steinwerck.</p>
<p>Got my photo taken in Kilts.</p>
<p>It has been twelve and one o'clock every night lately before D and I have got to bed  (<strike>lately</strike>)</p>
<p>Potatoes have been knocked off our diet list as they can't be got.</p>
<p>Portugese have taken over the part of the line at Laventie. I am not much impressed with them.</p>
<p>April 4th Wed. 5th. Thursday.</p>
<p>Plenty of operations lately  It was seven o'clock in the morning before we got to bed, having been working all night long.</p>
<p>Read a prophecy that the war is to end on the 28th of August 1917.</p>
<p>April 8th. Sun.<br/>Left the hospital to go up to the front line of trenches near Laventie.</p>
