<p>[Stamped: Headquarters. 7th Australian Infantry Brigade. No 114/255 Date 6th July 1918]</p>
<p>&quot;I am to inform you that the G.O.C., A.I.F., has approved as a special case of Lieut. Colonel R.J.A. Travers, DSO., 26th Bn., being permitted to return to Australia for termination of his appointment in the A.I.F.</p>
<p>Arrangements are being made by A.I.F. Administrative H.Qrs., for his early passage to Australia, and it is desired that he should proceed to London at a convenient date to await passage.</p>
<p>Please submit through Headquaters, Aust. Corps, your recommendation for the command of the 26th Bn., vice Lieut. Colonel Travers.&quot;</p>
<p>H.Q.,</p>
<p>26th Battalion .</p>
<p>With reference to above copy of letter from D.A.G.A.I.F.</p>
<p>Herewith Warrant for Lieut. Colonel R.J.A. Travers, DSO., to proceed to A.I.F., H.Qrs., London.</p>
<p>Please report date of departure to this office.</p>
<p>[Signature]</p>
<p>Captain, Staff Captain.</p>
<p>7th Australian Infantry Brigade.</p>
