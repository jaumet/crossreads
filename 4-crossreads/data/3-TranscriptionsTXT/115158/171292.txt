 Dec. 31.&nbsp; Early on New Year&#39;s Eve, however, we made the Western entrance to the famous Straits of Magellan, and entered into smooth water - a great relief after the tempestuous weather we had recently experienced. 
 Jan. 1. 1915.&nbsp; The day dawned extremely cold, accentuated by the wind borne from the snow clad mountains on each side of the Straits.&nbsp; The length of the Magellan Straits is almost 350 miles.&nbsp; At the western end the width is only about 2 miles.&nbsp; The scenery here is of surpassing beauty.&nbsp; Occasionally, the sun would come forth &amp; transform a desolate snow clad peak into a dazzling wealth of glittering colour.&nbsp; Generally speaking, the region on both sides is very bleak &amp; desolate, reminding one very much of the wildest parts of Scotland.&nbsp; There is more bare rock than vegetation, which only grows in the ravines where the glaciers flow, &amp; where it is more or less sheltered.&nbsp; No signs of inhabitants could be seen.&nbsp; During the forenoon watch, we sighted a small steamer heading for one of the many creeks to be found in the straits of Magellan.&nbsp; She was flying the Chilean flag,
