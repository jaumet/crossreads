<p>They got as far as Calais and were recalled to make trenches near Messines.</p>
<p>I am on picquet to night. From this on the picquet starts at 9 o'clock instead of 6, there is only 3 shifts instead of 4. I am on second shift from 12.30 to 3.30. I am doing Holmes picquet for 5 francs.</p>
<p>Saturday April 13. 1918. 1349<br/>Cloudy &amp; windy today, - a strong N.E. wind which is rather cold.</p>
<p>There has been intermittent artillery activity all day on this front, and to the S.W. of us with great intensity.<br/>[A line of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my wife tonight letter 123)]</p>
<p>Sunday April 14. 1918. 1350<br/>An exceedingly cold &amp; bleak day with a strong E.N.E wind.</p>
<p>Our camp is high up on the side of a ridge and the wind sweeps along it. This ridge is facing the south, is cultivated to the top, the fields being in terraces like steps of a stairs. In the face of the terrace, the numerous Transports camped on the ridge have dug in to protect themselves from bursting shells.
