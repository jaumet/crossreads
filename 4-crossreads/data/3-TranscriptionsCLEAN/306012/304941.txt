
we passed numerous Australian lorries taking back numbers of captured field guns & quantities of material that has fallen into our hands.  New railways are being built, signal wires put up & roads mended.  We marched into Villers Bretonneux which has of course suffered pretty badly and turned off to our camp site in an old shell-hole besprinkled apple orchard on the road to Corbie.  Here we ran up several tents and dug out some shelters to sleep in.  About 9 p.m. some taubes came over dropping bombs round about but none close to us.We are continuing our push along the line.  The prisoners taken now number 24000 & the French have taken Montdidier.
