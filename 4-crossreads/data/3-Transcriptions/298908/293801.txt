<p>broke loose &amp; carried him shoulder high all over the place &amp; finally down to his car where they had chalked all over it "Birdwood the King of the Diggers", old Birdie reckoned it was the proudest day of his life &amp; he would never forget it nor the Australians either.  They formed a ring round his car &amp; sang "For hes a jolly good fellow", Auld Lang Syne &amp; etc. before they let him go, so you can see old "Birdie" is still very popular with the A.I.F. &amp; needless to say he thinks that our lads are the finest troops in the world.  He has said so often, &amp; it nearly broke his heart when he had to leave us to take over what was left of the British 5th Army after the German break through at Cambrai last March.  I should like to see him visit Australia after the war, what a reception he would get.  He would hardly hit the ground.
