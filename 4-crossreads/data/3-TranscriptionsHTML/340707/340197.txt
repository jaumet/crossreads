<p>69<br/>at 6.30 but at 6 am it is at Abbeville A wool administrator a Miss Tompkins from Yorshire joins us in carriage.</p>
<p>On arrival I find that early boat is postponed till 12.30 on account of victory troops going across. When I come for my ticket there is some trouble as MLO wants the authority. I do not argue &amp; then WO at gangway will not let me on board &amp; then I return to MLO &amp; get a return ticket &amp; all is now well.</p>
<p>Biarritz pushes off about 2 pm &amp; we get to Dover at 4.15. I am unable to buy any pc of Dover as the train meets the boat &amp; the town is about 2 miles away. Dover is a harbour that has been erected at an enormous expenditure of money with too small openings for vessels. Within there is a fair amount of water. Over all on the hill is the old castle of Dover &amp; outside the harbour the German wreck that I saw when I first entered the channel</p>
<p>I arrive in London about 6.21 &amp; find that in spite of crowded condition of London I can still get room here though I may have to sleep one night on a stretcher.</p>
