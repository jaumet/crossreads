 (37) a strange republic of wild and wayward freedom in which the beauteous aristocrat of the garden and the scorned weed- bred plebeian of the wayside are equal and as sisters. Almost it seems that nature has given a charter of freedom to each rare plant and common weed that they may with a riot of growth over wrap and hide away from heaven the ruin and the wreck and shame of mortal man. War which has made terrified slaves of man has left all that we call the mean and least worthy of breathing things, free to devour and destroy.
