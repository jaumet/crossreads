<p>Alexandria Egypt<br/>May 22<br/>Wed. Finished loading gun carriages field kitchens etc. &amp; awaiting the arrival of troops for embarking.</p>
<p>23<br/> The 1st wooden standard ship arrived here at Alexandria from the U.S.A. Very poor specimen of a steamer. The K.I.Hind, Malwa &amp; Leasowe Castle embarking troops. Rumour of the new Orient 14,500 ton steamer the Orland going to replace the Omrah in this convoy. News through this day another 2 German subs sunk by French distroyers yesterday.</p>
<p>24<br/>This day embarked 2000 troops from Palastine &amp; 30 sisters for the Western front of France. Fine hardy chaps. Sunburnt &amp; the majority been away here for the last 3 years &amp; over.<br/>5 pm left the wharf &amp; all leave stopped for anchorage.</p>
<p>25<br/>Still at anchor. All the troopships full. 20,000 awaiting the escort to arrive. All troops told off to boat stations &amp; raft &amp; lifebelt drill. As per usual the deck swarmed with troops.</p>
<p>26<br/>Sunday. 10 am boat drill<br/>11 am religious services of different denominations</p>
