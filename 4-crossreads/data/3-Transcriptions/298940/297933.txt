<p>and really is I think, a very confused affair.<br/>After the usual sparring round, one commences the offensive &amp; always deals a blow different from that which the other is preparing to counter. The other not knowing really what to do, has a little half hearted attack on his own, so that it really resolves itself into each side attacking rather badly, and any old defence put up at all. It is, of course, like all new forms of skill: one knows the object in view, &amp; in the absence of any clear idea how to attain it, follows a kind of cross country route</p>
