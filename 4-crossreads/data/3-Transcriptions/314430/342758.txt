<p>halted in a field for dinner. At 2 P M march resumed. Travelled along the outskirts of Salisbury, and then we turned into a long avenue of oaks leading to Wilton. As we tramped through this long <strike>aisle of</strike> leafy aisle the bands played lively airs. We pushed on to the village of Barford-St-Martins, thence to Herdcott, passing the Australian Convalescent camp there. Reached Fovant Camp at 5 P M.</p>
<p>October 15th:- Quite settled down in the new Camp. This Camp is <strike>quite</strike> close to the villages of Fovant, Dinton, Sutton Mandeville, and almost six miles from Tisbury, the seat of the Earls of Arundel.</p>
<p>October 21st :- The Countess of Pembroke allowed me to view the interior of Wilton House, one of the most glorious homes in England. The steward conducted me.
