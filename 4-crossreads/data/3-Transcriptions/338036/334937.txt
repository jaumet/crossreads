<p>[18th Aug. contd]<br/>as Fritz would persist in shelling it.  We were again fortunate as a little later we got a lorry going to Ballieul for another load of 'Civvies' &amp; we hadn't left the town 5 minutes when a shell lobbed into it just near the station.  Back to billets safely.  No sooner dark than the nightly visitor came over fully laden with bombs &amp; didnt hesitate to drop a good number but left us sacredly alone as the Archies gave him a hearty welcome.</p>
<p>Sunday 19th<br/>Very pleased today to receive an Aussy mail &amp; the right sources too.  Took a case to Steenwerck C.C.S. in the afternoon &amp; just missed by shrapnel on the main road coming back.  Duck shooting at the balloon.  At night at 9.15 he made his usual nightly bombing trip but the Archies &amp; searchlights bluffed him off.</p>
<p>Monday 20th<br/>In the afternoon I took a stroll to St Jans Cappel on way to Mont de Cattes.  Very quiet place.  On the way I saw several old women making lace by bobbins that they used to move &amp; toss about like a machine.  It was very clever.  To night some trips by Fritz but again the Archies bluffed him.</p>
