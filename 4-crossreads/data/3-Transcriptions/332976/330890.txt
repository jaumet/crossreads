<p>July 1917<br/>5th   This morning the company went down to the canal just above the lock &amp; made a start to form a heavy bridge, calculated to carry 20 tons. (12" X 12" timber &amp; 12" R S Joists being used with 3 three part pontoons as a pier.)   At dinner time I was warned for guard &amp; had the afternoon off until 6 o'clock, when I mounted the Quarter Guard with three men.<br/>6th   I was on guard all day to-day &amp; was relieved by the 8th Field Coy at 6. P.M.<br/>7th   The Company were on the canal again this morning &amp; we formed another type</p>
