 on the limbres to day. I did two yesterday morning. Last night a number of enemy aeroplanes came over dropping bombs in the vicinity of Baileul &amp; again to night. I got some washing done by a Frenchwoman &amp; received it to night. She only charged 8d for washing a shirt, towel &amp; a pair of underpants. Tuesday Feb. 19. 1918. 1296. Cold &amp; frosty, but fine. I was again working on repairing Limbres all day. We went for a bath in the afternoon but could not get any, as they had not been notified.  Wednesday Feb. 20. 1918. 1297 I was on picquet last night &ndash; 2nd second shift from 9.10 to 12.00. I had a good fire and made myself a cup of tea also polished a jug made out of a brass shell case for Cliff Thomson.. It was not my turn for picquet &ndash; but done it for A Holmes and received 5 francs for it. I am picquet to night, as it is my turn. I was unfortunate enough to get 3rd picquet 12.20 to 3.30 Tonight I received the photos from the French Photographer, 6 bust and 6 group. They are fairly good &amp; cost 8 francs for the dozen. We had a bath tonight just before tea. Two of the hack men were sent to the hospital to day. "Darky" Tregenza, &amp;
