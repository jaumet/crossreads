 breakwater before breakfast. Here we have been lying for two days &ndash; feeling liked caged lions as no leave has been allowed. The harbour is full of shipping  - vessels belonging to neutral countries conspicuous by their prominent display of nationality both by flag &amp;  painted lettering. Transports crowded with Troops are coming in thick and fast. 3 boatloads of Australians have arrived so far today each containing about 2,000 men. Managed to get into signalling communication with them this afternoon. Hills rise all round the harbour &amp; near the city  are  houses can be seen on the slopes nestling among the forest green. Orders received to-night that we are to commence disembarkation to-morrow &amp; establish field hospital
