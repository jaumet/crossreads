 1915 May. 15 Sat. Am living very comfortably for active service at present:- share a very comfortable room with Sgt. Clerk; nice bed with mosquito nets, sheets, etc. &amp; hot baths, up to date lavatories; occasional plum puddings purloined from doubtful sources; puddings, tea, cocoa etc. from the Arab waiters. Lady Maxwell (wife of General Officer Commanding the Forces in Egypt) paid a visit during the afternoon. The patients gave her a great welcome &amp; we finished up with a big group photo. in front of Mena House. Later in the afternoon received orders to proceed to Gezireh on the morrow.
