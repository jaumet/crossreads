<p>29/4/16<br/>Serg Bostock leaves for Duada with wireless set. Hard work all day &amp; no breakfast  -  no dinner &amp; feeling very empty. Short rations from yesterday making it worse. Chase the train for a crust of bread but have no luck. Makes me think of all the puddings I have turned down at home because it was burnt.Wish it was here now. Tempted to eat a bit of candle. Horses also short of food. Such a godforsaken hole as this is the beginning of active service conditions. In the end we are forced to swap a hat for half a loaf of bread from Tommies.</p>
<p>30/4/16<br/>Food at last. Bully beef for breakfast.(from Tommies) Bread &amp; jam for dinner &amp;rice. &amp; raisins for tea. To satisfy our feelings we back four times .Each one in turn takes the lid of the dixie back for a little more &amp; each time  we get the lid filled. Glorious feed</p>
<p>1/5/16<br/>Picquet from 2am to 6.30 am word received that General Townsend has surrendered to the Turks.</p>
<p>Cook's fatigues for the day, chopping wood  &amp; spud barbering. Gows put on cable waggons. Trickett &ndash; a lad just arrived &amp; an amateur fiend drives us all mad by yapping all night &amp; day about wireless &amp; declaring that the engine in the pack set shook the ground &amp; the dynamo went.<br/> [indecipherable] &ndash; flinging his arms about. At last we persuade him to sleep by the instruments &amp; &amp; he did so to our complete delight.</p>
