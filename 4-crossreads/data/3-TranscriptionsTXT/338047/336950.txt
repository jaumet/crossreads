
1919Feb - Marfull of shells 5.9s & whiz bangs & Minenwerfers also a lot of flares, rockets & old clothes, & a truck load of second hand brass fittings (pinched from the froggies I suppose).  At night time these flares were to be seen going up all over the place.  I got a small bomb & unloaded it.  Another walk took us to Dimont & through there to Domechaux.  The day following we intended walking to Doulers, but the rain turned us back.  The winter seemed to be fast breaking up & there were signs of spring everywhere.
Mar 5On 5 March the second draft left for Aussie.  A great number of the 3rd Reinforcements went with this one & it include nearly all the old "hard heads"  Rattray McDermott etc.
Mar 10On 10 March we moved to fresh billets leaving Beugnies at 9 am in fighting order (ie no pack).  Our packs had been sent on ahead by lorry with our blankets.  Outside of Sars Poteries we picked up the 29th & 32nd Battns.  The 31st were a day ahead of us.  We marched
