<p>Northward of the same wood the 20th Battalion held ground while the 19th Battalion found a location somewhere to the Southward of it, and the 17th Battalion was operating in the same area. From these approximate positions, the opposite band of the Somme could be plainly seen, as well as the chalk cliffs of nearby Clery, and the high ground beyond, where troops of the Third Division were to cooperate, they already having encountered, in that area, severe hostile artillery fire. In the banks of the Canal de la Somme, and in the chalk cliffs, which almost beetle over it there were lines of German  dugouts, and strong posts, besides excellent cover for snipers. Before darkness set in a German train actually</p>
