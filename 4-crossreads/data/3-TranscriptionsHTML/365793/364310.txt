<p>Jly 29 crossing the mouth of the Harbour; old Sol has gone to rest full half an hour since, &amp; a sea mist is creeping slowly in. Soon the outermost cliffs will be hidden from view, &amp; I guess things will be getting a wee bit damp. Almost I can imagine myself back in dear old Tamworth &ndash; sitting in the same old easy chair &amp; gazing out over the familiar hills. The tide is setting in, &amp; there is just the slightest breeze &ndash; enough <u>only</u> to ripple the surface of the water, &amp; make one forget the heat of the fast fading day &ndash;</p>
