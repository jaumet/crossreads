<p>5th<br/>M.'s birthday.  Many happy returns.  Still anchored off Anzac, and a lot more sick and wounded came on board.  Some also put off "Salta" on trawlers, sent to Imbros.</p>
<p>6th<br/>Another Hospital ship came in through the night.  Two men died through the night, and were taken off this morning by the mine-sweeper "Loch Broom", and taken <strike>up</strike> out to sea for burial, both being covered with the Union Jack.  Left at 12 a.m. for Imbros, &amp; left Imbros 3 p.m. for Mudros, arriving there 7 p.m.</p>
<p>7th<br/>Left Mudros at 9 a.m. for Alexandria.  Saw S.S. "Aquitania" sister ship of the "Lusitania", with four funnels, in Mudros Harbour.</p>
<p>9th<br/>Arrived Alexandria 9 a.m.  Harbor full of shipping.  Left at 2 p.m. for Cairo, in Red Cross train.  Had a fast trip, arriving Cairo 5.30 p.m.  Taken in Red Cross Ambulances to Gezireh Palace Hospital.  This is a lovely place</p>
