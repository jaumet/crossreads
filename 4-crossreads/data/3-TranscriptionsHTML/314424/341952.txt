<p>Today the enemy is shelling villages behind us and also on our left.</p>
<p>I wrote to Cis today.<br/>[a line of shorthand &ndash; I also wrote to my dear wife this evening Letter 121]</p>
<p>Wednesday April 3. 1918. 1339.<br/>A fine day but threatening rain in the afternoon.</p>
<p>I was on cooks fatigue all day. It is not a bad job, but there is a lot to do.</p>
<p>There was great aerial activity by the enemy this morning &amp; occasionally throughout the day.</p>
<p>Thursday April 4. 1918. 1340.<br/>A wet showery day, with mud &amp; slush everywhere.</p>
<p>There was a constant bombardment last night which grew in intensity towards daylight, and continued at intervals throughout the day.</p>
<p>A. Holmes said in the cook house today, "rather than be captured he would put a bullet through his head &amp; was promptly counted out. I was on cooks fatigue today.</p>
