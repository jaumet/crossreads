<p>The course is 3 points N. of N.W., the sea is freshing and the wind is from the West.</p>
<p>I read another "Times" History of the War. Part 18. Vol. II, chapter XXXIV "The Battles of the Aisne" and Chapter 35 dealing with "Military Aeronautics and the British air service"</p>
<p>Wireless messages come through every day. Yesterday word came that the Russians captured 12,000 prisoners &amp; 45 guns also that the French &amp; British did well. To day a wireless stated further gains on all the fronts, but that the Germans succeeded in navagiating a submarine the [indecipherable] to America with 1000 tons of dye stuffs on board. She was chased by French &amp; British cruisers 20 miles from the coast which delayed her 4 days.</p>
<p>Before dark we passed a large ship on the Port side. Her Hull was below the Horizon.</p>
<p>Wednesday July 12. 1916.<br/>There was heavy rain early this morning, which drove those on deck below. The wind is W.N.W. The course is 4 points N. of N.West.</p>
<p>There was a short arm inspection this morning &amp; a bath parade.</p>
