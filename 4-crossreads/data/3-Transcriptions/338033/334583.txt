<p>taken ashore on tenders, and entrained in carriages this time, eight to a compartment.<br/>How splendid it was to look out opon green fields and civilized villages and towns. Until flowers were in abundance, which were a great relief to the eyes after two months of sea and desert. I thought the Hawthorn hedges looked very pretty, their dark green hue showing up in the green fields, which were of a paler shade. The fields are cut up into small areas, and every farmer goes in for a few sheep, cattle, horses and poultry as side lines.<br/>We travelled through Devonshire and Sommerset, which is noted for cidar apples.
