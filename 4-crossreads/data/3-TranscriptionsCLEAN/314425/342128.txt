A couple of duds from the anti aircraft guns fell near the horse lines this morning but did no damage.
Monday July 29. 1918. 1455.A fine day. I was on second picquet last night from 11.30 to 2. Soon after 12 there was a very heavy bombardment which lasted with varying intensity for about 2 hours. The second batch of ration limbers left here at 1.am and the first returned before 12. It helped to make the time pass more quickly.This evening I made an ashtray out of a Fritz shell case for J. Chippendale.The papers contain further news of the German retreat on the Marne. Allied cavalry and tanks follow the Infantry.
