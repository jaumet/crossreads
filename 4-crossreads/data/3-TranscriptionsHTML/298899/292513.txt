<p>them &amp; during a bombardment or barrage the din is deafening. The ammunition dumps are generally set alight by incendiary shells &amp; the spectacle although grand is fearful, the flames creep around the shells &amp; one by one they explode &amp; destroy everything nearby. Gas shells play an important part especially with artillery, the new mustard gas being very effective &amp; yet more humane than Phosgene. It burns the flesh even when the respirator protects the throat &amp; eyes &amp; having practically no smell men are often gassed without knowing it especially if they use a dugout which had previously been gassed. Time &amp; again I have seen men report from battery for several days in succession having been gassed from the same source. Very few of the Field units escape casualties now-a-days, the back areas being shelled and bomb dropping increasing daily. Even when men are relieved from the line unless taken well back they are harassed</p>
