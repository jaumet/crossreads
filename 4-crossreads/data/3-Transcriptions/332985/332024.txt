<p>[Transcriber's note:  This is the third diary of Farrier Sergeant Thomas Edward Goodwin during his time in the area of the Somme, around Albert and, towards the end of the diary, at Steenvoorde near the Belgian border.  He describes graphically the bombing that occurs in and around the area where he is attending to the horses and supplying ammunition to the front.  He also makes a note each day of the weather and the general living conditions.]</p>
