Anyway I'm allright myself so far. I hope this letter will get you at "Orvieto" Mum. I suppose Claude is training now for a commission I hope, - we are wondering wether he will marry before he comes away. I must write &amp; congratulate old Nance &ndash; I saw the announcement in the "Tas. Mail"</p>
<p>Mum darling I hope you are taking things quietly &amp; not giving that leg a chance to get bad again.</p>
<p>Well dear I'll say cheer-oh &amp; write a few lines to Nance &amp; Joan</p>
<p>Very best love from your boy<br/>John</p>
<p>"L" 'EARTS</p>
<p>P.S. The weather is lovely here at present, rather hot sometimes though. We're not far from the beach &amp; of course get plenty of swimming (often under shell fire from old man Turk) which is a Godsend to the troops (not the shell fire) most chaps are as brown as niggers now.</p>
