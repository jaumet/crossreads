After ploughing through mud knee deep we halted for a rest &amp; were lucky to get another lift on a Motor Lorrie. The roads were very congested &amp; miles of traffic were all held up on account of the hard pulling , we eventually reached Fricourt, which is the old line of trenches &amp; which was the first village taken in the British offensive. Of the village itself nothing remains save a few scattered brick &amp; bare tree trunks all twisted &amp; distorted.</p>
<p>The ground is all broken &amp; is heaped up just as through a Volcano had taken place &amp; misplaced the whole surface of the country.</p>
<p>The more pathetic sights are clearly evinced one either side of the road, where grave yards containing the bodies of British French &amp; Germans will always bear testimony of the furious fighting which took place.</p>
<p>Two trenches about 40 yards apart winding over hill &amp; dale showed the relative position of both force before the attack , the Germans having the better part on account of them holding the ridge which encircles the village.</p>
