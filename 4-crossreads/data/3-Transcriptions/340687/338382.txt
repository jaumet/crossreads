<p>March, 1917. Monday 19<br/>Day cloudy with strong N.E wind &amp; rain in evening. Cooked bacon for breakfast &amp; ran her at 10.30. No inspection &amp; only rations &amp; fuel came. Another run in evening. Went over to 3rd coy in evening &amp; got a letter. Home at 7 and wrote diary till bed at 9. News. Enemy 15 miles beyond Bapaume. [indecipherable] fallen. Success at Ypres. French success on 22 mile front to a depth 1-2 miles. Rumours of first german line captured from coast to here. Australians to spend another fortnight here &amp; then rest for a good while &amp; ten train journey.</p>
