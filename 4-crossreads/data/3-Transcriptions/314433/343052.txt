We then crawled down a Sap, a distance of about 1000 yards under all sorts of unpleasant things, including machine guns and 'iron founderies'.<br/>"The excitement started when we got to the first Fritz dugout, when we took 5 prisoners. One man stood at the door and roared out: 'Anyone down there? You could see nothing, but a little lamp burning in the distance below (It was a big dugout). We intended throwing bombs down, but on second thoughts found courage enough to walk down finding in all 5  prisoners, who were promptly 'souvenired' of all their German possessions, one of them being severely wounded, and decorated with an Iron Cross, First Class. He turned out to be a Sergeant Major. He hopped over, and calling me back, asked if I was a doctor as he wanted his wounds attended to. Lieutenant D. H. Authon+ M.C. ("A" coy) the came along and had a little chat, and sent 4 prisoners to the rear. I 'pulled the foot' of the Sergeant-Major.
