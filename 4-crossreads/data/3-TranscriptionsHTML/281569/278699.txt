<p>9 July, 1916 Sunday</p>
<p>We arrived at Oghratina OK about 6 last night &amp; took up our position in the same old place at dawn the different Regts moved out, one to Saqia the other to Debabis &amp; then commenced there advance on Oghratina burning &amp; clearing all the Hods on the way in &amp; very soon a huge ring of fires were to be seen the 2nd Regt got 5 prisoners at Saqia &amp; another five got away, they being too fast for our horses, they run up the banks &amp; slide down the other side while the horses have to go round,  we finished up at 2 pm we had a great signal station on the old battlefield &amp; were in touch in all directions.</p>
<p>Moved back to Romani at dusk &amp; got in amongst our tea at 11.30.</p>
<p>Tired out</p>
<p>10 July, 1916 Monday</p>
<p>91 wks from home</p>
<p>Reveille at 5</p>
<p>Had a quiet day today, nothing doing, the men went down for a swim after dinner &amp; it went great, plenty of surf etc, the horses enjoyed it even more than we &amp; several of them you could not get out of the water.</p>
<p>Got home in time for tea &amp; then wrote letters to home.</p>
<p>Nelson communications were up to putty again yesterday &amp; an inquiry is to be made into it today as to how he employed his signallers &amp; what he did with them.</p>
<p>All other units OK especially the third.</p>
