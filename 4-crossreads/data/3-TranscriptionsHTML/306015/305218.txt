<p>army stretchers.  The hospital is situated in a garden and after the dirt &amp; dust of the billets, life here is very easy &amp; pleasant.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; After the first day or so I felt all right and quite able to take the full benefit of this new-found liberty.</p>
<p>The boys here wish they could find a brand of cigarette that would keep their temperature up, in order to be kept here for a good while.</p>
<p>The orderly of my ward, "B. Pyrexia" is Murray Sinclair, whom I knew at the Varsity. &amp; strange to say the local Y.M. Rep is Harry Peake, also of the Uni.</p>
<p>Down here I have noticed some of the <strike>field</strike> toilers of the fields at work.   All the cultivation is done by the old men, the women &amp; children.
