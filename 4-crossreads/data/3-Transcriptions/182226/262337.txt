<p>Thursday&nbsp;11 November, 1915</p>
<p>Are making hay while the sun shines.</p>
<p>Sid Moss, a chap from the 13<sup><span style="font-size: 10.8333330154419px; line-height: 11.5555543899536px;">th</span></sup>&nbsp;M.G. have chummed up. We left the Sailors home at 7.45 a.m. had a breakfast &amp; went &amp; played billiards &amp; drank beer till lunch time. We then went &amp; sent a wire to Sids people at Gibraltar for&nbsp;&pound;5.</p>
<p>After that we took a ride on hired bikes about 3 miles out, at a wayside pub we patronised with the Maltese.</p>
<p>I took photos of the mob &amp; gave out &quot;picture cards&quot; to a lot of school kiddies. On the way back all went well till we were nearly home, then Sid ran into a donkey &amp; almost immediately after, into am[?]</p>
<p>There was a terrible mix up. The police took a whole host of names. It cost us a stollar[?] between us to square matters.</p>
<p>Slept at &quot;Hotel De France&quot; Valletta.</p>
