<p>Today is Good Friday.  There were some sports on board in the forward part of the ship.<br/>I was inoculated today, final dose and a large one.  I got a very nasty spill this morning.  I came down wallop on my seat.  It was a heavy fall and I was unwell all the day.</p>
<p>Saturday April 19.1919   160<br/>A beautiful day.  We expect to get to the Cape on Monday and to [indecipherable] tomorrow.<br/>I corrected the Colonel's shorthand lessons this morning and then continued the class from 10 to 12.<br/>The distance travelled yesterday for the 24 hours ending noon was 297 miles and [indecipherable].  The course is about the same.<br/>There was another concert tonight.  There was a prize given for the worst song.  [end of shorthand]</p>
<p>Sunday April 20.1919   161<br/>20 day on the voyage.  Dull &amp; cloudy. We expect to see land this evening and to arrive at Cape Town tomorrow.</p>
<p>Many letters are being written today as to morrow morning at 8  is the latest that all letters must be posted in the ships Post Office. They will go without a stamp. All letters posted in Cape Town must be stamped. This evening I wrote to Kitty Martin Kitty Gilmore Maggie Martin.</p>
