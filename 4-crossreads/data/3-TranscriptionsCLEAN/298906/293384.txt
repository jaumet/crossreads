
In the afternoon we went for a route march & hit the Main road & all along there were gangs of German prisoners working repairing the road & etc, one of the men in my platoon had a couple of ribbons of the Germans, a first class & a second of the Iron Cross & these he showed to Germans here & there, they recognized them at once you would have laughed if you could have seen the grabs they made at them.27th My luck was out last night just as we were leaving our billets I got grabbed for a picquet, we were all going down to Mericourt to see the fight between Mellish of our Transport & Norton of A.
