 They are still complaining at home of not receiving any letters from me &amp; are still quoting the fact of Arthur's letters having reached there.  They don't seem to be able to calculate the  change  chance of my letters having been posted not till a couple of mails later than his, and this is what actually happened.  I have sent Field Post Cards in acknowledgement of them all. 
 I have now learnt that the aeroplane (previous page) [Page 80] was forced to the ground owing to engine trouble &ndash; but that as soon as she landed the Turks opened &amp; damaged one of her planes with shrapnel.  The aviator escaped &amp; the machine was secured &amp; repaired. 
 It has been bitterly cold today &amp; it seems that winter is now on top of us. 
 Friday 15th Oct 1915 
  Ack. Dad's letter of 29th Aug. &amp; Mabs 16th, 23rd &amp; 29th Aug.  
  New Zealand casualties  - Christchurch Sept. 15 &ndash; "The casualties among the N.Z. forces at the Dardanelles are as follows &ndash; 
 Officers 78 killed, 201 wounded, 15 missing Men 1231 killed, 4083 wounded, 530 missing 
 Totals 1309 killed, 4284 wounded, 545 missing 
 This gives a total of casualties of all ranks of 6138. 
 Saturday 16th Oct 1915 
 [Text crossed out] Home letters tell me that Sydney went mad one day in August when the City was informed that the Dardanelles had fallen.  Flags were flown &amp; all were wild with excitement.  Perth too seems to have received this huge "canard".  What a huge joke considering our stagnation here.
