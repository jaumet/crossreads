<p>Jan. 12</p>
<p>During the next few days we passed several ships, which, however, were all British or neutrals.</p>
<p>Jan. 15.</p>
<p>Three days out from Albrolhos Island we passed the islands of Fernando Noronha, now used as a penal settlement for Brazil.&nbsp; There is a wireless station erected here.&nbsp; The highest point of the island is in the form of a pinnacle, &amp; so curiously formed as to give the impression of leaning &amp; overhanging.</p>
<p>Jan. 16.</p>
<p>The following day we crossed the Equator for the sixth time during the commission, and we ran into a belt of moisture which mae the atmosphere very oppressive.&nbsp; On several occasions we passed sailing ships beating their way South, a sight not commonly met with, as their tracks are generally away from the steamer tracks.&nbsp; Finally, we sighted the barren Cape Verde Islands &amp; we arrived at St. Vincent for coal.</p>
<p>Jan. 19.</p>
<p>We had visited this port on August 1st. 1913 when on our way out to Australia via the Cape of Good Hope.&nbsp; We had thus circumnavigated the globe.&nbsp; It rarely falls to the lot of the modern naval officer to voyage round the world in the same ship, &amp;, consequently, everybody was proud of the achievement.&nbsp; We ar once commenced coaling ship.&nbsp; During the coaling a very ugly incident manifested</p>
