<p>29 July, 1916</p>
<p>"The Pilgrimage"<br/>It aint no use in swearin,<br/>It aint no use to fret<br/>It aint no use agrousin<br/>Or getting all upset<br/>The wilderness is rotten<br/>All dust &amp; flies &amp; tears<br/>But the Israelites they stood it<br/>For years , &amp; years ,&amp; years</p>
<p>The Willie &ndash; willies choke you<br/>The dust storms get you down<br/>The red sun spoils your beauty<br/>And burns you blue &amp; brown<br/>The drought is simply shocking<br/>And the thirst our Squadron fears<br/>Will only be abolished<br/>By Beers, &amp; beers, &amp; beers.</p>
<p>The war wont last for ever,<br/>This scrap will soon be done,<br/>And we'll have done our little bit<br/>A straffing of the Hun,<br/>And when we get back home again<br/>And greet our little dears<br/>All thoughts of Egypt will be drowned<br/>In Cheers &amp; cheers &amp; cheers.<br/>By Bluegum</p>
