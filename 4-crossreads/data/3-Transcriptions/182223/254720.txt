<p>Thursday 2 December 1915&nbsp;<br />Orders at 10 am. to take Medical Charge of the S.S. Themistocles at Suez for Australia<br />Preparing all day to set out.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />Wrote letters to Hyman, Mrs Reardon, Peck, Sister Sutcliffe, Sister Johnson, Mdm Andebon, Dr Schuber.<br /><span style="line-height: 1.6;">Sent cables to Girls &amp; Mrs</span><span style="line-height: 1.6;">&nbsp;M. M.Knowles.<br />Everything ready just at midnight.</span></p>
<p>Friday 3 December 1915&nbsp;<br />Left Cairo 11-30 a.m. for Suez. Arrived Suez 4-30 am&nbsp;<br />Went on board S.S. Themistocles.<br />Good ship. Not too many to look after.&nbsp;<br />30 orderlies about 150 patients.</p>
<p>Saturday 4 December 1915&nbsp;<br />Left Suez wharf about 8. am.<br />A comfortable &amp; agreeable day.&nbsp;<br />Getting all into working order&nbsp;<br />Bed 11 p.m.</p>
<p>Sunday 5 December 1915&nbsp;<br />Mass 7-30 a.m.<br />Steaming comfortably down the Red Sea&nbsp;<br />Ordinary routine work during day</p>
