<p>To my mind it is fighters who are needed at present and not "Toy soldiers".</p>
<p>From the tone of our O.C. address everything points to us going away from here very shortly.</p>
<p>Large numbers of camels are used here for transport of water stores, etc across the sandy desert for the troops.</p>
<p>Mules and Mule Carts are in use also by the Egyptians. These carts have very large wheels.</p>
<p>Feb 17th. Thurs.<br/>Building a sandbag wall around our kitchen.</p>
<p>Beautifully moonlight at nightime at present.</p>
<p>Feb 18th. Fri.<br/>Read the "Sentimental Bloke" by Dennis, and Aust &ndash; poet. A very entertaining book.</p>
<p>Feb 20th. Sun.<br/>Mumps has broken out amongst the men.</p>
<p>Camp inspected by Generals Murray and Ford and about a dozen other hangers-on.</p>
<p>Two meningitis suspects to-day.</p>
<p>Feb 21st.</p>
<p>An in rush of patients, all sick.</p>
<p>A mess tent has been fixed up for us, and the usual gamblers congregate here at night-time. Gambling with them seems to have an intense fascination for some men. One thing about them they seem to take their loss and gains in a sportmanlike fashion.</p>
