Ourselves, we used to hold a Brigade front with a Battalion; & at last our Battalion could not have been much over 400 fighting strength.  When we were relieved by Tommies it always seemed that a fearful crowd came along.  Perhaps our system of having only a few men in a position was best.  It saved the chance of casualties; but then  men  were wanted to do the work. 
 While we were at Cercus, a town nearby, called Morbeque, was wiped out by shell-fire.  I was in there when Fritz dropped his preliminary shells: in spite of all that may be said he usually gives a few days warning before he pours shells into any place - or it may not be a warning but only range finding. A bright little kiddie, who did not seem to know whether to be frightened or not, was near me; so I gave him my hand & took him home.  I found his Mother with three younger children, one a baby.
