
8 ThursdayThe Atlantic sea doesn't seem to be so risky as regarding coming in contact with submarines.  Everyone looking forward to the termination of this journey. 2 Dead marches held on two pieces of pudding cooked on the ship (buried at sea).  Sea rough at early morning otherwise calm all afternoon day.  Rumored death of Lord Kitchener.
9 FridayGun on boat fired off, practising on a barrell thrown into sea.  Very sick from Colic all daydid couldn't eat anything, paraded & got some medicine, but didn't do me any good.  Sea rather calm.  Weather getting very much colder.
10 SaturdayStill very sick, had some breakfast & dinner but no tea.  A very cold day necessary to wear an overcoat all day. Stayed below all the afternoon.
11 SundayArrived at Plymouth at 4 pm disembarked & entrained for Parkhouse camp Salisbury at 8 pm. Very cold travelling in train.
