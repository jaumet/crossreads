 77 October 21st wound, as a result of too much walking yesterday. It is very painful. 
 Went to church this mng, when Commr Lewis officiated in the absence of the minister &ndash; It was not a bad Service. 
 Recd letters from Bert Father, Mabel Blatch Aunt Min &amp; Edie &amp; Aunt Sally &amp; Dot Poole. I was so overcome with Joy at receiving letters from Home. I was moved to tears especially when reading Aunt Mins letter &ndash; 
 Played Bridge with Olphert &ndash; 
 Dot had heard about poor Leslies death 
 October 22nd Walk held today, when the officers took lunch &amp; stayed out till 1 pm I did not go &amp; was too cold &amp; wet so I played billiards. Bell has undertaken to teach me, which Instr I am glad to receive &ndash; 
 There is a Bridge tournament being held. I have entered having Neame as my partner, we played Windebank &amp; Browning on our first round &amp; won by 500 odd &ndash; Played cards &ndash; 
 October 23rd Very cold again today &ndash; Cornish returned from Hospital today, he looks rather ill. His wound has not yet healed. It was a bullet. He is indeed pleased to be back again with the old boys once again &ndash; 
 5 officers left our camp for other camp on Sunday, &amp; 5 more came here today &ndash; Luckily some came from Strohen &amp; related one all the news  all of our officers are still there but they advise me not to transfer
