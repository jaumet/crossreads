<p>June 1915 &amp; July 1915<br/>29th &ndash; it from being blown away &amp; had to reinforce pegs &amp; put extra pegs in hospital tents. Dust in thick clouds made mess of everything. All slept in No. 5 Hsptl.<br/>30th &ndash; Bearers assisting in Hsptl work. Doing dressings <strike>hospi</strike> in dispensary all morning. Patients taken to seaside for wash in afternoon. Rotten headache &amp; cold. Another batch patients, chiefly <u>Medical</u> arrived about 4.30pm. Hospitals full up. Went for walk through Mudros at night. Saw them threshing wheat, by throwing up &amp; the wind blowing dust &amp; straw out of it. Grecian beauty helping. Back about 9pm. Erected Marquee for sleeping in. Patients dressings done in dispensary.<br/>July 1st &ndash; Took patients for wash on beach. Wrote Mater in afternoon. Bought box writing paper 1/3 &amp; packet rotten envelopes. Could distinctly read thro' them. Guthrie &amp; party returned through night. Had been on "Prince Edward", used as hospital boat. Had been at Cape Helles &amp; saw bombardment.<br/><strike>2nd</strike> 1st &ndash; Tom Stafford &amp; I went for swim. Saw wounded ex Anzac arriving Austn R wing had made "demonstration" feint attack. Captured Kaba-Tepe &amp; evacuated it same night. Demonstration enabled British troops, (first of Kitchener's oversea army) to land other side of Kabatepe, drawing their fire. Officially estimated 250 casualties. 5th L.H. led attack &amp; 6th 7th &amp; 3rd Bde Inf followed. 5th L.H. had 105 casualties. Most of stretcher bearers wounded.<br/>2nd &ndash; Still off colour. Xferred some of our patients to base hospital. Taken to Alexandria. Wrote Molly in afternoon &amp; got Dave's letter back (censored). Repirted it same night. Heard that Turks had again attacked Quinn's Post &amp; were repulsed losing heavily. Reported 15 Austn wounded by same.<br/>3rd &ndash; Same same. About 11am got word that we had to embark &amp; take charge of hospital ship. W.O went over &amp; when he came back said ship was very dirty &amp; no accomdtn</p>
