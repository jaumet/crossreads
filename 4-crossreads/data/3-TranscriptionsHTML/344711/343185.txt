<p>There are hundreds of vultures hanging round the barracks at meal times, and they come swooping down to take a piece of meat from one's hand, or to catch food thrown into the air.  A railway siding runs directly into the barrack-yard, &amp; there is a tennis court for the use of married soldiers and their wives and stables for the officers' horses.  The museum is only fifty yards away.</p>
<p>20th<br/>On town patrol from 6 a.m. to 9 a.m.  Had a game of billiards, and had a stroll in afternoon.  At 10 p.m. on town patrol again, and had to run in a few who were drunk or absent without leave.  At 2 in the morning we went home and Lieut. Cross engaged four carriages to take us and the drivers made a great race of it.</p>
<p>21st<br/>Town patrol 10 p.m. till 2 a.m.  Town very quiet.</p>
