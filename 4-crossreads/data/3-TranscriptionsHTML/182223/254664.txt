<p>Thursday 20&nbsp;May 1915<br />Went in Motor Car to Mahdhi &amp; Hellowan with Dr Will during morning.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />At No 1 G. H. with Jesuit Father at 6 p.m.&nbsp;<br />Otherwise about the grounds &amp; looking to business</p>
<p>Friday 21&nbsp;May 1915<br />At Matarieh Church this morning at 7 o&#39;clock. &nbsp;heard Mass. 20 French wounded &nbsp;from the Galipoli peninsula &amp; about 100 <s>Egypt</s> Cairo catholics, all men attended. About 20 received H. Communion.&nbsp;<br />The head of the Jesuit College in Cairo asked me to be present. Breakfasted with them. A priest delivered a sermon in French during the Mass. At office 9-15 am.<br />In Heliopolis from 8.30 p.m. to 12 midnight. Williams &amp; [indecipherable]</p>
<p>Saturday 22&nbsp;May 1915<br />Not out during the day<br />All going well.</p>
<p>Sunday 23&nbsp;May 1915<br />At Matarieh Church. St. Famile &ndash; at 7am. No Mass till 8 a.m. owing to special celebrations for Pentecost&nbsp;Sunday. Said my prayers look round &amp; back at 8-15 for brkfst.<br />Went to the Barrages below Cairo, between 4 &amp; 6-30 &nbsp;p.m. Captain Williams &amp; two privates went with me.</p>
