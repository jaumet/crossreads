In the Dunkirk area both French &amp; Belgian troops are working in the fields &amp; it is estimated that they will finish getting in the harvest in a fortnight. On account of the heavy drain on the purse of the French nation in keeping up the war, practically all food commodities are taxed. There is also a fixed price for everything to be sold at &amp; proclamations or "arr&ecirc;t&eacute;s" on these subjects appear from time to time, issued by General Eydoux, Military Governor of the North.
