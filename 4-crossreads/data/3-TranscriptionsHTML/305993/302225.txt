<p>Sept. 3<br/>Fine weather still prevails &amp; we expect to reach the Outer Harbor of Adelaide at 6 p.m. tonight.  We are putting up a speed of 15 knots per hour &amp; at noon 90 miles from Adelaide having passed the Neptune Island at 9 a.m., Althorpe at 11.30 a.m., our clocks having been advanced this day 45 minutes to South Australian Mean Time which makes the day 23h. 15m.  All well on board &amp; no illness of any kind of any consequence during the whole trip &amp; every one happy &amp; contented &amp; a bright day to the lot of us that have been away from Australia for nearly 2 years trooping in the War Zone.</p>
<p>At 6 p.m. arrived off Outer Harbor Adelaide &amp; had orders to remain at anchor on account of no berth available at the outer Harbor until the morning.</p>
<p>Sept. 4<br/>8 a.m.  The Quarantine doctor came aboard &amp; examined everyone on the ship &amp; Pratque granted so we proceeded alongside the wharf with the assistance of the Emus &amp; Eagles &amp; made fast at 10 a.m.  A band came down &amp; played on the wharf whilst we disembarked our troops &amp; thousands of relatives &amp; friends were down to greet the 174 soldiers &amp; their wives who left here.  At 1 p.m. we left the wharf &amp; proceeded to anchor at the Semefore roads awaiting stores to come off.  At 3.10 p.m. hove up &amp; proceeded to Melbourne.  At 6 p.m. off Cape Jervis, 7.11 p.m. off Woloughby Lt., fine weather prevailed.  Speed 15&frac12; knots.  The Tarkado troopship arrived at Adelaide this morning.  We made the trip from Fremantle 38 hours less than her.  At the Outer Harbor we found the Marathon.</p>
