<p>Capt Williams, quite a boy, joined our unit to-day. Capt East is our best man but like many more good men he has not had a fair spin.</p>
<p>Very wet to-day. The roads are terribly muddy. This brought home to us the truth of the expression "Muddy Flanders".</p>
<p>Oct 12th. Thurs</p>
<p>The Aus. F. Amb have been clearing out their patients preparatory to moving, the result is we have received an extra number of patients in to-day.</p>
<p>The N.Zders have taken over the line at this part and the relieved Aust are crowding into the village.</p>
<p>D.W. and I went down to the village photographers and had our photograph taken.</p>
<p>Went for a walk to the Grand Bois, but finding the Maison full up we did not have coffee but continued our journey and landed back at Madame Cuzet where we enjoyed a hot cup of black coffee and incidently a heated argument on conscription.</p>
<p>Oct 15th. Sun.<br/>A large number of minor cases had to be dealt with to-day. There being no Field Amb to deal with them so the F.A. are on the move.</p>
