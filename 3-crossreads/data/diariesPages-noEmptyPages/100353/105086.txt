Sheer up from the waters of the Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb rose the cliffs, the rocky barren peaks scarred torn by monsoons, beaten into all manner of shapes by the elements into sharp dizzy pinnacles, their tops swathed in the shimmering haze, into table tops, the sides of which fell away in steep precipices of long steep slopes the sides of which were covered with loose masses of talus fallen basalt.
