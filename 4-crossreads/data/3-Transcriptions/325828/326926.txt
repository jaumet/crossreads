<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My second reason is that I will get my leave to England just about twice as quickly while I am on D.H.Q <strike>that</strike> than I would if I transferred now.<br/>It is alright Rid &amp; I being so close together like this.  Last Saturday afternoon he came over to see my &amp; we were outside on the grass in the shade of some trees.  It was a fairly hot day &amp; as we sat there yarning it seemed almost impossible for a War to be raging<br/>Everything was quiet, way down in the village the church bells were ringing for the Sunday afternoon Mass.  Occasionally the silence would be broken by the roar of a howitzer battery not too far away.<br/>It is getting warmer here every day, &amp; I think we are in for a fairly hot summer<br/>I am keeping in splendid health now &amp; I do trust that this finds you all well<br/>I am enclosing a note for Ol in this letter.  Tell her I am sending her two XX this time &amp; ask her if she received my last one with the 2 kisses on.<br/>I must finish up now,</p>
