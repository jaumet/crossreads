<p>a sergeant, 2 corporals &amp; 20 men &amp; went down to Querrieu, a long walk of 5 Kilos, but it was along a beautiful road, the best I've seen in France, with avenues of tall green trees on the sides. I got a lovely Aussie mail this evening, 5 letters from Elsie, didn't have time to read them before we fell in, so when we were allowed to break off for an hour in Querrieu, I lay on the grass in the lovely grounds of a big chateau, &amp; read them. No civilians are in the town, &amp; of course the big chateau is occupied by Brigade Headquarters staff, old barns &amp; sheds are good enough for soldiers. We were lucky to get a ride back, hailed a Tommy motor lorry passing &amp; got inside. Raining again tonight, &amp; we are lucky to be out resting instead of in the slippery old muddy trenches, France stands alone for slippery soil. Saw Eldridge this afternoon, he's O.K. thank goodness; he gave me Boo's haversack with some things in it. Monday 15 July Taking part in the Battalion &amp; brigade sports is no bon, as it's rather strenuous, &amp; the chaps not taking part are having the real rest.
