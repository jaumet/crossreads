<p>Five hundred yards further we went and then a piece of shell passed through my right upper arm and glanced along a rib.  The advance continued for a further 300 yards and then whilst half of the men opened fire the remainder threw up a little cover.  They held on here all night and then had to retire with half of the original 4,000 men out of action.<br/>As my right arm was paralysed I laid down to dodge as many bullets as possible and decided to wait until dark before setting off for the rear.  I believe most soldiers feel that they will not get hit although they see their comrades falling.
