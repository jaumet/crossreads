<p>Tel-el-Kebir<br/>19th Jan 1916</p>
<p>My Dear Mother,</p>
<p>I just have a few minutes before parade to drop you a line or two. I've been getting letters &amp; parcels by the score every mail, &amp; the last parcel I got produced an excellent Barling pipe, which when filled with a delightfully sweet &amp; cool smoking mixture affords a "something or other which means excellent" smoke. I also have figs, chocolate, soups, cake, etc, etc, not to mention a very useful consolidated spirit lamp, the only thing I'm sorry about is that we were not on the Peninsula at the time of their arrival. We are having a fairly easy time of it now &amp; the battalion is almost fit to start its job again. The one great trouble is to get reequipped &amp; another small trouble is the limited leave.
