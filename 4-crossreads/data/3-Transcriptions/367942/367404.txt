<p>13th August.<br/>1130 Stopped somewhere off the N.W. end of Dutch New Guinea &amp; sent seaplane in over land to look see.<br/>1 pm. We entered a beautiful land locked harbour &amp; at 130 anchored, being absolutely hidden from seaward &amp; only a few native huts on the beach.<br/>200 "Matunga" followed us in &amp; tied up alongside. 300 Commenced taking the "Matunga" cargo &amp; coal onboard.<br/>5 p.m. "Matunga" crew came aboard "Wolf" with the exception of 2 cooks &amp; steward to look after passengers<br/>14th Aug<br/>Several canoes came off but we were all kept away from ships side &amp; not allowed to speak to natives. During the afternoon some of us, knowing that a particular sentry could understand English, were discussing the best point to land when we swam ashore.
