
No 9 & 12 Platoons were in the first line & I had charge of our extreme right, "the barrage" was marked by flares carried by men & according to how these flares were moved we had to act according when they were held high over their heads, it was meant that the barrage was going forward & when the lights were put down, the barrage had ceased to move forward & we had to halt & get down low, it was interesting to watch the different waves coming forward each maintaining their interval, & the line of flares looked very peculiar in the thick & heavy fog, the attack was very well carried out & the majority of the men & NCOs concerned seemed to under
