<p>Wednesday 24 April 1917 [1918]<br/>A long quiet day.  Sewing, reading, writing (letters to S. Peile, Frank Blundell, Eva for 14th June).<br/>Grey silk underskirt finished &amp; looks really well for me.  Calico Pt. 9 per metre for white one.  M.O. 10.30 a.m. takes swab, explains effect diph. has on eyesight &amp; orders large print (rather late).  Is kinder than usual as asks if am "worrying over anything Sister"?  I say "have given it up" but he evidently knows that this little pain in cardiac may be a worry &amp; it is.<br/>Diph. swabbings may be Positive, positive plus, Negative, Hoffmann's, latter, Miss McLean explains, is a sort of either one or the other &amp; not sure what it is.</p>
