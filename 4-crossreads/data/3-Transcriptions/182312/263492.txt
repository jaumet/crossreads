<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The country we are now in is by far the best piece</p>
<p>of Northern France we have seen.&nbsp; Lovely green fields unspoiled</p>
<p>by shell holes abound on all sides whilst a lot of the ground bears</p>
<p>evidence of having been recently cropped - for the benefit of the</p>
<p>Boche no doubt.&nbsp; We brought all our transport animals up and</p>
<p>turned them loose onto the grass - a diet which they had not</p>
<p>tasted since coming to France, or from Australia for that matter</p>
<p>as in Egypt they were always hand fed.&nbsp; Old limber horses which</p>
<p>for months had toiled about in the mud with heads down and</p>
<p>genuinely &quot;fed up&quot; expressions, got a second lease of life in a</p>
<p>day and went galloping round the paddocks, kicking their heels in</p>
<p>the air with delifht.&nbsp; Good luck to them, they have worked</p>
