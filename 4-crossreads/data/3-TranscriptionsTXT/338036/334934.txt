Monday 6th [Aug]Weather clearing today & prospects much brighter.
Tuesday 7th  Wednesday 8th  Thursday 9thWeather still against operations though improving.
Friday 10thLetters to home & M.B. & Ern.  Balloons in galore.
Saturday 11thShowery.  Fritz over last night dropping the usual quantity of bombs.  The searchlights put the wind up him a bit & he 'about turned'  Lecture by Archdeacon Ward on venereal Diseases etc.  Very forceful lecture..
Sunday 12th  Monday 13thN. Broughton one of our Reinforcements fractured his tibia by a water cart running over it.
MOUNT KEMMELAt night Fred Cooke & I went to Pierrots & had a hot bath also.
[Frederick Rupert Cooke. 11931]Tuesday 14  Wednesday 15A. MacRay (mock) broke his collarbone falling.  Our Division marching out for a rest.  All other Australian soldiers & divisions are now close to us.  Met Rupe (?) Thompson from 1st Fld Ambulance (Board man)
Thursday 16thWent up to Mount Kemmel tonight to watch the artillery & saw a beautiful sight.  Our guns were going their hardest & in spite of light haze we could [see] the bursts in enemy country.  One big gun in particular near Dickbusch
