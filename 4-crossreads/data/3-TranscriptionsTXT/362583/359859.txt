
Heliopolis CampCairo5th August 1915.
My dear Mother,
Our boat reached Suez on Sunday Augu July 25th. & we anchored there till the following morning all ignorant of our destination. On Sunday night we received orders to be ready to disembark at 10 a.m. the following morning, which order we carried out, & ten minutes after we were off the boat we were entrained and on our way to Cairo. The troops aboard moved off in three trains at intervals of two hours. The advance party including myself reached camp at Heliopolis at about 7 p.m. We were here exactly one week when we were ordered to move to Kasr-el-nil Barracks to garrison Cairo, that is A & B Coys. We were there two days, when we were ordered to return to camp. It was a great disappointment for two reasons, firstly because it has previously always been a stepping stone to the front, only a week or two sp separating the two moves, & secondly because most of us had just settled down in Heliopolis before moving.
