<p>3 June 1918<br/>News comes that result of a [indecipherable] last night was successful. We captured one tank gun, about 8 or 10 machine guns, 4 trench mortars and 250 prisoners. Our casualties where slight; perhaps 3 officers and fifty men. We caught the enemy changing over. Our artillery barrage was very good<br/>The artillery work generally was splendid causing much loss to the enemy in their forward and back areas.</p>
<p>Prisoners taken speak confidently of victory for German army.</p>
<p>This is the proper spirit for soldiers to [indecipherable] with, but they will be sadly disappointed. It is we who are going to win.</p>
<p>Today Fritz plane came behind screen of a cloud and shot from edge of it at one of our observation balloons bringing it down in flames.</p>
<p>The evening of that day when he brought down three, he got another one</p>
<p>Great events are in progress in the south office enemy threatens Paris, but they are held by our combined troops. The French will establish a wall of defence [defense] all will go well we shall win.</p>
