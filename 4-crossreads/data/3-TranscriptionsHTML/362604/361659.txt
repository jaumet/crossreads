<p>&amp; rhubarb in galore.  We had 10 different varieties of jam opened, eggs countless, &amp; in fact everything lovely.  I averaged 5 solid meals a day, and in these hard times too.  I went to Ramsay &amp; to Jurby head with Marie on the cycles.  Uncle &amp; Auntie drove &amp; Marie &amp; I rode over to tea to a neighbouring farm.  We had a nice time &amp; the weather was simply glorious.  I was never in a more bracing atmosphere than the mild, sweet air of Jurby &amp; Sulby.</p>
<p>Now, I am on my return.  Douglas &amp; the Island are a blur &amp; that's all.  On the London North Western I struck an Irish Cockney on the Boat &amp; we had dinner at Liverpool before we took the 3.15 p.m. train to London via Warrington &amp; Crewe.
