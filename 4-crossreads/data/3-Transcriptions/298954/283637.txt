<p>1918<br/>The Older Inhabitants here breed rabbits &amp; grow vegetables and are therefore fairly well off.</p>
<p>There is a good library, a tennis-court, (another being prepared now), a cricket pitch, swimming will commence soon, &amp; a bundle of well-worn skis in the hall look promising for winter sport.</p>
<p>Anderson (C.E.) showed me around today.   He arrived in the "Yarrowdale" &amp; has been in 18 camps in nearly as many months.   He met Godfrey Ludlow (the Sydney violinist) in Ruhleben.</p>
<p>Wed 17th Apl.<br/>Got my first real, "close-up", view of European forest scenery this morning.</p>
<p>At 9.30 we left the lager and set off in straggling files at a quick pace;  a sentry with side arms following.    We skirted the Roblinsee, thence swinging off into a pine wood, coming out into the beech forests near Augusta&ndash;blick &ndash; from where one looks down over the beeches to the lake far below.</p>
