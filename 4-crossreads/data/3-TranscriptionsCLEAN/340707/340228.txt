 Passing onwards & upwards to Escalier Daru rests the Victory of Samothrace without head or arm but yet of arresting workmanship - clothed in fine linen & not in heavy wrappings - Her marvellous body was not designed for daily tasks but was of the gods - a beauty that was in itself an ideal. Her movements though vigorous were always harmoniously balanced 
 This the best somewhat dimmed the undoubted beauty of latter] works but one in special took my fancy [Deane by Houdin] a light & almost living figure in bronze another was the Young Neapolitan [Teisher] by Rude a child with laughing face playing with a tortoise. 
 All the picture were not yet in their place Titian & the [indecipherable] school are missing But Van Eyck, Rembrandt & who can write of the play of light & shade that the master has shown in the two philosophers. Alone in the centre of a room stands Mona Lisa with her mystic smile of ever bewildering fascination.
