<p>8<br/>see the sloped lines of many rifles, he head of a house, the outline of a rider, and the edge of various military forms familiar to us.<br/>By dim reflection from the silvery sky we can, at times, see the round crown of a still helmet.</p>
<p>Where a spark flys from an iron shod hoof, we can see the legs of a horse, dangling harness, and the spokes of a wheel.</p>
<p>It is enough; - we learn that a battalion is marching in, under cover of darkness.</p>
<p>This stealing in at night is necessary to avoid a possibility of the enemy discovering our movements.</p>
<p>The men are not allowed to strike matched, and they dont.</p>
<p>Soon there is a low uttered command for the moving shadows to halt.</p>
<p>The front becomes stationery. The command passes back through the now wrinkling shadows.</p>
