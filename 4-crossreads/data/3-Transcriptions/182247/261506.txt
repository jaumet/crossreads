<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">and enjoying the comforts of a real home life once again.&nbsp; Found Mrs Linley very sick with &#39;flu.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Marh 14</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Rose 7.30.&nbsp; Breakfast 9.&nbsp; Left for London at 10.&nbsp; Took tube to Baker Street station.&nbsp; Got Motor Bus to Marble Arch and Hyde Park and walked through city via Oxford Street, Holborn, Cheapside to St Paul&#39;s.&nbsp; Went through the cathedral - a magnificent place.&nbsp; Saw over the famous Guildhall.&nbsp; Went through the meat markets at Smithfield via the Fleet Street Viaduct.&nbsp; Saw over Old Bow Church, St Bartholomew&#39;s Church and Hospital.&nbsp; Had luncheon at Lyons. Saw Bank of England, Mansion House, Stock exchange, Trafalgar Square, National Gallery, Museum.&nbsp; Spent afternoon at a matinee at the Coliseum.&nbsp; Met Mr Linley afterwards and called to see Mrs Forsyth.&nbsp; Arrived back at home after having spent a very full and most enjoyable day.&nbsp; Spent evening at pianola and turned in at 11.45.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">March 15</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Rose 8.&nbsp; Breakfast 9.&nbsp; Left home 10.&nbsp; Caught bus from Crown at Cricklewood for Victoria Station.&nbsp; Got off at Victoria and Grosvenor Hotel and met Mr Brooke there at 11.&nbsp; Went to Austtralian Headquarters at Horseferry Road and met Davidson, who got my permit to visit Dovercourt.&nbsp; Went round Lambeth Bridge and saw Houses of Parliament.&nbsp; Saw over Westminster Abbey.&nbsp; A most enjoyable and memorable visit with much of interest to the student of history and tradition.&nbsp; Saw Cromwell&#39;s Statue near Houses of Parliament.&nbsp; Got bus along the route of the War Office, Downing St., and all the hub of the universe.&nbsp; Lunched.&nbsp; Spent afternoon at theatre again, seeing Oscar Asche in &quot;Chu Chin Chow&quot;, with Mr Brooke.&nbsp; He paid.&nbsp; A great show.&nbsp; Had coffee after and took bus to Marble Arch.&nbsp; Took bus from there home.&nbsp; Had dinner and spent evening at home pretty much the same as I did last night.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">March 16</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Rose 9.&nbsp; Had breakfast and left for town at 10 from the Crown per bus with Mr Linley.&nbsp; Went all round the Thames Embankment and on to London Bridge to see the view of the wharves and docks.&nbsp; Lunched.&nbsp; Left Mr. Linley and spent the next hour or two wandering among the mysterious and fascinating Tower of London.&nbsp; Met Mr Brooke at Grosvenor at 3, as arranged.&nbsp; Introduced him to Mr Linley.&nbsp; We then set out through Hyde Park.&nbsp; Saw Buckingham Palace, Queen Victoria Monument.&nbsp; St. James Palace, Bond Street.&nbsp; Had afternoon tea.&nbsp; Called again on Mrs Forsyth to say good bye, and made homeward arriving there 6.45.&nbsp; Mrs Linley not too good.&nbsp; Spent evening at home.&nbsp; Got word that Jim Johnson had left for the front.</td>
</tr>

<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">March 17</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Rose 8.&nbsp; Went for a walk with Mrs Linley.&nbsp; Saw Lord Aberdeen&#39;s Dollis Hill Home, where the great and eminent Gladstone used to spend so much of his time in tree felling.&nbsp; Caught tram to Hendon with Mr. Linley to see the Aero works.&nbsp; Returned home for dinner.</td>
