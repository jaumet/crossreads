<p>- at any rate to our sand glazed eyes &ndash; consisting of a long valley about 5 miles long by 2-3 wide all under fairly intense cultivation with prickly pear boundaries marking off the fields &amp; very heavily wooded with orchard trees.  The town or village of Khan Yunis is the usual rabbit warren of mud houses &ndash; through which we passed.<br/>It has in the centre an old mosque or fort whose architecture appears to me &ndash; not that I know much &ndash; more gothic than the usual style of Arabic buildings, it is built of stone &amp; has a fine central arch which is certainly gothic in style.  Possibly the Crusaders had a hand in it as they were in these parts, &amp; buildings last a long time in this climate.<br/>One of our planes has just gone over &ndash; it is full moon tonight.  I am writing by its light.  More anon.</p>
