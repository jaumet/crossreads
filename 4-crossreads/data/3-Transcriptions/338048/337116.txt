<p>5.50 pm train back. We changed at Andover Junc. and got to Amesbury about 8-15 p.m. We had a good wait at Andover J. We looked through the chapel at Amesbury over 1000 years old was shown over by a lady who is continually writing to soldiers showing them over the church &amp; other busying about. Had biscuits &amp; some soft drinks &amp; got home tired &amp; happy.</p>
<p>Well naturally I consider London marvellous. The police are wonderful Big fine stamps of men with a wonderful knowledge. You ask them how to get to a certain place and they just say catch a No. 49 bus and change at Waterloo for a No 11 and all this sort of thing. They know it well &amp; they do not require to be told a dozen times what you want before they tell you like some daft people here. Patience! well they have their full share. They are continuously being seiged by soldiers all day &amp; yet you <u>never</u> get anything but a kindly &amp; courteous reply to the most outrageous query which you like to make.
