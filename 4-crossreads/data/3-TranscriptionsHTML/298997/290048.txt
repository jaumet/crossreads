The reports take all sorts of shapes; but such a lot of men seem delighted at anticipating the worst and crediting victories to the enemy.  We had more gas last night, having to wear masks.  Fritz seems to be giving the place a drenching.  I went to see the battalion, which is in Ridge Wood, but could not discover the C.O.</p>
<p>March 26<br/>It is pretty certain now, that the Germans are making a big headway in the south, <strike>&amp; have</strike> and have overrun a lot of country in  a day or so that it cost us many painful months &amp; thousands of lives to gain; besides which they have captured a mass of material.  We have received orders to get all the angle irons &amp; bared wire screws we can from the old barb- wire entanglements hereabouts, which in itself is significant of a need that looks desperate &ndash; especially as the articles are wanted in a hurry.It is nasty work, as the wire has practically rusted onto the irons.</p>
