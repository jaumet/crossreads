<p>Tuesday Nov. 20. 1917.  1205</p>
<p>A nice morning but rather fresh.<br/>We had a good breakfast this morning for a wonder. There is a very heavy bombardment this afternoon which has increased to great violence to night in the direction of Passchendaele.</p>
<p>I received another letter from my wife to night dated Aug 21. No 84.  [Shorthand transcribed as follows]  I wrote to my wife tonight letter 101, 6 pages.  [End of shorthand]<br/>Wednesday Nov. 21 1917. 1206</p>
<p>There is a change in the weather to day. There was some rain early this morning, &amp; occasional misty showers during the day.</p>
<p>I finished making a lot of finger rings for J. Mooney of the Q.M. store &amp; L/C Lawless.</p>
<p>Sergeant Partridge came back from leave in Scotland &amp; England to day.</p>
<p>I had another letter from T. Douglas today dated Sept 4.</p>
<p>Thursday Nov. 22. 1917.  1207.</p>
<p>Fine but cloudy.<br/>There was another big bombardment last night &amp; this morning.</p>
<p>L/C. J. Lawless was awarded the military Medal by Lieut Boyland to day for good work done in some of the recent stunts with the Pack mules.</p>
<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows]<br/>I heard today that Colonel Woods [indecipherable] of our Battalion is to take charge of the 38 Battalion, who went away last February sick.</p>
<p>I received another letter from my wife today dated August 26.  [End of shorthand]</p>
