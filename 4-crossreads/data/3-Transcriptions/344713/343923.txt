<p>Tues. Nov. 14th: -<br/>Marched along the muddiest roads imaginable through Montauban Carnoy &amp; Fricourt &amp;  occupied new Nissen huts at a camp near Sausage Gully.</p>
<p>Wed. Nov. 15th: -<br/>Marched through Mealte over Vivier Mill bridge; through Dernancourt to Buire.<br/>Have billeted in barns &ndash; Bitterly Cold.</p>
<p>Thurs. Nov. 16th: -<br/>Marched through Ribemont to Heilly for our wash at Divisional baths this afternoon &amp; it was badly wanted.<br/>No bath or Change for over a month.<br/>Aust. Mail dated Sept. 24th.</p>
<p>Fri. Nov. 17th: -<br/>Rode by motor buses via Amiens to Flesselles &amp; billeted.</p>
<p>Sat. Nov. 18th: -<br/>Snowed a bit last night &amp; all the district is mantled.<br/>Winter in alright &amp; bitterly Cold.</p>
<p>Sat. Nov. 25th 1916: -<br/>My first "Blighty" furlough has arrived &amp;  I am off for a rest to London for the first time.<br/>Left Flesselles by motor wagon at 4.30 A.M. &amp; went to Canaples Railway Station; entrained for Havre.</p>
