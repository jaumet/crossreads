<p>&amp; greenish fumes high in the air, while pieces of shrapnel &amp; shell casing drone overhead like so many bees.  Towards night we were shelled at intervals by the Turkish Guns, Beachy Bill &amp; Lonesome Liz, sending showers of shrapnel over our lines, Hill 971 also added her complement &amp; the miniture bombardment ceased.</p>
<p>On Saturday 6th November we saw an enemy air plane for the first time.  Ever since our landing our own planes have continually flown above the enemies lines, &amp; although shelled at intervals by the Anti-aircraft guns, still seem to do their required work.  But now we had a Taube above us, &amp; after dropping a few bombs over our lines, he soon disappeared once being fired at in the direction of Maidos</p>
<p>On Sunday 7th the enemy landed six shells on our position &amp; although one lobbed right in a dugout &amp; stripped a rifle clean of its wood &amp; scattered dust &amp; clods throughout</p>
