<p>I walked home at a terrific pace set by my temporary man a Tommy who struck out for dinner but generally in the wrong direction &amp; had to be yarded up occasionally.  His delight at seeing our tents &amp; lights was amusing.  He thought I had fluked it.  'A demain".</p>
<p>* (part or rather a small party did actually get out and went west like their forerunners, the rest were kept in our trenches by command.)</p>
<p>17th, 6.45 evening.</p>
<p>We returned from our reviewing expedition by nightfall and a good square table &amp; seats put up in our absence helped the comfort of warm grub.  I was conducted by the "Mission" from the a point on the beach where part of the Australians landed right up to "The Neck" which is the ground whereon the terrific sacrifice abovementioned took place.
