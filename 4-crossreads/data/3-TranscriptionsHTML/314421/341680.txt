<p>I have had two birthdays in the army, one in England &amp; the other in France at Blequin. I have spent one Christmas in France at Armentiers,  and there is every prospect of spending another Christmas in the Army, but I am doubtful if it will be in France, for I believe the war will be over before then, &amp; if not we will in all probability be removed from France to do Garrison duty somewhere else.</p>
<p>Cold and frosty last night but a very bright and beautiful morning. In fact we are getting remarkable nice weather during the past few weeks. This is greatly in our favour; for it enables us to bring up our artillery after &amp; during every advance and is also of great benefit to the infantry and airmen, in fact all branches of the service.</p>
<p>We were to move out at 9. a.m. this morning, but it has been cancelled, &amp; we don't know when we are going.</p>
<p>Last night &amp; this morning there was a terrific bombardment of the enemies position. Early in the night several enemy aeroplanes flew over the camp &amp; dropped bombs some distance away.</p>
