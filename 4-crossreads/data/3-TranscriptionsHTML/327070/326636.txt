<p>In the afternoon the time was spent in fitting &amp; rearranging the men's equipment &amp; in shaking down into our quarters. A number of things are very different to what we had been led to expect: for instance at Liverpool we were told that here only a private's jacket would be worn, whereas at Aerodrome Camp at least it is almost frowned upon.  Officers are expected to wear a distinctive uniform to the privates, &amp; while there may be something in it in a smart place like Egypt I certainly do not intend to wear anything but a private's uniform into action.</p>
<p>In the evening after mess the adjutant gave a lecture on some points in the battalion routine, &amp; after it I was glad to turn in early.</p>
<p>Tuesday 15 February 1916</p>
<p>Slept better than the previous night, altho' the bed seemed much harder than the shipboard bunk. My orderly had managed to annex from somewhere a native bed on which my bunk was made up: these are made of what look like split midribs of palm leaves, the longitudinal ones being bored &amp; having uprights inserted in them every 3 inches or so, making merely a skeleton box</p>
