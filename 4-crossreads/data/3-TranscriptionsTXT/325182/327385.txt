 8th Our guns began a concentration shoot early last night and continued practically all night.  The enemy retaliated strongly and caused 6 casualties in our Battalion. 
 2 men were killed at 'C' Coy. Hqrs. and Lt. Evans was wounded in the foot.  Another man of 'C' Coy. received a shell wound in the foot.  All the above casualties were caused by one shell.  Lt. Green of B Coy. was badly wounded in the head and died at 7 A.M. this morning.  He was buried  the  in the village of Aubigny.  Lt. Green was an officer in every sense of the word and his death is regretted by all ranks.
