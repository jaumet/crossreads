<p>In a second we capsized our carriage, groceries &amp; all, and waited till our opponent had passed;  after that reinstated ourselves and sailed home triumphant.</p>
<p>For tea, Alan &amp; I treated ourselves to sausages (tinned) and cocoa (own brew).   Later on we boiled a petrol tin of water on our stove &amp; had a fair-dinkum wash; - quite a novelty now-a-days.</p>
<p>A quiet smoke in bed ended the day, and our <strike>conscien</strike> consciousnesses floated away amid the languid wreaths of smoke &amp; left us, as helpless as the sons of Clovis, in the arms of the Infinite.</p>
<p>July 7.   We loaded timber for sleepers, angle-iron &amp; wire to-day.   We also visited and inspected a 'Tank' which had been put out of action &amp; a Fritz 6" howitzer that had suffered the same fate.</p>
