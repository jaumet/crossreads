<p>with a lighthouse on it; it appeared to be about 200ft high, rocky &amp; fearfully desolate looking land. The eastern end, lighted up clearly by the rising sun was very interesting, a heavy landslip having taken place &amp; left a fresh face of the cliffs in which highly contoured sedimentaries showed minor folds &amp; faults when examined thro' Father's glasses. Near the lighthouse was a dwelling house, but there was no sign of life, the whole place looking fearfully dreary.</p>
<p>Parades held as usual, but during the later part of the morning &amp; during the afternoon parade I was up on the boat-deck with the other officers attending a gymkana or sports meeting of 1st saloon &amp; A.I.F. officers. These included a march, blindfolded &amp; guided by a lady holding the reins, thro' a mazework of empty bottles, pillow-fight sitting astride a spar, a drawing &amp; whistling race in which the men had to run to their partners &amp; respectively draw a picture of an animal &amp; whistle a tune, the ladies writing down what they recognised &amp; returning it by their men to the winning post: an animal medley, hanging monkey, placing eye in a pig while blind-folded &amp; similar ship-board sports filled up the programme, from which, solely as an onlooker &amp; non-partaker in any, I got a lot of amusement.</p>
