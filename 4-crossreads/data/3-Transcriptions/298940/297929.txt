<p>done just sufficient work, for it to be barely noticeable, commenced a cross fire of tins projected by the end of shovels after the manner of golfing. Having discovered a rather into method of attack I got Walter to help the other chap on the opposite side of the heap, &amp; set out to beat them. By a set plan of campaign I easily evaded their fire, outflanked them in such a way as to bring Walter between myself &amp; his helper &ndash; so that his friend could not fire without putting Walter in danger -- &amp; then it came on to rain.</p>
