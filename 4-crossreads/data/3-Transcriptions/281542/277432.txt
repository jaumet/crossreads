<p>Tuesday 7th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Classed "L.O.C." Light Duty.</p>
<p>Posted Mab letter that mail had been send to 2nd Aust. Div. &amp; that I was making inquiries regarding it.</p>
<p>Wednesday 8th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Inquired at Intermediate Base re cable &amp; could get no definite advice as to its despatch.</p>
<p>Thursday 9th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Hot &amp; dusty.  Usual routine day.  Cpl of prison guard.  Two men in handcuffs.</p>
<p>Friday 10th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Paid &pound;5.  Sent setters to Sgt. Wakelin, Base P.O. Alexandria &amp; 2nd Aust Div Hqrs  re mail.  Told at I.B. that cable was sent on 29th Feby.</p>
<p>Saturday 11th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Kursaal at night.</p>
<p>Sunday 12th Mch. 1916</p>
<p>Attended Church Parade 1st Aus. Division.  In town with Cpl Stevenson.</p>
<p>Monday 13th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Finish money.  In town again.</p>
<p>Tuesday 14th Mch. 1916</p>
<p>Called at YMCA &amp; received following cable from Mabel, "Boy everybody well".  <strike>How relieved I am &amp; how glad. What a load</strike></p>
<p>Wednesday 15th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Feeling very seedy.  Pains in my right groin &amp; little strength in legs.  General lassitude.</p>
<p>Thursday 16th Mch 16</p>
<p>A little improved. Tucker bad. Fat bacon for breakfast, bread &amp;cheese &amp; jam for dinner, fatty  &amp; watery stew for tea. This is the daily menu, My stomach recoils against it.</p>
<p>Attended Countess de Lavison's Concert at YMCA hut.  Very enjoyable evening.</p>
