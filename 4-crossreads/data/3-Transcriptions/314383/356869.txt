<p>to see a mother, almost too weak to stand, clinging to a dead child or <strike>dead</strike> children wailing over the bodies of their dead parents.  Many were so weak that a push would fell them to the ground from which they could not rise without assistance.  The Bazaar turns &amp; twists along narrow streets the whole of which is covered over but here &amp; there holes are pierced in the roof to admit light.</p>
<p>The market square was full of women trying to sell their scanty household effects.  There seems to be a fair quantity of stuff in these bazaars, yet at least one fourth of the population is dying of starvation.</p>
<p>May 11th<br/>Rested all day but this evening Scott Olsen &amp; I went to an Armenian Cafe &amp; got a fine bottle of wine.</p>
