<p>Friday 30 March 1917.</p>
<p>Route march into Seyr &amp; out agn during morning.</p>
<p>A.S.C. fatigue in afternoon till 5.45. Late tea. Burnished spurs with marline spike. Visited L. J Laversha [Leslie Joyce-Leversha] in ward at Clg Hosp.&nbsp;[Clearing Hospital?]&nbsp;</p>
<p>Made bunk &amp; then over to the lines.</p>
<p>Latest furphy. &quot;Not sailing for 3 weeks.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">[Private Leslie Joyce-Leversha, No 16934, 24, a chemist&#39;s assistant from Melbourne, Victoria, embarked from Melbourne on 10 May 1917 on HMAT A42 Boorara with the Camel Field Ambulance. He was later transferred to the 14th Australian General Hospital, and returned to Australia in 1919.]</span></p>
