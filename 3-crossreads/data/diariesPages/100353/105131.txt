Farming fruit growing appeared to be the sole pursuits of the rural inhabitants, the old cow, so inevitable in Australian scenery, disappearing practically altogether. Cherry orchards appeared a mass of white blooms while peach trees were also showing in blossom. At 9 a.m. on Tuesday morning we ran into Lyons where we stopped for about an hour breakfasted. Hot water was supplied by the French Red Cross, so that we were able to make tea bovril. Between Lyons the Loire beautiful stretches of dark green spruce forests alternated with the farmlands.
