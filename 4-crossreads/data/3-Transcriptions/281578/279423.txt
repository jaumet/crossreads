<p>must be jolly cold these nights with the wind whistling up their bare knees. We went into a post-card shop, &amp; had great fun trying to speak to the pretty, smart, well-dressed French girls. There is a great charm about their manner, there&#39;s no doubt, quite different from our English folk. Windows were smashed in many parts of the town in air raids, I suppose. Many places bore &quot;cafe&quot; as a sign, &amp; contained soldiers drinking beer mostly. We looked for a restaurant, but it had just gone 8 o&#39;clock &amp; everything closes at 8, the women simply made grimaces &amp; said &quot;huit heures, impossible.&quot; We looked in a pub door as a last resort, but we were greeted with a shake of the head, the woman said &quot;mange&quot; &ndash; I knew that meant &quot;eat&quot;, &amp; said &quot;Oui&quot; &amp; she pointed to a door lower down, we went in, &amp; luckily struck an excellent meal with &quot;vin blanc&quot;, very nice to drink. The bread was a treat, nice white bread we never saw in England, &amp; no meat cards necessary. An English</p>
