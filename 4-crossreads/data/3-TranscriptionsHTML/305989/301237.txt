<p>Monday 1 January 1917<br/>Cruel day.  Taubes over early &amp; dust storm the rest of the day, wounded "2 bints".</p>
<p>Tuesday 2 January 1917<br/>Wretched day sand &amp; rained all day one of the most cruel nights I ever remember our "possy" was near the sea, was wet through by 8 o'clock.</p>
<p>Wednesday 3 January 1917<br/>Cold all day:  got good mail.  Got cable from home, price 30/-.  Mother Lucie (3), Theo, Lorna, Rex, Elsie.</p>
<p>Thursday 4 January 1917<br/>Cold &amp; windy:  also several heavy showers.</p>
<p>Friday 5 January 1917<br/>Fairly clear.  Section down for swim clear night up till 9.30.  Greased saddles made dugout.</p>
<p>Saturday 6 January 1917<br/>Got up early &amp; went to "Kilo 139" for reinforcements.  Taube flew over but did not drop any bombs.</p>
