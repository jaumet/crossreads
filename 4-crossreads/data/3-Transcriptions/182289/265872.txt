<p>about D.23.c.40.40.  Our guns completely relieved by 11th Company by midnight 22/23rd.  Five guns of 7th M.G. Co. relieved by 3 a.m.  Relief reported, complete by 3.30 a.m.<br/>Three guns not yet placed held in reserve in J.5.a. &amp; c.</p>
<p>22.4.18<br/>The following targets were engaged at irregular intervals throughout the night &amp; morning, 1500 rounds fired,<br/>(1)  Hollow in K.7.c.  <br/>(2)  Trenches K.7.c.10.75.  <br/>(3)  Sunken Road K.1.b. &amp; d.  <br/>(4)  Streets, Ville-Sur-Ancre E.25.d.30.45.  <br/>(5)  Road &map; Trench E.26.c.00.25.  <br/>(6)  Trenches K.2.c.1.7.  <br/>(7)  Cemetery, Ville-Sur-Ancre K.1.a.85.75.<br/>Enemy artillery quiet except for short burst of fire at about 4.30 a.m.<br/>Enemy Machine Gun fire, Normal.<br/>Enemy aircraft patrolled his own lines but owing to the large concentration of our machines did not attempt to cross.</p>
<p>23.4.18<br/>O.C. visited new Sector north of the Railway with a view to improving or altering existing positions and finding places for three guns now held in reserve.<br/>The following targets were engaged at irregular intervals throughout night &amp; morning, 1500 rds. expended.</p>
