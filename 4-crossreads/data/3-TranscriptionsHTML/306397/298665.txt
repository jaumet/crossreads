We got nothing to eat.<br/>In the afternoon they wanted to give us rice and curry, but we could simply not touch it for it was not a European but a Kanaka food. By 10 pm we had nothing to eat yet. In the afternoon I got a cup of tea and a slice of bread of Mayer who had got the gaol keepers wife to buy it for him. I then also had the opportunity to read a swiss paper which an English officer had brought us from Penang. My eyes were filled with tears when I read it.
