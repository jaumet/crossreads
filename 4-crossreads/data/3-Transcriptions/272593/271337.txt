<p>No 9 &amp; 12 Platoons were in the first line &amp; I had charge of our extreme right, "the barrage" was marked by flares carried by men &amp; according to how these flares were moved we had to act <strike>according</strike> when they were held high over their heads, it was meant that the barrage was going forward &amp; when the lights were put down, the barrage had ceased to move forward &amp; we had to halt &amp; get down low, it was interesting to watch the different waves coming forward each maintaining their interval, &amp; the line of flares looked very peculiar in the thick &amp; heavy fog, the attack was very well carried out &amp; the majority of the men &amp; NCOs concerned seemed to under</p>
