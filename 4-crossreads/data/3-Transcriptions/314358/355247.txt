<p>3<br/>It becomes a necessity that the troubles which are not our own should be forgotten.</p>
<p>And they are.</p>
<p>And so soon to, wise nature makes it so, otherwise the whole world would be a place of misery now.</p>
<p>So easily does the youthful soldier throw aside his cares and become the merriest of men in a place of peace; that you would think war had no terrors worth a moments consideration.</p>
<p>Also, the people living in the peace regions of a country at war in a like manner soon weary of the worries which they find are in other minds, and assume the even terror of their ways.</p>
<p>All these considerations are largely concerned with the superficial aspect; for that is the necessary assumption of brave hearts.</p>
<p>Look a little deeper' find out by sympathy; retain in a loyal memory</p>
