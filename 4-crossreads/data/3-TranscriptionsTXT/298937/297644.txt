 Frenchmen on wharves &amp; boats. There was a general interchange of pleasantness, the Australians trying their Arabic on the French, &amp; the latter essaying into such English as they knew.  Berthed &amp; disembarked into a yard at the back of the wharf. There we formed up &amp; marched out through the dock gates.  From there to our camp, a distance of about 2 miles we were cheered by men women &amp; children all along.
