<p>[Thursday 3 August 1916]</p>
<p>re written &ndash; whole page crossed out<br/>The MONITOR at Sea is a great help [indecipherable] send a 2000 lbs shell is lovely to listen to sailing thro' the air &amp; landing with a Crunch.  Clouds of dust etc.<br/>We have been watching the Turks now both night &amp; day for over 2 months &amp; no prospect of being relieved for a bit- they are getting closer.<br/>I Received Australasian &amp; D'quin paper note old JEH &amp; Albert [indecipherable] both gone.<br/>I am saving a days Field Messages, which will show you our work, but I dare not post it.  We average about 3 casualties a day per Regiment &amp; several Horses  lights blown out- it is one of my first jobs on returning to bivouac is to notify Brigade Head Quarters of our Losses</p>
