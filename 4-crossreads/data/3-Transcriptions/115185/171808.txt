<p>21 &ndash; how much he saw with it. At Manchester they said he'd be fit for home service but <u>not</u> foreign. I feel so sad for Father but I suppose we are lucky to have Gibson with his legs and one eye still.<br/>Cousin Harry came to see me. But I haven't seen Mrs Myers or Cousin Gordon or Bunt or Aunt E. &amp; Sadie &ndash; all people I want to see but its almost hopeless. This afternoon waiting for news of Gibson has been a great opportunity to write to you I have sent you "Times" with account of Lord Kitcheners speech and Sir I. Hamiltons</p>
