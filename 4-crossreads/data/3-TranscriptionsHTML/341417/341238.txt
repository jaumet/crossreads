<p>Now the Turks could see men going off but of course it has been a usual thing on both sides to send men away for a rest. They probably thought that others were coming to replace them.<br/>Again, we may have only been decoying them to come out &amp; attack us while seeming to have a reduced force.  Anyhow it is a puzzle to me.</p>
<p>So as to make any cessation of fire seem commonplace, we had been ordered about a month previously to abstain from firing for 24 hours &amp; try &amp; give the enemy the idea then that we were evacuating. Of course not knowing the sequel of the scheme which was our real evacuation, we all thought that it was a very weak attempt to draw the enemy from his trenches.</p>
<p>In some quarters the impression was that the enemy were only holding their line with a small force.<br/>Now the success of our move would be increased if in addition to getting off men guns &amp; munitions we could destroy what was inadvisable to move.</p>
