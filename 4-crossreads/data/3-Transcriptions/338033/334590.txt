<p>We were ordered to take them off.<br/>Within five minutes a deluge of rain came down, drenching us as we stood there bravely, like good soldiers, going dead crook the whole time. When the rain ceased we were told to put on our wet coats, which action caused many sarcastic remarks and jokes to fill the air. We arrived back to camp again, a very tired and washed out looking crowd indeed.<br/>On 26th I was granted four days leave to London, and with a number of others we caught a train at Ludgershall.
