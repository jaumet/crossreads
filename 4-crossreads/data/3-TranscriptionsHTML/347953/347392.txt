<p>Under Fire &amp;</p>
<p>In preparing for an attack in present day trench warfare in which you are a participant an immediate  sentiment of nervousness, anxiety,  &amp; excitement overrules one's temperament together with an almost certain feeling of death.  This is only natural considering the deadly &amp; murderous weapons utilized now days.  The thought of death instinctively elevates ones thoughts nearer than ever before to God &amp; to those who are dear to him.  But thought changes with alacrity &amp; the looking optimistically to the future, says it will be the other fellow who will go with me.</p>
