<p>Sunday 17 November 1918<br/>Left the River early this morn.  Lovely weather &amp; sea smooth.  Topping trip up the Irish Channel.  Settling down to the boat.  Ours is first boat out of Liverpool since Armistice signed.</p>
<p>Monday 18 November 1918<br/>Food continues good.  Best I've had for four years.  Getting up rough in eveng.  No submarines yet!!</p>
<p>Tuesday 19 November 1918<br/>Pretty rough.  Not feelg. too good but cleared my head later &amp; fed well.  Rup stickg. it well &amp; very nippy.  Bridge in eveng.  382 miles.</p>
<p>Wednesday 20 November 1918<br/>Still pretty rough.  Pretty bon now inside.  Sweepstake started on the day's run.  I had a slight argument with the promoter!!  354 miles.</p>
<p>Thursday 21 November 1918<br/>Still rolling a good deal.  Everyone seems to have found their sea legs by now.  Winning quite a lot at 2d. Bridge.</p>
<p>Friday 22 November 1918<br/>Big Head sea on &amp; we rolled all over the show.  Won anor. bob at Bridge.  Impromptu dance on in evening.  Rup enjoyed it.</p>
<p>Saturday 23 November 1918<br/>The wind gets behind us &amp; we get along at a good bat.  Wrote lres. in morning.  Struck heavy squall in afternoon wch. lasted over hour.<br/>Crossing the North Atlantic for the first time.</p>
