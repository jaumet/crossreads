<p>After the first couple of days they don't worry about it &amp; there is no public house to wander to the same as they have outside the military camps on land. In the crowded accommodation of a troop ship a few "elevated" men could destroy the comfort of every deck &amp; fights would be innumerable. On land &ndash; in Australia &ndash; the authorities make the mistake of insisting in "dry" camps where canteens could be run under proper supervision, which would save a lot of men from yearning to get out of camp to run into the greater evils of the city.</p>
