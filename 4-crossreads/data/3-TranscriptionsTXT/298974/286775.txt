 Royal Hotel. Durban. 
 16th Sep. 1916. 
 Dear Mother 
 We arrived in Durban yesterday &amp; got off Friday afternoon &amp; evening &amp; all day today, and I fancy we will on Sunday too. One boatload of Australians behaved dreadfully here &amp; men lying all over the streets drunk and broke up a shop, so the mayor closed all hotel to Soldiers. This is a great idea &amp; so our chaps are behaving very well indeed. The Mayor gave us the freedom of the City and a civic welcome &amp; we got nothing but kindness on all sides, especially from the ladies (matron!) They gave us books &amp; cigarettes, and lots of the chaps have been asked out to dine. I have an invitation from a dear old scotch lady for Sunday &amp; I intend to go if I possibly can.
