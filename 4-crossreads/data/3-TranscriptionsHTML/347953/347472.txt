<p>We were taking our course round the cape, and on the 26th February we arrived at Sierra Leone which is an important gold mining  &amp;  palm oil centre.  The weather there was dreadfully hot.  The place has always been known to be the white man's grave.  Six months active service there is counted as a year's service.  Sierra Leone is greatly being used now as a meeting place for transports and warships.  The inhabitants are very black, well built, and have very flat noses.
