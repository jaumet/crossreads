<p>Once more we were in the same old cattle trucks, bumping, squeaking &amp; scraping, until Somme or no Somme we wished the journey over. All the way down we passed through stations &amp; past old fashioned farm houses where the old French folk waved a greeting &amp; made grim signs which conveyed to us to kill every german we met.</p>
<p>It had been about 10 am when we entrained at Bailleul and about 7 pm we pulled into Longpre &amp; were told this was the end of our train journey. Bitterly cold, a semi Gale blowing, &amp; raining a treat, we were told to fall in as we had to march a matter of 5 kilometres to a place called Bussy, and after a lot of running round &amp; unnecessary talking on part of the officers, we got a move on.</p>
