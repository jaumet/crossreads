<p>April 3rd<br/>Received orders today to shift alongside to take on Troops, horses etc.</p>
<p>April 4th<br/>Have loaded 575 Indians (Sihks) &amp; 300 horses &amp; mules, as well as ammunition, guns etc.</p>
<p>April 5th<br/>Received "sealed orders" &amp; orders to go to sea.  On opening the sealed orders at sea we find we are bound for an Island (Lemnos) 45 miles from the entrance to the Dardanelles.</p>
<p>April 6th<br/>We have six British Officers on board &amp; 2 British Petty Officers.  The Sihks are fine soldiers, being big strapping fellows &amp; all well trained.  They are very religious too &amp; you see them put a blanket on the deck &amp; get down &amp; pray several times a day, now and again bending their foreheads to the deck &amp; then standing right up &amp; going down again.  They will not eat anything unless they cook it themselves &amp; we have four special native galleys rigged on the poop for them.  They are very clean &amp; wash their hands after everything they do.</p>
<p>April 7th<br/>Passed a small sailing vessel this morning with 3 Greeks in her.  It was about the size of one of those Yachts in Sydney Harbour &amp; was flying a "mainsail" &amp; a "jib".
