<p>all the shops are closed only three or four open. Fritz has been shelling the place lately, &amp; I was told killed two women, so the inhabitants have</strike> or what few were there have left.<br/>Monday. 9th  16 Trenches<br/>Arrived back in our old place in trenches, had a quiet night.<br/>Received letter from Lou this afternoon.<br/>Thursday 12th<br/>Still in trenches.<br/>Yesterday I received a small parcel from Mother &amp; a letter also.<br/>Will has arrived home safely.<br/>The rain has kept off for these last few days, but clouds are hanging about to day. This is a very windy place, gales of wind nearly every day.<br/>There is an arrangement now, so that we can change our socks every day. There are what are called sockmen, who bring clean socks to the trenches every day, &amp; then take back the dirty ones to the divn. baths where they are washed. No such a bad arrangement, but it does not take on at all, for after you give in a good pair, it is not nice to get an old pair back, which is what you are sure to get</p>
