<p>Wednesday 3rd May.<br/>New order of drill.  Called at 4 A.M. &amp; B. section marched off.  We started at 6.20 A.M. &amp; marched out to the desert for drill finishing up with stretcher drill.  Have been recommended for Staff Sergeant dispenser to the new section.  During afternoon had a lecture in physiology for which I was the model standing up without the proverbial figleaf.</p>
<p>Thursday 4th May<br/>Reveille 5 A.M. marching, company drill &amp; stretcher drill on the desert.  Half holiday during afternoon.  General Townshend surrender now made public a great pity that such a thing should be possible.  General rumours of something doing very shortly.</p>
<p>Friday 5th May<br/>A very hot day.  The drills on the desert being rather severe finishing up with stretcher drill on the wagons.  Lecture during afternoon. Orders out for divisional manoeuvres &amp; we start 4 A.M. in the morning.</p>
<p>Saturday 6th May<br/>Reveille 4 A.M. parade 5.30 after breakfast. Full marching order for divisional manoeuvres.  The plan was to advance &amp; attack a force of Turks 6000 strong 12 miles off.  We set off &amp; deployed to the attack</p>
