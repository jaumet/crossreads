<p>Sunday 23rd June<br/>A fine day again.  Had a busy night 57 cases coming through as the result of a raid &amp; a minor stunt.  Buried Harry Fleischmann today in Franvillers cemetery.  My M.S.M. has come through officially &amp; reads &ndash;<br/>His majesty the King has been graciously pleased to approve of the award of the Meritorious Service Medal to the undermentioned Warrant Officers Non-commissioned officers and men in recognition of valuable services rendered with the Forces in France during the present war.<br/>Extract from The London Gazette Supplement No. 30750. dated 17th June 1918<br/>   War Office 17the June 1918.<br/>No 1751 S/Sgt H.E. Gissing 14th Australian Field Amb.<br/>I am very pleased about it, mostly on behalf of those at home.<br/>Had arranged last night to take a trip up the line but the heads forbade it later, so had to give up the idea.</p>
<p>Wednesday 26th June<br/>Very quiet still and weather cold, not at all like summer.  Latest</p>
