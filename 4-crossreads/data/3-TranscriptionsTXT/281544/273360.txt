After dinner at about 2 Pm over came a shell &amp; exploded a few feet away from 296 Company R.E.'s Kitchen &amp; blew it over &amp; wounded the only man slightly who was in the Kitchen, it made a mess of the Kitchen which was built of Wood &amp; Iron. I signed on duty at midnight Monday 12th shunting in St Jean Yard &amp; during the night things generally were very quiet until about 4.30 am when a heavy Bombardment opened &amp; things did hum for an hour. Flashes  of lights  from our Fairy lights &amp; from the Guns lighting up the fireing line, at about 5.30 it all died away. I was standing at Reigersberg Cabin waiting to bank up at Train to St Jean while this bombardment was on &amp; after a while along the Train came &amp; soon I was back up at St Jean.
