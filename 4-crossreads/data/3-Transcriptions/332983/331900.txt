<p>10-7-1916<br/>Weather fine. A bit of a route march this evening, full marching order. The idea is to get all men on parade in marching order; the Q.M. Sgt then go around, and collects all equipment clothing,   &amp; etc, and returns the same to ordnance. Quite on our front.<br/>11-7-1916<br/>Marching orders. Left billet at 4-30 P.M. arrived at Godewaersvelde, 8 P.M. Entrained. Ready to move off at 10-30 P.M. Arrived at Doullens. 6 A.M. on the 12-7-1916<br/>12-7-1916<br/>Left Doullens at 7-30 A.M. Arrived at billet 6 P.M., march of 20 miles The Brigade camped together 1st, 2nd, 3rd 101st and B.H.Q. This village is Bourdon<br/>13-7-1916<br/>Boot and saddle 8 A.M. Ready to move off at 9.15 A.M. Order cancelled. Raining. A large number of horses with sore backs and necks. Camped almost on the banks of the Somme<br/>14-7-1916<br/>Battery manoeuvre. Very unlucky day. One man bumped off wagon and run over. He was the Bty fitter. One officers</p>
