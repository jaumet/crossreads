<p>Sunday 27 February 1916<br/>On duty as usual in R.E. yard.</p>
<p>Monday 28 February 1916<br/>On duty in R.E. yard.  Nothing to report.</p>
<p>Tuesday 29 February 1916<br/>R.E. yard.  Reported Greece in war.</p>
<p>Wednesday 1 March 1916<br/>Working in R.E. yard.  Reported another British boat sunk off Dover.</p>
<p>Thursday 2 March 1916<br/>Tremendous heavy wind, dense dust lasting all day.  Working in R.E. yard.</p>
<p>Friday 3 March 1916<br/>Same work as yesterday.  Having terrific wind and dust.</p>
<p>Saturday 4 March 1916<br/>Wind still very heavy.  Nothing fresh to report.</p>
<p>Sunday 5 March 1916<br/>Again windy &amp; dusty.  Went to Canal &amp; had a swim.  Usual R E yard work.  Nothing fresh reported.</p>
<p>Monday 6 March 1916<br/>Working in R E yard as usual.  Nothing fresh going.  Expect to leave tomorrow.</p>
<p>Tuesday 7 March 1916<br/>Left R.E yard &amp; reported to H.Q. to leave for new Coy.  Had to stay here for day.</p>
<p>Wednesday 8 March 1916<br/>Still with Coy. waiting for orders to move.  Nothing of importance reported.</p>
<p>Thursday 9 March 1916<br/>Left Serapheum about 7 a.m., arrived at Tel El Kebir about 1 p.m.  To be allotted new Coy. tomorrow.</p>
<p>Friday 10 March 1916<br/>Thirty-two F.F. Coy. allotted to 14th F. Coy. this morning.  Took up position site near Camp.  Waiting for Reinforcements.</p>
<p>Saturday 11 March 1916<br/>Very little doing today.  Waiting for [indecipherable]</p>
