<p>Well I must admit I was done before we had reached the three mile mark but I stuck to my gun's.</p>
<p>It was now dark and we nealy lost our way a few times, and we had to put our ear's down on the cobbles to hear which way they had gone, we were fully two hundred yard's behind the main body, so we done well to keep in touch as much as we did.</p>
<p>To make a long story short we came to the village where we were to be billited, that was after the rear guard had experianced some hardships, and given all their water away.</p>
<p>We were taken to a few places where some of the boy's were put, and the rest of us were marched over a railway line which was running through the village.</p>
