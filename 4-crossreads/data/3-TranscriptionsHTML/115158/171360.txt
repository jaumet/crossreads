<p>Oct. 11.</p>
<p>To expedite the work, however, the ship was moved from her buoy to alongside the Dockyard wall at Rosyth, a pleasant change appreciated by all on board.&nbsp; In addition to fitting the nickel steel covering over the magazines, some compartments were filled with several feet of shingle, in order to render the magazines less vulnerable.&nbsp; This decision had been taken as the direct result of the Jutland action of May 31st., when enemy dropping shots pierced the decks &amp; entered the magazines before exploding, resulting in the loss of &quot;Queen Mary&quot;, &quot;Indefatigable&quot;, &amp; &quot;Invincible&quot;.</p>
<p>Oct. 31.</p>
<p>At the end of October the Battle cruiser fleet, with light cruisers &amp; destroyers, proceeded to sea for tactical exercises.&nbsp; Smoke screens as practised by the Germans in the Jutland fight, were used during these exercises, which lasted over two days.</p>
<p>Nov. 3.</p>
<p>On returning to harbour, we completed with coal &amp; oil fuel.&nbsp; This was my last sea trip in the &quot;Australia&quot;, for I learnt that my successor was shortly to be appointed, &amp; I was to revert to the Royal Navy.&nbsp; However, I was to be allowed to remain for another month until my successor was thoroughly conversant with his duties.&nbsp; These duties entailed considerable work &amp; energy, &amp; organisation.&nbsp; The Engine Room department</p>
