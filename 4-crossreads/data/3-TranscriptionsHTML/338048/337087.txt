<p>Monday 12th<br/>Absolutely cloudless sunrise &amp; a most brilliant sunshining day.</p>
<p>News received by wireless about sinking of H.M.S. Hampshire &amp; Russian capture of 71,000 Germans. This is first news received since we left port and is very acceptable.</p>
<p>School of whales seen spouting about 1 mile to port. Spouting looking like a cloud of spray. In afternoon whale appeared about 300 yds away &amp; spouted. You see a brownish <strike>back or</strike> head appear about 6" above water &amp; then stream of spray about 6 ft high. Spray lingers in air about 5 sec.</p>
<p>Meal arrangements improving enabling one to have meal in greater comfort.</p>
<p>Tuesday 13th<br/>Fine day sea calm, further war news but not very important. Work as usual</p>
<p>Supposed to be rather north of the Cape &amp; southerly course taken during night. Expect to reach port about Saturday.</p>
<p>Wednesday 14th<br/>Another fine day. Sea slightly rough early but calmed down.</p>
<p>News re capture of 141,000 received. War news evidently usual thing now, we are in touch with Durban. Had finger which was slightly septic &amp; lanced about 4 days treated. Dead skin removed &amp; codine &amp; boracic applied. Nothing much the matter.</p>
<p>Thursday 15th<br/>Rather rough News re Kitcheners death announced. Weather boisterous in afternoon &amp; evening. S.W. gale raging.</p>
