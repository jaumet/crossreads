He was only young &amp; was well liked by his Squadron.<br/>We lost one of our 70 H.P  M.F.S.H last Saturday.  Two officers were up in it &amp; when they were at an altitude of 3500 ft the engine spluttered and stopped.  They brought the machine down alright but the only available landing place was a paddock under a heavy crop of wheat.  The machine running through this became entangled, &amp; it turned over.  Turning over at a speed of over 30 m. p. h. could only have one result, the machine was smashed up.  The Officers, however escaped with a broken arm &amp; a broken ankle between them.<br/>I am hanging on waiting for an opportunity to go to a school, &amp; then I intend qualifying for my pilot's certificate.
