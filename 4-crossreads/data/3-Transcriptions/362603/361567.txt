<p>3.  dead rather- on all the Railway Buffets &amp; in shops in London &amp; elsewhere.  I cannot eat them.  I used to wonder at "Snowy" eating the pollard that we gave the ducks, that pollard is wholesome &amp; beautiful to the taste.  These cakes are not fit to eat &amp; are as horrible to the taste as the vilest of medicines.<br/>I caught an early train back &amp; arrived in Bulford at 9 pm.  3 Hours from London is not too bad.  Ellis &amp; our next door neighbours soon had supper ready.  Fried steak, fried bread &amp; tea.  Don't ask how we came by it.  It is   <strike>not</strike>   rather a delicate question to ask in the army.  Granpa used to say "Get it Honestly if you can, but get it."<br/>It has been snowing all day pretty well &amp; it is going on now like Billy-ho.<br/>Well, Mum, Dad &amp; George, I've finished all duties for the night &amp; shaved, Boots &amp; leggings cleaned ready for the morning.  I was on Duty to-night.  Dave came up for a couple of Hours too, so my letter has been written under very great difficulties.  The writing is awful but I hope You will understand it.
