<p>were run for us, &amp; we were given a complimentary ticket to the "Pav." Cinema Show.  I walked up &amp; down King-William St. &amp; other parts of the town for a while &amp; then went in to see the Pictures.</p>
<p>The last train back to the ship was 10.5 p.m. &amp; we all had to catch this or miss the boat.</p>
<p>I was not able to see too much of the town nor could I form much of an opinion of what it is like generally, because it was too dark, and a person cannot see much the first night in a new city.  One thing I did notice though, is the very wide streets &amp; the wide Railway gauge &ndash; it is much wider than the N.S.W. gauge this being 5 ft. 2&frac12; inches wide.</p>
<p>We are taking a little more coal on board here, all the cranes are going as hard as they can, we are expected to be leaving very late to night or early to-morrow morning.</p>
