<p>Friday Jan 15-15. Journal Part 6 &ndash; The S.S. Morinda has gone out &amp; <strike>we</strike> I feel free &ndash; The Warrego &amp; Parramatta have come in &ndash; What is our next move? &ndash; I don't know &ndash;<br/>I went to the Beach at 4 pm on Thursday &amp; went to two hospitals &amp; held converse &ndash; ribald &amp; otherwise &ndash; with young Captains of the Medical Staff &ndash; Their moan is like own &ndash; A Commissariat moan &ndash; no supplies in Rabaul &ndash; nothing to be had for love or money &ndash; Why a ship was not run fortnightly between Townsville &amp; Rabaul &amp; fresh provisions &ndash; tinned fruit &amp; the hundred &amp; one necessities of life brought up God alone knows? &ndash; Why have men to lie on the floor of verandahs &amp; rooms? &ndash; men down with malaria &ndash; Why not have sent up 200 beds &ndash; in 3 pieces &amp; a thin fibre mattress &ndash; Four nurses are up from Townsville &ndash; Twenty are wanted &ndash; Where's the Grantala? She'd be some use now with her 200 cots &amp; swank staff &ndash; Here are all the Destroyers in &amp; no fresh meat for the men</p>
