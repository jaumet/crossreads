<p>the captain, and wounded the pilot who were on the bridge at the time.  Ahead of us the cruiser Proserpine which had been very successful in capturing German prizes, was anchored.  Situated on the quay astern of us was a French aerodrome which housed a number of hydroplanes.  During the period preceding the Turkish march on the Canaal these machines did a lot of reconnoitring, reporting the position, strength and equipment of the enemy; thus the probable time and place of attack was known to a nicety.  At 9.30a we heard a great humming noise, and, looking across the harbour saw a hydroplane travelling along the top of the water at a tremendous pace.  After proceeding a couple of hundred yards in this fashion it rose from the water, and, ascending every moment flew away down the Canaal</p>
