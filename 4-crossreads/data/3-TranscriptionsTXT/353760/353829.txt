 January 26 120. Saw last night first number of "Aussy", I say Digger, the only drawing of 1st Divvy's pages.  If I send in any paragraphs they'll be under "Valthick". 
 121. A cold day full of grey mists, and no mail that was expected, but from A.B. note of their keeping with other A.I.F. sketches the one of the Kruisstraat.  P.C. from Mat. 
 122. With Ridge to Y.M. Concert by Tommy party "The Dabs", the audience splendid! 
 123. Writing tonight. J.D.H. K.I.A. Oct. 1917 Grey clouds roll on a great grey sky And the mist from the ground's breast high And a sad song'd breeze Blows by bare torn trees, And the ghosts of the dead pass by. 
 Tier'd guns beat the whole night long, Over the hills their poor tired song.

 January 27 124. David MacKenzie's full this morning of impending revelations:  last night in the hut he betted his next week's rum against the crowd that a four days armistice would begin today.  In the Breakfast came to Bill he says, full of ernestness &ndash; "Can ye tell me why there's no gunfire this morning?", and so we heard of his prophecies.
