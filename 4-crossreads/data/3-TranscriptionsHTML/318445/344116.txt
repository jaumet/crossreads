In close contact came the 19th Battalion, picking its way through vast skeins of barbed wire;<strike>and</strike> some platoons took to the railway line, which provided a comparatively free passage, but this was quickly swept by the enemy machine guns. These three Battalions moved on, however, in good order, and with a precision that could only spring from a dogged determination to get through at any cost. In close reserve followed the 18th Battalion, which, later on the day, was to move across this field with sublime courage,<strike>always giving</strike> to give the firmest support, wherever the line was bent, or broken, <strike>under</strike> by the heavy shocks of repeated counter-attacks.</p>
