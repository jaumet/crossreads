<p>midday and had lunch then a stroll around the town and boarded the 4.30 train for Dunkirk we believed. After a wretched journey however we pulled up at the battered town of Ardinkirk. Here we were ordered out the conductor saying that the train did not <strike>leave</strike> go on any further: We refused to budge however believing that he was fabricating as usual. We were soon convinced <strike>however</strike> that for once the Froggy was right for we found ourselves in a siding <strike>with</strike> about 12 midnight pouring with rain and the engine off.</p>
