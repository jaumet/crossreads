Thus at the appointed hour of 10.10 on the morning of the 22nd, not without much agony &amp; tears of blood, was each man in full regalia standing behind his horse with all his saddlery &amp; steel (bright side upper most) laid out on a blanket in front of him. Along came Chauvel with the usual train of sycophants &amp; satelites trailing along after him &amp; on my being pointed out as a noble example of the 1st Contingent we had a heart to heart chat.
