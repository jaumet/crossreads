<p>5 p.m.  Fine day.  Retire 9 p.m.  Write to Mother.</p>
<p>Wednesday, May 24th:<br/>Weather fine during day.  Showery towards evening.  Rise 5.30.  Walk out to work at Army Medical Corps Clearing Station, erecting huts.  Retire 9 p.m.</p>
<p>Thursday, May 25th:<br/>Showery, but decent.  Rise 5.30 a.m.  Stop home to-day and help cook and do general cleaning up to billet.  Write to Jack.  Our first reinforcements arrive here about 7 p.m.  Retire 8.30 p.m.</p>
<p>Friday, May 26th:<br/>Rise 5.30 a.m.  Fine day.  Work all day, Army Medical.  Retire 8.30 p.m.</p>
<p>Saturday, May 27th:<br/>Rise 5.30 a.m.  Fine.  Work out at A.M.C. Clearing Station again to-day.  Quiet day.  Retire 8.30 p.m.</p>
<p>Sunday, May 28th:<br/>Fine day.  Rise 7 a.m.  Commence work at A.M.C. and complete at noon.  After lunch, we go to work in a shed near the Divisional Baths, flooring. Return home at 4.30 p.m.  Commence to draw plan for our Sergeant of our new huts 24 x 16.  Retire 9 p.m.</p>
<p>Monday, May 29th:<br/>Rise 5.30 a.m.  Fine day.  Work at our own transports</p>
