 We went up to Steenwerck R.E. Dump & loaded up with R.E. stores. Bud had a cracked cylinder & I had to tow his lorry with my load on & his also. I towed him to our destination OX Kent amunition dump & then towed him back to Park. I went into Bailleul & spent the evening with Jack & Dick. 12  th   Bgd. in their lorries - 
   
 Jan 9  th    Fine but cold in the morning & snowing all afternoon - in the evening it got much warmer - we did'nt go out to-day - I had letters from home 
   
 Jan 10  th    It started to thaw very much to-day, no lorries out on account of the thaw. it is quite cold on hands & feet - we can get no "metho" now, but manage to keep the lorry fairly warm with the primus - 
   
 Jan 11  th    Weather still soft - nothing to do - I read "the Harvester" J.S. Porter. I had a bit of an argument with Joe. which he took to heart  he went into Bailleul in the evening & he came home boozed at night - 
   
 Jan 12  th    Still thawing. very wet underfoot & there is practically no ice or snow left. I wrote away for Pelman course of memory training - Received a parcel from Marion as she is on leave in Blighty -
