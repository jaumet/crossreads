 out to our bitter cost. 5th.  A couple of minutes to go &amp; all were straining at the leash, the moon had almost gone down &amp; there was a faint ghostly light over all save when the flares lit every-thing up in a white shimmering light &amp; the ground could be seen to be all watery &amp; shiny all of a sudden the strain was over, &amp; so were we, I was the first one of my platoon (as I should be) to hop over &amp; I gave a few more a helping hand for the sides of the trench were steep &amp; slippery &amp; off we rushed, as soon as we showed up the flares were sent up in batches which lit everything up like day, &amp; showed us men falling everywhere &amp; the boys struggling through the mud bogged nearly to the knees for the ground was as soft as a well fallowed paddock after a storm of rain, I was forcing my way through as fast as I could &amp; calling for my men to keep up "&amp; box on"
