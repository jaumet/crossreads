<p>5th March 1915 Friday</p>
<p>The sea is wildish owing to the fresh breeze.  No land in sight but steamers are to be seen frequently.  Some of the islands we saw a day or two ago had never had rain for 25 yrs. (so says the Chief Officer).  We had another signalling contest <strike>yes</strike> today.  Lieuts. James &amp; Ireland (or our coy) judjing.  The Tassies won again by 9 points after a very close contest.  I was told my sending was quite superior to theirs.  That' s one in my cap.  The Lieuts. seemed very pleased that we showed such an interest in our work when the rest of the sigs. are neglecting</p>
