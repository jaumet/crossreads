<p>The Imperial Hotel<br/>Russel Square<br/>London . W.C,<br/>10.9.16</p>
<p>Dear father,<br/>The lack of correspondence lately, has been due to our state of<br/>unsettled anticipation; but at last things have come to a head and we are once more back into the old groove.;</p>
<p>The 3rd Div cyclist boy after a valiant struggle to keep the old title, has at last<br/> succumbed to overwhelming odds and is no more.  We are scattered to the<br/> four winds of heaven and if there is a</p>
<p>Yelled out in a gleeful voice  'Ah! Burnt to death the ----!</p>
<p>I have written to MacCallum but as yet have received no reply. This is not very surprising, as I suppose most of our mail will go astray now.</p>
<p>I have not been able to find out anything about Arthur's belongings here. If they can tell me nothing at A.I.F. headquarters at Horseferry Rd. tomorrow, I will write to the 20th Bn and find out what has been done with them.</p>
<p>Give my love to mother Dorothy and Catherine &ndash; and remember me to Mr. O'Reilly when you see him.</p>
<p>Your loving son Keith</p>
<p>Ps. I got in touch with Mr Pring the other day, but have not seen him yet.</p>
