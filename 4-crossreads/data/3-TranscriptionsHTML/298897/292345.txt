The gas used is chlorine chiefly &amp; is kept in cylinders being compressed under a pressure of 7 atmospheres &amp; is in liquid form.  60 lbs being in one cylinder when turned on last 3 minutes.  On a bright day it appears in greenish yellow column, on a damp day &ndash; white.  When strong such as 1-1000 two breaths only is fatal.  Carbonyl chloride or Phosgene has been used in conjunction with chlorine in warm weather &amp; two breaths of 1-5000 is fatal.  The attack in Juneaffected people 5&frac12; miles behind the line so that now all are supplied with Helmets   strange that our helmet differs from the German &amp; French ours being more successful than the French judging by number of casualties.</p>
