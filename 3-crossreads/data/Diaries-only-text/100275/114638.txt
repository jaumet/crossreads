For dinner we had bully, biscuits, tea so we are not living to flash as yet. All day long night as well there are great strings of motor lorries passing backwards forwards. All engaged in carting shells to the firing line most of them 9.2. Left the camp this evening about 3.30 marched to the railway station about 3 miles distant, here we had to wait until 5.30 in the meantime I went over to where an old Tommy was cooking I scored over here for I got a big joint of roast beef 2 loaves of bread, a dixie of hot tea
