The Australians have saved from villages in the forward area thousands' worth of valuable furniture, pianos, etc. &ndash; (News item)."] July 13, 15 263. Pasting from Anzac Bulletin:  to supplement pastings 86, 178 and the cover of Ghutz No. 4. 
 A day of ordinary workings, but for once, an altering and bringing to date the G.O.C.'s map:  usually Mac's job, but he's away. 
 Still the usual patrols, and more prisoners today. 
 No Mail. 
 Madame opposite charges 5' for her beer, so a boycott! 
 July 15 264. Yesterday to Cassel.  In the evening met one of the M.G. men and we spent till 9 drinking with some [indecipherable], a good night and enjoyed. 
 The Boche attack has developed again &ndash; on a 50 mile [?] front about Rheims, midday. 
 July 16 265. Midday:  to the west of Rheims the Americans have counter-attacked and taken 600 prisoners, taken till today, the third stunt of the Boche has not been any definite success:  the French are well holding him.
