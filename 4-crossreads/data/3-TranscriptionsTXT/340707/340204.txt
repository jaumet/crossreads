 After supper at the Strand we adjourn home. 
 There was one thing that I liked about the French procession &amp; that was that in front of all marcher were wheeled a 1000 war cripples. This had no counterpart in England but seats in the Mall were assigned to widows &amp; orphans, to Chelsea pensioners to wounded soldiers &amp; to school children. In France school children were provided for but I dont know about the others. Seats were made of empty ammunition boxes. Some was done at Paris I find 
 22-7-19 Rose at 5-30 am this morning &amp; breakfast was ready for me &amp; Lily was waiting to say good bye. This will be the last time I shall see her as she is being married on Bank holiday.
