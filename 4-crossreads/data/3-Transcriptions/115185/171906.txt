<p>14 &ndash; Then breakfast. I sit next to Matron But we do not talk much &ndash; a lady Miss Keys who came out with Mrs Vans Agnew to over see the distribution of Lady Hamilton's Gifts for soldiers at the front &ndash; fund &ndash; and has not been able to live in peace with that Lady &ndash; at the admiral's (the Governor) request has been given lodging by us. She had breakfast with us. I don't know what your Mrs Vans Agnew is doing across the bay in a camp by herself without a maid! This girl is going to help the rev Crieghton &ndash; (the Archbishop of London who was son) to run a canteen for the soldiers. He's a fine fellow &ndash; I have met him with Col Fortescue &ndash; is very delicate &ndash; has dipthen paralysis of a leg. Then I heard the N.Z. band and rushed to see the Auckland M.R. &amp; Wellington pass but they passed too far away down a side road to the pier. Then I was asked to talk French to an Algerian &ndash; who is sick in hospital here &ndash; not very interesting man. Then I went to bed &ndash; got up at 5.30 pm. Had dinner at 6. Sat beside Capt Lloyd for 20 minutes. He is much worse &ndash; and felt very sad about him &ndash; went and talked to Captain Boyes &ndash; I think I told you of before is a Royal Engineer.
