 enemy aircraft came over &amp; I could hear some of our chaps discussing them as they were fired at but I was too tired &amp; go &amp; look myself.  Then I heard one man say &ndash; here he comes now &amp; is going to drop his eggs &ndash; said jokingly but it was only too true.  Within a few seconds there was a tremendous explosion followed by 5 others at rapid intervals &amp; the place was full of dirt &amp; brickdust &amp; shaking as by an earthquake.  For a few seconds it was impossible to light a match, then I lit a candle &amp; groped my way into the next room which was fitted up for dressings &amp; I could hear men calling out, if anyone was hit. Once more we were extremely lucky as only one man was killed, [indecipherable] Saunders an original man, &amp; four others wounded besides numerous abrasions not serious enough to be evacuated.
