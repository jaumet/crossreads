<p>Sunday 9 April 1916<br/>No drill for Sec.  Was kept very busy most of the day preparing for work in trenches tomorrow.</p>
<p>Monday 10 April 1916<br/>Had party working in trenches, also Coy. of Infantry working revetting, etc.</p>
<p>Tuesday 11 April 1916<br/>Constructing trenches in Bridge Head defences about one mile from Ferry post.</p>
<p>Wednesday 12 April 1916<br/>Same as yesterday.  Supervising trench construction by our own Sappers &amp; Infantry.</p>
<p>Thursday 13 April 1916<br/>Still working in trench revetting &amp; instructing Infantry.  Tremendous heavy wind.</p>
<p>Friday 14 April 1916<br/>Still windy.  Heavy rain settling dust a great deal.  Working revetting trenches.</p>
<p>Saturday 15 April 1916<br/>Reported L. Horse had brush with party Turks off Serapheum capturing 40.  One L.H. man killed.</p>
<p>Sunday 16 April 1916<br/>Only morning Parade.  No work in Trenches.  Beautiful day tho' very warm.</p>
<p>Monday 17 April 1916<br/>Work in trenches in morning.  Handed over to No. 3 Section.  Musketry in afternoon.</p>
<p>Tuesday 18 April 1916<br/>Musketry &ndash; squad drill - <strike>trench work</strike>.  Nothing of importance reported.</p>
<p>Wednesday 19 April 1916<br/>Instructing Section in Musketry all day, training very hard.  Nothing of importance reported.</p>
<p>Thursday 20 April 1916<br/>Musketry all day again today.  Weather very warm.  Nothing of importance going.</p>
<p>Friday 21 April 1916<br/>Usual drill and Rifle exercises.  Nothing of importance going.</p>
<p>Saturday 22 April 1916<br/>Out with Section.  Rifle exercises, squad drill &amp; Demolition.</p>
