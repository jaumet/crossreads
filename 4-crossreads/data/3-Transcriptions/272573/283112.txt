<p>These men who came in wounded had their faces all blackened the reason being so that they would be less liable to be recognized in the darkness of the night.</p>
<p>July 3rd Mon.<br/>E. this morning.</p>
<p>11 Deaths to-day.</p>
<p>An Abdominal Case early this morning.</p>
<p>Return of C.B: C.M: B.C: D.W: from Paris leave.</p>
<p>July 5th. Wed.<br/>A Big number of serious cases came in to-day. Numerous deaths</p>
<p>The people who have Cherry trees are all busy picking the cherries of the trees. Tall ladders are used to reach them.</p>
<p>The Australian troops are on the march : and are passing this way to-day. These chaps have just come over from Egypt. They have done a good bit of marching since landing in this country and many of them were foot sore and limped along as best they could, some even had their boots off</p>
<p>On the whole however they seem a fine body of chaps.</p>
<p>The streets are now crowded with them, they are billeted in town in all kind of places any place that a man can stretch himself in the village is used up.</p>
<p>July 9th. Sun.<br/>Good report in the newspaper regarding British advance.</p>
<p>Aeroplanes overhead are pretty busy.</p>
<p>Witnessed a sale of furniture</p>
