<p>cabin 199 being in with 3 other Aussies. Boat cast off from wharf 4.30 pm but anchored midstream. Had dinner 6.30 pm &ndash; 5 or 6 courses and very nice after army food. Went to bed about 10.30 pm and rather warm in the cabin all night.</p>
<p>21.1.19<br/>Up 7.30 am. Boat stopped in midstream all night and sailed 12 noon today. Had breakfast about 8 am then had good walk on the decks. Not too cold. Lunch at 12.30 pm &ndash; during morning we were all allotted to our dining room seats. After lunch most of the Aussies on board got on one of the decks and played pitch and toss.</p>
<p>Couple of hours after leaving Liverpool we struck the Welsh coast and steamed along it for about 1&frac12; hrs. Had dinner 6.30 pm then wrote to Uncle Charlie and Baker St as calling at Brest tomorrow.</p>
<p>Went to bed 10.30 pm.</p>
<p>22.1.19<br/>Up 7.30 am, breakfast 8 am. Fairly warm in morning but afternoon became very cold and windy and little swell. Had hot bath 11 am. During afternoon passed 2 or 3 boats and sighted French coast about 3 pm. Did not go to a wharf &ndash; anchored out in the harbour.</p>
<p>Cough has not been as bad as usual today.</p>
<p>The place looked rather fine from boat after dark being all lit up, also all ships in port.</p>
<p>23.1.19<br/>American troops numbering about 2000 commenced to embark on boat about 9 am. Some of them only came to France in October.</p>
