<p>Apl 15  incidentally roars &amp; laughter from the mob, by trying to sing "My Mary". Shortly after Jack the Cook woke us all up with the information that sundry swine had bombarded the cook house, &amp; that unless an armed guard was supplied "per see" the Chief Chef would decline all responsibility for the Companys breakfast. The tucker in question I might add was &ndash; "pork". A couple of nights since the same swine ate our ration of pork &amp; for brek we had "tea &amp; tea" &ndash; (At first the boys fairly laughed the cook out of sight) but, becoming duly impressed with the importance of saving their grub, 2 volunteers hopped out &amp; brought the scran inside out of harms way. Our noble friend <u>Harry</u> was one of the heroes, &amp; so fatigued &amp; hungry was he after such strenuous labor that he forthwith ate much bread &amp; jam &amp; even the fact that I plonked my foot &amp; half</p>
