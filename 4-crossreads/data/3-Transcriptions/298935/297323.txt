<p>over a &pound;1 per head of the troops.  We were often asked for impossible things for a transport to carry.  One man wanted oysters and champagne.  Another, early in the voyage, looking very green asked for a seasick cure.  When told that we did not stock any he replied in a doleful voice "Oh well, give me a stick of chocolate instead!"  Eleven days from England we were ordered to constantly wear lifebelts to be ready in case we were torpedoed.  No lights were shown at night and boatdrills were frequently held.<br/>We made a short stay at St. Vincent, a naval base in the Cape Verde Islands.  On the 14th November half way through the Bay of Biscay a destroyer met us and escorted the ship to Plymouth.  The troops disembarked here and we sailed round to London.</p>
