<p>[Next follows some writing in shorthand]<br/>This afternoon the enemy sent over a lot of shells in this direction, many of them falling not far from the stables and five shrapnel shells burst in the air some of them right over our billets evidently in the hope of hitting our baloon which was promptly hauled down.</p>
<p>Monday June 18,1917</p>
<p>It is again very hot today.<br/>Some enemy planes are aloft this morning.  The first were about very early this morning &amp; were fired upon by our archies at 5 o'clock long before I was out of bed, and re-appeared several times throughout the fore noon.  Their purpose evidently was to observe the enemy's shots which he was continually sending over towards Bailleul.</p>
