<p>more for we were on high ground on mountains adjoining abrupt sheer hills.  The thick gorse and brambles were alight &amp; flaming furiously here there and everywhere.  Kuchuk{?}  hill was covered by Turkish shrapnell while our warships replied furiously &amp; set fire to the town of Anafarta{?} besides annihilating two Turkish heavy guns.  Regiments were moving for position everywhere &amp; in vain did they fail to push forward.  After severe fighting the lines were joined,  &amp; all remained quiet  [indecipherable] the few following days.  The land beyond was green and smooth &amp; would probably be very cultivating &amp; fertile.</p>
