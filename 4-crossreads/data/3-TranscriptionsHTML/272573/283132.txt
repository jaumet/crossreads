<p>To me there is nothing finer than to see a battalion of highlanders on the march. The kilt seems to enhance the appearance of the men and makes them big and brawny looking.</p>
<p>Amongst the S. chaps that I have seen to-day in the village G. McK., M., W., J. T. came up to the C.C.S. to see me and we had a talk regarding old times.</p>
<p>It is now almost two years since I joined the army and out of the thousands of chaps that I have met these few are all that I had known previous to leaving the old country.</p>
<p>I also met M.C. &amp; Piper B. of the 7th A. &amp; S.H.</p>
<p>Thursday Sept 28th.</p>
<p>Some of the chaps belonging to the Concert party had a night out to-night. The majority of them came home rather tipsy. One of them had a bottle of champagne in his hand and letting it drop, it fell and smashed on the floor causing an abnoxious smell throughout the night.</p>
<p>Sept 29th. Fri.<br/>On our way over to the "Grand Bois" we came across an aeroplane which had landed in a ploughed field. It apparently had had to come down through engine trouble as it was quite uninjured.</p>
