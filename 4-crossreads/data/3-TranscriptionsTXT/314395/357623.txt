 the consequence was that we came up too soon and came under a very heavy barrage and a good deal of machine-gun fire, from a ridge on the right.  We must have been in full view of the enemy and things became so unpleasant that we had to abandon the guns &amp; seek cover in a trench close by.  Whilst there, three enemy 'planes came over not more than 100 feet above dropping bombs and steel darts, also getting in some fine work with their machine guns.
