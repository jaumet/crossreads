 August 3rd  1916  The Monitor sea is a great help, she sends a 2,000 lb shell, lovely to listen to, sailing through the air and landing with a crunch, clouds of dust etc &ndash; We have been watching the Turks now, night &amp; day for two months &amp; no prospect of being relieved for a bit, they are getting closer &ndash; I am keeping a day's field messages, which will show you our work but I dare not post it &ndash; It is one of my first jobs on returning to bivouacs, to notify Brigade H Quarters, if our losses, which are fortunately slight &ndash; August 4th 5th  see Diary  August 6th A camel cacolet is like a baby's perambulator, with a little hood &ndash; I thought I was right but the girth slipped &amp; broke &amp; I was emptied out, so the M.O. sent me to Rail head in a sand cart. &ndash; Before arriving at the Field Ambulance I was put on a table given a dose of ether a bit of shell taken out, bandaged up, given a whisky, a cup of Bovril &amp; started for the Hospital &ndash; sent on in a goods train to the Canal, then Red X train, great luxury - &amp; tinned rabbit &ndash; Australian at that &ndash; Landed here No 3 AG Hospital Abbassia once more &ndash; lungs a bit sore but soon all right &ndash;
