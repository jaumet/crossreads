<p>we had fried bacon &amp; porridge for breakfast. We found a tin of milk, in a safe place, and had it to the porridge. It was good.</p>
<p>Some of the limbres were up at Ypres last night with rations &amp; report that there is scarsely a house standing. Bombs were dropped in from of them coming back.</p>
<p>I received a letter from my wife this morning dated July 23 &amp; numbered 80.</p>
<p>The pack stunt was detailed to day and are to go up to the front to night about 2. a.m. leading pack mules &amp; horses. The ration limbres went after tea &amp; before dark. I narrowly missed being detailed for the pack stunt, and am to remain in the camp and do picquet duty every other night.</p>
<p>Thursday October 4. 1917. (1158).</p>
<p>A cold wet showery morning.</p>
<p>Early this morning about 3. a.m. the pack stunt made a start. They had some breakfast before starting and all hands were roused up to help them saddle the mules &amp; get them away. They have not yet returned (11. a.m.) at time of writing.</p>
