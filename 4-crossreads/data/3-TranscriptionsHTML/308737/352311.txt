<p>careful how you bring her to earth in a glide. Piloting an aeroplane is to one like sailing a yacht. The day I was launched, that is, when the instructor sent me up on my own, I felt as happy as larry, &amp; king of the air. I stopped up for an hour that day.</p>
<p>One has no sense of speed or movement when well up in the air. All you get is the rush of wind past your head &amp; the roar of your engine. You get plenty of sensation when you shut your engine off, put her nose down to glide to earth, or when you stunt up at a good height. (Switch-back, &amp; steep banking)</p>
