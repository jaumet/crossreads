We ran out and held him while he untied himself. The parachute was made of silk. One of our fellows commenced folding it up for him, and his cobber warned the airman to watch him as he was a souvenir hunter and would probably make off with it. Aug 4&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On 4th August we packed up again for the line. We knew this time that something was going to happen although we had not been practising for it in any way. We marched through    Bussy-les   Daours, Aubigny and Fouilloy. After getting past Fouilloy it was getting on for dusk. The roads were crammed with transport of all kinds going both ways and with troops all making towards the line. One continuous stream of horses men and limbers.
