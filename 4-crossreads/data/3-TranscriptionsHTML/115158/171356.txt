<p>1916.</p>
<p>forenoon the Battle Fleet under Jellicoe was sighted, &amp; we moved South in search of the enemy, everyone being at &quot;action stations&quot;.&nbsp; One report from our light cruisers scouting ahead, was that the enemy had been sighted, &amp; we were some thirty miles off.&nbsp; I passed the news below to the Engine Room &amp; Boiler rooms.&nbsp; Here the enthusiasm was intense, &amp; this enthusiasm must have been transmitted to the steam pressure, for we could hardly keep it back.&nbsp; Later a Zeppelin was sighted by our light cruiser patrol, and this meant, of course, that our position was known to the enemy, &amp; also our strength, so that the prospect of an engagement was very remote, especially in view of our vastly superior strength.&nbsp; However, we remained at &quot;action stations&quot;, &amp; turned right round &amp; proceeded North, in the hope that the Zeppelin would be led into the belief that we intended returning home, &amp; so draw the German Fleet well out from their bases.&nbsp; During the afternoon, two Zeppelins approached our light cruisers.&nbsp; They were plainly visible to us, some 10 miles away.&nbsp; The Zeppelins dropped bombs on the light cruisers, without doing any damage, the ships replying with their anti-aircraft guns.&nbsp; The Shrapnel could be plainly seen bursting round the airships, but evidently did no serious damage.&nbsp; The airships were wonderful in the way they could manipulate the</p>
