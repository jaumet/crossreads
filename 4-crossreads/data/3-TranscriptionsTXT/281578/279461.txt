At breakfast time, two Fritz aeroplanes were up a great height, & I watched our shrapnel bursts of smoke in the sky, hoping they would bring them down, but though the shooting was good, & went jolly close, the Fritzies sailed away. About midday 6 of our aeroplanes were overhead like graceful birds, & they each wheeled in turn following in the wake of the leader. Where our 'planes are when Fritz is up, beats me. Had stew for dinner, & a rifle inspection in the afternoon. This is a lovely life here in reserve, better than camp, with only 3 hours work a day, & a nice cosy dug out to live in, as long as Fritz doesn't lob 'em over too straight. The boys had a game of football after tea (it's not dark till after 8 now), as happy as school kids - the Australians are grand chaps, friendly good pals always, reliable in an emergency, plenty of initiative, & I'm proud to be one, & among the fine chaps over here, the first best chaps are here alright, & the shirkers, who have no ties, at home.
