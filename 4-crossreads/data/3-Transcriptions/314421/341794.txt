<p>The Doctor was again round this morning, &amp; looked at the new patients.</p>
<p>I gave in all my clothes this morning to be fumingated, and am wearing hospital blues today.</p>
<p>The result of the Canadian election is a substantial majority in favour of the Borden Government &amp; Conscription. The people have approved of compulsory military service &amp; given a mandate to the Union Government to "carry on" that will hearten the fighting men, and prove to the Allies that she will see the war through to victory.</p>
<p>This afternoon I spent sorting out, reading and burning old letters.</p>
<p><strike>Friday</strike> Saturday December 22. 1917. (1237.)</p>
<p>A dull morning, but it is not cold, and there is no wind. This afternoon the sky was blue and clear. The Sun shon out brightly. It is a lovely afternoon. Too good to be inside. It is so good a day for observation that a number of enemy planes ventured out. Some big guns are in action on this front.</p>
<p>I wrote to Willie to day, also to my wife. Letter 104, 4 pages.</p>
<p>We all in this ward had a small bottle of Guinness extra stout for dinner.</p>
