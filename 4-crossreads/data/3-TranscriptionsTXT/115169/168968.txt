Each squadron of ships held Annual Regattas at a time to suit themselves, at any of the bases, and it was always eagerly looked forward to and well contested - For this occasion the boats crews of the "Birmingham" had been practicing a special stroke so as to carry off all prizes - This stroke was known as the Italian stroke, and which was performed by standing up as the oar was being into the water, putting the whole of one's weight upon the oar and falling backwards until the thwarts or seat was reached, then drawing the oar sharply
