 Everyone speaks of the Anzacs Of their wonderful brave display But does anyone hear of the Surrey Boys? Who were up in Sulva Bay? Their work was good and dangerous Their Trials and conditions hard But they have not been feted in London Or cheered on the French boulevarde No they are working damned hard out in Egypt From civilisation away In the farthest unsanitary quarters Where the Arab himself will not stay But Tommy's the boy to be cheerful And work like a slave night &amp; day And put up with sauce for a tanner Not six bob that's Anzac's Pay
