8/8/19 Durban
hall carpeted. Two seated men praying. Entering the native markets we found booths with all sorts of goods for sale and I bought a round little box. The old doctor sat on the ground and had boxes of all sorts of herb remedies. Men and sturdy looking women consulted them. Rows of stalls with meat cakes sweetmeats re for sale. Betel leaves and lime also popular. The star sight was the Zulu rickshawmen's canteen. Behind iron bars in a large shed are tables. These were crowded with the Zulus. All over 6 feet wearing only white ornamental shifts their heads crowned with spreading bulls horns and feathers all talking at top voice and gesticulating. They looked like a lot of beasts.
