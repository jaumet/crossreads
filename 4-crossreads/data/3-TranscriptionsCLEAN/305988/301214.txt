 We advanced in artillery formation & after we got through his barrage we met with very little opposition. It was impossible to see more than 4 yards ahead so you can imagine what it was like. Well we went forward for 4000 yards taking many prisoners, field-guns & hundreds of machine gun. We took the villages of Warfussee, Abancourt, & Lamotte-santerre, digging in just on the other side of Lamotte. At 8.30 a.m. the 5th Div came through us & advanced, with the tanks, to a depth of a further 8000 yds taking the village of Bayonvillers. The 3rd & 4th Divisions were operating on our left & Canadians & French on our right. After the 5th Div moved through us we made ourselves comfortable & got some sleep. The following day we moved forward again & at 4.20 p.m. we "hopped-off" from the 5th Div objective advanced 3000 yds, took Franerville & dug in 300 yds on the other side of it. 
 The following day the 10th our own heavy howitzers shelled us for an hour & a half before we could stop them. 
 The following morning the 19th & 20th Bns moved through our position & advanced another 2000 yards. We were then relieved by the 6th Bgde & came back up a tench in rear of Franerville. We stayed there for xis-days & were then relieved by "Tommies" & came back per motor to a place called Fouilloy near Corbie.
