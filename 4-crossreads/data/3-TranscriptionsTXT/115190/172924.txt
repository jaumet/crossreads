Koniak.  His luggage consists of a nose nose bag containing his wardrobe etc & a roll of blankets. He talks from monopolizes the conversation from Zahle to Beyroute where we arrive at 4.30 pm.  A few minutes afterwards I see him at the entrance of the Hospital sitting on his blanket roll with a dozen Askaris round him still going strong.  In his hands is the Koniak bottle which still contains about one nip.  We are at the 32nd CCH, a large building originally a barracks.  It is crowded with British Australian & Indian Askaris.  I sleep little that night for the Indians make a fearful row Coughing & Groaning & singing.
2nd Novr  Still at the 32nd CCH & I Have a look around the City.
