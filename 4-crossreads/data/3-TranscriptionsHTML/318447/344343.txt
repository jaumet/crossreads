<p>Dec 31st 1917<br/>Esdud</p>
<p>We stayed that night at Yebnah &amp; reached here late in the afternoon of the following day not without much agony &amp; bloody sweat on the part of the horses. Here we have quite a nice little camp inside a sort of paddock bound on three sides by a prickly pear hedge &amp; on the fourth by the railway &amp; altogether encloses about an acre, so it makes a nice tight little camp.
