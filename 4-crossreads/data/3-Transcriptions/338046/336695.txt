<p>1918<br/>Apl<br/>preference in the valleys leaving the hills entirely for cultivation. If marching up a valley, one encounters a string of villages but if on the hills only fields. The fields are not fenced in, the plots being divided only by marking stones at the corners. I noticed that each owner usually ploughs in the opposite direction to his neighbour. When seen from a distance, this enhances the chessboard appearance. Each house together with its outhouses (in the village) is built in the form of a hollow square, no garden in front but a garden at the back. The houses are built of wattle and daub, whitewashed over and have tiled roofs. Big entrance gates leading off the street, made usually of iron.<br/>[plan of village houses]<br/>This is a typical village street.
