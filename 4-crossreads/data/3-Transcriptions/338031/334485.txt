<p>three good meals a day we could store bread in the hut to make toast on the stove at night which with dripping &amp; an occasional onion made a very palatable supper. Moreover we could clear out of camp from 5 o'clock till 10 at night, so that we could club together &amp; take a motor to Wilton or Salisbury &amp; revel in the delight of ham &amp; eggs or an expensive fillet of steak. Half on to pay day the motoring would be retrenched &amp; the heroes would saunter to the village of Barford-St-Martin &amp; get in front of a great fireplace such as that described in Barnaby Rudge as the old Maypole Inn.
