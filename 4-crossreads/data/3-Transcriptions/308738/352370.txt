<p>Jan. 5 Tuesday 1915<br/>Another horse died<br/>Sea smooth, very hot<br/>Trooper died and buried from "Samata"</p>
<p>Jan. 6 Wednesday 1915<br/>German merchants"gun boats" in the vicinity.<br/>Firing during night<br/>Weather still sultry &amp; hot.</p>
<p>Jan. 7 Thursday 1915<br/>Distance 60 miles passed Cocos Island early 7 AM<br/>2 horse died<br/>a little rain fell, but no apparent difference in temperature.<br/>Fleet doing a little "manuvring during afternoon,   1 hour's delay<br/>expecting to reach Colombo next Thursday morning.</p>
<p>Jan. 8 Friday 1915<br/>Horse died making 13 to date.</p>
<p>Heavy clouds rising very sultry<br/>Fleet stopped 2 PM. "Servic"approached flagship &amp; sent off a boat.<br/>"Ceramic"did likewise, within &frac12; hour both boats returned to their respective ships.<br/>Signal full steam ahead.</p>
