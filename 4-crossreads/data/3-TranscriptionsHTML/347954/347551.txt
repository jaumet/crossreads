I suppose the depth of these gullies are at least 15 feet, possibly 20 feet, &amp; are screened from aerial observation by top covering.  The men firing are above the bottom of the trench &amp; rest on little niches attached to the wall.  At present the trenches are almost impossible owing to stench &amp; lime is freely sprinkled with a view to lessening the smell without effect though.</p>
<p>The Gallipoli during the present weeks has sprung from a mountain hamlet into a very large city.  I think at present we must have over a hundred thousand men in &amp; around the hillsides, so the Turk over distant brow aught to be severely alarmed.  I don't know what quality of fighter the newcomer is &ndash; certainly, according to report, he is going to give such a stir up the Turks will sue for peace almost immediate.  The weather conditions remain ideal &amp; we have nothing worse than a camp full of sick men to worry us.
