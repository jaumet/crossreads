<p>2.<br/>There he sees, as though he were gazing into a framed picture, a scene that an Artist might overlook as being barren of pleasing features but which to him is beautiful enough and full of interest.<br/>The sky is a pale blue with not so much as a whiff of cloud in sight. The air is vitalized with a light breeze that suggests the wafting of silver wings. The sunlight is a soft flood of gold.<br/>The foreground of the scene is a meadow of vivid color as though a mighty brush laden with green and gold from the pallet of Spring had been dashed across from side to side and had been carried with a broad sweep to the middle distance to strike against the darker green of a hawthorn hedge with the effect of a rich carpet.<br/>Through many grey days</p>
