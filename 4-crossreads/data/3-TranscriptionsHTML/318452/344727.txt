Looking away across these slopes it seems for miles the ground is all shell marked as if some gigantic disease has left its scars on the face of nature.<br/>Nature has done her best to cover the confusion The acres of shell ploughed fields saturated with human blood have been covered with a coat of green grass &amp; out of the shed blood of men the red flowers seem to have come The crimson poppies fringe the shell holes &amp; grow up through the tangled wires Blue corn flowers &amp; white marigolds weave the old Tricolour of Britain over the gory field of victory in melancholy liveliness<br/>The soldiers life is beyond the imagination of the outside.
