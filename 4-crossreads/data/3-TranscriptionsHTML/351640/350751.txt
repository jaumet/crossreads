<p>In Military matters the opposite appears to be the case.  If an officer or a man is alone killed sympathy and assistance flow forth in plenty.  If he be one amongst many, every one remarks 'Oh, 'tis the fortune of war', and individuals are lost midst the crowd.  Think you this to be true.</p>
<p>Still is my existence being lived here with pen ink books journals and a little surgical work.  Looking at the sun, the dessert, the pyramids, the eucalyptus trees with new shoots young leaves and fresh ripening fruit, the fruit of the mulberry tree which is just ripening, and other things which you know by now, from my letters, are in Egypt.  For the present good bye<br/>[A line of Xs and Os.] Car. [A line of Xs and Os.] Joseph. [A line of Xs and Os.] Kitty.</p>
