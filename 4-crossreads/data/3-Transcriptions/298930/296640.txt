We have often been glad to eat birds killed through concussion of English shells.</p>
<p>One day I was taken to work near a German Aerodrome, and was within twelve yards of a Taube machine when the engines <strike>thre</strike> were set going while waiting for the driver.    Seeing nobody near, I was trying to judge how the thing worked, and my heart started to throb as I really thought for a moment I might be able to escape, but not knowing enough about machinery, I had to give up the idea.</p>
<p>One afternoon I was taken to clean out some offices in the prison after a heavy day's work, and while doing this I came on a big French Flag, and the German in charge of me thought he was doing a noble thing by snatching it from me and tearing it into shreds.    With all our hardships, at times while working near the fighting line we used to witness some very good sport between the British and German planes, as our pilots brought down German balloons and planes, and whenever the German planes were being defeated the guards in charge of us would not allow us to stop work to witness the battle, but would yelp <strike>and</strike> at us as we would scold a bad dog.</p>
