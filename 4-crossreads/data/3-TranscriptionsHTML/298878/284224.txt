<p>may this place be termed the fastest coaling station in the world, for by 9am our full load of 1200 tons was aboard; hundreds of natives were employed who ran up along a plank from the barge below, each carrying a basket of coal on his head, tipped the basket into the bunker, and ran down another plank for a fresh load, thus making a continuous string of natives travelling the whole time:  They seemed to delight in noise for the air was pierced with their yells, which they made to break the monotony.  We were quite close to the Indian marine ship "Hardinge" which was resplendent in a new funnel the original one having been blown away by the Turkish guns in the Canaal engagement; the shot (which was said to have been the only good shot by the Turks) besides damaging the funnel killed</p>
