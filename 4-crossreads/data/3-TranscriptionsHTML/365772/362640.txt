<p>where the bulk of the convalescents go. This is the last stage for wounded the ones fit for duty go to camp on the sand in Cairo &amp; the unfit go to Australia or England, we have about 300 men here now to go back 250 went away yesterday to Australia, some are very crook cases:- arms off a few cases of blindness fingers off &amp; bad legs.<br/>I dont know what they are going to do to me yet they sent a lot back to Aust who were not as badly wounded as me for 6 months furlough, anyway I dont care as long as the leg gets OK I wouldnt like to be permanently lame. I am getting good massage treatment here which is doing me good.<br/>Thank Ralph for me for his long letter, the songs &amp; Bulletin havn't turned up yet, his advice about keeping your head down was driven into to me forcibly if I hadn't been on my feet I wouldn't have</p>
