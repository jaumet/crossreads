<p>&amp; immediately boarded the train for London. These trains can travell, we must have travelled  fully thirty miles an hour. Arrived at Waterloo Station at 12.30 p.m. &amp; there was all buz &amp; excitement round this quarter, all the Australians fell in, &amp; marched to Horseferry Road (A.I.F.) Hdqrtes), &amp; you should have seen the way we marched, Shoulders back, head up etc- to make a good impression you know. I was from 1 oclock till 6 before I was at least free from this military business, &amp; what a relief it was to be once again a free man.  I immediately made a dive for the nearest shop &amp; obtained new underclothing, then on to a bus &amp; eventually arrived at the Shaftsbury Hotel near Trafalgar Square at 6.30. Here I obtained a hot bath &amp; had every comfort once again. I felt a new man. I then swanked down the Strand, &amp; Picadilly you would have thought I owned the dam place. In the evening I went to the Lyceum Theatre, "seven days leave was the play", it was a very good &amp; worth seeing, there were plenty of nasty digs for those not in kharki. Enjoyed a good supper at the hotel, &amp; then arrives something worth going to England for <u>a good clean comfortable bed</u>, I slept like a top till 10.a.m. the following</p>
