 24th  A  The companies were paraded under their respective company commanders at 10 A.M. and the men told to move off to their respective battalions but not a man budged an inch.  They refused to recognise any other Bn for themselves  f  but the 54th.  The C.O. therefore called a muster parade and told the men that their efforts to reform the Bn. would be fruitless and asked representatives from each company to  come  go out to him and state their reasons for not obeying orders.
