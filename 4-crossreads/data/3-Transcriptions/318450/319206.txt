<p>We were all lined up for the inspection, and we were told to look to what ever flank he rode past and give three cheers, but that was no good we wanted to see him properly, and so there was a shout and a charge &amp; all broke loose.  I do not know what he thought of the boys, but I will bet he told his father plenty of tales about the Horsetralians.  Anyway we got down to Alexandria in good time for dinner, and as we had to wait on the wharfe for a few hours before the transport came in, we had to make the best of bully beef and biscuits.  The transport pulled in to the wharfe some time in the afternoon, and after a little fooling about</p>
