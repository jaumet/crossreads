<p>as a soldier in Morroco &amp; Madagascar and later went to the New Hebrides where he began life as a planter.  Unable to settle however he visited Australia and was attracted by the Goldfields.  What success attended his gold prospecting I have not so far been able to ascertain.  He spent most of his prospecting days in the West of New South Wales.  One day <strike>about</strike> early in 1916 Bill found himself in Dubbo and saw the soldiers being trained and heard the recruiting officials appealing for more men.  Bill's long-fostered hate of the Bosche <strike>bec</strike> burst into flame and he enlisted.</p>
