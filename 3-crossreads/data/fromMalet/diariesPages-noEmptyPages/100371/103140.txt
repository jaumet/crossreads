had dinner (Soup) on board train. We arrived at Waterloo at about 1.30PM was put in Ambula. given tea biscuit then driven with lady attendant through London to Richmond. It was a lovely trip Hyde Park looked very well, as well as Hammersmith bridge. The residences in the suburbs were very pretty with their gardens. Pink May, Pink and White Chestnuts were a blaze of blossoms. Arrived at Richmond at 3 PM. put in E ward not allowed to sit up. The Hospital is a lovely place nice ground etc. Nearly all E wards patients are able to get up, they do all the orderlies work they being Tommies Australians.
The staff are Australians, the sisters are different to the English Sisters at Rouen they appear to have different ways, a couple of these Aust. Sisters are hard cases. Monday 4th June lovely day, plenty of food barring dinner time when I didn't have near enough. Tuesday dull morning clear afternoon. Wednesday fine. Thursday fine. News of Battle of Messines Ridge. Friday fine Saturday fine. Sunday 10th June about a dozen little girls visited us under a Lady a Padre sang hyms. Monday fine. Tuesday fine, had a bath my toe
