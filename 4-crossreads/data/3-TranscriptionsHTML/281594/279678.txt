<p>in the hope that the cloud may pass over and they will then reap the benefit of their faith.<br/>The people of St Omer seem to have left the town. They are mostly Military people who occupy the town.</p>
<p>We are at present living under ideal conditions</p>
<p>Feeling a little bit sick to-day due to having been inoculated  O.T. got their photo taken in gowns.</p>
<p>June 14th.<br/>Went to Norpeene on cycle along with B. Weather good have got sick. Slight touch of Influenza. This we term the "Dog's disease." Quite an epidemic has broken out amongst the troops. This disease seems to knock one out completely for three days, making one very week and very miserable</p>
<p>June 20th.<br/>Two Operating teams from No 17 B.C.C.S. have been attached here for duty. I met these chaps before when I was on team duty up at Remy Siding so that is was like old pals meeting. The station at Remy has been bombed. We are being kept very busy many wounded are being brought in</p>
<p>The district around here has been made a very net work of trenches. This place lends itself to fortification and should Fritz endeavour to break through again he is in</p>
