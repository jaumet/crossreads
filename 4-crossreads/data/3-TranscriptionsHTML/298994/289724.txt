<p>visit There was an excellent divisional concert in progress in the theatre here. Loere is <strike>a</strike> characteristic of all types of French or Belgian towns.</p>
<p>I went to a <strike>Ch</strike> cinema show tonight 29th Feb 1918, I saw Charles Chaplin n his famous picture "In a Bank", also saw Sir Herbert Tree in "Old folks at home".</p>
<p>The weather is still very cold, we had a slight fall of snow today (March 2nd 1918) (March 3rd 1918) is still very cold, we marched to Loere this morning, it was a Brigade affair, one platoon from each company of each battalion of the 13th Brigade being represented. General Birdwood was present, he presented the medals won by the brigade He made a fine speech, this affair was the forerunner to a church service.</p>
<p>We are hearing very heavy bombardments on the Ypres salient</p>
<p>I walked to Dranoutre tonight (7th March 1918)</p>
<p>On Saturday (9/3/18) A Coy of the 50th Battalion left this camp, we proceeded to Lindenhock, about 4 miles distant</p>
