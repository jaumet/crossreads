<p>Feb 14</p>
<p>Usual full day's drill in mud &amp; ice. Evening in Y.M.C.A. Hut, where we heard a very stirring address on the grandeur of a pure life by an Anglican Canon.</p>
<p>Feb 15</p>
<p>Usual day's work.</p>
<p>Evening in Regimental writing room, where there is one of the best stoves in camp.</p>
<p>Feb 16</p>
<p>Usual work, especially musketry exercises. Throat very sore all day as a result of a cold caught while on leave.</p>
<p>Evening in Regimental Writing Room.</p>
<p>Feb 17</p>
<p>Route march with full pack &amp; rifle this morning. The mud on the roads was simply "out of bounds" &amp; we arrived back at camp with boots &amp; puttees pasted over with the stuff &amp; our overcoat tails were a disgrace to Australian dignity. However we are so used to it now that we paddle through it like young children &amp; quite enjoy the fun. Talk about wading through blood &amp; tears to</p>
