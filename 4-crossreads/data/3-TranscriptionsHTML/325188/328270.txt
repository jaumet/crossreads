<p>1918<br/>March 11th<br/>Raining still &ndash; A cake arrived from London, is'nt it wonderful of the Army postal Service &ndash; Turks being driven back &amp; we hope to be relieved tomorrow &amp; enemy Turks over but no bombing. A mountain Battery got a shaking yesterday &amp; [indecipherable] men have been passing us all day &ndash; We can see _ behind us quite plainly &ndash; it is built on a hill &amp; we expect to be back that way again soon &ndash; .<br/>Not withstanding the cold I am fit &amp; well<br/>12th<br/>Our outpost in a Cave &ndash; raining all day &ndash; Can see Jacko's fired in front &ndash; he is jumpy &amp; fires flares &amp; bombs at intervals &ndash; We have been successful in our Stand here as the papers will tell you<br/>13th<br/>Still drizzling, we have not had time to bury the dead Turks yet; this hill is littered<br/>With debris of the fight &ndash; Expect to go back to __  for a day's spell very cold &amp; rough on the horses up in the hills<br/>14th<br/>The cave I camped in had been used by Bedouins &ndash; I am therefore lousy not had my clothes off for a week</p>
