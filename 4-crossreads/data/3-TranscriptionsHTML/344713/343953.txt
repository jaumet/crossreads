<p>Sat. 5th. May-<br/>"Fritz" counter-attacked this morning &amp; we moved up to line but he was shoved back again on the right &amp; our fellows made an advance of 200 yds while the time was ripe.<br/>Of course there were plenty of casualties on account of the artillery fire but I really have never seen so many "walking cases".<br/>Went into front line again this afternoon relieving 1st Bn. on left of road.<br/>Communication saps in an awful state with old dead.<br/>Just missed getting blown up in a dugout by a direct hit; lost all equipment bar telephone. I am really pleased I <underline>did<underline>did take a stroll after some breakfast.</p>
<p>Mon. May. 7th: -<br/>Relieved tonight by 5th Bn. &amp; went to sunken road outside of Noreuil &amp; this is the finish of our "gift" stunt in front of Reincourt. The BN. had about 250 Casualties but only about 50 killed.<br/>S.Bearer Moss, Corp. Anderson. Bob Rutherford, Heuston, Murdoch &amp; Butler were killed.<br/>Kendrigan, Bob O'Keefe &amp; McMillan wounded.<br/>Signallers Joe Wakeley, Tom Bladen &amp; Mulholland killed. Burton &amp; Campey  wounded.</p>
