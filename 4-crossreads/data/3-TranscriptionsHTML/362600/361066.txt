<p>January 1 1918<br/>X1017 letters to here</p>
<p>[Letters preceding diary dates refer to weather conditions, i.e. F; W. In certain cases weather conditions are written in full; i.e. 'Dull', 'Ice'.]<br/>1 Tue<br/>Ice<br/>Recd.   E. is O. Sgt. 3 letters from Home. Dulce. Ida. Mrs Horner Bess (2) Mrs Winess. Dulce card. Lottie card Ida card &amp; photos. Get ready for I. of Man</p>
<p>Mrs Edmond &amp; Connie Wrote Dad. Mum. George. Sent cards &amp; Photos.</p>
<p>F 2 Wed am<br/>London. 3.45 pm. 5.30 to L'pool. Met WAAC in Bulford train   &amp; Go to dinner with Her. Travel with Lady who has lost her Hubby. Stay at YMCA in L'pool. New Zealander who coughs all night. L'pool very cold.</p>
<p>Sent t.gram to auntie Annie.<br/><br/><br/>F 3 Thu<br/>Lovely day. Catch 11.30 am boat See Leviathon also Faterland at L'pool. Nice trip travel with horses &amp; chap from Melbourne. Meet Qarllion on quay. Arr Aunties'  place at 6.30. No one at home. All arr 8.30 pm. Everything very good. I [indecipherable] as a stone image - [indecipherable]<br/>W 4 Fri S<br/>Just sit about &amp; generally get in the [indecipherable] morning. Music &amp;  fireside all afternoon in Drawing Room Auntie &amp; M have to help undress me. We do not chat together much &amp; nothing marvellous happens.</p>
<p>Dull 5 Sat<br/>Sit  &amp;  read [indecipherable] in<br/>Dining Room all afternoon &amp; night. Auntie Esm. again help undress me We do not chat together  &amp; nothing marvellous happens<br/>I try &amp; alter my Braches but makes no real success. We all yarn together &amp;  nothing happens interesting at all.</p>
