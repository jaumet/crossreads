<p>of Destroyers which were steaming along on patrol work going Northward.  As there are no lights along the North Sea Coast now, &amp; it being absolutely essential to keep in the channel marked by buoys, our anchor had to be dropped when night descended.  The pilot took us in close against a shoal so that a sub could not come anywhere near us.  In this position we should be almost as safe as in port.  We are about 2 miles North of Gainsbrough.  Though several towns can be seen near the shore not a light is visible anywhere.  As soon as the sun went down the atmosphere became rather thick[?] making bed a desirable place, so I turned in soon after I finished my watch.</p>
