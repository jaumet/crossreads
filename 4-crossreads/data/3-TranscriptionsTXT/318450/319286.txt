 motors and driven to one of the big stations, I think it was Flinders St, we were put into our carraige about 4 in each and when it was full up off we went with a huge cheer from right along the train. We had a fine journey up to Aulbury which is the border station, and I may say the place all along was under flood. When we got to Albury we had to change trains, but before doing so we had some tea provided for us on the station. We were put in sleeping berth's oh so lovely and all along the line we had visits from people who loaded us up with coffee and all sorts of refreshments in fact we got that much we could not eat it all.
