<p>Wednesday 23rd Feb 1916</p>
<p>Usual routine day.  Taking aspirin.</p>
<p>Thursday 24th Feb 1916</p>
<p>Quiet &amp; uneventful day.  Much improved.</p>
<p>Friday 25th Feb. 1916</p>
<p>Feeling generally better.</p>
<p>Saturday 26th Feb 1916</p>
<p>Still no news.  No letters at Zeitoun &amp; no ack. from Sgt. Wakelin.  I'm thoroughly disheartened.  But this absence of letters seems to be a general complaint for officers as well as men are suffering in this respect.</p>
<p>Sunday 27th Feb 1916</p>
<p>Quiet day.  Church Parade with "domain orator" parson.  "It is very difficult for a rich man to enter the Kingdom of heaven."</p>
<p>Monday 28th Feb 1916</p>
<p>Paid 10/-.  Went to Cairo with Woods, 19th M. Gunner.</p>
<p>Tuesday 29th Feb 1916</p>
<p>In town.  Slept at Ghezueh Palace Hospital.</p>
<p>Wednesday 1st Mch 1916</p>
<p>Thursday 2nd Mch 1916</p>
<p>Friday 3rd Mch 1916</p>
<p>Saturday 4th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Hot &amp; sultry weather.  "Khamsheen" conditions &ndash; dusty all day.  Have been classed "L.O.C." which means no further "active service".</p>
<p>Rumours of complete evacuation of troops on Canal, either to France, Mesopotamia or Bombay.</p>
<p>There must be some big movement in embryo for most of the hospitals are being cleared as much as possible of their patients, &amp; the men if fit discharged to the Base.</p>
<p>Sunday 5th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Big crowd of men to leave tomorrow for Ismailia &ndash; in fact &ndash; all except L.O.C. &amp; "A" Special classes.  Something is in the wind.</p>
