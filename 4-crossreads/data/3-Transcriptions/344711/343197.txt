<p>24th<br/>On observation.  An armistice was declared between 9 a.m. &amp; 4 p.m., to bury the dead.  The enemy and we were sitting on our respective parapets watching each other.</p>
<p>25th<br/>H.M.S. Triumph torpedoed &amp; sunk.  Could see her plainly from the trenches.  Sgt. Parkes, of "B", killed.</p>
<p>26th<br/>Observation &ndash; Quiet.</p>
<p>27th<br/>On fatigue.  Observation at night.</p>
<p>28th<br/>Observation.</p>
<p>29th<br/>Observation.  H.S. Brown, of our troop, killed by shell.</p>
<p>30th<br/>Observation.</p>
<p>31st<br/>Observation in day, ammunition at night.  Quiet.</p>
<p>June<br/>1st<br/>Ammunition day, observation night.</p>
<p>2nd<br/>Fatigue day, observation night.</p>
<p>3rd<br/>Observation.  King's Birthday.</p>
