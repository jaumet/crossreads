<p>Tuesday July 9. 1918. 1435.<br/>A fine day hot but cloudy. Revellie was at 7.30 instead of 6.45.<br/>This afternoon there were light showers &amp; again at night</p>
<p>Wednesday July 10. 1918. 1436.</p>
<p>A fine morning. A nice bright sun shining. We expect to move further up to the line at any time, in all probability tomorrow and to Corby or the vicinity. It was very windy and stormy for a while this afternoon with some heavy showers but the wind fell before 5 o'clock.</p>
<p>Ever since we have been at this camp we have been able to get the "Daily Mail" at an adjoining Y.M.C.A. every morning about 11. They sell it for 2d. which is 1d. less than the French girls charge for it.<br/>Late this evening orders came through from H.Q. that we are to move out early in the morning. Revellie is at 3.30 a.m. and the cooker drivers have to leave earlier than that in order to move off with the Battalion. The Battalion have breakfast on the road.</p>
<p>Thursday July 11. 1918. 1437.</p>
