<p>August 10th  To-day I am 21 &amp; have had a small turn out, eggs etc champagne.</p>
<p>August 11th  Move to Wargnies.  This evening I visited the Sousterrain at Naours.  These caves were built as a refuge from invasions as far back as 850AD &amp; can accommodate 300 families, from the surrounding districts (see copy of notice)</p>
<p>August 12th  Move to Herrisart a few miles further on where the Ambulance has congregated.</p>
<p>August 14th  Move to Brickfield Hill, overlooking Albert.  The march was about twelve miles &amp; no sooner had we laid our beds in the paddock than Fritz sent over about six shells fortunately doing damage [sic]</p>
<p>August 15th  Bearers A.B &amp; C march off to Becourt where we leave our packs &amp; then move off to various stations.  We are detailed for Pozieres &amp; the right where 6th &amp; 5th Batts are in.  Our carries are heavy especially the 1st which is through a narrow sap.  In the afternoon Harry Rogers came through wounded but it is only a flesh wound in the leg.</p>
<p>August 16th  This morning also a German plane was brought down in flames by one of our machines.  In fact there were two.</p>
<p>August 17th  Retire to C. Corner where we load wagons for 24 hours. (spell.</p>
<p>August 18th  Again at 5th Aid post where to-day the shelling is very severe.  This evening our relief was caught in a dugout, four being killed viz. Cunningham, Lehane, Elliot &amp; Smith.  Also one wounded at Pozieres.</p>
<p>August 19th  Back again at Casualty C. but we were called back to the Aid post as reserve for the 8th Batt's attack.</p>
<p>August 20th  Three attempts were made to take the German trench after a terrific bombardment  at 9 pm last night, but without success.  However they dug in in front of their objective with not too many casualties</p>
<p>August 21st [?]  22 nd  <strike>Again at CC for a spell</strike>  <br/>Yesterday &amp; to-day we have been on the Pozieres carry but not much to do until this morning after an attack on the left.  At 11 am. the Ambulance was relieved &amp; we move back to Brickfield.</p>
<p>August 22nd [23rd?]  March from Brickfield to Warloy.  One night lately a shell burst on C Corner &amp; wounded Capt Graham slightly.  We have since heard that he has died of gas gangrene.</p>
