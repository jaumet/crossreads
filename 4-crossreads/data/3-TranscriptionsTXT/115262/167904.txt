 Thursday 17 May 1917. 
 Still at sea and we are of the opinion that we are just now in &quot;bight&quot;&nbsp;&nbsp;judging by the very rough weather we have run into 
 The roughest day yet of the voyage,&nbsp;since leaving Melbourne. 
 1 st&nbsp; .Cl &nbsp; .&nbsp; Bgde&nbsp; &nbsp;Fd Amb. does guard. &amp; police duties on board. 
 Medical inspection in afternoon 
 A very rough night. Sat on upper deck till 9 p.m. &amp; then down below. 
 Have veered&nbsp; slightly &nbsp;in a Northerly direction but are stll about 500 miles from land.
