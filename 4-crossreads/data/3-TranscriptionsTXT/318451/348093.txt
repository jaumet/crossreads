
a bright afternoon & the aircraft of both sides have been very busy.During the strafe on Wednesday, Capt C- told me they timed the shells bursting all about his lines & there were 600 in half an hour & 900 in an hour.  That is a fair bombardment for a mans nerves.  The Trenches are nothing but a chain of shell holes, very few were hit.  One man was blown to pieces & a few wounded, but we have had a good many casualties for the week.
Friday 16This morning I went to B.H.Q. in the line & have to go to town again this afternoon for a funeral.  I find we were going out early tomorrow so I am packing up & going out tonight.
