 This may  not  or may not be true but certain it is that several girls have been left in this village in a wretched condition. The hospitality of the people here-abouts was proved first of all when we were on the way from Bertry to Rejet. One of our men fainted and afterwards took a fit. He was resuscitated by Lieut. Dickinson who was in a quandry then, as to how he was to get him to hospital as we were a detached company without medical transport of any description.
