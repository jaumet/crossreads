<p>9.9.1916   Gas alarm last night.  The Artillery fires furiously when this gas is coming over, to try and disperse it.  Drawing remounts today.  We are &frac12; mile from the village of Ouderdom in Belgium.  Tonight I went to Reninghelst about 1 &frac14; miles from here.  The same high price for fruit, Apples 1-30 per lb, grapes 1-50 per lb.</p>
<p>10.9.1916   Very quiet day, Half holiday for the men.  This is the first for months.  Weather fine, getting cold, mornings foggy.  Lieut Little, on leave to England.  Australian Divisions pouring in.  Very few French here, all Belgiums.  They speak Flemish</p>
