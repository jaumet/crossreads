<p>at the coal wharf.  Immediately we arrived a huge hoist was brought alongside &amp; coaling commenced.  The hoist lifted about 6 or 7 tons each trip.  Though the method is fast it is very dirty.  An Indian hawker succeeded in selling me a deal of fancy goods, though, of course I only gave him about half the price he asked for everything.  After dinner Purser 2nd &amp; 3rd Mates &amp; self walked around to a whaling station which is built facing the sea.  The head of one huge specimen was being dissected while we were there.  I had no idea that anything like this size of this mammon ever existed.</p>
