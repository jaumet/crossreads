We got out on the tape line to time at the zero hour (5.30) the machine barrage opened quickly followed by artillery. I shall never forget the sight. Just forward of the tape line the first shells dropped. Sending fountains of burning phosphorous yards into the air followed with bellows of white smoke lighting up the country with a weird light. Into this advanced the infantry, drenched by this, with not like war weary men as they undoubtedly were but like men going forward on parade.
