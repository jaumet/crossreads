<p>1919<br/>Apl 10<br/>haunts.  A lad I knew who had a roving eye caught one here, &amp; shouted a drink of wine for her.  She invited three of her friends to sit down also.  He had dropped between 60 &ndash; 80 francs before he got away, what with chocolates at 30 francs a box &amp; wine (bad) at 4 francs a glass.  We kept our glad eyes to ourselves.  The revue was a very ordinary one, playing up to the Yanks all the time.  A good amount of the revue was in English, but I suppose half the audience was of the English speaking races.</p>
<p>Apl 11<br/>We went out to Malmaison &ndash; Napoleons residence by tram.  It is a very nice place.  It attracted me a great deal more than Versailles as it seemed more like a home.  Near the entrance hall is Josephines little sitting room with her harp in the centre.  There is a tale to the effect that whenever she entertained, &amp; was asked to play, she always played the same tune.  The same tale asserts that it was the only tune she knew.
