<p>We were in Flers dug-outs for about a month, during which the camp was continuously shelled and shells were passing over us to other objects.</p>
<p>I was mighty glad when the time came for us to start our march &quot;back&quot; for a little &quot;rest&quot;.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>CHAPTER XVIII.</p>
<p><u>WE &quot;GO OUT&quot; FOR A REST&nbsp;</u></p>
<p>It was about 24th. February that we left the dugouts and marched first to Perth camp, then the following day to Becourt camp, remaining there for a few days.</p>
<p>It was here that I was appointed a Lance Corporal, what for I can&#39;t say, but as it might get me out of some unpleasant fatigues, I was glad to receive the promotion. &nbsp;I felt a man of some importance I can tell you, wearing a stripe.</p>
<p>We eventually arrive back at Buire, not a bad town, or perhaps I ought to call it a village.</p>
<p>I with about a dozen others are &quot;billeted&quot; in a large shed or kind of room adjoining a small house. &nbsp;There is no door to this room, except through a room of&nbsp;the house, so as the house is &quot;out of bounds&quot;, we have to climb through the an&nbsp;opening in the wall, which apparently was intended to be used as a window. &nbsp;The floor of this room was of earth, just like the earth outside only</p>
