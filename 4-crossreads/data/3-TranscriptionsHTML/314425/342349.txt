<p>Station where we were met and conducted to the A.I.F. at Horseferry  Road. We had some tea &amp; buns a lecture in Health &amp; Veneral diseases then to the Medical Room where we were questioned about our ailments wounds etc, I elected to go for a week into a convalesent camp so that my leave would extend over Christmas. I am to report at the A.I.F office at 10 o'clock tomorrow. Got some of my clothes exchanged and am staying at the War Chest Club tonight, where I</p>
<p>will have tea and do some writing.<br/>[shorthand symbols] I wrote to my wife tonight Letter 150  2 pages</p>
<p>Saturday Dec. 7. 1918. 27.<br/>At 10 o'clock this morning I reported at Headquarters. Previous to that I went to Church House and saw Fred Prichard and he paid me 10/- that he owed me since last January.<br/>I saw Harry Wight at Breakfast this morning. He is looking well and expects to be sent back soon. He told me that the new Pioneer Sargeant Charlie Handcock died of</p>
