  
 September, 1917. Thursday 13 Fine day. Up at 1.30 and off on pontoons at 2 through Ypres. Very lively and is shelled continuously A motor lorry was caught out of a convoy and set alight & he shelled heavily and we had to go round. Used incendiary shells with red glare showing up everything. On to Menin Road and to job. 
 I was laying bearers. Fair job. Shelled a dump and fired cordite and shelled continuously through which our drivers had to go. Home all night although lively all round. Planes were over at daylight low and kept us busy. Breakfast & slept a little in aftn. tea at 6 and bed 7.30 till next morning. Home 9.30. 3 horses wounded.
