<p>sad and always anxious, but we shall never think of you without pride. I have no fear that you will do anything to change that, or to bring discredit on the uniform you wear. You started your military career in too good a school ever to be likely to forget the obligations that your commission imposes on you. I know you will never think of the rank of officer as being merely a privilege, except in so far as it is a privilege to be entrusted with a solemn duty towards the men under you. They have the right to expect of you not only unceasing attention to their care &amp; comfort, but also the highest standard you can</p>
