<p>March 9, 1916 Thursday</p>
<p>Hopped out at 5.30</p>
<p>Scrubbed my horse this morning &amp; was Orderly Corporal so had to look after the defaulters.</p>
<p>The last aeroplane left us this morning &amp; are now established at Assuit.</p>
<p>Weather is getting frightfully warm &amp; no money to ease our thirst although Burns has gone for the Head Qrs.</p>
<p>Went down the town after tea with Perce Bates had a feed then came home at ten &amp; turned in.</p>
<p>March 10, 1916 Friday</p>
<p>Revielle at 5.30</p>
<p>Started to break camp at 9 all the last of the aerodrome stuff was taken down to the station, ours was taken during the afternoon &amp; evening &amp; as we had been paid early in the day the drivers had no difficulty in getting drunk in fact nearly all the troop were sprokked.</p>
<p>I was baggage guard &amp; had to put the night in on the station where what with mosquitos &amp; lice I had quite a lively time.</p>
<p>Went up the town before turning in for supper &amp; had a splendid time &amp; feed. Said goodbye to Kamel who is thinking of coming down to Assuit with us.</p>
<p>Bed at 9.30.</p>
