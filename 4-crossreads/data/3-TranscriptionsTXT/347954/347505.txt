 The Artillery had a night too &ndash; limbering guns practically the whole night &amp; preparing gun pits.  One poor fellow lost his life early in the morning through a fall of earth in one of the pits, &amp; an officer was shot through the forearm.  The night was awfully cold &amp; to me trying to get warm doing nothing the morning was a positive relief.  The weather remains perfect &ndash; hot days with cool nights &amp; no pest in the shape of mosquitoes.  We are more or less bothered with other vermin though, which causes a good deal of unnecessary words, also a large amount of scratching.  Grovelling in the dirt, &amp; mud stains also go unheeded, &amp; as regards shaving, well most of the boys in our Corp are cultivating both beard &amp; moustache.  My own experience at shaving was very painful &amp; is not likely to be repeated often.  With a fortnights growth of stubble to practice on, an old safety razor with blades used several dozen times &amp; a mirror &ndash; an inch in dimensions &ndash; I started in to make facial improvements with dire results to the face.
