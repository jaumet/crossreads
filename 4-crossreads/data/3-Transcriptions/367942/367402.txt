<p>He expected us the previous day &amp; had his seaplane up 3 times the last time at 6 p.m. she sighted us &amp; saw how we were steering, They expected to be alongside us at daylight, but as I had altered my course more to allow for set, during the night she found us away to Eastward of her in the morning.<br/>Myself &amp; officers were immediately ordered to proceeded onboard the S.M.S. "Wolf", cruiser &amp; mine layer<br/>Seven 6in guns<br/>Four Torpedo-tubes<br/>Two 3 pounders<br/>Machine guns &amp; rifles ad lib<br/>Also her main stay, <u>the seaplane</u><br/>On boarding the "Wolf" we were all questioned by various officers but thery got no information that would do them any good.<br/>One of the officers had been on the German survey-ship "Cormorun" &amp; knew the New Guinea waters well.They went carefully through all our gear, took all our navigating instruments &amp; books away, also binoculars &amp; cameras, electric torches etc.</p>
