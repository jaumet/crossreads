<p>the men are sorry to learn the position as they had quite made up their minds that they wd winter here &amp; were very comfortable &amp; quite conversant with their work, but a rest wont do them any harm. I hear from my wife the work the Ladies form are doing, which we are all most grateful for. Your son who was ill had I am pleased to say returned to duty &amp; looks very well, &amp; I have recommended him for promotion to Capt. So that fact speaks for itself. I am very proud of my officers all, &amp; my Battn. has so far an excellent reputation. Please give my kindest regards to Mrs Ferguson, &amp; convey my own &amp; the Regts thanks to the Ladies band for their great kindness &amp; good work in the Battn. Interest, &amp; with best wishes for yrself<br/>I am<br/>Dear Sir<br/>Yrs faithfully<br/>J Lawrock Lt Col<br/>OC 20th Battn.</p>
