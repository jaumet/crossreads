<p>What a refreshing tonic it was to me, but how much more to the Infantry, to leave behind the last mud hole &amp; see once again Virgin country &amp; actually green grass &ndash; I went through the Villages of Flers &ndash; Ligny &ndash; Guedecourt &amp; Beaulencourt &ndash; Guedecourt seemed to be principally a great death trap for the Indian Lancers who tried hard to take it, but went down in a heap &ndash; I found one of their swords &amp; gave it to a Dr. At a Dressing Station of Fritzs I found the graves of 4 Australians &ndash; 15th Batt., who must have died in Fritzs charge. Counted 4 of our Planes, which had fell in Fritzs line once. At Beaulencourt I got a few nice souvenirs &ndash; The Church is badly Knocked about &ndash; yet strange to say a few stations of the Cross &amp; statues are still intact &amp; almost seem to say why this sacriledge.</p>
