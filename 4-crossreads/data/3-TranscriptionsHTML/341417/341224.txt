<p>My brother is in the Light Horse (dismounted) here. He is about 5 miles from me &amp; at present he is safe &amp; well. Dr. Beeston was made Assistant Director of Medical Service but is now away "out on grass".</p>
<p>Its wonderful, the way apparently healthy men fall away after being here a while.</p>
<p>Not that the food is scanty or un-nutrious. No, not by any means.</p>
<p>We are better sheltered, clothed &amp; fed than the troops were in the Sth Africa War &amp; no forced marching to do, over extensive territory. Here we have more risks.</p>
<p>I visit friends at different parts of our line &amp; so obtain a good idea of the whole situation.</p>
<p>I visited Capt. E. Harnett, whose father is Sec of Pansy Lodge, a few days ago, on Quinns Post. There, the trenches are 10 or 12 yds apart. The Turks, probably in honour of my visit, started bombing &amp; being close, I was covered in dirt from one jam-tin "cracker" that came my way.</p>
