<p>9 August 1915<br/>Monday</p>
<p>4 am Sentry wakes us up to tell us the spot we are guarding is full of Turks.<br/>We open fire with rifles  &amp;  machine guns  &amp;  apparently drive them out<br/>No more are seen after daybreak but some snipers must have been left for we keep getting bullets over<br/>We lost several men killed  &amp;  some wounded.<br/>Expected relief does not arrive so make arrangements for another night</p>
<p>1915 August 10<br/>Tuesday</p>
<p>About 3 am Sentry in my trench yells out  &amp;  fires which immediately starts the whole line firing<br/>Find out later he has shot one of our own Sergts who had come to the front of our trench in mistake. He is wounded in leg  &amp;  foot. get him into trench   &amp;  bandage wounds. Get relieved at 7 am  &amp;  retire to have a much needed rest after 52 hrs continuous fighting<br/> <u>971</u>  still hotly contested 29th Division nearly killed but<br/>About 4 pm the Enemy attack</p>
