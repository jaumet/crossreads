<p>6<br/>It is a selfish wish, and now a useless one; but it comes to me nevertheless. &ndash;</p>
<p>That I might have had more leisure and more time to record the much which I have seen in a little &ndash; that is the wish.</p>
<p>To see the gold of vivid and cruel romance and real human tragedy spilling<br/>From the tilted post of memory and only the dross of incident remain, hastens a desire for leisure. Hastens again that wish.</p>
<p>Yet I know it is selfish, and if the time and opportunities so seemingly lost be but the sacrifice for something nobler, I must be and am content.</p>
