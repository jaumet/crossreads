<p>Report of 1st Squadron A.F.C.</p>
<p>to 5TH March 1919.</p>
<p>The attached congratulatory messages were addressed to C.O. 40th Wing R.F.A. of which this unit then formed a part &ndash; they concern work done by 1st Squadron, A.F.C., and were sent by C.O. 40th Wing R.A.F.</p>
<p>R.F.C.<br/>23.2.1918.<br/> "The whole of the photography referred to in the above communication was done by 1st Squadron A.F.C."<br/>2. At the conclusion of his inspection of the Squadron on 7th March, 1918, Major General W.H.G. Salmond, D.S.O., said:-<br/>"I desire to convey to the Officers and men of the Squadron my warm congratulations upon the splendid work they have done in the past, and are still doing. Their work generally is magnificent, and I feel that I cannot compliment them too highly upon it. The Officers, N.C.O's and men at all times display conspicuous ability in the discharge of their respective duties, with the result that they have rendered service of incalculable value throughout the operations on this front."</p>
<p>3. SPECIAL ORDER OF THE DAY:<br/>H.R.H. The Duke of Connaught personally commanded the G.O.C. to convey to all concerned his regret that the weather conditions had prevented the parade at which H.R.H. had intended to present medals. H.R.H. expressed his admiration for the magnificent work which has been done on this front by the R.F.C. and A.F.C. in spite of the difficulties of the country, climate and novel conditions.<br/>(Signed R.O. No. 76 dated 29 March 1918)</p>
