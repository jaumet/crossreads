<p>Kit &amp; proceeded on to the Station with another Pall &amp; at 2.30 the Leave Train arrived, which consisted of a large number of Covered Vans with seats &amp; a stove in the Centre.   After walking along the platform for a Van my friend &amp; myself came across some Australian Boys &amp; naturally we joined them.   At 2.40 Pm the Train pulled out &amp; proceeded at a slow pace stopping at <strike>several</strike> two places enroute namely Les Foutinettes &amp; Watteu &amp; eventually arrived at Bologne which was the Port we had to embark from.   At 6.45 Pm, after all hands had been lined up outside, &amp; formed into four Deep, we started on our way to the Rest Camp.  There was no mistake we all were a mixed lot, Lads from a large number of Regiments &amp; in all sorts of condition some no doubt had just come from the Trenches by the appearance, two lads were wearing their steel Helmets &amp; their clothes were all torn, anyhow they were all in good cheer, so their condition did not worry them in the least.   There were about 500 men in our Party &amp; after a steady walk through several winding streets up a steep Hill we arrived at the Rest Camp No 2 at 7.15 Pm, &amp; as we went through the Gates we were handed two tickets each, one was for our Dinner &amp; the other for our Breakfast.    We continued on until we arrived between a row of Tents &amp; the N.C.O. in charge allotted eight</p>
