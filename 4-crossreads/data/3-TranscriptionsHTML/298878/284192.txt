<p>four ships frantically cheered each other.  Our boat returned with 4 casks of salts and a bundle of newspapers.  The newspapers were soon distrusted among the officers, and a great time had not elapsed before ordinary, every day topics were being bandied about.  The latest papers were those giving an account of Germanys threat to blow up all shipping approaching England; as we have a cargo for England this appears very unpromising, but everyone aboard laughs the idea to scorn saying the British Navy will soon blow Germanys submarines up.  Their confidence in Englands sea supremacy is implicit.  Let us hope it is not misplaced.
