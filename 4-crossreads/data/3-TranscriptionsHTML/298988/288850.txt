<p>5.30 a.m.  The sun is just rising behind Gaba Tepe where our men landed &amp; with Shrapnel &amp; warship shells bursting all along the ridges of the hills the sight is weirdly beautiful, yet hellish.</p>
<p>Two Aeroplanes are now flying around us &amp; the baloon Ship is about to fly its captive baloon in order to get a knowledge of the Turks positions. There are also numerous Battleships, Destroyers &amp; Submarines here, however "cold feet" has not yet set in. It is now nearly 6 oclock &amp; we are returning to land our Mountain Battery.</p>
<p>8.40 a.m.  Still landing our Troops &amp; the other Ships are doing likewise.  There is one continual stream of small craft now plying backwards &amp; forwards from Troopship to beach, now &amp; again shells explode in the water or above their heads, but no notice is taken. The Destroyers are also rushing in with men &amp; tearing around at an enormous speed.  It was a great sight to see the Indian Mountain Battery, that we landed, rush the hill in front of them. The barges with the guns &amp; mules in had no sooner touched the beach, than they were hooked ashore, the mules made fast to the guns &amp; with one fiendish yell they raced for the top, the Indians keeping yelling all the way up.
