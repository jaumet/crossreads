<p>65<br/>termed who was forever being run in for drunkenness. He was always ready with a very earnest tale of how he came to have a drop too much.</p>
<p>Sometimes he would convince the Colonel &ndash; other times he wouldn't.</p>
<p>My next turn was the Gas guard. I may here say that it is always an unwritten law to put the new reinforcements on to all guard, fatigues &amp;c until they settle down and get more experience &ndash; then it automatically passes on to later arrivals.</p>
<p>We had one "old soldier" a Corporal. He was an adept at escaping duty, would get behind a door when we settled into new quarters and of course when the door was opened he would disappear from view behind</p>
