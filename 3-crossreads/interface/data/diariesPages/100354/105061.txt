Other teams would rush on behind by lucky chance may fight their way through. Thus the ceaseless procession went on.
Rain cleared off on the night of the 26th there was in consequence great aerial artillery activity. However the wind changing again brought up cold windrain.
Sunday 29th we received orders to move to the line in consequence set off at 8.30. Rain still continued to fall by the time we reached Delville Wood we were in a soaked condition. Here we bivoauced for a couple of hours building shelter lighting fires. At about 4 P.M. we moved down the road to Fleurs passing en route numerous howitzer batteries on the edge of the wood. The road was heavy with mud progress necessarily slow. Here there broken or disabled transport wagons lay foundering in the slush by the road side, while in places mules horses struggled in hidden craters.
