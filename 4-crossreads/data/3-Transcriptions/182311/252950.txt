<p>C o n f i d e n t i a l.</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Date</u> (at which this record is made up.&nbsp; June 13th 1929.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Surname</u>:&nbsp; P E T E R S.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Christian Names</u>:&nbsp; Charles, Harold.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Date of Birth</u>:&nbsp; January 25th 1889.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Where Educated</u>:&nbsp; Albert Park State School, until 14 years of age, since then by continuous night study and through books.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>THE WAR</u>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Enlisted</u> on June 20th 1915, at Melbourne, with rank of Private.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Discharged</u> on August 19th 1919 at Melbourne, with rank of Captain.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Period of Active Service</u> (in days).&nbsp; 1,520 days, (one thousand five hundred and twenty).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Fronts on which Served</u>:&nbsp; France, Belgium.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Unit or Units and in what capacity</u>:&nbsp; 38th Btn.A.I.F. as Platoon Commander - Scout Officer - Trench Raider - and Company Commander.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>Wounds</u>&nbsp; (not sickness.)&nbsp; Twice slightly, one neck, one thigh remained on duty.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
