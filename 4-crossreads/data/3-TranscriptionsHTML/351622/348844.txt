<p>15 June 1916 Thur<br/>Russians take another 1000 prisoners making the grand total up to 106,000. Very sick till dinner time. Captain Hale &amp; Mr. Barlow are now under my charge. 7th Brigade depart for Ypres. Rum issue to night again. I think now the war is the beginning of the end. Let us hope that [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>16 June 1916 Fri<br/>This has been a very busy day for me I have been on my feet going all the time from 6 oclock. Very tired as I layed myself down to rest on the old barn floor. A very interesting lecture delivered by Major Anderson of the gas school at 7 p.m. Another rum issue to night. Dont the boys roll up for that. Thousands more prisoners taken by the Russians. Good luck to them &amp; may they still continue. Told McGee off. Before closing for the night I</p>
<p>[Text continued in margin]<br/>think I might test my gas helmet.</p>
