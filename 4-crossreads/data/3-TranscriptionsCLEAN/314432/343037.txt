The German artillery had the bridges accurately ranged, & their guns in readiness to put down a barrage on them, & the casualties would have been very heavy. This was the opinion also of Captain Broadbent o/c D Coy whom I accompanied during the stunt. Therefore it may be conjectured that the Bde would never have got across the bridges, & consequently never have captured Mont St Quentin
