10/1/18 Oxford
an insignificant entrance into a courtyard encrusted with the usual old Tudory looking walls. The porter opened the Chapel door and it is in much the same condition as when opened in 1386. Most of the stained glass is original but some fine painted modern windows have been added. Saw the Reredos and the old Founders Pastoral Staff. Into the Cloisters now which run round the old green lawn and are shady and tranquil just the place for the thinker. On the walls and underfoot are inscriptions aged and numerous. Just alongside is St Peters in the East church, Norman and ancient ? a small place with a large quiet green graveyard beyond which is a prospect of other green places, all remote from bustle incongruous in a large city.
