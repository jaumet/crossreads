This was the Chateau of Francport in Compeigne [Compiegne] Forest, belonging to the Marquis de l'Aigle. The night was far advanced when the cars drew up in the courtyard, and the delegates were immediately shown the rooms reserved for them.
All of them were tired out but military necessity knows no law and early on Friday morning the Germans were awakened and after a hurried meal taken by train and motor to Marshal Foch's headquarters where without delay the Allied conditions where communicated to them.
