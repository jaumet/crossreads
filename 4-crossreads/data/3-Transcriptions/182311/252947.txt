
<p>186</p>
<p><u>NOTES RE. RAIDS</u>.</p>
<p>(Cont.)</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>EXTRA STORES REQUIRED</u>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>NOT LAID DOWN IN TABLE</u>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Traverser Bridges made by R.E.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Straw Traverser Mats 6 ft x 5 ft made by Pioneers or men accustomed to weaving.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>White Tape.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Reels for use in laying tape.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Telephone apparatus complete, including 2 phones, sufficient length of tested wire (D.2. recommended) to reach from our parapet to O.C.Attack&#39;s station; also wire direct and clear-ed from O.C.Raid&#39;s dugout to H.Q.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Flares and Rockets if special signals be used.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Direction notices for our trenches if unfamiliar to the Raider.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Corks or black grease paint for faces.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>A clean change of underclothing (from Divisional Baths) to be at billets for returning raiders.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Portfires to mark positions where demolition charges are laid and fuse lighted.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>White material to make distinguishing badges and sacking to cover same during assembly if more than one objective is to be attacked.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Sheaths of dark material for covering bright bayonets.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Lozenges or chewing-gum to prevent most of coughing during assembly.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>All officers willwear one star and all N.C.O&#39;s one chevron.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>(Sgd)&nbsp; Charles H.Peters.&nbsp; Lieut.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 38th Battalion.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 3rd Australian Division.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>26-2-18.</p>
