
3. Australian General HospitalA.I.F. at Abbassieh BarracksCairo 8 April 1916
My own beloved Mother & FatherYour letter dated the 5th & 6th March arrived this afternoon - such joy hearing from you - such dear letters - so comforting. My eyes fill with tears with the feeling that I do really belong to someone - which I always forget out here one looses all sence of being a "personality" I know that's not the word - but one merges into this vast machine which is being run to shoo the Germans back into Germany.I am afraid you had a very quiet birthday. I hated not sending you anything and I'd like to send you something by Col Jamieson - but I think I still owe something to dad and feel I better finish with that first - it seems rather a farce sending your parents presents when your in debt to them. I mean in a money way - of course the other blessed debts one can never pay. But I got two little things for Anne & Steward for Colonel Jamieson to take with him - and when I was down the Mouki the native bazaars
