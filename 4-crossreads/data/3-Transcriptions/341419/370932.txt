<p>heartily glad when after halting on the road past Es Salt from 3 a.m. till 2 p.m., we came out on to the top of the hills onto more rolling open country which did not smell of things dead.</p>
<p>Coming along at a good pace we came about 9 miles past Es Salt towards Ammon &amp; there made camp at about 4 p.m.  They were fighting at <strike>Es Salt</strike> Ammon all day today &amp; I now hear that it fell this afternoon yielding only 150 prisoners.</p>
<p>So I expect we shall move on again in a day or two.</p>
<p>Ammon is on the Hadjaz Railway hence its importance.</p>
