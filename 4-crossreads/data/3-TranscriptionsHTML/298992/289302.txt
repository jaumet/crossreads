<p>Things are very easy now, but I daresay when we are settled down properly, there will be little jobs found or made for us just to prevent us from getting too homesick.</p>
<p>We had a rather quiet farewell from Sydney. The crowd were not allowed on the wharf at all. Arthur was down there to see me off, and I had a yarn with him before we went on board. We passed few ferry boats on the way down from Woolloomooloo Bay, but they all cock-a-doodle-dooed on their whistles, and the cheers from the troops must have been heard miles away.</p>
<p>Tommy is with me, and was not sick either, so both of us have been particularly happy. Bob Newton is on the other boat worse luck so I do not suppose I shall see him till we disembark again.</p>
