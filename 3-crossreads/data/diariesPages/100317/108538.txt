Not unlike Victoria looks when the land in Spring is covered with the yellow flower of the cape weed. We had a look round from the Marett Wood O.P. and talked to a number of the men of the various companies on our way back. Slept most of the afternoon. In the evening the Q.M. F.O. came up. I revised and rewrote the opening chapters of our History of the Regiment which is being compiled by Pat Gorman. Shelling was brisk late in the evening owing to our batteries firing on their lanes of counter preparation. I turned in at about 11 pm but could not sleep a wink despite strenuous efforts. Some more gas went over towards the 24th. Otherwise the night was devoid of incident
