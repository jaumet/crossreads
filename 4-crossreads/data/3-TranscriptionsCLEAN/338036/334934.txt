 Monday 6th [Aug] Weather clearing today & prospects much brighter. 
 Tuesday 7th  Wednesday 8th  Thursday 9th Weather still against operations though improving. 
 Friday 10th Letters to home & M.B. & Ern.  Balloons in galore. 
 Saturday 11th Showery.  Fritz over last night dropping the usual quantity of bombs.  The searchlights put the wind up him a bit & he 'about turned'  Lecture by Archdeacon Ward on venereal Diseases etc.  Very forceful lecture.. 
 Sunday 12th  Monday 13th N. Broughton one of our Reinforcements fractured his tibia by a water cart running over it. 
 MOUNT KEMMEL At night Fred Cooke & I went to Pierrots & had a hot bath also. 
 [Frederick Rupert Cooke. 11931] Tuesday 14  Wednesday 15 A. MacRay (mock) broke his collarbone falling.  Our Division marching out for a rest.  All other Australian soldiers & divisions are now close to us.  Met Rupe (?) Thompson from 1st Fld Ambulance (Board man) 
 Thursday 16th Went up to Mount Kemmel tonight to watch the artillery & saw a beautiful sight.  Our guns were going their hardest & in spite of light haze we could [see] the bursts in enemy country.  One big gun in particular near Dickbusch
