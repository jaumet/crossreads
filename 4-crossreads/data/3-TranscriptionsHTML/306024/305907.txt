<p><strike>If only I had then known</strike> Madame came soon after I was admitted and did not seem too pleased with my announcement. She was then very keen on having someone there and her desire should have been satisfied if I had then known of the two fine kiddies there. However, after expressing her regret that the chap could not take it, I left.</p>
<p><strike>On the 9th of</strike> With everything so propitious for a pleasant time, it was with mingled feelings that I left with no 31 quota on the 9th of April. <strike>Still</strike> The thoughts of home were more than sufficient to counteract any feeling of regret, but the period to be spent in camp in England had no promise of pleasure.</p>
