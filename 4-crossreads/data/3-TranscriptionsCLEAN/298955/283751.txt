It was the hospital ship "Latisha" which was well known out of Halifax as she had for some time been carrying the sick & wounded back to Canada from the war zone. By accounts it happened in the following way. The passage up to the town is somewhat narrow & careful navigation here is absolutely required, but in this case as in many others somebody blundered. He pilot bordered her & asked (so the evidence goes) the officer of the watch had they passed a certain buoy & he was answered in the affirmative, he then (the Pilot) gave orders full speed ahead & within a few minutes with a fearful crash she was practically hurled her quarter length on the rocky coast, just here the coast was rather low lying otherwise had it been a bit more southerly there must have been a great loss of life as there were 600 sick and wounded soldiers on board. 200 of which were cot cases.
