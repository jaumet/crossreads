The following day Nov. 4th we entered the firing line, that night our first man was hit, being shot through the leg. On Friday 5th we were set to work on a sap to break through at the back of the trench. From here, the coast could be seen stretching towards Gaba Tepe, resembled in its seeming peacefulness the New England Plains. Right in front, separated from our lines by a ravine, by dense wire entanglements, lay the Turkish trenches, with their lines of sand bags their loop-holes looking innocently towards our lines. The position we now hold has been cut from a barley field the trampled grain stretches between the narrow lines of the trenches still harbours a few skylarks, which, altogether heedless of the flying shells, roar aloft like the true bird of Shelley's poem.
