<p>our Brigade Commander spoke to all the Officers &amp; N.Co &amp; gave a brief out line of what had to be done. We are going out on the extreme left to take hill 971. 36 is the number of our post &ndash; we have the Post of Honour allotted to us. All British troops have White bands balice on each arm 5 inches broad which goes right around the arm, also on the back a piece of same on the Right shoulder the reason being so the Navy can distinguish us from the Enemy. A Turk gave himself up sometimes or other anyway we saw the first of the particular fellow today &ndash; which of ours the (heads) squezzed all information out of him whether it was much good or not it is hard to say<br/>This and the surrounding gulies are filled with troops black Brown &amp; Brindle &amp; of course white. The Gurkhiur to be near us we are told. The was a box on movement this morning, the Turks sicked our boys out the M Batt. and held same for a while then our boys took a turn &amp; picked them out, sheld same, up till time of writing. Artillery still going strong.<br/>Tunak Bair right of [indecipherable]<br/>7.8.15<br/>We left camp last night at 9 PM &amp; marched around the beach in column of Route. The Turks must have known that we were coming. The boys were falling like logs as we were marching 2 miles along well we turned in to our right &amp; then thing started to hum. The Turks are good a anybody could be our chaps [indecipherable] &amp; feet &amp; then our men started to fall again. It was hell alright some of our boys are not to game, I think they are to ill to be fighting we advanced above &amp; made due east, we on course of our little game  went to our right to much but thing I hope will come right We were digging &amp; [indecipherable] or to be one I was working from about 12 till daybreak when we got shelled out of course we moved as the saying goes further down It was a beautiful sight to see the boats</p>
