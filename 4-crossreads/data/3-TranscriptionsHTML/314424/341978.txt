<p>Today orders were given to be ready to move, and the Brigade was to be relieved. Later it was cancelled &amp; we are to remain here for the present. as the 4 division that was to relieve us has to go elsewhere.</p>
<p>I am on 3rd picquet tonight.</p>
<p>Thursday April 25. 1918. 1361<br/>A cloudy day, threatening rain in the evening.</p>
<p>An enemy plane was brought down this morning by machine gun fire.</p>
<p>Friday April 26. 1918. 1362.<br/>Cloudy all day.</p>
<p>There was the usual artillery last night &amp; at intervals throughout today.</p>
<p>I received a letter from Willie today dated April 19 &amp; written from Weymouth. He wants to go to a Scotch port &amp; embark for Australia via the Panama. He is very jubilant at getting back.</p>
<p>Saturday April 27. 1918. 1363.<br/>Cloudy weather.</p>
<p>This front has been very quiet all day. The water here for the Horses has been condemned &amp; all transports have taken their animals a distance of about 4&frac12; kilometers to water.</p>
