 Beecham &ndash; 15.10.17 the day.  S.O.S. line was adjusted to conform with the advance of our Infantry posts. 
 16.10.17 Intermittent shelling throughout the day, and heavy bombardment of gas at night, but prompt measures on the part of Section Officers regarding wearing of gas helmets reduced casualties to one. 
 17.10.17 S.O.S. line again adjusted as two new posts were fixed out on the right flank of the Division.  Normal gas bombardment at night. 
 18.10.17 Nos. 2 &amp; 3 Sections relieved Nos. 1 &amp; 4 at 3.30 p.m.  Gun positions were again heavily shelled throughout the night. 
 19.10.17 Pack animals now being able to work as far as Seine, a reserve dump of 20,000 rounds per gun was established at each position.  Positions were greatly improved, and were much drier by use of tarpaulins.  During the afternoon an Infantry Batt. dug in behind gun position, and as a result positions were heavily shelled with H.E. and gas throughout the night.  Box Respirators had to be worn continuously for three hours.
