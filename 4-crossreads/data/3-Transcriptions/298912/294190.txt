<p>Camp Life<br/>Moore Park<br/>23rd August<br/>It was on <strike>6th September</strike>  23rd August 1915 that I reported at Moore Park Engineer Depot to Capt Fewtrell then OC. &amp; asked to be taken on the strength of the Engineer Signallers. At first I was told that no vacancies existed but on shewing my discharge he found a place for me &amp; I reported to  him 16th</p>
<p>September &ndash; Thursday at 11am<br/>Nothing occurred until 3pm, when I was given blankets (3) &amp; a waterproof sheet &amp;amp told to sleep for the time being in the Engineer Drill Hall. Here amongst the other sigs I found some old pals. One of them called Ron Gow who remembered me years back at school introduced me as "Smacker" &amp; that name remains with me &amp; whenever wanted in tram train or boat it is always "SMACKER" &ndash; Certainly an aristocratic name. Things went anyhow that day but I waited my chance when the uniforms were issued to some reinforcements then leaving &amp; rushed the joint as the saying goes &amp; got a complete uniform that night.
