  
 to 2nd F. Amb. Told that both 1st & 2nd Divis. are in France now & a casualty list is out. Ainger has been understudying me for a week & has a good grasp of the work.  Tolson is now to understudy Molloy & I understand that when they can carry on, we shall be able to rejoin the Australians. Town is now deserted for most Australians have left for unknown destinations (France ?), while Tommies are on Canal. Have heard from cousin Herbert at Suez & he wants me to visit him. A 6th L. Fus. man here told me
