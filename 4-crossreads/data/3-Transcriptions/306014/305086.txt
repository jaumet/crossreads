<p>Feb 11</p>
<p>Breakfast at Soldiers' Club at 9.30. We sat at the same table as a convalescent soldier who had stayed at the Club for the night instead of going home to his hospital &amp; he was on his last sixpence we had to pay for his breakfast but in return he told us all about the trenches &amp; &amp; fighting in France and gave us a very entertaining hour.</p>
<p>Mr Sames brought his car into town for us at 11 o'clock and drove us out to Mr Allen Gibbs' home at Moseley, to whom Alan had a letter of introduction. Mr Gibbs'  home was a very fine one and his family were most exceedingly kind to us and strove to their utmost, by deed &amp; word, to make us feel at home.</p>
