<p>Somewhere in France</p>
<p>13th March 1917</p>
<p>My Dear Brother Arch,</p>
<p>I recieved your letter the other day and was pleased to hear from you and home, it was just on three months since I&nbsp;had a letter from you but I&nbsp;and my mates blame that on to the postal dept. Well Arch I&nbsp;suppose you think I&nbsp;am either dead or wounded now that is not the case I&nbsp;am as healthy and feeling as fit as a pound of fillet steak at a 1/-&nbsp; per pound, as you say I&nbsp;am a lucky chap well I&nbsp;think the same, I&nbsp;am seeing what I&nbsp;never seen before and not likely to see again and I&nbsp;think it is making a better man out of me, you know brought up with a full stop, it has done me the world of good.</p>
