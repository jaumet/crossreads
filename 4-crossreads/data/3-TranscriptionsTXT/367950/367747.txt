28th December 1916 El Arish Thursday7.30 am, I am sitting under the shelter of my bivouac out of the rain Shanejust boiled the billy and had some "mongarie" [Arabic for food]  and I don't care now which way the wind blows. We are due for another 80 mile reconnaissance very shortly. We almost wish Sir Phillip Chetwood etc etc etc did not have such a open  good opinion of us because we are to do a much worse "stunt" than last time don't know if there is going to be any fighting at the end of it I hope so if we have as much success as we had last time. Just found out that amongst the lot of prisoners we captured at Maghdaba was a Brigadier General and a Colonel but the man we were hard on the heels of was the German Commander of the forces in these parts Von Krasse or Von Krans.
