<p>September 1917<br/>to shoot in this country there is generally a mirage &amp; no background to your target.</p>
<p>Sunday 30th<br/>Another inspection today.  It was equipment this time.  Pay day.</p>
<p>Monday 1st October<br/>One of the dustiest days we have had.  A &amp; C Squadrons played football.  The match ended in a draw.  Major O'Brian our Squadron OC went to Moascar.  Sgts mess meeting at night.</p>
<p>Tuesday 2nd October<br/>Several enemy aeroplanes were over in the morning flying very high.  They were heavily shelled &amp; made off.</p>
<p>Wednesday 3rd<br/>Got a letter from Uncle Charlie giving me the news of Aunt Carrie's death at Newcastle on Tyne.  Replied same day.</p>
<p>Thursday 4th<br/>Jack Cheater came back in the afternoon.  B Sqdn 2nds played A Sqdn 2nds &amp; beat them 3 to nil.  I played centre forward.  It was a pretty hard game.</p>
<p>Friday 5th<br/>Handed over SQMS to Cheater.  The Division had a practice turn out in full marching order &amp; they got out in very quick time.  B &amp; C Sqdns firsts played football in the afternoon  &amp; C won by 11 points</p>
