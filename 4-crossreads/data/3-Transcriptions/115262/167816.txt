<p>Monday 15 January&nbsp;1917.</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">Volunteered for the Australian Camel Field Ambulance for service abroad. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">Went through oral examination for same, and </span><strike style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">were</strike><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">&nbsp;was inspected by Capt. J. A. Heath, who is making the selection for this unit that is <u>supposed </u>to sail on Jan. 31st on Anglo-Egyptian transport.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>[Quartermaster and Honorary Captain James Alfred Heath, 44, soldier, of St Kilda, Victoria, embarked from Melbourne on 10 May 1917 on HMAT A42 Boorara with the Australian Camel Field Ambulance and 1st Reinforcements.]</p>
