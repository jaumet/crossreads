 it is an old, but truthful saying, "even the dark cloud has its silver lining" And while the battle to and fro is swaying Someone is watching o'er your fate and mine. 
 You hear the call for stretcher bearers singing From mouth to mouth sounds forth the first alarm And soon strong, willing arms are bringing The wounded quickly from the zone of harm 
 At "Red Cross Stations" nimble hands are waiting To dress your wounds &amp; ease your stinging pain Perhaps you have some soft voice sweetly stating "the fight is won, you have not fought in vain". 
 And then comes sleep! Per haps chance you may be dreaming Of flowers, sunshine, and the hum of bees. While some kind nurse with eyes of love all beaming Whispers the words "saved by the A.M.C.s"
