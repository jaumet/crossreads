<p>24 April, 1916 Monday</p>
<p>80 wks from home</p>
<p>Revielle at 5.15</p>
<p>Had miles of running about to do today fixing up for the concert tomorrow I have managed to scrape together 22 items the Gen has consented to preside &amp; Capt Chisholm is to be his off-sider the Mikadar of Sohag &amp; his escort have also consented to be present, the proprietor of the gardens has got enough food for an regt &amp; seating accomdation for 600 men, I shall be satisfied if half of the seats are full, fixed up the wicket &amp; marked the boundary &amp; had it watered.</p>
<p>The case against Lt Potts is to be gone on with straight away &amp; the business of collecting evidence is proceeding steadly, he stands a fair chance of falling in properly.</p>
<p>Played cards &amp; chess all night &amp; slipped down the town for supper &amp; then turned in at 10.15. Tired out.</p>
<p>25 April, 1916 Tuesday</p>
<p>ANZAC DAY</p>
<p>Reveille same old time</p>
<p>I spent the morning making final arrangements for this afternoon &amp; tonight, after lunch got the wicket ready &amp; played commenced at 1.30 pm. I was elected Capt &amp; lost the toss so they went in to bat they made 114 Cambell out of that got 57, Hal Bowled well, the heat was very oppressive we went in &amp; went down like fun for a start, I got 17 before being cleaned bowled we made altogether 63 so never had a look in, after the match went &amp; had a shower &amp; had tea then off down to the gardens &amp; found there an awful crowd of fellows present got things going straight away &amp; everything went off splendid, the programme turned out first class, I had to get up &amp; thank the Gen for his presence &amp; made a bit of a speech which passed off OK no comments. Got home at 10.30 satisfied with the way things went &amp; turned in.</p>
