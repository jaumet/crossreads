It is marvellous what men can go through in war. We left Daours at 5 p.m. for the line, &amp; today I had my first glimpse of war in France, &amp; all its hideousness. If people at home could see what happens, the cursed war would be stopped. We are to be the reserve company of the battalion &amp; have to dig a new line. Boo's company, B, are to go in the front line &ndash; may Good Luck always be with him. Our company set off along the road, &amp; on both sides of it near the station were shell holes &amp; dead mules &ndash; Fritz was evidently after the railway line. Of course it began to rain, but on &amp; on we marched without a stop, the captain in front was riding, so what would he know about how tired we were. Passed a field where there were 11 dead horses &amp; mules, poor brutes, that they should be victims of shells. The rain was running of the top of my steel helmet, &amp; underneath it  the perspiration was trickling.
