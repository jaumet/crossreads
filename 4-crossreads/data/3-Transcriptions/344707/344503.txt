<p>on Monday morning, reaching Walkers pier at 4.25 Am &ndash; doing in 20 minutes what used to take an hour &ndash;<br/>We ran all the way, with Soks on our boots to hide the sound &ndash; The big mine with all the surplus High Explosives being fired just as we entered the MAORI sap from No 1 Outpost, a terrific explosion &amp; now the Turks fired everything they had. At the wharf were Colonel ANTILL &amp; the embarkation officer &amp; about 5 others behind us as our barge steamed out they got with another tug on the opposite side of the pier there were no others &ndash;<br/>I left  in my dugout, Blankets, rubber boots &amp; kit that I could not carry &amp; a letter to Johnny Turk asking him  to give any of our prisoners or wounded a fair go &ndash;<br/>The hours were long waiting, to add to the deception we walked about the trench coughed &amp; kept firing from different positions.
