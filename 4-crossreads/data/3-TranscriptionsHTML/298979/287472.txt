<p>The Eighth Brigade having proceeded us by about a week the 14th Brigade "moved off" from Ferry Post on the Canal to Moasker. Starting early in the morning we arrived after one rest, at about 2 in the afternoon. As a Brigade had moved further on we simply walked into the lines they had occupied &amp; saved us the necessity of pitching tents etc. While here we had very little drill of any kind the principal thing being to join in a Bathing Parade &amp; have a "dip" in one of the Salt Lakes close by.</p>
<p>The supply of water here was very good &amp; opportunity was taken to have all soiled underclothing &amp; socks washed clean &amp; also the possibility of having a "scrub down" in fresh water was very acceptable</p>
<p>To show how small the world is &amp; how the war produces all kinds of unheard of meetings, the following incident may be recalled.</p>
<p>During one of these "washing days" after having washed all his underclothing &amp; socks, one of the privates in our Company, just as he was in the process of having a fresh water bath in a basin or "bowl" as they are called, noticed a man apparently waiting at a distance of about 20 yards, looking intently in his direction. The private washing, therefore hailed the other man, If you want a "bowl" there is another here not in use, which you are welcome to? to which a reply was called that it was not the bowl, but he wanted to speak to the private.</p>
