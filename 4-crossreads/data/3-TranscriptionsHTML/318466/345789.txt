<p>Sunday 5 May 1918<br/>Ede came out with Dorothy.  Plenty of visitors come out on Sunday.</p>
<p>Monday 6 May 1918<br/>The Australian Red Cross people are very obliging &amp; present me with a sponge, soap, writg. paper &amp; other good things.</p>
<p>Tuesday 7 May 1918<br/>Ede came out with some food for which I was very thankful.  Am nearly starved to death here.</p>
<p>Wednesday 8 May 1918<br/>Rup came up from Fovant to see me.  Bringing more food which again just about saved my life.</p>
<p>Thursday 9 May 1918<br/>Feeling much better &amp; sister says I may get up.  Cheers!!  Not to steady on my pins.</p>
<p>Friday 10 May 1918<br/>Rup left Fovant Hosp. for good.  Am able to get about more.</p>
<p>Saturday 11 May 1918<br/>Went to Uncle Baden to tea with Ede &amp; Gladys.  Mrs. Mehan came out, Ede, Gladys, one of the Bayne girls &amp; Rup.</p>
