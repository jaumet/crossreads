D has been up before the O.C. a few times on paltry offences. Capt Chapman delights in getting people into trouble when he is O.O. By his manner and attitude one would think that a second Napoleon had arrived.
Heartily sick of this kind of life. It is too irregular for me. From what one reads in the newspaper foodstuff must be pretty dear in the home land and also very scarce
So far we over here in France have not felt the pinch.
The grounds around the C.C.S. are being greatly improved. Men who have been used to gardening are on the job and they are decorating the corners of the C.C.S. beautifully Grassy turf is being laid down, nice trees and flowers and plants have been put in the ground so that altogether when the summer comes the C.C.S. will look quite an elaborate place. There is no chance of promotion in this unit so I have decided to content myself, do my work, have as much spare time as possible and devote it to the best advantage for Apres la. Guerre.
March 1st.Went to St. Omer in a Motor
