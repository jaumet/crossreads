  
 Australian waterside Workers would be vindicated & they certainly succeeded.   Five  Nine men working on each hulk (six men filling, one man on the winch & two on the trolley & basket above, tipped coal into the old Derbyshire at the rate of about 2 1/2 tons to the minute. At six o'clock they knocked off again for tea. 
 During this time the health officer had held a long conversation with the ships captain O.C troops (Major Jacobs) & the adjutant (Captain Rossiter) at the foot of the gangway.
