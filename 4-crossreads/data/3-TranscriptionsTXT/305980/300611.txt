 25:  Football comp. won by "B" squadron of 1st regt. who were undefeated. 
 29:  Inspection by Gens. Chauvel [indecipherable] &amp; Chaytor,"A.M.D",  Dressed &amp; waiting for their arrival from 10 a.m. to 2.30p.m. 
 30:  Boys counted out the officers of the unit, picquet having to fall in at 5 p.m. without having their tea &amp; on duty for 13 &frac12; hours. 
 December 
 3:  Brigade sports, our section placed third in Lloyd Lindsay race. 
 5:  Fearful weather; three inches fell in six hours; horse lines &amp; camps flooded. 
 8:  Fine weather again; rugby v. "A" sqdn.; we won by 11- nil.  Bits &amp; irons to be kept polished &amp; two hours drill each morning, so all the time we have  for pleasure is from noon to 3.30 p.m.; feed again at 7 p.m.  Daily routine: Stables 6 to 6.45 a.m. watering parade 9 to 10 a.m. Drill from 10 to noon, when we feed our horses.  Lunch &amp; we are free till 3.30 p.m. &ndash; having to clean our steel gear during these hours at our leisure &ndash; stables from 3.45 to 5 p.m., have tea &amp; feed up again at 7 p.m. when we may retire unless on horse picquet
