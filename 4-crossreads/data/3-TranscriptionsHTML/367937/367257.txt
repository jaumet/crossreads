<p>B Squadron<br/>1st Reg.<br/>A.L.H.</p>
<p>Sat. 12th Aug. 1916</p>
<p>My dear Father<br/>I might as well begin this letter to you now but I don't know when I will be able to post it as we are right away out in the desert from camp and have been ever since Tuesday night.<br/>Things were pretty lively out here at the start but have got very quiet the last two days; in fact today things were so good that I sat down and took off my boots for the first time &amp; and had a look at my feet.  Found them still there alright &amp; only want a wash to be quite respectable.<br/>I suppose that long before you get this you will have heard of poor Ronald being killed; I was not with him when it happened as he had advanced with the left of the line for a few hundred yards leaving us on the right to keep firing &ndash; I think a sniper may have got him.  He was shot dead through the heart at the head of his men - He was an awfully brave man and every body in the regt. misses him,  as soon as I get back to camp I will write to Mrs. Macdonald.  That Wednesday was an awful day; the Turks were much stronger than was expected (it was thought that there were only a small detachment of them</p>
