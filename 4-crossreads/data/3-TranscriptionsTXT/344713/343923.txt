 Tues. Nov. 14th: - Marched along the muddiest roads imaginable through Montauban Carnoy &amp; Fricourt &amp;  occupied new Nissen huts at a camp near Sausage Gully. 
 Wed. Nov. 15th: - Marched through Mealte over Vivier Mill bridge; through Dernancourt to Buire. Have billeted in barns &ndash; Bitterly Cold. 
 Thurs. Nov. 16th: - Marched through Ribemont to Heilly for our wash at Divisional baths this afternoon &amp; it was badly wanted. No bath or Change for over a month. Aust. Mail dated Sept. 24th. 
 Fri. Nov. 17th: - Rode by motor buses via Amiens to Flesselles &amp; billeted. 
 Sat. Nov. 18th: - Snowed a bit last night &amp; all the district is mantled. Winter in alright &amp; bitterly Cold. 
 Sat. Nov. 25th 1916: - My first "Blighty" furlough has arrived &amp;  I am off for a rest to London for the first time. Left Flesselles by motor wagon at 4.30 A.M. &amp; went to Canaples Railway Station; entrained for Havre.
