<p>Sydney<br/>Y.M.C.A.<br/>Australian Expeditionary Forces</p>
<p>14.5.1917</p>
<p>L. Cpl. Maurer<br/>No. 1 Hut<br/>No. 4 A.G.H.<br/>Randwick</p>
<p>Dear Miss Clark</p>
<p>Having just returned from leave have just received your letter re Cpl. Gordon.  I am very sorry to say that I can tell you very little about how he received his wounds as I was wounded at the end of July &amp; he was going along well when I left the lines &amp; it was in Hospital in England when I heard he was badly wounded &amp; learned later that he had died of his wounds.  I can only say how sorry I was when I heard of his death because a finer lad never lived than poor Gordon &amp; he was thought the world of by all the boys in the Bombers, especially those under his command.
