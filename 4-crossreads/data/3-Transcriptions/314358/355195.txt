<p>Should alone concern us.</p>
<p>Before and after are nothing as far as our duty lies in this instant 'now' towards other living feeling creatures</p>
<p>But regarding each thee or I of us unto himself, 'Before' and 'After' are stupendous in the mouldering of this 'Now'</p>
<p>From 'Before' do [stretch?] long arms of good and evil, each with a certain hand of measured force with a potters thumb long trained by generations to mould and shape</p>
<p>In 'After', while it is in its youth of future do prizes glean and glisten</p>
<p>The dross, as brilliant as the gold. And none know which be worthy.<br/>Also there behind fair hedges of alluring dreams calamites in ambush lie, where fortune seems</p>
