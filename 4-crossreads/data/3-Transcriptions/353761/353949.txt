<p>(18)<br/>Now there is a field hospital on our left.<br/>We can see the Union Jack and the Red Cross side by side like a pair of large black handkerchiefs slowly flapping against the silver sky. The large tents and the small tents are but outlines. There is peace and silence and slumber here.<br/>The burden of our equipment and packs has now become two fold in weight and we are wet though with sweat.<br/>The troops mutter and curse, sing and laugh and joke, grumble, whistle and endure; while they all hope for a spell and a rest.</p>
