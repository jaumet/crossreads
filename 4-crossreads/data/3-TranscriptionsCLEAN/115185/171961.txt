I have not any friends of my own in Cairo at present - but I help Sister Dowe look after hers. That is, I mean men I particularly want to talk to. But of course Rog Kinghorn is still with us and his younger brother Bruce is in the engineers - and I have asked them to tea at the club and told Bruce to come out here when he can. Everyone feels that it  is  important here to look after the boys. I had a letter from Capt Geffrey Streets' mother thanking me for looking after him in Lemnos. But I expect mother has told her how much we all thought of him out there. What a fine soldier he is making. I will write to Mrs Street, I have looked for Eustace but cannot hear news of him. On the 10th Feb - Alma & I met her two cousins Corporal Charlie Dowe of Aust Vet Corps & Lieut Eng Harden R.E. English Regiment at Groppis -  the tea shop  here.
