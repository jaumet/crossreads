<p>"Peace and quietness reigned about 12 <strike>p</strike> midnight".</p>
<p>24th<br/><strike>A</strike> The companies were paraded under their respective company commanders at 10 A.M. and the men told to move off to their respective battalions but not a man budged an inch.  They refused to recognise any other Bn for themselves <strike>f</strike> but the 54th.  The C.O. therefore called a muster parade and told the men that their efforts to reform the Bn. would be fruitless and asked representatives from each company to <strike>come</strike> go out to him and state their reasons for not obeying orders.  After a little talk with these men the C.O. advised them to rejoin</p>
