<p>Monday 24 June 1917 [1918]<br/>Bath 6 a.m., breakfast 7.30 a.m., then duty 8 a.m. with S. Jackson (her last day in ward).  In taking equipment over 1 fire bucket is missing but is ret'd on pressure &amp; now practically everything is right save 10 basins &amp; no one knows how those can be counted as pts. have the same.  Pts. 45 in number mostly malaria, debility, quinsy, abscesses on neck or arms &amp; in general run down.  Food only fair &amp; eggs bad, out of 38, 21 either badly cooked or bad.<br/>Wards are untidy but light &amp; airy, boys good but very careless of any etiquette, i.e., standing up, etc.<br/>Matron 9 a.m. where Red X things are <u>dragged</u> out one at a time.  Off 5-8.  Room large &amp; lovely window.  Andrews &amp; V.A.D. share it.</p>
