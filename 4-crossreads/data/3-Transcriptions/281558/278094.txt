<p>over the old castle there : good day again : changed shifts so I am on from 6 pm : got a few letters :</p>
<p>Monday March 18th</p>
<p>Misery : off at noon : strolled round &amp; then bed :</p>
<p>Tuesday March 19th &amp; Wednesday 20th</p>
<p>Misery : heavy gunfire all day &amp; intensified on Wed night to a terrific bombardment :</p>
<p>Thursday March 21st</p>
<p>Misery : plenty of work as the stuff from up the line is coming down : old Fritz has attacked in masses &amp; is coming forward by sheer weight of numbers &amp; guns</p>
<p>Friday March 22nd</p>
<p>Misery : went to Peronne in afternoon &amp; all civillians were then evacuating : very busy at night getting the trains away :</p>
<p>Saturday March 23rd</p>
<p>Misery : got orders to prepare to evacuate : worked all our trucks away &amp; after Brie left we were the next : our last was over 80 trucks loades with macadam two engines in front &amp; one at rear : before getting away old Fritz came over with eight planes &amp; flew over our train only a couple of hundred feet high : he had no bombs left so had to pass over : one came down by machine gun fire : the infantry were digging in &amp; Fritz came over the ridge beyond Brie just as we moved off : went right through Chaulnes &amp; finished up at Romescamp thence to Blargies</p>
<p>Sunday March 24th</p>
<p>On train : went through Amiens &amp; Romescamp &amp; finished up at Blargies : R.O.D. depot : slept in our van :</p>
