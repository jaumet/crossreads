<p><span style="line-height: 1.6;">Thursday 14 January 1915</span><br /><span style="line-height: 1.6;">Arrived at Alexandria 9am.Tied to buoy in harbour awaiting orders.<br />0.40 am. Mails came on board. Wondering are there letters for me. not likely.</span><s style="line-height: 1.6;">Replied at once</s><span style="line-height: 1.6;"> Letter from Joseph [Josephine Nash]. Replied at once &amp; posted on ship.<br />Posted letters to Mollie, the M. J. of A. N. [?]<br />Many went on shore intending to go to Cairo tonight. All the Mc mc&#39;ers.&nbsp;[A blue pencil note that&nbsp;looks like the letter K has been written&nbsp;over the previous word.]</span></p>
<p>Friday 15 January 1915<br />Still tied up to the buoy in the harbour. O.C. away. Not on shore so far 12 noon. Wire from O.C. this morning re men &amp; danger of infection.<br />O.C. not returned at 11-55 p.m. Col Ramsay Smith &amp; other senior officers away. Lieut. Col. Maudsley only other Colonel&nbsp;on the boat.</p>
<p>Saturday 16 January 1915<br />9 a.m. - O.C. &amp; other Senior officers, except Maudsley, away on shore during the afternoon. Lunch at Khedivial&nbsp;Club. Drove round City, to Pompeys pillar, &amp;&nbsp;Katacombs. &amp;c. Back at 6 pm.<br />Orders to disembark &amp; go to Cairo as soon as possible.</p>
<p>Sunday 17 January 1915<br />7-30 am Kyarra&nbsp;at Wharf.<br />12.noon. Baggage being packed now for removal to Cairo. At Jesuits College. Magnificent.<br />Baggage put on train for Cairo.</p>
