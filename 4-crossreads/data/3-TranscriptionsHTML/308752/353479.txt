After tea I was taken down to Marlow Bridge, from here the scenery was magnificent. A portion of the Thames runs through this two, boating &amp; fishing is indulged in here. The river at Marlow resembles that of Narrabeen Lakes, so you can imagine what a delightful spot it is. Arrived in London close on midnight. Two nice Scotch lassies were in the carriage, so of course butted my frame in &amp; everything in the garden was lovely.   The following day &amp; also my last day of leave, I had a wild tour of London, &amp; came across a good number of our Unit. Spent the day with them. Called on Mr Hooper &amp; gave him 5&pound; to keep for me, it is no use taking it to France, it would be wasted. Visited Madame Tussards wax works, &amp; the Zig Zag, in the afternoon, &amp; Oscar Ashe &amp; Lily Brayton in the evening n Chu Chin Chow, &amp; who ever do you I met there, I gave him the shock of his life, Eric &amp; Clarry Sullings. We had a good old yarn. I am glad I met Eric in London.<br/>On the 21st August I reported to the R.T.O. at Waterloo Station &amp; caught the 7.25 train for Folkstone, en-route</p>
