<p>Thursday 15th 1917<br/>All last night our Guns were making a great noise, when I went to sleep they were banging &amp; when I awoke at 7 am they still were fireing. After Breakfast word came in that the Boys went over at Daylight &amp; gained their Objectives. They took possession of Langemarch, a fairly size village &amp; had advanced for about 2 miles, &amp; had taken close on 2000 prisoners, Fritz evidently got wild of this offensive as he put his spite on us &amp; started after Breakfast sending over High Explosives &amp; kept it up until nigh Dinner time, the shells exploded all around the Camp &amp; Railway, one made a big hole about 20 ft from the Control Office in a Crop of Oats, another fell behind the Camp about 50 yds off one fell in a shed where a man was grooming a horse he was blown out of the shed &amp; the horse &amp; another near was killed outright, fortunately none of our boys were hit. After dinner I got a Pass &amp; went into Poperinghe for a change. I had several things</p>
