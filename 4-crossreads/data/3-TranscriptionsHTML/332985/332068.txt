<p>Bty. to pull right section guns out.  Seven guns on our front blown out today.  Also one 9.2 in.  Tonight the Artillery active.</p>
<p>11-1-1917<br/>Snowing, freezing tonight.  Left section coming out of action some time to night.  We were in action at Fleurs.  Three D.A.C. men killed.  Over 50 shells over.  16 reinforcements from D.A.C., 4 horses Vet. Hospital.</p>
<p>12-1-1917<br/>Snowing and raining all day.  Move out tomorrow at 9 a.m.  Shelled again last night.  Sgt. Galloway the new Sgt., wounded.  One horse wounded hit in eye.</p>
<p>13-1-1917<br/>Out of action.  A terrible job to get the gun out, had to depend upon one pair of horses;  we would never have got out without them.  In leaving the wagon line we had to double bank the teams to get on to the main road, 8 horses in a water cart.  My old horse well again riding him out.  Arrived at Buire 4-30 p.m.  Pronounced bue air.  When we started it</p>
