<p>the stables &amp; lofts to sleep in. The stall 4 of us are in is very hard being paved with cobble stones also cold and draughty but we make ourselves as comfortable as we can under the circumstances.</p>
<p>The weather cold and rainy.</p>
<p>Am interested in this mill it driven by water power from a locked canal &amp; the froggy millers very kindly show us all over it.</p>
<p>After dinner I walk up the town rather a large place with a B.E.F. (British Expeditionary Forces) Canteen where every thing can be bought by the troops from a needle to anchor.</p>
<p>In my wanderings I enter a place &amp; procure a cup of coffee from a French madame and her very pretty daughter of sixteen with a bunch of dark Greecian curls. We get quite friendly &amp; madame tells me her husband is fighting at the front &amp; she comes here &amp; opens a coffee room to be near him her home is in Paris hence I am bucked up with a spirit of contentment &amp; intent to make this my rondevous.</p>
<p>The Town is full of Tommies</p>
