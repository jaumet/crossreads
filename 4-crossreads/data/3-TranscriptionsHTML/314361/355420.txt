<p>to  Albert, &amp; there entrained for Bapaume, from Bapaume we marched to this village, we are in tents just outside. During the night it came on to rain, &amp; is raining still. Do not think that we shall be here long, only untill relief comes for the 2nd Div.<br/>Trenches May 9th 17<br/>Once again in trenches, this time near Bullecourt.<br/>We arrived here last night, it is a long walk, along road through villages then over land, we passed through our own artillery, &amp; at times there was a heavy bombardment on, guns going off every where, we got very few shells back. One man shot himself in the foot while we were going forward.<br/>May 10th 17<br/>Second day in the trenches.<br/>Last night was a night of experiences. There was heavy spasmodic shelling, very often uncomfortably close, &amp; we had three gas alarms, the first was lacrimatory gas (tear gas), I could smell it for a long while before I put the helmet on, which I did not do, until my eyes began to smart.<br/>Another time I was called out to repair a line, found the double cable broken in two places, but only about fifty yards out.
