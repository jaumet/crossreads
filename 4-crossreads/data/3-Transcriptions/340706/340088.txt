<p>134<br/>till our combination of cattle trucks moves at a rate resembling a train. Most of the country now resembles English countries in south the villages are uniformly situated in depths of valleys whether to escape winds or on account of water supply I do not know but there are few exceptions to this rule.</p>
<p>This route is new to me &amp; I have not remembered the names of the stations but we have missed all hitherto well known names. The nearest station to the camp is Harfluer so we proceed about 5 miles further on as this is the usual military way of doing things. We detrain &amp; then have to march back again to boy leading the way.</p>
<p>We go first to the old Conv. Camp for purpose of being deloused. Here rain has been heavy &amp; everywhere you flounder through mud. As night has come on &amp; lights are scase things are not pleasant but <strike>like</strike> luckily we strike Creighton &amp; he show us round let us wash in his room &amp; finally gives us a supper of preserved fruit &amp; tea in his room.</p>
<p>Our quarters here are damnable after the comfort of a bed for the last 3 mos. However I have the knack of being able to sleep anyhow &amp; anywhere so this not not inconvenience me much</p>
<p>4-5-19 (Mon)<br/>This camp is for purpose of delousing everyone &amp; organisation is very thorough</p>
