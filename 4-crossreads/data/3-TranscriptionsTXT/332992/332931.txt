Monday April 30.1917A beautiful spring morning.  The weather is idea and warm, in fact some days it is rather too warm after a long cold winter.There was constant artillery activity last night and about 4 a.m. there was a terrific bombardment from the Batteries all around us, which shook the house, a brick one, all over, and when at regular intervals the big guns went off it made the floor jump, & violently shook me in bed.  There were a lot of aeroplanes about this morning, & I saw one (the enemies) brought down by our archies & a battle plane soon after breakfast.  Fritz raided the 39 last night & inflicted some casualties, and the 37 had to be in readiness to stand to.  Fritz suffered very severely.  I saw a squadron of our aeroplanes, large & small fly over our billet in battle formation.  They made a hum like a hive
