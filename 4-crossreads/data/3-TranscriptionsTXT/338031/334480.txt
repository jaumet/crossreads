 steadily on through villages where everybody was in slumber under the thatched roofs. The soldiers were all curiously silent; there was no ribaldry or cursing as was invariably the case when returning to camp from a night march in Australia. The novelty of the surroundings, the sub.conscious whisper of heredity, the feeling that we were now on definitely preliminary stage of the Great Expedition &ndash; kept in check the cheap vulgarity which is inevitably associated with the congregation of soldiers and Members of Parliament.
