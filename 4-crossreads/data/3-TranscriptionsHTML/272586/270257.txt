<p>by the turks on the canal. they are not very large affairs made of iron they now resemble collanders more than anything else. They are too far gone to patch up &amp; to date the turk does not seem to have done much good for himself in the canal business.</p>
<p>From the zoo, we hired an aged heathen &amp; his boat &amp; continued our journey towards Cairo, which we reached after much hard work on the boatman's part. I would have given the old dog a spell, but on second thoughts came to the conclusion that a little work now &amp; then would not hurt him. I am led to believe that these natives have become utterly spoiled since the Australians came to Egypt, &amp; I don't doubt it the average Australian soldier believes that money was made to spend and acts accordingly.</p>
