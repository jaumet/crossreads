<p>July 20.</p>
<p>During July the Bishop of London (Dr. Winnington Ingram) visited the Fleet for two days.&nbsp; He came on board &quot;Australia&quot; &amp; gave an excellent address to the officers &amp; men.&nbsp; The Germans were now busily employed in laying mines, &amp; distributing fuel to submarines by means of motor fishing smacks flying Dutch colours.&nbsp; Our patrols quickly snapped up these craft, and some dozens werre brought into harbour under armed guard.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Aug. 5.</p>
<p>At last, all the surviving battle-cruisers of the Jutland action having been repaired, the Fleet left for Scapa Flow to carry out exercises.</p>
<p>Aug. 6.</p>
<p>Foggy weather, for the most part, delayed our programme, on arrival at Scapa Flow.</p>
<p>Aug. 16.</p>
<p>However, after 10 days we completed our exercises, &amp; left for Rosyth.&nbsp; Here we completed with coal &amp; oil fuel.</p>
<p>Aug. 18.</p>
<p>Only 24 hours elapsed before we were hurriedly despatched to sea, the German High Sea Fleet having come out.</p>
