<p>to the golf links so after bidding me welcome he offered his apology &amp; left.  Mrs Newton took charge of me &amp; very graciously made me welcome &amp; immediately at home.  She introduced me to Mrs Newton's sister, a very sweet young lady who lives with them.  I was then shown to a beautiful bedroom where I had a wash &amp; brush up.  I then descended to the dining room a very tasty meal was set before me to which I did justice.  Though the weather was threatening Witt &amp; myself went out to view the beauty of the countryside.  The term "beautiful Kent is not misapplied".  The lovely green fields, the densly leaved trees, perfectly built Roads and houses of the well to do were sights very pleasing to the eye.</p>
