<p>April<br/>Sunday  Apl 1st<br/>Was to have had an hr off but a fall of heavy snow spoilt everything &amp; I played cards instead.</p>
<p>Monday 2nd<br/>Very cold today.  An officer Lt Stratford was brought very badly wounded and in a low condition.  He had one thigh fractured &amp; had lost a good deal of blood.  We got to work on him &amp; after the eather he was still very low.  The Major ordered saline injection &amp; we injected about 2&frac12; pnts in about 1&frac34; hrs.  After that his pulse was better but towards morning he was down again &amp; Major suggested blood transfusion.  Ronny Hill volunteered to give him sufficient blood &amp; one pint was taken.  The patient did much better &amp; the next morning had rallied considerably.  It was a good safe. [save]<br/>[2nd Lt. Frederick William Stratford, 41st Btn]<br/>Tuesday 3rd<br/>We awoke to find it blowing a blizzard and with snow.  The flakes were about the biggest I have seen.  However as if by magic the wind stopped &amp; the sun came out &amp; it was quite pleasant.  At night we went to the NZ Pierrots &amp; saw a good  -   I got a program.</p>
