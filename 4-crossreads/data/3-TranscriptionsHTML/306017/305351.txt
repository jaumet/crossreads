<p>July &ndash; August 1916<br/>The 31st bombed the camps here.  There were several casualties.  They made 2 raids during the morning.  Our air craft guns didn't get in such good work as the other day when their firing was beautiful to watch.  A good deal of mail has come in &amp; I received 3 letters 1 from Doug 1 from home &amp; one from Doles.  The weather is still very hot, but the days are getting noticeably shorter.  I took over orderly Sgt.</p>
<p>Monday 31st [July]<br/>It is a solid job these [indecipherable] &amp; you are running about nearly all day &amp; still have to go out on all the stunts.  We went out again at midnight.  My troop was left flank guard &amp; before we got to Katia we were heavily fired on &amp; had to gallop back.  The artillery dispersed several bodies of the enemy during today.
