<p>Saturday 7th<br/>Received couple of letters from Australia.  Very Cold today.</p>
<p>Sunday 8th<br/>To Figheldean with H.O. Bastian to Church.  Saw the "Village Smithy" around which the "Village Blacksmith" was <strike>written</strike> said to be written.</p>
<p>Monday 9th<br/>Our week's bivouac commences this day.<br/>Pushed off from No. 3 Camp at 9 a.m. in conjunction with the Ninth Infantry Brigade.  Arrived at the Bustard Trenches after about two hours march and spent the greater part of morning in arranging out Camp.  A party was detailed and set out immediately to erect our Advanced Dressing Station.  I however remained with the Ambulance.  The day passed quickly without any action.</p>
<p>Tuesday <strike>9</strike> 10th<br/>Detailed as Cooks Fatigue all day.<br/>There was an action during the afternoon and the Permanent Stretcher Squads which had been formed had plenty of work to do.</p>
<p>Wednesday <strike>10</strike> 11th<br/>Acting as a No. 4 to a Stretcher Squad and took part in two actions 3 p.m. &amp; 8 p.m., obtained good practise.  During the afternoon's action I came across Geo Duckworth our Leichhardt C.C. scorer now attached 36th Batt.  He was a Casualty.</p>
<p>Thursday 12th<br/>Attached to the Ambulance Signallers Corps with Corp. Ted Wylie, Priv. Chas Wynne, Rutherford, Carroll &amp; Tucker.  Tucker &amp; I were working together today at the Advanced Station.</p>
