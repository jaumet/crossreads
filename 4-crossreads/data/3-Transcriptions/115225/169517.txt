
<p>In the afternoon we had a tin of metho burning (methylated Spirits issued for putting in radiators to minimise chances of freezing but we used our own discreation as to how we used it &amp; we found that we derived more comfort from it by burning it in an open vessel inside the lorry) &amp; sat round it. I stayed in &amp; read all the evening -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We had to set out our tools &amp; personal kits for inspection by Joe Grim. after that ordeal &nbsp;was over, Joe &amp; I walked into Bailleul &amp; had tea then we went to see the Dons at the concert hall &amp; walked home after&nbsp;&mdash;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Memo - The weather has been very soft all this week thoring consistently - Coal &amp; bread are almost unprocurable by the civil population here.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 6<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We went to Steenwerck railhead &amp; hauled a load of oats to 10<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;Brigade dump. Jim drove 21136 -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 7<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We did&#39;nt go out to-day. I painted the new lockers which I&nbsp;built in the lorry &amp; it is very cosy. I went to Bailleul &nbsp;&amp; had a hot bath. also went to ordnance &amp; changed a p<sup><u>r</u></sup>&nbsp;of breeches.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 8<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;A wild morning. snowing hard and cold. I had to go out on a job. so also did Bud -</p>
