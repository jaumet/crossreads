This morn the sandy areas were as rippling water, the movements of yesterday having quite obliterated all foot prints left by man &amp; animals; the trenches made by the soldiers were sanded inches &amp; feet deep on the windward side. <p>Midst the teachings of my childhood &amp; early boyhood, and even some years later, much was learned that has proved by experience to be illusory.  Of them the parading of the Arab Steed, as something surpassingly good in the horse line, produced effect which made me look forward to seeing him of the very best in Egypt.  In praise of him a song had verse as follows:/<br/>"My arab steed, my arab steed,<br/>That standest meekly by,<br/>With thy fondly arched &amp; glossy neck<br/>And dark &amp; firey eye.<br/>Fret not to roam the dessert now<br/>With all thy winged speed<br/>I may not mount on thee again<br/>Thou'rt sold, thou'rt sold, my arab steed."</p>
<p>In these lines it is conjured up as a thing of beauty and a joy forever.  From the Godolphin Arabian is supposed to be derived the blood, which runs in the <strike>blood</strike> vessels of the best racing strains in England.
