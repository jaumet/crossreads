 "John Bulls" from J.B.J : also a letter each from Bill &amp; Fat Mabbott &amp; Jess Muir: papers were plentiful &amp; it is amusing to read of our arrival in France: when will these paper hoodlums  stop talking such rot as they do: flowers, charming girls, real beds &amp; furniture &amp; other such rot; they must have either visited head Quarters or some of the "cold feet" that live in comfort back from the line: a trip to a hole like this would open up that paper hoodlums eyes a bit: it is up to me to get a letter from Ethel: 
 Tuesday July 18th   Cardonette: showery day: reveille 6.30 am: no physicaljerks on account of the weather: ordered to pack up ready to move off: moved off at 1.40 pm &amp; glad to see the last of that hole: passed through a few villages one was a large one  &amp; had a lot of Tommies in it also a lot of transports: passed a lot of transports on the move: the "padre" always marches with our Coy &amp; is a hard case: got to our billets at Rubempre about 4 pm &amp; were put in a big barn: on the ground again: scored some biscuits &amp; tinned fruit &amp; milk &amp; had a good eat up: after tea we had a good walk round the village which is a large one: plenty of Tommies here &amp; one showed us a German bomb &amp; a few souvenirs: got a democrat from C Beatty &amp; had a read: got a pretty P.
