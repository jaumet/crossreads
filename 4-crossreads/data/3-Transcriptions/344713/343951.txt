<p>Mon. April 30th: -<br/>Two Stretcher bearers of "C" Coy killed by shell on road.<br/>Walthers &amp; Herkett.</p>
<p>Tues. May 1st. 1917: -<br/>Left the large battered barn at Vraucourt &amp; went to other side of Vaulx &amp; then forward to frontage of small wood where there are some old German gun-posts &amp; stayed as screen for Heavy artillery behind.</p>
<p>Wed. May. 2nd: -<br/>Shifted at midnight on to Noreuil &amp; through communication trench running  from sunken road on far side of village to railway embankment &amp; waited  till daylight.</p>
<p>Thurs. May 3rd: -<br/>We are moving on from the railway embankment up to out front line (which was lately "Fritz's" second line)  &amp; which the 2nd Division took off him  in the "hopover".<br/>But as they have suffered too much in holding it we were "lent" out to them to supply relief.<br/>Artillery fire is horrible &amp; constant &amp; it is supposed to be a bit of his "Hindenburg" Line so he is piling the ironmongery on to us.<br/>This is our share of the Bullecourt business &amp; we are about 800 yds in front of Reincourt.</p>
<p>Fri. 4th: -<br/>Went back to supports on</p>
