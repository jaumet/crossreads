April 5: still camped at Pommera, but proceeded thro' Doullens to Vignacourt to load.  Great congestion in yard.
April 6: Struck camp at 7 a.m. and proceeded via Doullens and Picquigy to Ailly-Sur-Somme to load. Later pitched camp at St. Saveur, 2 k.m. away.  Three of us billetted. 
April 11: Went to Amiens to load at 10 a.m. City deserted and being shelled.
April 14: Railway yard shelled.  One of our Section hit in stomach with part of a shell, subsequently dying of wounds.  Another taken to hospital with the shock but recovered.
April 16: Loading again at Ailly-sur-Somme at midday.  Nice warm day.
April 18-19-20. Very cold and unseasonable weather. Snow fall during the day of 19th and much snow and frost on ground on morning of 20th.
May. Medical Stores
May 31. Loaded at 6 a.m. at Ailly, and after dinner packed up at short notice and proceeded to Raineville Rd, between Allonville & Poulainville, near Amiens.
