<p>remember the city fairly well but was again amused by the cable trams when they came to corners &amp; had to be helped round them. The conductors with their tickets decorating their jackets also caused a smile.</p>
<p>The day was perfect, sunny with a cool breeze, in which respect we we[re] fortunate seeing that the men on shore the previous day had had a dull grey day, with a very sudden &amp; rather severe rain squall during the afternoon.</p>
<p>Attended to some business (the purchase of writing paper &amp; comforts for men) &amp; then called on Uncle Walter, staying with him about 10 min. After lunch walked out to the Botanic Gardens &amp; greatly enjoyed the stroll through, part of the time being accompanied by one of the Directors' assistants to whom I made myself known. The West Austn Red Gums (Eucalyptus calophylla &amp; E. ficifolia) were specially interesting, being in full bloom. Flowers are about 1&frac12; in[ches] in diameter, the clusters of stamens differing in colour in the varieties from cream to pink, scarlet, crimson, &amp; red. The crimson one in the distance looked very much like the N.Z. Christmas Bush (Metrosideros tomentosa). The short time I had was spent among the eucalypts chiefly, tho' a lagoon containing many very beautiful water-lilies in full bloom &amp; very gorgeous, also attracted attention.</p>
<p>At 3 o'clock had to tram back to Flinders St to catch the train to Box Hill. The first part of the journey proved rather uninteresting, &amp; as a result of the guard work I found myself constantly dropping off to sleep. Nearing Box Hill where glimpses of the Doncaster orchards were caught</p>
