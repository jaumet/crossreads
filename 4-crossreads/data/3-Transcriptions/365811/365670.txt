<p>2<br/>in the colour photograph line. The weather is perfect in between rainy spells. &ndash; warm spring days &ndash; wonderfully clear atmospherically.</p>
<p>I have written Dorothy in regard to my visit to Jerusalem &amp; in any case it was not an incident that impressed me very greatly. But I have some few bits of needlework stuff from there that I am going, when an opportunity offers, to send to Mrs. Ferguson. I shall have to wait a chance to pack them but if you get some such stuff one of these days you will guess whence it came.</p>
<p>I was in Port Said, Suez &amp; Cairo on a round trip a few weeks ago. Only a day in each of the two former &amp; four days in Cairo. It was a good change but I cant say I enjoy Cairo by myself. If one had a congenial companion a good time is easy to obtain but alone its awful &amp; I was glad I was at least partly on duty which kept me occupied.</p>
<p>I have not seen Blackwell but the 1st Brigade are camped near by now &amp; I may get over in the next day or two. Saw Beaver in Suez &amp; he told me Alfie Rofe had been there &amp; gone on a week previously. I was sorry not to have known. Probably I should not have been able to get down any sooner but I would have wired him at any rate.</p>
