<p>When he got these returned to his boat, and we started going again.  Ahead could be seen a bank of fog.  By 8am we were in amongst it, and could not see &#188; a ships length ahead.  The whistle sounded a warning every few minutes.  Away in the distance could be heard other ships blowing their warnings.  By 7.30 it had cleared away <strike>&amp; the coast of England could be seen ahead</strike>.  At 8am heard Lands End calling warships &amp; informing them a steamer had been chased by a sub &amp; caught, &amp; the sub was shelling her.  At noon we were abeam of Beachy Head the white chalk cliffs with a background of green fields looked lovely.  Away to the Westward Newhaven could be seen &amp; to the North, Eastbourne.  Beachy Head itself is a Cape on which there is a lighthouse &amp; signal station.  We signalled the signal station &amp; passed</p>
