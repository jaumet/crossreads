<p>deafening &ndash; cant talk &ndash; nor can you imagine our Bulgarian hill climbing necessities &ndash; The usual rumour-o-grams are passed from mouth to mouth all day &ndash; though I generally scatch my pointed beard &amp; wait for confirmation.</p>
<p>Miss the swimming up here but have obtained permission for small morning &amp; afternoon parties to go down &amp; wash &amp; rinse their clothes &ndash; often they are covered in blood from gravel &amp; bullet scratches.</p>
<p>I have not heard any tidings of Geof yet. Have not seen the Sicilia again &ndash; She was at Hellas Cape you recollect &amp; I suppose ([rations?] on board &ndash; We have our own ships coming daily. Most slight wounds &amp; breaks go to Mudros in Lemnos &ndash; sick &amp; some wounded to Malta &ndash; &amp; bad wounds etc to Alex.</p>
<p>Extraordinarily hard to write a decent letter &ndash; &amp; I hope you'll understand &ndash; one gets cramped &ndash; &amp; every minute a shower of dirt spurts all over the paper from some rotten Turk who has the ridge above me marked &amp; [indecipherable] hits the top every time.</p>
<p>The men's humour is good &ndash; the Cairo restaurants are frequently alluded to &ndash; Parbury, Keyes &amp; many Scone boys are well &ndash; one was hit on the head last night but is quite well.</p>
<p>Corporal Gabriel</p>
