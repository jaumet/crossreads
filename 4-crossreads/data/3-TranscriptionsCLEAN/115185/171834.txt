The sea is gloriously smooth. I slept from two to four after lunch. I feel it almost a duty to get the benefit of these last days before our work begins. The officers and the sisters have not spoken to each other. I do not know if they should or shouldn't. Our Colonel looks after us well and the Captain of the ship & the Commanding officer have discussed our comfort with Mrs Wilson, the Matron - and are very considerate for us.
