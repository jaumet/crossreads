<p>Received beautiful letters from Claude &amp; Home. Big Naval Guns shake the billet.</p>
<p>7 September 1916 Thur<br/>Reveille 6 am. The big Guns are going for their life at different intervals throughout the day. It has been on the whole a very quiet day. I went to the estaminet with Cliff &amp; Dick at dinner time &amp; partook of a few Belgium Beers. I went on a message to headquarters at Poperinge at 7.30 in one of the cars. Brought the mail back. [Text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>8 September 1916 Fri<br/>Started night work again which is a very good job here. I slept nearly all day &amp; before tea went down to the old Belgium Brewery. C Section passes through on their way to the Asylum . Ernie Doust whilst driving one of the motor Ambulances was wounded just outside Ypres to-night. He has been evacuated.</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>Along the road passed the cream of the Enlish Army.</p>
