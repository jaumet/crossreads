<p>26 February 1918 Tuesday (X to X crossed out)<br/>Left Camp at dawn, lunched at<strike> Akik</strike> in an olive grove weather quite warm compared to the Hills-  Miles of Barley Crops are now up 6 inches high-  Our government are providing the Bedouins with the seed free &amp; all ploughing is done with the old wooden plough<br/>X We will be doing nothing now for some weeks as horses are suffering from stunt.<br/>[indecipherable] on arrival back in our Camp at Richon-le-Zion.<br/>Love to all &amp; I hope this travels safely-X</p>
