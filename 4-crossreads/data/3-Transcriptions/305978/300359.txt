<p>backing them up. In case the camp is shelled we have orders to saddle up etc without noise, then file down to the sea &amp;  take cover under a great sand dunne.<br/>Our squadron is on duty today dispatch riding gaurds etc, There are plenty of shells flying overhead, making some noise as they go. I don't  know a great bombardment must sound like at the Dardanelles or in France but I expect I will know before I reach home.<br/>We hear very little news</p>
