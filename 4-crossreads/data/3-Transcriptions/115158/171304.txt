The Admiral decided that as her full speed was only 14 knots, &amp; we ourselves had to proceed at 17 knots, she should be scuttled &amp; sunk by gunfire.&nbsp; Accordingly her crew were made prisoners, &amp; I was sent on board to scuttle the ship.&nbsp; My party consisted of 3 Engine Room artificiers &amp; 2 Carpenter mates; each armed with a double ended spanner, shifting jaw spanner, &amp; a heavy hammer.&nbsp; Arming myself with revolver &amp; ammunition I was taken over in a cutter to the ship &amp; I climbed on board.&nbsp; To get into a cutter on a broad ocean swell is not an easy feat &amp; to get on board a ship from a cutter up a Jacobs ladder in the same circumstances, is equally difficult.&nbsp; Once on board, I ordered the Chief Engineer to accompany me, &amp; show me where the sea connections were in the Engine Room.&nbsp; Fortunately, the Cheif Engineer, an exceedingly stout man, spoke very good English.&nbsp; I first assured myself that all ports, doors, &amp; hatches were open &amp; that everybody was on deck.&nbsp; All boilers were in use, &amp; the dynamo was running for the ship&#39;s lights.&nbsp; After opening all the furnace doors, I opened the door leading to No. 1 Hold.&nbsp; Here was a lot of machinery parts, hidden under sacks.&nbsp; Personally, I formed the opinion that this ship was on her way with stores</p>
