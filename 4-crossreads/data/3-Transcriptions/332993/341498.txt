<p>1051 Tuesday June 19, 1917</p>
<p>The weather is very close &amp; sultry with occasional loud peals of thunder, and a little rain.<br/>Enemy aeroplanes were about at daylight this morning &amp; were engaged by ours active air craft artillery.<br/>The enemy also shelled Steenwerck this morning but with what result I have not heard, but I anticipate nothing serious.<br/>Another Zeppelin has been brought down in flames in an East coast field.<br/>A  Kent town was heavily bombed before dawn .  The casualties are 2 killed &amp; the injured.<br/>At a monster meeting in London a unanimous motion for air raid reprisals was carried and immediate action demanded. This resolution was sent to the King &amp; the Premier, there were dramatic incidents and stirring speeches.</p>
