<p>all our bones every time.  We kept very warm for we had extra clothes which we had made a great effort to carry.  We had 19 hours in the train passing Amiens &amp; Rouen.  We saw many ruined Houses in the former place &amp; could see the trenches &amp; shell holes stretching away on either side.  We arrived at our base about 10.30 &amp; Roy Tredrea, my old pal, was the Sergeant Major to receive us.  He gave us a royal welcome &amp; we asked Him to march us out to our Hospital at once if He could.  He worked very hard &amp; was fortunate enough to get all arrangements made with the result that we all marched out again at 5 p.m., thereby saving endless messing around</p>
