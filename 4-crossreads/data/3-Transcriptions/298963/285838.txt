<p>14th<br/>Infantry "hopped over" at 4 a.m. &amp; took 1000 yds., fritz no where to be seen.  A few shells landed on our right &amp; left, the day was quiet.</p>
<p>15th<br/>Midnight was barely passed when fritz began a barage all along our front.  Shells fell everywhere &amp; I thought our hour had come.  We lay in our dugouts &amp; waited &ndash; what more could we do?  This murderous fire contd. till day break toward which I said the first prayer for many a long day.  At 4 a.m. the enemy "came over" in massed formation but after appaling losses retired without any gain.</p>
<p>I hear some say they lie 3 &amp; 4 deep in front of our trench.  Prisoners told us that 3 regiments came all the way from Cambria to attack at this point.  While I write the shells</p>
