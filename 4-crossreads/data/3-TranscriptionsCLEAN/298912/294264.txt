I happened to be above & felt the stern rising unusually high & a moment later there was a great row down below as the wave came in & swamped the deck.  Previous to this a great number of the men were holding their heads & feeling queer (you know) after the pork dinner. Brother Pierce gets quite angry when we say he looks bad.He was as white as a sheet & said he felt crook, but when antone says he is seasick he gets quite annoyed & swears it is only the pork. An hour later the men rush to the side & let go. One fellow made a terrible rush & ran into the railing with such force that it assisted him nicely. Laughter from the "old sea dogs" rang out everywhere & I must say that I felt as lively as a cricket

& found plenty of amusement. For tea only five turned out of ten because of the pork, & also because there is cheese for tea. I did not object, as it meant more for us & less washing up. After tea an amusing mystery is cleared up. Ever since Ivers, Houston & Symons entered the troop they have stuck together like glue.They always walk down for meals, never say a word to anyone other than their little band - called the three angels by the rest - but simply eat their meals & then walk out again, one never seeing or hearing of them throughout the day. Through a peculiar incidence caused by stray papers we find that they get away together & write effusions, some example of the stuff being found by one of our troop, evidently written by Houston.
