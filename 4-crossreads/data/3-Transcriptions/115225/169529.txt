<p>Feb.6<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Frank &amp; I went up to Suzanne&#39;s in the morning to learn a little bit of French. there was a family of three Mother &amp; two daughters aged twelve &amp; fifteen respectively &nbsp;<s>Suzanne &amp;</s>&nbsp;Lucienne &amp; Suzanne, they used to keep open house to the soldiers &amp; sell coffee at 1<sup>d</sup>&nbsp;per time. they always had a warm fire &amp; a smile for the boys &amp; we appreciate a place to spend a few dull minutes round the home fire - Joe went to Blighty on leave. I went to Meteren in the afternoon to see Jack as it is his birthday - Coming home at night I caught up the fellows on their way home all boozed &amp; walked with them - they had been stopped by the Military police in Bailleul but had given a good account of themselves &amp; got away -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Feb.7<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;The bunch are feeling a bit dopey after last night&#39;s jolification - Bud went to Blighty on special leave to be married - Frank came on with me as second driver as Jim is in hospital in Blighty &amp; expects not to be back again, thinks he can work it for Aussie.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Feb. 8<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Last night was very warm. to-day</p>
