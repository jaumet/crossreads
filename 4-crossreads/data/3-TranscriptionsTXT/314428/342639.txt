Sunday 28 May 25. 1919. 195Cloudy this morning but fine in the afternoon. I had a look around the grounds this morning. The Red Cross has a large building here containing recreation, reading & writing rooms also tea rooms. The Y.M.C.A. have similar buildings. All the wards are kept scrupiously clean and neat.
Monday May 26 1919. 196A fine day.
I saw an eye specialist this morning at 10 and I am to go to Ward 18 tomorrow for the glasses.  I saw several that I knew, Corporal D. Varden of the 37 Btln. Who was a prisoner in Germany and Walton Moor of Heywood who is now a corporal with a decoration.
I wrote to Wally and also to my wife today. [written in shorthand]Tuesday May 27 1919. 197.A beautiful day. This afternoon I went into the city by train to see one of Sir Harry Lauder's concerts in the Kings Theatre. It was a real treat.
