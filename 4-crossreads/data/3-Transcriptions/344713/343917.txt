<p>Numerous tanks have been busted up around this place.<br/>Bapaume is about 3 miles on the top of furthest ridge. Houses, Chimney Stacks &amp;  church-steeple open to the eye.<br/>Shells passing over us both ways night &amp; day but not many coming near us.<br/>"Fritz" shells the knocked-out village of Flers to prevent us getting supplies of water from the wells there but we get all we want just the same.<br/>I think we only do 3 days in supports here &amp; one day in line.<br/>It is heartbreaking in the mud here, after a short while you are absolutely caked from head to foot.</p>
<p>Fri. 3rd. Nov. 1916: -<br/>Took over firing line from A Coy. about 4 A.M. this morning. "Fritz" is supposed to be from 500 to 800 yds from us, but we don't know where, neither does he know where we are &ndash; judging by the scarcity of "iron-rations" around our position &amp; plenty on supports behind.<br/>We are directly in front of the Bapaume Ridge &ndash; the town showing up between the trees &amp; a little village in front of it to our right all intact though no doubt when "Fritz" gets shunted back he will knock that lot over.
