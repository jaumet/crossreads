<p>On board the boat the Amputation cases were provided with an orderly to look after their wants The Cabins were on deck so that in the event of a submarine attack we could be quickly transferred to the boats I had no dressing whilst on board The cabin was neatly appointed The walls painted white There were two berths in my cabin we lay at Calais about 12 hours</p>
<p>Upon landing in England German carriers were detailed to carry us from the boat "Brighton" to the waiting Red Cross Trains We soon started for our destination</p>
