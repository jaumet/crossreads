<p>comforts that one does not get in a Field Ambulance. Plenty of water for washing purposes, hot showers, good tucker &amp; a decent table to eat it off, plenty of blankets to sleep on etc etc. I am attached to the Orderly Room registering deaths ( averaging about 18 per day) &ndash; not a very strenuous job. The weather has changed suddenly and is now cold, wet and windy. Winter has commenced! And what a prospect! Memories of last winter rise up before me.</p>
<p>Sund. 7th Oct 1917 (9 pm) :-</p>
<p>The weather is continuing rotten and the days are rapidly getting shorter. At 1 am after midnight to-day we reverted to winter time; all clocks &amp; watches being put back one hour. Now it is dark at 7 o'clock in the evening.</p>
<p>Tuesd. 16th Oct 1917 (7 pm)</p>
<p>Things have been going quietly for the past few days. Our chaps had several hopovers with very heavy casualties but little progress made. The ground is a perfect quagmire</p>
