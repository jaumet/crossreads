  
 (15) The dull silver of the sky is as a wall. Against it the ragged tree shapes at regular intervals and the line of waggon hoods closer spaced but of equal regularity combine with dark masses and doubtful edges to form a strange frieze, fretted, as it were, out of shadow on a gigantic scale. Now we have left the slumbering wagons behind and are marching on. Here we are passing almost the same scene. But the long sleeping line is part of a supply column this time.
