<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">Hebron</span><br /><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">Hebron one of the oldest cities in Palestine. Josephus says in his day it had been founded 2300 yrs&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">Home of burial place of the Patriarchs. David resided there for 1st 7 yrs of his reign. As City of Abraham it is called by Mohammedans Al Halil (City of friend of God). Upon traditional burial place of Abraham, Isaac &amp; Jacob, a magnificent church was erected by Empress Helena. Subsequently this was converted into a mosque accessible only to Mohammedans. A special firman [decree] of Sultan of Turkey was required for the admittance of the Prince of Wales in 1862. Marquis of Bute 1866, &amp; Crown Prince of Prussia in 1869, Population 18,000 chiefly Mohammedan. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.6em;">It lies low in a narrow valley&nbsp;&ndash; Valley of Eshol [Eshcol]&nbsp;&ndash; famous now as of old for its grapes.</span></p>
