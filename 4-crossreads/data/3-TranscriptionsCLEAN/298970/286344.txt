Therefore we really did render great assistance to our other divisions at Pozieres by holding reinforcements up in the North.  It is said that the Germans brought up 11 divisions for this encounter.  Whatever results were attained ours was the first Australian Battle in France.  Disgust and feelings of angry disappointment reigned for days afterwards when a report something like this appeared in the "Daily Mail".  "A raid was carried out south of Armentieres in which Australians took par.  We captured 140 prisoners".  What an insult! The total casualties numbered 7800 out of no more than 1200 troops if there were that many.
