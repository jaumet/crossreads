<p>we passed along streets fearfully damaged, occasionally meeting a disconsolate old man or woman standing by their doorways, and looking alike the survivors of an earthquake. "Bon Soir, Monsieur," was their usual and only expression. The footpaths were encumbered with wreckage. In one house I saw the front wall gashed terribly. The second floor had been shifted to an angle of 45 degrees, and hung in mid-air, supporting a mass of bricks, plaster, and distorted <strike>fu</strike> bedroom furniture.
