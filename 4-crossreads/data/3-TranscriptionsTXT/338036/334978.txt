The positions were evacuated during night &amp; became probably No mans land a couple of days later as the flanks had had not advanced Chas Hamblin witnessed two shells lob on to one of our parties &amp; blew the whole thing to atoms. 
 Nothing of interest during the night but I read some mail that came during my absence. 
 Thursday 15th Slept until 6 pm.  Meeting of sports Committee at 6 pm.  Saw Arthur Mealey tonight.  Quite [quiet?] night. 
 Friday 16th Meeting of sports Committee at 3.45. 
 Saturday 17th Was quite surprised to see young "Swinny" [Swinnerton] (now a Sgt) Gordon Dawkins &amp; L/C McCure who all reported to the Ambulance for duty today. 
 Sunday 18th In the afternoon John Perry &amp; self journeyed to L'Estrade D.R.S. &amp; met Clarrie Power (Corp M.M.) of the [Water] Board who I had not seen for some time &ndash; 5th Fld Amb.  Also met Jimmy O'Sullivan late of the 9th.  On the road saw Bert Crooke &amp; directed him to the Ambulance for particulars of Sams death.  He had been in the lines at Ypres been gassed
