<p>as many N.C.O's are short owing to the 1914 men going away, there are also 34 men on U.K. leave &amp; the unit is much understrength.<br/> Wednesday 25th Sept.<br/> Carrying out duties of W.O. Prepared lists of bearer squads etc &amp; very busy trying to rake enough men together Officers &amp; others treating me well. Helped the other dispenser until he got a grasp of things &amp; will now leave this work to him as all is square. Grave trouble over our 54th .Battalion. Each brigade has 4 battalions &amp; it has recently been decided to reduce the battalion to 3. In our division the 54th. were chosen to be disbanded but the men refused to go. All officers &amp; N.C.O.'s left but the men have since carried on in the camp,keeping excellent order, drilling etc &amp; doing everything as if their Colonel was in charge.
