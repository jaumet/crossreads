<p>1917<br/>Dec. 9<br/>Communion.  Have lost my voice.</p>
<p>Dec. 10 &amp; 11<br/>Off parade &ndash; light duty.</p>
<p>Dec. 12<br/>Getting better but quality of rations nauseating.</p>
<p>Dec. 13<br/>Left Tingry with Lt. Mackenzie as advance party to Courset.  H.Q. at Courset, Companies at Sacriquier.  Not feeling too well so hired room &ndash; clean sheets &amp; plenty of eiderdown quilts.  Oh for peace!</p>
<p>Dec. 14<br/>Bn. arrd. at 11.30.  We had provided billets for everyone but the guard &amp; I soon fixed 'em up.  The Colonel was billetted at a Chateau owned by the Baron de Givenchy &amp; has to take his boots off before going upstairs to his room!  I have lived on the best for two days but the issue rations were awful.</p>
<p>Dec. 15<br/>Mde. Fournier provided a fine breakfast this morning &amp; I then adjusted the billetting claims.  Started off to catch the battalion which had left earlier &amp; was lucky enough to get a lift in a car to Blequin via Senleques.
