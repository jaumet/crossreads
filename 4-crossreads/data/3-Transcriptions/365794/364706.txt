<p>Tuesday 30 October 1917<br/>S. Moreton on day duty &amp; my cup of humiliation is complete.  Why oh why did I not leave her business alone!  Now I am justly punished, she is kind but oh, so cold &amp; when I weep for a sight of Dr. F. says, "you will will get well quite as well without seeing him".  I weep most of morning &amp; feel relieved &ndash; T. 102, 102.4, etc., P. 84, R. 26.<br/>Thorough exam of chest again by Capt. C., now they incline to Ty.<br/>Lady Super. Miss Oram comes to see me, is kind &amp; cool.  Miss German most sympathetic &amp; sub Matron.  Miss &hellip; also kind.  Capt. C. says they must again trouble me with a blood spec. this time Lieu. Crowe appears, questions me closely &amp; to Aust. &amp; I find knows T'mba.  Ligature is now placed on R upper arm &amp; hypo syringe of blood taken from vein at elbow &amp; poured into bottle with thick fluid.<br/>A glorious thing happens &ndash; Cobbie bursts in 8 p.m. &amp; just her old self.  Oh the joy of seeing a home face.</p>
