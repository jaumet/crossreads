<p>in good time &amp; Jim took me round to show me all the places of Interest, for this being my Mothers &amp; Fathers native City, there were a few places I was glad to see.</p>
<p>I had the experience of going over one of the biggest cotton mills, "The Warwick.", in England &amp; it simply flattened me to see the machinery in that building, &amp; the process the cotton goes through is simply wonderful. The cotton goes in one end from the bales &amp; comes out 6 floors up on reels ready for the shops. Of course there are girls on every floor looking for &amp; mending breaks Etc, but still not doing anything in the way of Spinning.</p>
<p>I think I can safely say I was the first Australian to get out to Middleton, a suburb of Manchester, and many were the arguments I heard as to what nationality I was, or from what Dominion I came. Some said I was a Yank, Canadian, or South African, in fact anything but an Ausie.</p>
