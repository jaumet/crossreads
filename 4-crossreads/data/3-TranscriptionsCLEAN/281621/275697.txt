
Sunday 6Through the ordinary channels to [indecipherable] & then in Hospital train.
Monday 7To St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital at Etaples.
Tuesday 8Still at St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital where we are having glorious weather.  In my case the M.O. is frightened of bleeding since the bracheal vein has been divided & I cannot be moved to England for about a week.
Wednesday 9From the papers it is apparent that the fighting round Bullecourt & Riencourt is of a very important nature & heavy & mixed fighting seems to be continuous.The Australians have done all asked of them.
Thursday 10At Etaples yet & the warm weather continues.
Saturday 12Passed for EnglandSister Higgins St John Ambulance B.H. Etaples.
