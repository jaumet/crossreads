<p>was serenely peaceful.  At Boulogne Capt. Bean was awaiting us with two cars &amp; we conveyed all our goods to safe storage. After an excellent meal - by the way the French bread is far superior to our British War loaf - we left for Headquarters, which are situated at Hazebrouck. Even here one is scarcely conscious of a war, except for the great numbers of transports &amp; Khaki men.  To me the ride was filled with interest. Everywhere tiny villages were surrounded by closely cultivated lands, &amp; the golden grain, partly mowed, lay in long rows of sheaves, with here &amp; there great conical ricks. The roads were in excellent order &amp; planted on each side with long rows of poplar &amp; other trees. Darkness soon came on &amp; before reaching St. Omer we took a wrong turning</p>
