<p>We then went out again &amp; round the back to Joseph's Well. This well is where Joseph stored water during a famine in Cairo years ago, also where corn was stored. We entered a kind of cow shed &amp; dropped through a hole in the floor. A native followed us, demanding &frac12; a disaster to go down. He had but little success however &amp; we groped our way down a dark &amp; very slippery, steep incline. It was pitch dark &amp; deathly quiet. At last we came to a small window that opened into the well. This well is 15 feet square &amp; drops sheer for 350 feet to the water. Bats abound here, &amp; there is an uncanny appearance about the place. The well is cut out of the solid rock all the way &amp; is a marvellous piece of work.
