<p>Saturday 12 February 1916</p>
<p>A bright clear day, hot in the sun but with a fairly cool breeze blowing from the N most of the time. The sea was most wonderfully calm, quite glassy , with not even a swell on. The waves made by the ship were the only ones visible &amp;amp: it was very hard to realise that we were out at sea &amp;amp: not moored alongside a wharf in a harbour.</p>
<p>Parades were held &amp;amp: were chiefly devoted to checking over &amp;amp: fitting equipment etc so as to be ready for disembarkment tomorrow. No drill was done in our company.</p>
<p>About 10 o'clock low rugged ranges of eastern Egypt came in sight on the port side &amp;amp: we kept them in sight during the day. About half a dozen vessels were in sight at the time &amp;amp: during the day several more were seen, in one case only funnel &amp;amp: 2 masts being visible, floating on the horizon.</p>
<p>In the afternoon the mountains of Sinai Peninsula came in sight on the other side. Forgot to mention that abt 10 o'c in the morning 2 flat sandstone (?) tables of rock rising perhaps 20 ft above sea-level, one of which had a lighthouse on it, were passed on the starboard bow: they are known as "The Brothers".</p>
