<p>7 October 1916 Sat<br/>Fairly quiet day save for the usual few [indecipherable] put over down by the road. It is 8.30 pm. now &amp; next door in a large dug-out of infantry the strains of My Little grey home in the west come through my door. Poor beggars they long to [indecipherable] but many of them will never</p>

<p>8 October 1916 Sun<br/>Capt McClelland whilst having a yarn to him I find out he is a brother to Captain McClelland of Newtown. On our right a big boom bombardment is going on lasting something like three or four hours. It sounds to be down St Eloi way.</p>
