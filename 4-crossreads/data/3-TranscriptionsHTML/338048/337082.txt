<p>Diary of Active Service Abroad of Sapper S. Liebert.</p>
<p>1916 May <strike>26</strike>27<br/>Revielle at 4-30 a.m., dressed in marching orders returned surplus equipment. Was picked out as mess orderly and got breakfast at 5.30 am after roll calls on parade ground. I got some cold meat &amp; toast as the dixie in which stew was obtained was dirty. Rolled overcoat instead of wearing it so as to accommodate pyjamas. Left camp, was cheered by Sig School &amp; train left Broadmeadows about 8 am.</p>
<p>Arrived Flinders St where engine changes after waving to people en route. People on platform shook hands gave us apples biscuits magazines &amp; newspapers. Saw Mother &amp; Father &amp; all from train going to New pier Port Melbourne from which we <strike>were to</strike> embarked after roll call on pier &amp; distribution of cards re pay &amp; waiting. Messes arranged in mess room &amp; on the word we were allowed on deck.</p>
<p>There was a great rush &amp; I got a good position. Lass soon sighted me &amp; communication shortly opened. Received parcel food &amp; [indecipherable] of papers for chap in 39th. Mother Father Dais Will Lass Amanda Harvey Walter Mrs Sims Bert Rudolph present to see me off. Saw Mr &amp; Mrs Blackmore. Streamers of which I had five presented a very beautiful appearance. Boat moved off about 1.30 p.m. the pier look very pretty &amp; animated with flags</p>
