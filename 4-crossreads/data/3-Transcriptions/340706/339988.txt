<p>34<br/>siding to allow a passenger express with dining saloon &amp; wine on tables filled with civilians to pass through. Peace is not yet signed &amp; yet civilians are taking not equal rights with soldiers but the preference.</p>
<p>At 3.45 our train moves out &amp; at 8 pm has done 18 KM &amp; anchors for the next 6 hours outside the La Bassee Canal. Night is clear &amp; cold but as I have blankets in my valise I am quite comfy. All this day I have had nothing to eat when I bethink me of the bread &amp; butter I got in Brussels &amp; a pot of cheddar cheese in my valise &amp; this constitutes all the meals I have this day.</p>
<p>I have an ASC officer with me but otherwise the carriage a 1st class is to ourselves &amp; we can stretch out</p>
<p>10 January 1919<br/>Train is alternately crawling &amp; halting &amp; at Calonne Ricquart. When all my friends leave me to catch fast train for which we have been side tracked. Having my valise I remain as it is too much trouble to move it. This day there is nothing to eat &amp; though train often halts 3 to 4 hours yet she may go at any minute &amp; it is not safe to move away from train.</p>
