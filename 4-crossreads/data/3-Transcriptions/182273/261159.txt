
<p>1918&nbsp;&nbsp; August</p>
<p>29 THUR&nbsp; Fine day. Warm. further</p>
<p>advance all along the</p>
<p>line. Moved off at 2. PM.</p>
<p>&amp; bivouacked near Estrees</p>
<p>Guns well forward. Very quiet.</p>
<p>Little opposition. Inf. crossed Somme.</p>
<p>Guns pulled out at 12 P.M. Quiet</p>
<p>Reveille 4 am. Moved off at 6 am.</p>
<p>30 FRI&nbsp; Heard Peronne had fallen.</p>
<p>Passed through Foucaucourt, Proyart etc.</p>
<p>&amp; camped near Sailly Laurette. Moved</p>
<p>in aft on River Bank. Fixing up</p>
<p>camp. Rec. 3 letters from Else P.</p>
<p>1. Gladys C. Quiet night.</p>
<p>31 SAT&nbsp; Fine day rain on &amp; off. Fixing</p>
<p>Camp. Rode over to Behencourt</p>
<p>in aft. Did not see Tense. Saw</p>
<p>Mme Le Ferme. called at Bavelin-</p>
<p>court came home via La Houssaye</p>
<p>Bonnay &amp; Vause&nbsp; Rec. letters</p>
<p>from Reg C. Amy. Card</p>
<p>from Julie &amp; letter Tense.</p>
<p>Home. 9 P.M.</p>
