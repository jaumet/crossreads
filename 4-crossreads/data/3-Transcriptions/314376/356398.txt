<p>87<br/>with expressions of goodwill. They were a cheery lot despite the fact that they were to "hop over" so they thought the next day.</p>
<p>Coming back to our camp we had to move up to the line that night and it proved to be one of the worst experiences I ever had. It was at Flers. Whilst camped at the Mud Hill the men received a lot of letters that had been written by the women of Australia at the instigation of Chaplain Major MacKenzie, the "padre" of the 4th Battn. They were much appreciated by the lonely soldiers.</p>
