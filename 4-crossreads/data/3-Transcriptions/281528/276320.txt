<p>16/4/18 &ndash; Lavieville<br/>There was a heavy bombardment by our guns just at dawn and the roar was terrific.  Dawn was ushered in by this and the heavy reply of the enemy.  Our ration parties still continue to be upset and stuff does not reach its destination.  Aspinall was in charge of the rations when he was killed.  The General came in and discussed barrages and S.O.S.  Several artillery officers on the same errand.  After lunch a fair sized shell landed fair on the corner of our shelter knocking in a great deal of earth on us and burying many things.  Except for being a bit bruised and shaken</p>
