<p>it in, using a 3 wheeled vehicle and driving with one rein.  All driving on the farms of N. France too, is done with one rein, and axle-grease seems to be an unknown quantity.  All along the roads may be seen little shrines, and crucifixes, one we passed was dated 1649.</p>
<p>Windmills are still largely used for power one not far from here is over 200 years old and is still working.  In many respects, the people are miles &amp; miles behind the times.  The girls still use the wooden yolk across their shoulders for carrying water and use the flail for threshing.  One seldom sees a two-storied building, and in all cases the colour scheme is white-washed</p>
