<p>things the University Buildings, which are at present used as a hospital for soldiers, we also went through Bournville, Cadbury's model village, Chamberlain's residence, and much of the English country areas. We then went for tea to Mr L. Sames' home &amp; spent a very pleasant hour or so there.</p>
<p>In the evening we motored back to town &amp; the three of us occupied a box at one of the Variety Theatres &amp; enjoyed the programme very much. Oyster supper at Pape's Hotel followed &amp; Alan &amp; I then dodged off to the Soldiers' Club &amp; arrived there by midnight to find that all the beds were taken.</p>
<p>Nothing perturbed we got two blankets &amp; a cushion each for 3d &amp; slept on the floor of the billiard room in company with a score or more of other "swaddies" as happy as could be.</p>
