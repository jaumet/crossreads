 A newspaper was published on board, which did not fail to find a ready sale.&nbsp; Sports were held, to which entries were not lacking.&nbsp; Whist drives were well attended.&nbsp; Dances, sometimes fancy dress, sometimes plain - were always popular.&nbsp; Concerts nearly every Saturday night were given by the &quot;Kigoma Stars&quot; which were always very well patronised.&nbsp; Everything was done to make the trip [handwritten amendment of t] as enjoyable as possible. 
 On arrival at Fremantle, a few are on the wharf.&nbsp; I am met by my own people, but as my pay duties are not completed it is necessary for me to continue on the boat to Sydney.&nbsp; We remain for a couple of days in W.A. and then proceed to Melbourne where we stay about a day and then on to Sydney where we arrive in the dusk of evening. 
 This is the first time I have seen the harbour.&nbsp; I had heard a lot about Sydney Harbour and the impression it at once created on me was that its beauty was beyond expressing.&nbsp; Victorians boast of their marvellous Melbourne.&nbsp; New South Welshmen boast of their harbour.&nbsp; Melbourne is indeed a very fine city, it&#39;s streets well planned and laid out, it&#39;s buildings substantial and noble, it&#39;s parks and gardens beautiful.&nbsp; Yes! the Victorians have something to boast about.&nbsp; Melbourne is indeed a very fine city.&nbsp; Sydney Harbour. - well! words cannot express it&#39;s beauty.&nbsp; Speech cannot convey it&#39;s grandeur.&nbsp; Pictures fail to reproduce it&#39;s splendour.&nbsp; One must see it, and travel on some of the ferry boats, to appreciate this wonderful work of nature.
