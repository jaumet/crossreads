<p>12<br/>evening &amp; then walk back. The French villa is a square edifice ugly but well placed back from the road &amp; seems like a box in a paddock ie in this part. On arrival in camp receive orders to leave for battalion to morrow.</p>
<p>31 December 1918<br/>Unable to leave camp as we are under orders. During morning draft is cancelled &amp; then is changed to a stand by &amp; finally we report 7 pm to Camp Adjutant No 12 camp &ndash; get into motor lorries &amp; whizz off to station Gare des Maritimes &amp; find the train a collection of goods wagons with one 3rd class carriage &amp; 1 first class with the windows broken.</p>
<p>I find myself in a 3rd The 3rd class here either are a long carriage with wooden seats &amp; backs or else these backs are continued for 5 ft &amp; thus make some little privacy. However sleep save from exhaustion is impossible The soldiers are happier because they can stretch their limbs at full length &amp; have a brazier whilst officers must perish as compartment is too small for a brazier</p>
