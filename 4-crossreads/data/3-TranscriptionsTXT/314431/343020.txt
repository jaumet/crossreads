wood  Shells had smashed this wood, torn the branches of trees or broken them from the trunks, smashed many to matchwood, &amp; those that had withstood the rage of battles were almost blackened stumps with a few boughs still hardy enough to retain their greenery.  Not a building was left intact upon the mount, not a roof stood upon its walls.  Walls had been thrown down bodily.  Many were gapped &amp; torn &amp; large holes were knocked in out of the broken walls
