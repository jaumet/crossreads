These indispensable mechanised transports become the very homes of those who drive them. There is a driver and an attendant to each. As we pass them now we know each beetle-hooded shape hides two sleeping men, and we envy them their slumber and their peace. There is a dim light in one or two of these waggons but there is not a sound down the long line of them as we march by. That hushed hour has come when all things acquire a weird presence.
