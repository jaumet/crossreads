<p>Dec 14<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Very warm to-day. but a fine misty rain was falling all day - went on supplies as usual. I swopped batteries with one of the other lorries, I now serve 18<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;batt. instead of 17<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;batt. Fred left for Boulogne with his lorry. Fritz shelled Hazebrouck heavily -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 15<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Beautiful day. but still wet underfoot - We did our supply trip - Jim had a liberal lot of rum up at the battery &amp; kept it up in the evening - The 5&nbsp;<s>A.S.P.</s><sup>D.S.C.</sup>&nbsp;have had several lorries catch fire during this month. some of them burnt pretty badly -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 16<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Cold &amp; windy. I had trouble starting my engine &amp; had to get a tow to start, the magneto is crook (Dixie) &nbsp;I carried on with the supplies the rest of the 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;D.S.C moved off to Boulogne to-day -</p>
