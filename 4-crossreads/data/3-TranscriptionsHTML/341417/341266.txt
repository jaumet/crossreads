<p>We have just finished one of the most successful "stunts" so far. Sort of, got a very good value for our expenditure. Sometimes even after a success, it is hard to read the Bill of Costs, in the shape of a casualty report where many a good comrade is included.</p>
<p>Along with the N. Zealanders &amp; Irish we took [dash]Ridge. Everything went off (besides the mines) with such precision that all objectives were reached &amp; at the arranged times too.</p>
<p>Not that it was a one-sided affair by any means. <strike>Not</strike> The Germans knew of it &amp; we knew that they knew. They also knew that we knew that they knew. Do you get me?</p>
<p>At present we are resting &amp; this bulletin is being pencilled in a loft over a farm house. The surroundings are varied by a collection of harness, timber, grain, farming implements &amp; the household furniture of refugees. I have not included the rats for they are accepted on the same terms as myself &ndash; lodgers.</p>
