<p>He was killed alongside his pals.  It was a brilliant moonlight night</p>
<p>Fritz gave us a few souvenirs in the way of shells. We somewhat ungracefully retired to our deep dugouts. We stayed in these homes till the 30th Sept 1917 (Sunday)</p>
<p>Leaving in batches, we finally reached Ypres a couple of hours after. We passed through Ypres and finally reached Halifax Camp, a rest camp some four miles from Ypres. We had a welcome bath the next day at divisional headquarters. We needed it too!</p>
<p>On Oct 1st 1917 we left this camp, in motor lorries, for new billets. We finally reached Steenvorde, (Belgium) A two kilometre march brought us to our new billets, some 2 miles from a fairly large Belgian town, Watou. We had the usual style of billet, haysheds.</p>
