Saturday March 30. 1918. 1335.Very quiet last night & no rain but cloudy. There is great enemy activity in the air this morning. & for several hours after day light our archies were in almost constant action on our right & the 6 in Howitchers on our left.
There is constant reciprocal activity all morning by the artillery. I had a good look at the Howitzers in action yesterday. The report behind the gun is ear splitting.
I had half a bottle of cider before breakfast & some after. I was detailed for a pack sprint yesterday, but it did not come off.
The enemy is shelling Heilly this morning. I was in the village yesterday for a few hours on duty. During this past few days there are persistent rumours that Ostend & Zeebruggie has fallen to the Americans
