<p>On St Johns Island</p>
<p>17th October. 1914<br/>Arrived in Singapore.This English colony is situated on a big island. We were taken in a small steamer and then a big tram to the other side of the island to Singapore. From there we went to St John's Is.<br/>Here we were greeted by several Germans . We were placed in camps. In camp 3, were besides myself others who had been employed as sailors &amp; stewards on English ships, Plantation Manager, two businessmen, the 4th Engineer, assistant Engr, the telegraphist and the cook from "Markomania" we were received well.</p>
<p>18th October 1914<br/>Life in the camp is lively and harmonious. The food is very good. Each man receives 6 eggs a day. In the evening we sit at a long table and play cards</p>
