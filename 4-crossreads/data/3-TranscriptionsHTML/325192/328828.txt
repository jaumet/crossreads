These sume wher dividet amongst 16 woundet &amp; Invalides of H.M.S. Emden. The first few rows in the Theather the Pris payd willingly &pound;2.10 &amp; a few Seats at the last Bench realized "after american auction &pound;11.6.6. The Programme wich got auctionered after the same style brought &pound;13.10. The result of the sale of Coffee and in Camp manufacured Cakes addet another &pound;6. These wher a shure sign that Germanes allways are willing to asist in the best of their ability, in case of need. I do not wishe the Readers to think that we all had a banking acount so could well afford to help. No. Allthough I give the wealthy all their Credit due, for they helped in a great Majority that the takings wher so high. However I also thank the Working Class wich brought all their hard earned shillings to the Ticketbox &amp; so assisted to the best of their ability in the Susccess. These Evening of the Benefits" will be a "memory" to live in my Memmory for</p>
