<p>1915<br/>Nov. 13 Sat.<br/>The Nile is the second longest continuous waterway in the world, 4037 miles. As practically no rain falls within its limits Egypt would cease to exist as a fertile country &amp; would become a desert valley, similar to those of the Sahara were it not for its constant supply of water from the Nile.</p>
