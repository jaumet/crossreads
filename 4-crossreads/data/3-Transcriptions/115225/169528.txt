
<p>Feb.2<sup><u>nd</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We went out on a coal stunt at 1.P.M. for 1<sup><u>st</u></sup>&nbsp;Anzac C.T.S.C. &amp; did one load. when we returned we parked up with the amunition section in a very deep muddy position &amp; are now attached to 1<sup><u>st</u></sup>&nbsp;A.S.P. &nbsp;- We have to be on parade at 7.30 A.M.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Memo. The weather has been colder since I returned from Paris. but not too bad &amp; we still have quite a bit of sun. There is a bit of strafing going on at intervals again.&nbsp;&mdash;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Feb. 3<sup><u>rd</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Fritz was over here three times last night &amp; dropped some bombs, but none within two miles of us - To-day has been fine &amp; moderately warm - I worked on the lorry in the morning. In the afternoon I went along to 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;A.S.P. &amp; saw some of the fellows I know.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Feb 4<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We went out at 10 A.M. to draw for 1<sup><u>st</u></sup>&nbsp;Anzac C.T.S.C. &nbsp;I had to have a tow to start my engine - It was a beautiful day just like spring. Joe was told to be ready for his Blighty leave</p>
