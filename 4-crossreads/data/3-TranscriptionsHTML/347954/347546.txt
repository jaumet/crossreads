<p>of old Cairo &amp; you also see the Nile in all its glory.  Nearby the dragoman will show the identical bush under which Moses floated &amp; mostly you are rewarded with a sprig which you are supposed to gratefully accept.  One little village, devoted to students &amp; their parents I liked very much.  This was Choubrah, over the railway section.  It is rather the Latin portion of Cairo &amp; provides many a little adventure worth recalling.  I almost invariably wandered along there during bankruptcy periods with either French or native Copts where we indulged in Floury cakes &amp; a glass of water at &frac12; a piastre.</p>
<p>During the latter portion of my stay in Cairo I developed a mania for automobiles &amp; a good deal of my money went in this direction.  The road to Cairo from Mena House Hotel is perfect for motoring &amp; the scenery en route is very varied.  I have described a portion elsewhere but it is worth recording the beauty of Avenue at present.  Sheets of quiet water, over which rustic bridges are placed &amp; the verdant green is everywhere to be seen.
