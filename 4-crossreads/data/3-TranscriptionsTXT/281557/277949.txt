 "rum jars" &amp; aireal torpedoes but we dodged them all: they have "some" concussion &amp; the torpedoes go in a good depth before bursting &amp; throw up a lot of stuff: mine exploded on our right a little after 8 pm  &amp; the ground did tremble some: Had a yarn with some of the Canadians down in their tunnel &amp; got a maple leaf off one of them: Fritz is very close to us here &amp; the flares come right over: a nice view can be had at the rear Ypres in the distance  water &amp; ruined houses in mid view &amp; desolation &amp; shell hole in near view: on our left is Sanctuary Wood where so many gallant Canadians lie buried: the trees are all stripped &amp; broken by shells: hill 60 is a little to our right: our stokes's gave Fritz a shake up in return for his "rum jars" &amp; we also sent over some heavy stuff: 
 Friday Sept 22nd  Trenches: fair day: got a "P" of Sanctuary Wood: plenty of our planes about &amp; one was very daring &amp; gave Fritz some target practice: our guns bogged a few heavy ones over in return for some whizz-bangs he gave us: Fritz put a flare into our "posi" 
 Saturday September 23rd Trenches: good day: quiet day few shells etc kicking around at night: Fritz put a flare into our "posi" again &amp; our dug out almost caught fire: Jack Coulter got sniped 
 Sunday Sept 24th  Trenches good day: carried poor old Jack down so as he could get a decent burial: it was hard luck for him after going through that Pozieres hell &amp; carrying all the time: trench mortar &amp; artillery dual in afternoon: our "apples on the stick" &amp; stokes's shook him up &amp; he replied with "rum jars" &amp; torpedoes: our bombers also gave him a few "mills grenades" to play with: 
 Monday Sept 25th  Trenches: good day: the nights are getting very cold now:
