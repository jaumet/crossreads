<p>Houses here and there were in complete ruins!</p>
<p>In due course, we reached this camp, We had a drink of tea served out to us and on we went again. Another four miles brought us to a transport camp. (Our camp the 50th)</p>
<p>We (most of us) for the first time saw shell fire in the distance, the thunder of guns and the flashes did not look very home-like! They were some dozen miles off I suppose judging by the flashes.</p>
<p>We stayed the night at this camp, and next morning, the officer in charge of the camp, formed us up into respective companies A. B. C. &amp; D. We had the option of getting into any of the said Coys.</p>
<p>At nightfall, we proceeded to go up towards our line. Our battalion was in the line towards Warneton (near Ypres). About 8 miles was in front of us. Destruction was paramount in every direction!</p>
