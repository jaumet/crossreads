<p>I slept very well &amp; the bed was very comfortable. I took over the eng again just after leaving Fives and worked it through to Mons, passing through a lot of country &amp; towns which had been terribly knocked about, all along the line the stations were all smashed. Foundries &amp; houses raised to the ground. Railway lines blown up also bridges &amp; trucks blown up in several places. After travelling over a very humpy road I arrived at Mons &amp; after going into the depot for more coal, &amp; out again on to the train &amp; while I was waiting for line clear my time was up &amp; my relief took over at 9 pm &amp; I turned in again after some supper.</p>
<p>At 6 am I awoke after a good sleep &amp; found that we were at our destination, Montignies. After the engine was again coaled, &amp; all hands had breakfast at the Depot, I received orders to be prepared to work a train further on so prepared my engine and at 10.45am was brought out and on to a train for Gembloux near Namur on another line.
