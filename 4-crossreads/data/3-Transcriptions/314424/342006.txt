<p>Owen Wister a tale of a horseman of the wild Western plains of America. It is interesting &amp; thrilling story of a cow boy who made good &amp; was not a tenderfoot.</p>
<p>Saturday June 1. 1918. 1397.<br/>a beautiful morning. There was heavy bombardment last night &amp; this morning.</p>
<p>This evening we were issued with new &amp; clean underclothing shirt, underpants &amp; socks. I then had a bath in the river put on the new ones &amp; handed in the dirty clothes.</p>
<p>This is the first of June. On Monday it will be two years since the battalion left Victoria.</p>
<p>[a line of shorthand - I wrote to my wife this evening.  letter 130.]</p>
<p>Sunday June 2. 1918. 1398.<br/>Another beautiful morning. There was the usual intense artillery activity last night on both sides this morning the enemy is shelling roads railways &amp; villages all around us, and again this afternoon.</p>
<p>[Two lines of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my sister this evening in answer to her last letter.  This afternoon was a half holiday.]</p>
<p>The Sargeant is sick today. He was slightly gassed a few days ago.</p>
<p>I wrote a long letter to my wife this evening 8 pages and enclosed a cutting from the Daily Mail about Transports [One line of shorthand &ndash; letter 131 dated June 3. 1918.]</p>
<p>Monday June 3. 1918. 1399.<br/>A beautiful morning.</p>
<p>It is two years today since I left Victoria. When I left I had no idea that the war would last so long. I thought that I would have been back home long before this. But we all underestimated the strength of the enemy. He appears to be as strong as ever and there is a lot of fight in him yet. But the allies have also increased in strength.
