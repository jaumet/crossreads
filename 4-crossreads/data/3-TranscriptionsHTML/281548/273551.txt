<p>we crossed over to Toranto on the troopship "Bermudian", and thence by rail ac<strike>c</strike>ross Italy &amp; France to Calais and then to Dover.</p>
<p>This is the end of the manuscript.  On the reverse of the last page there is a note as follows:</p>
<p>Dear Jack<br/>I went to Col. Farr today &amp; got this M.S.<br/>He &amp; his expert literary man think v. highly of the substance &amp; say there's plenty of good stuff in it.<br/>Get the sanction of the Ny. Board &amp; then hand it over to Farr again.<br/>He'll see the matter thr.<br/>Good luck. <br/>6.15 pm.  Monday 21st Nov. 19.<br/>Signature illegible.</p>
<p>Transcriber's note:  Parts of this diary were typed up shortly after WW1 and there are a number of typing errors throughout the document.  They have been left as they appeared</p>
