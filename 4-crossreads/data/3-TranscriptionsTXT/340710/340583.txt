 She told me of her beloved Gaston fighting somewhere at Verdun.  Soon he would be coming to visit her "en permission" soon and then back to the dreadful war again. Yes, her mother was living and I would meet her someday, yes I must come and have some coffee- no tea the English drink tea always- and she would play the piano.  In her home in Armentieres she had a beautiful piano but it was lost with everything else and here she was with her little shop what would you?  C'est la guerre, monsieur, c'est la guerre.  Then I left and promised to come again. I was back in five minutes, I had forgotten the braces.  Again I repeat "C'est la guerre" Estaires&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Initials. France. 14.6.16.
