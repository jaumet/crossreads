Connell), a couple of doz. oranges, a mirror, pen knife, in fact anything I needed.<br/>Mina, Lil, Reg &amp; Uncle then hired a taxi &amp; accompanied me to the boat again in drizzling rain.<br/>Soon after arrival on board a muster roll call was held and only three of our boys failed to answer.  They subsequently arrived at 6 p.m.<br/>About this time another troopship "Beltana" arrived with 36th Battalion and 10th Field Ambulance and the cheers that were exchanged were deafening.  Posted letters to home &amp; M.B.  No more leave was granted tonight and as messages were being exchanged between the new troopship and ourselves it was very late when we turned in.</p>
<p>Monday 12th<br/>Before <strike>the</strike> daylight we made preparations for casting off again and by breakfast time we were out in stream again close to the "Argyllshire" which had been there for about 5 days.  Later in the morning Lieut. Col.</p>
