A New Zealand Battalion passed us here during the dinner hour. From here we can see the hill that is studded with windmills &amp; on which there is also a large Monastry. We are now in the land of aeroplanes, and within hearing of the big guns &amp; bursting shells. About 2.p.m we started off again and passed along country roads on the outskirts of villages until we came to St. Sylvestre-cappel. We passed through it, and it is rather a pretty little town, with a magnificent brick church large enough to be called a cathedral in Victoria, and we halted at 4.p.m. for the night in a fine grass paddock, and the mules &amp; horses got a splendid feed of green suculent grass.</p>
