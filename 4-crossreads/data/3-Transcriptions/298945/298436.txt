<p>Wednesday 11 Sept.</p>
<p>Spent all yesterday afternoon in stringing up a false roof of blankets for, &amp; in fitting as a dressing room, an almost intact brick building with a courtyard. We stayed on duty till 10 pm when we were relieved by C Section on night duty. Fritz shelled fairly near, but evidently was trying for a camp, on the ridge to our left front. Our brick building is backed by four Nissen huts, &amp; across a field, about 50 yds from <strike>it</strike> the building, is <strike>the</strike> a collection of huts dating from our 1917 existence here, &ndash; now dismantled with iron roofs rusted. On the fringe of this, underneath a bank on the side of some ruined huts I share a dug out with Freddy Cocks, George Goode &amp; a couple of others.</p>
