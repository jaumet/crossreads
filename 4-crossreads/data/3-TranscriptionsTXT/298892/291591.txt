 of London. It was composed of all sorts and conditions of elderly gentlemen from top hats and frock coats down to caps and sand shoes. The greater number though look like gentlemen of affairs. I find that the people here are very deceptive in this respect. Even the grocers boy carries a cane and walks with a swagger. Most of the soldiers too swagger around with sticks   most of them   looking as if they owned the town. Members of the 9th. F. Ambulance are, I am sorry to say, particularly offensive in this respect. Remember me to Aunt Kate and Uncle Vesey and with best love to all at home I remain, Your loving son Eugene.
