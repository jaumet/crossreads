Friday July 12. 1918. 1438
I was on 3 picquet this morning from 2 to 4.30. It was a quiet time and no shelling in this quarter by the enemy.About 7 a.m. there was a very heavy artillery activity south of us on the French sector. Today is cloudy, windy and occasional light showers.[shorthand symbols](I wrote to gunner  R. Dusting this evening)
Saturday July13. 1918. 1439
A beautiful day. Today I have been repairing Lewis Gun Carriers for the limbers
This evening I wrote to Kitty Martin & to Cis in reply to their letters.This afternoon an enemy shell fell a few hundred yards from our camp. Just before dark I saw the enemy planes shoot down two of our baloons in quick succession and then make off admidst a hail of machine gun bullets & shrapnel.
Sunday July 14. 1918. 1440
The fourteenth of July is France's great national day. The French soldiers are to get extra rations today also wine & a cigar.Bill Wilson & I celebrated the French Fete day by getting a bottle of Port Wine 5/Francs. It was the Real Thing. We
