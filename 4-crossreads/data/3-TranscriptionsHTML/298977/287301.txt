<p>Thursday 28th September 1916  Train left at 6.5 a.m.  Reached Boulogne at 2 p.m., missed boat and went to Rest Camp.</p>
<p>Friday 29th September 1916  Left about 9.45, March ed to wharf and boat.  Left at 12.45, about 2 hrs. across the Channel and reached London at 5.30 p.m.  Went to Horseferry Rd. and got fixed up.  Engaged a room at Kenilworth Hotel &ndash; some class &ndash; Had a hot bath, went out and had a feed and then to Cinema.</p>
<p>Saturday 30th September 1916  Went to Commonwealth Bank and drew &pound;20/.  Bought some clothes etc.  Went to Euston Station and caught train to Birmingham.  Took the girls to the pictures at night.</p>
<p>Sunday 1st October 1916  Went for a walk in the Park and lazed the afternoon away with ditto.</p>
<p>Monday 2nd October 1916  Met the girls in town, went to pictures.  Up till 2 a.m.</p>
<p>Tuesday 3rd October 1916  Left for Leeds this morning, and went to see a wounded friend in hospital, and from there down to Leicester.</p>
<p>Wednesday 4th October 1916  Stayed the night and came down to London again this afternoon &ndash; then going to Eastbourne.  Talked all night.</p>
<p>Thursday 5th &amp; Friday 6th October 1916  Having a good time at Eastbourne, including a motor trip round Beachy Head, Pevensey, etc.</p>
<p>Saturday 7th October 1916  Left at 2.25 and are now at West Ealing.  Went to pictures.</p>
<p>Sunday 8th October 1916  Went to Church in the morning and Brotherhood in the afternoon and listened to a speech by a Conscientious Objector.</p>
<p>Monday 9th October 1916  Went into London for the day, visited St. Pauls, Art Gallery, Guild Hall, and theatre, Daddy Long Legs, at night.</p>
<p>Tuesday 10th October 1916  Up early this morning and caught train at 8 at Victoria, arrived Folkestone about 10 and Boulogne at 1 p.m.  Very rough crossing, stayed in Boulogne till 6p.m.  Saw the city &ndash; narrow streets and dirty &ndash; Reached Harebrouck at abt 11.30 and stayed in a Rest Camp the night.</p>
