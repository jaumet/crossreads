
[23rd  Nov. contd]Pair tear shell goggles 1 steel helmet 1 mess tin filled 1 tucker bag, filled 1 pouch filled as well as sheepskin vest & greatcoat.  Entrained at Amesbury and was lucky enough to be with John Driver Ron Hill Jack Perry the four of us being only ones in our compartment - the majority carrying eight.  Without much delay in 11/2 hours we arrived at Southampton at about 9 and heaving our packs on wharf we were allowed to walk the wharf.  We were there until 7 pm.  Then we fell in & marched on board our ferry transport boat the [indecipherable] Queen a paddle steamer.  We pushed off at about 8.Things went well for about an hour or so - although it was raining upstairs & crowded downstairs.
At sea we struck bad weather and then the fun commenced.  Soon ninety percent of those on board were sea sick.  I must confess that altho' we were 10 weeks on our trip from Aust - yet I never felt it as I did this trip.  I went upstairs for air on one occasion and the confusion was indescribable.  There were men everywhere throwing up also downstairs - none were particular.(We had on board the whole of our Ambulance (less transport) the Light Blues, Purple & Red (A) and English Drafts)Owing to the rough seas we did not arrive in France until 6 am next morning.
