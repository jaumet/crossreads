  
 3/11/16  Friday 
 Drizzling rain & very cold. Heavy bombarding night & day - Fritz shelling all party's on the skyline. Capt Helstrom got some shrapnel thro the sleeve of his coat & is very nervy. While waiting for cases outside of R.A.P., we noticed two fellows just on the brow of the hill fall after a H.E. burst, 4 of us doubled up quick as possible to investigate, both were only slightly wounded - one fellows helmet undoubtedly saved him. Whilst bandaging them, we were under observation - Fritz sent over more H.E. & shrapnel - I thought my time had come - got them in safely - so much for the respect to Red Cross men. Were relieved about 6 pm by the 7th F. Amb & believe we the Division is going out for a spell - Going home we were all tired, foot sore & weary, but we had to hickory as Fritz followed us up with shrapnel. Had a good feed of hot stew when we got to the dump - also issued with a cake of chocolate & rum - had a very fair sleep.
