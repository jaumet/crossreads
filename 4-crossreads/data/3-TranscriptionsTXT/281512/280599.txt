 &amp; rushed into a flock of sheep. These scattered at our unexpected visit &amp; fortunately we only damaged two. Bean endeavored to arrange the damage with the herdswoman without success. Naturally she assessed the damage exorbitantly.   After much haggling we proceeded on our way &amp; as we wended our way towards Hazebrouck, the sky gradually grew brighter with the flickering quiver of hundreds of guns in action. It was the most awesome sight I have ever seen. As we neared Hazebrouck the guns could be heard - almost synchronising with the flashing.  We took up our quarters with a number of the headquarters' staff beneath canvas. 22nd  August
