<p>[16th Aug. contd]<br/>lake was a beauty.  The Cloth Hall in Ypres could be distinctly seen &amp; seemed quite close to us.  Also Warneton &amp; other villages were easily seen.  We came down the mount on the north side &amp; endeavored to get a train near Dranout for Ballieul but failed &amp; only got a lift for couple of miles by some American drivers.</p>
<p>Friday 17th<br/>To night Fritz made a determined effort to bomb the aerodrome adjoining us and at same time put the wind up us a bit.  Three machines approached from different directions fully laden and soon searchlight go to work.  One was picked up and the phosphorus machine guns &amp; archies opened &amp; put up a good fight putting the Fritz to flight.  However the other two had had an opportunity to get closer &amp; soon one was picked up directly over us &amp; instantly a tremendous explosion was felt &amp; the wind rushed past us accompanied by numerous sparks &amp; we knew that one had been dropped on the hill about [above] us.  Swish &amp; another came but it was a dud.  A few seconds after 5 explosions in quick succession followed seemingly a hundred yds away, to discover that they fell in aerodrome grounds &amp; damaged a plane.  The holes were quickly covered with grass to prevent the photo</p>
