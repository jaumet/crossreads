<p>was sent to Salonika with the "Munster Fusilers" &amp; took part in the fighting there at the time of the great "Servian" retreat, I asked him what he thought of the Servians &amp; he said that no one could give that little nation praise enough he reckon they are splendid, great big good natured chaps, you would hardly know them from an Englishman until they start to talk, he reckons they fight as fierce as tigers, &amp; are as hard as mountain ponies," the chaps name was Mullarkey &amp; he was always a very fine fellow, he had 2 stars up but he dropped one to get back to the Australian Army again<br/>5th.  Last night I had a party of 20 men repairing the blown in portion of trench, &amp; we had just nicely started</p>
