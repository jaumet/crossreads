In the afternoon, a mass parade of all hands, including cooks etc, was called &amp; this was followed by a medical inspection &amp; a kit inspection.
Mch 10
Route march as usual in morning. The roads were very muddy &amp; we were thoroughly coated with mud &amp; clay. Alan was a bit tired in the afternoon so I went for a long walk through Shrewton &amp; Oncheston &amp; out into the fields beyond &amp; must have covered about 8 miles between lunch time &amp; bed-time. I had tea in a small old-fashioned house in Shrewton at the cost of 10d. I noticed that the lady only charged the other lads 9d &amp; came to the conclusion that the extra tariff was put on me because I put some sugar on my bread &amp; butter.
