It is now nearly midday and still they keep pouring down &ndash; Marines, some of them mere boys and New Zealanders are supporting us, but getting frightfully cut up.<br/>I am told to go and rest, which I do upon a hill held by the Marines. I lie down in the sun for a bit, but sleep I cannot.</p>
<p>May  4<br/>Rest day -  go to a new Rest Camp a little more sheltered from the snipers &ndash; expected to be away from the trenches a few days, but at 6.30 p.m. have to return &ndash; we are very short of men.<br/>Enemy has been busy all night with bombs through which we are losing many men &ndash; when I see the men going into the trenches I  often wonder how many are coming out &ndash; a few minutes after they have been in there is usually a scream, then the awful cry "Stretcher bearers", and then the mangled heap is brought out.
