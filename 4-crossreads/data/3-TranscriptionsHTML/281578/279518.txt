We marched out along the road a couple of miles, &amp; formed <strike>off</strike> up at the starting point. We were reserve battalion. The moon rose before we started luckily, so a chap could see where he was walking. We had to go through a wood &amp; thick crop, which was in ear &amp; up to our shoulders. The forest was a beautiful sight in the moonlight, lovely green trees overhead, &amp; brought thoughts of romance rather than war. Our searchlights were playing about the sky trying to locate a Fritz 'plane which could be heard, &amp; they found him, &amp; all the lights were concentrated on him, he looked like a pretty, silver bird in the light. Then our anti-aircraft guns opened out, &amp; we saw the flash of bursting shells, &amp; hoped to see him come down. Some shrapnel burst in front of him &amp; I thought they'd get him, especially as the rap, rap of the Lewis guns , firing on all sides, joined in. However, he turned after a while, &amp; got out of the rays of the search-lights, &amp; they couldn't pick him up again. Later on we heard the awful roar of bombs exploding. We went through</p>
