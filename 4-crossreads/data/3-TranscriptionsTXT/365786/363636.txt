 Socially Cairo was dead.  It was summertime &amp; the few  British  English women residents who had not been able to get away to England were down at Alexandria which during the summer is always 10 degrees cooler than Cairo.  In 1915 owing to the war there were still a good many Britishers there &ndash; people like ourselves, for instance.  Cairo is a most wonderfully fascinating place but in spite of the British occupation it is wonderful how little English is spoken there.  Business is mostly transacted in French &ndash; greek is very common also Italian for these two nations form the greater part of the European population.
