<p>However, during the course of the day the Admiralty ordered us back to our base, so evidently the German ships had returned to their base.&nbsp;</p>
<p>March 7.</p>
<p>Another &quot;coal ship&quot; found us all ready again.</p>
<p>March 11.</p>
<p>After a few days spell, we were off again, but only for a few hours, as we were ordered to return. It was rumoured that we were part of a fleet employed in a sweeping operation to catch some German minelayers which were known to be out laying mines. The light cruisers were said to have caught them, &amp; brought in many prisoners, but the whole operation was kept extremely quiet. The movements of the fleet had to be kept a dead secret, only known to the Admirals in command, on account of the activity of enemy agents &amp; spies round the Firth of Forth.</p>
<p>March 14.</p>
<p>A few days later we left Rosyth for Seapa flow, where we carried out gunnery &amp; torpedo esercises, in perfect weather. Our stay here lasted a week, &amp; then we returned to our new base.</p>
<p>March 22.</p>
<p>Our next adventure commenced two days later,&nbsp;</p>
<p>March 24.</p>
<p>when we went out to support an air raid by seaplanes on Tondern, in Schleswig-Holstein where there were Zeppelin sheds. We got to the north of Heligoland, &amp; close to the Dutch coast, arriving there about 5am the following morning, when &quot;action&nbsp;stations&quot; was sounded off.</p>
