  
 8th Dec 1918 
 To-day is Sunday and the weather has been simply glorious, a great contrast to the week days.  The sun shone warmly and the mud seemed a little less troublesome, although the puddles & holes were still full of soft mud. 
 During the day I continued to arrange matters in readiness for Charleroi, & this evening a meeting was held to elect a new secretary for the Central committee, & to discuss the arrangements.  Harry McNeil, who was secretary of our concert committee, was elected secretary. The attendance at the meeting was not good, & the sporting demand - mostly of the transport was conspicuous in their apparently intentional absence.
