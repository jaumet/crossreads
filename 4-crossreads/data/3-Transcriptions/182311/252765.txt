<p><u>Camp</u>.</p>
<p>10-8-16.</p>
<p>Lark Hill Camp.</p>
<p>Salisbury Plain.</p>
<p>My Dear Ones,</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; It is from dry land that I write these notes after landing from our transport quite safely, avoiding the perils of the sea and also the wily submarine.&nbsp; The coast of Devon was our landfall, soon we passed the famous Eddystone Lighthouse and entered Plymouth Sound, then entraining from the Quay rode for hours through the glorious county of Devon, a fresh green garden with ponds and streams everywhere, particularly welcome to our ryes, worn with gazing so much at the sea.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Yes, I like Devonshire, its green hills, its streams, its small fields bounded by hedgerows, its little grey homes built of the chalk of the country, its beautiful churches and occasionally even a picturesque ruin.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; From our new camp we can distinguish Stonehenge through our glasses, standing as it has since the days before the Druids.&nbsp; Our new camp is on the historic Salisbury Plain, in the midst of an historical land; today (Sunday) Capt.Trebilcock and my small self took an eight-mile walk around the N.W. of the camp, at Figheldean we</p>
