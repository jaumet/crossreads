<p>was a long queue lined up trying to get to the refreshment counter, &amp; as long a queue filing along to a table where a woman was handing out a sheet of paper &amp; envelope. Raining tonight &amp; the Huns didn&#39;t make any air raid. Spread the blanket I drew at this camp on the floor, &amp; had my own blanket &amp; waterproof sheet over me, took off my boots, puttees, socks, tunic &amp; top pants, but it was jolly cold in the tent during the night, especially on the feet. <u>Wedn. 3 April</u> Reveille went at 6, we didn&#39;t get up in our tent till 8. We expected to move on today, but no word came. Some of the Jocks, Irishmen &amp; Tommies marched out, but thousands of Tommies came in, &amp; I&#39;m blowed if I know where they&#39;re putting &#39;em all. I&#39;d like just to know how many troops must have come across from England this last fortnight, the camps there must be pretty much emptied.
