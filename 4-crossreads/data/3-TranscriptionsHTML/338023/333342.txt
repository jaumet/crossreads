<p>14th August 1917</p>
<p>My Dear Mother</p>
<p>This is written in haste to catch mail carrying my previous letter. It is just to let you know that yet once again the chopper has fallen and my last address, in all detail save the first, is naPoo &ndash; that is to say a wash-out, a thing of the past. This time, however, the transfer is actually acceptable to all hands for it is a sort of double action affair. You must join me in rejoicing that the dreaded English winter will now have to look for welcome from other than this one. Shall write at first opportunity, meanwhile please dont</p>
