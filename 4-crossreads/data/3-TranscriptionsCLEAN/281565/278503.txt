One goes down a multitude of stinking lanes (streets they call them) where the houses almost meet over head, & where water carriers with their squelching goat-skins & peddlars of all kinds cry their wares, through a huge gate in a wall kept by a blind & deaf Christian of about 90 summers who howls in a voice most pitiable to hear for "backsheesh" then through sundry subterranean passages till at last you come out into a dark room where, when your eyes become used to the light a cross like a Maltese Cross shows on all sides.
