This one has   too  four rooms with a sort of hall between, it also had a window to each room, but the order of things being reversed  they  the doors & windows were facing the enemy. The view from the pill box which was in Glencourse Wood, was desolation itself, over to our left was a low stretch of country, every yard a shell hole, & here & there a pill box, these were still in enemy possession, every now & then our shells would burst near them. I saw some terrible sights in this front. Our lines led up to another pill box about 400 yds ahead & from there to the other coys. There were no system of trenches, the men dug in, in the side of shell holes, during the day nothing was done.
