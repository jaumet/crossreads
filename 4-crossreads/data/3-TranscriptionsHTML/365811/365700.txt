<p>4<br/>hundreds &amp; thousands the war will continue. When we can push every alternate day for a fortnight with an effective days barrage for each advance we shall be within a very few weeks of the end.</p>
<p>All my old pessimism has changed to optimism &amp; my view is of course only the reflex of what one deems to be the best informed opinion in France.</p>
<p>The losses will of course be terribly heavy: that is inevitable, altho' as we move faster they will become less. Depend upon it, take heart in it: we can after all our misgivings win decisively in France &amp; smash him right down.</p>
<p>The fighting in Flanders was immeasurably more intense than ever before. The bayonet work was small &amp; the bombing unimportant. The advance was a sheer artillery advance: the great bulk of our casualties were from shell. The pushing of the guns &amp; a marked development in long range guns on the relatively back areas were a heavy addition to our artillery, pioneer &amp; all drivers'</p>
