<p>November the 2nd <strike>and 3rd</strike> 3 German Officers visited us but didn't want to hear any complaints or have any conversation with us<br/>November 16th another 33 Prisoners arrived from Angora, also some clothing and Blankets from the U.S. Embassy<br/>November 26th had a heavy fall of snow</p>
<p>Provisions allowed to Prisoners at Kingre 307 men<br/>Two meals a day, one Loaf about 1 &frac12; lbs, 8 &frac34; lbs of cheese, 9,lbs of Onions, 18 &frac12; lbs of Salt, 6 &frac34; lbs of Fat, 15 lbs of Flour, 41 lb of Crushed Wheat</p>
<p>November 30th &amp;pound 5.10.0 per man was sent from the Geneva Red Cross for A.E.2.s Crew, we received one Turkish Pound from Commandant<br/>December the 4th &amp;pound 4.0.0. arrived from American Embassy per man, for A.E.2s Crew, in the meantime money was sent up for the other Prisoners which we did not share<br/>December the 6th we received &amp;pound 2.0.0. from Commandant<br/>December the 18th received &amp;pound 1.0.0. from Commandant also received letter from Sister dated 22nd of November</p>
