
February 1917brought in.  Many have given themselves up since Rafa.  They seem fairly well clothed & in good condition, but they were very hungry & thirsty.
Febry 5thGot back to Mazar with the goods. Sent over to the camp for 2 timber waggons & soon had the things in the camp.  We expect to be at Mazar only a few days.  The farriers are very busy shoeing, so it looks like hard country for us.
Febry 6thWe are just doing  a few patrols & a little outpost work & as the whole Brigade are here, it is very easy.  The amount of work done out this way since we were here last is wonderful.  It speaks wonders for British energy & organisation.  Turkey's chances of getting into Egypt are getting more & more remote.  It can never be more than a dream of theirs now.  At night the Royal Welsh Fusiliers gave a Concert in the YMCA tent & it proved one of the best I have yet heard in Egypt.  Their part singing was splendid.
