<p>most places are only wide enough apart to allow one vessel to proceed though it at a time; at many places the Canaal is widened to form sidings for ships to tie up, when other ships have to pass them.  Ships coming from the opposite direction could be detected miles ahead by, first a dim glimmer in the sky, then as they approached, by the flaming lights, like the head lights of a motor car.
