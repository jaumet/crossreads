<p>[Transcriber's notes:<br/>Pg 3. 1st-8 Jan. 1917 From Vignacourt moved between Cardonnette, Ribemont, Mametz and Longueval<br/>Pg 5. 7 Jan. Transferred from stretcher-bearing to cook house duties (at enlistment, Hardy was 161 cm (5ft 3&frac12; in) tall and weighed 50.8 kgs)<br/>Pg 8. 21 Jan. Becordel-Becourt rest camp. Providing hot drinks and other duties for the wounded.<br/>Pg 19. 28 Feb. Transferred to No 2 Australian General Hospital, Wimeraux, near Boulogne, working in the ward kitchen<br/>Pg 28. 5 Apr.Contracted tonsilitis and sent to England, Norfolk War Hospital, Norwich<br/>Pg 34. 24 Apr. Transferred to 2nd Australian Auxillary Hospital, Southall<br/>Pg 36. 3 May To Monte Video Camp, Weymouth<br/>Pg 37. 7 May  To No 2 Command Depot, Westham<br/>Pg 39-46. 11 to 25 May - 14 day leave. Stayed in Bow, London &amp; visited Edinburgh &amp; Glasgow<br/>Pg 51. 12 Jun.  No 5 Camp, Perham Downs<br/>Pg 76. 20 Aug.  Classified unfit to take exercise (gymnastics). Service records show "anaemia &amp; debility"<br/>Pg 77. 22 Aug.  To Verne Camp near Portland<br/>Pg 91. 27 Sept.  Embarked on HMAT "Suevic" for Australia<br/>Pg 111. 18 Nov.  Arrived Melbourne and travelled to Sydney by train]</p>
