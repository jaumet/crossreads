<p>Thursday 22&nbsp;April 1915<br />Working all the morning. Letter to Mrs Hughes (schoolteacher), &quot;Airlie&quot;, Chatswood, N. S. Wales.<br />Writing &amp; reading all day&nbsp;<br />Acting for the O.C. during his absence.<br />B.W.Hosp Salvarsan named Kharsivan &amp; Neokharsivan</p>
<p>Friday 23&nbsp;April 1915<br />Operation for Varicocele this morning.<br />Ride on the plateau between 7 &amp; 8 a.m. Collected some alabaster. Air of the best.&nbsp;<br />Wrote to Dr D. Dunlop, Ed. dptmnt. in Cairo.&nbsp;<br />Ride across the dessert during the morning.</p>
<p>Saturday 24&nbsp;April 1915<br />Saw General Ford at 10 a.m. He was corteous and civil, &amp; I have hopes that he will bring to me relief. Good.<br />Back at Mena at 2-15 p.m.<br />At The Medical school till 1-30 pm. Saw some specimens &amp; looked through some journals. Very good.</p>
<p>Sunday 25&nbsp;April 1915<br />Mass at 7-30 a.m. in Light horse lines.<br />Ride between 5 &amp; 6-45 p.m.<br />Reading &amp; writing rest of day.<br />Day of the gallant charge of the Australians at Anzac Cove, Galipoli. A great feat of arms</p>
