I was delighted to get a card from Boo yesterday, saying he had arrived in "Blighty", he didn't say much about his leg.Sunday 21 July Had a bonza day's outing today; two motors have been at the disposal of the battalions of the brigade in turn, & today about 50 of the 13th Bn. went to Abbeville. We got up at 6 a.m. had breakfast walked down to Bn. Headqrs at Querrieu, where we got aboard the two motors about 8 a.m. It was raining when we got up, but the sun soon came out & it turned out a glorious day with a nice breeze. The country everywhere looked beautiful, green trees lined the road on both sides all the way, & everywhere the grass & crops & trees are green & luxuriant. We passed through Les Allencons, Amiens (now a deserted city), St Sauveur, Triancourt, Belloy, Flixecourt, Monflis & Ailly, & got to Abbeville about 11 a.m. We got out of the lorries & were given a free leg till 8.30 p.m., when we all had to assemble at the lorries to go home.
