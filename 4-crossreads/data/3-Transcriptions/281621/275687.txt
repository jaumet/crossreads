<p>Monday 26<br/>Last night the long expected attack was made by the 9th and wild rumours are current as to the result. The objective was gained without much opposition and it is hinted the Fritz has begun a big retreat. The infantry say that he cannot be found. Many casualties are coming through.</p>
<p>Tuesday 27<br/>Good progress is still being made up the line and casualties are coming in.<br/>Duncan Robertson was wounded in the neck &amp; shoulder last night.<br/>Lately I have been ill, running a temp. &amp; aches in legs. Three times in twelve days I have been so &amp; the major says it's trench fever.</p>
<p>Wednesday 28<br/>The papers now confirm the above advance of two miles on an eleven mile front. Le Baque, [indecipherable] &amp; Marlancourt fell to the Australians who hold from Guedecourt to Le Sars. Casualties have slackened off &amp; the distance from Baupume is only 1800 yards.</p>
<p>March, 1917<br/>Thursday 1<br/>Fighting in the open is now in progress.<br/>The colonel &amp; Reg Shergold were wounded to-day, the former in the chest &amp; the latter in the leg. The colonel is not expected to live &amp; he is not to be moved.</p>
<p>Friday 2<br/>The colonel died this morning. The weather is rough &amp; March winds are going in full swing.</p>
