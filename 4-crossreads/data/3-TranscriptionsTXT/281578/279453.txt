Drew rations &amp; marched out of Berteaucourt les Dames, kissed little Angele good-bye, she's a bonny kid, I wrote to Elsie today, &amp; enclosed a letter the youngster wrote to her in French. I gave her Nin's address, &amp; she is going to write a letter in French to her tonight &ndash; hope Nin gets it, as she will be so interested. I hope I see the dear little girl again. We marched about 3 kilos to another village &amp; sat down by the roadside waiting for a train. One with wounded on ran through, also one carrying refugees, driven from their houses by the German menace. Boarded the train at Pernois; Before leaving Berteaucourt les Dames we were paid, I drew 70 francs, entered in pay-book as &pound; 2-11-4. 30 N.C.O's were in our truck, one chap bought a pack of cards &amp; some of them were soon playing poker for francs. The train went slowly along, &amp; we got out at some station about 11.30 pm &amp; then had a hellish march. In the distance the sky was just like as if a storm was on &ndash; the flash of the shells lit up the sky like sheet lightning, &amp; the roar of the guns resembled thunder.
