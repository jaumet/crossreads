Prices are high &ndash; the limit, in fact &ndash; and so these institutions, though they may benefit military mankind in some ways, take from him in another manner what little worldly cash he may be possessed of. Fancy a city of 15,000 souls, all compelled by circumstance to deal with one firm for every blessed thing they want. These institutions work together, &amp; each sells its own particular. Thus, no competition, plenty profits.</p>
<p>Nothing much to write about. We don't seem to stand much chance of getting away from here for donkeys' months &amp; are therefore being specially trained to look pretty by numbers in order that the elite of Toorak &amp; Bendigo may be pleased to gaze upon us &amp; applaud. So far we have stood it. Only last Sunday morning we kicked a bit &amp; caused some little scare at H.Q. Next time the M.G.s have to kick it may cause some flutter, but they have promised if we're good, things will be much improved for the N.S.W. men &amp; more liberty granted. Love to all &amp; your self</p>
<p>Harry</p>
