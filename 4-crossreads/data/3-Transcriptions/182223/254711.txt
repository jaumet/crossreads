<p>Monday 1 November 1915&nbsp;<br />In Cairo during the morning.<br />Back at 1-45 p.m.&nbsp;<br />Reading, writing, &amp; sleeping during the afternoon.</p>
<p>Tuesday 2&nbsp;November 1915&nbsp;<br />All souls day.&nbsp;<br />Visited Old Cairo today with Drs Henry of Grafton &amp; Cunningham of India. Coptic Churches, one Mosque &amp; the Nilometer.&nbsp;<br />Letter from Car&nbsp;Joseph. Wrote to them as also to Mrs Keohan &amp; Mrs Ryan.</p>
<p>Wednesday 3 November 1915&nbsp;<br />Writing during morning.<br />From 2 till 6-30 p.m. in Cairo <s>most</s> around the bazaars off the Moushky with Drs Henry, Hollywood and&nbsp;[blank].&nbsp;Felt rather miserable, and about 6 p.m. wretched&nbsp;noises in ears, rushing in the head, heaviness in legs below the knees, miserable all over, too talkative words flowing correctly &amp; with much facility; worse coming home in tram at dinner wretched, 8 p.m. same but becoming worse inclined to go to bed at 8 p.m., some visitors too talkative to them; during night no sleep a great battle in progress all over body between lucocytes and some invading organism. Passing much urine 6 times in 12 hours, large amount each time. Imagining all sorts of things. <s>Not fit to get up for breakfast</s>. Treat &ndash;&nbsp;Salacine &amp; x grs,<sup>.</sup> Dormoporde grs.&nbsp;Phenactin [phenacetin] xx grs&nbsp;[indecipherable] Laxative 3 tabs.</p>
