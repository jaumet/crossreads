<p>20 April, 1916 Thursday</p>
<p>Revielle as usual</p>
<p>What horses were left went out to the Palms for exercise. The 2nd L.H. accompanying them, old Charlie Chatman keeping them company.</p>
<p>Paid the dentist a visit this morning &amp; he informed me that all would have to come out so he gave me an appointment for Saturday morning at 10.</p>
<p>Received a letter from Ern &amp; he says that everything is OK at home.</p>
<p>Received a Power-Plus catalogue &amp; thats the engine thats going to revolutionise the motor world.</p>
<p>Bob &amp; Claude Jackson are both in Egypt so dropped them a line.</p>
<p>More mail arrived but none for yours truly although all the others got theirs expect mine &amp; parcels tomorrow.</p>
<p>Played chess &amp; turned in at 10 pm.</p>
<p>21 April, 1916 Friday</p>
<p>Hopped out at 5.15</p>
<p>Great news came to hand today for the Russians are credited with taking Treligond &amp; now they will take some stopping, our forces are advancing slowly up the Tigris although the floods are heavy, Verdun has just about blown itself out at last.</p>
<p>Both Hal &amp; I were recommended by the Bde Maj for those jobs, we will know how we got on in a few days.</p>
<p>Tommies move out that leaves us with sole command of this district.</p>
<p>Parcel mail arrives &amp; I got one beauty of clothing viz 2 shirts 6 handkerchiefs &amp; a pair of socks, they will come in exceptionally handy, as I'm about run out.</p>
<p>Played the C O chess &amp; won &amp; then turned in at 9.30 tired out. Day has been extremely hot about 129&deg; in the shade.</p>
