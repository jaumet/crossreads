<p>15 January 1915<br/>Friday</p>
<p>Stables per usual<br/>The Third Regt were tried on Tactics today 2 acquitted themselves real well only procedure is still slack.  The 1st are still on station work but are progressing slowly.  [indecipherable] ie all breakages &amp; losses due to carelessness are to be paid for by persons sustaining such losses.  The truth is that the Boss is getting rather short of cash &amp; consequently must find some channel to restore such funds.  It will take a better man than him to define the word [indecipherable].  Toby received a note from home &amp; is answering.  Rumour has it that we move at any moment now so expect to be in France shortly thank goodness.  Lamp work for Reg Sigs.  Bed 9.30</p>
<p>16 January 1915<br/>Saturday</p>
<p>Stables per usual<br/>Men ordered to pack kit bags in readiness for move which does not come [indecipherable] bad boys picqueting bags to be on horses in fact everything ok but the [indecipherable] 5 staff dodge same.  I have the afternoon off.  Another mail to hand. I get one from home. Spend afternoon and evening letters, one home and another to Dot &amp; [indecipherable]. I sent a cable home this morning for money I hope that [indecipherable] springs off his tail in replying.  All married men to remain at base [indecipherable] not yet confirmed.  Got in touch with [indecipherable] on Pyramids by Aclis I gave him a [indecipherable] I also asked him re Dinner next Saturday night answer satisfactory.  No sign or word of machines yet hope they keep them for good.  Bed at 11.30</p>
