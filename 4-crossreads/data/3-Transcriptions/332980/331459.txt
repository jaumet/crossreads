<p>&amp; the Day's Paper will be waited for anxiously by the Boys. I am off duty &amp; expect to be called on before Dinner, &amp; as things are fairly quiet I may remain off longer, The Weather now is cold &amp; Wintry. Yesterday was the limit, Raining all day &amp; I was on my way home from Boulogne on a Mixed Train &amp; had to Shunt every station enroute &amp; I had my Over Coat on but it soon was Wet &amp; I was glad when I reached St Omer after being on duty 13 hrs after Coaling &amp; my Mate cleaned the Fire. I left for Malhove &amp; soon was off duty.</p>
<p>Friday Oct 19th.<br/>After resting all day I was called on at 9.30 Pm to stand by on an Engine in the Depot, &amp; after remaining on same until 11.45 I was ordered with my Mate to leave the Eng &amp; proceed on a Train to Hazebruick &amp; there relieve a set of Men on 5324, a Big Great Western Engine &amp; take a Supply Train on past Eypres as I did not know the Road beyond Castre very well, a Pilot Driver was sent with me to Hazebruick, we left Malhove at 12.30 am &amp; after <strike>an hour</strike> a run of 1 hour arrived</p>
