<p>The Fisher's Wooing</p>
<p>I<br/>Give me a <u>plaice</u> in your <u>sole</u>, love,<br/>In spite of my <u>'erring</u> ways,<br/>I'm weary of <u>whiting</u> for <strike>your</strike> thee, love,<br/>Lonely &amp; sad are my <u>dace</u>.</p>
<p>II<br/>Life has its catches of <u>ills</u>, love,<br/>So <u>carp</u> not but heed my call:<br/>'Twould be a sad <u>bloa-ter</u> me, love,<br/>To lose you, my <u>haul</u> in <u>haul</u>.</p>
<p>III<br/>The <u>net</u> result up to now, love,<br/>Has left me <u>crab</u> -by and sore;<br/>With a kerchief I <u>dab</u> my eyes, love,<br/><u>Oy-ster</u>, full of vim, no more.</p>
<p>IV<br/>Give me a <u>plaice</u> in your <u>sole</u>, love,<br/>We'll pair like the finest <u>Dover</u>,<br/>And ever &ndash; no <u>cod</u>! &ndash; I'll remain, love,<br/>Your own loving <u>Salm-on</u> clover!</p>
<p>Transcriber's note:<br/>P. 12 &ndash; "Pelman" is referred to several times in the diary and is "a system of training the mind in the United Kingdom during the first half of the 20th century".</p>
