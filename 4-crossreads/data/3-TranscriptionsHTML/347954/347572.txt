<p>when the broaching was fully realized &amp; to a commercial world person it seemed an act of extravagance to allow gallon after gallon of good wine to run to waste when a little tact &amp; common sense would have served to convert the <strike>goods</strike> waste into useful comforts for the men who probably would have appreciated that tact in months to come.  Things continue to still remain in a quiet mood, &amp; the only thing of importance is the large gun duels which shake &amp; quiver my poor old dugout almost to bits &ndash; salvoes &amp; tons of shells are weekly expended from the various batteries on land &amp; sea.  We had a quiver of excitement during the present week when a party of infantry met &amp; advanced across the intervening space into the next gully in front of the right wing &amp; about on a level with a knoll facing Kabe Tepe &amp; straight direct from Hellie Point.  I witnessed the departure of the various companies who in addition to arms bore picks &amp; shovels for trench digging.  It promised to be a sharp attack but it enventuated into nothing more than an ordinary midnight sap &amp; by morning sufficient earth had been removed to make things fairly comfortable.  The following night the same event happened so we have now in our possession more trench &amp; closer contact</p>
