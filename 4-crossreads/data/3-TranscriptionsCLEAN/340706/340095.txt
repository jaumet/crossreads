  
 9-5-19 At 7 am we breakfast & up anchor. On this boat we pay for meals (3 fr each). There is little interesting in the somewhat flat shores I try to ascertain the name of a point & ask one of the crew & find that still I am unable to speak English as an Englishmen speaks it for his reply was Idenasay & it was only sometime afterwards that I released that this was not the name of the point but his inability to name it. 
 We berth alongside the big Olympic & are disembarked into a shed & when all assembled we marched round to another shed & are given a meal in return for a ration ticket which had been issued at gangway. After this we are reassembled & then put through a [indecipherable], given a train ration & entrained by embarkation staff & we are off. 
 We duly arrived at Codford & are shunted back to a siding but even this leaves us 1/2 hour march in the hot sun for weather is very sultry & our camp is No 3. which turns out to be tents only with 12 men to a bell tent. Administration here seems to be always the same high(?) order. 
 Whether from the day or the night watch or the travelling I am very tired & turn in.
