Two guns were put out of action and we were completely demoralised.  The telephone lines of course gave a great amount of trouble, and seeking loose ends in 3 feet of mud on a pitch black frosty night + shells &amp; bullets was no catch.  After 3 weeks we shifted to Gudecourt [Gueudecourt] where the bty. was when I came on leave.  By this time the ground was so much cut up &amp; so muddy that wheeled transport was impossible and ammunition &amp; rations had to be brought up on pack horses.
