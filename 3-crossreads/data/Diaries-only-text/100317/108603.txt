Now it is a monument to the devastation of modern War and nothing but a tangle of rubble. We rode along passing an old spinning factory outside of which much material was still lying. From the shell torn houses in many places came the quite unmistakable stink of human dead bodies and crowds of large flies. To the initiated this told a tale of dead men rotting in cellars where they had been killed when our chaps made their advance an mopped up the village in the moonlight. Across the streets in places
