When our wounded were being sent back from  Anzac & Suvla Bay  Gallipoli, they were taken to Lemnos or  Alexandria  Egypt, Malta or England - some were even landed at Gibralta.  Those going to Egypt were disembarked at Alexandria & if not destined for that port were placed aboard special trains & taken to Cairo.  These trains were specially built for the wounded & being very long could carry a great number of men.  Some of the carriages were like a Pulman Sleeper, where cot cases (i.e., those unable to sit up) could recline in comfort - others were like an ordinary carriage & one could walk thro the train from one end to the other.
