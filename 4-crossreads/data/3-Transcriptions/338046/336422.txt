<p>1916<br/>December<br/>that we were going to German East Africa, and still another, that the Zulus were likely to rise on Dingaan's day  -  Dec 16th I think.</p>
<p>The real reason was that the Beltana a NSW transport had been compelled to put back to West Australia with a fire on board and we were waiting for her, as she belonged to our convoy.   The Mumps didn't trouble me much.   We had a gramophone and we played the records all day long starting at 5 am and finishing at 10 pm.    This resulted in my learning some new songs, and becoming heartily sick of others.   One record in particular "Poppies" was so painfully doleful, that a wrathful digger arose one day, and smashing it across his knee threw the pieces out of the porthole.   "Wait till I'm as old father came near sharing a similar fate.</p>
