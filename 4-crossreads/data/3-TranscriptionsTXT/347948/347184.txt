 The North Sea Battle June 1st 1916 
 A lot of German dreadnoughts came a steaming from the Keil Relying on their Majesty &amp; might To frighten any merchantmen the darkness might conceal They thought that they could put them all to flight 
 But some of Britain's Cruisers were a cruising round the sea They fastened like a bulldog to a tramp And though they lost some chips they played hell among the Ships And lots of German dreadnoughts left their stamp 
 They pommelled at each other and our cruisers held their own 'Gainst German's High Sea Fleet so proud and bold But though our boys were up against the best the Germans had They strove the old traditions to uphold
