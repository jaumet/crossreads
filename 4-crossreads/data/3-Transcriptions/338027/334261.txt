<p>Monday, 26 October, 1914<br/>More ships in Sound, 22 in all Orvieto the flagship of the fleet anchored alongside us.  Cruiser Melbourne also anchored first of our convoy.</p>
<p>On Pickett, 24 hrs duty, 9.30-11.30 am 3.30 5.30 pm &ndash; 9.30-11.30 &ndash; 3.30-5.30 pm.  Rained all day &amp; night, Horse on main deck bad with colic.  Five injections of morphine to deaden pain.  Wrote to Edie to-night chancing reply whilst on voyage.  Had a good look at my own pony whilst on picket, happy &amp; well.</p>
<p>Tuesday, 27 October, 1914<br/>Still in Albany more boats arrived from Sydney now awaiting N.Z. troopers &amp; Convoy.</p>
