 25-12-1916 Xmas day.  Dreary, cold, wet and windy, shelled again last night, one landed fair in our horse lines, luckly it struck a soft spot, and failed to explode.  A few more landed about our tents.  Fair dinner, Ham, and tinned peas, Plum pudding and sauce, Preserved fruit.  Artillery firing like fury all night. 
 26-12-1916 Still raining.  Some nasty time shrapnel over at 4-30 p.m.  One large shell lobbed in D.A.C.  Artillery very active, 100 rounds of ammunition sent to Bty.  Shoeing smith Heyden returned from leave.  Weather mild. 
 27-12-1916 Fine bright day.  Aeroplanes busy.  Five Hun planes tackled one of ours, and brought him down.  Three of ours brought down altogether.
