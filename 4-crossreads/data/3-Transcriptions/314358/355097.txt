<p>Wareham</p>
<p>23 Dec 1916<br/>Dear Beatrice<br/>'Hope deferred makes the heart sick'</p>
<p>This old saying expresses my feelings better than anything else just now.<br/>Sometime ago the Colonel commanding this dreary camp agreed that my case was a hard one, and warranted special consideration. He therefore agreed to let me have furlough and recommended an advance of &pound;5- to enable me to live during that spell of freedom</p>
<p>Unfortunately he found [indecipherable] the sanction of higher authority was necessary before I could do. I am now waiting the pleasure of that higher authority which I no doubt is enjoying its Xmas holidays amongst friends in the [indecipherable] and hospitality of true kinship and cheer, quite forgetful of the</p>
