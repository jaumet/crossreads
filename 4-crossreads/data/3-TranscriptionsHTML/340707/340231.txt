<p>101<br/>Medici is a masterpiece by Pierre Lescot but the old dame not relishing the propriety of being shown in the nude created another tomb or effigy showing herself &amp; husband clothed.</p>
<p>Tomb of Francois 1 also merits attention with black marble in top &amp; sculptured in the marble the tombs of Marignam &amp; Ceresole. Behind by Pierre Bontemp is an urn containing his heart. On the crypt is the burial vault of the Bourbons.</p>
<p>Taking train we went on to Enghien where we wandered through vegetable gardens until one found one of Brise friends Alece &amp; her husband. Here we were invited to stay for dinner. Women wages pre war at highest was 7 f per day This railway employee [indecipherable] 3 fr 50 per day [free] passes for himself &amp; family.</p>
<p>Thence we set out for Montmorency &amp; its forest. [indecipherable] associations of JJ Rousseau. The view from the terrace of the church is very fine. The town is very old &amp; some of its streets are fully 4 to 6 ft wide &amp; often so steep that the authorities have placed a handrail along the building to support you in the task of ascending the street.</p>
<p>The Mairie Arts park reminds one of a little chateau de Trianon &amp; is exceedingly artistic We overlook the Orangerie &amp; enter the Chataignerie but this is for most part dotted with building &amp; the ancient</p>
