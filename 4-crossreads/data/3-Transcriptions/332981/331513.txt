<p>The Speech.</p>
<p>The King in his address said:-</p>
<p>I thank you for your loyal addresses of congratulation on the Signature of the Armistice and on the prospect of a Victorious Peace, at this moment without parallel in our History and in the History of the World.</p>
<p>I am glad to meet you and the representatives of India and the Dominions beyond the Seas in order that we may render thanks to Almighty God for the promise of a peace now near at hand, and that I may express to you, and through you to the Peoples whom you represent, the thoughts that rise in my mind at a time so solemn.</p>
<p>I do this with a heart full of grateful recognition of the spontaneous and enthusiastic expressions of loyalty and affection which I have been privileged to receive both personally here in the metropolis and by messages from all parts of these islands; as well as from every quarter of the Empire.</p>
