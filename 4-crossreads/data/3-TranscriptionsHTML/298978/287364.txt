Our artillery was firing into us as well &ndash; then came the cry from the top, "We must have ammunition" &ndash; to run down that slope was instant death &ndash; there was no officer to give the order and, aware of the urgency of the case, I got up and tore down the Hill, for being a signaller I felt it was my duty to take the risk, so went down to Headquarters with the message.<br/>On my way down, in the gully, I came across the reinforcements coming up &ndash; one of the chaps said to me "Who are you? Where do you come from? What is your name?" Meanwhile drawing his bayonet &ndash; I had to answer pretty quickly, otherwise I could have had more steel than I wanted.<br/>I then came across Lieut.Braishaw lying wounded &ndash; "Who are you?" I said &ndash; "Don't you know me, Silas?" he replied. "What is the password?" was my next remark &ndash; "Garra Gabba &ndash; don't you remember, I came over in the same ship with you from Melbourne?"
