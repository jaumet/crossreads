<p>Bayonet fighting and gas drill. The time soon went round, &amp; it is more interesting than grooming.</p>
<p>I received a letter from my wife today dated Jan 3 No 10 &amp; one from Eric &amp; his mother also one from Kathleen Miller.</p>
<p>Sunday March 10. 1918. 1315<br/>A beautiful day.  A heavy frost last night &amp; bright sunshine &amp; blue sky today.</p>
<p>The clocks were put forward one hour last night.<br/>[Five lines of shorthand:  Lines 4 and 5: I wrote to my father also to Eric Wilson, and my wife letter 119]</p>
<p>Monday March 11. 1918. 1316<br/>Another beautiful day. I was blacksmithing most of the day, repairing and plating two broken shafts of the mess cart. I made some needle cases out of some German cartridge cases for my sister this evening.</p>
<p>The Transports were paid today. I did not draw any pay.</p>
<p>The Canteen was sold out early this evening. No condensed milk was to be had, except Ideal</p>
