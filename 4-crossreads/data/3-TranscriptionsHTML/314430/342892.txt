<p>July 3rd :- Billeted in reserve trenches before Glicy. Enemy searching these parts with 9 inch stuff. At 10 AM <strike>o'clock</strike> ordered up to complete the digging up of a cable trench above Blangy Tronville. Had no dinner, but got a good hot tea. The stunt opens tomorrow. <strike>night</strike> Some Yankees* are going over the top with our boys, for which reason "the glorious 4th", Independence Day, was chosen as the date for this action.</p>
<p>July 4th :- "Stood to" <strike>at 8 P.M.</strike> early. Battalion ready to move up, if required, at a moments' notice. Barrage commenced at <strike>dusk</strike> very early this morning. Opened apparently between Villiers Bretonneux and Hamel. Maintained for hours with fiendish intensity. Row terrific.
