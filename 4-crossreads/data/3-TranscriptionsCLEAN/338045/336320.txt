
make my final departure.  Parade at 8-30 a.m. on station.  Plenty of German prisoners with their French guards.  Obtain a few badges as souvenirs.  Several aeroplanes going up the line.  An ambulance train of wounded arrived & another to Havre for England.  Hundreds of English Reinforcements from England.  Waiting round until 12 noon talking to some Tommies & having tea in Y.M.C.A. Hut, & leave the station for lunch & last walk around that fine old town Rouen.  Good-bye to Legge, Palliser, Scotty & all the boys, & go up town to buy a knife, soap box & some food & cigarettes.  Parade again at 3 p.m. & equipped
