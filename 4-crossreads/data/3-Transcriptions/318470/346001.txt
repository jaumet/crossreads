<p>Sunday 13 February 1916<br/>Had holiday today.  In morning walked to Toussom Signal Station.  Bridging train took over bridge this morning.  "Kiarra" thro' today.</p>
<p>Monday 14 February 1916<br/>Took charge party twelve Saprs. to Rail Head about 8 miles from Canal to supervise receiving &amp; stacking stores.  Also issuing.</p>
<p>Tuesday 15 February 1916<br/>Supervising receiving &amp; stacking stores at Rail Head.  Large quantities coming in.  Much work going on.</p>
<p>Wednesday 16 February 1916<br/>Same work as <strike>usual</strike> yesterday.  Large amount of stores still coming in.  Remainder No. 1 Sec. now here.</p>
<p>Thursday 17 February 1916<br/>Same work receiving stores.  Sec. Officer.  Mr. Keast took charge this afternoon.  Country nothing but sandy waste.</p>
<p>Friday 18 February 1916<br/>Working in R.E. yard same as last few days.  Nothing of importance reported.  Hot &amp; windy.</p>
<p>Saturday 19 February 1916<br/>Very windy, warm &amp; dusty.  Usual working in R.E. yard.</p>
<p>Sunday 20 February 1916<br/>Working in morning, had afternoon off.  Usual R.E. yard work.  Nothing of importance reported.</p>
<p>Monday 21 February 1916<br/><strike>Working R.E. yard.</strike>  Working R.E. yard, nigger driving &amp; supervising stacking timber.</p>
<p>Tuesday 22 February 1916<br/>R.E. yard work.  Same as usual.  Nothing fresh reported.  Getting very warm.</p>
<p>Wednesday 23 February 1916<br/>Usual work in R.E. yard.  Nothing of importance reported.  Windy &amp; dusty and very warm.</p>
<p>Thursday 24 February 1916<br/>Usual R.E. yard work going on.  Great activity Railway water scheme etc.  Took snaps R.E. yard.</p>
<p>Friday 25 February 1916<br/>Working in R.E. yard same as usual lately.  Nothing fresh reported.</p>
<p>Saturday 26 February 1916<br/>Working in R.E. yard as usual, receiving &amp; stacking stores.</p>
