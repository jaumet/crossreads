  
 24th Everything going well.  Usual exchange Artly rifle fire. 
 25th Usual work in tunnels & Fire trench & same old exchange of lead. 
 26th Work in new firing line going well as is everything with us.  Nothing fresh -  usual exchange shells & bullets. 
 27th Nothing out of the ordinary today.  Attended Church Service about 7.30 p.m.  A goodly crowd gathers to these Services on the hillside & all appear to  look forward to having them.  As at last service that I attend here there was a beautiful sunset & instead of the loud gun that was firing on that occasion there was a Destroyer firing in the same position work still going on in new trenches. 
 28th There was tremendous bombardment of Enemys position by our boats & land Guns near Cape Helles today.  Along the hillside the smoke from bursting Naval shells resembled a bushfire.  In the afternoon we made an attack on some Turk trenches in front of our Right Flank & some of the Light horse with part 11th Battln advanced about 800 yds & found a great many dead in some of the trenches that the Turks had abandoned about 8.30 p.m. we opened fire along the line again & under cover of this fire our boys abandoned the trenches captured earlier in the afternoon & returned again to our lines.  Our casualties were about 250.  During the night some troops were landed down the coast also British French & Indian advanced about 1,000 yds near Krithia & firmly hold position gained.
