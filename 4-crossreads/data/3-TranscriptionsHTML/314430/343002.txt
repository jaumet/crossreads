<p>Landscape artist. In the foreground, and close to the shore, appeared the "Gyppo" boats, whose masts stretched <strike>themselves towards the </strike> sternwards, as if ambitious to convert themselves into a complete circle. On either side of the Canal, and for considerable distances, there were <strike>are</strike> two protecting lines of barbed-wire entanglements, and other evidences of military occupation. Before noon we passed a Flying Corps Camp on the bank of the Canal. Tommies <strike>in khaki</strike> crowded down to the <strike>bank</strike> edge, and shouted:- <strike>"Are you going home?</strike> "Where are you going?" Home, you dopes, home," shouted a Digger. "Where else do you think we are going? Are we looking for another war?"</p>
<p>January 27th :- Left Suez late last night. It is a beautiful morning. The sea is as <strike>smoo</strike> smooth as a sheet of glass. We are now in the Red Sea, or about to enter it.</p>
<p>February 6th :- About noon today <strike>we</strike> saw on the horizon, the first glimpse of the Coast of Ceylon. Gradually it rose higher and higher out of the water. Then <strike>the</strike> palm trees became visible, next the breakwater and the <strike>square set</strike> houses. We passed into the Colombo harbour and cast anchor at 3 o'clock.</p>
