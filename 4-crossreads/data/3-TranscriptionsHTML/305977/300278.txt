<p>October 1915<br/>went on ration guard at Brigade.  Had a very uncomfortable night as it rained nearly all night &amp; we had no cover at all.  Went round to the 3rd Battery to get what information I could about Ted Rabbitts.</p>
<p>Monday 18th<br/>Weather cleared up against.  Got things fixed up &amp; a tarpaulin fixed.  There was fair bit of artillery fire in the afternoon &amp; we got well showered with dirt.  Long Tom had another premature burst &amp; 4 men were hit.  Were relieved at 6 pm &amp; went back to the trenches.  I am on orderly sergeant for the week.  Wrote a letter to Violet Blair.</p>
<p>Tuesday 19th<br/>Had very busy morning.  We had the trench mortar going strong all the morning on the Turkish trenches.  Porter &amp; Garden came back from Field Ambulance.</p>
<p>Wednesday 20th<br/>We are getting our winter quarters ready.  The weather has turned cold &amp; we get very little sun &amp; occasional showers.</p>
<p>Thursday 21st<br/>Jack Turk is very quiet.  There has been nothing doing except the usual</p>
