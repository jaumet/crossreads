
<p>&amp; then eyes are turned skywards to see her approach, for she is rather deliberate in her flight, up she goes up &amp; up rapidly then when she commences to fall becomes visible turning over &amp; over, making a noise <sub>^</sub><sup>like an awful loud whisper</sup> which we say is &quot;Wheres your Bivvy&quot; &quot;Wheres your Bivvy&quot; all the time until With &quot;Here it <u><strong>IS</strong></u>&quot; she burst with a rending crash or &quot;Crump.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; By night the burning fuse is visible &amp; as one of the boys said &quot;Its better than Henley on the Yarra&quot; &amp; another &quot;Guy Fawkes day is a fool to it.</p>
<p>&nbsp; &quot;Minnie&quot; has one great advantage &amp; that is when once the first has dropped it <s>is</s> probably marks the spot where the rest will fall &amp; again being able to see her one can often avoid her kind attentions.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Her size is various but I have seen &quot;Duds&quot; - 3 feet 9 high &amp; 10 inches in diameter.</p>
