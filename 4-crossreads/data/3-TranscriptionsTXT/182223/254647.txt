 Monday 22 March 1915  Wrote  Commenced letters to Girls &amp; Mollie.&nbsp; Wrote to Dr Dunlop, Educational adviser, to the Egyptian Government re visiting schools.&nbsp; Riding between 7 &amp; 8 a.m.&nbsp; Riding between 5 &amp; 6-20 p.m. Went along the Cairo road.&nbsp; Writing to Girls &amp; reading after dinner.&nbsp; 
 Tuesday 23&nbsp;March 1915 Ride across dessert between 7 &amp; 8 a.m.&nbsp; Reading &amp; working till 1p.m.&nbsp; Two papers Table Talk &amp; Sydney Mail War No XXIX from Mrs M. M. Knowles&nbsp; of Melbourne.&nbsp; Riding during afternoon 5 p.m to 6-10 pm. 
 Wednesday 24&nbsp;March 1915 Went Cairo by No 15 Ambulance waggon at 10 a.m. Called Ministry for Education. Spoke with Dr Dunlop (Educational advisor) &amp; Mr Daniels (an inspector) chatted with them for half an hour. The former a Scotchman from Glasgow, Acute &amp; Knowledgeable.&nbsp; Went to a primary school under aegis of latter.&nbsp;Examined the classes of each year, four years in the course. Teaching in Arabic. Maps in Arabic ( British genius) Nile from origin to mouth. Mentioned to them Livingstone, Burton, Speeke, Baker, Stanley, Eunis Pash[a] &amp; their travels&nbsp; Luncheon at  Sheppehards  Continental &nbsp;with Mrs Stokes.&nbsp; Went to Christian brothers school at Koronfish&nbsp;off the Mouski (Principal Native Street in Cairo) St Josephs. Band played &amp;c. To call again. Sheppheards to dinner. Home 11 pm.
