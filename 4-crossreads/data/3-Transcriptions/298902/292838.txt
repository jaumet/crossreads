<p>performers, giving a high class performance of good music.  In an adjoining room dancing was going on.  We sat here until midnight engaging in conversation with a well dressed civilian who worked at the Ministry of Justice.  He spoke English &amp; was very interested on learning that I had been to Gallipoli saying &ndash; then you must be an Anzac.  I found generally that that our reputation was of the best, all the news apart from what the Germans gave them, coming from Dutch sources.  All Colonial &amp; Scottish troops were firm favourites opinion being divided concerning the English proper, many expressing the view that they were all for business, money first &amp; were too insular.  They also had taken offence at a very proper act of Gt Britain, which was to send all the Belgians</p>
