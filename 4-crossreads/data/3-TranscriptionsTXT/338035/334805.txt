
AugustTuesday Aug. 1stAfternoon typewriting for Lieut. Oxley and worked to 8.30 p.m.
Wednesday 2ndAlso typing all day.
Thursday 3rdDrilling morning.  Afternoon, Typewriting for Orderly Room Amesbury at night.
Friday 4th2nd Anniversary of proclamation of war between Germany and Britain.Fatigue work - stables.
Saturday 5thAfternoon, Cricket match between "A" & "B" Section (return match).  Scores "A" Sect. 61 (Dyer 28) "B" 56 (Eric Cunningham 29).  After the match it was ascertained that "A" Section had played 12 men to our 11 men.  Received 2 Referees 2 Sunday Times & a Telegraph from Sydney.
Sunday 6thChurch Parade morning in Military Cinema.Later.  Having received a note from Harry Martin at Parkhouse I decided to pay him a visit today.  Received a pass being lucky to draw one by ballot and at 11 a.m. set out to walk it.Came to Bulford Village in time thence on to Bulford Camp and on to top of hills.  Going over the hills I could not see Parkhouse and enquired of some people the way.  They were from London on holiday (Bank Holiday) and they offered me a drink and cake.  Received with thanks.  Arrived Parkhouse at 2.30 and was surprised to not only see Harry but also Billy McLean of Field Hosp.  Altho' busy they found time to chat and Harry told some of his Gallipoli experiences.
