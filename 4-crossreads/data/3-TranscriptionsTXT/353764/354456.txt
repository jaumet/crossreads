 25.4.16 Tuesday Anzac  To  day This was celebrated right throughout the Army &amp; was observed as a Holiday, although our Unit had to work just a hard as ever.  However they gave our Train a Concert &amp; free spread at night &amp; it was enjoyed by the boys. I was unable to go but heard glowing accounts of it from Cousins.  Loaded plates of dainties very rarely reached the Tables before they were empty. There was a little rioting in Cairo where several Native Houses were burnt down.  It is a good thing that there were not many of Aust Troops here, otherwise there would have been the devil to pay. The Artillery is practicing Shrapnel Firing on a range of Hills at the back of our Camp &amp; it is, at the present time one deafening roar. Weather still very hot &amp; unbearable in the middle of the day.
