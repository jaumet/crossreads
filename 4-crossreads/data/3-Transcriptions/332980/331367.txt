<p>making my quarters during my Stay in London, after Booking my Bed which was No 314 &amp; up on the 4th Floor I then walked upstairs, as I mentioned in No1 Diary during my Stay last year before going over to France &amp; described the Rooms, so I need not go into particulars, As usual my Room was a single one &amp; everything nice &amp; clean &amp; well up to date, I then had a Wash &amp; a Brush Up, then went down for Tea which cosisted of Boiled Cod &amp; Potatoes, &amp; Bread &amp; Butter which I thoroughly enjoyed, I then took a Stroll over Waterloo Bridge &amp; along the Strand &amp; booked a Seat in the Stalls at the Hippodrome where Mr Harry Tate was playing in a Revue called "Box of Tricks" supported by Miss Dorothy Vane &amp; a splendid Coy, likewise an Orchestra of 50 Performers, &amp; they played beautiful &amp; the Scenery &amp; Effects were put on in grand style &amp; the Singing &amp; Dresses were first Class some very beautiful Costumes were worn by the Ladies the Revue started at 8.15 Pm &amp; finished at 10.15 Pm &amp; I must say I spent a most delightful Evening, afterwards as it was very light, I strolled along the Strand</p>
