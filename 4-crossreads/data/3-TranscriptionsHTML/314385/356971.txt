<p><strike>Saturdy Aug 26th</strike><br/>We got into the motor cars &amp; moved off &amp; after &frac34; of an hour found ourselves at Beacourt 11.30 p.m. were we were conducted to our bedours half a roof over us &amp; a brick &amp; stone floor what luxury no blankets</p>
<p>Sat Aug 26th<br/>Up at 7.30. breakfast of bacon etc nothing in the way of duty doing.</p>
<p>I took a walk out towards the line of trenches a vast mass of shell holes being constantly played on by shells from the enemy. On returning we were given duty unloading and carrying the patients into the dressing room.</p>
<p>There are a good number of casualties comming in but fortunately not many deaths. Our bearers (6th) have just returned from the trenches (being releived) and look a sorry lot with about an inch of beard.</p>
<p>We have just received orders to leave for our H.Q. in cars &amp; arrived at noon Badencourt to take up duties there.</p>
