<p>While we were in Cape Town we took on board 24 deserters from a previous transport that had left a few days previously. Several of our own men who broke off from the march and came on board the next day were fined &pound; 2 each and given 7 days in the [indecipherable]. The yellow flag was hauld down when we were out a few hours.</p>
<p>Saturday July 1. 1916.</p>
<p>A fine morning. The ship is rolling a lot and we are in the South Atlantic Ocean, enroute to England. The course is N.N.West and the wind is from the N.W. Up to 8. a.m. we made 176 <strike>miles</strike> knots since</p>
