<p>1917<br/>Raided Signal school cookhouse &amp; secured four tins M &amp; V rations.  Bought two loaves of bread, I alone having any money &ndash; 7 francs!  Slept in barn.</p>
<p>Nov. 16<br/>Left p.m. per lorry for Ramillies-Werquin where we rejoined battalion.</p>
<p>Nov. 17<br/>Spell at Ramillies-Werquin.  I left after tea per lorry for Blequin.  Slept in empty house.  No rations or money to buy food.</p>
<p>Nov. 18<br/>Battalion arrd. p.m.</p>
<p>Nov. 19<br/>Mess orderly.  Left per boat for Tingry nr. Samer, a distance of 22&frac12; kilometres.  Billetted in large empty house with fire-places &ndash; tres bon.  Hope to stay several days.</p>
<p>Nov. 20<br/>Kit inspection a.m.  Pay p.m.</p>
<p>Nov. 21<br/>Formal parade for 5 minutes.</p>
<p>Nov. 22<br/>Ditto.  Settling down, Samer.</p>
<p>Nov. 23<br/>On billeting scheme as interpreter with Lt. Marr.</p>
<p>Nov. 24<br/>Still engaged on scheme.</p>
<p>Nov. 25<br/>Communion.</p>
<p>Nov. 26<br/>Parade.</p>
<p>Nov 27<br/>Escort to Heningen for court martial prisoners.</p>
<p>Nov. 28<br/>Parade a.m.  Borrowed 100 frs.</p>
