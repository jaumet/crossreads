<p>fires (coke) both in the Office &amp; in the hut, electric light, hot baths, day &amp; night, &amp; fairly good tucker   I often remark that it is not soldiering at all; we are only civilians in uniform.<br/>I am going to have a lot of trouble this winter with rheumatism, I am afraid.  Even now at nights I am constantly awakened with rheumatism in the knee joints.<br/>We have been issued with rifles &amp; ammunition, &amp; there are rumours that were are going over to Ireland as a dismounted Unit, in connection with this Irish trouble.  It is going to be rotten fighting your own countrymen, practically.  I hope it never does come to that though, for goodness only knows there is enough trouble in the world as it is.<br/>Thank you so much, Father, dear, for fixing up my financial business.  Sometimes I <strike>ev</strike> feel I would give all I possessed to be transplanted into the middle of the home-circle again for 1 brief hour.  It is going to be a long struggle, &amp; it be several years yet before we see Sunny New South Wales again I am afraid.<br/>I sincerely trust that this finds you all at "Cairnleith well &amp; happy.  I had a nasty cold whilst in London a fortnight ago, but have lost it now thank goodness.</p>
