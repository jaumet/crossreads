Later, English voices, those of Lancashire Fusilliers principally, kept coming out of the dark asking who we were.  We gave a "Coo-ee!".<br/>The ship's speed had to be reduced to about 5 knots per hour so that the wash would not injure the canal banks and all night a searchlight hung from the bows to assist the navigators.  The canal is 100 miles in length and we anchored at Port Said early the following day.  Almost immediately we commenced taking coal and water on board.
