 &amp; really made a bad start, not being quite ready for the order to charge when it did come. The artillery on both sides were by then pounding away, but I had no fear as I clambered over the parapet &amp; went forward in the darkness with the boys. It was a weird sight to see the crouching forms going forward. In our sector we met with little opposition in the way of machine gun or rifle fire, &amp; I for my part did not at the time see a German, tho' several of our men had hand-to-hand encounters : my Davies shot one. With shrapnel bursting over us &amp; H.E.s around us we pushed on beyond the 1st German line now held by D &amp; C companies, losing men here &amp; there but still going forward into what was a wood, but now a churned up mass of earth &amp; splinters.
