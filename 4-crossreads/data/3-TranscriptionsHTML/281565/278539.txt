<p>snow-covered fields at the exit.  When night fell we we passing the more gently undulating country, with the prospect of running into Paris at night.  Again we settled down for our night's rest &amp; being tired slept well, to be awakened at 4 a.m. Thursday 30th for hot tea, &amp; to find that we were still "on our way" to Paris.  A heavy frost spread over the country;  red checked peasant girls in sabots &amp; shawls, were already abroad, waving excitedly to our train as we <u>crept</u> along;  farmers carts were leaving the houses for the fields &amp; snowy ox teams slowly drawing the ploughs in the whitened <strike>field</strike> paddocks.
