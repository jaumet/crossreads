<p>entanglements and across country riddled with shell holes, and racing through a wood with trip-wires etc. etc. on every hand. This we go through with fixed-bayonets in the 'on guard' position, ready to stab every 'Fritz' we see. Sand-bags &amp; chaff-bags filled with straw take the place of Fritz for practice purposes.</p>
<p>Our skill with the bayonet was tested by means of a number of small tin rings on rods at which we had to run &amp; try &amp; pick with the bayonet-points. Not bad sport at all!</p>
<p>After lunch we each threw a live bomb and were then lectured on First Aid in the Field &amp; sundry other matters of a similar nature.</p>
<p>In the evening I took a stroll down the road as far as Etaples Railway Station to see the locomotives etc; some of which are quite novel &amp; really interesting.
