At 6.45 the Newarp light ship was abeam, 8.30 fog lifting & thinning going full ahead again.  Instead of going down to breakfast I got the steward to bring it to my cabin.  This saved me the trouble of dressing.  After partaking of breakfast I turned into my bunk again and slept till the steward called me at 11.30 for my bath.  After having a good cold tubbing I felt quite alright.  I then paced the deck until dinner time.  At noon we were abeam of the "Sunk" lightship, & shortly after passed a destroyer of the latest type & several trawlers
