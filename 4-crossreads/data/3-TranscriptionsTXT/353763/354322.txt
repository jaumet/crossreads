 The polished instrument is far younger &amp; as I remarked belongs to the Neolithic or  new  stone age  -  Then came Metals &ndash; Copper &ndash; Bronze &ndash; gold silver &amp; last of all the age we are now in  the iron  age  - 
  All  mankind comes under this big generalization Old stone &ndash; New stone &ndash; Copper &ndash; Iron  -  That's a nice little bit of anthropology for my son which these old axes will enforce  -  I then returned to the ship &amp; I learnt that we had orders from the Encounter to proceed to Sydney on Tuesday at 2 pm &amp; pay off  -  Well  -  That's our finish on this commission  -  After lunch I went on shore again in the mean time we had pushed off &amp; anchored in our old spot &ndash; for the last time  -  I spent some time in the Museum, talking to the Curator examining Fijian products &ndash; their war clubs &ndash; axes &ndash; pottery &ndash; models of canoes &ndash; models of houses, cloth for dresses &ndash; Kava bowls &amp; cups  -  Also Fijian shells which form a fine collection  -  They have a small number of fossils in the limestone collected by Sir Everard im Thurn a former governor
