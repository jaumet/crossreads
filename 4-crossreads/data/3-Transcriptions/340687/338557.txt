<p>September, 1917. Monday 10<br/>Fine and warm. 6.00 check parade and told marching order with blankets at 8. Fell in cleaned up and off at 9 to [indecipherable] where we caught motor lorries 14m each and come via Outersteene Bailleul &amp; Dickebusch to behind Ypres. on main road. Did numerous fatigues &amp; rested &amp; read paper</p>
<p>Artillery &amp; aircraft lively, 10th not moving, Bailleul fine town and only slightly shelled. Guns, cross roads railway &amp; balloons close by. Fritz shelled heavily at 7 and Summerfield was wounded in left buttock. I standing alongside. Took him over to dressing station and home about 10.</p>
