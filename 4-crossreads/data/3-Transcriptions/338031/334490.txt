Once a man is warned for draft he mustn't leave camp. If he does he is disgraced &amp; gaoled. These departures as a rule took place in the evening. As soon as word came through the sergeants &amp; corporals hastily commenced to beat[?] their men up. Every man had his equipment ready to adjust, so there was no unnecessary delay. A large number would be having their last farewell</p>
