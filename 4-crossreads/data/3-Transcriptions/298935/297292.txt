<p>occupied by a sister of the late Sultan.  In its composition there is mahogany, ebony, ivory and silver and it cost &pound;6,000.  Altogether the mosque contains &pound;52,000 worth of silver.  There are several tombs and around each are solid silver stands 5 ft high each supporting a single electric light.  Some very old copies of the Koran are resting upon a silver table presented by Lord Kitchener.<br/>The Egyptian Museum is a fine building containing thousands of relics from the days of the Pharoahs.  The Nile Barrage, 17 miles from Cairo, is very interesting.  The river is dammed here so that the annual overflowing, <strike>of</strike> so necessary for the well-being of the country, can be regulated.<br/>On the 29th March 1915 we were reviewed by Sir Ian Hamilton</p>
