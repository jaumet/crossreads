After fixing up tents, we considered ourselves free &amp; as it was just on dark, a lot of us when down &amp; had a good old swim in the Canal &ndash; it is about a couple of hundred yards wide where we went &amp; very deep &ndash; it is the  only  best swim I've had since I left Bundaberg &amp; of course I was in my glory. Almost immediately one fellow got into trouble &amp; we got him ashore safely after a lot of trouble. 
 Several large Boats passed. Ismailia is a very pretty Town &amp; quite refreshing to see the quantity of trees &amp; the fine avenues &amp; parks, it is built on one of the many Lakes of the Canal. In the lake there are several Monitors &amp; Cruisers. It is marvellous the amount of shipping that goes thro the Canal daily. We are now Camped on the Arabian side of the Canal &amp; in the first line of defence &ndash; It is a very large Camp &amp; all units are armed to the teeth.
