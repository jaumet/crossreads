Of course the majority would lose the limb affected as gangrene had set in &amp; made a lot of headway.  Indications that we are to have another move so are tipping Sunday for it as that is the usual moving day.  We received the congratulations of all the heads during the day, our unit by far doing the best work having treated more than double the number of patients treated by the 15th who were working alongside.  Strictly speaking, we the 14th, should only have taken men of our own brigade but we did a large number of the 15th &amp; also the 8th.  Everyone is pleased at the result as all hands worked well &amp; 'tis good to receive recognition.</p>
