<p>and feel relieved at getting away. On the way through Ploegsteert just missed a shell which burst just behind us. Got to wagon lines at 12 oclock.</p>
<p>26th June 1917. Woke at 8 oclock after a fair night's rest. Fooled around the lines all day. Got to bed at 6 oclock.</p>
<p>27th June 1917. Reveille 5 30 am  on cooks fatigue all day. Battery with the 3rd Div was inspected &amp; commended by Gen. Monash today.</p>
<p>28th June 1917. Reveille 5 30. left camp at 9 30 for leave. Went to Baileull &amp; had a very fair day. Visited our friends and had meals in there. Went to the Show &amp; got back in a blinding thunderstorm at 9 oclock.</p>
<p>29th June 1917. Reveille 5 30. Fooling  round all day. General Plumer the head of our big fight spoke to us today. Complimenting us on the work done by the artillery.</p>
<p>30th June 1917. Reveille 5.30. On stables all morning. Visited by the 1st Division band today &amp; they tuned us all the afternoon  Plenty of beer etc floating around. Band left a 4 oclock  &amp; we did stables till 5. Did not go out  Got to bed early.</p>
<p>1st July 1917. Reveille 5.30. Very easy day. Wrote some letters home. Got to bed early</p>
<p>2nd July 1917. Reveille 5 30. On bath parade. Fooling round rest of day. Part of Battery left for new position tonight. Posted letter to Rana.</p>
