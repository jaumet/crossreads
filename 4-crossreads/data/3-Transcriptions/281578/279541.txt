<p>as we filed along to get our stew, &amp;  was talking to the chap in front of me, when a shell burst right in my ear up on the parapet, it seemed to force the words back down my throat. Then when we were filing back, one hit the parapet &amp; knocked the dirt in one chap's tin, &amp; just missed mine as a chap was pouring my tea into it. There were 4 or 5 of us at the spot, &amp; we had a miraculous escape, as the shell was a dud, &amp; there it was poking through into the trench without having exploded. Tuesday 18 June<br/>Slept till noon when old Mitchell &amp; I had a hot drink &amp; a snack. In the afternoon, I walked through the sap back to B Coy. in reserve to see Boo, &amp; got a nice lot of letters which the Bank in London had sent on to him. I got 4 from Elsie, &amp; 1 from Mother &amp; Mone. Min was then up at Gwydirfield for a holiday. Just as I am writing this in my diary, LT Col Murray, V.C. DSO &amp; bar, D.C.M. walked by &amp; asked me how I was getting on. He is a grand chap, very brave, &amp; very, very  few men live to gain the honors he has. Our company are relieving C Coy in the front line tomorrow night, &amp; I have to go up tonight in advance for our platoon. Wednesday 19 June Raining at 2 a.m. when the advance party from the different platoons assembled at Coy. Headq'r's.
