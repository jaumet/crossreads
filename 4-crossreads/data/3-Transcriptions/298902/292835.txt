<p>that none should wed his daughter except a greater artist than himself.  Quentin thereupon went to Italy and returning to Antwerp, visited the home of the artist &amp; looking into the studio saw that it was empty, with a partly finished picture on the easel, he thereupon painted a fly upon it &amp; then hid behind some curtains.  Soon the artist returned &amp; began on the picture &amp; on noticing the fly, flicked at it with a cloth, discovering that it was not natural, calling his daughter he said &ndash; the man who painted that fly is a greater artist than I, and fit to be my son in law whereupon Quentin discovers himself and all ends happily.
