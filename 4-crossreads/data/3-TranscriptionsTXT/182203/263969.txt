 of patients here. I had sent my   cable  or should have said I had seen the dear boy.  He is doing splendidly but will take a long time to be quite well again. He was shot through his R.   chower  lower cheek, splintered the jaw bone a little then it passed through to the other side  He can talk alright  &amp; is able to eat food now. The poor kiddie was awfully delighted to see me    &amp;  got a great surprise.       He is liked very much &amp; seems to be very happy. I see him every &ndash; day. He is walking about a little now. Am in a fearful hurry. Fondest love  &amp; kisses to you all  &amp; love to my friends  &amp; I am is perfect health. Hope you are all well &amp; happy. I have not received any letters yet. More wounded soldiers arriving. Yours lovingly                                                                                                                                                                                          Edith
