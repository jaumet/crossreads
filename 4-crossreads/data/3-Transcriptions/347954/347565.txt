<p>our side reach 250 &amp; further down they amounted to 2000.  Things are much the same as ever &amp; today is the (70) seventieth day of our occupation.  I haven't been half as energetic as of old partly due to the severe heat &amp; also to a feeling of lassitude due to patient swatting in a confined area.  The Turks appear to be awfully busy in the region of Achi Babe &amp; probably before long our eyes &amp; <strike>eg</strike> ears will be alive to moving forms &amp; gun roars.  A Turks gun managed to hit a French ammunition magazine which disappeared in smoke some two hundred feet in the air.  It caused a very spectacular scene &amp; was admired by a wondering crowd who never doubted for one moment it was John Turk's effects going in the air instead of our Allies.  Luckily no one was hurt &amp; no more premature bursts occured to mar the day.  The conditions for fighting during the day are awfully rotten &ndash; the heat is so intense &amp; in consquence the scrapping is left entirely to the big guns.  They roar &amp; appear to be doing great works but as we don't see results, we are inclined to think poorly of them.  The Turkish guns &ndash;</p>
