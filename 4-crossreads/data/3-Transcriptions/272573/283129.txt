<p>The days show signs of shortening and there is also a slight touch of cold about the air.</p>
<p>Sept 5th. Tues.<br/>"Leo John" got his stars.</p>
<p>While out this afternoon we got some lovely Blackberries growing by the roadside.</p>
<p>An exceedingly heavy bombardment is going on to-night with our Artillery.</p>
<p>Sept 7th. Thurs.<br/>Evac by barge on the Canal. Plenty of aeroplanes flying about to-day.</p>
<p>Not much dressing doing. Have commenced painting the D.R. white and brown.</p>
<p>Sept 12th. Tues.<br/>The Harvests seem to be all gathered in.</p>
<p>The trees are putting on their autumn garb. The variety of tints shown on the trees are gorgeous.</p>
<p>Sept 13th. Wed.<br/>Met Willie Zatt who had travelled from Steinvoorde to see us. He had set out to walk but was fortunate in getting a lift part of the way. He looks well but has had a good deal of roughing it down in the Somme where he has been this last month.</p>
<p>Continuous bombardment nearly all night by our own artillery.</p>
