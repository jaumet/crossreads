<p>We stand in this sunken road, cold and miserable for about an hour and then our officer informs us that as we may be here for a couple of days, and as all the available dugouts are occupied by another battalion, we shall have to set to and make homes for ourselves.&nbsp; We say very little, but think an awful lot.&nbsp; However there is nothing for it, so some go looking round and commence making a shelter for themselves, while others just lie down anywhere.</p>
<p>Another chap and myself, &quot;fed up&quot; with everything, promptly make up our mind&#39;s to commit suicide, but don&#39;t like to do the thing in a way that may reflect discredit on ourselves after we have gone, so we decide to let the enemy be blamed.&nbsp; We go up on the higher land where the shells are thickest and lie down, with just a blanket over us for shelter.&nbsp; Of course nothing happens to us, shells burst all round but not one comes near enough to harm us.&nbsp; We come to the conclusion that we are being saved for some reason.</p>
<p>We wake (oh yes! we got some sleep) and after breakfast an order is sent round that all rifles and ammunition have to be cleaned.&nbsp; Of course we all naturally jump to the conclusion that we are again going to &quot;move forward&quot;.&nbsp; We always did think the wrong thing.</p>
<p>The whole of that sunken road looked like a huge rabbit-warren. There was hardly a foot of the sides which had not some hole in it, made either by the labour of mans hands or the labour of mans brain (shells).&nbsp; Stretcher bearers are still passing along with their burdens, and the shelling still continues to add to their numbers.</p>
<p>The terrible picture of last night is still before me.&nbsp; The long lines of stretchers with their wounded, being added to each minute.&nbsp; The continuous cry of &quot;stretcher bearers wanted here&quot;, and &quot;make way for the stretcher bearers&quot;.&nbsp; The inferno of bursting shells.&nbsp; The rain and mud.&nbsp; The constant rushing here and there to avoid a shell, and the climax when we find that we are to stay there for two days, after our late experiences fairly</p>
