<p>The wood with all its straight stems might be of stone.</p>
<p>Nothing moves</p>
<p>Ah what is that I hear in the very distance of distance?</p>
<p>I have heard the like before, but only in France</p>
<p>It is the drumming of bombarding guns.</p>
<p>Guns of the western front.</p>
<p>As I have heard them on the Somme. The battle raging now is more terrific than that of the Somme.</p>
<p>God have mercy on our enemy if that be so.</p>
<p>I can hear the guns or armies</p>
<p>I feel quite certain of that</p>
<p>Suddenly a pine cone falls a few yards in front of me.</p>
<p>The stillness is broken for a moment</p>
<p>Naturally I must look upward.</p>
<p>I see a little brown squirrel high up amongst the needle plumes</p>
<p>He has noticed my movement and becomes part of the petrified stillness</p>
<p>I play his own game and might</p>
