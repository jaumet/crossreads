<p>and proceeded on her long trip, also ours! The scenery on the trip was very good; one thing I noticed was the absence of men officials on the railway, in nearly every case a woman served in that capacity.</p>
<p>We reached Calais about 1 o'clock. I know we were going a round about way to our final destination. The war traffic was the cause of this. All the peace time running of trains was considerably altered.</p>
<p>We travelled al that afternoon till 7 that evening. We finally reached Bailleul, (alas now in crumbling ruins!) Here we disentrained, and having had rations served out to us, we proceeded to a transport camp, about a mile distant. The streets of Bailleul was dotted with "Aussies" out on leave from their billets. Bailleul was considerably knocked about then.</p>
