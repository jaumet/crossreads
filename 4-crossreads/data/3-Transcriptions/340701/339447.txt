<p>Artillery to-day.  He pasted everything he could see &amp; he did'nt miss much because he had nine Balloons up.  I hav'nt seen that many German Balloons in the air at once before.</p>
<p>Monday 8 Jan. 1917<br/>Moved to Miault Dump yesterday.  Elson &amp; I secured a good Camp with some details.  In the evening we went to Albert which is practically the same as when we were there before.  We had a bottle of Champ &amp; Biscuits in a deserted house and came home after a very quiet time.</p>
<p>Tuesday 9-1-17<br/>Was working on night shift in the Mill making Duck Boards.</p>
<p>Wed. 10-1-17<br/>An awfull lot of <u>heavy</u> gunfire during the last two days.  There must be something doing alright.</p>
<p>Saturday 13-1-17<br/>Surprising how little snow we get here.  We had a slight fall the other night.  Rather difficult to see the Duck Boards on night Shifts.  The German prisoners working in the mill do quite a trade in souvenirs.  They have numbers of every trade among them and work like</p>
