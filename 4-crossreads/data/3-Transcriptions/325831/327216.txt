<p>3.<br/>occasion when [Piet] Retief, a prominent Boer, &amp; some of his countrymen were treacherously murdered by Dingaan, a Zulu chieftain, whilst negotiating some treaty. The festival is a Public Holiday. It has not been kept up the last 2 years on account of the War. But was celebrated this year. It was thought that the discontented Dutch &ndash; followers of De Wet, &amp; other truculent fellows &ndash; would seize this auspicious occasion to cause trouble. They have ben apparently talking very tersely in these parts of late, &amp; showing much "bloody-mindedness".</p>
<p>Rumour stated that we were held up at Durban in case we should be required to do some small deed on them. But we were denied even that</p>
