<p>Sunday 25 August 1918<br/>Packed up in the morng.  Went into Havre &ndash; met Miss Irwin &amp; came back to tea at 40th Stat.  Caught six o'c train up to the Line.  Played Bridge on the trip.</p>
<p>Monday 26 August 1918<br/>Arrived Corbie at about 8 a.m. &amp; walked out to Rein. Camp with Mehan.  Met Capt. Gilchrist &amp; Col. Bennett out there.  Hear that the Div. is to do a hop over in the morng.</p>
<p>Tuesday 27 August 1918<br/>Got up early but did not move forward until after lunch.  Had a long march to the Transport line over old Boche ground.  Slept in a tent &amp; it rained.</p>
<p>Wednesday 28 August 1918<br/>Pitt &amp; a few others came along from the Bn. to a school.  I went forward &amp; joined the Bn. &amp; was sent to C Coy.</p>
<p>Thursday 29 August 1918<br/>Had orders to move at 10.30 &amp; went forward &ndash; the Hun having gone further back.  Spent a very cold &amp; draughty night near Estree.  Ground shows much sign of Hun retreat.</p>
<p>Friday 30 August 1918<br/>Moved nearer Peronne in the early morning.  The news is still good &ndash; that he has gone still further back.  We were shelled out of our original position &amp; moved to quieter quarters.</p>
<p>Saturday 31 August 1918<br/>Moved round N of Peronne &amp; crossed the Somme in the evening.  Went up to some old French trenches for night.  Saw Kernaghan &amp; he got whacked a minute or so after.<br/>Marched further during the past three days than ever I've done in France!!  Jerry <u>can</u> move.</p>
