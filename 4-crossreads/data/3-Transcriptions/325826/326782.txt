I went there to find out why my military cheque did not come in the middle of the month &amp; the man in charge of that part smilingly told me that it was there already filled in but they had lost the address &amp; did not know where to send it!  Sublime isn't it?  Anyhow I think Wal's address is quite safe like this &amp; there can be no mistake.<br/>Lieut W.L.W.<br/>14th Reinf<br/>2nd Battalion<br/>1st Aust Div Base Depot<br/>A.I.F.<br/>Perhaps the A.I.F. is redundant but it helps to make it noticeably clear I think.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I will have another try to find out the address best to use before I come away from here, I meant fully to do so again this morning but I think the sublimely simple explanation of the delay in my cheque rather upset me, I had been prepared for all sorts of trouble &amp; to find it only that, sort of stunned me &amp; made me forget other things.<br/>The S.17 in Wal's letter is a code for a certain town &amp; I think it would hardly do to use that because they might be moved any time, though of course it may mean that the Army P.O will be always there, I don't know.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It is so different not having Rid to go &amp; ferret out things for me, I daresay he would have been able to get at higher officials than I could at H'quarters.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wal's letter may seem a little strange to you but there is a reason for that and I am sorry I can't tell you about it now.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Why this mail that has brought his brought nothing from Rid I can't think: I am getting</p>
