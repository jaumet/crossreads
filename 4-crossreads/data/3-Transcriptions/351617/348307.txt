<p>Next morning we were up with "Reveille", had breakfast, and at 9.30 were called out on parade, where camp orders were read out, Bounderies Etc made known, &amp; other things too numerous to mention. The following days were days of hard training with Rifle &amp; Bayonet &amp; later with Bombs.</p>
<p>6 am, Reveille, out on parade, for physical exercises, 7.30 breakfast, 9 am parade, 12 30 Dinner, 2 pm, parade till 4 pm, then if one wasnt on Guard or fatigue, have tea &amp; go for a walk, or to the pictures, or a concert, or Down to the YMCA &amp; write home.</p>
<p>Canteens were very plentiful where we could get cigarettes Etc, &amp; where a hot meal could be had up till 11 pm at night &amp; as you may guess this part of the business was well patronised especially after about a 10 mile walk round the the surrounding</p>
