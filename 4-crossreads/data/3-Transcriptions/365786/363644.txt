<p>supplied thanks to the continued efforts of the various Red Cross centres in Australia &amp; the good new management in Egypt.<br/>When we arrived in Egypt at the beginning of May the first of the wounded &ndash; our wounded had arrived.  When a man is wounded he is first attended to on the spot by a doctor if one is available or the A.M.C. orderlies or his own mates &amp; hurried back as soon as possible to the nearest clearing station or Field Ambulance.  These are like small base hospitals &amp; there urgent work is done.  Limbs needing immediate amputation are removed etc. &amp; from there the men <strike>are</strike> were transferred to the Hospital ships &amp; removed to the <strike>Base</strike> General Hospitals at the Base</p>
