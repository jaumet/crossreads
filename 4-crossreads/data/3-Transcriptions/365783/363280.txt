<p>Westland &amp; his brother in law can then go over it with the fork &amp; get out &amp; burn as many of the blackberries as they can. The ploughman must be careful of the new fruit trees &amp; of the three little jacarandas in the corner near Miss Cripps tennis court. He should also not interfere with any of the little gums that you would like left. I think one of the first things to do is to take down the remainder of the paling fence; it serves as a harbour for the pest; when taken down it should be stacked in a place where the blackberries are not likely to grow. The strip of ground between the tree plantation and the fence should also be dealt with at the same time. I have no faith in chemical eradications.</p>
