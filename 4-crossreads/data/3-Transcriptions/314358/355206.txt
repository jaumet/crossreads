<p>7<br/>Of a place where a community lived and laboured in peace.</p>
<p>Bare, marked skeletons of beautiful trees which a few weeks ago were brave and glorious in their summer green, now with the mere bones of their poor shattered fingers spread out to the sky in fearful hopeless supplication for the curse of war to pass, and by their still tottering presence excentuating wreck, and marking with utter desolation the place of ruin.</p>
<p>Only the staggering bones of trees stand there, where a village, almost a town housed a community.</p>
