
<p>September&nbsp;&nbsp; 1918</p>
<p>22 SUN&nbsp; Dull. windy day. raining at</p>
<p>night. Boys left</p>
<p>for Australia 8 am. Good</p>
<p>send off. No amm.</p>
<p>Guns active on both sides. Yanks</p>
<p>started to arrive. Rec. letters</p>
<p>from Maggie &amp; Amy Mc. Writing</p>
<p>at night. Quiet.</p>
<p>23 MON&nbsp; Fine day. 1<sup><u>st</u></sup> Div. Inf went</p>
<p>out of action. Yanks in galore</p>
<p>coming in &amp; taking over. Our</p>
<p>artillery to cover them. Guns</p>
<p>active on both sides. Rec. letter</p>
<p>from Amy. Writing.</p>
<p>24 TUES&nbsp; Fine day. Amm. going</p>
<p>up for stunt. Americans &amp; flying</p>
<p>columns &amp; B.A.C&#39;<sup>s</sup> arriving in galore.</p>
<p>Tanks going up. Quiet. Few</p>
<p>bombs at night. very handy</p>
<p>Little rain. fine after. Posted letters</p>
<p>25 WED&nbsp; to Amy. Maggie &amp; May. Amm</p>
<p>going up in galore also guns</p>
<p>etc. Good news from the east.</p>
<p>&#39;Hun&#39; counter attack in morning</p>
<p>repulsed. Quiet. Few bombs</p>
<p>at night.</p>
