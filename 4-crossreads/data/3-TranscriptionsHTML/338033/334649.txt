<p>and was not found until twenty six hours later. His<br/>Lower limbs were paralized and he had to be spoon feed for a long time. The easiest way to get about, was to crawl on all fours, but this was the cause of many cases of trench hands.  Every day we were losing men with trench hands and trench feet. Some poor chaps had both, and if anyone suffered it was these men. Just as we left this sector, still we were lucky in striking tracks into our next sector at Le Sars.</p>
