<p>Fri. 3rd March 1916.<br/>Sandstorm yesterday  &amp; today. Could not leave the tents.<br/>Not enough water &amp; a shortage of tucker.<br/>The "Gyppo" engine drivers are selling us the water out of their engine boilers.</p>
<p>Sunday. 19th .March.<br/>The Prince of Wales was to inspect us today but a soon as he arrived with Birdwood the boys broke from their lines &amp; flocked around him &amp; his horse.<br/>I suppose this is a prelude to our embarkation for France.<br/>Lt. Col. Macnaughton left Bn. yesterday for hospital.</p>
<p>Wed. March 22nd.<br/>Struck camp this afternoon  &amp; crossed Canal to Serapeum East Railway siding.<br/>Bivouaced for night &amp; entrained early next morning for Alexandria.</p>
<p>Thurs. Mar. 23rd.<br/>Embarked on the SS. "Simla" &amp; left Alexandria tonight for France.</p>
