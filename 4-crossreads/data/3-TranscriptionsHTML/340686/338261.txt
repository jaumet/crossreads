<p>and we had a good chat in the dugout. Ingram returned at 8 and ate excellently well. Wrote diary up read a little and went to sleep. Our job to continue at 4 to-morrow. Jack left a packet of cigarettes and a bottle.</p>
<p>20-12-16<br/>Very cold all and snow lay unmelted on the thick ice. However, the day was quite fine and sunny Aeroplanes active &amp; Fritzs artillery very active bombarding all day long billets adjacent to here Appeared to be a stunt on our right (French) and we heard of French success at Verdun and British at Arras. Roads were extremely hard and [motor carrier] had difficult in moving owing to their slippery nature.</p>
