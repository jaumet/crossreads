<p>July 1917<br/>but they paid very little &amp; we hardly got our expenses out of it.  However we were very satisfied &amp; the girls enjoyed themselves immensely.  After dinner Sergeant Long came round &amp; took us to an English friend's place where we had a quiet evening.  I was terribly tired &amp; managed to go to sleep on the sofa so also did Gurney, so we apologised &amp; explained &amp; went to the Hotel to bed.  On Monday morning the others went away.  I went down to the beach &amp; spent most of the day there with the girls, also the next day right up till the time I had to catch the 3.30 train back to Ismailia.  It was hard breaking away.  Got back to camp about 9.30 pm.  We sent a big batch of reinforcements out to the Regt at midnight 37 altogether, which leaves us little more than 50 here in the camp hardly enough to carry on with.</p>
