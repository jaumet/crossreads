
August 1915of the older chaps had gone away sick.  Pickering & Elliott were wounded & Cheater went away sick, so also did Johnson & Bacon.  Received my mail which included letters from Kate, Doug & Mother & a big parcel from Cousin Meg, containing a shirt, sox & a handkerchief also a cake & several other odds & ends which were very acceptable.  Went into the firing line at 7 pm.  Things were very quiet.  The Turk hardly fired a shot.  Major Oatley came along & rather surprised me by asking if I would put my name in for a commission in the infantry.  I told him I would think it over & let him know next day.
