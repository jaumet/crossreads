<p>25 Nov. 1918</p>
<p>We started from Bohain at 9.30 on Saturday &amp; marched through very miserable battle wrecked country to Massinghien, where we stayed in the same billets as the 3rd had occupied when they were there the week before.  They were underground in a factory, &amp; the beds were composed of wire netting stretched over planks, &amp; for these we had to thank our mutual friend Fritz.</p>
<p>A few days before we started it had been found out that we were to garrison [indecipherable] &amp; that we should have to march most of the way.  Then rumours commenced of the expressed desire of Mr. Hughes, in contradiction to our Corps leader General Monash that we should not go to Germany, but go back to Australia as soon as possible.  How much truth there was in this I am unable to say,</p>
