<p>(34)<br/>This was about the normal number of casualties, occurring mostly at <br/>Night (when the opposing troops were on the alert). For our division had<br/>"spoilt a good home" according to the Tommies and fairly frequently raided<br/>the enemy trenches, always entailing heavy casualties such as fifty out of<br/>eighty men sent over. These men were specially trained and were know as<br/>raiders, receiving concessions in other matters and a good rum issue after <br/>the stunt.</p>
<p>These raids usually took place about midnight and were generally proceded<br/>&nbsp;by about 10 minutes barrage fire from our guns to cut the barbed wire in front<br/>&nbsp;of the enemy's trenches and prevent him from reinforcing his front line.<br/>&nbsp;Usually however he was content to retire to the cover of his strong dugouts<br/>&nbsp;and leave his artillery barrage to contest our mens advance and catch them<br/>&nbsp;on the their return over no mans land. This stretch was mostly from 150 to<br/>&nbsp;400 yards wide.</p>
