<p>the sunset. At last, at exactly 7 o'clock, the order was given to advance. Up the gangway we went &amp;   proceeded to descent to out troop deck; but  the proceeding part of it was as far as we got. The men went down very gingerly, a few at a time, &amp;   came back very fast in a bunch. Even now when I think of it, I can feel the bite of those fumes in my lungs. That night the men ate &amp; slept on deck.</p>
<p>Our stay was fairly prolonged, but I think the men appreciated the daily route marches. The inhabitants were so kind &amp;   generous; &amp;   all along the route fruit cigarettes &amp;   even books were showering into the column. There was only one fly in the ointment. Nobody could get leave.</p>
<p>About five days after our arrival, we went for a march through the city itself &amp;   I was given permission to break off for an hour &amp;    a half to see the sights. Mr. Conway was with me, &amp;   when we had satisfied the inner man, we hired a   <strike>cab</strike>  taxi &amp;   saw what we could which was not very much. That march seems to have put the finishing touch on things, &amp;   during the evening several men broke ship.</p>
