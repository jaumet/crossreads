 After the heavy work of packing, carrying medical panniers &amp; marquees over to the waggons etc, we lay about awaiting orders to move. Finally, as nobody seemed to know what we were to do, Tom Smith Ritchie &amp; I led the way by depositing ourselves &amp; our kits in an Ambulance waggon about to move off to the A.D.S. 
 We went at a walking pace along the main road from the sugar refinery, through Marquaix &amp; then Roisel, which village had been, in the three offensive, shelled to atoms:
