<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;put in most of the day reading, writing &amp; sleeping- in fact I was asleep when your letter was brought into the tent with some for the other boys.<br/>I am very pleased to hear that dear old Mrs. Morrison is still keeping well.  Please remember me kindly to her, Father dear, &amp; tell her I hope I will be able to shake her by the hand before long, if I am spared.<br/>I am keeping in splendid health &amp; am in tip-top spirits.<br/>Must close now with love to you all at home,<br/>Your loving son,<br/>Leo.<br/>[Sideways on page]  1st Sept.<br/>Three or 4 days ago Wal paid me a surprise visit &amp; I found out then where he was camped &amp; yesterday I went out &amp; paid him a birthday visit.  I was able to give him later home news than he had received.  He was looking well, &amp; will probably be writing by this mail also  Drenching rains.<br/>Love Leo.</p>
