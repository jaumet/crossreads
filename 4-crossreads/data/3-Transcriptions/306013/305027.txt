<p>Dec. 7. Slept on the fore-hatch last night. When I went up on deck with my hammock etc. at 9 P.M. there was not an available spot on which to lie. Every corner contained a sleeper &amp; every flat part of the deck was covered <strike>like blacks</strike> with bunks like a floor with tiles. After wandering about in the moonlight for a while I found one small space and by pushing one chap's pillow up a little &amp; another's blanket over, I made room for myself. When lying down my head was within 3 inches of another's feet;  and in front of me were three heads, one opposite my neck another opposite my waist &amp; a third against my feet while yet another fellow was lying at my back parallel to myself. Despite the fact that I woke up with my feet on one man's pillow, we all slept soundly &amp; well &amp; at any rate did not feel at all lonely.</p>
