<p>Sunday 6<br/>Through the ordinary channels to [indecipherable] &amp; then in Hospital train.</p>
<p>Monday 7<br/>To St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital at Etaples.</p>
<p>Tuesday 8<br/>Still at St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital where we are having glorious weather.  In my case the M.O. is frightened of bleeding since the bracheal vein has been divided &amp; I cannot be moved to England for about a week.</p>
<p>Wednesday 9<br/>From the papers it is apparent that the fighting round Bullecourt &amp; Riencourt is of a very important nature &amp; heavy &amp; mixed fighting seems to be continuous.<br/>The Australians have done all asked of them.</p>
<p>Thursday 10<br/>At Etaples yet &amp; the warm weather continues.</p>
<p>Saturday 12<br/>Passed for England<br/>Sister Higgins St John Ambulance B.H. Etaples.</p>
