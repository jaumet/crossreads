<p>ginger of soldiering.<br/>On turning out about 8.30 the same morning we found a scratch breakfast in the shape of tea &amp; sandwiches. We were strictly guarded against going into town. a gigantic sausage shaped observation balloon was hovering well over our point of embarkation. about midday we were marched aboard a big ferry boat &amp; under the vigilant guardianship of a destroyer made a safe but not direct passage to Boulogne. Like England the inhabitants had got used to the arrival &amp; departure of troops, so our arrival created no surprise or excitement. The most enthusiastic</p>
