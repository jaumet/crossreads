  
 121 to descend in street you would be wet or your satin shoes would be spoilt in traversing the footpath (In cafes & hotel a flunkey man with a large umbrella protects you from rain). [A small sketch here]. There is no front door at all & no grand & official exterior entrance. 
 On returning we changed our wet clothes & light a huge fire in the open fireplace to dry our things. For here there is no place - no court to dry clothes hence even the poorest is forced to send them to a laundry which even in the poorest families cost 4 to 15 francs per week. 
 Handkerchiefs cost - 10c Kitchen Towels - 15c 1 small Tablecloth - 40c 1 apron (noir) - 10c 1  serviette  dish cloth - 15c 1 Blouse - 1.00 1 Bed Sheet - 1.25 1 pyjamas - 2.00 
 Never will I be able to adjust my ideas after the prices here. These prices are all a consequences of guerre & the high cost of living. 
 In front of huge fire we stretched a eiderdown & read to our hearts content while outside the rain fell & the wind howled. The following day & the rest of week the usual regime of study. I am really worse off than a prisoner in the [indecipherable]
