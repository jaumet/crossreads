<p>saying they don't see why the <strike>Australians</strike> should come and have the best of everything.  But soon he added that that wasn't his view &amp; thinks that our boys should have the best for they deserve it all &amp; are grand fellows.  He led me to believe this Hospital was the best in England &ndash; And if the boys have a chance at all they will surely have it here.  The building was originally an Infirmary &ndash; built in 1866.  The Indians took it over in the beginning of the war for their wounded soldiers &amp; spent &pound;38,000 in equipping it up as a Hospital.  Six months ago the English took it and now its to be ours but I think the upkeep of it will be a heavy expense on the Aust Government.</p>
<p>21st  Up till now it has been real dinkum active service &ndash; 3 convoys have come in &ndash; chiefly English with an odd Canadian or Australian.  The E. Sisters &amp; V.A.D.s are all gone &ndash; They are going to Salonicka.  The pretty V.A.D.  They were treasures in the ward.  I wish we could have them instead of so many Orderleys.</p>
<p>We 3rd have climbed upwards haven't we?  Twas tents first &ndash; then barracks &amp; now this &ndash; wherever they put us we are like old barn Chucks &ndash; immediately settle down &amp; become part &amp; parcel of the place.
