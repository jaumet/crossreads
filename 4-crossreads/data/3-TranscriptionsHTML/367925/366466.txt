<p>[This story is on a form similar to the previous ones and sent to Mr A.G. Stephens, Editor NSW Anzac Memorial]<br/>Name and Rank Pte J. Miller<br/>Battalion 21st<br/>Date when left Australia on service. 8th May '15<br/>Date when you came back to Australia. 31st May 16<br/>Answer this question:<br/>What was the BRAVEST DEED you saw with your own eyes from the day you left Australia till the day you came back? [The words "BRAVEST DEED" have been crossed out by Pte Miller]<br/>Prose<br/>They were just true sons of Australia as they faced the shot and shell<br/>Fighting with stern devotion for the land that they loved so well<br/>They laid down their lives like heroes when they stormed the rugged cliff<br/>And made a name of undying fame on that day the Twenty Fifth.</p>
<p>And so as ceaseless years roll by our childrens children read<br/>In historys pages 'mongst the roll of brave mens honored deeds<br/>Theres some will shine amidst them all so true, so great, so grand<br/>The lads of Anzac, Flanders, France in Europes blood drenched land.</p>
