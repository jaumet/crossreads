<p>The girl conductress on the tram have a running fire of wit &amp; badinage which keeps everyone in good humour &amp; offends no one. On return to camp slept on the floor.</p>
<p>30 December 1918<br/>This morning we have to go through a gas test with our respirators &amp; walk nearly 1 &frac12; miles to reach the place. The road winds through a picturesque wood up a hill through some old monastery &amp; along to a tear gas shed &amp; then along to chlorine cave &amp; whence we can obtain a splendid view of St Addresse where once stood a Casino now a hospital.</p>
<p>In the afternoon so that I do not waste any time I go down to [Montevillier] one of the quaintest little towns I have seen. In the centre is a large square &amp; on its edge a church now time &amp; weatherworn but still maintaining its grandeur &amp; sanctity which ages have not been able to mar. In the centre of the square is an old village fountain from which every woman draws her water &amp; from an adjoining trough horses are watered.</p>
