 found numbers of European plants flourishing, though un-tended.  And this old Fort was a thing of much interest too, commanding the entrance to a splendid bay, Hou gh ts Bay, the old cannon and shot seemed to our modern ideas particularly futile but we read that ships were sunk by such as these. 
    And then on to view a group of coloured convicts working on the road and a crowd of native women returning from lunch to a lobster canning factory.  Then a very ordinary lunch taken under beautiful conditions, for Hou gh ts Bay Hotel was new, beautifully designed, and unusual in that it had many of the characteristics of local architecture combined with the beauty of a modern Italian villa.  After lunch, coffee on the "Stoep" (verandah) then on thru' the hills to the other side of the Peninsular to visit Simonstown the naval base and forbidden ground to all but officers. 
    For forty miles we travelled over beautiul roads rarely seeing a white face, hindus and kaffirs partly, but mainly Cape Boys, a mixture of Malay, Dutch and the local native, the white folk class them all together and term them Coloured Folk.  Then Muizenberg, a resort where we watched girls surfing at midwinter.  Back a bit then away to Simonstown, forts, and more forts. war boats and Tommy Atkins, the real thing in soldiers so  they  think, why, a lance-corporal walking out has much more swank that an A.I.F. officer.
