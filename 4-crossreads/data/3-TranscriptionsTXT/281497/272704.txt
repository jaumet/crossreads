 For dinner we had bully, biscuits, &amp; tea so we are not living to flash as yet. All day long &amp; night as well there are great strings of motor lorries passing backwards &amp; forwards. All engaged in carting shells to the firing line &amp; most of them 9.2. Left the camp this evening about 3.30 &amp; marched to the railway station about 3 miles distant, here we had to wait until 5.30 &amp; in the meantime I went over to where an old Tommy was cooking I scored over here for I got a big joint of roast beef 2 loaves of bread, &amp; a dixie of hot tea
