<p>(34)<br/>As we march through this quaint old town of the French, now changed by the touch of evil war from a place of homely peace and rest, to a weird scene of wreck and desolation, I could tell you much which may not be told.<br/>However that cannot be, for while it is war, grim silence must seal down many pages.<br/>Aye, and even after war it were best that that seal remain unbroken.<br/>Of many of the simple things which I may yet tell you, to give some impression <strike>of</strike> so that you may see as in a picture that which I see in reality I must weary you with <strike>some</strike></p>
