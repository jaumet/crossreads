After tea I met a Miss Hilda Mundy and friends and went to Salisbury Cathedral and the service was indeed excellent and much more extensive than I was used to.<br/>After seeing the ladies safely home I rejoined my friend and then followed a bustle for a car back to Camp.  However we arrived safely without incident and soon dropped off to sleep.</p>
<p>Monday 21st<br/>Received 2 letters from M.B. &amp; several papers.  Route march in afternoon &amp; the boys gave their famous hymn, Growling, Growling, Growling,<br/>Always b--- well growling<br/>When you're dead and in your graves<br/>You'll b--- well growl no more.</p>
<p>During the morning we had first aid &amp; stretcher drill and the Colonel took a snapshot of my stretcher squad to send to Australia.</p>
