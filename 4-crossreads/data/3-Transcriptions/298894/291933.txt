<p>Friday 4th June.   Began taking on wounded in earnest.   Another big attack on the hill &amp; our chaps suffered rather heavily.   We have taken on practically all Englishmen very boyish some of them &amp; taking them on the average, they are not as cheerful nor do they bear their wounds as well as the Australians.    I started at 5.30 A.M. &amp; then had to continue on for night duty till 7 A.M. the next morning, a shift of 25 &frac12; hours.   When I knocked off I started reading till breakfast at 8.30 but fell asleep four times in 5 minutes &amp; had to walk about.   I turned in at 9 A.M. &amp; slept splendidly till 4.30 P.M.</p>
<p>Saturday 5th</p>
<p>Slept in &amp; started on again at 7 P.M. till 7 A.M. Sunday Morning.   On duty attending to wounded with about three operations.</p>
<p>Sunday 6th</p>
<p>Slept in.   Night &amp; ;morning duty rather a busy time.   Sailed for Alexandria about 9 A.M. much to our joy as we were a full ship of wounded practically all Englishmen.</p>
<p>Monday 7th</p>
<p>Slept in.   Duty at night.   Several operations, one arm taken off, others head cases [indecipherable], all very wonderful.   I feel very safe now watching these gruesome sights but have a dread of it all &amp; will be thankful when all is over.
