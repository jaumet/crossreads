At last we reached the entrance to S.P.Y. &amp; after winding through a maze of trenches bearing the mark of a terrible strafe &amp; in places leaving far too much of one exposed to the bullet of the sniper, we arrived at Capt. B's Dugout.  This is shell proof &amp; is reached after descending about 8 steps.  Its of concrete but even this is no home.  Water was oozing out of the walls &amp; dripping from above &amp; this is the life.  After chatting awhile I left him &amp; returned to Square farm feeling that it was almost out in the country.<br/>Went to town this afternoon.</p>
<p>Saturday Mch. 31st<br/>Fine morning.  Came into Billets this evening.</p>
<p>Sunday Apl. 1st<br/>I had a service at Y.M.C.A. this evening, "A man shall be as the Shadow of a Rock in a weary land".  It was a fine service.  Br. Gel. English, Wesleyan Chaplain called.</p>
