On Sunday afternoon I went for a walk through Vauchelles l'Domart where I got some nice biscuits at a Royal Flying Corps canteen and on to Monflers a very small village where I also dug out a tommy Canteen. The occupants seemed inclined to talk, so I put over some very Tall stories onto them. They appeared to take them all in. Re these villages. Every one seemed to have a peculiarity of its own. Bouvieres was noticeable for the size of its pump handles, they being very long and graceful.[drawing of pump]Brucamps for the [indecipherable] little doors in their stable doors, presumably to take a last peep at their favourite cow;[drawing of door]Vauchelles l'Domart for the size of the iron gates leading into the house enclosure.
