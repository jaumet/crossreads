<p>January &ndash; 1918</p>
<p>1:  Rained heavily all day, only coming out of our bivvies to feed &amp; water our horses.</p>
<p>4:  Section went to Paylon with the 2nd regt. taking over the line from the 4th Bde. I left for Zietoun gun school at 4 p.m. from Ezdud.</p>
<p>5:  Reached Cairo at 6 p.m. stayed at National hotel, Kursaal at night "Girl in the Taxi" &ndash; poor show.</p>
<p>6:  Left National as it was placed out of bounds to N.C.O.s &amp; men at 9a.m. Went to school camp to report at 2 p.m.</p>
<p>7:  Classes commenced at 9 a.m., after lectures on saluting etc., camp in charge of Imperial officers &amp; failure to salute then means being sent back to the unit.  Leave granted 3 days per week; 4 .p.m. to midnight, but we go out every night without a pass.</p>
<p>9:  Madame Bey's to dinner, had an enjoyable evening.</p>
<p>12:  Heliopolis races, winding up at the Kursall, seeing the Million Dollar Girl, which some people would have handed the bird.</p>
