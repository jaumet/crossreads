<p>At 4 p.m. on Monday, 10th I said goodbye to my friends at the Railway Station and in three hours time arrived at Lyon. I did not leave for Paris until 10 pm so was able to see some of the town by night. The broad streets, fine statuary, tinkling trams, glittering caf&eacute;s and the big shops made an impression on me &ndash; there was no war here. British khaki was conspicuous by its absence. Plenty of French, naturally, and also a few Americans.</p>
<p>The following morning I arrived in Paris at 8.30. My train companions were all French, one of whom being a smartly dressed young militaire who much bemoaned his fate at being a common or garden cook in a motor column or something when he asked to get his pilot's certificate in the Flying Corps.
