<p>It was at Mailly, in June 1917 that I made his acquaintance, and I found him all that I had thought him to be. Word came round that Father Kennedy was back. Immediately, men of all kinds left the huts, just to see him, <strike>say good</strike> greet him, and shew that they had not forgotten him.</p>
<p>[Asterisk and note in margin:] personality sketch</p>
<p>In those days, the Catholics were numerous <strike>indeed</strike> in the Brigade, but they were not of one denomination only, that crammed the church at Mailly, and afterwards at Beaussart. And during the week, he was around on parade, and never absent during hours of relaxation, &ndash; and always with the Colonel. <strike>And</strike> What fitting comrades they were!</p>
