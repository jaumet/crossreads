<p>As tea time came we set to a very plain meal but it is marvellous what a fellow will eat &amp; enjoy when he is hungry. First of all we had a slice of dry, stale bread &amp; an onion.[raw] with a little cheese &amp; a mouthful of water, followed by a dry biscuit. The onions I pinched from the cook, &amp; although they brought tears into our eyes they were very good, the cheese we nipped, so that I can honestly say that I enjoyed the feed. We often used to growl at home because the meat was tough, the bread stale or the pudding not nice. Yes! &amp; how often we were told that we should be thankful. We naturally never thought</p>
