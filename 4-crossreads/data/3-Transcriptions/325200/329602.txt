<p>Just imagine bringing such guns as those 60 pdrs i.e. almost the size of the naval 6": across 30 miles of shifting deep loose sand. It is almost a par with Hannibals passage over the Alps. They achieved their purpose by putting down bushes in front of the wheels  &amp; running the wheels on planks when the gradient became too seep. There are piles  &amp; piles of thick planks out their some still in position left after the gun had run over them. An enormous number of men  &amp;   - they estimate &ndash; 30 men to each gun must have been</p>
