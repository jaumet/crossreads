<p>Had it not been for his steel hat he would certainly not be alive today.  The steel hats have saved thousands of lives in like manner since they <strike>were</strike> first were adopted by the British early in 1916.</p>
<p>3rd</p>
<p>10 AM.  I awoke and had a look round the dug out.  I found John Howells at the table with a blanket over his knees <strike>and</strike> a pencil in his hand and a long screed which he had been writing.  He had been up all night and was very sleepy.  He turned in as soon as I came along.</p>
