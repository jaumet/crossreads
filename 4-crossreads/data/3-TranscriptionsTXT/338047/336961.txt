
1919MarchMolly Malone (alive alive oh), & Faddy would give a Figian melody & a bit of Hindustani.  They had a book of songs (without music), & I used to sing these also - "Le pauvre petit oiseau", "Les petit orphelins" (both very mournful), a couple of war songs about "le roi Albert", & a few local ditties - of course the favourite was "La Madelon".  The only fly in the ointment was young Jules, the son, a boy of about 20.  He was in Cologne when war was declared & had been taken prisoner.  He tried to escape once or twice but was caught.  He eventually got away by bribing a sentry at Aix la Chapelle.  He swapped clothes with a Tommy once so that the Tommy could escape.He did not do a lot of work but loafed around all day & disappeared at night.  He was very fond of handling our rifles & bayonets.
