 Cairo is a very large city &amp; the first thing noticed is the traffic, To explain this concisely, one needs an eye in each elbow &amp;  one at the back of the head to dodge the garries &amp; trams. The trams are electric &amp; of French design &amp; make. They are painfully slow however. The tram conductor blows a small brass trumpet instead of a whistle. The stops are very few indeed &amp; very far between. Air brakes are not used. The other means of conveyance are garries or cabs
