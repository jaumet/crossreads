25/1/18 Chester
Did not get up until late. A beautiful fine day with a pleasant sun. Chester is, I think, excepting Oxford the oddest and quaintest town that I have yet been in. The streets are full of old gabled houses with timbered carved fronts which project over the footways. In places the footpaths ascend stairs and there are two rows of shops and dwellings. The old city walls are in an excellent state of preservation the many stairs leading up to them allow one to walk right along the top of the them. Went to the Cathedral a fine quiet old building. Inside a service was in progress ? all choir and no congregation. Some mosaic work on the walls and the usual old epitaphs and tablets. A grey graveyard surrounds the Church. There is a very fine carved wooden choir screen and the choir stalls are very fine indeed. Leaving here I
