<p>these we learnt that there was very little ashore worth seeing. Hardly a white person was seen &amp; the blacks were very suspicious of the soldiers &amp; armed themselves with sticks in case of need &amp; one of the lads returned with a scar on his nose which an aggravated native had given him.</p>
<p>I believe there are no papers here;  in fact one "pommie" officer who came aboard told us he had not seen a paper for 10 months. No wonder we were glad to leave the place!</p>
<p>Jan 13.  On guard today. My duty was to guard the mumps, quarters on the ship. We have about 120 cases of mumps in quarantine at present.</p>
<p>Jan 14. We left Sierra Leone at 7.30 this morning after a four days' stay. We are now under escort of an auxiliary cruiser, which, in peace time, is a fine large South American Mail boat. It has been fitted with several guns &amp; having a speed of 22 Knots is well able to protect us.</p>
