 Tuesday 20th November 1917 
 Moved on to DEAL &amp; anchored there. 
 Passed a big lineR with stern blown in &amp; being driven by TUGS. 
 We have at our table a Capt. HURLEY who has been twice to the Pole, &amp; has been on the French salient photographing &amp; is now en route to Palestine. 
 Cold &amp; foggy. 
 The tucker is good &amp; no restrictions like in England. All the Stewards are Indians &amp; we have passengers for ports as far as Japan.
