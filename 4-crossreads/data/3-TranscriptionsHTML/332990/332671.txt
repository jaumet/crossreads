<p>[Short hand not transcribed]<br/>I have a long walk this evening along with Handcock to Ralestin camp where some Australians from Egypt is camped. It is about 2 miles from Lark Hill Camp. In the distance about 4 miles away was pointed out to me Longfellows "Village blacksmith under the spreading chestnut tree"</p>
<p>[Short hand not transcribed]<br/>Monday August 7. 1916</p>
<p>A nice day but very cool in the morning. Again staffing today. I finished reading "On and Off the Turf in Australia"  by Nat Gould. I also finished reading yesterday "Stonehenge" yesterday and today" by Frank Stevens.</p>
<p>Tuesday Aug 8. 1916</p>
<p>Very cloudy this morning but bright &amp; sunny this afternoon. I was up at the cook house today. It is much shorter hours than stoking the hot water boiler. "A" [Short hand not transcribed]<br/>Wednesday Aug  9. 1916</p>
<p>Very cloudy &amp; misty this morning but hot in the afternoon. I am again cleaning up the cook house &amp; washing tins. I posted [Short hand not transcribed]</p>
