<p>For the latter part of the journey the tram line runs between an avenue of (I think) flame trees, distinguished by their pinnate leaves &amp; large pods. Away in the distance was the Mena Camp on the right &amp; the Pyramids on the left, &amp; round to the south, following the low ridge skirting the fertile flats was another large pyramid abt 15 mls away, the edges appearing coarsely serviate due to large steps apparently.</p>
<p>The final stretch of the avenue was perfectly straight &amp; was done in fine style by the tram with scarcely a stopping place.</p>
<p>At the terminus was a fair-sized refreshment room &amp; close by it a large building of several stories height known as Mena House. At present it is being used as a hospital, inmates in the light blue woollen dressing gowns being seen all over the grounds.</p>
<p>Close to the refreshment room on the other side was a small Bedouin encampment that looked quite interesting, - from a distance. Tiny squallid tents of rough horse-hair material that had once been striped &amp; gaudy-coloured but now are frayed &amp; torn &amp; muddy were occupied by dirty looking women &amp; children sharing their space with mangy dogs &amp; shaggy goats. Fires emitting plenty of smoke were smouldering in some of them, &amp; for a very good reason since the fuel is simply manure puddled &amp; made into thin round pancakes by the women who then spread it in the sun to dry: numerous parties of women engaged in this work were seen along the route as we came out.</p>
