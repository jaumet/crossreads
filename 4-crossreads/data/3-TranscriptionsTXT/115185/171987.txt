 3. Australian General Hospital A.I.F. at Abbassieh Barracks Cairo  8 April 1916 
 My own beloved Mother &amp; Father Your letter dated the 5th &amp; 6th March arrived this afternoon &ndash; such joy hearing from you &ndash; such dear letters &ndash; so comforting. My eyes fill with tears with the feeling that I do really belong to someone &ndash; which I always forget out here one looses all sence of being a "personality" I know that's not the word &ndash; but one merges into this vast machine which is being run to shoo the Germans back into Germany. I am afraid you had a very quiet birthday. I hated not sending you anything and I'd like to send you something by Col Jamieson &ndash; but I think I still owe something to dad and feel I better finish with that first &ndash; it seems rather a farce sending your parents presents when your in debt to them. I mean in a money way &ndash; of course the other blessed debts one can never pay.
