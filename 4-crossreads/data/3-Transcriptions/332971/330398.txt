<p>Lone jolly sailor boys passed up this afternoon from their little camouflaged boat.  They had sunk an enemy submarine &ndash; and a few days back saved the crew off a torpedoed Norwegian boat that sank in 3 minutes.</p>
<p>Spied two Anzac's coming up the walk, so waited for them.  Then we went into Mrs Pengillys and had tea Together &ndash; She had an interesting autograph book that people from all parts of the world had written in.  I was a bit puzzled over.  This one from three Anzac's &ndash; And its taken me till now to grasp the meaning of it &ndash; This is it</p>
<p>Only Anzac's we may be<br/>Yet we enjoyed Mrs Pengilly Tea<br/>High Street was an awful climb<br/>But the scenery was sublime.</p>
<p>So they trust that as they who run may read &ndash; will never die until a dead horse kicks them and the hairs from their tail turn into candles to light them to glory.</p>
<p>I do love meeting our boys.  They all so ready to see the bright side of things.</p>
