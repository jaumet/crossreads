 Monday 10 February 1919 Disinfecting saddlery &amp; classing horses out to be shot &amp; skinned tomorrow. Letter from Daisy, December 15 &ndash; sorry poor little Mother not been too well &ndash; of course I will telegraph from Western Australia my movements. Joe will be home by this.  I hope he escaped the flu.  Dear old Day has been very good in writing so often &amp; so much. Jim Ayre may come.  Nowell has no intention but I have not seen or heard of him for a long time. We have no Padre only a drunken priest at present.
