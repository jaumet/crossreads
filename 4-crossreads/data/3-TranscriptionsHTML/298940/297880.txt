<p>followed by others at  short intervals, simply a hollow noise on landing as if the shell had fallen &amp; not exploded. Of course we soon tumbled to it that they were gas shells. The wind was dangerous, for it was light &amp; parallel with our front line, if anything blowing towards our back.<br/>It soon became evident that it was a systematic attack by gas, yet we had not much to fear <strike>ye</strike> up to that <strike>the</strike> time, for they were falling some distance away. Soon, however they began to fall closer, &amp; a slight whiff of the gas (like mustard &amp; cress)</p>
