 at 5.30.  We embarked in a ships boat &amp; were towed out to the Anzac trawler.  There was a fresh breeze blowing &amp; a good sea, so we had a good tossing.  Several of the chaps got sick.  We arrived at Suvla Bay &amp; were surprised to see all that had been done there.  There were several warships &amp; a big monitor.  The "Talbot" &amp; "Venerable" did a lot of firing &amp; the land batteries assisting.  There was an engagement on a big hill on the left which lasted a couple of hours.  We left Suvla Bay at about 3.30 &amp; got to Anzac about 4.  It seemed strange coming back.  We hadn't gone far before Beachy Bill greeted us.  Reported back to the Squadron about 6 pm but wasn't on any duty at night. [Beachy Bill was name given to a Turkish heavy gun battery]
