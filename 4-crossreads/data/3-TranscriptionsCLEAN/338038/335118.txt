  
 Gallipoli Penin 31.7.1915 
 My dear Father & Mother, I really don't know why I am writing this week (except that I think a letter, be it ever so short, is better than the F.S. post cards) as there is nothing fresh since I last wrote you. Things have been most quiet since then, and scarcely any "hate" flying about at all. A small demonstration on the Turks' part took place yesterday afternoon. We saw them fix bayonets in their trenches and anticipated an attack but as usual it died out. I met Viv Benjafield a few days ago. He is acting Medical Officer for our Brigade.
