<p>in the vessel's hold. Whilst waiting, several aeroplane scouts treated us to a magnificent display of diving, turning &amp; manoeuvring. So apparently easy &amp; graceful were their movements, that there appeared no more danger in their evolutions than if they had been birds on the wing.  I met Wilkins on board; &amp; so we left the shores of dear old England for the grim duties of France. The weather was beautiful, &amp; our vessel convoyed by two destroyers did her 17 knots without a movement. Soon the french coast came in sight &amp; the details of Boulogne gradually became more distinct.  We had a particularly fine view just before entering the port, of the beach, where great numbers of folk were bathing.
