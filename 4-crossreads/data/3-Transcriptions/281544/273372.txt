<p>different altogether to what we get in the Military, it was just like home sitting there &amp; the Lady could speak a little English, after thanking her &amp; paying the damage we bade her Good Day &amp; off we went for a stroll &amp; another Tram ride, we went out in another direction for about 3 or 4 mile &amp; back again &amp; then we had to make a few purchases of Cards &amp; other things &amp; after this was done we went in for Lunch &amp; had a good Meal of Chops &amp; Chips &amp; Eggs &amp; a Bottle of Ale for 6&frac12; Franc's the two, we then made our way to the Church Army Hut for a while until it was time to leave by the Train at 1-20 Pm, I had to produce the Pass &amp; Movement Order in the Railway Transport Office for Inspection &amp; in on the station we went to board the Train awaiting there, Calai station is fairly large &amp; has 6 Platforms &amp; a large entrance on both sides, the Platforms are only about 1 ft high, &amp; you have to step up into the Carriages, there were about 20</p>
