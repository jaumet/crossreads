<p>Monday 28th January 1917 [1918]<br/>Breakfast 8.30 a.m. then mounted donkeys and away to the granite quarries along the riverside for a space then out through the camp where such funny mud huts, tiny streets, &amp; dirty dark hovels and human beings do abound, then on over desert plains until we come to the famous outcrop of red granite &amp; one sees where all the statues were cut from.  Method of cutting, holes made <strike>green</strike> wood put in &amp; water poured in the holes until rock split &ndash; then on to Phylae [also spelt Philae] of wonderful beauty, then Assouan dam, where we got lunch baskets &amp; took boat to the lock, through, &amp; sailed home &ndash; then Cobs &amp; I went to bazaars, a dance in evening &amp; bed 11.30 p.m.</p>
