Soon the smoke "barrage" became so thick that is was impossible to see a yard on either side, & the only thing for it was to stumble forward & follow the, rapidly leaping barrage. Dawn found myself   & several other officers in the tangle of Grand Priel Woods. Here we gathered in some 50 Germans who wet & horrified by the intensity of our barrage were quite willing to surrender in their machine guns  &  mortars without firing a shot. After a struggle among the wired stumps of the wood we emerged on the flat stretch before Villeret, the trench in front of which was our objective.
