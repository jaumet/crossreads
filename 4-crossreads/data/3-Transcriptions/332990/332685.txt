<p>Bulford is now used as an Hospital for sick soldiers. It is a 3 storied building.</p>
<p>Thursday August 17, 1916<br/>Showery all day. It was unpleasant working in it.<br/>[Short hand not transcribed]</p>
<p>Friday August 18, 1916</p>
<p>No rain today.<br/>In the evening, with Matthews and Handcock I walked into Amesbury and had a look around the Village. It is the largest Village that I have been too on Salisbury Plain. In fact it is a little town, and has many fine new buildings including a new Police Station, school, Bank and several private houses. There are a lot of shops, and I noticed 4 stationers shops and 3 or 4 hotels, or rather public Houses as they are called here.</p>
<p>Saturday Aug 19. 1916</p>
<p>Cloudy but fine today. This morning I was out to Rolleston Camp in the wagonette &ndash; to help unload a load of ashes.<br/>In the afternoon I walked into Amesbury with Handcock and went to the station [Short hand not transcribed]</p>
<p>Sunday Aug 20, 1916.</p>
