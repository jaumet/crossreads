<p>stripped bare with shell fire &amp; in many cases only the stumps are left. The ground is all honey combed with shell holes. On our left going up half a dozen heavy batteries were spitting fire the spurts of flame only were visible. Soon after we arrived at Quarry the batteries all around us opened fire &amp; for about &frac12; an hour the din was deafening. Fritz took it all without a word not a shell came over. Our grins gave him h-l.<br/>Coming back the sun was setting &amp; the sight from the Peronne road was one that will live in my memory. We passed old German trenches &amp; also their grave yards cared for in that they are not interfered with. It was an eyeopener to travel through such country I assure you.</p>
