The weather is cool and cloudy19/10/191550 Turks captured by French last night by all accounts they are in a terrible state, 1/4 rations, cholera, Dysentery and Typhoid raging amongst them. About 100 Artillery reinforcements went through our camp this morning. All night the Artillery and infantry were firing, with an occasional shot from a monotor. Blowing a gale20/10/1915More rain this morning, windy and cold. A sharp attack at   10 5.30 A.M. a great deal of rifle fire, dozens of shells coming over at the back of our lines. Another hundred yards or so of a switch and they will be on to us. At 5 A.M. a private in the French army was shot, trying to kill his Sgt. One man killed and 3 wounded by a high explosive shell, these men belong to some infantry Regiment going to the trenches. The 2nd City of London Royal Fusiliers has landed here,
