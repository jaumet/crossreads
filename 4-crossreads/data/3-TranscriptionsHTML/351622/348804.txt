<p>31 March 1916 Fri<br/>Every second house in this village is an inn &amp; they are open selling beer &amp; wine only from 11 till 1 pm. &amp; from 6 till 8 p.m. They are well patronised &amp; are always full. We have two tommies attached to us who are in charge of the motor. Fine coves &amp; we get on well together. Very sick to-night [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>1 April 1916 Sat<br/>There is a beautiful church here &amp; very old, but inside the work of art is magnificent. The cave with Christ's body after being crucified. The big guns are booming some to-day. 39 motor ambulance in one line passed through this morning. Everyone is doing his best to pick up French &amp; the expressions at times are very queer. Toby, Arthur &amp; Norm come to see us &amp; stay for tea in the barn</p>
