<p>3<br/>Men whoes minds cannot be robbed of their golden grain by the flail of despair and left as husks to be swept unheeded, by drear winds of oblivion</p>
<p>Men, who although they may be little known till death, live honored after.</p>
<p>Men who are not moulded by custom or state or age, but rather [indecipherable] by the laws which come of them.</p>
<p>Men who pass from us with their greatest thoughts unsaid and are noble for their silence</p>
<p>By that which is unknown and that which is unrevealed, do the Gods remain</p>
<p>By discovery comes loss to those who see too far.  Doubt haunts the great &ndash; they are most brave who pluck at the ravelled hem of fleeting hope<br/>[following line is crossed out]<br/>And dare not take the that faith extends</p>
<p>And dare not take the hand of waiting faith</p>
