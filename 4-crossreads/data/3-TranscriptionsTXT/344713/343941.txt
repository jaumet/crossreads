 Mon. Mar. 5th: - Left tunnel &amp; went by Eaucourt Abbey &amp; Clarke's Dump to "A" Camp Bazentin-le-Grand &amp; slept at huts for night: It looks as if  y  we are finished with Le Barque &amp; its "Yellow-Cut" &amp; "Blue-Cut" Roads its' "Misty corner", "Cough-Drop" &amp; "Factory Corner" for a time. Bn. Hqrs were at Wheat Trench &ndash; I wonder who chooses the names for the christenings of all these points of reference. 
 Tues. March 6th 1917: - Left Bazentin Camp &amp; went via Contalmaison to Fricourt Farm Camp. 
 Thurs. March 22nd: - Left Fricourt Farm Camp &amp; moved with whole Brigade to Ribemont. Plenty of training going on for some "stunts" 
 Tues. April 3rd. Moved from Ribemont to camp at Montauban &ndash; about 12 miles. A nice Sunny day though it was snowing heavily yesterday. We are all possessors of different coloured arm-bands to denote our speciality. 
 Wed. April. 4th: - Left Montauban huts &amp; marched by Bernafay Wood &amp; Trones Wood, old Sugar Refinery outside of Delville through to Beaulencourt &amp; on to Fremicourt where we are stopping for night. Snowing heavily &amp; having a "rough spin".
