 September 1915 
 Saturday 4 
 praises the education system here.  He advised me to get a billet teaching English after the war is over. 
 It is worth thinking over at any rate for the wages are good. 
 The educated class here is in great evidence. 
 In the native quarters one sees squalor in its worst form. 
 Houses built of yellow mud looking stuff, crumbling to pieces from each other across a street of a few feet wide. 
 Filthy with black piccaninies play about the middle of the alley way &amp; dirty natives in multi coloured dress lounge about or go about their occupations.
