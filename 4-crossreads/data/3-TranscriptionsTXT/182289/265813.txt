 Le Bizet &ndash; 14.2.18 C.O. (Capt. D.A. Whitehead) visited line.  Arrangements were made for special practice barrages to be carried out the following day, chiefly for the instruction of Senior N.C.Os.  Orders were given that N.C.Os were to do the greater part of the calculations and which were to be checked by their Section Officers afterwards.  The barrages were to be carried out by the daylight firing battery and as dull movements. Men were again questioned as regards A.A. Defences and they seemed to have showed better knowledge of the sight.  Grounds were still left for further improvement. Between 7 p.m. and 2 a.m., 2000 rounds were fired at the following targets:- (1) Tracks, C.11.b.50.60 to C.11.b.70.60, (2) Converging Tracks, C.11.b.85.22, (3) Tracks, C.12.a.33, (4) Track, C.11.d.50.10, (5) Church surroundings, C.11.c.7.7. Enemy's Artillery showed a little activity during the day and night.  About 7 of probably 4.2 were dropped on the road in Le Bizet, C.13.b.30.40 at intervals of about 5 minutes.
