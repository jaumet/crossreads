  
 The 6th of September was a grey showery day in Ypres. Hilton had a slack time at plan work but he found plenty to do, in working with other sappers at improving  in other directions. Part of his time was consumed in assisting other sappers in building extra sand-bag protection to the Cupola Huts. The drizzle of rain added wretchedness to the desolation of ruin. The chaotic noise of guns firing,  and  of shells [whining?] and bursting and of  the crash of tumbling walls  smitten walls crashing was continuous throughout the day Later in the evening Hilton wandered alone through   part of  the ruins.
