By this time it was nearly one p'clock and as I wished to spend the remaining afternoon of my holiday in seeing Glasgow we walked down to Waverly and Mr Mann returned home to dinner while I caught the one oclock express for Glasgow.</p>
<p>Arriving at Queen St Station an hour later I walked over to the fine large square in front of the Municipal, buildings which were of an imposing nature. The square was largely taken up by monuments but otherwise bare looking owing partly to the rainy climate I suppose. It is remarkable the difference in the weather conditions of the two cities, Glasgow and Edinburgh.</p>
<p>I had a very interesting walk round about the shops of Glasgow. The city in its buildings, shops, streets and planning reminded me much of Sydney which cit I consider it resembles very much as regards the "city" part.</p>
