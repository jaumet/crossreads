<p>a population of about 22,000  is situated in pretty undulating country and is linked to the port of Calais by a canal about 20 miles in length. There is nothing much of interest to see except perhaps two or three old churches &amp; of course the gardens which are very pretty. The ride back took 4 &frac12;  hours, the most "interesting" (?) features being the number of hills I had to climb &amp; the fact that the latter part of the journey was done in darkness. In all I did about 70 miles &ndash; not a bad performance. The other two chaps who had started off with me only got as far as Escuilles, nearly halfway to St. Omer &amp; then gave it up as a bad job and went back.
