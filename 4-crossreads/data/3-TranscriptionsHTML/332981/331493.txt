<p>An hour had been fixed for the arrival of Herr Ergberger and the two German generals and a French General and a number of other superior officers were on the spot to meet them. For hours they waited, but there was no sign of the Germans.</p>
<p>Bonfires and flares were lit to guide the expected visitors, and eventually their approach was heralded by the appearance if the German road menders sent to fill in the mine &amp; shell holes. Finally, about nine o'clock the headlights of three big open touring cars signalled the approach of the Delegates.</p>
<p>They alighted from their cars and, in the words of a French Officer, "They were not proud".</p>
