<p>Friday October 19. 1917. (1173.)</p>
<p>Dull &amp; cloudy, with light misty rain.</p>
<p>I paraded sick this morning, because my feet have been sore for about a week, through being continually in the mud at Ypres &amp; have broken out at the heels. I was given light duties, and had them dressed with Iodine.</p>
<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows]<br/>I wrote a letter to my wife today, letter 96, 5 pages.  I sent it in the official green envelope.  I included a German post card taken from a German prisoner.</p>
<p>I also wrote to Cis tonight, in answer to her letter of August 7, No. 5.  [indecipherable].</p>
<p>We were paid later tonight.  I drew 20 Francs, 14.8.  [End of shorthand]<br/>Saturday October 20. 1917. (1174.)</p>
<p>It is cold &amp; frosty this morning.<br/>Between 9 &amp; 10 O'clock last night we were startled by the explosion of bursting bombs. There were 6 or 7 explosions in all. They were dropped by enemy planes and <strike>were</strike> judging by the sounds, fell about a mile from this village.</p>
<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows.]<br/>I sent silk post cards to the following today.<br/>L. Douglas, WGamble, W.Curtis, Miss K.E. Miller, Miss Alice Miller, Robert Wilson, Eric Wilson Maggie Kittson, and also to my wife &amp; sister, 10 in all. [End of shorthand.]<br/>[Shorthand transcribed as follows.]<br/>In the forenoon I went out and got some more French post cards and sent them to the following. [End of shorthand.] My Mother Irene Ring. My Uncle. J.Gilmore, Kitty Gilmore, &amp; Kitty Martin, making a total of 15 silk Postcards in all, cost 7 &frac12;  francs.</p>
<p>Sunday Oct. 21. 1917 (1175.)</p>
<p>Frosty last night. A fine day.<br/>There was a half holiday yesterday and again this evening.</p>
