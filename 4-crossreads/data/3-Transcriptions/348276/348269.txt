<p>War Diary<br/>1916  6th May<br/>Weekly inspections.</p>
<p>7th May<br/>Church Parade</p>
<p>8th May, 9th May, 10th May<br/>Training restricted on account of troops being in a semi state of readiness to move out &ndash; A number being used for escort to convoy, mostly water.</p>
<p>11th May<br/>C. in C. inspected left of line; quiet satisfied with all he saw.</p>
<p>12th May<br/>Every effort being made to fill reserve supply tanks. (canvas) in the front line &ndash; tanks leaking.</p>
<p>13th May<br/>Pump at Railhead broke down &ndash; all ranks on 1 gallon &ndash; tank at 9th L.H. pumped full from canal.</p>
<p>14th May<br/>Pump in use, water normal &ndash; Church Parade.</p>
<p>15th May<br/>Starting water and ration convoys off in the evening on account of the heat.</p>
<p>16th May<br/>redistribution of front lines started, 13th Aust. L.H. Bde. taking over from 12th Inf. Bde. and 4th Pioneer Battn., the latter being relieved by the 49th Bn.</p>
<p>17th May<br/>Water supply to Railhead short, and causing much distress &ndash; Abnormally hot.<br/>52nd Battn. relieved 46th Battn.</p>
<p>18th May<br/>Convoys at night working well &ndash; water short &ndash; second watering for horses completed at midnight.  50th Battn. relieved  the 45th Battn.</p>
<p>19th May<br/>two 60-pounder guns came into position at N road 200 yds from turning &ndash; water for horses at Div. Hd. Qrs.<br/>11th L.H. arrived from Tel el Kebir yesterday, less 1 squadron left with horses</p>
<p>20th May<br/>Brig. Gen. Glasgow assumed command of front line and Railhead during absence of G.O.C. 3rd. L.H. Bde.</p>
<p>21st May<br/>Gen. Cox inspected 60-pounder gun positions, and Hebeita camp.  Church Parade &ndash;</p>
<p>22nd May<br/>Inspection of line by Staff of 53rd Divn. who are relieving 4th Divn.</p>
<p>23rd May<br/>Indian Camel C. moved from Railhead.</p>
