<p>The North Sea Battle<br/>June 1st 1916</p>
<p>A lot of German dreadnoughts came a steaming from the Keil<br/>Relying on their Majesty &amp; might<br/>To frighten any merchantmen the darkness might conceal<br/>They thought that they could put them all to flight</p>
<p>But some of Britain's Cruisers were a cruising round the sea<br/>They fastened like a bulldog to a tramp<br/>And though they lost some chips they played hell among the Ships<br/>And lots of German dreadnoughts left their stamp</p>
<p>They pommelled at each other and our cruisers held their own<br/>'Gainst German's High Sea Fleet so proud and bold<br/>But though our boys were up against the best the Germans had<br/>They strove the old traditions to uphold</p>
