 Walking back to the ship we passed a house with a candle burning near the window. Naturally we just glanced at the window &ndash; &amp; further back &amp; saw a lady in the act of removing a stocking. Of course we were shocked, so much in fact that several of us let out a stifled moan of anguish, to the lady's evident discomfit for she immediately blew out the candle. 
 20th October  At eight oclock the pilot boarded the A13 &amp; after breakfast the took his place on the bridge. At nine am the A13 gave a long blast on her whistle &amp; slowly drew out. There were some very pitiful scenes between the soldiers from the Geelong &amp; the A13. Some were brothers &amp; others sons &amp; never have I seen such a heartbreaking farewell. Civilians were blocked by the police who took no chances &amp; erected the barrier several hundred yards up the street.
