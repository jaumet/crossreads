 4. same troubles to contend with. 
 As January came on, severe frosts set in &amp; the ground became frozen. Just now the place is clothed in frozen snow which has been there now, I think, for about twelve days. The cold has been rather intense but our fellows are fighting it very well under the circumstances. 
 At the same time they would give a lot to be back on the deserts of Egypt. We have had to take great care of their feet; certain precautions are taken every day in this matter &amp; strict attention enforced. Of course we are having a fair amount of sickness &ndash; colds, rheumatism, bronchitis, sore feet &amp;c. I myself had been sick for some weeks &amp; I was finally sent off for a while to this place which is up in the fighting area &amp; well within hostile shell fire
