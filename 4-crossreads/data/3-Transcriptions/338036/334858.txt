<p>[31st Dec. contd]<br/>a path to the cookhouse as we expected all our ground to be flooded tomorrow.  Had on gum boots &amp; saved some salvage.  This afternoon had afternoon tea with Madame in tuck shop in Pont de Nieppe with Sam &amp; the boys.  Walk to Nieppe.<br/>A terrific bombardment is just commencing probably to usher in the New Year (11 pm)  Forgot to mention that 5 of boys were transferred to A.D.S. today</p>
<p>1917<br/>Monday Jany 1st<br/>New Years Day<br/>More bombardment &ndash; and at 2 am this morning a daddy one which lasted some time. Water still rising though not so quickly.</p>
<p>Tuesday 2nd<br/>River has gone down a little so danger at present is averted.</p>
<p>Wednesday 3rd<br/>Very cool &amp; strong wind today.  To Pictures at night  Saw Chaplin in "Pangs of Love" &amp; "Mary Queen of Scots"  awful [indecipherable]  Then had fish &amp; eggs at tuck shop.  Very tricky the bowls without handles to drink out of.  Was up in night &ndash; not too well</p>
<p>Thursday 4th<br/>Spent morning in bed  Did not eat anything</p>
