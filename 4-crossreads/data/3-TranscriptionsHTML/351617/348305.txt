<p>All along the line wherever we stopped we were looked after by the ladies of the town &amp; Cakes, Tea, &amp; Cigarettes, &amp; fruit were very plentiful. The card below is one that was given to each of us by the Mayoress of Exeter where we were going through &amp; with it was a meal fit for a King.</p>
<p>[Photograph of G. M. Lyons with the following caption.]<br/>Owing to the card being misplaced I am filling this space with my pal G.M. Lyons.</p>
<p>There is no getting away from the fact that the women of England took the place of our own mothers &amp; girls 13000 miles away &amp; looked after us as if they had known us all their lives &amp; I defy anybody to say otherwise.</p>
<p>It was about 9 pm. when we ran into the small station of Aylsbury on Salisbury Plains &amp; were told this was the end of our Journey by train.</p>
<p>It did not take us long to fall in &amp; very soon we were swinging</p>
