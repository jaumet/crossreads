 Jan 7th 1918 Monday Wild stormy night with Tropical deluge conditions moderated considerably during the day &amp; only occosional showers fell. Capt Hurley &amp; myself went out after mud pictures in which the country is at present swimming The great field in front of&nbsp;Esdud Station was submerged about four feet &amp; with it valuable supplys of fodder &amp; rations A great string of over 2000 camels had to pass over it &amp; I&nbsp;pitied the wretched creatures &amp; their destitue drivers to see these fine beasts of the burning desert wallowing in the mud of Palestine winter is pitiful indeed &amp; their drivers with hardly any suitable clothing or protection from the fierce weather it is no wonder that they perish&nbsp;in larg numbers.
