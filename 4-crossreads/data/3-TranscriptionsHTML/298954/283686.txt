<p>1918<br/>Sat 2nd Nov.</p>
<p>English Theatre.<br/>"The Bosun's Mate"<br/>W.W. Jacobs.<br/>Mrs Waters.   a young widow.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;R. Alexander<br/>Geo. Benn ... a retired bosun  ..  C. Hampson.<br/>Ned. Travers &hellip;    "&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  soldier ..&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   Newble.<br/>villagers etc.</p>
<p>The mounting was perfect, worthy of any stage.   Also the stage has been enlarged &amp; the orchestra is now in a sunken pit &ndash; great improvements.   Scene-painter Cecil Tooke devised the old English Inn-parlour, with a bar &amp; wonderfully good antique settees, chairs, &amp; a grandfather clock.</p>
<p>&hellip;&hellip;.</p>
<p>Mc. (<u>our</u> Mc) gave "The Shooting of Dan McGrue" &amp; is a really good elocutionist.  (hateful word)<br/>Smith &amp; Heck in "Santa Fe" &amp; "Hello, Winter!"   The Serbo-Laundry duo in execrable ragtime.</p>
<p>&hellip;.</p>
<p>Smithy was excellent.</p>
<p>Sun. 3rd.<br/>With Tellier (the lovely Lieut.) Geo, Mc. &amp; Rodell;  we obtained permission for an afternoon walk and, glittering and gorgeous in borrowed plumes we passed down to the gate at 2 pm past the openly-jealous V.O. barrack.</p>
