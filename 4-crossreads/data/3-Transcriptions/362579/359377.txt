<p>be pleased that I'm away from that danger, but I don't think our C.C. Station will ever go back there. After we left the shelling was fearful   &amp;  about 10 p.m. a Sgt Saunders   &amp;  Cpl Thorson lived 24 hrs. He was a lovely boy &ndash; such a good, clean healthy minded boy who worshiped his mother. I had a letter from Dorothy to-day   &amp;  she told me the awful news. Some of the Stafdf (Sgts   &amp;  Cpls,) had been to tell them the sad news.</p>
<p>Two other sister returned with us. They had been only with us a very short time but suffered from shell-shock, so are glad to get back to safety.</p>
