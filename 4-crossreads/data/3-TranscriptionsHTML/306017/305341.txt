<p>&amp; a strong hot wind was beginning to blow from the south east across the desert.  The water in our bottles was very warm &amp; at 11 oclock it was that hot as to make it undrinkable.  The heat was intense &amp; the wind was blowing very strong.  The flies were down to it &amp; gave us no trouble at all.  Our throats were already parched &amp; the sun being on the back of our heads made us feel giddy.</p>
<p>At 11.15 we came to an oasis in a hollow &amp; we got into the shade of the trees.  It gave us little relief as there was scarcely a breath of air &amp; the temp must have been 120&deg;  There was a well here but the water smelt strongly &amp; we were ordered not to touch it  But it was impossible to keep a lot of the men away, some were staggering &amp; a few fainted.  I had my bottle half full of hot water &amp; so I made a fire &amp; boiled some tea.  This made me feel much better but my throat was just as dry after I had drank.
