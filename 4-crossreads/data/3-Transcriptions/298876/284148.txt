<p>fixed up blankets, rations for 24 hours etc. Took off in Hospital charge for Suez &ndash; 26 Isolated cases &ndash; 24 Mumps &amp; 4 Cot Cases &ndash; My Party moved off with the 14th 2nd Light Horse &ndash; to whom we are attached with Lt Trickett in charge &ndash; Left Suez at 12.30 &ndash; unknown destination &ndash; The troop train consisted mostly of 3rs Class Carriages &ndash; Our Party were allocated half a carriage &amp; were quite an important body as we were the only 8 Ambulance men on board &ndash; luckily we had no cases of accidents etc to attend to.</p>
<p>From the time the train started we began to see wonderful sights &amp; Curiositys &amp; the further we went the more we saw &ndash; the Lazy Nig &amp; the Energetic Nig &ndash; The desert in itself is one big object lesson, together with the must be seen to be believed Irrigation scheme &ndash; The Line thro desert is one straight line &amp; very level</p>
