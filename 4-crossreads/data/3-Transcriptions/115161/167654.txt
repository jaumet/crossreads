<p><u>AMIENS.</u></p>
<p>A large city, the fifth largest in France, I believe.&nbsp; Situated about fifteen miles from Buire.&nbsp; We usually went by train, which travelled at really quite a decent pace, arriving at the city at about ten a.m.,(we would have to be back in our billets by 10.p.m., or I should say we were supposed to be).&nbsp; It is a glorious feeling to be practically free for a whole day, and we &quot;squeeze&quot; as much enjoyment into the short time at our disposal, as is possible.&nbsp; We make - as the saying is - every post a winning post.</p>
<p>The journey is interesting.&nbsp; On the outskirts of the city are innumerable vegetable gardens, which have the appearance of so many islands, in the Somme, with boats as means of conveyance to the city.&nbsp; The picture reminds one of the Chinese gardens in our own country.&nbsp; The country is mostly well under cultivation, the soil being apparently good.</p>
<p>On our emerging from the station we proceed to seek out our separate and individual forms of enjoyment.&nbsp; Many of the belles of the city meet the trains and by their actions lend us to understand that they belong to a world-wide association which has for it&#39;s objects the uplifting and relief of mankind.&nbsp; They are prepared to lay themselves out to make our short period in the city enjoyable.&nbsp; There is no formal introduction necessary on these occasions.&nbsp; They do not appear over-dressed, and succeed in making you feel quite at home in a little time.&nbsp; The language is not any handicap, the French nation depends largely on actions, so that it is not hard to make yourself understood to these fair damsels.&nbsp; They are invariably bright and lively, and are not shocked at the idea of a glass of wine and a cigarette.&nbsp; They are at times most masterly and yet of a &quot;retiring&quot; nature.&nbsp; Their actions were always thoroughly appreciated by the majority of us, and in some cases men lived in the</p>
