<p>(Thursday 12th)<br/>Went to Anzac beach for water as water supply is very short up here Tommies took farm house this afternoon relieving trenches at 7 pm<br/>(Friday 13th)<br/>Very quiet night Turks are entrenched in front of us about 250 yards range will be relieved at 7 (pm) went on water fatigue at 8 (pm) and did not get back to camp till 1 (am)<br/>(Saturday 14th)<br/>Turks send over a lot of shells wrote letter to Kitty and card to Mum go into trenches at 7(pm) Patrol went out in charge of Capn Howden at 8 pm and returned at 10 pm<br/>(Sundy 15th)<br/>Quiet night got some good targets this morning on our left They are digging a machine gun trench or for big guns any way we made them knock off getting relieved at 7 (pm)<br/>Have dysentry from drinking the water from the wells we dug here Went on sick parade and got a No 9 received a letter from Kitty Relieving trenches at 7 pm Had first watch very quiet tonight<br/>(Tuesday 17th)<br/>Still bad with dysentry nothing doing this morning Two German Aeroplanes flew over this afternoon and dropped bombs and d?? doing no damage getting relieved from trenches at 7 pm<br/>(Wednesday 18th)<br/>Feeling better this morning very warm and the flies are very bad relieved trenches at 9 (pm) We are sapping a new firing line on spur that runs out in front of trenches we will be on this till morning Have to keep a good look out as the Turks can creep up the sides without us seeing them.<br/>(Thursday 19th)<br/>A little rain this morning relieved from sapping at 7 (am) after having no sleep Wrote letter to Kitty and card to Mum Relieved at 7 (pm)<br/>(Friday 20th)<br/>I believe two that two battalions of the fifth Brigade landed at Anzac last night we are going to advance to trenches in front of ours to morrow relieved trenches at 7 pm<br/>(Saturday 21st)<br/>Very quiet this morning BCD company are charging this afternoon A company 40 men are going out to clear snipers out of gully in front Advanced at 4.30 pm and drove turks from the [Indecipherable]</p>
