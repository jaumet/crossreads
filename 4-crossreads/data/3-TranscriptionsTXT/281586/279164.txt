
Six months in Camp8 July: A wet, windy morning, pavements and streets shining and slippery under horses, and pedestrians few, trams crowded, and footpaths a moving forest of umbrellas where there were no verandahs  to shelter the passers by from the inclemencies of the weather.  With Kit Bags and equipment up, I made the best of my way down to the P. and O. wharf, where lay the S.S. Mongolia, which was that morning to bear me away to where so many of our "boys" had gone before me, personal contact, with, and personal experiences in (to be chronicled hereafter) "The Great War".The Mongolia was to sail at 10.30 and we had arrived at the Wharf in good time.
