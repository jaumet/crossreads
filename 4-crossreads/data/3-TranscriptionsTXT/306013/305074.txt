 The Snow is very dry now &amp; is just beginning to thaw &amp; it is hard to walk on it especially on the roads which are very slippery. 
 In evening we heard a lecture by an Indian on the Wonder &amp; Mystery of India. He was very well educated &amp; spoke most eloquently &amp; most passionately &amp; closed his address with an expression of the hope that the Greater British Empire might be united  with  in a firm brotherhood. He closed the meeting with a passionate prayer to God to deliver us from the darkness of our present struggle. The impression he created on me was rather sublime than otherwise. 
 Feb. 9    Our leave of four days commenced to-day. We arose early &amp; had dressed, breakfasted &amp; marched to Amesbury Station (6 miles) by 9.30 A.M. Our train left at 10 &amp; we detrained at Waterloo Station, London at 2 P.M.
