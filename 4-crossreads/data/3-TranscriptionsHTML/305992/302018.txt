<p>Alexandria<br/>towing observation ballons. 2 sloops of war &amp; submarine chasers &amp; trawlers. At 8.10 a sub. periscope was sighted 1000 yards for a few seconds. One of the hydroplanes encircled around &amp; a Jap. distroyer but disappeared in deep water.<br/>At 10.00 am anchored in the inner harbor &amp; moored in the afternoon at the transport wharf. Since our departure from Alexandria last trip we find that miles of nets &amp; mines have been laid two miles from the entrance. One of the latest methods of catching submarines.</p>
<p>29 &amp; 30<br/>Sunday discharging our cargo of gun carriages &amp; travelling kitchens &amp; ammunation.</p>
<p>June 1st<br/>Shifted to the bouys &amp; had orders for a general overhaul &amp; docking our stern shaft port propellor having been badly worn on the bush. So have had orders to drop out of the convoy &amp; miss a trip. The Orient Ormond turbine 3 propellors 15,000 &amp; the Union Castle Norman 10,000 having replaced the former torpedoed ships.</p>
