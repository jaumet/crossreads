<p>The Infantry are wonderful in their callousness of casualties &amp; generally will brave anything. The little parties of 20 or 50 that sometimes run or creep out, according to the job are heroes. The N.Z. Engineers are fine chaps; work quietly away &ndash; sometimes going out to blow up enemy earthworks, some times being carried down the hill themselves.</p>
<p>The hills are now much better since the Armistice I wrote of &ndash; they are not covered in dead &amp; one can breathe better. Still there are signs of insufficiently covered bodies above our heads, here &amp; there where they were only covered with soil thrown out by accident &amp; not buried.</p>
<p>No advance has been made since we were here a fortnight ago, except a few yds of pushing closer together. I have the post well organised now &ndash; I wish I cld. send you interesting sketches: bomb throwing &amp; parafin oil flare throwing specialists sitting by their wares &ndash; sniping pits &ndash; observation pits etc.</p>
