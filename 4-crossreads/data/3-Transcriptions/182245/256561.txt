
<p>280</p>
<p>1917 FEBRUARY</p>
<p>15 THURSDAY</p>
<p>Fine &amp; Sunny. Cleaning harness</p>
<p>ect all morning &amp; out exercising</p>
<p>horses all the afternoon. I</p>
<p>rode down to Croix du Bac</p>
<p>&amp; sent a wire to mother that</p>
<p>I was well. Bill Hunter</p>
<p>&amp; Reg Callaghan went up</p>
<p>to the battery &amp; Jimmy Cooper</p>
<p>&amp; Jim [Gannon ?] came down to</p>
<p>the waggon lines. We have</p>
<p>reviellie at 6.30 AM now</p>
<p>&amp; stables at 6.45 &amp; are not</p>
<p>dismissed till 5 pm in</p>
<p>afternoon, so instead of having</p>
<p>4&frac12;&nbsp;hours parade we have 5&frac34; hrs</p>
<p>The right section were called</p>
<p>out at mid night to take up</p>
<p>ammunition, &amp; &nbsp;B [Sub?] team</p>
<p>took a gun down sniping.</p>
