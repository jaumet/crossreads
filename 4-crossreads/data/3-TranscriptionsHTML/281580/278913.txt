<p>15 December /17<br/>At 2 p.m. we sighted Port Said and by four pm dropped anchor inside the breakwater. Almost immediately a large number of native boats put off clamoring for fares. After passing through the usual examining of passports, I had all my great quantity of paraphernalia transferred to the Customs. Here I deposited instruments and materials, taking only immediate necessities on to Cairo. I never in all my life saw such a multitude of clamoring useless beings as the Porters at Port Said.  Each tries to outdo the other in noise and apparent effort, so that nothing is accomplished and everything hopelessly mixed up. With my 20 pieces of baggage, I felt helpless. I finally managed, however, to unravel the tangle, and caught 6.30 pm</p>
