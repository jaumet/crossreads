<p>beat both in body &amp; spirit, we stopped a lot of them &amp; tried to get some idea of how things were going but they could tell us very little I have my doubts if a lot of them had been near the firing line at all, by the silly yarns they told, after breakfast we "fell in" &amp; marched away up through the deserted streets of what had once been a thriving &amp; busy little village (Borre), here we dumped our packs in a big house &amp; threaded our way through the horses waggons limbers &amp; etc. which were rushing up &amp; down the road, when we got outside the village the platoons got into "Artillery formation" for we were gradually coming under long range shell fire &amp; we wanted as few casualities as possible yet a while</p>
