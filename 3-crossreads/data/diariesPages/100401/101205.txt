Sunday 6
 Through the ordinary channels to then in Hospital train.
Monday 7
 To St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital at Etaples.
Tuesday 8
 Still at St Johns Ambulance Brigade Hospital where we are having glorious weather. In my case the M.O. is frightened of bleeding since the bracheal vein has been divided I cannot be moved to England for about a week.
Wednesday 9
 From the papers it is apparent that the fighting round Bullecourt Riencourt is of a very important nature heavy mixed fighting seems to be continuous.
 The Australians have done all asked of them.
Thursday 10
 At Etaples yet the warm weather continues.
Saturday 12
 Passed for England
 Sister Higgins St John Ambulance B.H. Etaples.
