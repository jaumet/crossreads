<p>No! we cheered them as they marched off, often with the tears in our eyes, but still we <strike>proudly</strike> waved them good bye &amp; god speed.  We were proud of them &amp; gloried in the fact Australia will be there.  And if we are so squeamish about sending <strike>sound</strike> men now, is there no responsibility upon us for the lives of those who are already there?  If we vote No we are killing these men to a certainty &ndash; whereas if we vote yes, those to go in the future have a very good chance of returning themselves &amp; will give those already at the front at least a chance to see their homes &amp; loved ones once more.  For all doctors are agreed</p>
