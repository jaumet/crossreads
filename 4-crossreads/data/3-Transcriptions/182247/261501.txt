<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">&nbsp;</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Castle and Museum, Town Hall, etc.&nbsp; Bought books of all the sights to send home to give them some idea of the beauty and historical interest of this famous old City.&nbsp; After roaming round until dusk, we had a light tea and caught the 6.30 train back to Brightlingsea, arriving there about 7.15 after having spent a most enjoyable day.&nbsp; Found out that Herb Larsen is in barracks at Colchester on a Physical Culture and Bayonet instruction course.&nbsp; He expects to return to Brightlingsea on Saturday next.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 4</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 9.30.&nbsp; Church.&nbsp; Spent rest of day in billet writing mail home, as it is too cold to put one&#39;s nose outside the door.&nbsp; Sent books of views and cards home.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 5</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Snowing heavily.&nbsp; Marched to mine fields through the snow, 4 miles.&nbsp; Very cold but quite a beautiful sight to see all the snow hanging from the trees drooping under the weight of their white mantle.&nbsp; Started work after lecture and worked all day in spite of the snow which fell continuously all day.&nbsp; Spent evening in billet writing.&nbsp; Had letter from O. G. Brooke, the first I have received from him.&nbsp; Very pleased to be at last in touch with someone whom I will have the pleasure of meeting when I go up to London.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 6</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Very cold morning again.&nbsp; Mining fields all day.&nbsp; Left there 2 for town.&nbsp; Dismissed at 3.&nbsp; Went to billet.&nbsp; Had tea.&nbsp; Left billet 5.30.&nbsp; Parades at 6.&nbsp; Marched to Church and did some wire entanglement drill in the dark till 7.45.&nbsp; Returned to town and dismissed at 8.15.&nbsp; Returned to billet and spent rest of evening writing.&nbsp; Letters from home.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 7</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Cold day but fine.&nbsp; Went to Recreation Ground for Jerks.&nbsp; Stayed there 45 minutes.&nbsp; From there went out to mine fields.&nbsp; Spent all day there on trenches etc.&nbsp; Returned town 4.15.&nbsp; Got papers from home.&nbsp; Spent evening in billet after posting mail home.&nbsp; Got first intimation of official news of Arthur Goold&#39;s death.&nbsp; Feel better to-day than I have for some time past.&nbsp; Feet still very sore and tired.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 8</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Rifle drill till 9.&nbsp; Then to mine fields.&nbsp; Spent day on mining.&nbsp; Very cold and bitter wind blowing.&nbsp; Left there 3.15.&nbsp; Got big bunch of home mail.&nbsp; Feel terribly elated at so much mail from the home end.&nbsp; Had hot bath and spent evening reading and writing to various friends and relations.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Feb 9</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Robinson and I told off to write passes.&nbsp; Went to billet this time and did the job for fear we would again be discovered and sent flying to our work.&nbsp; Left town at 11.&nbsp; Arrived mine fields noon.&nbsp; Had dinner on arrival there - such as it was - two pieces of dry bread with a piece of cheese between the first</td>
