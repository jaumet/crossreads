<p>29 January 1917<br/>Still very cold.  Heavy snow lying on the ground, everything frozen.  Fairly lively artillery from both sides.</p>
<p>30th<br/>Still working on C.R.E. Defences.  Very cold.</p>
<p>31st<br/>C.R.E. Defences.  Still bitterly cold.  Heavy artillery activity on both sides.</p>
<p>1st Feb. 1917<br/>4th Div. hopped over at night &amp; took some of Jerry's trenches but were compelled to evacuate them early next morning.  Took about 40 prisoners.  We lost fairly heavily.</p>
<p>2nd<br/>Heavy German shelling during the early morning  Still very cold.</p>
<p>3rd<br/>Still on C.R.E. defences.  Very heavy shelling by our Guns today.</p>
<p>4.2.17<br/>Same work on C.R.E. Defences.  Nothing of importance going.</p>
<p>5th<br/>Still having cold weather.  Same old shelling.</p>
<p>6th<br/>C.R.E. Defence work.  Nothing fresh going.</p>
<p>7th<br/>Nothing fresh going.  Artillery active.  C.R.E. Defence work.</p>
<p>8th<br/>Having cold weather still.  C.R.E. Defence work.</p>
<p>9th<br/>Heavy shelling both side.  C.R.E. Defences.  Nothing fresh doing.</p>
<p>10th<br/>Having a series of Lectures by O.C.  Still cold.  Artillery activity.</p>
<p>11th<br/>Still on Deep dugouts.  Fairly lively shelling both sides.</p>
<p>12th<br/>Still on O.C.'s Lectures.  Nothing fresh going.</p>
<p>13th<br/>Artillery activity brisk both sides.  Cold.  Still on C.R.E. Defences.</p>
