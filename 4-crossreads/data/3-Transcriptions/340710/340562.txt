<p>old man," said the O.C after the scrutiny was over "make yourself at home.  Would you like something to eat?"  "I would like a little bwead and butter, sir, if you don't mind".  We looked at one another, he lisps thought everyone of us.  However the "Bwead" was produced and Cuthbert as we afterwards called him settled down and we resumed our bridge.<br/>That was our first introduction to Cuthbert.  We were all frankly puzzled with him from then on, for we had not met his type before.<br/>He came to us outwardly laden with the entire kit of a military outfiter's shop but inwardly we discovered he was laden with original ideas of how to finish the war and these he expounded at every opportunity.  His listeners looking frankly bored the while.</p>
