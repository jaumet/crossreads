  
 September 1918. Wednesday 4 Attached to 15th A.1. Brdge [indecipherable] Weather fine and clear. Laid a line 5 miles in length to 15th Bdge Hdqrs, where Major and Mr. Kerr were  observing from. Naturally felt fairly tired when finished. Received information to the fact that we were pulling into another position tonight. Packed up all kits and loaded all equipment, a pretty long job. Went across to see Lt John, Battery pulled out to new position up to [indecipherable] and we pulled in  alongside 51st and 55th Batteries.14th Bgde received great praise for the capture of Mont St Quentin
