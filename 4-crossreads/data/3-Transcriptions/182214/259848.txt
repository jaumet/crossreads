<p>a great number of the infantry bathed their sore feet here &amp; again got left behind no one seemed to care how things went, I might say here I felt real well &amp; very fit perhaps because I did not carry my pack or gear&nbsp;still us cooks have always to be getting a meal ready for tired men after a march in spite of the fact we have to march with the men We crossed the Canal by the pontoon Bridge at Ferry post &amp; went a mile out into the desert where our march was ended. 40 miles over desert. a journey said to have been done in 1882 when the Battle of Tel-el-Kebir was fought. I will leave the reader to think for himself</p>
