<p>27 April 1915<br/>  <strike>Tuesday</strike><br/><u>Monday Contd.</u></p>
<p>Have considerable trouble about drawing rations so have nothing to eat but the dry rations we brought with us.</p>
<p><u>Tuesday</u></p>
<p>Find sanitary arrangements in a shocking state but am glad to see that men are at work making a new set of latrines for us</p>
<p>1915 April 28<br/>Wednesday</p>
<p>Settled down again and begin our usual parades but as very different hours<br/>Reveille 5.30<br/>Breakfast 6.15<br/>Parade 7. To 11<br/>dinner 12<br/>Parade 2.30 to 4.30<br/>Tea 5.<br/>by which can be seen that we have practically no time in the morning for either washing ourselves   &amp;  again for breakfast while we have 3 &frac12;   <strike>3   &amp;  1/2</strike> hours for  <u>dinner</u> time</p>
