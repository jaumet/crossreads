After visiting various cafes, listening to street performers &amp; so on, I wended my way to the station for the 6.30 train.  Here a young gippy youngster about 6 years old surprised me by calling out &ndash; you Australian to which I replied &ndash; No.  O you Scotchman Harry Lauder from Dublin he replied with a grin.  These youngsters are wonders in picking up things in more ways than one, language &amp; thieving.  I then went to "Ismailia" in order to visit the No. 1. A.S.H. who received me as an old friend &amp; put me up in the Sergeants quarters.  I was taken to the pictures but they were a medium lot.  The next day I spent yarning &amp; looking around the place which is very pretty &amp; was fortunate in that the Prince of Wales chose the same day to visit the Hospital so that I had a unique chance of viewing him.  He struck me as being very decent, shy &amp; reserved &amp; conscious of his position.  His walk is execrable &amp; reminded me of an old man walking flat footed through a plouged field.
