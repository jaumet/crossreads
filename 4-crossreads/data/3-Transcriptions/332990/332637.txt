<p>I am again stoking this evening from 12 . until 10. p.m. From the church parade grounds the furrows stone hinge of the early Britons can be plainly seen.<br/>[Short hand not transcribed] I read a book on Stonehenge today.</p>
<p>Monday July 31. 1916</p>
<p>A nice morning. We had breakfast early and left Lark Hill camp <strike>at 10</strike> early  marched into Amesbury Station which we left at 10.25. reaching Clapham Junction at 12.26 arriving at Waterloo Station about 1. O'Clock, [indecipherable] we marched to the Australian Artiliary depot and were dismissed. We are to reassemble at Waterloo Station on Thursday August 3. at a quarter  to 9.p.m.</p>
<p>We had dinner at the J.U.C.A. near the Military Depot but had to go to the Waterloo Branch for beds which are 6 d each.</p>
