<p>dawn. It was murder to send men over the top in this weather; some were shot down as they stood waist deep in the mire; unable to move. I rejoined the Company. These trying conditions have been telling on the boys. There are some bad cases of trench feet. Word was received, that our Colonel has died of wounds. We will miss him for he was a very gallant gentleman.<br/>6/11/16.<br/>Conditions are very trying here  have no tobacco, not the least of my troubles. Two young Huns came in and surrended to me this morning; one was a cool specimen  said his mother told him to surrender  a good many Huns have given themselves up to us on this sector; they are mostly out of Saxon Regiments;</p>
