<p>We left again on Sept. 21st 1917, we had a 3 Kilo march to the motor-lorries. Hop fields grew in profusion just round this area and particularly well they looked too!</p>
<p>We left by motor lorry about 7 and travelling till about 1 in the morning, we came to a halt <strike>to</strike> at a place, called Belgian Chateau. We passed innumerable war traffic on our journey, all seemed to be intent on reaching the line, a big push must be on the cards, it was too!</p>
<p>Fritz had been doing his dirty work, in the shape of "laying his eggs" on defenceless towns. He was right above us in one sector, about "a mile up" I should guess. All manner of anti-aircraft guns and its helpers, the machine guns, had a cut at him, but he flew unharmed away.</p>
