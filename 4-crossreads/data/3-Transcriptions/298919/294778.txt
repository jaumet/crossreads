<p>signal stations.  On the southern side and extending from the waters edge to half way up the hill is the town &amp; barracks.  The dock we were taken to was a well built stone one.  Running parallel with it galvanized iron sheds were piled high with coal, from which a crowd of Spaniards were employed carting the coal in buckets to our bunkers soon after we arrived.  After breakfast couple of the other officers &amp; self went ashore.  Luckily for us we went with the old man, and when we were bailed up for permission to leave he was able to make an immediate application to the Admiral for passes for us.  It is impossible to leave or enter the dockyard without permission.  Passing through the dockyard gates where our passes were endorsed we hired a carriage and drove</p>
