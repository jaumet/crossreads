<td style="text-align: right; vertical-align: top;">18</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 23</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Marched out to Trench works and put in day digging trenches.&nbsp; Pretty hard work for soft men.&nbsp; Glad of it to make the body feel a little something like warm and alive.&nbsp; No letters to-day.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 24</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Lecture in hall on revetments etc. till 10.&nbsp; Marched out to Trench works and spent all day on hurdle making for revetments.&nbsp; Saw demolition attempt on bomb-proof shelter with 10 lbs of Guncotton, with no serious hurt to the shelter.&nbsp; Had a look over the beautiful old church during dinner hour.&nbsp; Went to public baths after tea and enjoyed the luxury of a warm bath which cost the large sum of 3d.&nbsp; Received letters from home to-day.&nbsp; Spent evening writing etc.,</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 25</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Lecture in hall on cover, shelters, etc.&nbsp; Marched to trench works and spent day in making hurdles again.&nbsp; Visited old church again.&nbsp; Wrote letters at night.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 26</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Very cold morning.&nbsp; Marched to works and did revetting.&nbsp; Works inspected by General.&nbsp; Left work 2.45.&nbsp; Dismissed 3.15.&nbsp; Spent evening by fire in billet reading and writing.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 27</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 9.&nbsp; Route march till 10.&nbsp; Then inoculated by &quot;Dr Walrus&quot; who prolonged the agony somewhat.&nbsp; Went to Foresters Hall and got paid 30/-.&nbsp; Bought some gloves.&nbsp; Arranged for bike ride for next day.&nbsp; After dinner, made towards Post Office and was unfortunately seen by S.M. Wilson and detailed by him for duty.&nbsp; Marched to the Hard and had to load iron rails on a lorry, with others.&nbsp; And this with a sore arm consequent upon and incidental to inoculation.&nbsp; From there went to railway and put them on to a truck.&nbsp; Finished up and posted registered package of first instalment of Diary and some keys home.&nbsp; Spent evening at home writing out job for Smithy, and lectures.&nbsp; Got new boots to-day.&nbsp; No letters.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 28</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Sunday.&nbsp; Usual Church parade.&nbsp; Dry sermon.&nbsp; Went for walk after round the oyster beds and the Hard.&nbsp; Went for mail after.&nbsp; None.&nbsp; After dinner, went out on bike with Frank Ward.&nbsp; Left billet 1. Returned 4.&nbsp; Rode about 17 miles.&nbsp; Rather tired after it.&nbsp; Went to within three miles of Clacton-on-Sea.&nbsp; Had cup of tea at Flag Inn at St Osyth.&nbsp; Very cold riding.&nbsp; Wore gloves, muffler, balaclava, mittens, great coat, and even then felt cold.&nbsp; Spent evening at home copying lectures and writing diary.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Jan 29</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 8.&nbsp; Lecture in hall.&nbsp; Marched to Trench works and carried on with trench work all day.&nbsp; Nice day.&nbsp; Snowstorm at mid-day.&nbsp; Got very cold after.&nbsp; Left works 3.&nbsp; No mail as usual.&nbsp; Stayed in at night and wrote and read papers.</td>
</tr>
