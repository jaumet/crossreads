<p>Thursday July 13. 1916.<br/>It is hot to day. There are clouds around the horizon but none overhead. There was a strong head wind last night &amp; its continues with slight moderation today. The course is 4 points N of N.W.</p>
<p>A wireless message states that the British captured a heavy howitzer and 3 field guns. After ten days and nights of continual fighting our troops captured the whole of the enemys first system of defence on a front of 14000 yards.</p>
<p>Friday July 14. 1916.<br/>It is dull &amp; cloudy &amp; misty this morning. The wind is from the North. The course is 2 points North of North West.</p>
