<p>13-11-16 Orders to shift camp at 7 am to day. 11 pm now no sign of waggons moved camp at 5. pm about a mile and half E of Morogoro above aviation camp better camp than the last close to water</p>
<p>14th Nov Wrote to Vi and Aunt.</p>
<p>15 Received letter from Addie and one from Harry Butler. <strike>Wrote</strike></p>
<p>16 Nov. Unfit men left for Daresalam 7. a.m. to day. 200 <strike>hundred</strike> observation cases expected in from Kilossa</p>
<p>17th Wrote to Vi. Ad. Harry. B.</p>
<p>18 Visited Dennis went for a ride up to old Detail camp in Phaeton</p>
<p>19 First train from Dar essalaam to Morogoro arrived on the 17th visited Dennis had lunch.</p>
<p>20 usual Routine</p>
<p>21st walked up the hill trying to purchase poultry or eggs no luck</p>
<p>22 Visited Dennis. Met Tim Thompson in hospital Gen Britts in Town</p>
<p>23/11/16 24-25-26 Usual Routine Camp</p>
<p>27 Camp Routine Y.MC.A sent up a few necessaries soap etc</p>
<p>28-11-16 News of surrender of 63 Germans 200 askaris one Howitzer three machine Guns somewhere in the Iringa District.</p>
<p>29 All saddles and bridles to be handed in tomorrow</p>
<p>30/11/16 Routine as usual attack of fever again</p>
<p>1.12.16 Routine still fever</p>
<p>2-3-4 Authentic news of Gen Northeys Force having had an encounter with Germans somewhere South G. N. force buried 71 Germans 300 askaris 500 prisoners &ndash; result of months work. Good Work.</p>
<p>5-6 Routine as usual</p>
<p>7th News of 3 of our motor cars being captured and burned on the Morogoro Kisaki Kisaki Rd</p>
