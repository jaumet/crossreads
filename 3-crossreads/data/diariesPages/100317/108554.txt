After lunch went around to present different medal winners with the ribands of their decorations but found most of them away either up the line or at the nucleus. Not a single shell came anywhere near us right throughout the whole operation of walking round. The men all getting ready to go up forward and packing up their belongings. At 10.30 left our home walked up to Marett Wood where we conducted a quiet relief of the 21st. It was a dark night inclined to rain and the gun flashes and reports lighting up the dark sky were weird. At 1 a.m. under cover of a barrage we put over a gas projector attack evacuating portion of our own trenches and wearing gas respirators. Although there seemed to be a good clod the Hun took little notice and replied with a very small retaliatory shelling
