<p>the others, set off &amp; look for a place to have some breakfast. At last 9.30 am find a Pattiserie &amp; get some tea &amp; cakes. Rouen is a big city    &amp; the Cathedral is a great model of Architecture. Cobbled streets but very fine &amp; rather busy. Some fine shops &amp; the old Seine runs through the town, &amp; is crossed by many bridges. At the English Hdqrs find the boys are still away &amp; am directed to the Australian Branch at Rue de Lessard 25 &amp;  on the way there meet the rest of the boys coming back. All arrangements made &amp; we are to commence work to-morrow morning (Sunday). Receive 30 francs each to carry on &amp; the next thing is to find some diggings, as we are all billetted in the town &amp; allowed 4/- per day to pay for it. After a great deal of trouble, Legge, Palliser &amp; I find a room at the Hotel Normandie  The old Irish Contingent still together</p>
