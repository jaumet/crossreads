<p>Monday 3rd Feb  The day of my departure for Australia.  Up about 8.30 &amp; put my things together &amp; said goodbye.  I did not feel the parting so much as all  my best friends had gone &amp; I had become accustomed to the idea of going.  The Colonel when saying goodbye said - 'Today this unit will receive one of its greatest blows" , referring to the departure of myself &amp; Shepherd, some kid.  Went by car to Beaumont, 8 kilos, &amp; left our gear at a house where we had some men attending to local sick, then joined the infantry party who had just marched in headed by a band.  The march to the camp was rather impressive.  It was at the front of a hill &amp; was overlooked by a castle &amp; one of the bandsmen mounted the parapet &amp; played 'Goodbyee' which rang out clear on the frosty air, it</p>
