<p>ran within a mile of the Germans, &amp; in front of our own guns. They stayed in Hazebrouck at a foundry, &amp; were shelled out. Nevertheless they got plenty of food which was found in the deserted houses. It was in Hazebrouck that Billy Roscoe was wounded.<br/>Then they came to St Sylvester Cappel where I found them on Sunday afternoon.</p>
