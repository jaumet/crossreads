<p>Far below &amp; behind us was a grand panorama of the Camp, the Village &amp; the Harbour, while in front was a stony sandy plain quite flat for miles &amp; in the distance some more low hills we never saw a living thing stirring all day. A message came along that the column got in touch with the enemy late in the afternoon.</p>
<p>We were relieved at 6.30 pm by the night picket &ndash; 20 men &amp; a Sergeant &ndash; All today we have been "standing by" not a very exciting share in a battle Several motor ambulance loads of wounded have come in, and some more waggons left this afternoon with supplies &amp; I expected to be</p>
