<p>May 4 old buildings their red tiled roofs lending an added charm. In front, showing above the buildings of the Hosp, glimpses of the Harbor; a little to the left, the dark mouth of a Railway Tunnel &amp; beyond that again the spire &amp; dome of the Cathedral, &amp; 2 windmills (the old &amp; the new) standing side by side. And stretching away on all sides that glorious carpet of green.<br/>Napoleons Monument is a fine big stone column for all the world like a big lighthouse &ndash; with a figure of Nap' perched on top. It was from here that he intended invading England, but evidently the British fleet made him alter his mind. The Wesleyan parson a decent little chap, has been round too see me a couple of times. No quiver about him either. Not a bad gramophone in this joint. [in margin] M</p>
