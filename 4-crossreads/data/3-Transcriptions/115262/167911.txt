<p>Thursday 24 May 1917.</p>
<p>Now well out into the open sea and nearing the &quot;danger zone&quot;. A very calm sea today hardly a ripple on the water &amp; a fresh breeze blowing.&nbsp;(11 Knots an hr today.) Awnings spread today. Got into shorts and put away woollen clothing. The opening concert on board ship this afternoon (Empire Day)</p>
<p>False &quot;fire alarms&quot; at 2.15pm.</p>
<p><u>Absolutely</u> no light of any description after dusk from now on</p>
<p>Cruiser on alert now. Tonight sea is &quot;as smooth as glass&quot;.</p>
<p>Compulsory washing parade in morng.</p>
<p>Cake cigarettes, lollies &amp; apples issued by Y.M.C.A to all on board.</p>
<p>A scare l<s>ast</s> this morning at 3 a.m. Cruiser prepared for action when we came acrosss a sailing boat that altered her course quickly &amp; put on speed.</p>
