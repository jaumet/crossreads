<p>O.A.S.<br/>5.5.17</p>
<p>My dear Judge,</p>
<p>I have received your letter of 18th Feb written a week after your return from New Zealand. In spite of some rough weather you seem to have had an interesting return journey.</p>
<p>The news about Caesar &amp; Walter MacCallum was very welcome, also that about Keith, though at the present moment I am still in absolute ignorance as to the extent and nature of his wound. His Division is on the Somme &amp; has been through some of the thickest fighting, I believe, though of course it is a good many miles away from us. You have no doubt had particulars, and all I can do is hope that it is nothing more serious than<br/>would give him a good rest out of the line for a while.</p>
<p>With the fine weather which we are now enjoying I fancy there will be continued hard fighting at different points along the whole of the Western front and on the other fronts too as the year advances. When activities are going to be extended up to our part of the line I cannot say. I hope it may be soon as we are beginning to fret against</p>
