The house used for the clearing station was a stone building right on the left of the arena; it seemed such an incongruity for it to have been build there; a modern humpy in amongst such grand old ruins - but anyhow it suited me in a way, for as soon as we had&nbsp;had dinner, and been inspected by the Dr, I sneaked off with my camera to have a goof look at things. Generally I cant enthuse over ancient ruins, and sacred place - they are all so dead and dusty, and it seems such sacrilege to see the people fighting over&nbsp;their&nbsp;holy places but all joining together to rob you, and take you down, when you come to look at them. I would much rather poke about in the bush for an afternoon, than go out to look at the grimy old sphinx and pyramids; but this Roman theater seemed to thrill me wonderfully - it seemed so complete in its old grandeur and yet <s>it</s>&nbsp; fitted so splendidly into the green valley and hills which surrounded it.
