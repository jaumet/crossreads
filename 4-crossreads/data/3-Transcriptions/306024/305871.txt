<p>We did nothing much that afternoon but from rumours &amp; prisoners we heard of the second advance &ndash; carried out to improve the strength of the line and to enable the 55th &amp; 56th to come into positions in the front. By about 5 pm the brigade was along the red line shewn on the map. It was then that their casualties were so severe.</p>
<p>Soon after dark we all went up with ammunition. We collected it from a dump on the road above Halle and took it to Major Murray's position beyond the wood. The whole area was drenched with gas and the shells were falling</p>
