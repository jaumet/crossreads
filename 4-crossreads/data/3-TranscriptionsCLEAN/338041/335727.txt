  
 Aug 23rd. We make an early start for Becourt-Wood where we relieve 1st Fld Amb and take up our new duties. (Day-duty dressing room. "Serious cases.) Plenty of solid work - Indescribable wounds - (*North, Fusiliers) 
 Aug. 24th (Elsie's Birthday) Many more serious cases Our Dressing station formerly a private chapel. Family vaults under the chapel. 
 Aug 25th. Another successful Anzac attack against the Huns. Our good work proceeds. 
 Aug 26th As a result of the previous nights work we are extra busy. Our room resembling a slaughter yard - During the day we have one man killed & 12 wounded from our ambulance Inspected by two Generals
