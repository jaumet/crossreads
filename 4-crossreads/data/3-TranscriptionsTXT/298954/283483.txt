 She is the "Winslow" 496 tons, owners Hall of San Francisco, and was 21 days out from Sydney to Apia (Samoa) with 250 tons of coal,1500 firebricks &amp; 50 cases of benzine.   A crew of 10 &ndash; mixed Scowegians.   The Captain says that seeing the two columns of smoke from the steamers he took it for a distress signal from Sunday Island (charted as uninhabited) and was coming to render assistance. 
 Sun. 17th June. This morning the boarding party stripped the copper pipes from the "Wairuna's" engine-room, brought same with them in the launch &amp; left two bombs aboard the "Wairuna" timed for about 15 minutes.   The only one I saw explode was in the port well. 
 The vessel failed to sink &ndash; merely listing to port. 
 The forward bulwarks were dropped and the guns came into action.
