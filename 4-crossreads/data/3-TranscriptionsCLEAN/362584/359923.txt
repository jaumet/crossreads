  
 At Sea Today 
 My dear mother, 
 Letter writing was never my strong point, but when there is nothing to say & when even if there was something to sat, a rigid censorship would prevent me from saying it, the task becomes impossible. 
 We are all well except Jack Mant who has a touch of some sort of fever & is just recovering from the effects of it. Judging by the amount of food & chocolates he puts away I should say that he has quite recovered, but he has been in bed for some days & is still a bit weak. 
 Yesterday I was captain of the day, & had to accompany the ship's captain & O.C. Troops on their inspection of the troop-decks, & quarters.
