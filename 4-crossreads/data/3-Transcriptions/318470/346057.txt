<p>14-2-17<br/>Still working on Deep dugouts.  Brisk shelling both sides.</p>
<p>15th<br/>Nothing fresh going.  Still on C.R.E. Defence work.</p>
<p>16th<br/>Large amunition Dump blown up this morning by "Taub" continued to burn &amp; shells to explode all day.</p>
<p>17th<br/>Big Dump still burning &amp; shells exploding.  Said to have been &pound;2,000,000 lost.</p>
<p>18th<br/>Weather getting warmer &amp; wet weather set in.  Still on C.R.E. Defences.  British Success on the Ancre.</p>
<p>19th<br/>Very wet &amp; ground getting very muddy.  Heavy artillery fire.</p>
<p>20th<br/>Still on C.R.E. D.  Very foggy weather and wet.</p>
<p>21-2-17</p>
<p>Finer weather.  Fair amount of shelling from Jerry.  Still on C.R.E. Defences at Ginchy and 2nd Line.</p>
<p>22nd<br/>Nothing fresh to report.  Things going well.</p>
<p>23rd<br/>Hand over C.R.E. Defences to 8th &amp; 15th Field Coys.  Look over work from other Sections.</p>
<p>24th<br/>Working on various jobs Delville Wood, Montauban, etc.</p>
<p>25th<br/>3 Sections has gone to camp at Fleurs.  No. 2 Section is doing back work.</p>
<p>26th<br/>Still on back works.  Germans are said to be retiring to new Lines.</p>
<p>27th<br/>Still on back works.  Nothing fresh going.</p>
<p>28th<br/>Still working on hutting &amp; other back line work.</p>
<p><strike>29th</strike></p>
<p>1st [March 1917]<br/>Good advances are being made along our front in small Sectors.</p>
