<p>29th<br/>Arrived Albany, Western Australia and anchored in King George's Sound.</p>
<p>31st<br/>Left Albany 9 a.m., a fleet of 16 ships and one submarine, AE2 (since lost in the Sea of Marmora).  Sailed in three columns, "Ceramic" leading one, "Ulysses" (flagship) one, and Themistocles leading the Berrima in front with A.E.2, and Ayrshire falling rapidly astern.</p>
<p>1915<br/>January<br/>1st<br/>New Year's Day.  On guard again.  Concert held at night.</p>
<p>10th<br/>Crossed Equator.</p>
<p>11th<br/>Vaccinated.</p>
<p>12th<br/>Most of the fleet left us to go to Colombo to coal.  The Suevic, Persic and Ceramic held on their course for Aden.</p>
