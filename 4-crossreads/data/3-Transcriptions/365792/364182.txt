<p>Apl 11  guns, roosters, bugs, mice, rats, a yelling kiddie &amp; - "Kelly" &ndash;<br/>No bath parade today on a/c of a Div' inspection so we cleaned up the farm-yard; &amp; it was <u>some</u> mucky too after rain. Have to carry gas helmets on a/c of aerial gas bombs. The mice are so numerous that they parade in platoons &amp; companies, &amp; even march in columns of route, but owing to lack of rafter- width they are exempted from forming fours.<br/>The big guns are beginning to speak to some purpose, guess the big bombardment has commenced in deadly earnest. Reckon id sooner be in a charge than under fire in a trench, specially if the big naval guns were at it.</p>
<p>12  Turned out at 6.30 this morning &ndash; full marching order &ndash; for inspection by General Walker, &amp; by gee we did a freeze for an hour or so. They say this is Spring, but I reckon its more like midwinter. Not a bad</p>
