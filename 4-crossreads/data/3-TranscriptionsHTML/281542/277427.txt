<p>Thursday 13th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Still little tucker.  We live on Arabic bread, &amp; roots from the surrounding gardens.  Sgt. decides to go to Cairo, &amp; arrives back about 4 p.m. with bread, meat &amp; vegetables &amp; we are saved.</p>
<p>Friday 14th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Weather very cold.  We are now being catered for by a Dago &amp; everything is O.K.  Beginning to feel my old self again.</p>
<p>Saturday 15th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Paid &pound;3.  Went to Cairo.  Came home on trolley from Cairo Station, pushed by two natives who ran along the rails.  Asst. Station Master accompanied me.  The ride was quite enjoyable though a little cold.  Bright moonlight, calm, with violet sky common to Egyptian nights.</p>
<p>Gave the natives 5 piastres between them.</p>
<p>Sunday 16th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Left for Cairo at 10 am via Shubra village.  Pleasant train ride into Cairo.</p>
<p>Monday 17th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Uneventful.  Weather cold &amp; damp with occasional showers.</p>
<p>Tuesday 18th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Dull, grey damp day.  Sharp shower early morn.</p>
<p>Wednesday 19th Jan 1916</p>
<p>Relieved Main Cairo Station Guard during afternoon.  Posted 9 per shift also 1 am</p>
<p>Thursday 20th Jan 1916</p>
<p>New position a sinecure, excellent food &amp; plenty of spare time &amp; leave.  Reasonable Officer &amp; Sgts  in charge.  Sgt  Major Robins, Senior N.C.O. a good fellow.</p>
