<p>Monday 19 February 1917</p>
<p>Still rumours of peace this year, I hope, as Kipling says "WAR should be made by men without wives, Bangles ring softly &amp; sadly" &amp; one cannot help feeling different when married.</p>
<p>[The following crossed through]<br/>Thank you for paying my Insurance Premium, if I live 6 years I draw the Principal &ndash; Use the balance of Interest from W &amp; A for anything wanted in the House &ndash; I pay nothing now, but often think of you all &amp; the old Pater who has gone. Your letter of Dec. 31st was such a nice long one, it came last night, also May's song, too much forcing conscription to sell &amp; not enough about GIRLS to take on with the Soldiers.</p>
