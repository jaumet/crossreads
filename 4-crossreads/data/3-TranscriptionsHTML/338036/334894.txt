<p>[20th April contd]<br/>Met Wearne of Liverpool Field Hosp who had come across as Reinfs to 11th Fld Amb.</p>
<p>Saturday 21 [April]<br/>Completed our work at about 2.  To date we had collected about 130 tons of bricks in 4 nights.  Germans bombarding Pont de Nieppe &amp; Armentieres rather heavily.  I had quite an experience trying to dodge some whilst passing along the road.  Met Geo Francis (now Sgt) QM.  Fritz very savage today.</p>
<p>Sunday 22nd<br/>Tonight was to be our last on working party.  It was &ndash; but we won't forget it in a hurry.  When about half the job was finished &ndash; the Germans raided the trenches just opposite to where we were working.  At once our barrage fire opened &amp; the noise gee it was some.  We finished loading the waggons that were down there expecting any moment for a shell to come over but we were fortunate.</p>
<p>A couple of machine bullets whizzing quite close decided the issue for us &amp; we decided to run the gauntlet along the open road to safety notwithstanding that during a scrap it is always shelled &amp; open to machine gun fire.  However after a very anxious quarter of hour we negotiated the road &amp; heaved a sigh.</p>
