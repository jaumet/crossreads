 Friday 30&nbsp;November 1917. 
 Everything quiet until &quot;Jacko&quot; started with  6 inch  5.9 inch Howitzer shells to shell the watering place of the British Yoemanry at 4 pm. 
 Shells went wide at first and landed short near Hospital tent. Cpl. &amp; W.O. knocked over by concussion of shell. 
 Village stopped several, killing &amp; wounding  natives  bedouin inhabitants. 
 Shelling ceased at dark. 
 On picquet at 6 pm.
