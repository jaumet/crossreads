  Intoxicating Liquores   No Intoxicating liquor are allowed in the posession of Pris. of War without special permission.  Smoking   Pris. of War are allowed to smoke at all times &amp; places except after "lights out" and during Parades.  Correspondence   No verbal cuminication to persones outside of the boundarys will be allowed. Letters dealing with the political situation or Naval or Military operations, will be stopped. The use of Cipher Code unintelligible marks or signs or shorthand in letters to &amp; from Pris. is entirely forbidden.  Letters must be written in Ink &amp; on one side only of the Paper. Pris. of War are allowed not more than two letters a week, each containing not more than 150 words.  If both Writer &amp;  Sender  Adressee understand English, Letters must be written in English.  Letters written in that language hawe precedence in censoring over letters written in other languages.  The restrictiones in regard to number &amp; length of letters may be released in case of buisness  correspondence if the Camp Comandant or Officer in charge is satisfied
