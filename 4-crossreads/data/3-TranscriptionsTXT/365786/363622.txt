
Strathfield Red + 13/3/16
When one of our men is wounded, he is first attended to by his own mates or any Army Medical Corps men who happen to be handy & hurried back to the nearest dressing station.  There a Doctor binds up his wounds, checks haemorrhage etc. & sends him back to the Field Ambulance.  This is a series of tents arranged like a small hospital, having & has in war time a personel of 240 (or thereabouts), Doctors & Orderlies, stretcher bearers etc. but no nurses.  Here small necessary amputations take place & cases requiring immediate operation are attended to & from here the cases patients are drafted to hospital ships & sent to the big base hospitals - far from the battlefield where the men get every attention & hope have the comfort of an ordinary hospital, thanks mainly to the Red Cross Society which supplies
