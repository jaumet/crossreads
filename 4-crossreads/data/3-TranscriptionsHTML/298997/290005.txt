<p>a wounded man past.  He was a ghastly sight, looking greenish and pain- racked, &amp; covered with dark slimy patches &ndash; blood &amp; mud.  From some of the men following him down, we learnt, while we were still halted, that he had gone out on a patrol the previous night, and the party did not know he had been hit until they missed him when they came in.  He was lying out in the slush &amp; snow, wounded for 30 hours: it was impossible to go out to look for him before tonight.  This brought to me the possibilities, and gave me a sick feeling.  It was very pleasant, when we arrived</p>
