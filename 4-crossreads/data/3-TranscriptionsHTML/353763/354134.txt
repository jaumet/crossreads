<p>S.H. is in New Britain &ndash; in the centre of the German archipelago &amp; Riboul<strike>t</strike> is its town &amp; now we are steaming at 8 knots per hour &amp; we are but 400 miles off  -<br/>We are on an even Keel &amp; all joly once amp; again but she's a cranky, rusty old tub - always something going wrong with her  -  The Cook's mate says she frightens the engineers &amp; firemen  -  Fine weather she's all right but when she gets her tail out &amp; races one wonders how things hold together  -<br/>We are off the S.E. of N. Guinea &amp; going thro a whole lot of islands &ndash; mostly wooded but not the cocoa nut palm evidently   -  N.G. went S. E.  &amp; there sank &amp; these islands are the tops &amp; knobs of high land or else coral islands  -  A small, white bellied stormy Petrel &amp; flying fish are the sole life  -  I saw a Frigate or it might be a Bosun bird with the long tail feathers this morning  -<br/>Friday  -  Sept 11  - 14 <u> Raboul</u><br/>We all put in a very good day  -  we met the Fleet  -  Australia with Admiral Patey  -  Cruisers Sydney &amp; Melbourne  -  The Berrima with her mob of soldiers &amp; The Parramatta - a destroyer  -  We got to Simpson Haven &amp; as the Protector &amp; Upolu steamed in &ndash; the</p>
