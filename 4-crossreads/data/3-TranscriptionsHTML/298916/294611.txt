<p>Melbourne." In nature he was reserved &amp; rather sulky looking &amp; seldom smiling. When "getting busy" he was a demon &amp; quickly the word would pass round &ndash; "Look out, the old man is on the move" &amp; there was no need to say it twice. Everybody was afraid of him &amp; he knew it. Even the Sucanies would hide in the ash hoist until the way was clear. He was known for his "don't stop" way in a storm. Nothing like that ever worried him, providing there was no deck cargo liable to shift, &amp; also that he was in good trim. He was very like the Chief Officer in some things &amp; would roar like a bull over nothing, &amp; lose his onion as Teddy put it, over a trifle. He would calm down again just as quickly as he started. Very exacting in detail &amp; when he said No! it was better to leave it at no rather than try &amp; alter it. During the afternoon Teddy as usual went to sleep &amp; I went up the Domain &amp; joined in the amusement caused by "Old Dick" a religious fanatic spouting away.</p>
