<p>War Diary<br/>1916 17th April<br/>at Rail Head</p>
<p>18th April<br/>Corps Commander inspected Major Scott's Jifjafa party and expressed his great satisfaction.</p>
<p>[In right margin]  Lt. Wilson E.H. 8th L.H. died in Hospl. Ismailia</p>
<p>19th April<br/>Triple typhoid innoculation in the Bde. was commended. [commenced]</p>
<p>20th April<br/>Training continued in intended work and protection &ndash; men feeling the effects of being innoculated</p>
<p>21st April<br/>"Good Friday", voluntary Church Parade, remainder of day observed as a holiday.</p>
<p>22nd April<br/>Regimental inspection.</p>
<p>23rd April<br/>Church Parade</p>
<p>24th April<br/>[Bde Major to Tel-el-Kebir] to look into matters with Training Regt.</p>
<p>25th April<br/>Enemy planes reported more active.  Anzac Day treated as a holiday as far as possible</p>
<p>26th April<br/>Patrol of one troop under Capt. Dunckley towards Moiya Habib to report on presence of enemy and water &ndash; nothing seen &ndash; Lt. [name not shown] with one troop escort to water convoy supporting</p>
<p>27th April<br/>ordinary patrols sent out and regimental training continued.</p>
<p>28th April<br/>Regimental Training and building of stables continued.</p>
<p>29th April<br/>Regimental inspection</p>
<p>30th April<br/>Church Parade.  G.O.C. 4th Divn. inspected right of line &ndash; redistribution of troops on front line.</p>
<p>1st May<br/>G.O.C. inspected front line &ndash; 9th L.H. concentrated at Railhead all horses being sent out from Railhead.</p>
<p>2nd May<br/>G.O.C. to Tel-el-Kebir to inspect training regiments and 11th L.H. &ndash; test concentration of 9th L.H. at Hill 580 &ndash; a good turn out</p>
<p>3rd May<br/>Permanent signalling stations (3) established ahead of front line.  Front line inspected.</p>
<p>4th May<br/>G.O.C. inspected left of line, arrangements made for moving 2nd Batts. up into front line</p>
<p>5th May<br/>Corps Commander inspected left of line;  intelligence indicates enemy troops and guns moving east.</p>
