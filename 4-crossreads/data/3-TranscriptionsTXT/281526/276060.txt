
11/1/18 Oxfordthe Cathedral of the city. On entering one is struck by the beauty of the chancel - stone fan tracery ceiling and a round rose window of delicately coloured glass. The Church is small. The mural tablets almost without exception are in Latin. There are a number of old tombs one of a mailed warrior a comrade of the Black Prince. The Lady Chapel is remarkable. The framework of two old brick doorways partly below floor level attest the existence of a previous church. As usual in most of these places most of the floor stones are tombstones too and their inscriptions are gradually being worn off by the feet of passers by. Left the Cathedral and walked back to the Hotel there to read and write. After lunch walked down round Magdalen College and round the Mesopotamian
