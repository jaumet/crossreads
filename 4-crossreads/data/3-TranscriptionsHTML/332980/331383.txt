<p>where a large number of steamers could be seen passing both Ways, we kept the view of the Ocean for a long way &amp; eventually arrived in Aberdeen on time 2.25 Pm, had a nice Luncheon at the <strike>The</strike> Red Cross Free Luncheon Rooms, then asked a Scotch Lad to pilot me up the Brae (Hill) to the Aston Hotel which I intended to make my quarters, It was only a few minutes walk &amp; I soon was at the Hotel &amp; up four flight's of stairs &amp; introduced myself &amp; registered my name &amp; book a Bed, after a Wash &amp; Brush Up I went out &amp; had a shave &amp; started on a Tour until Tea Time, The first Car I took was going out to the Bridge of Dee along Union Street which is the principal street in <strike>Glasgow</strike> Aberdeen, &amp; then along Holburn St, for about 1&frac12; Miles passing through some beautiful House's built of Granite, of a Grey colour, as Aberdeen is Known as the Granite City, it is a beautiful Sight, as the Houses of 1 to 4 story's are built back from the Footpaths</p>
