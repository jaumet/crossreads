<p>Came across a poem translated by Longfellow which is very appropriate for the present time.</p>
<p>"Not thy councils, Not thy Kaisers, <br/> Win for thee the Worlds regard<br/> But thy painter Albrecht Durer, and Hans Sacks thy cobbler bard."</p>
<p>Aug 8th. Tues.<br/>Had a walk to Meteren this morning. Had coffee in a little house. They seemed a very nice family. The eldest, girl, the prettiest girl I have seen for a long time, can speak splendid English. She says that she learned it from the English troops who had passed through here time after time. This girl was a hand lace maker. It was very interest to watch her at work making it. It was a very slow process, tedious, and not much pay attached to it.</p>
<p>Aug 9th. Wed<br/>We are not supposed to leave the grounds when off duty, but this did not prevent B &amp; I from slipping out along the railway and walking to Outersteen had some coffee, getting back to C.C.S. without being noticed</p>
