<p>20 February 1915<br/>Saturday</p>
<p>Revielle at 6.<br/>In the morning I had to [indecipherable] Cairo to trans cut [indecipherable] for the boss. I went to the [indecipherable] office &amp; saw the pontoons captured from the Turks &amp; the effect shell fire had on them they were riddled from stem to stern. After that I went by train to the Motor br [indecipherable] concerning the wireless which according to the [indecipherable] are costing [indecipherable] due to arrive within the next day or two.<br/>No mails have arrived this week &amp; we hear that [indecipherable] has [indecipherable] out at Mena. The three heads went on leave again today &amp; had a bonza time finishing up at the Kursaal &amp; the Obelisk for tea. No chance of us leaving for a considerable time. Bed 11<br/>21 February 1915 (page annotated 29)<br/>Sunday</p>
<p>Revielle at 6<br/>Hal &amp; I went to church this morning whilst the men cleaned their paddles for tomorrow when the staff are out for instruction. After lunch Harold &amp; I went on a visit to a Frenchman's home &amp; had lunch there after that we had a bath not at the [indecipherable] returned back to camp in time for tea [indecipherable] L has two friends here &amp; after tea the whole lot except [indecipherable] &amp; myself go to [indecipherable] Park. Heard today that there's no chance of us leaving for months yet as things are altogether against it. Small pox has broken out at Mena 8 deaths &amp; 16 down with it. Mena is to be quarantined &amp;  all troops to be revaccinated in 1915. Filled in afternoon printing photos taken by Toby &amp; writing letters to Nell &amp; home. Bed at 9.14 pm</p>
