<p>Pension Wales&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Recd 3.5.16.<br/>Cairo.<br/>Monday March 27th. 16.</p>
<p>My very dear Father and Mother,<br/>I know I finished up your last letter in an awful hurry, and I forget just how much I told you in the haste.  First though let me acknowledge the home letters that came on Friday (24th inst.).  There was the long general one from Father dated 13th.  Feb. and a personal one dated 21st., &amp; one from Mother the last part of which was 20th. Feb.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; I was absolutely amazed to find comments on Rid being in Hospital at Heliopolis &amp; though you both said that you had had word of it you did not happen to mention how, and I puzzled over it till Wal suggested that perhaps they had counted it a casualty &amp; cabled you &amp; would put it in the paper also.  It had never suggested itself to any of our minds that they would do such a thing, not for a moment, or if it had I should certainly have sent you a cable to relieve your minds about it.  It worried me badly to think how careless it must seem not to bother about letting you have a cable, while all the while I was congratulating myself that you were in ignorance of it and that the first you heard would be that though Rid was off colour &amp; quite sick really, still it was nothing to really worry about, &amp; I think he was quite on the mend too, when I wrote.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;However you will have heard all the little details about it long before this from my letters, but I am afraid you won't understand the seeming carelessness until you get this explanation.
