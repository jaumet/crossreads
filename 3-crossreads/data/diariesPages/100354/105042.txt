This knocked our parapet in pieces towards the heavens , smashed in duckboards, knocked corners off dugouts, made a  terrificterrificrow but killed no- body. That night we went out to reserve billets in Fleurbaix. This rather once flourishing little town is now a straggling battered collection of houses, where the German vents his fury if anything in the nature of a reverse takes place. The houses looped-holed barricades mined are prepared for street fighting in case of a German advance. On the outskirts is the church a once imposing edifice but now a mass of ruin. Shells bullets have smashed through the walls  roof. Images of splendid workmanship, bas-relief mural decorations are smashed battered or lie ruined the brick-strewn floor. The organ smashed by a shell to matchword lieshangs into few remaining pipes over the gallery railing while the magnificent stained glass windows are smashed hand broken blackened. The tower with its monstrous bell still stands in fairfairly good order. 
On the 27th we left at 10 P.M. for fatigue to the trenches. This consisted of taking up some two hundred of more to the firing line. It had been raining all day as usual .
