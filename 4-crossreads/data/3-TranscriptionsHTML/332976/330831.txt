<p>April 1917<br/>1st   We carried on with the same work to-day, but during the afternoon I received instructions to discontinue dismantling the huts &amp; stack one, ready for transport, at the railway siding about two hundred yards away.<br/>This morning to took a photo of Smith's grave &amp; at night one of the lads in the C.R.E. office told me he had heard that George Hart had been sent back to Australia with mental trouble.<br/>2nd   This morning I got "an infantry" party of 50 &amp; with 30 of them got the huts we had dismantled taken to the railway siding &amp; stacked there.  <strike>With</strike>   The other 20 I got on cleaning up &amp; making a few alterations at our old horse lines about half a mile away.</p>
