<p>March 19th. Sun.<br/>Closed down "C" ward this afternoon.</p>
<p>March 20th. Mon.<br/>On G.D. carrying stones to make a road down the steep incline of the banks of the canal, so as to make the transport of patients to the hospital boat easier.</p>
<p>March 21st. Tues.<br/>Kit inspection to-day.</p>
<p>March 22nd. Wed.<br/>An R.A.M.C. Unit has arrived here to take over our hospital from us.</p>
<p>March 23rd. Thurs.<br/>Loading stores on to the trucks at Serepeum Siding. Exceptionally hot to-day. Had a bathe in the canal.</p>
<p>A Dutch Steamer, a French Transport with Black troops aboard and a British oil boat which is of a peculiar shape passed down the canal, the time that I was in bathing.</p>
<p>Read "Rhona Keith" by Annie Swan.</p>
<p>March 24th. Fri.<br/>A sure sign that we are on the move again, Biscuits in place of Bread issued.</p>
<p>March 25th. Sat.<br/>Swimming Carnival held by No 1 A.C.C.S. and No 11 C.C.S.</p>
<p>A very good afternoons sport.</p>
<p>March 26th. Sun.<br/>On Guard over our stores at the Railway siding.</p>
<p>The flies are very troublesome at present.</p>
<p>March 28th. Tues.<br/>Ordered to have full packs on at two o'clock this afternoon. Twenty four hours iron rations issued.</p>
<p>Marched over to the siding at Serapeum earlier than others in order.</p>
