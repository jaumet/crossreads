<p>21st April<br/>12.45 Left Frankfort &amp; spent  a most cold &amp; uncomfortable night in the train.<br/>730 A.M. We were taken out &amp; walked to Hotel Kaiserhof Hotel &amp; there had breakfast. Coffee, cheese sub. Meat paste sub. &amp; bread. Entrained again &amp; at 930 arrived at Warburg, &amp; there we were sidetracked until 3 pm. Nobody seemed to know what to do or which way to send us for Fuchsberg. Officer in charge nearly demented. 310 We were, after much shunting attached with a carriage load of German wounded, to a freight train &amp; off we went into the heart of Germany along a typical country line. 6pm At Puderborn the wounded Germans got off for Hospital &amp; we were again sidetracked. After wildly telephoning all over the place the officer had us made a special prisoners train we went off in charge of an engine to ourselves.<br/>930 pm Arrived at Soest &amp; were taken to Station Restaurant for supper much to amusement of some &amp; indignation of others, men &amp; women who were enjoying their evening beer.
