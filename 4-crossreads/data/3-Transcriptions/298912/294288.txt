<p>generally at the back of the town, but the real native quarters are well out of town &amp;; are interesting in a way . Squalid buildings with rough &amp; broken furniture  &amp; youngsters quite naked crawl all over the floor &amp; into the streets where as soon as they can walk &amp; talk they begin to beg for money. All, or most of the natives carry their money in their mouths &amp; their capacity in this respect is wonderful.</p>
<p>The Australian pound note is exchanged for 19/- at the GPO but some natives will give 19/6, but one wants to make sure that he does not get any Ceylon money, some of the coins resembling English money but are worth very little. A golden sovereign brings over its worth  by exchange.</p>
