<p>4-6-16<br/>Sun 4th<br/>Today C Sqd is on inline piquet &amp; we went into a clump of palms close by the main watering troughs &amp; put the day in there.  I believe the New Zealanders find an inline piquet daily to guard the back of our position &amp; the 1st L.H.Bde find one for the front.  they are mainly precautionary measures against aeroplane raids &amp; we are there ready to catch any horses that break loose;</p>
<p>6,000 Infantry came out to Railhead a few days ago &amp; are busy at present making a strong post on one of the sand rises about 3 mile from where we are camped, so it looks as though we shall soon have to push on;  These strong posts are only trenches dug around the top of the hill &amp; barbed wire entanglements built around it &amp; are practically impregnable to infantry attacks as all strong posts are defended with machine guns;<br/>We got back to camp tonight at 7 oclock;  The 3rd L.H.
