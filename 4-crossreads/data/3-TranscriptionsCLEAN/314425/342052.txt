 Sunday June 16, 1918. 1412 A beautiful morning. It was rather cold on picquet last night & I kept walking about most of the time. The time passed away uneventful until 10 to 3 when I heard some noisy fellows coming up the road. On investigating I found that they were both merry but one was very drunk & 
 constantly falling down & singing out. To my surprise I ascertained that it was Mr Chapman bringing home Mr Boyland, who had been indulging more freely than wisely. These are our two Transport officers. There was the usual half  holiday this afternoon The weather changed this evening. It is now cold & windy & looks very much like rain. I went for a walk this afternoon & had a good view of the surrounding country from a height above our camp. I counted 17 of our observation baloons up.
