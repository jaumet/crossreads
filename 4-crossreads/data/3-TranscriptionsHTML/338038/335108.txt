<p>Later: Thursday 8th July. Have just received Divisional Order No70 in which, among others, appears my promotion to rank of 1st Lieut. Dated 6th July 1915</p>
<p>Gallipoli Penin<br/>Wednesday 7th July 1915</p>
<p>My dear Father &amp; Mother<br/>I was awfully pleased to have your letters of 11th May (both Mother's &amp; Father's) and also Pats letter of 11th &amp; PC of 12th May on Monday. I might tell you they came at a most opportune time for as it happened I was feeling pretty seedy and wanted something to buck me up. We had been in action from 4 o'clock in the morning till 8, and it was about the hottest 4 hours I have had since I landed. We had the misfortune to have a shell burst right in our gun pit, killing the Corporal &amp; wounding 3 of the detachment. Of course we have to become used to these sad occurrences but it makes one feel pretty sick just at the time.<br/>Since last writing I have had</p>
