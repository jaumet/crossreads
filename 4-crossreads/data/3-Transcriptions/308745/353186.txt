<p>&amp; so was still on the ship.  Brian was brought off from the shore to the "Berrima" about 11 am. &ndash; He had been carried down some distance, &amp; thence the time between the wounding &amp; coming aboard.  As soon as he came aboard we saw his case was hopeless.  He was conscious &amp; recognised us all.  We made him comfortable  with morphia.  I asked him if he had any messages or any business I could fix up, but he said "No", he had given messages to Henderson.  I remained with him till he died, at 1.45 pm.  Towards the end he lapsed into unconsciousness &amp; the end was quite peaceful.</p>
<p>We buried his body at Herbertshohe in the cemetery at dusk (about 6 pm) the same evening.  The body was dressed in full uniform &amp; buried with full military honours.
