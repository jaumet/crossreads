<p>Not a medical detail was left to do the work of attending to them,  They lay about in various attitudes in the semi dark dug outs, where even the curtains were drawn to exclude fresh air, &amp; a candle fitfully illumined them, some in a stupor, &amp; others sitting up wiping their steaming eyes.<br/>The Sergeant of the medical details was lying near the table, &amp; he told me, when I had reached the table with difficulty, that a solution of sodie bicarbonate was the best relief for the eyes. Accordingly I set about making the solution, as soon as I had lifted the curtain.<br/>As soon as the solution was made</p>
