<p>&quot;<u>THE RAIDING DAYS</u>.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Prize-Winning Essay in the A.I.F. War</p>
<p>Records Competition - Award, Silver Medal.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><u>ARMENTIERES</u>.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>DECEMBER 1916</u>,</p>
<p><u>Jan.Feb.March 1917</u>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Those early days, while the Division was still untried, when we were as soldiers very young, but none could deny our keenness, our eagerness to &quot;Get on with the War&quot; and to &quot;Kill the Bosche.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; And &quot;Raiding Days&quot; were as schooldays, the minor operation<s>,</s> a school for &quot;The Magnum Opus&quot;, for a Raid, carried out with thoroughness was indeed the miniature of a battle.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Raiding trained our officers in the marvellous amount of correct detail necessary in a Fighting Force, trained them too, in leadership, taught us to regard an artillery barrage as a good friend, how near to that friend we could move unharmed; taught us to adhere to organization and plans in the most trying circumstances; and most of all it revealed the Australians&#39; superiority over the Bosche.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; But &quot;The Raids&quot; served Military purposes other than as schools, for our clean steel was dipped in enemy blood, our activities fatigued and demoralized the Bosche and after every raid we knew his strength was minus many casualtied and his morale much lower.</p>
