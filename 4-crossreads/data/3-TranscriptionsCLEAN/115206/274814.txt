
September 1918.Sunday 1
Same position3.30 am Awoke to find Mr Mays flashing a torch in myeyes. Told that the line was down and that I had to toddle at the Suites. Went out in the rain to the O.P. Phil Thresher wounded in face at 7pm. Had a heave of very heavy stuff across, gas galore.All "D" subs gunners gassed. Richards and Williamsbadly gassed.Orders came through to the effect that we will have tocross the Somme tonight. I expect  twill be a very roughspin, however good luck.Peronne captured today by 14th Inf  Bdge. A very dirty outlook tonight. Left home all clear at 7.30pm.
