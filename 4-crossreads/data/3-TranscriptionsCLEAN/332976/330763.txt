  
 December 1916 8th   To-day we did the same as yesterday but were working this side of the Ginchy Road. & returned home about 8 P.M. At about five to twelve Fritz landed a high explosive just outside our dugout, not two yds. from where I was sleeping.  It burst through the side of the "dugout," which is partly underground, & built of iron cupolas, & wounded 12 of us.  There were four stretcher cases & we had to carry them about a mile to the dressing station.  The other 8 were "walking cases" & were all admitted into hospital.
