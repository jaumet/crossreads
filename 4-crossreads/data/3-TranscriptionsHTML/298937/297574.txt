<p>I <strike>seem</strike> have been fortunate in my fellow patients for they are not only decent companions but interesting too.<br/>Some hail from Mesopotamia where they have had a bad time, while others are from the Suez trenches.<br/>One of these is a sturdy &amp; fat fellow in with dysentery whose main trouble seems to be a dislike for his medicine, &amp; who presents a healthy appearance quite out of temper with his position.<br/>Another, equally healthy &amp; like the abovementioned, one of the old Suvla boys, is troubled with cynovitis of the knees.  <strike>This</strike> His cure is to rest in bed</p>
