<p>October 1st :- The 20th Battalion left Villiers-Faucon this morning, and resumed the onward march. Rain yesterday had made the roads today very muddy and slushy. The sun shone brightly, and a cold wind swept across the country. We plodded through the blood-stained highway of Tincourt, thence past <strike>on the</strike> Templeux, <strike>and</strike> reaching Hargicourt at noon, where we constructed billets; as it was stated we would rest here for the night. We are in the midst of great artillery activity. Near this place is the Bellicourt Tunnel*, where, some of the troops tell me, was concealed one of the German Corpse factories for boiling down human bodies to get the fat from them. Some men who had been working about here for the last two days told me they had gone into the tunnel where they had seen mutilated German bodies, a big boiler, and a profusion of grease.
