<p>in England. We are doing pretty well here and making old Fritz sit up and take notice of us, we give him a good shaking up and naturally he objects to it.</p>
<p>I am keeping wonderfully well and have not had even a cold since we have been over this side. I could do with a bit of leave though it would be quite a boon, there does not seem to be much prospect of any at present but I suppose it will come along in due course.</p>
<p>I met Harry Donovan to day, he is on the medical staff here and having a nice quiet time. I also met Clive Miller, Denison Miller's son and we had quite a long chat over Waverley and old times, nearly all the fellows are over this side, it is wonderful who one does meet here</p>
<p>We are getting pretty good news every day here and</p>
<p>[Captain Harry Carysfort Edmond Donovan, 34, medical practitioner of Randwick, NSW, embarked from Sydney on 11 May 1916 on HMAT A8 Argyllshire with the 9th Field Ambulance.</p>
<p>Driver Clive lancelot Miller, 26, stockbroker of Coogee, NSW, embarked from Sydney on 18 October 1914 on HMAT A8 Argyllshire with 3rd Battery, 1 Field Artillery Brigade.]</p>
