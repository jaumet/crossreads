<p>2.  But. D. &amp; I will go again &amp; probably I'll remember next time.  One striking thing about all the old Churches &amp; Cathedrals is the grave yard inside the Church.  Under every slab in the Aisles there is a Tomb of some one &amp; the epitaph is cut in the slab.  After we had been round the old Cloisters we went to one of the Canon's Homes for Tea.  He was a lovely man &amp; made us very welcome.  He is a widower &amp; childless.  After Tea we went a short walk with the Canon, who was taking a service some where in the neighbourhood, &amp; then returned to Service in the Cathedral again.  We went right up into the front seat.  The Service was nice but the Parson was very long winded &amp; I got a bit tired.  We hurried back to the Canon's place for Supper as we had only a short time in which to catch our Train.  We left rather abruptly &amp; had to run like the wind &amp; just caught the Train.  Thus endeth my first visit to Salisbury.  On the 20th we had snow &amp; on the 22nd we had 4 very heavy snow storms, but the sun came out after each one &amp; the snow all melted very quickly.  Reg Keirle was over on Escort Duty again.  I received a box of flowers from Auntie Annie &amp; they were lovely.  They are still on my Table but are pretty droopy now.<br/>On Saty. the 24th.
