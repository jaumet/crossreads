<p>Soldier's Club &amp; set forth. Not wishing to live right in the town for one thing, as it is across the river &amp; our office is this side (The office was formerly a barn for hay) &amp; also wishing to find lodgings this side. We strike out in the suburb St Sever, &amp; near the Jardin des Plantes (Garden of Plants) &amp; have at least a great stroke of luck. The first street to which we have come, Rue Valmont de Bomare, <strike>ask of</strike> meet an old lady &amp; asking her if she knows of any places in the neighbourhood where rooms are obtainable, prepare to move on, and a another lady, devining our intentions shows us her house &amp; we find a beautifully clean double room, two beds &amp; every comfort. Only her daughters with here. They seem most contented &amp; the small place is really a palace. Just pure luck    &amp; I thank my stars for speaking French  Return to Hotel de Normandie    &amp; settle up</p>
