 About 5 pm we set out in single file for the A.D.S. to the tune of the roar of big guns. We traversed the deserted streets and continued along a dusty road alive with military traffic. About two kilometres from our starting point we arrived at our destination &ndash; the A.D.S. which consisted of several more or less sandbagged dugouts &amp; shelters on the side of the road. The sound of the guns was deafening &ndash; a big naval gun not more than 50 yards from the side of the road being the chief offender. 
 The Dressing Station was surrounded by batteries &ndash; gone are the old Red Cross protection days. B Sect went on night duty and I have been busy recording particulars of smashed &amp; battered victims of Boche artillery fire, carried in on stretchers &ndash; A welcome respite at 1.30 am is enabling me to scribble a few lines for my diary.
