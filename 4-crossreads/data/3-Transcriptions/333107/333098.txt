<p><u>Copy of Board Paper from S.V.</u></p>
<p>May 1918<br/>30.5 D.A.H.<br/>Dil Ht  H.B. disp d and o  Ep pulse<br/>No murmur</p>
<p>2nd sound changed and accentuated<br/>At 2 left side of base of curifore<br/>Cast. Rec'd for Weymouth<br/>Brasher.  Capt.</p>
<p>Evacuated by Capt Sampson 9th F. Amb<br/>from Halle &ndash; France.  10 Sept 1918.<br/>marked "Persistent Tachycardia Hypertrophy"<br/>arrived at No 3 Stat Hosp Touen &ndash; examined<br/>by M.O. and marked :-  12 Sept/18<br/>"Tachycardia, some Hypertrophy."<br/>and sent on to No 2 Con Camp to see<br/>Heart Specialist. He said "my heart <br/>couldn't be bad, or I wouldn't look so well."</p>
<p>Funny ideas some people get.</p>
<p>At the Base the Blacksmith marked me <br/>"A class" for Hospital duty"</p>
<p>Abdominal crash.</p>
<p>19 Ward &ndash; Sutton Veny Hosp.</p>
<p>4031. Brett J.T.  32 Batt. B coy.</p>
<p>A winter night<br/>May chilly be<br/>Beneath December's moon<br/>But that's no more<br/>To toy or me<br/>Than of the month of June.<br/>Old memories set<br/>Our Hearts aglow<br/>Old yarns again are told<br/>And loving feelings overflow<br/>As you my arms enfold.</p>
