<p>24 August 1916 Thur<br/>It is midnight whilst I writing these few lines. I am sitting on a box of dressings &amp; McGee is stretched out on some newspapers having sat my feet having long since gone to sleep. I received letters from home &amp; Pongo Fay to-day. Ridge one of our bearers has received the military medal for distinguished service [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>25 August 1916 Fri<br/>Letters from Jay &amp; from home. Sorry to hear that Dad is so unwell. I did some washing during the morning after coming home from Walroy where I spent an enjoyable morning. There are now in hospital resting some five hundred patients. Slept after dinner till tea time. Visited the American Bar several times throughout the night.</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>Very sick to night a bad billious attack.</p>
