 Imbros 
 Imbros is an island situated directly opposite Anzac at a distance of about fourteen miles, and further south lay the island of  [indecipherable] which was of no military value but of interest as being a visiting place of St. Paul. 
 Imbros, however, was far more important as a military base containing a most suitable harbour as well as supplies of fruit and groceries.  It was the headquarters of Sir Ian Hamilton &amp; also of the aeroplanes, &amp; was the sheltering place for the warships and transports.  A bakery was erected there from which all supplies of bread were obtained.
