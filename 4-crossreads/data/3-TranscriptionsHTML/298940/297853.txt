<p>the brother of the young Kollische with whom I worked on the Peninsular. This one, although much the larger and apparently the elder, is really the younger. The other one is small &amp; very youthful looking, but this one is large &amp; manly.<br/>Yet when one comes into contact with him daily the sheer boyishness of his years (20) is apparent. Any tomfoolery or laughter provoking nonsense in word or deed he enters with zest.<br/>He is tall, well-built yet peaceful, with legs the shape of which admirably suit the fairly well cut riding</p>
