<p>May 4<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;Fritz came over bombing during the night - We got up at 6.30 A.M. &amp; loaded up with Fodder rations &amp; cooks furniture etc &amp; took it to&nbsp;<s>Ravelcourt</s>&nbsp;<sup>Ravelincourt</sup>&nbsp;. the Q.M.S came on my lorry &amp; brought to light some champagne &amp; 1908 Sheblis which we all enjoyed, as was evident by the way the telephone poles went by - we got home at 6.P.M.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 5<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left the park at 5.A.M. for Querrieu where we picked up men of a labour battalion &amp; took them to Bertangles - It rained most of the day - when we got back to the park &nbsp; We had to shift our amunition &eacute;chelon back to Allonville &amp; Park with the 3<u><sup>rd</sup></u>&nbsp;M.T. Coy. again &nbsp; x see&nbsp;<u>memo</u>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 6<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left the park at 9.A.M. &amp; went to Ferreres &amp; picked up a load of ammo &amp; took it to Querrieu - lovely day -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 7<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left park at 6.A.M. to work for 1<u><sup>st</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;Aust. corps troops (old Anzac.C.T.S.C.) took two loads from Vignacourt railhead one to Poulainville &amp; another to Rain<sub>^</sub><sup>n</sup>eville &amp; got back to park at 6.P.M.</p>
