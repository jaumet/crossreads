 Feb.2  nd  &nbsp;&nbsp;We went out on a coal stunt at 1.P.M. for 1  st  &nbsp;Anzac C.T.S.C. &amp; did one load. when we returned we parked up with the amunition section in a very deep muddy position &amp; are now attached to 1  st  &nbsp;A.S.P. &nbsp;- We have to be on parade at 7.30 A.M. 
 &nbsp; 
 Memo. The weather has been colder since I returned from Paris. but not too bad &amp; we still have quite a bit of sun. There is a bit of strafing going on at intervals again.&nbsp;&mdash; 
 &nbsp; 
 Feb. 3  rd  &nbsp;&nbsp;Fritz was over here three times last night &amp; dropped some bombs, but none within two miles of us - To-day has been fine &amp; moderately warm - I worked on the lorry in the morning. In the afternoon I went along to 5  th  &nbsp;A.S.P. &amp; saw some of the fellows I know. 
 &nbsp; 
 Feb 4  th  &nbsp;&nbsp;We went out at 10 A.M. to draw for 1  st  &nbsp;Anzac C.T.S.C. &nbsp;I had to have a tow to start my engine - It was a beautiful day just like spring. Joe was told to be ready for his Blighty leave
