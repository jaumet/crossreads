<p>October 14th: -<br/>Another beautiful day, cool and mild.</p>
<p>Being still on leave we took train at 9 am to Lyndhurst Road, which is in the centre of the New Forest district. From the station we went by motor along a perfectly made road for 6 miles until the village of New Forest was reached. Here we left the car and spent the rest of the day in rambling around this glorious forest.</p>
<p>In history it is famous as the retreat of Robin Hood &amp; his gang, the church containing a fine painting of the afore-mentioned and his comrade Friar Tuck. The trees are in this district, of every conceivable green and the farm houses very picturesque; we had lunch at one and enjoyed it immensely. After dinner we saw a pack of hounds used for the hunt, numbering 120; they were the property of a Lord whose home stood in the centre of the forest, and the sight was a very pretty one as they stood or lay on the green in front of the inn which is in the village.</p>
<p>At 5 pm we had a stroke of luck, for a motorist passing invited us to join him and he kindly drove us back to Southampton, the trip being a fine one.</p>
<p>A concert was held on board at 8 pm and the items, all good.</p>
<p>October 15th: -<br/>Went, early after breakfast to the baths and from there to the Common where we had a pleasant stroll.</p>
