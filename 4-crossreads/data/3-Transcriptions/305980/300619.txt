<p>April</p>
<p>1:  Arrived Fremantle 10 p.m. anchoring outside.</p>
<p>2:  Men from this state went straight into quarantine: no person allowed ashore.  Steamed out at 4 p.m.</p>
<p>4:  Fine weather in Bight.  Harrison A.F.C. buried at sea 10 a.m., died of pneumonia.  Beautifully conducted burial; firing party &amp; the whole of the ship present.</p>
<p>5:  Admitted to ships hospital, malaria.  Reached Port Adelaide 7 p.m., heavy rain.</p>
<p>6:  S.A. troops disembarked 10 a.m., coaling till midnight, when we steamed out for Tasmania with only 20 on board for that state &amp; men discontented at being taken home in such a roundabout manner.</p>
<p>9:  Reached outer harbour of Tas. 7 p.m., anchoring at quarantine station.  Health officials stated it to be a clean ship.  Very pretty coastal scenery.</p>
<p>10:  Tas. men went off at 9 a.m., steaming out at 11 a.m.   Message received at 10 p.m. stating men for Tas. to be taken off at Melbourne, after they had been taken ashore at their own state.</p>
