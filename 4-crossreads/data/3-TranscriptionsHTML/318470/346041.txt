<p>Sunday 19 November 1916<br/>Very wet, cold &amp; miserable.  Was out very little.  Had a look round in morning &amp; went to Stratford at night.</p>
<p>Monday 20 November 1916<br/>Left Leytonstone about noon &amp; went to City.  At night Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith and I went to "Palladium".  Good show.</p>
<p>Tuesday 21 November 1916<br/>Had look City today.  Did not get anything of importance.  Did some business.</p>
<p>Wednesday 22 November 1916<br/>Went to City in afternoon &amp; had a look over Tower London.  With [Mr.?] Smith to show Stratford night.</p>
<p>Thursday 23 November 1916<br/>Left Leytonstone in afternoon &amp; missed troop train at Waterloo.  Went to Royal at night &amp; saw "Best of Luck".</p>
<p>Friday 24 November 1916<br/>Left Leytonstone &amp; caught troop train in afternoon.  Left Southhampton about 9 p.m. for France.</p>
<p>Saturday 25 November 1916<br/>Disembarked Havre about 7 a.m. &amp; went to Rest Camp.  Left for Rouen about 11 p.m.</p>
<p>Sunday 26 November 1916<br/>Arrived Rouen about 7 a.m. &amp; went to Rest Camp.  Left for "Front" about 4 p.m.  Travelling all night.  Much delay.  Frosty.</p>
<p>Monday 27 November 1916<br/>Left Rouen Camp about 1.30 p.m. and arrived in Albert about 7 p.m.  Very slow journey.  Much delayed.</p>
<p>Tuesday 28 November 1916<br/>Stayed in Albert all day with Capt. Chappel &amp; Capt. McNab.  They left for Front about 10 p.m.  I stayed Albert.</p>
<p>Wednesday 29 November 1916<br/>Left Albert about 9.30 a.m. for Front with Lt. Morgan 15th Trench Mortars.  Reached Coy. H.Q. about 2 p.m.</p>
<p>Thursday 30 November 1916<br/>Taking things easy as I still have bad cold &amp; I do not want to get worse one.  Waterloo Farm.</p>
<p>Friday 1 December 1916<br/>Went to Trenches today &amp; had a look around.  Sec. working in various places.</p>
<p>Saturday 2 December 1916<br/>Working Needle Trench also Deep Dugouts &amp; laying Duck walk track.</p>
