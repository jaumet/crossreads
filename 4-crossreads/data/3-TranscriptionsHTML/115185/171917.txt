<p>3 Australian General Hospital<br/>On M.M.H.S. Oxfordshire &ndash; Jan 21.1916</p>
<p>My dearest Mother &amp; Father &ndash; Willie Ernest Constance &amp; Edith &ndash;<br/>I think when I last wrote I had already given up my ward to the quartermaster's care &ndash; all the patients having gone away to the base &ndash; and I was packing &amp; sorting and resting in my hut. There is not anything very interesting about the last two weeks at Lemnos.<br/>Our marquees were gradually dismantled &ndash; and the equipment packed and stacked down by the South Pier ready for embarkation. It was all done in a very orderly and quiet manner and we are now steaming away to Alexandria I suppose. There to receive orders from headquarters.<br/>I was very comfortable the last two weeks on shore &ndash; as Major Man got me a stove, which, besides heating the hut very well &ndash; boiled a kettle and made toast. I stayed in bed till about eleven each day and washed clothes and packed in the afternoons. I went to lunch on the Minatonka with Major Man and was asked to take three other sisters.
