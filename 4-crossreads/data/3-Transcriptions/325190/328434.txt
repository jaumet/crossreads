<p>That night he howled his way through the empty huts, looking for the familiar faces.<br/>When the next draft containing most of the remainder of the 16th, left, the same thing happened, &amp; again when my draft left he came with us to the Station.  Somebody took him on the train &amp; so, to our surprise &amp; greater to his own, he was to be seen trotting gaily by in a fish out of water style through the streets of Southampton with us.  It was his first visit to a big town &amp; slightly dazzled him.<br/>When we left the camp to go into town he followed</p>
