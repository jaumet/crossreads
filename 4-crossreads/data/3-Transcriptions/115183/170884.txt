<p>everyone went to bed tired but happy and some perhaps a little "in excelsior".<br/>In the earlier part of the evening  I had been down to the YMCA service but came back to see the Ball instead of staying to the YM free treat.<br/>26 &ndash; 31 Dec. 1917. The closing days of the year were without import. The weather was cold and windy and everyone stayed indoors as much as possible. Unfortunately no Xmas parcels or comforts arrived, but there the AIF was rescued by its well developed faculty of "Improvise"</p>
<p>I am afraid this diary is not what it was intended to be but is a roughly and quickly written recollection of events happening some tme before .<br/>But going on my first leave to "Blighty" tomorrow I am sure that this, such as it, will be of interest to my dear ones waiting patiently at home for my return, which we all hope may not be far distant but still in all honour.</p>
