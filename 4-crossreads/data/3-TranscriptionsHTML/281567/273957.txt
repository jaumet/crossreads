<p>in getting up was a stoppage in Hisebecourt where the R.G.A. were getting in some 8 inch howitzers. We got out on the tape line to time &amp;  at the zero hour (5.30) the machine barrage opened quickly followed by artillery. I shall never forget the sight. Just forward of the tape line the first shells dropped. Sending fountains of burning phosphorous yards into the air followed with bellows of white smoke  &amp;  lighting up the country with a weird light. Into this advanced the infantry, drenched by this,  <strike>with</strike>  not like  war weary men as they undoubtedly were but like men going forward on parade.
