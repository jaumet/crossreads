<p>When the sentry came to hunt us out to work, we refused to go, on account of the armistice.  He said very well, we would get no food from them, and we would be locked in the barrack until we did work.  He said we could have our parcels if they came.  He then went away to report to an officer.</p>
<p>The next day, an Officer and a Sergeant came to our barrack.  The Officer drew his overcoat aside and showed us a revolver, and asked us were we going to work.  We answered absolutely</p>
