<p>course they get plenty firing to keep them amused:  poor beggars they will get it in the neck in a day or two:  one does exactly as one's told, but feels a sort of sneaking relief when the other fellow gets told to come and relieve you.</p>
<p>I got the photograph of Sing holding Tom III, also one of Graham who grows every time I see him.</p>
<p>I must make an effort in future to write you more amusing or more interesting letters:  this one is the first for a fortnight, and a pretty poor effort at that.</p>
<p>Goodbye &amp; cheer ho! best love to old John &amp; Graham &amp; to you.  Keep well &amp; fit, and don't let the Australian workers union ruin us all:  of course the taxes will do that, though:  we shall all be paupers &amp; have you thought out what you will do for a living:  you must practice sewing, I think &amp; cooking.</p>
<p>Goodbye again<br/>Jock C. Ellis</p>
