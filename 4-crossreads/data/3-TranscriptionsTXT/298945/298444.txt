 Sunday 15 Aug. 1918 [Transcriber's note: actually 15 September 1918] 
 We are now established as A.D.S. at the Sugar Refinery, Tincourt, just behind Hamel. The remainder of B Tent Sub. &ndash; Burnett, Tom Smith, Kak Smith &amp; I, walked back just before dark, &amp; found a marquee in use as A.D.S. on the border of the village next to the Sugar Refinery. Most of the bearers had made bivouacs on the opposite side of the hill, &amp; some had established themselves in cellars. Of the latter Snowy Eva came up &amp; offered me a pozzy in a cellar occupied by him &amp; a few others. We finished cleaning up &amp; lit a fire to smoke  to clear  the cellar of foul air, before the Gothas came over.
