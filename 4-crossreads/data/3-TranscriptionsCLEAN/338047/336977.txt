The quadrangle was littered up with guns & aeroplanes, & the rooms adjoining contained a number of Napoleons relics, & some very fine battle paintings.  One that particularly struck me was that of a gunner & gun which seemed to be always pointing at wherever one happened to be in the room. Napoleon's tomb was at the back of this place.  It is in a big chapel with a dome.  The altar, owing to an ingenious arrangement of yellow glass, appears to be always in sunlight.  The tomb is in the centre of the chapel & is sunken with a stone rail all round it.  The decorations are emblematical.  There are a couple of side chapels, containing the tombs of his chief friends.
