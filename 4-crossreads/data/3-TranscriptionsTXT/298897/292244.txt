 on a vacant plot till daylight.  Myself &amp; a friend put up a covering by laying a waterproof sheet over some guy ropes near as protection from the dew &amp; then slept till daylight.  After breakfast we moved off to our permanent spot &amp; our gear having arrived immediately pitched 9 marquees &amp; several other tents, a real good days work.  Altogether our trip from Lemnos was splendidly managed &amp; worked without a hitch throughout which is unfortunately an exception.  I met friends of the 1st Stationary but did not have much time to have a yarn. 
 The Camp is a splendid one, water laid on etc &amp; everything points to better management than formerly.  The horses of A.S.C. &amp; artillery are in splendid nick &amp; the men all look well &amp; bright.
