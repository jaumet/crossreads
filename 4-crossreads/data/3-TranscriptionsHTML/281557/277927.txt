<p>Friday July 21st  <br/>Warloy- Baillon: fair day: Reveille 6.30 am: went to Holy Communion in St Pauls C of E. It was just a large room but over a hundred of us got in including a good few officers: The altar had a couple of shell cases as vases &amp; were filled with nice flowers: it was a nice service: parade at A.M.C. for instruction at 10 am: ordered to pack up &amp; fall in 2 P.M. moved off &amp; when well out were told it was only a test to see if we could get off the mark: the boys did swear some: came home a different way &amp; got home about 7 pm: we seen 13 planes &amp; 10 observation up of ours at one time: Jim &amp; Doy &amp; knight came down for a talk: had a good feed of tinned fruit. got a letter from Mum &amp; Aunt Myra</p>
<p>Saturday July 22nd  <br/>Warloy &ndash; Baillon: fair day: Reveille 6.30 am: physical jerks 7- 7.30 am: packed up in morning: fell in to move off at 2 pm but did not move off till 6 pm: marched to Albert &amp; finally bivouacked on a grassy patch: plenty of 'planes balloons &amp; gunfire: there are a fair few monoplanes  down this way: cold night: terrific bombardment toward midnight &amp; plenty of coloured flares going up: read a Mercury &amp; seen about "Granny's" death</p>
