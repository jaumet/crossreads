<p>Feb. 12</p>
<p>On arrival we completed with coal.</p>
<p>Feb. 25</p>
<p>The next trip to sea was for the purpose of carrying out exercises, in conjunction with the Grand Fleet.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Feb. 28</p>
<p>These passed off without incident, &amp; we returned after a few days to our base, again completing with coal &amp; oil fuel.&nbsp; About this time a raider (German) attmpted to break the blocade, &amp; get out into the Atlantic.&nbsp; One raider, named the &quot;Mo&euml;we&quot; had escaped from the patrols previously, &amp; she had succeeded in sinking several ships in the Atlantic.&nbsp; However, this raider called &quot;Grief&quot; was caught in the act, &amp; engaged.&nbsp; We lost the &quot;Alcontara&quot;, an armed merchantman, but she succeeded in sinking the raider first.&nbsp; This episode was kept strictly quiet, so that the German Admiralty would be kept quite ignorant of the actual state of affairs.</p>
<p>March. 6</p>
<p>One day the Fleet was hurriedly ordered to sea, in order to intercept a strong German fleet which had been reported steaming North from the Dutch island of Terschelling.&nbsp; As soon as we weere out of harbour this information was piped along the mess deck, &amp; was greeted with tremendous enthusiasm by the men.</p>
