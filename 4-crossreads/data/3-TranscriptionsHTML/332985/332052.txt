<p>[See image for details of the sketch.]<br/>21-12-1916<br/>Shortest day.  Wet cold, and windy.  With the exception of early this morning, no shells over.  30 cases through dressing station, result of stunt yesterday.  This does not include killed.  Most of the shells were 9 inch armour piercing, high velocity, 3 ft. 6 in. high.  Two casualties at Bty. yesterday.  Corp. Redshaw wounded in chin and arm.  He was just sent up from wagon line, and was in gun pit, taking over gun and stores, when a shell carried away one wheel and dial sight.  Gr. Allen was wounded in head.  Fierce Artillery activity in our quarter all night, and all day.</p>
