<p>September 1915<br/>away wounded a month ago, came back today looking very fit.  He reported having a good time at Malta &amp; Alexandria.  There was a lively bombardment on the left for about &frac12; an hour in the afternoon.  There was heavy rifle fire on our left about midnight.</p>
<p>Saturday 11th<br/>Things are very quiet today.  There is a mail on the beach, but they won't let anyone down to get it.  It is over a fortnight since we had any.  The mail came in the afternoon.  Received only one letter from Frank.  Things were very quiet all day.  There was a little rain early in the morning.  The night are getting quiet cool.</p>
<p>Sunday 12th<br/>There was a heavy bombardment in the south consisting mostly of naval guns.  The firing was continuous for several hours.  Major Genl Legge &amp; Staff inspected the trenches</p>
