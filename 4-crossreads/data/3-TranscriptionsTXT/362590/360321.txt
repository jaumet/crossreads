
5.They have taken me away from my Regiment again, & made me Camp Commandant. It is interesting work, but there is too much of it - & I would far rather be with my men. The powers that be have promised I shall go back to the 4th if there is going to be any scrapping.
I got an awfully nice cable from General Birdwood last week, which was the first intimation I had of getting the C.M.G. [Companion of the Order of St Michael & St George]. When I saw the Honours List, I was furious at so many gallant men being passed over. I went to the General & refused the Decoration, but he would not allow it - insult to the King & all that sort of business.
6.Again, both Canada & Australia has something over 90,000 & under 100,000 fighting. Our casualties have been more than twice as heavy as the Canadians, & we have had much fiercer & more savage fighting. Yet look at our honours compared to theirs. I was & am very sick about the whole question.
By last night's mail, I got a copy of The Times, which paper alone contains the full text of Hamilton's Despatch, wherein he has been good enough to mention me by name.
