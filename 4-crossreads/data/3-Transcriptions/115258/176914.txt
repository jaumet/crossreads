<p>23rd July 1918</p>
<p>My dear Mother</p>
<p>Is not this awful paper and it is made expressly for the A.I.F. but you always want to be w&#39;are of things when they are made expressly for soldiers. This is like trying to write on blotting paper - I&#39;ll have to go back to pencil I&#39;m afraid. No! I&#39;ll keep going for a bit! I posted youa letter yesterday afternoon - and to continue it. After coming back from Alex I stayed at Rossmore House in Cairo. This is a sort of boarding house run by English ladies <s>I</s> and I reckon they are doing just as good work as Mrs Chisholm. It is not a cheap YMCA joint of the Salvation Army style. <s>T</s> The tarif is 40 Pts per day and everything is of the very best, including the meals. There is no bar and it is just like going into a private family at home as there are a lot of officers wives staying there and they are all so nice. Miss Granwell is the manageress and she asked me to afternoon tea with her the last afternoon I was there and she reminded me very much of Aunt Katie in some ways.
