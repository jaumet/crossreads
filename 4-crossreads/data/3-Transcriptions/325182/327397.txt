<p>more beautiful to a wounded or sick man coming in from the battlefield than to <strike>hear</strike> have delicate hands tenderly dress the injured part and to hear a sympathetic voice speak to him.  A chap feels that he has earned some such treatment, but what if she were not there.  So we must be thankful.</p>
<p>19th<br/>Reached Rouen and were taken by car to No. 8 General Hospital where I was looked after by an American nurse.  As I was quite blind by this time I needed some little attention.  The gas in our eyes was just like grit and inflamed them</p>
