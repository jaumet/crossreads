We take over our part to-morrow.  I believe we were to march out of Boulogne on 11th when the Armistice was signed, but at 3 p.m. there was so much noise going on in the Town, for we were camped on a high hill behind the town, that the Boys would not wait to fall in at 4 p.m. &amp; they all rushed the gates &amp; down into the Town, so we could not leave until the next day.
