<p>18 Thursday<br/>Beachy heavily bombards the Beach Taube flies over guns chase it off finds out our Bivouac</p>
<p>Friday 19<br/>Wind goes down water very scarce very cold Beachy gets 90 men</p>
<p>Saturday 20  Turks fire heavy shells on to our Bivouac one lands left of my dugout, several fall in Gully, we are not disturbed</p>
<p>Sunday 21<br/>Church Parade, Gilbert goes to Hospital, officer &amp; men killed first day they relieve us officer puts his head over parapet finish officer</p>
<p>Monday Nov 22<br/>Leave Anzac on water tank, then on to Princess Ena very cold cast anchor 6 AM arrive Lemnos after very cold and rough passage 12 men stay on board all day and night</p>
<p>Tuesday Nov 23<br/>Leave Princess Ena at 8 AM. Board Ferryboat Waterwith, very cold and hungry no food for two days land at 9 AM. March four miles to camp 2nd Brigade Band plays us in sleep in large tents 45 men</p>
<p>Wednesday 24. 25<br/>Rested in camp received bottle Porter</p>
<p>Friday 26<br/>Visited villages chummed up with women folks, learned how to make cotton, and</p>
