<p>Le Bizet<br/>again attended by Lieut. T. Graham and 1 N.C.O.<br/>Line visited at night by C.O. and Lieut. L.H. Mathieson.<br/>Between 8.30 p.m. to 2.30 a.m. 3,000 rounds were fired on the following targets,<br/>(1)  Duriez Fm. C.5.d.28.93.<br/>(2)  Laundry, C.5.C.70.53.<br/>(3)  Pineapple Emplacement, C.17.a.55.65.<br/>(4)  Soap House, C.5.C.70.28.<br/>Enemy Artillery showed slight activity.  Machine guns quit throughout day and night.<br/>A little activity shown on the part of enemy aircraft.<br/>During the bombardment on our left from 3 a.m. to 3.30 a.m. the enemy sent up a large number of light signals chiefly double green stars also double red and double gold.  These signals appeared to be repeated by stations to the rear.  Weather dull and cloudy &ndash; good visability.</p>
