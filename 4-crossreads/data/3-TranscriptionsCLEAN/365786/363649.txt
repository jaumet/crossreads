Those able to move about visited their friends in other wards or sat on each other's beds & played games & yarned.  One boy was wounded  thro both  in the legs - the bullet passing thro both & I said to him, "You ought to be glad that you have not to be operated on for the removal of that bullet", & he said "might I?  What have I got to show now?  I can't show my legs! but I could show a bullet", but one of the other boys said, "He's got plenty of spent bullets in his pockets & they'll all be "out of his legs" when he gets back" & there was a laugh all round the ward.  They tell one many a tale but never mention themselves.  How the A.M.C. & A.S.C. work at the Front.   How a turk was taken prisoner & fought on our side.  About 50 turkish prisoners
