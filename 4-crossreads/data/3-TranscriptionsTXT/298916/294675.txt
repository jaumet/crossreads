The N.Z. soldiers are well fed &amp; the boats fully manned. The Australians &ndash; judging by the A13 &amp; a few others there is room for great improvement. Of course one cannot expect as many comforts on board a transport as on a passenger boat, but there is no excuse for the following: About this time the soldiers began to complain of the tainted food so an enquiry was made with no results &ndash; only natural. However the Steward admitted to Teddy &amp; I that when he heard that an inspection was to be made he had the meat  washed in Condy's Fluid  "to kill 'em". The meat Teddy &amp; I saw being put on H.M.A.S. Geelong in Hobart had an odor that would run rings around bi-sulphide of carbon. It was actually slimy. The substitute &ndash; bully beef &ndash; well a man would need a carding machine to masticate it with, &amp; enough salt in it to make the men always thirsty.
