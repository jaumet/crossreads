After a fairly good sleep I was called at 4.45 am by the Guard &amp; after having some Fried Bacon &amp; Bread &amp; Butter &amp; Tea we set off at 5.20 for the Railway Station in Poperinghe. Saturday 30th 1917. It was inclined to be wet now &amp; again a drizzly rain would fall, as it was 2 miles to the station &amp; having plenty of Time, both of us jogged along quietly. Traffic on the Road is very quiet this time of the Morning, eventually we arrived at the station at 6 am &amp; after producing our leave Pass in the Railway Transport Office, we were both issued a Moving order from Poperinghe to Steenwerck &amp; return. This order is like a Consignment Note, &amp; it is issued instead of a Railway Ticket. We made our way on to a Platform which was very long &amp; low only about 12 inches high.
