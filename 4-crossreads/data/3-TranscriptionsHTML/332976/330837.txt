<p>April 1917<br/>10th continued.   of Fritz's, on our left towards Arras &amp; on the other side of it.   The cavalry are playing an important part on that part of the front as well as this.<br/>11th   The weather is just the same &amp; we had a heavy fall of snow about tea time.   At noon we received instructions to pack up our gear ready to move any time, &amp; at 4 P.M. we were inspected with "full marching order."   The 4th Divn. have been doing well yesterday &amp; to-day.  They have broken through the "Hindenburg Line" &amp; are fighting two miles on the other side of it.  It is believed that their casualties have been large.<br/>On our left, (towards Arras) it is officially announced that over 160 guns &amp; over 16000 prisoners (including this front) have been captured in the last 3 days.</p>
