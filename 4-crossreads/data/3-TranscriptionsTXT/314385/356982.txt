 1916 
 is Leiut Col Mosley a very smart and practical man who introduced himself to us by giving us a lecture &amp; drilled us in the new gas helmet drill. He has made a good impression with us. 
 The weather is getting cold &amp; wintery. 
 Sunday Sep 17 Usual duties. 
 The heavy guns were very active along the line last night. 
 A Football match to day. In the evening there was a church parade, the first we've had in France so we marched to Poperinghe to a hall "Talbot House" Cap Clayton was the Chaplin of the high church order. 
 It again rains at 10 p.m. 
 Monday Sep 1 Raining heavily thro the night &amp; morning the hut leaking badly. 
 Guns firing incessently. 
 Tuesday Sep 19 Weather still very wet. 
 Same duties in wards the ground sloppy &amp; muddy. 
 Wednesday Sep 20th Nothing unusual happening, weather clearing, heavy firing.
