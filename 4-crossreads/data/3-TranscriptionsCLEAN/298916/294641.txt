It was like a huge iron foundry, & a wonderful sight - 38 ships laden with good men setting out on a serious mission. Sharp on seven a.m. H.M.A.S. Melbourne steamed out towards the entrance. She was stripped for action & flew a large union jack at the foremast & the Australian naval ensign from the gaff at the mainmast. As she passed each transport saluted & was saluted in return, but again the N.Z. transports showed their utter lack of courtesy. A little astern, followed the Flagship also flying an ensign at the foremast. Each transport thereupon hoisted her ensign (over the stern from the flagstaff there) her code number & her speed cone. This latter was a canvas cone that was hoisted according to speed, for instance at full speed it would be hoisted to the full & when the engines were at half speed the cone was lowered to half & so on, thereby indicating the speed to the transport following.
