<p>Started to leave Brest about 4 pm. Wrote letter to Baker St during morning. Fairly cold in afternoon but warmer after tea.</p>
<p>24.1.19<br/>Just 2 years today since I sailed from Australia on [HMAT] Anchises.</p>
<p>During morning washed my dirty linen. Last night had talk with American YMCA chap named Wear and found he is a Mason.</p>
<p>Did 316 miles to 12 noon today.</p>
<p>25.1.19<br/>Nice fine sunshine. Yanks had boxing contests during afternoon.</p>
<p>Did 382 miles to 12 noon.</p>
<p>26.1.19 Sunday<br/>Had hot sea water bath during morning.</p>
<p>Did 390 miles to midday. Heavy swell and fairly rough. Strong wind.</p>
<p>27.1.19<br/>Covered 367 miles to today. Passed 3 masted sailing vessel between 2 and 3 pm.</p>
<p>Went to concert after tea but it was very stale. Mrs Vernon Castle [American Dancer] performed and Sir Horace Plunkett [Irish home rule advocate] was chairman.</p>
<p>28.1.19<br/>Did 361 miles to noon, being now on edge of Newfoundland Bank. Vert much colder today. Yesterday sea very rough. Fall of snow after tea.</p>
<p>There are 3 or 4 Yanks on board who were on the Archangel front. They say it was bitterly cold there even last November.</p>
<p>29.1.19<br/>Distance to noon &ndash; 364 miles. Saw 2 passing boats up to lunch time.</p>
<p>In afternoon went to lecture on Psychology by a Yank expert. Snow started falling about 3 pm and continued till 7 or 8 pm. Sea Rough and weather cold.</p>
<p>30.1.19<br/>Distance to noon &ndash; 380 miles. Passed 2 vessels during morning both bound towards New York. Supposed to arrive there sometime tomorrow afternoon.</p>
