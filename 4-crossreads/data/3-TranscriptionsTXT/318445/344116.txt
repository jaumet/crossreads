In close contact came the 19th Battalion, picking its way through vast skeins of barbed wire;and some platoons took to the railway line, which provided a comparatively free passage, but this was quickly swept by the enemy machine guns. These three Battalions moved on, however, in good order, and with a precision that could only spring from a dogged determination to get through at any cost. In close reserve followed the 18th Battalion, which, later on the day, was to move across this field with sublime courage,always giving to give the firmest support, wherever the line was bent, or broken, under by the heavy shocks of repeated counter-attacks.
