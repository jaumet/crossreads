<p>with the advance. <br/>Tuesday 31st July 1917<br/>At 3 am the Bugles sounded the Reveille &amp; all the Ammunition Columns around about were up &amp; getting ready. I bade my Companions Good Bye &amp; Good Luck &amp; they left me. At 4 am the Bombardment started in real ernest, it made a Terrific Noise, I got up &amp; put on my Big Coat &amp; went outside &amp; had a look. What an awful although grand sight the Flashes of Fire all along the Line as far as the eye could see was wonderful, reminding me of a Huge Bush Fire. The Heavens were lit up with Red Fire &amp; the Booming of the Guns were making a Terrific Noise. There were no more sleep for me so I turned in &amp; listened to the Noise until 6 am, when I dressed &amp; went over to the yard to hear of any news.
