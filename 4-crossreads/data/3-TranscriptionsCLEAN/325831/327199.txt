 3. notice boards scattered around, which were once perfectly sane & helpful are now painful & misleading. One perched by the side of a road and[?] deep in mud bears the inscription "Do not raise the dust" & another pointing towards the enemy over a disused trench into what was once a much bombarded "No man's land" "Speed not to exceed ten miles per hour". There are any amount more, pointing nowhere & lying cheerfully - probably left standing to make things harder. 
 A generous Government allows an A.D.C. two horses & one set of saddlery. Mine are excellent beasts--- one a bay & a chestnut, but as yet, I have only ridden the chestnut. The bay is unclipped, & so long as we are in the line & the mud, it would only be cruelty to work him. The slush would bind up his unclipped hair like glue.
