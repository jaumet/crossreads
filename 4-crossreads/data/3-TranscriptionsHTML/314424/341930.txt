<p>Today I received a letter from J. Douglas Jan 20, and 4 from my wife dated Jan 10, No 111. Jan 15, 112. Jan 20 No 113 written from Geelong Jan 25, written from Melbourne.</p>
<p>Friday March 22. 1918. 1327<br/>A fine day, foggy early in the morning. We are to move of to the front at any time perhaps tomorrow. This early shift puts an end to souvenir making for a while.</p>
<p>Yesterday I sent some 6 needle cases to Sis &amp; 2 matchbox covers, also the same to my wife, with a brass [Indecipherable] stand extra.</p>
<p>Today I gave a brass jug made out of an 18 pound shell case &amp; several matchbox covers to Lieut N.G. McNicol.</p>
<p>We are to leave here early tomorrow 5 new men were added to the Transports today and some of the attached men made drivers also some are going on leave tomorrow.</p>
