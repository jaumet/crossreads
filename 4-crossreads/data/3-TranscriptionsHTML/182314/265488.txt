<p>23<sup>rd</sup> October, 1919.</p>

<p>121/H/3449.</p>
<p>Madam,</p>
<p>I am commanded by the Army Council to inform you of the wish of H.M. Government that the next-of-kin of all officers and men who have fallen in the defence of their country should receive a plaque and scroll, bearing the name and regiment, as a memorial of their patriotism and sacrifice.</p>
<p>In order to ensure that the plaque and scroll reach the next-of-kin of the late Maj: Gen: William Holmes, C.M.G., D.S.O., V.D., Spec: List. Staff Aus: Div: H:Q: I am to enquire whether you can inform this Department of the name and address of the late Officer&#39;s <em>nearest</em> relative <em>now living, </em>in the following order of relationship:-</p>
<p>Widow</p>
<p>Eldest Son</p>
<p>Eldest Daughter</p>
<p>Father</p>
<p>Mother</p>
<p>Eldest Brother</p>
<p>Eldest Sister</p>
<p>Eldest Half-Brother</p>
<p>Eldest Half-Sister</p>
<p>The Council regret that owing ot the great number of these memorials it is not possible to say in any individual case when their receipt may be expected.</p>
<p>I am, Madam,</p>
<p>Your obedient Servant,</p>
<p>[Signed] [RM Drade?]</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Mrs R.J.A. Travers,</p>
<p>Cairnleith,</p>
<p>Littleheath Road,</p>
<p>Mosman, N.S.W.</p>
