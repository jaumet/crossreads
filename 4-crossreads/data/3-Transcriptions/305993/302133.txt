<p>the Mediterean.  Some talk of commencing to run the regular mail &amp; passenger service to Palastine, Greece, Italy &amp; France.  Thousands of troops in town every night &amp; still lots of drunks causing mischief on the face of a very strong picket being about in charge of a officer.  Our Naval Captain (Commodore) left today.</p>
<p>19.11.18<br/>Called at the camp of the 52nd Welsh division &amp; amongst the artillery section saw dozens of howitzers &amp; various guns just back from the Palastine front much worn &amp; hundreds of artillery horses.  The majority of these chaps have been up Palastine for the past 3 years &amp; from what they said they have endured some great hardships, such hardships that no one but those who have gone through it can realize.  Some I spoke too that did not have the opportunity of sleeping otherwise than on the hard ground for the last 3 years.  Food was scarce also &amp; very limited, also sad to say during their fast last advance beyond Beyrout &amp; Haifa men stricken down with fever &amp; no medical requirements within hundreds of miles &amp; consequently suffered immensely through the bad management.  Hardships which have happened which would never do to be reported publicly &amp; printed.  Their present camp consist of temperory tents the majority 6 feet long &amp; only 3 feet high.  Just sufficient room for them to sleep &amp; lay under &amp; otherwise every thing else have to be done in the open air.  All exceedingly glad the war is over &amp; are most anxious to see their dear old homes again.</p>
