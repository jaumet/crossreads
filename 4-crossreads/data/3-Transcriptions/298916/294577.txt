<p>an operator would begin at a table transmitting at his speed of receiving &amp; from there working upwards to the "Cracks" who operated at thirty words per minute. Twenty five words per minute is the necessary pass in wireless telegraphy, &amp; it takes long practice to achieve this rate with a maximum of one per cent errors in five minute continuous tests, which is also required. Some operators progressed very quickly &amp; others could not rise above twelve words per minute, although such a low rate is not achieved easily. It requires continuous practice for months, so that those who only rose to twelve words did very well.</p>
<p>After some three or four weeks attending the class &ndash; during which time we were living on our means &ndash; there being no intimation of any troopships being likely to leave in the near future, it became necessary to place the position before the authorities &amp; ask them to pay enough at least to cover tram &amp; train expenses incurred in attending the class. It was agreed to pay 30/- per week then to cover our expenses.</p>
