<p>(6)<br/>And strangely enough, [afterward?] such scenes of sacrilege, in the very roar of battle, would fall the shadow of the cross; cast by a towering crucifix; Intact, Unblemished, with not so much as a splinter lost from its simple shape of wood, nor a fragment chipped from its metal effigy of the tortured Christ who preached Good will to man and <u>Peace on Earth</u><br/>Not a stone on stone, nor brick on brick was left.<br/>Land marks were obliterated and lost.<br/>Fields were ploughed and scarified deep with shells and a thousand villainous variations of destruction</p>
