
Very dejected and downhearted.We were inoculated again next day for cholera and fever, so had 24 days rest to get over the pain in our arms. I might tell you that there were 40,000 troops in the camp and it had a frontage of three miles.All transport was done by mulis or camels, as horses are not hardy enough for the job.On the afternoon of the 28th we left Tel-El-Kebir by train, being more crowded than on the previous occasion. We passed through Zag a Zig, a very up to date town for Egypt, and after aSix hour train ride, arrived at Alexandria much cramped
