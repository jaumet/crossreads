 In the afternoon we had a tin of metho burning (methylated Spirits issued for putting in radiators to minimise chances of freezing but we used our own discreation as to how we used it & we found that we derived more comfort from it by burning it in an open vessel inside the lorry) & sat round it. I stayed in & read all the evening - 
   
 Jan 5  th    We had to set out our tools & personal kits for inspection by Joe Grim. after that ordeal  was over, Joe & I walked into Bailleul & had tea then we went to see the Dons at the concert hall & walked home after - 
   
 Memo - The weather has been very soft all this week thoring consistently - Coal & bread are almost unprocurable by the civil population here. 
   
 Jan 6  th    We went to Steenwerck railhead & hauled a load of oats to 10  th   Brigade dump. Jim drove 21136 - 
   
 Jan 7  th    We did'nt go out to-day. I painted the new lockers which I built in the lorry & it is very cosy. I went to Bailleul  & had a hot bath. also went to ordnance & changed a p  r   of breeches. 
   
 Jan 8  th    A wild morning. snowing hard and cold. I had to go out on a job. so also did Bud -
