<p>At 1 am we were still plodding along &amp; by this time we had covered 16 kilometres or 10 miles, and it was about this time the Colonel discovered he was lost &amp; then things went anyhow. Somebody fired 2 shots that were said to be fired at the colonel but nobody took any notice of them &amp; nobody knows who by whom they were fired.</p>
<p>After going another 2 kilos myself &amp; Tim Quinn decided to drop out as we were just on our last gasp and the battalion by now was only about &frac14; strength, the rest being all along the road for miles back. It was still raining so we crawled under the shelter of a haystack &amp; wet as we were, with our packs for a pillow we were soon sound asleep.</p>
