<p>none of them occupied a higher place than  the young officers who left Liverpool as my senior platoon commander in that splendid  but unlucky B Company, &amp;  who rose to the command of the company himself by sheer merit. When he left Anzac a very sick man, I thought he would not return, but he had grit, &amp;   eventually persuaded the Medical officers to let him return although not nearly well again.</p>
<p>Then in the Flanders trenches when he was losing men daily, his quiet confidences kept the spirits of the company up; in fact it was a pleasure for me to visit his company &amp;  speak to his officers &amp;  men. He, Connor, Francis, Blanchard &amp;  Barlow were grand fellows &amp;   an ideal team &amp;  when they were knocked out the morale of the men dropped to zero.
