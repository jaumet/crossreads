<p>We then were taken by our aforetime guides right round the town for about five miles walk, down country lanes, across green-fields, over rustic bridges and right through the town again. Rissoles &amp; green peas made a very palatable supper, and we then made the best of our way home across the monotonous undulations of the notorious English camping reserve.</p>
<p>April 15</p>
<p>Church Parade this morning. After this the whole Training Battn. Was drawn up &amp; sub-divided into parties of six and set to work at tent-pegging. In about &frac12; an hour we had 250 tents up and then it was dinner-time; (roast-pork and cabbage &amp; Burgundy, - I don't think!)</p>
<p>In the afternoon Alan &amp; I went to Mrs Pratt's at Shrewton, and there had tea and spent the evening with quite a number of soldiers, including a few from the Royal Flying Corps. Mrs Pratt is an admirable hostess and is the very personification of kindness.</p>
