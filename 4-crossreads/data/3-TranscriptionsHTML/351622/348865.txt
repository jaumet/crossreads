<p>While resting on the road some battalions of the Royal Fusiliers Middlesex &amp; Royal Sussex passed us on their way to the firing line. Bivouaced</p>
<p>[Text continued in margin]<br/>in trenches just outside Albert.</p>
<p>27 July 1916 Thur<br/>Awakened from our slumbers at 4 a.m. Worked at the transport lines till 7 am. We then packed our kits. Passed our kits into the QM. Store. I have nothing now bar what I stand up in save my great coat waterproof sheet &amp; Balaclava [cap]. Left Harponville 8.45 in rear of 2nd Pioneer batt. Passed through Hedauville &amp; Bouzincourt [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>28 July 1916 Fri<br/>Slept in an old mill in Albert last night. Could not sleep owing to a big 12 inch Howitzer just outside where we were sleeping. The first Division has been cut to pieces. Learnt that Hubert had fallen &amp; Cockie wounded. What has befallen Vere I do not know, I am hoping for the best. At 9.15 am left for the old chateau 2 &frac12; miles out of Albert where our dressing station is situated. Big bombardment going on to-night &amp; the 2nd Division are going over so</p>
<p>[Text continued in margin]<br/>expect plenty of work. Isles wounded.</p>
