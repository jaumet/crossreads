<p>The Captain growled out from the bridge to the First Officer "We will turn round and go back to the anchorage." He was in a tremendous passion. The men were knocked speechless. We went back to the anchorage, &amp; at 8 P.M. the West Australians left the ship in a tender for the Shore.</p>
<p>February 19th :- Sailed at noon <strike>today</strike> en route to Adelaide.</p>
<p>February 24th :- Arrived at Adelaide this morning; left later for Melbourne.</p>
<p>February 26th :- At Melbourne. Anchored off New Pier, whence I had embarked nearly two years ago.</p>
<p>March 1st :- Saw Tasmanian coast for the first time, about noon.</p>
<p>March 2nd :- At Hobart. Tasmanian soldiers disembarked, &amp; the "City of York" left for Sydney at Midday.</p>
<p>March 4th :- Off Sydney Heads, time nearly midnight.</p>
<p>March 5th :- Left the "City of York" in a ferry boat. Landed at Woolloomooloo. Red Cross Lady gave me a flower and a packet of cigarettes at the wharf. Glad to be almost near home.</p>
<p>April 11th :- Arrived at Brisbane. Reunited with my family.</p>
<p>April 16th :- Discharged from the Australian Imperial Force having served 2 years and 3 months.</p>
<p>F. J. Brewer</p>
