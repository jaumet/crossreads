The bearers, while not fighting, run almost as many risks as the infantry, taking good times &amp; bad together, but what they miss in hopping over, patrol work, advancing against opposition &amp; the rest constitutes the difference. And, as I said, the bearers are not always in the forward area so long as the infantry. Out of the line our chaps get a far better time than the drilling, marching &amp; polishing infantry. They are the ones who might truly be said to do the dirty work of war, whether it is fighting or working.</p>
