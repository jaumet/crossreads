Our horses &amp; ourselves had started in the morning without water &amp; we thanked the Tommies for clearing out when we found their water supply, so we got our water bottles filled again.  We went no further that night so improved the wells &amp; managed to get our horses some water which was slightly brackish, but which they drank eagerly.  They were terribly thirsty being on the march practically 2 whole days without water.</p>
<p>Wednesday 26th<br/>Some more provisions horse feed etc were obtained from the other deserted camp of the Yeomanry about a mile away.  Our troop went out early to Katieh the scene of the fighting &amp; brought in 5 wounded men, but one man died on the way in.  We also found a dead sergeant who had nearly got back to camp.
