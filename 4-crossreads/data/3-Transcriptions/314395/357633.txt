<p>brought from the depths of his cellar the very best he had.  Also his champagne (which had reposed for eight years in his cellar) was excellent.  It was <strike>the</strike> only the second time during the war that he had broached the particular brand, and it was in my honour.  Well I certainly knew it was better than one buys in an estaminet for 6 sous, so I sat back and tilted my glass like a connoisseur.  I discussed various brands and named his my choice, at which he was highly pleased, and produced a liqueur, genuine Cognac of a celebrated make, and again I congratulated him on the excellency of the vintage.  To finish up, Madame related with great pride, that the coffee cup I was using was the identical one that Prince Alexander of Teck had used a few weeks previously when he was billeted on them.</p>
