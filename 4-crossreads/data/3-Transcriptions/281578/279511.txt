<p>a perfect treat, &amp; we did enjoy it. It is a peculiar river, more like a canal, very narrow &amp; has a swift current. Has no high banks like our Australian rivers, &amp; if it rose a few feet it would be well over the bank. Saw Boo on the opposite bank, he was dressed ready to march back with B Coy. as we got in. I was surprised to see Strangman -Taylor there, he is in the 15th Bn. now, I used to be in the same Coy. as he in camp at Liverpool. We got back about 6, &amp; after tea all sat out in the grass reading, then some had a game of leapfrog &amp; jumping. We were having a read in our tent after turning in, when the throb of a Fritz 'plane was heard on a night raid, so we promptly put our candles out.  Wedn. 22 May We moved on again, &amp; some brainy "head" in his wisdom, made the hour of starting 12 noon. It was a hot day, &amp; as we carried fighting order &amp; oilsheet, greatcoat &amp; &amp;c ., the march soon became positive torture. We were absolutely soaked in perspiration in our heavy uniform, rifle &amp; load, but we simply had to trudge on, &amp; with more than a feeling of relief we turned into the beautiful grounds of a fine chateau, &amp; halted under lovely big green trees in the shade beside the river.
