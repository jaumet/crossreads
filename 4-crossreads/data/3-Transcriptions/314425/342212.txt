<p>We had an issue of lollies a handful per man from the Comforts funds today. Enemy planes were over last night &amp; at least one was brought down in flames. I am on second picquet tonight from 11.30 to 2 a.m.</p>
<p>Tuesday September 17. 1918. 1505<br/>Fine but cloudy with S.W. wind. Enemy planes were over last night but not in force. Some of them were caught in the light &amp; turned with machine gun &amp; archie fire.<br/>There was a very heavy shower accompanied with thunder &amp; lightning when I was on picquet. It was bright moonlight when I went on and pitch dark when I came off. Later in the night there was a tremendous storm with high winds thunder and lightning, with torrential rain.<br/>This morning when returning from watering up, the mule ridden by J.T. Ferguson slipped &amp; fell on his leg, he got caught in the stirrup &amp; was dragged a few yards. He was bruised but not seriously hurt. The roads are very slippery since the rain &amp; the country is very hilly. I estimate that we ride about 5 miles every morning &amp; evening going to and returning from water at Courcelles which is the nearest watering place here.
