These are absolutely indispensable on account of the immense supplies of coal, foodstuffs etc. necessary for such a large body of troops. The camps are all arranged in their separate Regiments as far as the English are concerned, the lines being named after the various Generals now operating at the front such as Haig, Hamilton Lines and so on. The Australian Lines are arranged according to their states. In this large area are covered all the houses, private and otherwise have all been taken over by the War Authorities for military use and include several large public buildings. One immense place seems to have been a big church with spire and bells and is now a Post and telegraph office. Most of the other large places are all administrative offices and Quarters for the big gun officials.
