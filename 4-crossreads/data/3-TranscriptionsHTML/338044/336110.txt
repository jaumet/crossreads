<p>5th Nov:-<br/>Usual routine again to-day with the exception of pitching some tents. I was taken for picket duty in the horse lines but managed to scheme out of it    &amp;  had nothing to do all day except mess orderly  I would like to give a vivid description of the Camp Zeitoun. Sights are here daily seen, which will never be seen again &amp; which people in Australia cannot imagine. Early morning Parade is very impressive. We rise earlier than the infantry &amp;  whilst on Parade, out they march. First come the Maori contingent. These are a fine body of men, of splendid physique &amp; very intelligent. They present a good spectacle, as they go through manouvres on the desert, giving blood curdling</p>
