<p>1919<br/>Feb 4<br/>sold them to the civvies &amp; there were none left.  The civilians dye them &amp; make suits out of them.  I was too late to look for an hotel, so I hunted around &amp; found a mattress &amp; a bag.  Popped into the bag, pulled my overcoat over me &amp; slept warm till morning.</p>
<p>Feb 5<br/>Bought some picture post cards of the town &amp; some others poking fun at the various sorts of Fritz (the shops were full of these, especially ones of the Kaiser).  Then as I felt  a bit hungry (I had a bit of breakfast at the camp) had some chips &amp; as the ordinary bus was not running hopped on another &amp; came home.  It snowed on the way back &amp; we had about a 6" fall (end of letter)<br/>One night the Alpine Chasseurs Band came to our Cinema  Show.  They are known as the "Blue Devils" &amp; were billeted at Solre le Chateau.  They played several selections.  The whole of this battalion had been decorated with the Legion of Honour.
