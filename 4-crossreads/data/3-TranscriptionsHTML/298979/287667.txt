<p>After tripping round &amp; inspecting the sights of Belfast &amp; testing its "Ginger Ale" making one or two tram journeys into the suburbs, &amp; a thorough inspection of the Dock &amp; Ship building yards, the Sentry was lucky by a little persuasion &amp; tact, to get a very close view of the sister ship to the "Renown", (that had sailed from the Clyde).This vessel looked every inch a fighting ship and as she was nearing completion she even then looked fit to go into the biggest action &amp; punish the Huns properly.</p>
<p>A Rail journey through the green fields of Erin's Isle brought the Sentry to Dublin where owing to the ravages of the recent rebellion things were more strict from a military point of view. Numbers of military police &amp; patrols were moving about seeing that every soldier conducted himself, &amp; was even dressed, properly. There appeared to be an unseen watchfulness being exercised that at first was a little bit irritating however as time would be short in Dublin, it did not matter.</p>
<p>Being anxious to see the effect of the shell fire during the rebellion, the Sentry climbed the Staircase of the Nelson's Column in the main st &amp; from there obtained a splendid view. On the balcony at the top of the Column, the Sentry met a typical Irish school boy, evidently about 12 years of age, &amp; casually asking him what he knew about the fighting in Dublin, was pleasantly surprised to find that the youngster had evidently made a study of the whole plan of the campaign &amp; his explanation was so clear &amp; lucid that he spoke as if he had been at the same spot watching the attack &amp; defence, the whole time.</p>
