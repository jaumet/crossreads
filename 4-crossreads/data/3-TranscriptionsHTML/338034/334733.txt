<p>Besides the thousands of men which compose the various A.S.C.'s, A.M.C.'s etc a number of black troops are employed here chiefly in pioneer and transport work. These include Indian Sikhs, British West Indians, &amp;  the Arab labour corps employed on road &amp; railway construction. Enemy aeroplanes have been accomplishing much reconnaissance work over our encampments during the past day or so but have not bombed at all. The rail-head is now about six miles East of Khan Yunis,</p>
