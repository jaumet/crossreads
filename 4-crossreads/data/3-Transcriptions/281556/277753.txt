<p>had orders to get what sleep we could. We were alongside old much knocked about trenches &amp; knew not our whereabouts, front or anything else. About one hour later wakened with great fear, fierce bombardment on all sides, guns of all sizes, &amp; as light as day almost by the flash of the guns. I was absolutely afraid to move &amp; curled myself up under great coat &amp; tried to stop trembling from cold &amp; fear as they must have warned us if in any danger. Prisoners passed through gully later.</p>
