
<p>by the catterpillers 8&quot; &amp; 9.2&quot; Howitzers.</p>
<p>Apl 3<sup><u>rd</u></sup> We did&#39;nt go out in the morning but went to Vermozeele at 1.P.M. &amp; brought out all the Australian tunnellers attached to the company (1<sup><u>st</u></sup> Division) Our troops are all on their way to the Somme &amp; the <s>men</s>&nbsp;fifth army is coming up here. they are arriving every day -&nbsp;&nbsp;In the evening Tiny came up with two lorries &amp; relieved our three lorries &amp;&nbsp;we went back to the park which is now at Meteren</p>
<p>Apl 4<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; We had to drain the gear box &amp; thoroughly clean it, <s>for</s>&nbsp;<sub>^</sub><sup>and on</sup>&nbsp;inspection found two bolts of the star pinions sheared so had to take the differential out. Rainy day again.</p>
<p>Apl 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Joe Grim came home boozed at 1 A.M. this morning &amp; had us all out to drain our radiators which we had all (but two) omitted to do. there was no danger of frost but orders were to drain and we had&#39;nt done it - we worked on the gear box all day - rainy day - The 1st M.T. Coy are going down to the Somme to-morrow.</p>
<p>Joe Grimm came along in the evening</p>
