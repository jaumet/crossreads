<p>Sunday 4 August 1918<br/>4th Anniversary of the war.  Played billiards with Williams in the morn.</p>
<p>Monday 5 August 1918</p>
<p>Tuesday 6 August 1918<br/>Warned for draft conductg. &amp; could not get out of it tho' I did my best!!</p>
<p>Wednesday 7 August 1918<br/>Had an easy day, spent most of time at home.  Off to France with the draft at 10.30 p.m.  Most of the Draft were well oiled!!</p>
<p>Thursday 8 August 1918<br/>Arrived Folkestone early &ndash; brekker at rest camp &ndash; then to boat and good crossing.  Lunch at "Ostrohove" Camp.  The diggers took French leave &amp; toddled into Boulogne!!</p>
<p>Friday 9 August 1918<br/>Entrained at 12 HO &amp; made a good trip to Rouen.  Kept thr. 8 hrs. during air raid.  Played bridge &amp; read a good deal during the trip.  5 officers left behind at B.</p>
<p>Saturday 10 August 1918<br/>Arrived Havre at 12 noon &amp; handed over all without a man missing.  Saw Mrs. Hendry at 40th Stat.  Into Havre later &amp; bought cigarettes.<br/>"Love is not to be reasoned down or lost in high ambition &amp; a thirst of greatness.<br/>"Tis second Life, it grows into the soul, warms every vein &amp; beats in every pulse."  Addison.</p>
