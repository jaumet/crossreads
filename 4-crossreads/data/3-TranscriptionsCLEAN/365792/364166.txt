  
 Mch 31 There are hundreds of trees almost completely covered with ivy, & a certain other parasitical (bird-nest) growth which is rather common in  Australia  In many cases the ground itself (beneath the trees) is matted with ivy. Passed Paris in the distance about 1 or 2 pm, & what appeared to be the Eiffel Tower. The country here (after crossing the ranges) is mostly flat, or undulating, & of course few of the trees & hedges are yet showing their spring leaves - with the exception perhaps of fruit trees. The roads are excellent, the best ive had the pleasure of seeing & everywhere railways intersect the countryside. Most of the 2nd Division is in France & we arrived  here  this morning about 4.30 & detrained within sound of the guns - whose sullen booming could be heard dully through the thick fog blanket. "We're getting closer & closer." A Coy is temporary billeted in a  [in margin] Renescure
