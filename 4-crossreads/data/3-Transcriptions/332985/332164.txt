<p>Lines as clean as a new pin.  All manure carted away in G.S. wagon.</p>
<p>31-5-1917<br/>Contests, Picking out best men for certain events.  Weather fine.</p>
<p>1-6-1917, Friday<br/>Boxing contest.  Driver Cook, heavy weight 1st Bty.</p>
<p>2-6-1917<br/>Fine day.  The 3rd Bty. won the tug-o-war.  We meet the 101st Bty.</p>
<p>3-6-1917<br/>Lieut. Hogan, to be Captain.  Harness competition on this evening.  Horses looking well, Singing to be carried out on a few with long hairs.  The horse master here almost every day.  King's birthday.  Lost the Tug of war to the 101st Bty., who will pull the 3rd Bty.</p>
<p>4-6-1917<br/>Harness competion, One section from each Bty.  Col. McLaughlan, assisted by Capt. Little, Capt. Lucas V.O. and Lieut. Strong, judges.</p>
