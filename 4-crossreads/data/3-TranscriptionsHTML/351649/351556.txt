<p>18 February 1917</p>
<p>St John's Wood Barracks,<br/>London, N.W.</p>
<p>Dear Mrs. John</p>
<p>I heap reproaches on myself that I haven't written to you for a whole month:  such, Madam, is the gratitude of man, for I used to like getting your letters, specially in action:  and now all that is past, pro temp, and I have a soft job here for two months before I go back to France.</p>
<p>I must own I feel rather a rotter sitting here safe and sound when I'm fat &amp; well and sound:  but there you are &ndash; when I was offered this work, I jumped at it.</p>
<p>And now am fed up a bit with myself for taking it on:</p>
