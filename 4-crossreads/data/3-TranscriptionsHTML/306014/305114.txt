<p>&amp; hayricks it led, bringing me finally on to a high vantage ground &amp; thence I could discern the whole out-lay of a winding rivulet, with green banks &amp; with a track meandering alongside. In the distance it lost itself behind the rolling hills &amp; in the foreground it slowly crept round &amp; through a picturesque English Village.</p>
<p>I scarce know yet whether the Village draws itself out along the winding banks of the stream, or whether the stream goes peeping in &amp; out the hidden parts of the village. All sign of artificiality is absent. The whole scene seems as natural &amp; as touching &amp; as suggestive as the blue-brown smoke that slowly rises to heaven from the chimney-tops. There is a suggestion of happy homes, of cosy firesides, of peace with the world. Such were the impressions which the first sight</p>
