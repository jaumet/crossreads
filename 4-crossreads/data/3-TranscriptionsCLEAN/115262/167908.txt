  
 Monday 21 May 1917. 
 Land sighted at 9 a,m, Heavy mist. Sea calmer. Anchored 10.30 a.m. just out of Fremantle. Shore quite close. Everyone pleased at seeing houses from distance. 
 Motor launch "Dauntless" came along side our boat A 42. "Boorara" and discharged her cargo of fruit (Y.M.C.A. Comforts Fund gift) 
 Fruit issued in afternoon. Sea now calm as mill-pond & sun shining on Fre e mantle - a Pretty sight. The 2 escorts "Encounter" & "Doris" nowhere to be seen. 
 4 transports weigh anchor & steam slowly to Fremantle up "Swan River" 
 Cleaned  up all equipment. A Japanese Cruiser (1 mast ed ) entered "the Rose" & anchored. A great night on upper deck. 
 Transports actually with us now are:- 
 " Shropshire", "Boorara (A42)', "Clan M c Guily", 'Port. Sydy." A15, "Ascanius", "Tofua (NZ25), Marathon", "Canadian", "Benalla" "Armadale", "Ulysses".
