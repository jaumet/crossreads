Whilst at Lemnos an enemys aeroplane flew over the bay, I suppose they were out for a bit of a spy, they never dropped any bombs, there were about 40 battleships here of all sorts &amp; 5 or 6 submarines &amp; 5 hydroplanes I had the pleasure of seeing these go up one day to do a bit of scouting, well Mother I will push along a bit we were here till the 24th April at 6-30 that night we sailed out of Lemnos, amid cheering, &amp; cheering &amp; coo-e's of the battleships, the sailors were giving us cheering remarks &amp; wishing us all good luck, they evidently knew what we had to face as they were there bombarding the forts in march.<br/>Well out of Lemnos we went with our pouches full of bullets &amp; our haversacks full of biscuits &amp; bullybeef. We were all given a lecture before leaving &amp; told by the <u>head</u> that we were fighting a treacherous foe &amp; that we were new to this game they call war, but he was sure that we would make a name for ourselves, that Australia would be proud of. At 8 o'clock we arrived in some little shelter bay called Mudros which hid us from the lookouts of the Dardenells we were now only a few miles from the peninsula- just before it got dark you could see away over on the horizon a dozen warships</p>
