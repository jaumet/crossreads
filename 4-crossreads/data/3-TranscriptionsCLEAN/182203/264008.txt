  
 The Palace,                                                                                                                                                                                                 Heliopolis.                                                                                                                                                                                                           Sept. 9th  1915. My dearest Mother, I have just heard that an Australian mail closes at 3.30. p.m. so shall just have time to scribble a few lines to you. I am out of bed - both Essie & I are sitting-up in chairs writing, so you see we are getting on splendidly. In fact I should have been quite alright only for a wretched abscess I got under my R. arm, that, is alright, but more pleasure in store for me - have two other boils just near the old one & shall have to have them opened in a couple of days. I'm put on Stout. Horrid stuff. Anyhow I'm feeling grand, so don't worry. Col. Maudsley may be leaving us soon  he has an appointment offered him, in a London Military Hospital, which he will accept.
