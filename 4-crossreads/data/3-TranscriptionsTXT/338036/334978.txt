Joe Nankivell was killed in almost the same circumstances.The positions were evacuated during night & became probably No mans land a couple of days later as the flanks had had not advanced Chas Hamblin witnessed two shells lob on to one of our parties & blew the whole thing to atoms.
Nothing of interest during the night but I read some mail that came during my absence.
Thursday 15thSlept until 6 pm.  Meeting of sports Committee at 6 pm.  Saw Arthur Mealey tonight.  Quite [quiet?] night.
Friday 16thMeeting of sports Committee at 3.45.
Saturday 17thWas quite surprised to see young "Swinny" [Swinnerton] (now a Sgt) Gordon Dawkins & L/C McCure who all reported to the Ambulance for duty today.
Sunday 18thIn the afternoon John Perry & self journeyed to L'Estrade D.R.S. & met Clarrie Power (Corp M.M.) of the [Water] Board who I had not seen for some time - 5th Fld Amb.  Also met Jimmy O'Sullivan late of the 9th.  On the road saw Bert Crooke & directed him to the Ambulance for particulars of Sams death.  He had been in the lines at Ypres been gassed
