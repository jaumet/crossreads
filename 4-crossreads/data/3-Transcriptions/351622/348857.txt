<p>11 July 1916 Tues<br/>Had my foot dressed early. Inspection 11 am of Gas helmets, discs, iron rations field dressings &amp; boots. 11.30 Jennings &amp; myself left the billet going ahead of the main body to transport lines. I rode on the waggon with Bluey Gledhill. Arrived St Omer about 5 pm passing through a fair portion of the town. We entrain [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>12 July 1916 Wed<br/>Left Amiens 5.30 am after a rough night spent in the cattle truck. Walked right through the town &amp; halted on the outskirts for breakfast. After walking about thirteen miles we arrived at [Picquigny] General leave granted from 3 pm. till 9 am. I went round the town visiting the different estaminets meeting many old Enmore boys. Tomy [Materson], Doc Grey, Morris &amp; Loten</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>Took salute from Generals Birdwood &amp; Legge.</p>
