<p>Tuesday 3 April 1917.</p>
<p>On no duty or fatigue.&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">Field day till 1.30 pm. in the frills by the river</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">In afternoon&nbsp;&ndash; pay, drill; &amp; lecture by Major Morton</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">1st time&nbsp;&ndash; up to Orderly rooms for talking on parade. Absolutely no crime or charge agnst me.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">Pte F. W. Bayley had a hamper sent to him from Nagambie so we had a good tea &amp; supper in tent.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">Some AMC. Reinfts cancelled. a ?</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">[Major John Morton, 47, surgeon, of Macquarie Street, Sydney, embarked from Sydney on 15 May 1915 on RMS Mooltan with the 3rd Australian General Hospital. He returned to Australia in mid-1916.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.6em;">Private Frank William Bayley, 19, a clerk from St Kilda, Victoria, embarked from Melbourne on 10 May 1917 on HMAT A42 Boorara&nbsp;with the Camel Field Ambulance.]</span></p>
