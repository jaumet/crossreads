Kittson to night - the "Age" of Sept. 8. and a 1/4 lb tin of Perfection tobaccoMonday Feb. 11, 1918. 1288Cold & cloudy also windy at 10 o'clock there was a heavy bombardment, and later our boys carried out a successful raid on the enemy. The 37th brought in a lot of prisoners (about 20). Our casualties are about 20. Mr Dickson  & Mr Crowe are reported missing. Other Battalions in the Brigade did equally as well.I am fitting new brake screws on the Limbres todayTuesday Feb. 12, 1918. 1289.Fine but cloudy.I am carrying on with the same work as yesterday. There will be at least another day's work on the same job.I saw Dave Mc Kean this evening at tea time.
