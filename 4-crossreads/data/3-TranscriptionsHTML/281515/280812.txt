Also Gas alarms are given sometimes during the night as it has happened that a prisoner has been taken &amp; gives away the information that a gas attack is planned by the Huns to take place at a certain time &amp; sure enough it did &amp; we got the alarm through by telephone so we had to put on Gas masks &amp; stop in them until a certain time had passed when it was advisable to take them off again.</p>
<p>After leaving the Huts near Contay we marched back farther to have a spell this time to Steenvorde where we found a nice billet in a barn full of new straw &amp; we had many a good nights sleep on that same straw.  the first few days there we did as we liked cleaning ourselves up &amp; washing our clothes &amp; going to the baths where you Hand in your dirty creepy clothes &amp; after a good hot bath we used to get clean clothes &amp; we felt fine after as we used to get quite lively &amp; sometimes we would be 8 weeks between changes of clothes &amp; at the baths our tunics &amp; Pants were ironed with Hot irons down the seams to kill (or hatch) the eggs laid there.</p>
<p>Our turn came round again all too soon to go back again to the line, this time to another sector called Le-Transloy &amp; it being very wet weather we were given out long rubber boots to wear in the trenches &amp; it was hard to march in them being slippy in the mud which came up over our knees in places &amp; being the month of October 1916 the nights were getting a bit cold &amp; what with the rain also some miserable nights &amp; days were put in that awful winter of 1916 &amp; 1917 which according to the French people was the worst winter for twenty years &amp; the men who went through the miseries of that</p>
