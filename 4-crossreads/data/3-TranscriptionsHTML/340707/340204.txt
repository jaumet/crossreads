<p>with numbers like this Illuminations there are none for the electricians have gone on strike. The decorations are I think better than those of Paris &amp; some of the private firms are excellent eg Frascatis &amp; Army &amp; Navy</p>
<p>After supper at the Strand we adjourn home.</p>
<p>There was one thing that I liked about the French procession &amp; that was that in front of all marcher were wheeled a 1000 war cripples. This had no counterpart in England but seats in the Mall were assigned to widows &amp; orphans, to Chelsea pensioners to wounded soldiers &amp; to school children. In France school children were provided for but I dont know about the others. Seats were made of empty ammunition boxes. Some was done at Paris I find</p>
<p>22-7-19<br/>Rose at 5-30 am this morning &amp; breakfast was ready for me &amp; Lily was waiting to say good bye. This will be the last time I shall see her as she is being married on Bank holiday.</p>
<p>I get a good seat &amp; run into Folkestone at 10 am By mistake of MLO I get on Boulogne boat &amp; dont bother to change when I see Calais boat or tub, a Belgian affair. MLO tells me I can run</p>
