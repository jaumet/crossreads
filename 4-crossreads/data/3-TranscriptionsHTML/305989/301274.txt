<p>Sunday 16 September 1917<br/>On Day Stableman tied up 30 horses:  played bridge at night.  Wrote Nos. [see images for possible Arabic numbers for 8 and 4e].</p>
<p>Monday 17 September 1917<br/>On fatigue on Officers Cookhouse.  Saw Guthrie.  Preparing to move to Kilo 9.</p>
<p>Tuesday 18 September 1917<br/>Left at 6.30 for Kilo 9, arriving about 11.30 &amp; struck camp, picquet at night.  Awfully dusty &amp; windy.</p>
<p>Wednesday 19 September 1917<br/>Making ourselves comfortable as possible.  Windy &amp; Dusty.</p>
<p>Thursday 20 September 1917<br/>On Q.M. fatigue, bought some canteen stuff at Tommies canteen, windy &amp; dusty.</p>
<p>Friday 21 September 1917<br/>Early morning parade &amp; quiet day.  Wind lesser.</p>
<p>Saturday 22 September 1917<br/>Quiet day.</p>
