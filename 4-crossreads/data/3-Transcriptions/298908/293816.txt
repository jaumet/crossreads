<p>&amp; told him how things stood.  He had a look at our Pay books &amp; said "certainly you can draw money, how much do you want Sgt.  Oh a fiver will do me I said, so he entered it in my book &amp; I was paid in a few minutes &amp; so was old Mac.  That afternoon I caught the train for Littlehampton.  We left London at 12.50 &amp; went round through Brighton, had to change 3 times first at East Croydon then at Brighton &amp; again at Ford.  A civilian nurse was travelling in the same carriage as I was &amp; she had a big trunk, so at the various changes I took possession of her things &amp; helped her all I could for nurses are people I have the greatest admiration for &amp; will always do them a good turn if possible.  When I arrived at Mr. Dukes place they were just having tea &amp; I blew in on them unexpectedly.  They were surprised to see me for they had imagined me far away on the bounding sea but no such luck.</p>
