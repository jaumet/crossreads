The next day the eight  the coy. suffered its first casualty since coming in on the 25 of Sept. &amp; was a wounded case "A" Coy part of the front line consisted of sentry groups on the further lip of the craters &amp; this part of the line is called the Bluff.  On the night of the 12th our Battalion raiders payed a visit to Fritz trenches inflicting a number of casualties on him &amp; and only four on our side &amp; the raid on a whole was fairly successful.  We were releived by the 14th Battalion on the night of the 13th &amp; I had to guide our releiving platoon to our position &amp; then guide our platoon to Ypres where we entrained &amp; on disentraining our band met us which was kept out of the line &amp; played us to our camp at Dominion lines which we reached about 11pm &amp; turned in. We were up early next morning &amp; started on the march passing through several villiages &amp; through the outskirts of Cassel &amp; finally reached our billet at Godewaersvelde in the afternoon &amp;  rested.  That night accompanied with a cobber we had a look round the town which is a fairly decent little place &amp; had tea there &amp; returned early.  Next morning 10th we were on the march again &amp; went as far as Arneke &amp; billeted for the night &amp; this place is nicely situated &amp; the people are of a good class.
