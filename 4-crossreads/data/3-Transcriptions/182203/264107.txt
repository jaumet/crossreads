<p>were competing but don't think they have ever ridden a horse before. Of course it was slow for us because we were strangers. Afternoon tea was also provided. Had to return for duty 6. p.m. so had a longer afternoon off.<br/>I did intend to go to church to-night but it is raining &amp; sounds as if it had set-in for the night. Shall try to write more letters if staying in. We are supposed to vote to-morrow for or against <strike>Mi</strike> Compulsory Military Service etc. Shall tell you next letter how I've voted.<br/>I have not received one Melb. Paper since I've left Egypt. &ndash; The Una, I did receive last week &ndash; but no newspaper. We did wish to read what the Argus was saying about it. Received a paper "Manifesto to Australian Soldiers. from Mr W. M. Hughes, - read it &amp; talked it over, so have quite decided which way to vote.</p>
