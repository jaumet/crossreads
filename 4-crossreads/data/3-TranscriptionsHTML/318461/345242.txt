<p>Shell smashed Bapaume. Where the Town Hall was is a huge mound of Brick &amp; Mortar There were some very large &amp; fine buildings here but not one has escaped should judge it to be a town of anything up to 25,000 People now theres not one to be seen all soldiers. Tommies &amp; Ausis</p>
<p>Wednesday 18/4/17<br/>Very cold again Rain &amp; Snow, slush &amp; mud<br/>Guns very busy last night, all quiet now.<br/>should be going back again today<br/>Pay. 14/3. &pound;24.1.0.<br/>leaving here on the 21st long journey perhaps Armentaires. hope so.<br/>letters this afternoon should get some.<br/>having good spell here &amp; plenty to eat but it is not always so. More prisoners this morning.<br/>have been to Picture show just fancy a show here in Bapaume a few weeks ago &amp; even now shells were flopping in to it, such is life, Matty Walker took me.<br/>Nearly Blind with smoke from fire eyes running water &amp; Blood shot, very painful<br/>to be bed at 9.30.</p>
<p>Thursday 19/4/17<br/>at last letters 2 from Nell, 2 cards from Carol letters from Jack &amp; Tony one from Loo &amp; Viv ea. Quite a budget, must answer them to day.<br/>French &amp; Russians doing well we hear Our guns never cease.<br/>Leaving here tomorrow for the North some say Belgium. feeding us well so I suppose its Per Boot as usual.<br/>Carol's Cards caused many a laugh.<br/>Rumour says we are to be bombarded here tonight, hope not as we leave tomorrow.<br/>Gas alarm on.<br/>Letter also from Syd.<br/>Pay 15/3 &pound;24.2.0</p>
<p>Friday. 20/4/17<br/>Wrote to Nell, Maude, Loo, &amp; Viv not posted yet.<br/>off this morning to Albert, going long journey, not carrying Blankets this time but as we only go about 6 miles today will be cold tonight that however is better than having your shoulders Broken.<br/>Pay 16/3. &pound;24.3.0<br/>left Bapaume at 12.30 got here Hot Cross Camp. about 5.30 distance about 11 miles feeling better than on other marches</p>
