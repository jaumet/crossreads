
Thursday 18 March 1915Ride before breakfast across dessert Wrote to Royal College of Surgeons. Dr Paton. Dr Peck.O.C. away for most of the day, in charge during his absence.Received copy of The Times from Dr Peck of date 2.3-15Wrote to  Ride during afternoon, along the Canal banks.
Friday 19 March 1915Not out this morning Birth of Dr Livingstone 1813.Wrote to Lieut Gordon Bennett, The Minister forEducation, Egypt, The Christian Brothers St Catherines Alexandria. Wrote some sheets for the Girls & Mollie Writing & studying French all day long. No surgical work to do.
Saturday 20 March 1915Riding before breakfast. At sports of No 2 Battn.during the afternoon. Much drunkeness amongst officers & men. Wrote to Senator Pearce, Fred Flowers, Girls & Mollie. Letter from Dr Dunlop adviser to Minstry Ministry for Education.
Sunday 21 March 1915Mass at 7 a.m - Gallop across dessert thereafter. Po Cs to Sir G. Strickland, Girls, Mollie, Posted letters to them.Wrote to Mrs M. M. Knowles & posted letter.  Wrote to Mr Begg Old South Head Road Bondi - Father to a lad I operated on in the hospital. v. [vide: see] Note book NoFor a ride through the farm lands between 5 p.m. & 6.30 p.m.
