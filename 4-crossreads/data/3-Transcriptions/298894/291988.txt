Then after collecting some reading matter I said farewell to the boys who were leaving for Anzac &amp; returned back to camp to find 1 paper, two parcels &amp; three letters awaiting me, truly a splendid day. The parcels contained a sheepskin vest, cap comforter scarf, socks, writing paper, photos &amp; an autograph camera &amp; films in a lovely leather case. I spent a real good half hour opening my treasures.<br/>Friday 5th spent the morning in stretcher drill which proved somewhat of a stretcher. In the afternoon had a game of cricket &amp; managed to knock up 13 runs being second top score on my side. Wrote some letters at night in the Ymca tent.<br/>Saturday 6th Mess orderly for the day. The artillary camped alongside left for Anzac leaving a lot of stuff behind &amp; we all managed to get a supply of jam, tobacco, boxes tea &amp; writing paper etc etc also some vessels for carrying water. The weather beautiful and warm &amp; wearing shorts a pleasure. Splendid concert at night in the crest of a hill run by the New Zealanders. A Bonfire was built in the centre &amp; the piano obtained from the YMCA &amp; the effect was very picturesque. Several nurses were present also Naval officers as guests.
