<p>Dec. 21 Tuesday 1915<br/>Up a short time today<br/>The Sisters are giving me fish &amp; vegetables with custard &amp; plenty of milk</p>
<p>Dec. 22 Wednesday 1915<br/>Feeling stronger today. It is nice to be able to get about &amp; see things<br/>All the patients here are receiving quite a lot of dainties from Australian Friends I have been answering letters of thanks</p>
<p>Dec. 23 Thursday 1915<br/>Walking about a bit today<br/>The orderlies are making the place look like Xmas Palms &amp; bushes &amp; flowers are too be seen every where.</p>
<p>Dec. 24 Friday 1915<br/>Feeling better today<br/>Got letter from Home also one from Major Fuller, the Major has been having a good time in England &amp; is now in command of the Regiment. Good luck to him.</p>
