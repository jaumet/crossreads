<p>Sunday 7 May 1916<br/>Very hot day.  A lot of boys vaccinated.  Had easy day.</p>
<p>Monday 8 May 1916<br/>Setting out Hutting, also supervising work.</p>
<p>Tuesday 9 May 1916<br/>Whole Company went to Rifle Range in morning and had Rifle practice.  Lecture afterwards.</p>
<p>Wednesday 10 May 1916<br/>Had fairly easy time in Camp.  Had short lecture on Demolition morning  practically afternoon.</p>
<p>Thursday 11 May 1916<br/>Went to Rifle Range in morning practically in morning &ndash; little Camp fatigue in afternoon.</p>
<p>Friday 12 May 1916<br/>Turned out 7 a.m. &amp; took part in Divisional Battle movement.</p>
<p>Saturday 13 May 1916<br/>Had charge work erecting Compound at Canteen &ndash; wet.  Things quiet.</p>
<p>Sunday 14 May 1916<br/>Very hot and dusty, got orders to pack up ready to go to Rail Head tomorrow.  Y.M.C.A. night.</p>
<p>Monday 15 May 1916<br/>Left camp 5 a.m. &amp; marched to Rail Head, about 7 miles distant.  My Sec. No. 2 marched another 4 miles &amp; took up duty.</p>
<p>Tuesday 16 May 1916<br/>Stationed "Duntroon".  We are in charge work Hutting, water supply etc. at "Gundagai", "Katoomba", Mt. Kembla &amp; Duntroon.</p>
<p>Wednesday 17 May 1916<br/>Extraordinary heat yesterday &amp; today.  Reported yesterday to 118&deg; in shade in No. 2 Officers tent &amp; today 125&deg;.</p>
<p>Thursday 18 May 1916<br/>Still very hot but not so hot as yesterday.  Working on water reservoir &amp; other camp work.</p>
<p>Friday 19 May 1916<br/>Heat wave has passed over and it is now much cooler.  Working on water conservation scheme.</p>
<p>Saturday 20 May 1916<br/>Same as yesterday.  Nothing fresh reported.</p>
