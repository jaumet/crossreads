<p>A sniff at the briny &amp; a look at the sea was a great change from the surroundings of Cairo. Already I have walked along the sea front and looked out towards the [indecipherable] and the forts reared on every promontory, thos which in 1882 were bombarded by a British fleet.</p>
<p>After dinner Jerom and I will tour round making an excursion midst the sights, of this cosmopolitan town. The people here suggest at once a sea port, the mean whites, male &amp; female, cross ones vision at every step, proclaiming the <strike>business</strike> floating population &amp; the shipping business of their location. More of this anon.</p>
<p>An Australian woman, probably a nurse, &amp; a young officer are seated on a lounge to my left front, each is smoking a cigarette, and sipping from a glass, no doubt both wish that I was in another room, that they might be the sole occupants of this one, which on the first floor is for working in. They will be pleased I must away as I have to report myself at the Hotel Metropole at 7 p.m., then to return here for an evening meal.</p>
<p>For the moment good bye. This will be posted tonight with some other material for you that it may catch the earliest mail for the South.</p>
