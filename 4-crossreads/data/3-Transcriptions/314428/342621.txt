<p>Wednesday May 14.1919. 184<br/>A fine morning.</p>
<p>The reveille was at 6. And breakfast about 7. which was an hour earlier than usual.</p>
<p>The customs boat and the medical boat came alongside about 8.The medical officer came aboard and inspected all hands commencing with the crew and then the S. Australian &amp; Westralian troops on a Deck. As the temperature of every man had to be taken it was a long job.</p>
<p>The South Australians troop with a number of officers &amp; one nurse left the ship in a harbour boat about 11 this morning. The Shropshire blew several blasts on the siren at intervals, and the soldiers on board waved farewells to them.</p>
<p>It has been very warm this afternoon and no breeze.  It was stifling below.  There was no draught owing to the ship not being in motion.</p>
<p>The Shropshire weighed anchor and sailed at 3 o'clock. I was down below when she started, waiting my turn to be paid. All troops were paid this afternoon. This is their final pay on the boat. All men</p>
