<p>11 March 1918 Monday<br/>Maison Lyons Cake Oxford St London&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;L.H.<br/>Raining still, a cake arrived from London isnt it wonderful the Army Postal Service- <strike>2 letters from Dolly also</strike>  Turks being driven back &amp; we hope to be relieved tomorrow, still raining, 4 enemy Taubes over but no bombs-  A Mountain Battery got a shaking yesterday &amp; the men have been passing us all day-  We can see <strike>Jerusalem</strike> behind us quite plainly- it is built on a HILL &amp; we expect to be back that way again soon.<br/>Notwithstanding cold I am fit &amp; well.</p>
