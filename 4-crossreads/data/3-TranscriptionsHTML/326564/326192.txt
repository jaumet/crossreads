The station at Amiens is simply crowded  with traffic, troops munitions, guns, stores, horses &amp; etc coming in &amp; being unloaded as fast as possible, while the outgoing trains are carrying everything of any value out of the city, &amp; they are very wise in this respect nothing like being on the safe side, when we got off the train we marched down to a little park &amp; here all hands had a cup of cocoa previous to moving off to our billet, we then saddled up &amp; set off off <br/>our way from St Roch led through the centre of the city itself, &amp; the one time busy town was as quiet as could be only a handful of people remaining but what were left showed their appreciation of the Aussies in no unmistakable form, I only saw</p>
