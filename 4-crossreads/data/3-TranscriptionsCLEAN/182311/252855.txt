 From Neuve Eglise after rejoining the 38 th  on Messines Hill. 
  BELGIUM . 
  July 25th 1917 . 
   
 My Dear People, 
    "Back at the War".  After my telegram saying that I was staying a further six weeks at Cambridge for the Clare College Staff Course, you will wonder at this.  But this is just what happened. 
    After completing successfully a five weeks preparatory course at Tidworth, the A.I.F. Headquarters sent us on to Cambridge but upon arrival there we found the school full and ourselves unexpected; there was no other course but to ask for further orders which came "Report at London."  Then at Horseferry Road we heard that we were wanted in France and received orders to return by next boat.  What!  No leave?  said we, and it was so, though we made a very dignified request. 
    However, it is our job to fight and not to go taking leave around the pleasant places of Britain; for I had mapped out a very nice tour during the leave that I fully expected to get.  Now I'm back with the Regiment, we have just come down from the trenches for a few days rest, when we will go back again, also on territory recently captured from the enemy. 
    Lately I've taken walks to places of much interest in our neighbourhood.  I'll tell you about them.
