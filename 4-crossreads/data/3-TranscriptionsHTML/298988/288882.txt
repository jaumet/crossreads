<p>May 15th  One of our wounded mules died last night, so we put out to sea to bury him.  On our return we got aground &amp; just at present are endeavouring to get off.  The Veterinary Officer here performs operations on the Mules daily &amp; has got quite a number of shrapnel bullets, pieces of shell etc.  One piece he extracted weighed 6ozs.</p>
<p>1 p.m.  Have managed to get afloat again after two hours work.</p>
<p>May 16th (Sunday)  Today has been a beautiful day, real N.S.W. spring weather.  We went in for a swim this morning &amp; enjoyed it very much.  It gets a bit tiresome knocking around a ship &amp; never touching land, &amp; as it is now six weeks since we were last ashore, we all would like a change.</p>
<p>During the afternoon the headquarters ship (on board which is Sir Ian Hamilton) the S.S. Arcadian, played some music &amp; they have a fine band too.  It made me imagine I was watching a band recital in Wynyard Square.</p>
<p>May 18th. 8 p.m.  We are now bound for Alexandria having said Au Revoir to the Gallipoli Peninsula at 2 p.m.  We are all more pleased than words could convey.  Having had no letters for over a month &amp; as we are running short of provisions we look forward to having both wants, especially the former satisfied.  If I don't get a bundle of letters there will be an awful row.  How our departure was arranged was thus:  Our Captain &amp; the Veterinary Officer</p>
