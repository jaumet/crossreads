A most uninteresting place to be sure despite the fact that the Dervish trenches are still visible, that Martini cartridges bits of shells bullets can still be found there. The usual strip of of arable land fringing the Ismailia Canal supporting the same miserable crowd of fellahin with their mud villages, donkeys, camels. But the once odoriferous unimportant village of Tel-el-Kebir has in the month of January 16 reached a position of high importance. Thousands of troops are encamped on the flat bordering the canal their presence has drawn the more enterprising Egyptians Greeks with their usual wares to the mud city.
