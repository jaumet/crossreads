H.Q. later with bullets through their limbs and bloodstained clothes. Mostly slight. Meantime the Hun commenced a heavy gas bombardment and flooded our dugout with lethal tear gas. Our curtains were soon down but men coming in brought it on their clothes. One of our drivers (Green) was killed and his driverless horses were found at dawn. The gas soon had us choking and sneezing but our curtains kept most of it out until splinters pierced them. Our communications all went early in the fray.
