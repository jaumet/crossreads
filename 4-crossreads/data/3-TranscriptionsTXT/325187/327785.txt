 [Wednesday 9 February 1916] 
 A draft of 40 men arrived today to fill the places of sick men  [Following paragraphs are crossed out] since we left the Aerodrome Camp. The leakage &amp; waste even here is tremendous &amp; a man is lucky to keep fit &amp; well. One man of our field Ambulance died lately of Cerebro Spinal Meningitis I suppose brought from Australia. Mumps &amp; Pneumonia &amp; Measles claim most at present, but as a whole we are a healthy crowd &amp; plenty of good Tucker for we Officers cooked on a Primus stove &ndash; as no wood on the Desert &amp; the issue is all used by the men
