<p>11 May 1918<br/>Saturday<br/>A beautiful fine day Nothing special doing. Went for a stroll down to Barford in the afternoon.</p>
<p>1918 May 12<br/>Sunday<br/>After Ascension</p>
<p>Just the same old Camp Sunday Had a stroll through the woods between our camp and the Dinton Rd in the afternoon Went to a Y.M.C.A. Service in the evening Nice fine weather. Heard today that 2nd Lft Rochester has been killed in France. He was one of our original 1st Coy boy &amp; only [indecipherable]</p>
<p>name="a4712032"></a></p>
<p>[Page 32]<br/>13 May 1918<br/>Monday<br/>Nothing of importance to note. In the evening went to a Concert in Camp. Very good.</p>
<p>1918 May 14<br/>Tuesday<br/>Just going along as usual. Still looking after No. 8. Canteen. Weather very warm.</p>
