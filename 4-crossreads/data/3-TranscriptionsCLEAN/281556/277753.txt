  
 had orders to get what sleep we could. We were alongside old much knocked about trenches & knew not our whereabouts, front or anything else. About one hour later wakened with great fear, fierce bombardment on all sides, guns of all sizes, & as light as day almost by the flash of the guns. I was absolutely afraid to move & curled myself up under great coat & tried to stop trembling from cold & fear as they must have warned us if in any danger. Prisoners passed through gully later.
