Many were tonicked but no notice was taken by the heads and we moved out at 4 P.M. after various farewells had been said by those who had friends seeing them off. One old lady gave tracts to us with words of advice &amp; so on, it was a strange experience altogether this leaving London. As one man said &ndash; he did not wake up until the train moved out. For 5 minutes conversation was brisk after that we all seemed to get sad at once &amp; not a word was spoken for the rest of the journey, truly a sorry crew. At Southampton we boarded the "Donnegal" &amp; at 8 P.M. set off for Havre reaching there about 3 A.M.
