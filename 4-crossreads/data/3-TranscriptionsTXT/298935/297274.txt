At Port Melbourne 1,100 of us embarked on troopship "Clan Macgillivray" No. A.46 and at 3 p.m. we set sail for the great adventure accompanied by the cheers of a tremendous crowd on the pier. Early the next morning we were joined by two other transports, the "Chilka" and the "Hessian".  A third, the "Mashubra", joined us as we passed Freemantle.  The "Hessian" had formerly been a German merchantman. When the seasickness had left the majority of us and life became sweet again the Doctor vaccinated and inoculated against typhus the whole company.
