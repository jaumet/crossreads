Feb.2nd  We went out on a coal stunt at 1.P.M. for 1st Anzac C.T.S.C. & did one load. when we returned we parked up with the amunition section in a very deep muddy position & are now attached to 1st A.S.P.  - We have to be on parade at 7.30 A.M.
 
Memo. The weather has been colder since I returned from Paris. but not too bad & we still have quite a bit of sun. There is a bit of strafing going on at intervals again. -
 
Feb. 3rd  Fritz was over here three times last night & dropped some bombs, but none within two miles of us - To-day has been fine & moderately warm - I worked on the lorry in the morning. In the afternoon I went along to 5th A.S.P. & saw some of the fellows I know.
 
Feb 4th  We went out at 10 A.M. to draw for 1st Anzac C.T.S.C.  I had to have a tow to start my engine - It was a beautiful day just like spring. Joe was told to be ready for his Blighty leave
