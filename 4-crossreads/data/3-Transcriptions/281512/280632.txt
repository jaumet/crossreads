<p>"Fritz" began shelling the batteries immediately on our right &amp; left. The result was rather discomforting as the shells burst only a few hundred paces away, sending up high columns of mud stones &amp; shell fragments. The waiting seemed an Interminable time under these circumstances, but as a fair number of Fritz's shells were "Duds" (failed to explode) we got off Scott free. I however got rather badly burned by my own flashpowder prematurely exploding.</p>
<p>We returned to Camp, thoroughly enjoying the cool moonlight evening &amp; arrived at camp 9.30 p.m. We finished developing at 2.30 AM, but were many times interrupted by the anti-aircraft guns opening fire on the Bosch aeroplanes, which now drop bombs on the neighbouring Towns &amp; villages at every opportunity.</p>
<p>Fortunately we are &frac34; mile from town &amp; not likely to be a mark. The whole night this thing continued, &amp; the explosions of the bombs could be felt for they shook</p>
