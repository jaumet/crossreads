<p>After the Gaza line however there was nothing. The work there on our side was nevertheless particularly brilliant. It was one of those rare shows in which all troops excelled. The Light-Horse were as they have always been &amp; the Yeomanry as they have never been; while the infantry (who really stormed &amp; broke the real line forces) performed superbly &amp; marched in a wonderful manner in the follow-up.</p>
<p>The excellence of the British was <strike>excelled</strike> good for Australia. Our fellows had no opinion of the British before then owing to poor performances earlier in the campaign due to bad staff work &amp; leadership.</p>
<p>Now we are a very happy Imperial family altho' in our hearts we can't quite forgive the [indecipherable] Yeomanry so far behind our men in all the finer points of patrol &amp; for the series of bulldog charges of which they jumped into the class of the world's best cavalry.
