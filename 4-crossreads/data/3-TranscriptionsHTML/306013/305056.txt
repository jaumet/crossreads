<p>hills &amp; dales, past country farms &amp; homesteads towards Salisbury Plains. There we remained till 8 in the evening. Although the air was bitingly cold, &amp; ice <strike>lay</strike> snow lay on every land we were a happy little party. Eight of us were in our box carriage &amp; the fact that we were all strangers in a strange land united us all &amp; as the hours wore on, and we shared our bully-beef &amp; biscuits, we grew more &amp; more contented with our lot &amp; entered deeper &amp; deeper into one another's confidence.</p>
<p>At three in the afternoon we were warmed up through the thoughtfulness of some kind people. We all got out of the train at Exeter &amp; had our water-bottles filled for us with hot tea &amp; each soldier received a bag in which was a fine currant bun &amp; a note;  one of which is enclosed.</p>
<p>At 8 o'clock we left our train at Amesbury &amp; then marched six miles to the A.I.F. Training Camp which is situated at "Rollestone", a part of the celebrated Salisbury Plains, about 15 miles from the city of Salisbury itself. We arrived there at about 10.30</p>
