We went sailing along &amp; then were put into a lock which lowered us down &amp; down until we were on the ocean level &amp; the gates opened &amp; we entered the Pacific ocean. 
 A small boat came alongside &amp; put some fruit &amp; fish on board, that was the last seen by the diggers of the Fruit &amp; fish &amp; while we were coaling at Colon the Marines Band came on board &amp; played for two hours also some American Ladies came on &amp; sang songs &amp; gave round cakes &amp; Fruit to the troops also papers &amp; Books which was very kind of them. 
 We had sports on the voyage &amp; concerts several times also small dances to pass away the time.  When anyone moved about he always had to have his lifebelt with him &amp; now as we were in fairly safe waters the lifebelts were collected &amp; stowed away. 
 We started from England about August the 22nd &amp; arrived at Melbourne on Oct 10th &amp; went to Broadmeadows Camp for one night &amp; came on to Sydney by Train as far as Albury where a spread was laid ready for us &amp; after we had a good feed, we got on a N.S.Wales Ambulance Train &amp; started on our last lap.  we had Breakfast at Moss Vale &amp; then on again &amp; arrived in Sydney on Sunday morning when we got into
