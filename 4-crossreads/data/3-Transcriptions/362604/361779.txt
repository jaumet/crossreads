<p>I went up to Mr. Edmonds.  We had music &amp; chatter until 9 p.m., more supper, &amp; one of the Daughters &amp; myself took the two prentice Girls Home.  To learn their trade which is Stationery or Haby., their mother pays &amp;pound;10 per annum &amp; although the Girls receive no pay, all the Firm find is food, worse than Army tucker, &amp; lodging.  Three years they must serve too.  Just fancy our Shop girls having to do that.  I stayed all night &amp; set off after a nice breakfast to cycle Home.
