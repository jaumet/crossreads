<p>[Transcriber's note:  Concerning the Arabic prayer on Page 5 from a Tomb at Benhasa, there is a desert road connecting the Nile Valley to the area around the oasis known as Zeshzesh. This road is called today Darb El-Benhasa (the Benhasa road)]</p>
<p>NOTHING is to be written on this side except the date and signature of the sender.  Sentences not required may be erased.  <u>If anything else is added the post card will be destroyed.</u></p>
<p>I am quite well.</p>
<p><strike>I have been admitted into hospital<br/>(sick) and am going on well.<br/>(wounded) and hope to be discharged soon.<br/>I am being sent down to the base.</strike></p>
<p>I have received your letter dated 14/4/15<br/><strike>(telegram/parcel)</strike></p>
<p>Letters follow at first opportunity.<br/><strike>I have received no letter from you lately/for a long time.</strike></p>
<p>Signature Only.  Gordon C. Cooper</p>
<p>Date 3.6.1915</p>
<p>(Postage must be prepaid on any letter or post card addressed to the sender of this card.)</p>
