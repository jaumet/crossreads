Engineers had made roads for us and put up sign posts along the way & in the captured village of      When we came to the Batt.  we halted for the night & slept in our clothes. Our camp is in a deep narrow ravine the scene of a German Battery. There are numerous guns here of 4 in calabre and heaps of enemy ammunition & shells. We passed some German cemeteries this evening.
Friday Aug 9. 1918. 1466A beautiful day. Fortunately the weather has kept fine for the push.This ravine leads down to a lagoon where we watered the animals this morning under shell fire. Only about a dozen could go in at once & some of them had narrow escapes. The sides of this ravine is lined with enemy dugouts & camps and there are some dead about. Strange to say I never saw any dead yesterday but we did not stop to examine the trenches.We don't expect to stop here long as this gully is under observation and some Tommies on our left are held up & we are subjected to an enfilading fire from the enemy.
