 and a pick or a shovel each.  We set off slowly in column of four formation but as the fire against us increased we moved into extended order.  The ground we passed over was strewed with daises and big shell cases.  It was a lovely sunny day.  One of those days when you wish to be at peace with all men.  The hill before us appeared to bristle with guns and the flat surface of the ground afforded us no shelter.  Breaking into a run we reached our first line trench in a very fagged condition due to our packs.  We expected a spell here but no it was "Forward, Australians!"  On we went again at a run, flopping down occasionally (not every 25 yds as the text books direct) for a few seconds rest.  I now began to notice our fellows dropping down wounded.
