<p>"Pushevillers"  Here we loaded up the train with the aid of the Infantry, all our wagons tentage etc going on &amp; had a supper of biscuits &amp; bully &amp; tea about 10.30 P.M.  We then boarded  the train which consisted of trucks, everyone being in a happy frame of mind, singing &amp; cracking jokes.  We lay down in our overcoats &amp; although the truck had been lately inhabited by horses I slept right through the night.<br/>Tuesday 31st. July<br/>   Arrived in "Arques" about 9.30 A.M. detrained &amp; received a cup of cocoa from the Y.M.C.A. their sign read "Buckshee cocoa for the troops"  We then marched about six miles to "Eblingham" distant about 6 miles from Hazebrouck" &amp; arrived feeling rather tired &amp; hungry about 1.P.M.   At 2.30 we had dinner which saved our lives &amp; did nothing till teatime.  The village is very scattered</p>
