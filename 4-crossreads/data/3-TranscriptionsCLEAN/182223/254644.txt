  
 Thursday 11 March 1915 Hot weather today. 2 p.m. No letters for me from home.  Rode for one hour midst the cultivated land, & the palm groves & the irrigated land on this side [of] the hill  Left Mena House at 8.30 p.m. for the Heliopolis Camp. Arrived at the Camp at 11 pm 
 Friday 12 March 1915 Up 6 A.M. with Lieut. Col. McGlynn from W. Maitland. Started about 7-30 a.m. on divisional Manouver with Colonel Monash & his staff.  Out all day till 4-30 pm., riding all the time. Home Mena House 10.30 pm bath & bed officers & men still crude but plenty of good & fair officers there. General Maxwell, Birdwood, Godlee [Godley], Bridges &c. 
 Saturday 13 March 1915 Posted letters to  Carrie  the girls & Mollie. Replies to Car, Joseph & Kitty. Wrote reply to one from Doffie. Not out all day. Rode during afternoon through the country. 
 Sunday 14 March 1915 At mass at 7 am. Rode  Visited the Barage across the Nile river about 2 miles below Cairo. It's used for conserving the water, that then may be distributed to the farmers (fellahas) throughout the whole of the Delta. Flood mittigation & water supply are the functions of the barrage. storage of water is thefunction of the Assouan dam. The director of the Barage works showed Colonel Monash, Col. McGlynn, Captain McAuliffe, & Mr Smith over the place, with me.
