<p>Sund. 19th Aug. 1917:-</p>
<p>The last couple of nights have watched the taubes over Hazebrouck. The sky has been lit up with searchlights, flares, star-shells &amp; bursting shrapnel with the boom, boom of anti-aircraft guns. I reckon the old town is doomed unless the Germans can be driven back. Yesterday evening went for a walk over to the next village, a quiet little place called Staples. The distance is 3 kilometres along a very pretty, winding road.</p>
<p>Tuesd. 21st Aug. 1917:-</p>
<p>Yesterday afternoon went to Arques &amp; St Omer with patients (12 or 13 kiloms). Spent a couple of hours in St Omer &ndash; went for a walk through the pretty Public Gardens and also had a good look inside</p>
