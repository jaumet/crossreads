<p>1919<br/>April 3<br/>not publishable.  Another bright specimen was  ?  (I cant think of her name) a girl of about 16 who wore no pants. She would let the troops pick her up, turn her upside down &amp; walk around the Corporals mess with her.  But strangely enough that was the extent of the liberties she allowed them to take.<br/>We went out towards Phillipeville (I think) &amp; then turned north, reaching Marcinelle Haie about 2 pm.  We did not see the Battalion as they had gone by a different route    Marcinelle Haie &ndash; suburb of Charleroi, is a coalmining centre &amp; partly farming.  We pulled up at a farmhouse &amp; unloaded the packs &amp; then sat down &amp; waited.  The lads arrived about 4 pm.  On being told that their billet was in a barn covered with straw, there was great disgust.
