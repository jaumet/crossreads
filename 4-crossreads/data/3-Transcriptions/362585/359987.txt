<p>&amp; they've kept him there ever since.  Just as a motor-car can't go far without juice, a mess won't run without spondulicks so there you are.  To see me inveigling the necessary funds from the pockets of numerous unwilling &amp; blaspheming Generals &amp; Colonels is a sight for the gods.  They daily send frantic wires to Boulogne to know when the money is returning.  Haven't had any letters parcels or papers from home for about a month, but they'll probably come with a rush in due course.</p>
<p>There is a rule in the army which says that sand-bags should never be more than three quarters filled &amp; I think that the same rule should apply to papers.  It looks ever so much better that way than crammed to its fullest capacity.</p>
<p>Give my love to father &amp; Dorothy &amp; remember me very kindly to Catherine.</p>
<p>Your loving son<br/>Keith</p>
