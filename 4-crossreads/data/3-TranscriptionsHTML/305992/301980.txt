<p>position.<br/>2 pm off Malta 40 miles south of the island. 8 Japanese destroyers sent out to relieve the 6 Jap. escort the 1st lot having to call in &amp; obtain oil fuel, whilst the convoy still proceeds without delay. Passed a Swedish steamer painted pre-war colors with their country's colors painted on her side.<br/>5 pm Passed a large convoy of 14 cargo ships escorted by sloops of war distance 5 miles off. Bound for the Suez Cannal.<br/>72nd [74th] Scotch pipes band played various airs for 2 hours, continually marching around the boat deck.<br/>8 pm Scotch concert held in 2nd class saloon. Sung &amp; played splendid. 1000 troops ordered to sleep on the various decks tonight as we are passing through a very dangerous passage south of Sicicly Italy having been warned regards submarines.</p>
<p>May 5<br/>Passed south of Scicly during the night 12 miles of C.   No lighthouses lit on the land &amp; it being a clear night &amp; very low mist no land was sighted. Zig Zagging whilst the moon was up &amp; straight courses whilst the (after moon set.<br/>At 7 am&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;miles NW of C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scicily  3 torpedoe destroyers being ahead scouting. The one nearest 300 yards ahead of the Indarra</p>
