Far away astern of us there are a few grey specks getting ever smaller - the last sight of Australia - 'tis most fitting that the dusk should be creeping on, for it was seven years ago that I saw these same specks of land at the dawn of a glorious day - that was my first of Australia, the land which has been so much to me, which gave me my great chance in life, which I now leave perhaps for ever. 
 Dec: 31 Sea quite calm - usual routine. 
 Jan; 1. 1915 Burial at sea, perhaps the first of many - a brilliant sky above, glorious blue below - we are all lined up on deck - all the fleet is stopped - somewhere, from one of the ships , a body has been cast to the deep; way back in Australia some mother will mourn a son - there is dead silence throughout that huge fleet; just the gentle heaving of the ocean, each man to think of Death as he may. The "Dismiss" is sounded; we all feel relieved to get back to the work of living, though I can't help thinking of the, perhaps not far distant, date when I shall see my comrades dying in hundreds on the field of battle.
