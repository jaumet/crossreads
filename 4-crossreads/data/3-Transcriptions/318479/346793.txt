<p>3 May<br/>Arrive home.  Saw Mrs. Lawler.  Mother and I spend evening at Mr. West's.</p>
<p>4 May<br/>Mother and I went to Mrs. Lawlers.</p>
<p>5 May<br/>Father arrived here at 4 p.m.  I rang up Nance and asked her to meet me at Coota.  Leave Stock for camp.</p>
<p>6 May<br/>Arrive at camp.  Saw Harry Proctor.  Scale into Sydney.</p>
<p>7 May<br/>No parade, wet day.  Scale into Sydney with Harry Proctor.</p>
<p>8 May<br/>Platoon drill.  Get my photo taken.</p>
<p>9 May<br/>No Church parade.  Coy. on duty.  Issued with second uniform.  I am on as Batt. orderly till 10 p.m.</p>
<p>10 May<br/>Get an issue of underclothes and boots.  I go aboard the troop ship with the advance guard.  Lieut. Shawe and Serg. Miller in charge of Advance party.</p>
<p>11 May<br/>Aboard.  Troop ship towed into Woolloomooloo.  Draw blankets and hammocks for "A" Coy.</p>
<p>12 May<br/>Sail at 5 p.m. after a great send-off.</p>
<p>13 May<br/>Receive two letters from home.  Issued with shoes.</p>
<p>14 May<br/>Smooth sea.  Pass a large school of dolphins.</p>
<p>15 May<br/>Sea-sick.  Rough sea.</p>
<p>16 May<br/>Seas coming aboard.</p>
<p>17 May<br/>Measles break out on board.</p>
<p>18 May<br/>15 miles from Albany.</p>
<p>19 May<br/>See the last of Australian coast. Grand concert in which "Blue" Tindall sang:- "Ammunition Van".  Rifle drill and lecture on visual training.</p>
