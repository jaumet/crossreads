1/7/18
Matthews and Dundas left today for England. At 10.30 with Stewart set out again for the line and this time went over the same ground as yesterday save that we kept out of the village. Went up to Diggers Support past the usual bivouacs of the men and had a look round. Not much could be seen from the right but on the left we could see Vaire Wood, sundry direlict aeroplanes and a double telegraph post which are good landmarks. Some fine shells of ours were bursting on the Hun trenches in a wealth of thick wooly smoke and dust. Returned with Colonel Forbes who happened to also be up and came back to firing line B.H.Q.
