In the afternoon we had a tin of metho burning (methylated Spirits issued for putting in radiators to minimise chances of freezing but we used our own discreation as to how we used it & we found that we derived more comfort from it by burning it in an open vessel inside the lorry) & sat round it. I stayed in & read all the evening -
 
Jan 5th  We had to set out our tools & personal kits for inspection by Joe Grim. after that ordeal  was over, Joe & I walked into Bailleul & had tea then we went to see the Dons at the concert hall & walked home after -
 
Memo - The weather has been very soft all this week thoring consistently - Coal & bread are almost unprocurable by the civil population here.
 
Jan 6th  We went to Steenwerck railhead & hauled a load of oats to 10th Brigade dump. Jim drove 21136 -
 
Jan 7th  We did'nt go out to-day. I painted the new lockers which I built in the lorry & it is very cosy. I went to Bailleul  & had a hot bath. also went to ordnance & changed a pr of breeches.
 
Jan 8th  A wild morning. snowing hard and cold. I had to go out on a job. so also did Bud -
