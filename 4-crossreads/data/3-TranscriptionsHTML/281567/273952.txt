<p>we had little other indication of war save numerous captured batteries, dumps  &amp;  the passing of columns of Australian Infantry. The main road was a cloud of flying dust through which could be seen at intervals the vague shapes of motor-lorries, red cross cars, ammunition limbers etc, passing  &amp;  repassing on their way forward. With No 4 Section I was attached to the 12th Batt  &amp;  we went forward  &amp; camped for the night by Harbonnieres. At 6 am I received my operation order &amp; we went forward at 8 to the attack in support to the e11th Batt. We halted to the night between Roseres &amp; Vauvilles  &amp;  at 4A.M. on the morning of the 10th attacked through Crepey Wood  &amp;  took Lihons. He shelled us fairly heavily here &amp;  we were relieved at 12 midnight  &amp;  returned</p>
