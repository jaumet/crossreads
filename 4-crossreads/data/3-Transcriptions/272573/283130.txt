<p>Sept 16th. Sat.<br/>Not much dressing doing.</p>
<p>Still continuing painting the D.R.</p>
<p>The fields look very picturesque at present.</p>
<p>The manner in which the beans are taken up is very interesting. They are pulled up by the the roots in bundles and turned upside down. There they are left for a few day. Then long poles about 3" in diameter and 7 ft long are placed in the ground. To the poles the roots of the beans are fixed and encircling the pole. This leaves the beans outside and as the reason of this procedure is to allow the beans to dry the idea seems to be very good indeed.</p>
<p>Sept 18th. Mon<br/>Owing to an unsuccessful raid this morning we were kept very busy. Evact.</p>
<p>Sept 19th. Tues</p>
<p>The concert party of our unit are getting up a pierrot show. They have been getting special dresses made, and a few of us spent some time making pom. poms. for these dresses.</p>
<p>Sept 20th. Wed.<br/>Very few troops are to be seen on the streets of Estaires</p>
