
<p><u>1917</u></p>
<p>Jan. 1<sup><u>st</u></sup>&nbsp; Another beautiful day, sports were continued, we have only one parade a day on account of the sports. The ships company gave a concert in the evening but I didnt attend it I wrote some letters instead.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan. 2<sup><u>nd</u></sup>&nbsp; Rainy &amp; a very lumpy sea. we all stayed below all day with port holes closed. played 500 in the evening &amp; then had to sleep below.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 3<sup><u>rd</u></sup>&nbsp; Beautiful day &amp; the sea has gone down a lot. We had a death on board &amp; he was lowered over the side with ceremony at 11.A.M.&nbsp; We watched the boxing in the evening when Darkie M<sup>c</sup>Leod fought Morris.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 4<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Very hot day. I sat in the sun all morning perspiring, then had a shower. In the afternoon I lay in the shade &amp; read &quot;The Quaker Girl&quot;&nbsp; We played 500 in the evening.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp; Very hot sun &amp; choppy sea, also strong wind.- on sweeping fatigue in the morning. We sighted land at 6.P.M. &amp; cruised off Durban all night.</p>
