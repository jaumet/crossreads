<p>so far he has been quiet enough. Yesterday &amp; today, I have been lines man, when there are not many shells coming over, the work is very interesting, &amp; it is in the open air, quite a change from telephone work.<br/>This afternoon we were paid again, drew another forty francs.<br/>Sunday Aug. 6 16<br/>We are having a change out of the trenches into reserves, into billets once again. Our billet this time in a filthy farm house some little distance from Fairbark the little village near the firing line. This afternoon I had a look at the church that was knocked to pieces, it certainly is in pieces. &amp; what is left, is in a state of collapse. It had beautiful stained glass windows which are all broken.<br/>This village has a very battered about appearance. This afternoon I sent off an inquiry form about Will &amp; also one to forward on any letters that may be there.<br/>The boches last night brought down one of our planes, <strike>whe</strike> it got its propeller hit I believe, &amp; had had to [oolplane?] down &amp; landed behind our lines</p>
