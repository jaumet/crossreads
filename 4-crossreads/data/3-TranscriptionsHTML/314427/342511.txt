<p>Thursday March 27. 1919   137<br/>Cloudy &amp; rather windy. Yesterday I paraded to the dentist to get a new tooth on my plate &amp; today to the doctor to get a pair of light boots. I have finished making security handles for my two kit bags. I have only the locks to  buy.</p>
<p>I finished reading today a very interesting &amp; instructive book by C.W.Salelby M.D. F.R.S. on "The progress of Eugenics.  Eugenics is the new science which deals with nature and nurture or the betterement of the human races.</p>
<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows]  I sent a post card to Mrs. [indecipherable] Gibson telling her that I am leaving here by the Shropshire early in April. [end of shorthand]</p>
<p>Friday March 28. 1919.   138<br/>Fine day.<br/>I had a warm bath early this morning.<br/>At the Q.M. store I changed my service boots for a pair of very light tan boots, on the doctors recommendation. Cpt. R.J. Vercoe</p>
