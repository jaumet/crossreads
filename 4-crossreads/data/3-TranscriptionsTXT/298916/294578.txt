Telefunken station on this floor so it was decided to operate this station. The class was distributed around the set in the form of a semi-circle & the Marconi operator in charge proceeded to explain how to start the alternator.
Each & in turn then started the alternator & numerous fuses were blown by unexperienced handling. At times the machinery threatened to tear itself to pieces as they run at a very high speed - about 1800 revs per min. This was the extent of the instruction received & left a lot to be learned by those who had never seen a wireless station.
Of course our friend Mr Knowall was there amongst the operators & gave his advice etc. etc. which was altogether wrong at times, & threatened to ruin any wireless set that was treated as he advised.Communication between the different branches of Marconi House, is carried on by a Morse Sounder & of course anything being sent by this instrument was open to be read by those who could.It was after five weeks in the class that the long waited for message was heard on the sounder
