Through a telescope could be seen the Jap sailors in white uniforms working like trojans &amp; their officers were magnificently dressed &amp; were here, there, &amp; all over the place. Both cruisers had been scouting around the Pacific for two months &amp; were rather dirty looking &amp; rusty, especially so were her torpedoe nets. Soon after her arrival, an oil tank steamer came into Hobart to fuel the Ibuki &amp; two colliers coaled Minnetau which was the British cruiser's name.<br/>During the afternoon the Second officer, Military Doctor - Captain McKay Teddy &amp; self went sailing in the cutter &amp; when out about an hour got "becalmed" &amp; could not move. Later on we sailed round the Ibuki &amp; took some photos &amp; once when a steam pinnace came our way we thought we were to be arrested as spies. The Ibuki had some large guns &ndash; 12 inch &amp; when close alongside, it could be noticed how every available space is utilised without crowding. A beautiful little steam pinnace lay snug against her funnels &amp; her wireless station was situated aft on the after deck.</p>
