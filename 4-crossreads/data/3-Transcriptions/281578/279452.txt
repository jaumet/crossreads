<p>&amp; it's alright for us. The funeral was only held today for the man killed by the bomb last Friday, &amp; all the village turned out, dressed up &amp; clean looking for a change. In front marched the choir boys dressed in red cloaks &amp; caps, then the priest &amp; pall bearers, &amp; men dressed up in tall hats, &amp; behind them the women in deep mourning, a dismal procession. There are some rotters amongst our chaps, two shots rang out today, fellows firing at fowls, &amp; two old Chows came to the orderly room &amp; said "Australian soldier stealee all their money" they grabbed their purses &amp; bolted with 57 francs, all the Chinks owned. All the soldiers are "broke" but I hear pay will be brought here tomorrow. Had a bonza sleep tonight, a chap in the orderly room had a big double bed in a house next door &amp; I slept in it too &ndash; tres bon it was after the barn, lovely &amp; soft &amp; warm. Tuesday 16 April A military dentist is in the village, &amp; I got a tooth filled that broke the other day. Merrett &amp; I had a look at a flour mill this morning, the machinery was all worked by a water wheel, very primitive, but very effective &amp; simple &ndash; marvellous, the different uses water</p>
