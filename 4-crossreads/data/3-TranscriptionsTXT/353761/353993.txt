Rest seems to be the need of the majority of the men here, especially of the Australians who have had a long spell in a bone rattling train and have seen all they care to see for the day. As a rule they potter about and dig up whatever interest a place may be capable of producing but now hardly a man of even the wakeful amongst them is interested in anything. Scarcely a man of them lifts his head to gaze at the spectacle of two German officers being brought along by an armed escort from a more forward part of the dock, or at the batch of German Red Cross prisoners following in their wake.
