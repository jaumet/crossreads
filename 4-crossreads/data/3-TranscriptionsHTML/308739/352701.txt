<p>Sunday 30 April 1916<br/>Posted letter Home.  4 Turks supposed to be seen by our protrol early this morning.  Officers further investigating.  Major Foster inspecting our Camp &amp;amp; Outposts.  Message as usual received from Air O Plane re position of Enemy.<br/>General Chavel &amp;amp; Gen. Cheater [possibly with Brig. R. inspecting Camp &amp;amp; surrounding Country.  Romani &amp;amp; Qualia &amp;amp; Kalia were visited after which the Staff met the 5th A.L.H. protrol who conducted them to Dunidar.<br/>The message is encased in a specially prepared cloth pocket, which is weighted.  Streamers of colored cloth Balances the Bag which rapidly drops from the plane.</p>
<p>Monday 1 May 1916<br/>Left Camp for Cairo with Officers.  Kits &amp;amp; other equipment laying at Rail Head to place same at Cooks.  Arrived Cairo 12 midnight stayed at Anzac Hostel.<br/>Col. Fuller with Brig. Ryrie out all day with General Cheavel  &amp;amp; Gen. Cheater of N. Zealanders around Karta.<br/>Aus. Mail in, got letter from Home.  The Brigade sends Orderleys from the different Regiments to R.H. &amp;amp; they sort the letters before sending them to the different units.</p>
<p>Tuesday 2 May 1916<br/>Spent all Day in Cairo storing Officers Kits, &amp;amp; buying eatables, etc.<br/>Saw Mrs. Chisholm at Shepards.  Met Lieu. Black at National Hotel, left by train for Cantara at 6-15 p.m. arriving 9-45 p.m., got Cases of Stuff to Siding over the Canal . Train left for Rail Head 12-20 a.m. midnight.<br/>At Railhead every preparation is being made against an attack.  Trenches &amp;amp; entanglements are very much in evidence.  A Division of Infantry occupy the firing line with New Zealanders as supports.  The R.E. are in charge of the building of Line.</p>
<p>Wednesday 3 May 1916<br/>Arrived at Rail Head 4-30 a.m. from Kantara, secured Camels &amp;amp; left for Romani 7-20 a.m., arriving 10 a.m.<br/>Reports that one of our Air O planes had to descend, &amp;amp; all men are to carefully watch for any signs of the Pilot.<br/>Major Darrel arrived to-day to find equipment that was left behind when His Regiment evacuated this Camp.<br/>Lieu. Chisholm laid up with malaria in Camp.<br/>Reports that Turks concentrating at different points in the Canal Zone.</p>
