Two looked very nice, my French combined with the little English they knew, enabled us to have a talk & laugh for a few minutes before they went. Slept in the barn fairly "comfy"".  Sunday 14 April Up at 6.30, took the Brigade sick along to the Divisional orderly corporal, & at 7a.m my day's job was done. A chap & I went to church in morning to see what a French service is like, & it is very novel to us. The choir boys wore a red cloak & small red cap, & the priest rattled out the sermon in French, too fast to understand it, though he was a good speaker. A sad part was the sight of almost every woman in mourning, nearly all wore deep black & some had long black veils nearly to the ground. It was a long service, plenty of chanting. We had to watch the others to know when we stood up, or knelt or sat down. It was a queer way the collection was taken, the priest went round with a little girl, he carried a silver cup & the girl a little bag, he passed the cup along & I noticed it only contained coppers, & I was surprised when the girl poked the bag along too, but not many put coins in the bag.
