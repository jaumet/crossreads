<p>with the buildings. It was nice to be off the chain for the day, &amp; to see a decent town &amp; some women once again. There was a very pretty French girl, dark, 19 she told us, where we had dinner. She had picked up English well, &amp; her quaint way of expressing herself in English language, added to her charm. It was after 9.30 before all the chaps turned up at the motor lorries to go home, &amp; they were all full, &amp; carried sundry bottles of vin blanc, which were emptied on the journey, in between songs. We got home, very tired, at 1 a.m.  Monday 22 July Up at 6 a.m. Races were held today, near Allonville. Our Company were marched there, about 12 kilos, &amp; being a hot, muggy day we were knocked up when we got there. Two chaps &amp; I looked at one race, had a bit of dinner, &amp; then waited on the road for a lift of some sort to come home. I was too weary after yesterday at Abbeville, &amp; the walk, to stop there, &amp; there were no conveniences like a big meeting at Randwick. They were all army horses &amp; army riders; a very sad accident happened in the first race, two horses fell down, &amp; two officers, both captains were killed. We got a lift home in a wagon of the Australian engineers. Got a bonza mail tonight, letters from Mone, Elsie, Will, Boo &amp; Bon Tuesday 23 July Had to go down to Bn.
