
Thursday 24 May 1917.
Now well out into the open sea and nearing the "danger zone". A very calm sea today hardly a ripple on the water & a fresh breeze blowing. (11 Knots an hr today.) Awnings spread today. Got into shorts and put away woollen clothing. The opening concert on board ship this afternoon (Empire Day)
False "fire alarms" at 2.15pm.
Absolutely no light of any description after dusk from now on
Cruiser on alert now. Tonight sea is "as smooth as glass".
Compulsory washing parade in morng.
Cake cigarettes, lollies & apples issued by Y.M.C.A to all on board.
A scare last this morning at 3 a.m. Cruiser prepared for action when we came acrosss a sailing boat that altered her course quickly & put on speed.
