Our train accommodation varied. The men were mostly in 3rd class - 6 to 8 in an uncomfortable box carriage, while the Sergts & the majority of the officers & the few nurses that were with us travelled 2nd class. In our compartment there were only three - one chap "dossed" on the floor and the other two of us had a whole seat each: not too bad at all.At 5.30 next morning we passed through Lyon with its wide streets & river spanned by many bridges.
