<p>Ginger Allan is in the thick of it now. He was right through the Bullecourt stunt without a scratch, whilst I was ignominiously knocked flat a week or so before the first onslaught. His brother has sent me two letters &amp; two telegrams within a week, the former explaining why he was coming up to London &amp; the latter why he wasn't.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<strike>Whi</strike> Wishing to seem officious &amp; a war-warn veteran, I reproached him roundly for being no more than a private. If my well-meaning advice has any effect, or if short-sighted authorities can see the right stuff when it is before their very eyes, he will go to a school, which will teach him nothing, but may result in the alteration of his uniform.</p>
