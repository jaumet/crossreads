<p>C/o Medical Dept:-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;R 14.5.17<br/>Headquarters Staff<br/>A.I.F. Offices<br/>130 Horseferry Rd.<br/>London Eng.<br/>Monday 12/3/17</p>
<p>My dearest Father,<br/>Since I last wrote  about 10 days ago, I have received 3 letters from home- two from you dated 8th &amp; 14th of Jany, &amp; one from Ol dated 20th of December, for which I thank you both very much.  This is the first home news to hand since the 13th of Feby (exactly a month ago.  None of your letters between the 10th of Decr. &amp; the 8th of Jany. have reached me, &amp; altho I have written to every possible place I can think of, very little <strike>of it</strike> mail has been reaching me since I left France.<br/>In my letter to you on 2nd inst: I explained how I came to be transferred to H.Q. London.  It is purely a temporary job, &amp; I may get imsheed tomorrow as there is a Medical Board sitting and I believe they are combing the Staff out pretty carefully.<br/>I can't understand that cable at all "Progress excellent no further cables Dec 14th Waterhouse."  I know for a fact that <u>I </u> didn't send it &amp; can't for the life of me think who did.  As I have explained thoroughly before, I was operated on at Amiens in France on November 25th &amp; sent to the 1st L.G.H. on Decr 11th.<br/>I have already acknowledged the following amounts cabled for through the Agent General:- &pound;5 (about Jany 4th) &pound;5 (about 23rd Jany) &amp; &pound;8 (about 23rd Feby).<br/>I am very very glad to have the operation over &amp; done with &amp; I feel as fit as ever I did, although</p>
