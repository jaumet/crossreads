<p>THE MILITARY GOVERNMENT OF NEW GUINEA.<br />ADMINISTRATION ORDER No. 1<br />Colonel W. HOLMES, D.S.O.,V.D. Brigadier Commanding, 11th September 1914</p>
<p>1. All inhabitants are to submit to the directions of the Officers of the occupying force.</p>
<p>2.Inhabitants are forbidden to be out of doors any night between the hours of 10 p.m. and 6 a.m. without special permits.</p>
<p>3. Inhabitants are forbidden to hold or attend meetings.</p>
<p>4. No newspaper, circular, or printed matter is to be printed, published or issued without permission.</p>
<p>5. No spirituous or intoxicating liquor shall be manufactured or sold without permission.</p>
<p>6. Descriptions of all privately owned boats and vehicles are to be handed in at once to the Provost Marshal of the occupying force.</p>
<p>7. It is forbidden to injure or cut the telegraph or telephone lines.&nbsp; If the telegraph or telephone lines are injured and the offender cannot be discovered a fine will be imposed on the inhabitants of the neighbourhood where the damage was inflicted.</p>
<p>(Sgd) Francis Heritage<br />Major<br />Brigade Major<br />&nbsp;</p>
