<p>Of Red Cross goods and medical comforts, an enormous quantity is kept in stock. Pyjamas, socks, shirts, hats, mufflers, Convalescent suits, ties, handkerchiefs, razors &amp; strops, tooth-hair-nail &amp; clothes brushes, combs, tooth-paste and soaps are in abundance and are issued to any patient upon approval being given by the sister-in-charge of the ward wherein he is.</p>
<p>As, of course, the patients cannot all have similar food, a diet sheet has been prepared and 5 different courses can be had, the medical officers deciding on which the man is to <strike>have</strike> be fed. Arrowroot, sago, custards, milk foods, sterilised milk, biscuits, cocoa, beef-tea, chicken, tapioca, rice and tinned fruits comprise the special diet list and they are in most cases prepared in the wards by orderlies only the ordinary diet being drawn from the kitchen.</p>
<p>In every other respect as well as those mentioned the ship is conducted and managed admirably, and Australians at home will be proud indeed when they see her returning with a batch of unfortunate wounded who have done their bit towards helping the Motherland at this critical juncture.</p>
