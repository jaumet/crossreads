
22bombed.
The 2 pm train which has a staff of 5 officials on engines gets as far as Bonlieu & all its passengers transfer to our train. In the carriage I occupy there were 3 Railwaymen & they are like monkeys. They find some netting in the carriage & they tear it all down. Still they are courteous to me & finding a warmer carriage invite me to accompany them & to share their food They were very rough but hospital to a stranger nevertheless.
After Bonlieu the carriage is full beyond even standing capacity but this worries no one & eventually I reach Bruxelles at 6 pm. Re my luggage - there is some dispute but as I am a soldier it passes off I then look for RTO but cannot find one only a Belgian Colonel who directs me to Cafe deil'Ete' near [Bouise] for all information.
On arrival I find that this is released prisoners depot in charge of a Scottish officer. He outlines a plan of tour for me & the best way of spending evening. Apparently I can still get in at the Opera & [indecipherable] I go without dinner or lodging. Opera is too good to miss. On way I get my hair brushed which costs me f50 & buy a little piece of bath brick alleged to be soap for 3 fr.
