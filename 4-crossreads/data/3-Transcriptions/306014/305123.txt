<p>Gas is a very potent opponent in the field unless full preventative measures are take; if such measures are taken it can do no harm.</p>
<p>Chlorine seems to be the most powerful gas used. It is packed in liquid form in large metal cylinders and is also used in "gas-shells". Chlorine is so strong &amp; poisonous that even when mixed with air in the proportion 1/10,000 it proves fatal if inhaled. Thus with a fair breeze it will kill 10 miles behind the point of attack &amp; its effects have been felt up to fifteen miles away.</p>
<p>To nullify gas effects helmets or box respirators are worn. The helmet is made of a double thickness of flannelette with eye-glasses &amp; mouthpiece, the latter being provided with a valve. The helmet is soaked in certain chemicals</p>
