<p>It so happen to-day that as one of the wounded men was lying on a stretcher having his wounds attended he reminded us of some of the humorous remarks made only a few days ago. As he said himself little did he think that he would actually be in hospital and that in his case at least "a true word was said in jest".</p>
<p>He said that a shell had landed in amongst a dozen of them and some had been killed.</p>
<p>July 17th. Mon.<br/>Have had quite a busy time lately. Feeling quite fatigued. Have been on from very early morning till late at night.</p>
<p>C.N. has received a telegram that his brother had been killed at the front.</p>
<p>News of splendid advances having been made by the British troops.</p>
<p>A great deal of anesthetics are being given in the D.R. New dressing rooms are being opened in order to cope with the rushes which have been our experience lately.</p>
<p>July 19th. Wednesday.</p>
<p>Very cold during the night</p>
<p>Heavy bombardment all day long.</p>
<p>Evacuation this morning. Things pretty quiet. It is said that a large number of our casualties have been caused by short dropping by our own artillery.</p>
