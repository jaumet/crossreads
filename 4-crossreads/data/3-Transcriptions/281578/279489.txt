<p>was dying for a drink, asked several chaps but their water bottles were empty also, &amp; they were hungry &amp; thirsty. No one knew where to find the cooker in the dark, but the poor platoon sergt &amp; officer found 'em alright, &amp; I just devoured the nice hot stew &amp; tea &ndash; it was like new life. I yarned to the two chaps in my dug-out then &amp; we went to sleep soon after. Just as we got here, 3 shells nearly hit us. It put the wind up us, tired as we were, &amp; it's the queer part of war to think that in the front line no shells troubled us, &amp; now we are a couple of miles back we nearly got whacked. Just about here are several of our guns, &amp; he must be after them. Sunday 5 May It was a perfect treat to have a wash today, the first for a week, &amp; it was nice to have a clean face after a week's growth. The chaps are looking less like bushrangers today. Had a lovely loaf all day &ndash; I see the usual sight of dead horses lying about. In the distance is the city of Amiens which the Germans are anxious to take, but I think they are about held now. In the afternoon I went to find B Coy's dugouts in a sunken road near us, &amp; was delighted to find Boo well after his long week in the line, also Eldridge who was as jolly as ever. While I was down there, I saw Stone give himself a nasty wound.
