
<p>May 8<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;Left Park at 5.30 A.M. for Ailly-S. Somme on supplies to the brigade at Freshencourt &nbsp;the . Lord Strathcona&#39;s Horse, Royal Canadian Dragoons &amp; Fort Garry Horse (Canadian Cavalry Brigade) were standing to all day expecting action, but there was nothing doing, they are camped at Fr&eacute;chencourt. When we got back to Park we had to change a wheel as our tyre was done.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 9<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left the park at 6.A.M. for Flesselles railhead &amp; took Amunition to a dump at &quot;Daours&quot; on the Corbie road - We saw one of our planes bring a Fritz machine down over Amiens - the road was very rough.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 10<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left Park at 8.A.M. for Flesselles &amp; took a load of Amunition to Daours dump - We watched some French naval guns in Action just outside Daours.The weather is much better now but not too warm - We got back to Park at 5.30.P.M.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>May 11<u><sup>th</sup></u>&nbsp;&nbsp;We left park at 6.A.M. for Ailly-S.S. on Supplies to Fr&eacute;chencourt - We brought a load of wool back to Ailly.S.S. in the afternoon - got home at 5.P.M.</p>
