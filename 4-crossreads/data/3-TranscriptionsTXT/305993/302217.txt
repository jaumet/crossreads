 Sunday, Aug. 10 Still coaling, picnics, concerts etc. organised &amp; all having a good time ashore.  The labor at South Africa very uneasy, strikes &amp; lockouts amongst the white workmen all over the Country. 
 Mon. 11.1919 2 p.m. coaling finished &amp; washed the troopship.  In a dreadful coalic state everyone full of coal dust.  Orders to proceed to sea at 7 a.m. tomorrow &amp; all troops ordered to return to the ship at 10.30 p.m. tonight. 
 Tues. Aug. 12 At 6 a.m. all troops mustered on the wharf &amp; counted, ship searched for stowaways, one soldier (single) found missing &amp; (a doubtful character).  At 7 a.m. left Port Natal for Fremantle, West Australia.  At 9 a.m. it commenced to blow hard &amp; increased to a strong gale &amp; heavy sea.  The transport rouling [rolling] heavily &amp; the best part of our crowd sea sick &amp; feeling very miserable. 
 Wed. Aug. 13 Strong gale &amp; very heavy sea &amp; rolling fearfully.  At 1 a.m. hove too put the transport head to sea &amp; eased the engines to slow, the women having suffered bad through the weather.  At 11 a.m. weather moderating &amp; turned around again &amp; made our proper course.  At noon we were only 180 miles from Port Natal &amp; averaged 6.4 miles per hour since we left, 28 hours steaming.
