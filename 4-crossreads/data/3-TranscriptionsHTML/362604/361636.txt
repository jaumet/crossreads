<p>Bulford<br/>Sunday<br/>31.3.18</p>
<p>Dear Dad, Mum &amp; George,</p>
<p>Here it is Sunday again &amp; I am doing as you are probably doing, writing letters.</p>
<p>This week has passed off very quietly.  On Good Friday I rode into Salisbury to Mr. Edmonds place.  It was a very windy day &amp; I had a rough passage on the way in.  But I left Mr. Edmonds at 9.25 p.m. &amp; at "Lights Out", 10.15 p.m., I was sitting up in bed eating bread, butter &amp; peach jam.  I am still as fond of jam as ever.  I get some good Lemon &amp; Melon, Aussie Jam sometimes.  I have much better meals than Ellis.  I will tell you how I get 'em when Home again.  I don't want any censor to find out.  It has rained nearly all day on three days this week.  The country was badly in need of it too.  I have just received a letter from Marie in which she states that Aunt Jennie's Husband, George Kewley has been killed in action.  She is left with one little Boy.  It is up to Harry to offer Her a Home.  But He is more likely to suggest sending Her to the work-House.  Jennie is</p>
