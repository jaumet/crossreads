<p>22 November 1916<br/>Wednesday</p>
<p>Off duty at 8.30 a.m. Sleep all morning. In aft. B &amp; self visit Tony at Niaulte Baths where he has been for past 4 weeks. 2 miles distant  Find him in all his glory dealing out clean clothes to men. Delighted to see us. Stay all aft. &amp; he treats us to high tea, 3 meats cold roast Pork &amp; tongue, Toast &amp; butter &amp; &amp; such a feed we never had for days &amp; weeks. Do full justice much to [indecipherable] pleasure. Then we have hot bath another luxury; &amp; he fits us out with complete set of brand new  warm underclothed. We feel new creatures. Good to be rid of chats for while. Leave for our camp about 7pm.  One of the densest fogs. Can't see 2 yds in front of us. follow the railway &amp;amp arrive to time.  Go on duty at 8.30 p.m.</p>
