<p>Saturday 28 [27] July 1917 [1918]<br/>Am doing better in the sleeping line these days, have about 4&frac12; to 5 hrs. daily.  Heat is intense &amp; one perspires profusely.<br/>One pt., Laing, has rash 'tween loins, known as "Doby rash" &amp; said to be contracted from camels, very irritable, calomile lotion eases somewhat.<br/>Pt. Martin, A.A.S.C. [Australian Army Service Corps] supposed mal. of stomach is on olive oil [indecipherable] hrly.  South Aust. man going on 1st boat possible.<br/>Wrote letters to Ina Palmer, Mrs. Dudgeon &amp; May Witrels re her son.</p>
