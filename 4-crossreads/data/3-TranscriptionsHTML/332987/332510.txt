<p>Left Colombo on night of 4-11-1918 at 10 p.m.  Took on board, Potatoes, fruit, meat, coal and water.  A big roll on, ship very light.</p>
<p>11-11-1918<br/>Recieved news by wireless that the armistice was signed.  No excitement on board, just read in a casual sort of way.</p>
<p>16-11-1918<br/>Troops on board all fitted with new clothing.  Weather very windy, sea rough.</p>
<p>17-11-1918, Sunday<br/>Arrived at Freemantle  at 2 a.m.  Ship quarantined, some cases of Spanish Infleunza on board.  Very rough last night.  Sent a telegram home.</p>
<p>18-11-1918, Monday<br/>The West Australians went ashore to quarantine at 11-30 a.m.  We moved out from Freemantle at 3-30 p.m.  Sea rough 1,874 miles to Melbourne.</p>
<p>24-11-1918<br/>Arrived at Portsea, 7-30 p.m.  In harbour</p>
