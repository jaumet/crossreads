 The men all held a meeting about 5 p.m. to discuss the matter and decide what they should do.  It rained heavily all the while but the meeting lasted a full hour out in the open near the Stadium.  After the meeting a deputation of  5  representatives of all Coys. waited on the C.O. to put their case before him.  I asked the C.O. what their case was and he told me that the men took a reasonable view of the whole affair but that they did not wish to be broken up without a  protest  making a protest.
