<p>5<br/>Is only gorse and heather round us.  Thick, wild and tangling gorse, and dark browned heather of a vanished season.</p>
<p>We are on the moorlands.</p>
<p>Soon we pass a cottage almost hidden in a little belt of pines and gorse.</p>
<p>Falcon is seen on the roadside. One of the soldiers slips out from the ranks and catches it.</p>
<p>There is no fight shown by the frightened bird.  It seems ill and suffering.</p>
<p>Almost every one of those noble soldiers expressing filthy speech some frightful method of putting the defenceless creature to death.</p>
<p>Not one notices the beauty of its feathers.  The pitiful terror of its aching eyes; nor its helplessness</p>
<p>Not one seems to understand that it is a work of God as much as we ourselves.</p>
<p>A few calm words guardedly spoken induce them to let it go.</p>
<p>Did they notice the grace of its short flight.</p>
<p>Not they.</p>
