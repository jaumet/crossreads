<p>Sunday 11 June 1916<br/>Wrote Home.<br/>Mr. Larcombe is again in Camp with some more extras for the Regiment.<br/>Church parade is to be held tonight instead of morning owing to the Heat.<br/>I went to Cantara with Major Bruxner &amp;amp; also to Transport Depot.<br/>Taube (Enemy's) dropped 7 Bombs killing two men &amp;amp; wounding &amp;amp; killing a number of Horses at Kantara.  Also opened Fire on the Camps whilst returning only doing slight damage.  Our Planes went in pursuit.<br/>A large Plane is now at Kantara equipped for 3 men, one to Pilot, 2nd to use Machine Gun, 3rd to operate the 1 pounder.</p>
<p>Monday 12 June 1916<br/>Major Bruxner, Pardre, went to Cairo tonight on Holidays.<br/>Serg. Ross before the O.C. for absenting Himself without leave 4 days.<br/>News of the Russians doing good work.<br/>Col. Fuller arrived from Cairo at 11 p.m., came from Kantara by Brigade Motor Car.</p>
<p>Tuesday 13 June 1916<br/>Capt. Ferguson returned from Cairo the afternoon.<br/>Major White who was 2nd in Command has been compelled to return to Aus. &amp;amp; leaves Friday next.<br/>Col. Fuller at Brigade tonight.<br/>Another alarm given of Enemies plane in vicinity of Camp.<br/>Wet &amp;amp; Dry Canteen is now a paying concern, under Lieu. Britton &amp;amp; is much appreciated by the men.</p>
<p>Wednesday 14 June 1916<br/>Capt. Stracker took lunch with the Col. to-day.<br/>Capt. Darcey returned from Cairo &amp;amp; paid visit to Col. Fuller.<br/>Orders out that a Camel Corps is being formed, &amp;amp; Officers &amp;amp; N.C.O. are required.<br/>Lieu. Chisholm went to Port Said in connection with Charge against a Trooper who was Drunk.<br/>Col. Fuller up to 7th Reg. tonight.</p>
