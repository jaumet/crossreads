<p>Monday July 15. 1918. 1441</p>
<p>A fine day but cloudy. We have about a mile to ride every morning taking the mules and horses to water at a trouch in the village of Daours.<br/>There was an inspection of the Transports today in drill order. These inspections will be every week in future until further orders.<br/>This afternoon I saw the enemy shoot down one of our baloons in flames by one of his planes. I brazed 8 serviette rings made out of copper driving bands for some of the Transports. Late this evening some</p>
<p>of the Battalion officers put together a German machine gun and fired a large number of rounds into a trench. The cartridges kept jamming because the web belt was defective.<br/>I received a tin of Havelock tobacco from my sister.</p>
<p>Tuesday July 16. 1918. 1442</p>
<p>There was some very heavy rain, thunder and lightning before revellie this morning &amp; the water came through some of the tents. The ground was covered with water</p>
<p>Wednesday July 17. 1918. 1443</p>
<p>Very close &amp; cloudy with occasional light showers. The heat is very muggy.<br/>This past few days I have been repairing machine gun carriers.</p>
