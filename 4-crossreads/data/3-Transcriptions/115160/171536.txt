<p>camp. &nbsp;We had to go about 15 kilos to draw but it was not unpleasant for the roads are like billiard tables away from where the traffic is. &nbsp;The clerks used to proceed to and fro in the car which added to the pleasantness. &nbsp;France is really pretty where war has not touched it, with its low hills, cultivated wee plots of crops, its small forests, and old-time villages. &nbsp;It was lovely whisking along the roads in the car.</p>
<p>But we&#39;ve been very busy since we&#39;ve been on this stunt, due to the exigencies of the advance. &nbsp;It took just on 12 hours to reach our own lorries in a lorry, where we put in the night in a cellar. &nbsp;Next day we pushed on to here and have been our own bosses since. &nbsp;We have seen some of the liberated French peasants coming down from their villages to places of greater safety.</p>
<p>German signs and German work are evident everywhere about here.</p>
