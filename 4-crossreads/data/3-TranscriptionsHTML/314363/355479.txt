We were relieved by the 56th a couple of nights ago &amp; just now are in the road again, but a little higher up.<br/>This afternoon I went into Fouilloy &amp; took a look at some of the houses. They are in a dreadful state. The civilians evacuated in such a hurry that they could not take away their belongings, now they are going to ruin.<br/>All the rooms <strike>are</strike> have been turned upside down, clothes &amp; furniture, thrown every where.<br/>I saw several pianos &amp; a pianola. I got a few books to read. Do not know when we are going to be relieved, but the boys are getting very tired of the line.<br/>4.5.18<br/>The night before last part of the 4th Div. &amp; the French hopped over, but the attack failed I am told we put up a very poor bombard, &amp; the bosche put up a good one.<br/>I am sending a couple of books over to England one on Botany &amp; the other on Zoology.
