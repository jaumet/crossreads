<p>September, 1915<br/>Wednesday 8<br/>We are a most jolly crowd, it is the pleasure of clean sheets beds, good Tucker, Baker Barber, deli make me forget all troubles.<br/>Cutler came in to see me today he still drives Brigade car. He is a good boy <strike>&amp; has only knocked over a small number of niggers &ndash; The latter do not love us, - &amp; we are in an unfriendly country &amp; it is fear not respect that keeps them under &ndash; not to mention Motors</strike>. At times one feels a bit lonely away from the Regiment &amp; all mates in Hospital out here one is only a Number. is of no interest unless dangerously ill or wounded.</p>
