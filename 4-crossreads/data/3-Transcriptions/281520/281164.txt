<p>Camp near Le Barque<br/>23/3/17<br/>Fine but cold wind.  Morning programme of drill and training.  The NCO's were taken apart under one officer per company.  Saw &amp; questioned the NCO's in the reinforcements who joined up last night.  After lunch the General &amp; Bill came down and told us that there were some more villages to be taken, to be ready to move forward and that we are going out for a month rest. Horses brought round and a few of us rode to Pozieres to put a cross up in memory of Mackay Curnow &amp; others.<br/>A 5 mile ride through the tangled ruins of Le Sars.</p>
<p>Many tanks sprawling uselessly where shells had knocked them out and salvage gangs were at work on them.  A few aeroplanes also lying about.  Between the Sugar Factory &ndash; a heap of twisted bullet-punctured and wrecked machinery &ndash; and the head of Pozieres village the ground is full of the largest shell holes I have seen.  The 5.9's seem to have just poured in there and ripped the place to bits.
