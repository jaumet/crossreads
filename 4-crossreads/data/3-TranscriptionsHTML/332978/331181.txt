<p>102 December 27th<br/>It was simply delightful to be in conversation with Eng. people again. At some of the Stations bands played popular airs, commencing with our national anthem. It was a great time, we seemed to be able to forget all of a sudden the bad times we had endured.</p>
<p>December 28th<br/>Arrived at Bern at 2 am when we were welcomed by the British Ambassador Lord Newton &amp; other Swiss authorities, we had an excellent meal, plenty of varied drinks. At such an early hour, it was surprising to see so many smiling faces &amp; all doing something for our comfort. It was a grand reception &amp; an occasion that one cannot forget &ndash;</p>
<p>Everybody sympathised with us, &amp; expressed how pleased they were that we were at last released. <strike>Were entrained again at 4 am</strike> we were split up having to catch different trains, all for Montreux entrained again at 4 am &amp; arrived at 9 am &amp; all along the line, we were cheered &amp; cheered. It was a thrilling experience. We never felt so happy since being in captivity &ndash;</p>
<p>On arrival at Montreux we were accorded a civic reception &amp; welcomed to Switzerland. Some very eloquent but flattering speeches were made by the Swiss authorities &amp; in the absence of General Williams who is very bad, Colonel Anderson, responded very nicely &amp; thanked the Swiss people for the very hearty welcome accorded us. Swiss military Band was in attendance &amp; played all the national anthems.</p>
<p>It was here that we made our debeau numerous sympathetic friends introduced themselves &amp; extended invitations to visit them &amp;c. <strike>It was indeed a happy day for all of</strike> After we had dined we were allocated to our various hotels, I being drafted to Hotel Beau Sejour Vevey with Tremlott Wilcox Toogood &ndash; Stones has gone to Pension de Comte, quite close to us &ndash; I have a room to myself mostly elaborately furnished, with every convenience. We have two very good friends already Mr &amp; Mrs Peak who have supplied us with clothing &ndash; pyjamas &amp;c. Spent night at Hotel &amp; rested &ndash;</p>
<p>Today has indeed been a happy one.</p>
