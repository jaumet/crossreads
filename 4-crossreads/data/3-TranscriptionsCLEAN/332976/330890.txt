  
 July 1917 5th   This morning the company went down to the canal just above the lock & made a start to form a heavy bridge, calculated to carry 20 tons. (12" X 12" timber & 12" R S Joists being used with 3 three part pontoons as a pier.)   At dinner time I was warned for guard & had the afternoon off until 6 o'clock, when I mounted the Quarter Guard with three men. 6th   I was on guard all day to-day & was relieved by the 8th Field Coy at 6. P.M. 7th   The Company were on the canal again this morning & we formed another type
