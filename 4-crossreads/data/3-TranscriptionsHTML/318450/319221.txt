<p>When we got to this place we were within easy range of gun fire, so we could not go roaming about, doing rout marches, or even do bayonet drill in the open, as the German's aeroplanes were after taking trips over, and woe betide you and the billit if they got on to you.</p>
<p>We remained in this place for 16 day's as we were in reserve for another battalion which was in the firing line.</p>
<p>Every night we were on fatigue that meant we had to go down and work in the firing line or take provisions up on a small railway, which was a very dangerous job as the machine gun's and snipers used to get on to you.  As I have said we remained in support for 16 days, and then</p>
