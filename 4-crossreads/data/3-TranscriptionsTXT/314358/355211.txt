 We really do not fight our enemy, our shells and shrapnel murder him and his murder us. There is no fight in it. 
 Through the crack and the moan and the cutting shell-shard and the rain of shrapnel poor man of feeble flesh and blood bravely digs trenches towards the enemy suffering losses as he must: and when he has thus approached as far as he dare his brave battalions leap up from the trenches and charge across no-mans-land under shell and machine gun fire.
