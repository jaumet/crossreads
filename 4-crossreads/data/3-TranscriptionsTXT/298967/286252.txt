 DATE &ndash; TIME &ndash; PLACE &ndash; REMARKS 
 22 &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; Transport steamed off to Lemnos and Imbros. Submarine scare. 
 23 &ndash; German plane dropped first bomb. 
 24 &ndash; Armistice arranged 7.30 A.M. to 4.30 P.M. 
 25 &ndash; H.M.A.S. sank of Gaba Tepe.  Still in same position 11th 10th 12th 9th Batts. 
 June 4/5 &ndash; Anzac &ndash; 5/10 reinforcements arrived, very hot, and flies a pest. 
 13 &ndash; Bread issued for first time. 
 30 &ndash; Both sides digging in. 
 July 1 &ndash; 9 A.M. &ndash; Batt. relieved by 9th Batt. &amp; rest in rear trenches. 
 8 &ndash; 3.30 A.M. &ndash; 18 officers and 500 other ranks went to Imbros. 
 11 &ndash; 11 P.M. &ndash; Returned to Anzac. 
 13 &ndash; 10th relieved &ndash; 11th Battalion. 
 August &ndash; Sulva landing, Lone Pine.  Chunuk Bair in the seccond great attempt to open the narrows to the fleet.  Attempt failed though ground was won and lost. 
 16 &ndash; Men getting sick and run down 4.5 per cent diarrhoea.
