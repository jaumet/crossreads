 It was always the same with me, I would image that I was gaining a point on the other fellows, but in this case as in others my &quot;smartness&quot; resulted in my own undoing.&nbsp; I had considered myself very wise in selecting the position under the chimney, but had failed to take into consideration an unseen force, over which I had no control.&nbsp; I suffered accordingly.&nbsp; Well! it caused mirth to others, so I didn&#39;t mind.&nbsp; I smiled a bit myself too.&nbsp; I changed my position for sleeping. 
 The civilians occupying the house adjoining our &quot;room&quot; were like most others hereabouts, in respect to cleanliness and modesty. (or lack of these virtues, I should say).&nbsp; We had to wash in the open, and some of us would wash more than our faces, the civilians talking and joking with us during our albutions. 
 There were hot baths in this &quot;town&quot; and we were marched down every few days to obtain this necessary source to good health, at the same time procuring a clean change of &quot;under linen&quot;.&nbsp; In many cases the &quot;clean&quot; flannel shirts contained &quot;life&quot; more vigorous and ravenous than that existing in the garments we handed in.&nbsp; It appeared absolutely impossible to get rid of these pests.&nbsp; We spent many francs in purchasing stuff for their destruction, but they only seemed to thrive on it.
