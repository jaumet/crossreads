<p>weary, hungry and cold and a battle staring us in the face. With the prospect of ultimate victory <strike>staring us in the face</strike> moved farther from our grasp than ever. We did not know that Foch with his Magic Wand was waiting, unperturbed, contemptuous of the mad enemy on rushes, for a favourable moment to wave his Wand and annihilate or entrap the victory-intoxicated tyrants. But it has happened. Yet I believe that had we known, the British Army generally and the Aussies in particular could hardly have gone into action with better heart than they did.<br/><u>20th</u> We left Sains this morning</p>
