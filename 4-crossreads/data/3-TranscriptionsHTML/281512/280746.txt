<p>one does not linger more than necessary in this place over which hangs the pall of gloom &amp; death. Guns boom all around, yet everyone dodges the awful loneliness &amp; hazards of Chateau wood. Just in front of Chateau Wood I came on a 9.2 howitzer battery: three guns had been knocked out before they were set up in position. Westhock ridge was fairly quiet for the moment till I got near the crest. Then the fun began. I took shelter in a thin sandbag dugout, &amp; had the cinema trained on bursting shells of which there was ample sufficiency. They screamed overhead like a flight of rockets. One fell a little short &amp; threw mud over the dugout &amp; fell but a yard away. I owe my life to it being a dud. <br/>The position became so unhealthy, that with Joyce I decided to run the gauntlet &amp; get out of the barrage if possible, as it seemed fatal to stay in our</p>
