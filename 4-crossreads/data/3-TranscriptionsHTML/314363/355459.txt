<p>is reached, then they dig in &amp; wait for the inevitable counter attack, which as it is expected generally fails completely.<br/>We moved through the racecourse in Polygon Wood, though I saw no sign of a racecourse or of a wood they both long ago ceased to exist. My battalion captured what was afterwards called 'the bluff' this is a large mound, it had a passage running through it &amp; formed a capital dugout. On the second day we moved up to this bluff, &amp; were much more comfortable, along the passage were bunks.<br/>While back with the Co, we had a small pill box which came just within the enemy's barrage, this barrage was bad enough although nothing like our own. I endeavoured to keep up telephonic communication with the forward brigade, owing to the enemy barrage this was exceedingly difficult, &amp; after a while had to be abandoned. The line was continually going &amp; by the time we had reached the far end, repairing the breaks on the way it was generally broken again behind us, &amp; there was so much leakage on the wire, that it interfered with the Power Buzzer at the advanced brigade</p>
