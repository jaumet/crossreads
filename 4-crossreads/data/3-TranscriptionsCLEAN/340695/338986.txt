We were in a direct line with a Railhead Fritz was feeling for. We remained there for the night & moved off early next morning & pitched our camp in the open near to the 14th Coy. An exciting experience I assure you. The poor chaps were buried next day in the Bapaume cemetery without any fuss. A clergyman officiated. They were all fine fellows.
