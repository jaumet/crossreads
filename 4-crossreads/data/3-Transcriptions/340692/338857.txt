<p>But one kind of gets used to these dangers, &amp; they don't concern you much after you know all the pieces of flying iron have safely passed your head.  I consider my steel helmet saved me a "Blighty" on one occasion.  The were shelling MacCormacks, &amp; were after a mule amunition Transport, who were sheltering in the sunken road there.  I, like everyone else, ducked into the nearest funk hole, which happened only to be a kind of fireplace in the side of the bank.  The fourth or fifth shell (the previous ones had already killed one mule, &amp; stampeded the rest) sent a piece of iron that dented my helmet &amp; knocked it off.  I also felt a sting on my arm</p>
