[Printed letterhead] Savoy Hotel2, Rue Royale24, Avenue LeopoldOstende
Ostende, le 17th Oct 1919
Dear little Barbara,Beatrice &amp; Pat are at St. Ediths school, Brackley Northamptonshire &amp; Tom is at Eton College so we brought Carleton &amp; [indecipherable] to see the battlefields of Flanders   We saw Dixmude Ypres &ndash; Poperinge &amp; Neuport it is all terribly sad but I am not going to depress you with all account
This is a most lovely place full of beautiful buildings &amp; in July &amp; August is very jolly.  The peasants still wear wooden sabots &amp; the coal &amp; milk vegetable carts are drawn by dogs who [indecipherable]
In a few moments I am going to see Bruges Cathedral which is about half an hour from here in a train &amp; tomorrow we go to Ghent &amp; Brussells.  After a few days there we will go to Paris.I enclose a little hankey with some Belgian lace made with bobbins on a pillow. you must whip the edge of the linen I think or it will fray -My best love to all your family.  As ever your affectionate friend
Ethel Kelly
