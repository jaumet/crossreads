<p>It was at Cricklewood, a suburb of London and employed 1500, mostly girls. &nbsp;They worked from 8 till 8 with two breaks for meals. &nbsp;I was in at Madam Tussaud&#39;s famous Waxworks in London, and it&#39;s marvellous the life-like portrayals of the various famous and historic men and women. &nbsp;A nice orchestra played, but 20 minutes was enough for us - it was so eerie. &nbsp;You saw the Tubes, didn&#39;t you? Aren&#39;t they handy and fast? There were always plenty of Kids (also porters &amp;c) at the stations only too willing to show us where we wanted to go, and they didn&#39;t forget to put the hard word on us for some of our Cobar. &quot;Hev you gotta copper, Choom?&quot;, and were a positive nuisance. &nbsp;Our chaps&#39; invariable experiences in &quot;Lunnone&quot; are that it costs something to eat, but we found the Y.M.C.A. hut at Euston very handy.</p>
