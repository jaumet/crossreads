<p>Oct 15th.  We moved off at daylight after having received the usual ration for 24 hours on the march viz- One loaf of Black Bread &amp; 24 Olives.  <br/>The travelling was v. Hot &amp; after the usual 20 miles we came to a small village Kaledjik.  Here to our delight we had a hot meal of crushed wheat cooked with olive oil &amp; a piece of bread each,   This was provided by a kind Armenian.<br/>We also divided into groups of 12 &amp; showed our best table manners as we dipped into the one common dish.  The result of this meal was A.1. &amp; it saved us going to bed hungry.  Before turning in we were separated from each other but each man had plenty of company nevertheless- little things who never pay rent but give one a personal welcome<br/>Oct 16th   Roused on daylight &amp; did the usual 20 miles.  Toward end of day the Turks said <strike>Yarrin Sahat</strike> "Yarri sa'at." which means "1/2 hour" but actually means 2 or 3 hrs more walking<br/>The stress of the journey &amp; short food</p>
