side. Saturday 26th March, we sighted land, which was the cause of much conjecture aboard. The general opinion was that it was the southern coast of Sicily, as we passed through the Straits of Bonifacio the following morning it is probable that this is correct.
Our Strait of B. was also a matter of a good deal of conjecture, as during the afternoon we passed north along the coast of an island, which was said to be Sardinia. Ships were plentiful hereabouts as we passed close in shore we had a good view of the fields, green with early spring the white cottages of farmers nestling on the sides of hills.
Early on the morning of Monday 27th land became dimly visible ahead by dinner time we were well in shore, heading in to the port ? not of Marseilles as we had expected
