<p>Monday 18th Dec.<br/>Spent day writing letters.</p>
<p>Teusday 19th Dec.<br/>Report came through that man with query throat had Dypheria.  I had to isolate all his mates and had busy day throat swabbing etc.</p>
<p>Teusday 19th<br/>Fresh Dyp. case came in to-day with patrol.  I am in a dicky position having no Doctor here, however worked like a nig. all day.</p>
<p>Wednesday 20th<br/>Anniversary of evacuation.  Officers, N.C.O's and men celebrated it by a wild impromtu night in the mess.</p>
<p>Thursday 21st<br/>Many sore heads about to-day. More regimental promotions.  Great news reached the camp that El-Arish had fallen with practically no casualties, enemy abandoned it.  Full particulars not here yet.</p>
<p>Friday 22nd<br/>Rode over to regt. to see Doc., he was away, spent day there and just as I arrived home I let Zigi roll after unsaddling and she ran in behind another horse who lashed out and opened up her forearm, bursting an artery  I was awfully upset over it but dressed and stitched it up.</p>
