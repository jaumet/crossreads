<p>[13th  Dec. contd]<br/>Heard that a message had come thro' in night to say that a strafing was expected.  Therefore additional picket were put on but without event.<br/>Sent P/N's to W Hough Amesbury for watch.</p>
<p>Thursday 14th<br/>Nothing of note. Pay Day.</p>
<p>Friday 15th<br/>Warned against leaving our huts without shell helmets and masks.  This has been done for past three nights now.  Good mail for [from?] Australia.  Received about 6 letters.<br/>Sergt Williams has recovered and came back today but has a couple of days on light duty.  Feet not too good.  First sign of chill blains.  Put it down to not  <strike>being</strike> having so much exercise of late.</p>
<p>Saturday 16th<br/>At tea time had some fun with bon bons etc.  Hogged the contents of a parcel the major gave us.  Later 3 parcels for Harold Williams &ndash; more hogging.<br/>Clear day again and more aeroplane activity &amp; anti air craft guns.<br/>Laundry Hands.  Pay Day</p>
<p>Sunday 17th<br/>Had rest.  Sergt Williams &amp; Geo Pickering carrying on in office.</p>
<p>Monday 18th<br/>Letter from home.  Restarted work on Sanitary Party.</p>
<p>Tuesday 19th<br/>Recd letter from bro Ernie from Weymouth.  snowing in afternoon.</p>
<p>Wednesday 20th<br/>Fine day.  more aeroplane activity.  Some good stunts.</p>
<p>Thursday 21st<br/>Letters to H J Baldock</p>
