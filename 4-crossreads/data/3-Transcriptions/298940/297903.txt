<p>of light of varing length &amp; width; also the chopping of Verey lights from time to time. All this combined to mark the first part of the performance almost like an entertainment. Naturally there were the usual heated arguments about it: which were our machines &amp; which were Fritzs; why &amp; by whom the Verey lights were droped &amp; the usual others.<br/>Then a diversion seemed to be created over our heads a little towards the north: strictly speaking over the railway line. Searchlights flashed on to the spot, wavered, - apparently failed to locate the machines which we could hear, &amp; then a gun opened out.</p>
