<p>He is OK. again, - took part in a three mile cross country run last week &ndash; and enjoying a rest down on one of the old rivers. He would not exchange la belle for blighty however so &ndash; thus you see him converted to the creed that spreads faster than any other. Who was it said "every enlightened man has two countries, his own &amp; France." Guess it was right for the 'dinkum' Australians who have been over and have seen a little of the real France where the war is not, all say "Blighty is not a patch on it or the people either!" Of course there are thousands who never look beyond the war worn districts of the 'dirty north' &ndash; they don't ever see any difference between the Belgians and the French. Some of the returned chaps create fun over matters of language. A few nights ago I heard a very heated dispute re the literal meaning of 'tres bien'  One said it was pronounced 'Trat-bang' and meant 'hurry on'. The other party was insisting that it was pro. <p>I fancy several of my letters posted in May will not have reached you. I cant remember what was in them, but I hope nothing special as my memory will not permit me to review the trifles of last month. The army seems to induce a kind of softening of the brain after the 6 month limit is passed.</p>
<p>Jack Howell is running and has gained a high rep. for ginger and grit &ndash; good luck to the old beggar!</p>
<p>Love to all the people at home from Harry.</p>
