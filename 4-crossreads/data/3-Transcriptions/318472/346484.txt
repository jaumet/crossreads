<p>16 September 1918<br/>Monday<br/>Arrived Port Adelaide about 9.30 A.M. Went on shore at midday &amp; were entertained at Lunch by the V.A.D. Nurses who gave us a beautiful dinner. In the afternoon Sgt Brindley, Clarke &amp; I went to Adelaide &amp; in the evening to Prospect to see Mr &amp; Mrs Le Messurier Very nice people. Adelaide impresses one favorably. Today was rather a Gala day in aid of the 7  War Loan Also a big effort was being made to obtain recruits. Got back to Boat about midnight</p>
<p>1918 September 17<br/>Tuesday<br/>Left the Boat about 9.30 A.M. &amp; travelled to National Park in the Mount Lofty Ranges &ndash; getting out at Belair. We were again entertained by the V.A.Ds &amp; some of the other local people. We were splendidly treated &amp; had a very enjoyable day indeed. Got back to the Boat about 3 P.M. &amp; pulled put about 5 P.M. The people of Adelaide have done everything in their power to make our visit a pleasant one &amp; have truly succeeded in doing so. We were taken for a motor drive while at National Park &amp; the trip was a very pleasant one driving through natural bush.</p>
