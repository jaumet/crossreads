 we went to Picquigny to the canteen. We went for our French lesson in the evening & I took a letter I had written in french (to Suzanne, who with her family had evacuated La Cr&ecirc;che when Fritz came through up North & they now live south of Paris) for Zita to correct for me . I was so confidant that it was pretty well correct that I told Jno I'd give him a franc for every mistake that I had made & I may say that the same letter cost me twenty francs 
   
 June 12  th    We just eye-washed the lorry in the morning (washed with a mixture of petrol & oil to please the eye of the beholder, if said beholder be an officer) & rested in the afternoon. we were ready for the road. We took Zita & Marie to a concert in the evening. given by some Tommy cavalry (6  th   or 7  th   Brigade) whho were camped in the neighbourhood. Jno had to leave us at 9.P.M. to mount the guard but returned before the end & we all went home to-gether
