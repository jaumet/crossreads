  
 saw the "Queen Elizabeth", or as she is affectionately called "Lizzy" steaming along, in the distance, bound South; at intervals a huge flash could be seen at the muzzle of one of her big guns, and some seconds later a boom louder than any of the others announced the fact that she had sent a huge projectile, weighing almost a ton, forward to one of the Turkish positions.  Although we could not clearly see anything going on at either point the almost continuous bombardment indicated that the fleets had marks to fire at, and were firing.  A message received said that one of the hospital ships detailed for the Australians had 400 wounded and 32 dead including 2 military and 1 Naval officer aboard; considering the difficulty of the position attacked this casualty list was not extraordinary.  Though the telescope was constantly in use all day nothing of interest could be observed.
