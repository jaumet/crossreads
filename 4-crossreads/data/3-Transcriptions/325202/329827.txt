<p>Australians but when one is close to the line &amp; in action &ndash; no-other force gets the job done with less fuss than our fellows &ndash; But apart from that when more or less off duty they persist in neglecting to salute every little Tom, Dick or Harry who has just got a Star up most probably through family influence. It is a silent expression of contempt for the social "caste" that they attempt to keep up</p>
<p>It may have been alright &amp; necessary in peace days when the officer &amp; man were each drawn from a different education &amp; home &amp; were <strike>doing it</strike> soldiering</p>
