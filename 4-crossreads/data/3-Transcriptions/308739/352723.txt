<p>Wednesday 7 June 1916<br/>Pay Day in Camp.<br/>Edwards sentenced to 7 days field Punishment for using insulting words about the Col.  The Brig. down visiting the Col. this morning.<br/>Air o planes circulating about camp to-day.<br/>During the past few Weeks all leave has been stopped, &amp;amp; the different Cities &amp;amp; large Towns in Egypt have be thoroughly overhauled by the Police for Desertion.  Something like 800 men have been found, also a few nurses who appear to have gone astray whilst in Egypt.<br/>Inoculated this afternoon against Typhis.</p>
<p>Thursday 8 June 1916<br/>Sick all Night &amp;amp; laying down to-day.  News received that Lord Kitchener &amp;amp; Staff has been drowned.  Another National Hero gone.  A Noble Life spent in the Welfare of Humanity.  Egypt's Greatest Friend at Rest.  The rich &amp;amp; poor, the high &amp;amp; Low, &amp;amp; the Great &amp;amp; Small, all come at last to the Portal of Death.</p>
<p>Friday 9 June 1916<br/>Went into Cantara for Shirts, etc.<br/>Col. Fuller left for Conference at Divisional H. Quarters, &amp;amp; then on to Cairo for well earned Rest.</p>
<p>Saturday 10 June 1916<br/>I took &amp;pound;E.60 into Cantara last Evening &amp;amp; handed same to Col. Fuller on Railway Station, returned to camp 9-30 p.m.  Regimental Funds.<br/>Cleaning up day today.<br/>5 per Cent leave has been granted to Men to Port Said only.<br/>Ted Reed left at 6 p.m. on leave to P. Said.<br/>Lieu. Black returned to Duty to-day.<br/>Another Medical inspection by M.O. of the 3rd Section in Canal Zone.<br/>These Officers are particularly keen about this work, &amp;amp; go into close details, regardless of whose corns they may tred on.<br/>Lieu. Chisholm &amp;amp; Capt. Beith went into Cairo last Evening for 3 days.<br/>The road from Kantara to Hill 70 is under extensive repairs, there being 3 or 4 Steam rollers continually working, besides many Arabs.  It is purely for Motors &amp;amp; Waggons.  All Horseman to keep on the sand.</p>
