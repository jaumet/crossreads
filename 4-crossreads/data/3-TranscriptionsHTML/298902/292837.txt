<p>billy tea, stockman etc.  Had several other interesting meetings, one with a chemist who on learning my calling welcomed me effusively as a brother &amp; shewed me over his establishment pressing me to stay with him etc.  The whole family spoke a little English &amp; I was loath to leave.  A Belgian soldier spoke to me saying I was the first English soldier he had seen, having been a prisoner in Germany.  Everywhere people gazed at us with great curiosity as we were practically the first Australians to be there.  After tea we went to the pictures finishing up at a large Cafe as our train did not leave until 12.30 midnight.  The cafe was a very grand one held several hundred well dressed people &amp; officers in uniform.  In a gallery was a fine orchestra of 30</p>
