 Friday 5 July On Thursday morning the Boshe raided 10th Battn. but he came a gutzer. Very quiet all day &amp; an Australian mail to-day. To be relieved to-morrow.  Glorious weather continues. 
 Saturday 6 July Absolutely quiet all day &amp; not a shot fired by Fritz.  Glorious day. An American Sergeant was attached to this post &amp; he is good to talk to. The Australian ideas as to the American words of command were very amusing to him. They were Aust &ndash; Slope arms American &ndash; Guys muskets on your shoulders put. Aust &ndash; Eyes right or left American &ndash; Guys look at 'em Aust &ndash; Attention American &ndash; Guys stiffen Aust &ndash; Stand at Ease American &ndash; Guys Slacken However, the Sergeant exploded all these ideas &amp; was much amused. Their words of command are similar to ours.
