<p>30 October 1914<br/>Friday</p>
<p>Reveille at 6<br/>Drew feed from No 1 hold then had breakfast which was good for a change.  Boat unknown flying the yellow flag came in.  Semaphore practice in the morning.  Received rather a personal message from A M to the [indecipherable] concerning Pte Farrell who is not to come with the troops.  Beer strike heads have cut down men's supply from 2 pints to 1 result boycott by reservists.  Captain goes ashore.  Melbourne has joined an Imperial Fleet for she came in with two white bands round rear &amp; bow funnel, she came in this morning &amp; cruised out again at night.  NZ Transports coming &amp; going all day.  Horse passes us this morning partially devoured by sharks.  [indecipherable] steams out this evening for dear old Melbourne.  Wrote letters to Nell &amp; Ern &amp; posted them.  Got extraordinary special as Toby &amp; I traced up positions of Allies in the field, played quoits, had tea, played chess.  [indecipherable] horse under the vet, entered Hold No 2.  Medical inspection final OK</p>
<p>31 October 1914<br/>Saturday</p>
<p>Reveille at 6.<br/>Drew feed for No 5 Hold &amp; then had breakfast after which practical semaphore &amp; morse for the benefit of Toby was indulged in.  then we entered No 2 Hold to see after bikes then looked for jam &ndash; found other stores also raisins then had a washing parade, but water very scarce as only one gallon allowed per man.  No mail today, &amp; we are off tomorrow morning thank goodness.  Steamers ordered at 2 PM today &amp; we move out at 6 in the morning heard also the America has declared war on Japan but find out later that it is unfounded.  We saw a beaut concert &amp; it was surprising what talent there is on board from the Captain downwards during the performance a fire broke out but was instantly suppressed.  Tim Elder paid us a visit during concert.  SS Sydney came in during night &amp; has joined the Imperial fleet more chess then went to bed, looking forward to the roll of the ocean again.</p>
