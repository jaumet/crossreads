<p>have better chances of seeing the country without the usual worry of entraining 300 horses wagons etc, which up to the present time has fallen to my lot.</p>
<p>I fancy it is for the best as I am breaking away from all old groves &amp; one very certain thing, I was sure of that was that I suited too well as a CSM &amp; would have ended my days as same, if I had not of stepped out.</p>
<p>War news is pretty much the same thing with not much doing. The aeroplanes are very busy &amp; the anti aircraft guns are spitting out their shells in hundreds. In one corner I can count as many as 30 planes circling about, &amp; from their actions it appears as though they are fighting hostile machines although it is too far away to discern very clearly.</p>
<p>I am heartily sick of the whole affair &amp; I can assure you that it would be with a feeling of great relief if the war were to suddenly end today but no such luck, &amp; I suppose that it will continue to drag on right throughout the winter thus adding a few more hardships to a rotten &amp; disagreeable life. When I look at all your photos &amp; think of the disgraceful manner things are carried out like here well, it makes me bile, &amp; I can assure you I do</p>
