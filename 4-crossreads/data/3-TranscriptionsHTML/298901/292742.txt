<p>whole ambulance is now together which will mean new arrangements in regard to carrying on this place.  Weather fine and a large number of our planes were about, a couple of the enemy also across.  Very little shelling apart from some salvoes of heavy stuff about 1&frac12; miles away.  Fritz's balloons have us under direct observation &amp; also a portion of his lines looks down the valley at us</p>
<p>Monday 17th June<br/>Very few wounded coming through but there is a lot of influenza about.  Tis a type of French fever &amp; probably spread by the louse, the matter is now being investigated by a special commission.  Had a busy day myself fixing things up at the Station, arranged a buffet, blanket store &amp; so on.<br/>Arrangements of hospital not altered.  I still carry on in charge  C section being employed on digging etc.<br/>Weather fine &amp; beautiful.  Had a mess meeting Sgts in which my stand for a better mess was backed up.
