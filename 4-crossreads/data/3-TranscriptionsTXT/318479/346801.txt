31 AugustAm on sniping.  "A" Coy relieved by the Essex.  Leave at 7 A.M. for Reserve Gully.
1 Sept.Rest in Reserve Gully.  Move off at dark to Monash Gully on the right.
2 Sept.17th in Monash Gully,.  "A" Coy move into the firing line at Quinns Post.  Harman &amp; I in the trenches, together with the 3rd Light Horse.
3 Sept.Relieved at 1 AM.  At midnight the 3 ALH leave us to it &amp; march back to relieve the Essex.
4 Sept.Harman &amp; I go down to Anzac for a swim.  Egan &amp; McTague killed with their own bomb.  Groom wounded.  Have bread for tea.
5 Sept.I join the bomb throwing and go into the bomb trench to relieve Durand &amp; Materson.  Have the best dinner since we landed on the peninsula.  Roast beef &amp; bread.
6 Sept.Bomb throwing from 6 AM to 12 and from 6 PM to 12 midnight.  We have a bit of a rally at 12, turks cut down the wire netting with the machine gun &amp; threw in 3 bombs none of which do any damage.  Durand &amp; I send back a dozen in return.
7 Sept.Breakfast on tea &amp; a desert spoon of rum &amp; go into the trenches at 6 AM.  Turks complete their barbed wire entanglement.
8 Sept.Receive mail and a letter from Lou.  Issued with bread and syrup, &frac12; a loaf per man, &amp; 3 tins among 18 men.
9 Sept.Issued with rum before going into the trenches at 6 AM.  Mack &amp; Day have a narrow shave from Turks bombs.  We have a big rally 9 PM firing 5 rounds rapid &amp; then yelling rattling the bayonets as if we were charging.  The Turks replyed with a heavy fire that lasted &frac12; an hour.
10 Sept.Am out of the trenches for a spell.  Bully beef stew &amp; flies for breakfast.  Dinner:  beef &amp; biscuits.  Tea:  a slice of bacon fat and a biscuit.
