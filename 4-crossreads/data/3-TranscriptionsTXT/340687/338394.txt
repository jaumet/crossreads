 March, 1917. Saturday 31 Day squally with hail. Fair S.E. wind. Up at 7. and cleaned hut and engine room thoroughly. Good deal of water going out. In intervals read reports of Intelligence. After dinner started up but cylinder head packing blew out &amp; had to cut a new washer. Worked at 4.30 to 6 about and tank nearly empty. Tried bombs. One a smoke other red flare. 148th R.E take over on Sunday. No ration came. Bed at 10.30.
