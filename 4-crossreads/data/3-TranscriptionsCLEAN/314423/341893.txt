We had a long talk together about old times at the college & other students at the front Monday Feb. 25. 1918. 1302. Showery this morning. The roads are covered with thin liquid mud. I was grooming & cleaning saddles today. Tonight Joe Carr came down from the artillery stables & with B. Dowden we had an interesting talk about old times & old mates at the college. Last night I saw L/C. H.T Flugner of the 3  [indecipherable] Battalion. He was at one time a schoolteacher at Dartmoor & Drik Drik. Tuesday Feb 26. 1918. 1303. There was a heavy frost last night, - consequently the roads were in good order this morning. The baloon which is stationed here ascended early this morning & the enemy sent 5 schrapnel shells around it, but done no injury to the baloon or to us. [Shorthand Not Transcribed] Wednesday Feb. 27. 1918. 1304. Fine but cold. Last night I walked up to the artillery lines 108 Battery 8th Brigade to see Joe Carr, a driver there. I stayed until 9 P.M.
