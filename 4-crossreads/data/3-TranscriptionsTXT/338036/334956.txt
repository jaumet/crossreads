N Zealanders also on the march had arrived at this place that afternoon & so the town was full of them.  Passing thro Arques we continued in sth easterly direction & reached our destination just on night fall.  It was the village of [no entry]After looking after the horses at which I assisted it was bedtime & we slept in a barn.  I had to do picket from 4 to 5 am next morn & it was raining slightly.
Thursday 27thUp at 7 am and set out again  on our march at 8.30 am.  Passing Ebblinghem, Syvestre [?] Cappell, Eecke we came to Godewaersvelde (where our party from C Section were at CCS) passing to the south of Cassell.  We arrived at Godewaersvelde at about 8 pm and in the dusk could see Mont des Cats quite plainly.  To night was exceedingly clear & our planes were continually travelling across  to Fritz laden with bombs.  Slept in a barn again.
Friday 28thLast day of our march.  Set out for last spurt at about 9 am and passed thro Steenvoorde - where we saw hundreds of Anzacs
