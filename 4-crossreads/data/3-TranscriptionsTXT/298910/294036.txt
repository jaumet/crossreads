At any rate the wounded were not coming in freely enough (Regimental S Bearers should bring to first aid post &amp; Field Ambulance attend from there on). At 2 am about 8 15th Field Ambulance volunteered to go up to the first line &frac12; mile away &amp; get them in &ndash; tho this was distinctly against our Colonels wishes &amp; orders, we worked from the firing line right thro till the lull about mid day Thursday &ndash; by 8 pm on Thursday, we went everywhere &amp; felt  quite satisfied, our services were required by no more.
