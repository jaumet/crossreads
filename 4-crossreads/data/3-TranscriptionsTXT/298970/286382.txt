 loads of timber arrived &amp; we got a good bit of the framework up.  Planes &amp; battle planes up all day.  As darkness set in the sky was spotted with shell marks &amp; it was a great sight watching the flashes from a machine gun on one of our planes. 
 25th August (Friday) 
 Guns &amp; aeroplanes again busy.  Party of 12 more men arrived from our unit to dig a drain.  2 9.8 howitzers dragged along the road by tractors.  One of our planes brought down. 
 26th August (Saturday) 
 Heard that Wilcox &amp; Stanton who enlisted and were in camp with me in Aust. both suffered on 19th July.  The former was wounded &amp; the latter is a prisoner of war, also wounded.  Advance Guard of 1st &amp; 2nd Aust. Div.
