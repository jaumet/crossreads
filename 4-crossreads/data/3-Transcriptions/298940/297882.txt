<p>&amp; I got away with the first case. By the time had gone half of the way along the first portion of our track, that was the duckboard on narrow trench, we were safe for the time being, for <strike>a ridge</strike> we had left a ridge between us &amp; the place we had left where the shells were still falling. Without further adventures we got that patient down to the next relay, having to carry him all the way on account of a missing truck.<br/>Nothing of importance occurred on the return journey, &amp; just when we were settling down with an uneasy feeling, that plenty of work would come through that night, Koll &amp; I had to take another patient, a walker.</p>
