They must have spent some cash, as you will understand when I mention that fruit is dear &ndash; apples 3/- per doz, oranges (any good) 2/- doz, grapes 1/6 lb etc. A little route march round the Bluff Beach [indecipherable] Sunday's programme. On Monday, after hours of delay, a dredge took our crowd across the waters &amp; dumped us down on the Point &ndash; the shipping centre. From there we marched to the residence, about two miles, meeting a great reception all the way. I wont say much about our marching, we had been over three weeks caged up, &amp; the city was busy flinging things at us, thus you can imagine a very soldierly procession (?)<br/>Proclamation closed the pubs to us &ndash; not necessarily to Australians only &amp; this was a very good thing; it defeated the aims of the mad element while in no way limiting the fun of others &ndash; we had not dismissed five minutes, before self  &amp; pals were hooked into a big caf&eacute; &amp; found ourselves hitting up a four course 'luncheon'.   Finished, we asked the Jewish proprietor how much we owed him. He laughed, shrugged &amp; murmured "von bob &ndash; come back at five &amp; have dinner!"  We paid. Followed a trip (per ricksha of <u>course</u>) round the city. The zoo proved to be small &ndash; but very complete &amp; its collection of African beasts must be hard to beat. They have one specimen, carefully caged, that reminded me of a rather handsome tabby cat with black paws. It seemed quiet. The label said 'BLACK FOOTED CAT'!  We came away wondering.
