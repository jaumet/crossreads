<p>21 June 1916 Wed<br/>Cutting stakes all the morning. 1.20 General parade also gas drill running round the paddock with helmets on. Issued with new helmet &amp; small bottle of iodine. Final of the tug-of-war resulting in a win for the motor transport. Indulged in a hot bath at 6.30 retiring to bed after it. We [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>17 aeroplanes up to night together.</p>
<p>22 June 1916 Thur<br/>Cutting roadways the best part of the days. A beautiful day &amp; slightly warm. General Legge comes &amp; inspects camp also General Holmes. After tea went to Louis estaminet where fifteen boys were sitting round the table partaking of a few drinks. We had a grand night &amp; a</p>
<p>[Text continued in margin]<br/>great sing-song.</p>
