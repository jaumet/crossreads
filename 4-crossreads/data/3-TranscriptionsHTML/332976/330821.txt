<p>March 1917<br/>22nd.   We carried on with the same job this morning with the aid of an infantry fatigue of about 20 (the first fatigue we have been able to get) &amp; also two wagons carting bricks from the village.  The hole was filled in &amp; the road is open for traffic.   Guns have been coming up ever since we arrived here &amp; an ammunition dump has been started just outside our billet.   We had a fairly heavy fall of snow last night &amp; it has continued through the day, &amp; with a strong North wind things have been very cold.   I went into Bappaume this afternoon.   The town has been smashed about a great deal &amp; Fritz has blown up the main road in three places, making</p>
