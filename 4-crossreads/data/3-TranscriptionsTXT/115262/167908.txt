 Monday 21 May 1917. 
 Land sighted at 9 a,m, Heavy mist. Sea calmer. Anchored 10.30 a.m. just out of Fremantle. Shore quite close. Everyone pleased at seeing houses from distance. 
 Motor launch &quot;Dauntless&quot; came along side our boat A 42. &quot;Boorara&quot; and discharged her cargo of fruit (Y.M.C.A. Comforts Fund gift) 
 Fruit issued in afternoon. Sea now calm as mill-pond &amp; sun shining on Fre e mantle&nbsp;&mdash;&nbsp;a Pretty sight. The 2 escorts &quot;Encounter&quot; &amp; &quot;Doris&quot; nowhere to be seen. 
 4 transports weigh anchor &amp; steam slowly to Fremantle up &quot;Swan River&quot; 
 Cleaned &nbsp;up all equipment. A Japanese Cruiser (1 mast ed ) entered &quot;the Rose&quot; &amp; anchored. A great night on upper deck.
