<p>Australians still attacking &amp; holding their gains. Gen Birdwood daily visits the firing line. Our chief is very pleased.</p>
<p>July 26th<br/>After a good day we depart from Vadencourt &amp; proceed towards the line of battle (14 miles away). On our way we pass vast numbers of troops, munitions etc. We end our march &amp; bivouac on the outskirts of Albert.</p>
<p>July 27th Shortly after rising we meet &amp; converse with many of the "first Brigade" who left the trenches the previous night after doing such wonderful fighting. &ndash; I again meet Billy Hain &amp; Roger Morgan of the 2nd Batt. A.M.C. &ndash; In the afternoon I part from Harry &amp; Rog who are in the Stretcher Party for trenches &ndash; Good luck be with them.</p>
<p>July 28th<br/>Our tent division return to Vadencourt &amp; take over from R.A.M.C. a D.R.S &ndash; Plenty of work. (Night Duty)</p>
