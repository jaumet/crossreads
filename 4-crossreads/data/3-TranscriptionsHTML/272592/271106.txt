<p>been pounding old Fritzie unmercifully nearly all day, there has been an almost constant stream of big shells howling &amp; wailing overhead all bound for the same place, old Fritz's lines, he made 2 attempts to retaliate but was forced to play second fiddle both times, as I sit here on the damp &amp; muddy firing step I can plainly see one of our planes hanging well to the rear of the German lines observing all that is going on</p>
<p>13th.   We were relieved last night by the 9th Batt. &amp; moved off from the firing line about 12.30 had a long &amp; tiresome walk of 4 miles or so to the railway we passed through Ypres again</p>
