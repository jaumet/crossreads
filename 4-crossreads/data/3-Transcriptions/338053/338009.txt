<p>the concrete steps 2 officers &amp; 25 men who very quickly were escorted back to Capt. Herrod. With the corporal (Campbell) I then went downstairs with electric torch (the gift Harry gave me!) in one hand &amp; revolver in the other. The place was enormously strong. On the first floor down were 3 machine guns &amp; 27 boxes of belt ammunition together with automatic pistols, rifles, ranging instruments, field glasses, plenty of rations &amp; beer &amp; an enormous quantity of other booty. It was rightly termed a "stronghold", &amp; if only the Huns had had "the guts", - to put it forcibly, if rudely &ndash; they could have done a lot of damage before caving in. Leading down from the first flight was a second flight of stone stairs &amp; here we found a room used as officers' quarters in which was a wounded captain with his soldier servant : we had him carried up later (strange to say one of the Saxby's &ndash; was it Eric S. &ndash; came along &amp; when he heard my name spoke to me) &amp; taken to the dressing station</p>
