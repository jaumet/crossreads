<p>All other companies carried out their allotted duties, but of course, what happened with them I don't know, I can only describe my own experiences. At 3.2 a.m. the usual morning strafe was put into Vaire Wood by our artillery for 8 minutes, &amp; at 3.10 crash came the barrage from all our guns, &amp; we moved forward slowly &ndash; one would think the awful crash of artillery &amp; bursting shells would deafen or drive you crazy, but your blood is warm, &amp; really, I felt as cool as could be, just slightly "Keen" I'll admit. We moved to within 30 to 50 yards of our barrage, &amp; one felt he could go through to Berlin under it. However, there is a temptation to go too fast, &amp; one of our shells burst fair among our company, &amp; played havoc. I was pleased to see the tanks moving forward, they had arrived to time without mishap. After 3 minutes our barrage lifted another 100 yards, &amp; we moved on, the companies were now rather mixed up. Came to a trench, &amp; I saw a Fritz with his hands up surrendering to some of our boys, they fired into other dugouts, in case there were some hiding in there before we moved on. I was amongst some Yanks &amp; A Coy now, the tanks still trudged along, &amp; day was breaking. I then saw the tank in front of me hit with a shell. After going about 800 yards, D Company's objective was reached &amp; we immediately began to dig in, while A, B &amp; C Coys.</p>
