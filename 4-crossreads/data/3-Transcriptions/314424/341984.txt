<p>Transport lines.</p>
<p>I was on second picquet last night from 11.25 to 10 to 2.</p>
<p>This evening I went for a drive on the water cart with A. Valentine to Behencourt about 4&frac12; kilometers away. While there I was able to get a paper and had to pay 3d for it.</p>
<p>The Americans are now in the thick of the fighting and are giving a good account of themselves.</p>
<p>Saturday May 4. 1918. 1370.<br/>A fine but cloudy day.</p>
<p>There was the normal artillery duel last night and at intervals throughout the day. As I write it is being renewed for the night.</p>
<p>Snowy Price our cook returned from the Base tonight. So my position as second in command in the Cook House finishes tonight.</p>
<p>We heard a few days ago that Trevena has had his leg taken off.</p>
<p>We had to remove from our billet (at least those who were in it, for most of the men slept in other billets &amp; in tents) to make room for other troops. My mate Attenborough &amp; I are sleeping under a cart in the cook shed.</p>
<p>Sunday May 5. 1918. 1371<br/>A dirty drizzling morning.</p>
<p>[three lines of shorthand &ndash; I wrote a further letter to my wife last night and this morning x letter 127.  4 pages of a post card and 2 pages in green envelope.]</p>
<p>I am on my old job again doing odd repairs to Limbres, etc.</p>
<p>Tonight if required I am breaksman on C.C. McGlouchens Limbres for R.E. duties.</p>
<p>This evening I received another letter from my dear wife dated March 5. No 123.</p>
