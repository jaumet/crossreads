 Thursday 11&nbsp;March 1915 Hot weather today. 2 p.m. No letters for me from home.&nbsp; Rode for one hour midst the cultivated land, &amp; the palm groves&nbsp;&amp; the irrigated land on this side [of] the hill&nbsp; Left Mena House at 8.30 p.m. for the Heliopolis Camp. Arrived at the Camp at 11 pm 
 Friday 12 March 1915 Up 6 A.M. with Lieut. Col. McGlynn from W. Maitland. Started about 7-30 a.m. on divisional Manouver with Colonel Monash &amp; his staff.&nbsp; Out&nbsp;all day till 4-30 pm., riding all the time. Home Mena House 10.30 pm bath &amp; bed officers &amp; men still crude but plenty of good &amp; fair officers there. General&nbsp;Maxwell, Birdwood, Godlee [Godley], Bridges &amp;c. 
 Saturday 13&nbsp;March 1915 Posted letters to  Carrie  the girls &amp; Mollie. Replies to Car, Joseph &amp; Kitty. Wrote reply to one from Doffie.&nbsp;Not out&nbsp;all day. Rode during afternoon through the country. 
 Sunday 14&nbsp;March 1915 At mass at 7 am. Rode&nbsp; Visited the Barage across the Nile river about 2 miles below Cairo. It&#39;s used for conserving the water, that then may be distributed to the farmers (fellahas) throughout the whole of the Delta. Flood mittigation &amp; water supply are the functions of the barrage. storage of water is thefunction of the Assouan dam. The director of the Barage works showed Colonel Monash, Col. McGlynn, Captain McAuliffe, &amp; Mr Smith over the place, with me.&nbsp;
