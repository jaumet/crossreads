  
 Dec 31st 1917 Esdud 
 We stayed that night at Yebnah & reached here late in the afternoon of the following day not without much agony & bloody sweat on the part of the horses. Here we have quite a nice little camp inside a sort of paddock bound on three sides by a prickly pear hedge & on the fourth by the railway & altogether encloses about an acre, so it makes a nice tight little camp.
