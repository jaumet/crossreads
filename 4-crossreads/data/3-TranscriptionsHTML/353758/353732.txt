<p>26/5/15 [26/4/15?]<br/>Gun fire started at daylight again shelling the hills (the "Queen Elizabeth" is now with us &ndash; troops still landing from transports being taken ashore by torpedo boats which transfer them to barges close inshore &ndash; guns from our ships keeping up fire to cover them &ndash; our shells and schrapnel bursting over top of hills and in gullies and throwing up columns of smoke &ndash; behind our boat an observation balloon has gone up to great height &ndash; our aeroplanes are not [now] scouting &ndash; fine day and cool &ndash; had breakfast aboard about 7.30 a.m. and immediately after went aboard torpedo boat which took up near shore and transferred us to a punt with open ends (like a horse punt) from which we landed getting wet to the waist in doing so &ndash; schrapnel was bursting all round us, the boat was making for the shore, and one sailor was hit on the torpedo boat and one of our chaps on the Punt. Most of the landing parties had some men killed or wounded in the boats &ndash; one felt inclined to "duck" ones head when one heard the schrapnel burst overhead &ndash; heard our chaps had captured nine Turkish machine guns and also various rumours as to our own horses &ndash; Also that our [one] party of Turks had shown the white flag and then fired on our men with a machine gun when they advanced to take their prisoners &ndash; troops landing all the time and ships bombarding as they pick up the Turkish positions &ndash; aeroplanes are out and no doubt  directing fire on ships &ndash; balloon is also up about 4 miles out at sea &ndash; We (A company of the 13th ) awaiting orders &ndash; told that on first lot of Australians landing on day before, they had met the turks [Turks] right at the waters edge and charged up the steep hillsides which rise from the sea &ndash; the turks [Turks] also had machine guns firing on our chaps in the boats as they landed, but our chaps jumped out of the boats up to their wasts [waists] in water and went straight for the Turks (leaving many dead and wounded in the boats) having fixed bayonets while still in the boats. Charging up the steep hills and cleared the Turks out of their Trenches &ndash; but of course we lost a good many &ndash; The artillery are now alnding [landing] also horses and mules &ndash; a bayonet charge is now going on above us while we still await orders on the beach and</p>
