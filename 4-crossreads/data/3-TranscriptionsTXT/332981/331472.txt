 After obtaining some tea we had some bullie beef &amp; bread for breakfast &amp; two of us went down to the yard where our boys were in charge &amp; the Sergeant Major sent a light engine down on the Rang du Fliers line for our kits, &amp; we obtained a barrow &amp; loaded our kits on it &amp; pushed it out &amp; down the street to the line about 50 yards away &amp; soon had everything aboard &amp; away to the Camp, passing the gravel pits on the way where two Marion Steam Shovels were digging into the bank for gravel, I was very interested at that work, it is a wonderful affair, the shovel is about 3 ft wide &amp; about 4 ft deep &amp; at the
