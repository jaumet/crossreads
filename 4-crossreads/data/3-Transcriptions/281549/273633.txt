<p><U>PART  IV</U></p>
<p>Concerning my stay in Germany as Prisoner-of-War from April 1917 till December 1918.</p>
<p>From Friday the 13th until Monday the 16th. I was travelling in the train through France Belgium &amp; Germany, The town or Cities that I passed through was Cambria in France, Leige, Namur &amp; Charleroi in Belgium, and Minden &amp;   Hannover in Germany.</p>
<p>We were in cattle trucks all the while which were opened on two occasions to feed us, Our wounds were not dressed at all during the trip , which lasted about 60 hours. Some of the wounds were in an awful state, &amp; which, when we reached hospital, was the cause of a lot of limbs having to be amputated.</p>
<p>We were all in a filthy condition swarming with vermin, and with our hair all matted together with mud.</p>
<p>On the morning of Monday the 16th we arrived at Munster-Lager station , where we detrained  were marched a distance</p>
