<p>We stayed that night in a wood, with no shelter &amp; it was still raining. <br/>The following day, the 8th, I, with fifty others, was sent back to a village to rest. I stayed there two days &amp; then moved to another village. <br/>The next day we were sent up to join the Bn at Gentelles Wood. Twenty-two kilometres. Some march.<br/>The following night we relieved the 34th in the front line. <br/>After three days we were relieved by the 19th &amp; came back to a reserve possy on a bank. We stayed there for three days &amp; was then relieved by "Tommies" &amp; came back to Gentelles Wood again. <br/>The next day we marched to a place called Quiverisse. Stayed there for the night &amp; the following morning we moved up to reserves in a gully near Bayzieux.<br/>We stayed there for six days then we moved forward to close-reserves. Five days there &amp; we were again relieved by "Tommies, &amp; came back to Warloy.</p>
