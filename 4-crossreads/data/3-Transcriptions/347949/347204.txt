<p>made ourselves as comfortable as possible.  The trenches were cut in chalky ground about 5 feet deep and by spreading ones water proof sheet over the top one made a little shelter. But  it was too wet  so I went down to the village secured an old door some pieces of wood &amp; did the best possible to keep the rain off got some cover &amp; laid on that. Then we sigs had to lay a wire to headquarters &amp; make a dug out for the phone.<br/>The time spent in reserves was given up to preparing. I made certain of a bath &amp; shave because I knew it would be the last for many days. Went down into an old ruined cottage in the village secured  a bucket &amp; there performed my last ablutions - occasionally being interrupted by other soldiers prowling around for the same purpose whilst in a state of - (well with nothing on).</p>
