<p>July 24th 32<br/>[Wa]kened at an early hour &amp; informed that I with 40 others was being shifted to another camp &ndash; quite a horrible shock especially as I had strict orders to remain in bed yesterday so as to rest my foot "Cpl Major" informed the Commdt that I was not fit to travel &amp; he said I was being specially sent for electrical treatment</p>
<p>Left Karlsruhe at noon after various inspections of kit &amp;c. Leickler &amp; I were conveyed to the station in a carriage. Had special car to ourselves &ndash; train left at 1 pm &amp; arrived FRIEBURG at 6 pm. My suitcase containing all my necessaries &amp;c was left behind on Karlsruhe Stn, not being able to attend to this myself, I was obliged to ask one of the interpretors look after same for me.</p>
<p>On arrival at Frieburg hundreds of German civilians rushed out to view the 4 prisoners appear "clad in all sorts of odd clothes imaginable Much to my great annoyance I was carried shoulder high thro the crowd, feeling very embarrassed.</p>
<p>We were conveyed in a motor amb. from station to camp. We are billeted in an old university, there being 20 in our room, including [indecipherable] with myself. The beds are wood with straw pailasses &amp; are very hard.</p>
<p>July 25th<br/>Could not sleep much, owing to new surrounding[s] &amp; hard bed. There is a decided improvement in the meals here &amp; there is [indecipherable] bread ration.</p>
<p>My bag was sent on from Karlsruhe this morning &amp; received in good order.</p>
<p>Paraded to the adjt today, with a view to having my name included on the list of officers to be exchanged. He is going to make enquiries to ascertain whether I will be in time for the first draft. We have 3 messes in our room 2 of 7 &amp; one of 6. I am in the last with Capts Cornish &amp; Hill. We have one gas ring allotted for each room.</p>
