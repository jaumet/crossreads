
continued.  At 11am the Glengyle was a little astern of us & gradually overhauled us till by 1pm she was a couple of ships lengths ahead.  During the morning we heard rumbling sound similar to a bombardment.  At 2pm there were no less than 6 big steamers in our vicinity 1 overhauling us 2 going in the opposite direction & 3 we were overhauling.  By 3pm the fog had quite cleared off leaving the atmosphere clear.  No land sighted since passing Flambrough till 5.30pm when we passed close to the shore immediately below the Wash.  During the afternoon we passed dozens of steamers, of all nationalities almost.  At one time we were in a bunch of no less than 18 steamers all within a radius of about 3 miles.
