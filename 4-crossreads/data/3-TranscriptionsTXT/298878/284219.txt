As we had only to wait long enough to make arrangements to proceed through the Canaal no shore leave was granted; while we were at anchor a fairly stiff breeze was blowing, and a big sea running, but this did not deter a number of natives in sailing boats from crowding around us, attempting to sell fruit, sweets, smokes, curios, etc; the manner in which they handled their boats was masterly; when sailing they travelled through the water at a tremendous pace, and kept hatches battened down to prevent swamping.  Being a transport we were not required to wait our turn to proceed up the Canaal, but allowed to go as soon as the necessary papers were adjusted, and the Canaal pilot aboard.
