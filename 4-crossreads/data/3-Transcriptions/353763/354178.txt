<p>the same down Warnambool way  -  The ashes of the volcano &ndash; which by the way is still active ? &amp; we had an earthquake yesty  -  one per diem is the rule  -  One we go &amp; I named the trees &amp; shrubs  -  A new fine tree was the Breadfruit &ndash; a tree 30-40 or more feet high &ndash; Large leaves &ndash; incised &ndash; at the end of branches -  Artocarpus <u>incisa</u> Fruit was there  -  I had not seen the tree before but soon spotted it  -  Its very shady &amp; also plentiful  -  The vegetation was mostly Queensland  -  Presently down the track came a sulky with a fine military gent &ndash; a small child &amp; a nigger  -  He stopped &amp; we saluted  -  (We are in uniform so as to be known as Naval officers)  -  He said &ndash; Go to my house on the top &amp; call on my wife &amp; daughter &amp; have some tea  -  We spotted who he was &ndash; General Wylde of the Marines  -  We said 'You've been having a pretty rotten time of it General'  -  We talked &amp; then went on  -  We walked on past the Hospital &amp; then on the Right was the General's house  -  I made Haggard put his pipe out &amp; close up &amp; up we went  -  Mrs Wylde came out</p>
