Gray who came from England with me, &amp; during the long stay at Caestre was my sole companion. I first met him on the Peninsula, then &amp; ill from the severities of some months of that campaign. The incident that sticks out in my mind is the appointment of him to a light job as runner, owing to his state of health. Everyone then referred to him as a fine chap, brave, &amp; a striker. 
 The next time I met him was at Parkhouse when I, one night, became aware of him as a listener at one of our debates. Later Sgt Clarke &amp; I got into touch with him, &amp; he took part in the memorable "Reprisals" debate.
