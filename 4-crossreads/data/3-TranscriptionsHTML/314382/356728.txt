<p>for Weymouth at 10 pm and we tumbled into our train, very very tired, with the prospects of a 4 hours journey before us. At 4 am next morning we arrived at Southampton &amp; remained on the station &amp; wharf, combined, until 6 am.</p>
<p>Tuesday 24th: -<br/>Although feeling extremely tired we went aboard a hospital yacht, the "St David" elaborately appointed for use by officers only and donated by an English Lord.</p>
<p>At 11 am with happy hearts our unit was marched to the rest camp lying 1 mile out and there began an encampment which I knew was to be extremely pleasant for the park surroundings were glorious. I thought that Southampton was as ideal a spot as one could wish for to spend a week or so in and I hoped that I should not be disappointed.</p>
<p>The night was quietly spent for all were too tired to do anything but sleep and we did until 9 am next morn.</p>
<p>August 25th: -<br/>A beautiful day. Parade held at 10 am &amp; the glorious news announced that leave may be given us in a few days.
