<p>was never shelled. C Squadron came back from Quinn's Post just after 6.a.m. and a sad spectacle they presented too. About 27 men were lying in front of the trench dead and are still there and very large part of the <strike>reman </strike> remainder were wounded and were being carried down to the beach. Those men of C Squadron had had no sleep the previous night and then at 2.pm or thereabouts they were ordered to get out over the parapet of the trench and they were out there in front for over two hours and just at daylight the lucky ones got back.</p>
