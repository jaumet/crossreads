<p>only put a couple more in though.<br/>The Coy. was ordered to move at once &amp; I with Ferranti &amp; a few more of the 14th who had arrived by that time gave a hand &amp; soon had them clear. We were in a direct line with a Railhead Fritz was feeling for. We remained there for the night &amp; moved off early next morning &amp; pitched our camp in the open near to the 14th Coy. An exciting experience I assure you.<br/>The poor chaps were buried next day in the Bapaume cemetery without any fuss. A clergyman officiated. They were all fine fellows. I shall never forget Easter Saturday. We were only a few days</p>
