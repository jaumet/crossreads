<p>Jan: 3<br/>Usual routine.  At mess to-day a youth is rather rude to me &ndash; my vis-&agrave;-vis, a rough Bushman, turns to the youth and says, "Aven't you got an eart, mate; Can't you see' e ain't used to this kind of life like we are?"  Though rough, the boys were very kind to me.</p>
<p>Jan; 4<br/>Usual routine.  Am mess orderly all day. So occupied with painting forgot to draw rations &ndash; am in a terrible state; 22 hungry men will be no joke to face. Every morning at 5 I sneak into the sergeants' mess to get a cup of coffee &ndash; up to date have had nothing stronger than water to drink as I cannot drink the tea, it being always sugared.</p>
<p>One morning , just before Reveille, went to get some tea; it was given me in a metal cup which I cannot drink out of; I said to the cook, "Wait a minute, I'll get a china cup" &ndash; this was too much for the lads, who immediately began to call out for their valets and shaving water!  I never heard the end of this incident for some days.</p>
<p>Jan; 5<br/>Recently I have been sleeping up on the sergeants' deck; strictly against regulations, but no matterl</p>
<p>Jan;  6<br/>Usual duties. Am reading Victor Hugo's "Les Miserables";</p>
