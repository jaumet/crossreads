<p>1 March 1918 Friday (X to X crossed out)<br/>Inspection by Gen <strike>Chaytor.</strike>   Windy &amp; cold-  The Australasians arrived with local Papers-  I notice from one Dec 22, an article about [indecipherable] Grain at Corowa- [indecipherable] lot for the horses &amp; they do well on it, mixed with Barley Straw, the latter is known as Tibbin in Egypt &amp; we did not like it at first, but realize now what excellent fodder it is-   X As a result of inspection lost 6 horses they are wonderful animals many been tied up for 3 &frac12; years issued with horse shoes &amp; like Crimea all <u>HIND</u> &amp; no coal to forge them!! X</p>
