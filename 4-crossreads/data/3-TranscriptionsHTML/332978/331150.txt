<p>71 October 6th<br/>Went for a walk this mng &ndash; very cold out &ndash; Concert held this evening when a French sketch was produced with great result &ndash; visited every room with Lt Montague who was my wife for the occasion &ndash;</p>
<p>October 7th Sunday<br/>Church service conducted by Comm Lewis this mng &ndash; Did not go &ndash; stayed in bed till 11 am &ndash;</p>
<p>Played Bridge &ndash; Raining all day &amp; very cold.</p>
<p>We have pushed at Ypres advancing 5 Kilos on a 20 mile front &ndash; 5 German Div. were concentrating preparing for an offensive &amp; were mostly taken prisoners. Our losses were slight &ndash; The Australians took part &ndash; we also surprised a Turkish force taking several thousand prisoners, including a Divisional Hqrs &ndash; General Holmes our Div Commr has been killed &ndash; The Commdt has returned &ndash;</p>
<p>October 8th<br/>Walk held this mng, but very few attended as it was so cold &amp; miserable I did not go &ndash;</p>
<p>Recd letters from Army &amp; Navy Store, Gertie, Birthday Pcs from Aunt Min, Mother Father, Doris Edie &amp; Bill &amp; Victor all addressed care Roffey &ndash; The verses are indeed most appropriate &ndash; Recd letters from Annie with photo of Capt Davis; PC from Van Der Zeyde advising me of books that have been despatched a letter from Bert, another</p>
