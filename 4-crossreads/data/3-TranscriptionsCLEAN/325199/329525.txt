 Thursday July 13. 1916. It is hot to day. There are clouds around the horizon but none overhead. There was a strong head wind last night & its continues with slight moderation today. The course is 4 points N of N.W. 
 A wireless message states that the British captured a heavy howitzer and 3 field guns. After ten days and nights of continual fighting our troops captured the whole of the enemys first system of defence on a front of 14000 yards. 
 Friday July 14. 1916. It is dull & cloudy & misty this morning. The wind is from the North. The course is 2 points North of North West.
