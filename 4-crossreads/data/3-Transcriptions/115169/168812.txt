<p>a port in Colombia South America, - we thoroughly examined the harbour as "Karlsruhe" and her supply vessels were still a mystery, and we knew a German steamer was harbouring inside &ndash; We left here at 8 am. for "Savanilla" also in Colombia, arriving there at noon and anchored; British Consul came on board, and at 6 o/c PM we left for St Lucia</p>
<p>[The Karlsruhe was destroyed by an internal explosion on Nov.4,1914]<br/>July 26th [1915]  en route at 7.30 am we passed and examined the island of Curacoa [Curacao] and Santa Marta</p>
<p>July 30th  at noon we arrived at St Lucia and tied up alongside coaling wharf at 1.30 PM and remained a few days</p>
<p>Aug 4th  at 6 P.M. we left for Barbados, and during the night, exercises was carried out.</p>
<p>Aug 5th  at 8 a.m. we anchored off Barbados, and remained a few days, having a most enjoyable change, in beautiful weather</p>
<p>Aug 9th  Early a.m. the Conde arrived &amp; anchored</p>
<p>Aug 10th  A gale sprang up suddenly, as a result the "Conde" took her departure also the R.M.S.P. "Chaleur" and a Norwegian steamer, we remained</p>
