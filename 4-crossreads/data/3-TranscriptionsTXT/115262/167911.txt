 Thursday 24 May 1917. 
 Now well out into the open sea and nearing the &quot;danger zone&quot;. A very calm sea today hardly a ripple on the water &amp; a fresh breeze blowing.&nbsp;(11 Knots an hr today.) Awnings spread today. Got into shorts and put away woollen clothing. The opening concert on board ship this afternoon (Empire Day) 
 False &quot;fire alarms&quot; at 2.15pm. 
  Absolutely  no light of any description after dusk from now on 
 Cruiser on alert now. Tonight sea is &quot;as smooth as glass&quot;. 
 Compulsory washing parade in morng. 
 Cake cigarettes, lollies &amp; apples issued by Y.M.C.A to all on board. 
 A scare l ast  this morning at 3 a.m. Cruiser prepared for action when we came acrosss a sailing boat that altered her course quickly &amp; put on speed.
