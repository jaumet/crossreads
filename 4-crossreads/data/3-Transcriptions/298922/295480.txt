<p>Wednesday 13 June 1917</p>
<p>Last night a terrific bombardment went on on our left. The SKY was bright with Flares &amp; SHELLS bursting &ndash; result an important forward position of Johnnie Turkstaken.</p>
<p>"Camels" had a day's sports &amp; Carnival &amp; Concert in the evening. <strike>I went &amp; saw Nowell, he's a great man among the English Aristocracy now &ndash; LORDS &amp; ragged EARLS, etc:</strike>  One item <strike>a</strike> Musical Chairs by mounted Camels was screamingly funny &ndash; Camels are riden in a circle, bags on ground at intervals, on the Band stopping, Camels are made to sit on nearest bag.</p>
<p>[The following paragraph crossed through.]  BEER night again &amp; many have yet to learn "How much they can carry". Broken noses &amp; more SKIN off the result.</p>
