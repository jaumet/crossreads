<p>Sept. 28 Tuesday 1915<br/>Replyed to Mr Palethorpes letter also Mothers.<br/>We hear that a postal staff is coming direct from Australia to work the Office here. They are on their way.</p>
<p>Sept. 29 Wednesday 1915<br/>Another mail went out today sent Maude short note.<br/>One of our chaps was caught out after hours tonight, but broke away from the Guard &amp; ran for his life. He got rid of His pursuers &amp; arrived back to his room knocked out.</p>
<p>Sept. 30 Thursday 1915<br/>Working as usual</p>
<p>Oct. 1 Friday 1915<br/>Working all day went out to see young Watkins, He is leaving the hospital this evening.<br/>15000 Greeks marched through Alexandra to embark for Greece. Great excitement.<br/>The procession was a very plain one, a little music,  but no military look about it. All were dressed in civilian clothes, &amp; walked along in an easy way.</p>
