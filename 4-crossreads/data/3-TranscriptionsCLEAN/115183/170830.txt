In the afternoon I walked into Douleiu village and had a yarn with Jim Prentice, then on to the YMCA where I spent the afternoon writing home. 
 (Written 19/2/1918 in a captured German pill box near Messines, a relay post) 21 June 1917. We fell in at 9.30 and marched with full kit back to Westhof Corps Main Dressing Station and took over there. This dressing station was like the one at Pont d'Achelles about 2 miles away built for the Battle of Messines and composed of Nissen huts and Nissen hospital huts. It was run by the New Zealanders and Tommies. Here we were camped in some of the large marquees floored with tarpaulins and painted in strange colours to avoid observation.  22 June.
