<p>captured positions.  As before Coys were moved in accordance with the location of their work.  By end of July 3&frac12; miles of trench was deepened to 5' 6" deep &amp; 5 feet wide while 1&frac12; miles of new trenches were dug.  A saw mill at Heilly was operated by the battalion under Lt Hassall &ndash; timber being felled near the river floated down to the mill broken down &amp; sawn into suitable sizes  in all some 40,000 lineal feet was thus dealt with. Just before leaving this sector 'B' &amp; 'C' Coys took part in another operation near Morlancourt where trenches leading to the captured positions were again successfully dug - 'C' Coys work under Capt Buchan on this occasion being particularly fine.  On the 30th July the battalion moved out of this sector back <strike>&amp;after one night at the</strike> to its old camp at Bussy-les-Daous.  <strike>after one night</strike><br/>Here a thorough rest was indulged in, bathing concerts, &amp; sports meetings being regularly held.  It was evident however that from the amount of movement of guns, tanks &amp; transport generally that was taking place at night that some big <strike>movement</strike> "stunt" was pending.  Rumours of all sorts were naturally current but strict secrecy was kept &amp; all ranks were much relieved on Aug 7th when <strike>it was announced</strike> a move forward that night was announced to a bivouac just behind Villers Brettoneux which was to be the assembly position for an operation on a unprecedented scale to take place at dawn the following morning.</p>
