<p>Friday 17th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Dusty &amp; hot.  Unbearable.  Attended Kursaal at night.  Egyptian opera by College Students.  Poor acting.</p>
<p>Saturday 18th Mch 1916</p>
<p>"Khamsheen" all day.  Huge clouds of sand, dust &amp; other debris enveloped the camp all day long &ndash; the wind blew with hurricane force &amp; <strike>was</strike> spitefully <strike>but</strike> played with our tents, mess huts, etc.</p>
<p>Applied for duty as Shorthandwriter &amp; Typist at Base Office.</p>
<p>Sunday 19th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Heavy rain storm early hours.  Many men, without tents, woke up like half-drowned rats.  Much grumbling &amp; growling.</p>
<p><u>Sunday dinner</u> - tea, cheese &amp; jam &amp; bread.</p>
<p>Monday 20th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Routine day.  Base band played us down to Ghezueh/Ghizeh Gardens &amp; back.</p>
<p>Tuesday 21st Mch 1916</p>
<p>Reported at 10 am at Agricultural Hall in reference to position as Shorthandwriter.  Sent from there to AIF Hqrs where I was detailed for duty in Correspondence Room, Hqrs Staff.  No mention made as to extra rank.</p>
<p>Wednesday 22nd Mch 1916</p>
<p>Heavy day's work.  Commenced at 7.30 am &amp; finished 6 pm  Billeted in large house, (ex Premier's).  Mess allowance 3/- day.  Wet canteen.  Credit given.  I appreciate the change of conditions.</p>
<p>Received PC, from Alexandria that matter of mail was being looked into.</p>
<p><strike>Sent Mab 7 P.C's &amp; Mum (1) Grace (2).</strike></p>
<p>Thursday 23rd Mch 1916</p>
<p>Another heavy day.  Have met Noel Hueston who is on the Staff (of Kogarah).  Informs me that his brother Clyde had shot himself.</p>
<p>Friday 24th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Inoculated for para typhoid.</p>
<p>Saturday 25th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Paid &pound;5.  "Went mad".</p>
<p>Sunday 26th Mch 1916</p>
<p>Pocket book, Paybook, photos etc. stolen.  Also &pound;2.10.0.  Also references.</p>
