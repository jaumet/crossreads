<p>33<br/>cases hundreds of yards apart &amp; it is through the gaps that the enemy may with ease force his way to the canal provided we were not on the alert &amp; qui vive.<br/>The work is tiresome &amp; very fatiguing &amp; demands great strength and endurance. The heat of the sun is terrific &amp;at times almost unbearable but as usual the night are delightful, and freshy.<br/>Light horse camps are situated at different points quite close to the firing line &amp; so are always handy in case of an attack. The horses look well &amp; fresh &amp; seem to bear the weather admirably.<br/>A road runs out within a mile or two of the firing line; also a railway line at rail end are stores,water supply</p>
