  MELBOURNE . 10th February 1920. 
 &nbsp; 
 Dear Sir, 
 &nbsp;&nbsp; I have much pleasure in forwarding hereunder copy of extract from the Second Supplement No.31583 to the London Gazette, dated 4th October 1919, relating to the conspicuous services rendered by yourself whilst serving with the Australian Imperial Force. 
 ---------------------------------------------------- 
  AWARDED BAR TO THE MILITARY CROSS . 
 &nbsp; 
 &nbsp;&nbsp; With reference to the award conferred as announced in the London Gazette, dated 8th March 1919, the follwoing is the statement of service for which the decoration was conferred:- 
 &nbsp; 
 Lieutenant (temporary Captain)&nbsp; CHARLES HAROLD PETERS, M.C. 
 &nbsp; 
 &nbsp;&nbsp; Near Bony, from 29th September to 2nd October, 1918 he was in command of a company, which he led across country swept by heavy machine-gun and shell fire, and which mopped up two enemy trenches.&nbsp; Later, with an additional company, he mopped up the Hindenburg Line on his company&#39;s frontage, finally consolidating on the St.Quentin Canal Tunnel.&nbsp; Throughout he showed great gallantry and able leadership. 
 ---------------------------------------------------- 
 &nbsp;&nbsp; The above has been promulgated in Commonwealth of Australia Gazette No.10, dated 29th January, 1920. 
 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Yours faithfully, 
 (SIGNED)&nbsp;&nbsp; W .MACKINTOSH, 
 Captain, 
 for Officer i/c Base/records. 
  Captain C.H.Peters, M.C.
