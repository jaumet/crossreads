<p>26 July 1918<br/>Friday<br/>Weather getting considerably warmer. We passed through a large school of whales this morning of a large Kind. One was almost run down by us &amp; then passed quite close by the Port-side of the ship. A little Wireless war news through Franco American army doing well. Nothing else of importance.</p>
<p>1918 July 27<br/>Saturday<br/>Nothing eventful to note. Reported that submarines are opperating rather plentifully in these waters &ndash; several ships being sunk &amp; some submarines sighted. Rather an anxious time &amp; a very strict lookout has to be Kept. Reported good news from the front in France. First pay day at sea.</p>
