<p>The company in the salient (a horseshoe bend in the line) was being relieved when the bombardment started. It was the heaviest bombardment by the Germans I have ever seen. Even on the Somme the shelling though more prolonged was not so intense. The whole of the front line was a mass of fire and the noise was fearful.</p>
<p>There were just on two hundred casualties among the two companies and the Germans got into the trench. They got away with several prisoners and two Stokes mortars, a gun which was, we tried to keep secret. The 20th haven't recovered from that setback yet, and they are often met with the gibe &ndash; Who lost the trench mortars.</p>
<p>We "stood to" all that night and I had another narrow escape. I was fusing a box of bombs</p>
