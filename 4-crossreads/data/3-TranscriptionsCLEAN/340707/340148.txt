  
 To night I find a little cafe where one can dine conveniently well for 3 to 4 francs & which several American officers have turned into a mess. I meet here [Maintor] a fine chap & destined to be my sole companion. 
 13-6-19 This day I have nothing to eat till dinner & have a long yarn with Madam who runs the hotel. Louvre again this evening at 3.30 till 6.0 pm & then [indecipherable] invites me to the concert organised by the Americans prior to their departure. 
 I spend all the morning in the garden de Luxemburg the prettiest gardens in this pretty city. The play of fountain & walls of spray from the watering pipes under shaded walk among sculptures lends an enchantment to this fairy dell of green grass & greener trees. 
 Alongside of me is a musical student who is huming Il Balen & on the other side a would be artist who is spending his time feeding the sparrows with morsels of bread which he has specially brought hither for the purpose 
 14-6-19 Sat Rose in time to go to concert which is really excellent A W Browning an American
