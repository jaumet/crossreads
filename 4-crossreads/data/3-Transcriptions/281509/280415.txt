<p>11 July 1916<br/>Tuesday <br/>Lignacourt</p>
<p>Reveille at 5.30.a.m. breakfast of tea &amp; toast 6 a.m. To work after,-- striking camp, &amp; clearing up. Leave camp at 8.30 a.m. March to Bailleel 6 miles with heavy pack up! Oh those packs! Won't we be glad to see the last amp; switched  of them.  Entrained at 11.30 a.m. passed through Hazelbrouk &amp; switched off side line &amp; arrived ultimately at Doullens. Disentrained &amp; because of some hitch in railway arrangements commenced a march destined to be one <strike>of</strike> long &amp; renouned.  Marched with heavy pack up from 7.30 to 3 a.m. 1 &frac12; hrs rest. Lost our way in dark. marched on &amp; on &amp; on. such a nightmare of weariness &amp; pain  Hour after hour <br/>&amp; no nearer our goal. At long th at 3 a.m. Colonel gives up &amp; we are allowed to billet in stable. Stiff &amp; sore round at 10.30 a.m. breakfast on bully &amp; biscuit &amp; renew our march.</p>
