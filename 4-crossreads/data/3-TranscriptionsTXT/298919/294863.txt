  Monday 16th August 1915.   Did not relish arising at 2am to resume watch, but as the weather was only moderate cool, the hardship was not very trying.  I took a stroll around the deck before listening in &amp; could see the riding lights of many other vessels in our vicinity.  About 4am when dawn was beginning to break, the anchor was picked up, and our journey resumed.  We had not been going 15 minutes when we were enveloped by a light fog, which obscured the land from our sight and necessitated the navigators keeping a very strict lookout.
