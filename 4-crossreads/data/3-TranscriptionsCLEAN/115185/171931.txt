
5 - The food is very good & plenty of it. Bread & butter delicious - plenty of hot milk tea & coffee at every meal. The french woman is rather charming - and has been very gracious to me, she cannot speak English - but has an interpreter woman with her.The room I share with Sister Dowe & Sister Louise Young R.P.A.H. both - and Sister MacDonnell our home-sister. It has two large French windows onto a balcony overlooking the citadel, the desert in the distance and pyramids of Gisa - a wonderful view. The sky is wonderfully vast. The first day here I went to tea with Lady Graham at the Continental hotel - her son Capt. Stewart Graham was with us at Lemnos. He was at P.A.H. too. She was very kind & motherly - she works at this hospital no 1. A.S.H. daily. While she chatted to us after tea - she knitted away at a baby shirt - of a new young Barber whose mother is staying at the hotel with her husband who is with his regiment in Egypt.
