<p>Sunday 16 June 1918<br/>Had some shootg. at Toseland.  Quite good sport.</p>
<p>Monday 17 June 1918<br/>Called at Hunts &amp; saw the Mater.  Food is plentiful at the Hotel we are stayg. at.</p>
<p>Tuesday 18 June 1918<br/>Tennis at the Knights.  People all round the place are treating us extremely well.  They have not had many Australians down here.</p>
<p>Wednesday 19 June 1918<br/>Hear that Seward one of the farmers has two Aussies staying with him.  Must go over &amp; see if I know 'em.</p>
<p>Thursday 20 June 1918<br/>Tennis at the Knights.  Bought three fowls at the market for Mater.</p>
<p>Friday 21 June 1918<br/>Drove into Hunts &amp; had tea, with the Taskers &amp; with Miss Birt, Matron of the V.A.D. show, who is an Australian girl.</p>
<p>Saturday 22 June 1918<br/>Cycled to the Knights for Tennis.  Heard that Reg was over in Blighty.</p>
