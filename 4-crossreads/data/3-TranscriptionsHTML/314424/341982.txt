<p>[four lines of shorthand &ndash; I had 3 letters from my wife this evening No 120 dated February 23. 121 February 24 and 122 March 4 the latter being written from Merrigum x ? ? ?x]</p>
<p>This evening several of our planes surprised an enemy plane that had just emerged from a cloud &amp; forced it to land behind our lines.</p>
<p>Tuesday April 30. 1918. 1366.<br/>Showery most of the day.</p>
<p>This is the first village that I have been in, in France where there was any difficulty in getting water. There is one well in the village &amp; it is 250 ft to the water. Most of the water is carried 4 kilos &amp; when the tanks are empty we have to fall back on the well.</p>
<p>[one line of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my wife today letter 126]</p>
<p>Wednesday May 1. 1918. 1367.<br/>May Day! What a contrast to May Day in Australia &amp; especially in Melbourne. Here all is war &amp; preparations for war. There war is unknown &amp; its horrors unrealized except by the few</p>
<p>Cold &amp; cloudy this morning.</p>
<p>[one line of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my father today at dinner time]</p>
<p>This evening &amp; tonight our artillery severely straffed the enemy.</p>
<p>Thursday May 2. 1918. 1368.<br/>A fine day.</p>
<p>I was up early this morning &amp; got the breakfast ready. The artillery continues to bombard the enemy this morning. Yesterday 4 tins of cocoa &amp; milk were issued to the Transports for 40 men, &amp; today we had it for dinner. It was rather wishey washey.</p>
<p>Friday May 3. 1918. 1369.<br/>A fine day but rather cloudy.</p>
