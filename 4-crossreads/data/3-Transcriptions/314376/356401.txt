<p>90<br/>roof came in. we had to set up and rebuild our domicile as best we could. Lights were unprocurable.</p>
<p>Leaving this place we set off for still nearer the front trenches in what are called "supports".</p>
<p>The same (proceedings) procedure was again repeated as regards dugouts which were in an even worse state than those of Switch Trench.</p>
<p>It is the duty of troops who occupy supports to do fatigue work i.e., carry duckboards, galvd iron &ndash; supply parties for cleaning and widening trenches and take provisions and rations to the troops who occupy the front line.</p>
<p>The cooks were quartered in the cellars of an old house</p>
