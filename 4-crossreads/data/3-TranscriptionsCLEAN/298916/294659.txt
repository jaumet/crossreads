Several fainted & had to be carried up. I went up "forrard" after my watch & saw the soldiers sleeping on the top deck. How sad it looked. Some 120 men crowded together in hammocks & some on the bare decks sprawled out in all positions to try & keep cool. I turned in about nine pm but could not sleep.
11th November. Sucani woke me at a quarter to two, & I was just dozing again when there was a vivid flash that lit the ship up like a searchlight & a swish like a cane cutting the air. Following this came a tremendous crash that shook the crockery in the pantry & made the A13 shiver. Teddy came rushing inside in his "panjamas" in a great state. I was rushing outside & met him at the door. Everybody's thoughts turned to the Karlsrhue which was supposed to be with the Emden.
