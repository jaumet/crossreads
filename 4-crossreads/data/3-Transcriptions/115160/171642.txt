<p>April 5: still camped at Pommera, but proceeded thro&#39; Doullens to Vignacourt to load. &nbsp;Great congestion in yard.</p>
<p>April 6: Struck camp at 7 a.m. and proceeded via Doullens and Picquigy to Ailly-Sur-Somme to load. Later pitched camp at St. Saveur, 2 k.m. away. &nbsp;Three of us billetted.&nbsp;</p>
<p>April 11: Went to Amiens to load at 10 a.m. City deserted and being shelled.</p>
<p>April 14: Railway yard shelled. &nbsp;One of our Section hit in stomach with part of a shell, subsequently dying of wounds. &nbsp;Another taken to hospital with the shock but recovered.</p>
<p>April 16: Loading again at Ailly-sur-Somme at midday. &nbsp;Nice warm day.</p>
<p>April 18-19-20. Very cold and unseasonable weather. Snow fall during the day&nbsp;of 19th and much snow and frost on ground on morning of 20th.</p>
<p>May. Medical Stores</p>
<p>May 31. Loaded at 6 a.m. at Ailly, and after dinner packed up at short notice and proceeded to Raineville Rd, between Allonville &amp; Poulainville, near Amiens.</p>
<p>June 1 Loading at Paulainville at 8 a.m. &nbsp;An aerodrome not far away &amp; much stunting including flying at few feet above the ground and tree tops. &nbsp;Under canvas &amp; dug in</p>
