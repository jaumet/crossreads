<p>Tuesday Aug 12th 1919.<br/>On handing in the Paper, I then was given an order for my Railway Holiday Pay, one day for each Month I was on Active Service which amounted to 32 day's, also my Railway Watch which I handed in when I enlisted, then I proceeded to Perth and received my cheque also a Free Pass for myself, Wife and Family from Aug 12th to September 17th 1919, And made arrangements to resume Duty <strike>make</strike> on Sept 1st instead of Sept 18th 1919.<br/>On Sept 10th 1919, I cease to be a Soldier of the Australian Imperial Force's after serving for 2 Year's and 345 day's. And I honestly confess it has been the best 2 year's and 345 days of my Life and I have no regret's over inlisting, and putting the Bad times against the Good Times, the latter by a long way outweigh's the former, I honestly Thank God for giving me strength <strike>to be</strike> in carrying out my Duties and sparing me to return to my Dear One's once again, also I have enjoyed the best of health and had a splendid Education and a Grand Trip and a very pleasant one whilst I was a Soldier in the Australian Imperial Force's<br/>Working on the Railway's of France and Belgium as a Locomotive Engine Driver<br/>Doing my Bit for the Cause of Civilisation &amp; Freedom.<br/>So at last I have finished my Diary of my Life as a Soldier and I trust it will be as great a pleasure to whoever Reads it in the day's to come, as it has been to me to write it.<br/>Benjamin, Alfred, Cohen No 882. 5th Aus Broad Guage Railway Operating Coy A.I.F. <br/>1916 to 1919</p>
