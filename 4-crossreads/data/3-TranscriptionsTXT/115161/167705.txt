 In our endeavours to forget the past four and a half years of barbarianism and savagery, let us not forget the ideal for which we fought and suffered.&nbsp; Let the absent ones be ever in our thoughts to remind us of the &quot;cause&quot; for which they laid down their valuable lives.

 CHAPTER XXXV111. 
  IMPROVED CONDITIONS . 
 We remain at Cavillon till the middle of this wonderful month, and then move to a town only very lately vacated by our late enemy. 
  BUSSIGNY . 
 where we secure very comfortable billets.&nbsp; The whole atmosphere is now changed, the troops generally have better times, and greater freedom.&nbsp; Of course everybody and everything has to be still in readiness in the event of things not being as they appear, but the main weight has been lifted, and there is a general feeling that very soon demobilisation will commence. 
 The &quot;liquid&quot; celebration of the armistice lasts a long time. We are ever running across old friends, who invariably ask you if you can &quot;stop one&quot;.&nbsp; The estaminet keepers become affluent and are doubtless for many reasons quite sorry that the troops may soon be departing.
