<p>you fully thereon.<br/>MacLaurin was killed on Tuesday April 27th. His dug out was near the top of the ridge &ndash; now known as MacLaurin's Hill &ndash; just behind the firing line. He had just come out of his dug out, when a sniper got him through the head. He lived about an hour &amp; twenty minutes, but never regained consciousness &amp; of course suffered no pain.<br/>Irvine was shot almost the same moment. He was bringing a party of men across the valley, and was warned about the snipers, when he was shot through the heart. Death was instantaneous.<br/>King was shot on Friday or Saturday April 30 or May 1. Not a bad wound, through the fleshy part of the thigh. He had no complications &amp; was back again in 3 or 4 weeks.</p>
<p>There is now a certain amount of organisation at Alex. but one would naturally expect that after 5 months.<br/>Nasty thing to say, but I am afraid this Dardanelles show has been most grossly mismanaged from the very start. I cannot of course write very much, but really some of the things have been quite appalling &ndash; there is an old saying "The mistakes of Politicians &amp; Staff Officers have to be paid for by the infantry" This show proves it. When I left Alex. we were very little nearer the Narrows than on the landing in April, but the casualties have been enormous. I do not think I am wrong in saying about 80,000. Thanks to a merciful Providence, I have been blessed with good nerves &ndash; if not I could never have stood some of the things I have</p>
