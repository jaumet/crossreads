<p>7<br/>having stopped us twice at Gaza (at the [indecipherable] Gaza!!) grew stupidly over confident &amp; amazing as it seems the Germans seemed to share in his arrogance. He had a wonderful natural position &amp; on that he was content with <u>one</u> line &amp; even that line was for the most part unwired.</p>
<p>Your son (we were so pleased that he was forced home to you) will know what that means &amp; how keen a joke it is after France that the Light-Horsemen were able to gallop <u>over</u> the enemy trenches at Beersheba. It was grand work even against rifles and machine guns but of course a little wire &amp; it had been impossible. Likewise a few lines of wire across the narrow plain from the sea to the mountains would have at once put an end to our triumphant pursuit.</p>
