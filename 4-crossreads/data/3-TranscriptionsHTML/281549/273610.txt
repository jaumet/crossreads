<p><u> PART  111 </u></p>
<p>Concerning the trip across the English Channel from Folkestone to Boulogne &amp; the Fighting in france till 11th April 1917</p>
<p>When everybody was got on board, there were two full boat loads of men belonging to different Armies, Australians, Canadians, &amp; British all boxed together.</p>
<p>At about 3 o'clock we left the wharf &amp; proceeded at full speed across the channel toward Boulogne.</p>
<p>Four British Destroyers escorted us across, The Channel was slightly rough &amp; every body on board had to wear lifebelts in case we were torpeaoded by an Enemy Submarine,</p>
<p>We eventually reached Boulogne without any accidents &amp; disembarked at Dusk.</p>
<p>Boulogne Harbour is not a very good port for shipping it is much too small for very large ships, and at low tide it smells something awful especially in the upper reaches.</p>
