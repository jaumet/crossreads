<p>Needle Trench. HQ &amp; Sig officers are in a large German dugout. We came in last night. Fritz sent over five shells as we were coming along, the first landed only about fifty yards behind, howitzer shell I think, it gave us a fright. It is all mud again owing to the rain &amp; thaw. There are duckboards all the way from [Bernajay?], it is a good walk, but when that first shell came over we forgot all about our fatigue. Yesterday I got an old parcel from Wilfred it was broken &amp; had been repacked<br/>Feb. 23rd Needle Trench<br/>We leave to morrow for the front line. The Somme is a great deal quieter now, to what it was when we first came to it. Fritz occasionally takes it into his head to send some over. Yesterday afternoon he put a good few into [indecipherable] woods which is about a mile to our right, it was very interesting watching.
