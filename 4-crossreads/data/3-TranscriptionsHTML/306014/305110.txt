<p>Mch 13</p>
<p>A final rehearsal of our drill took place in the morning before the Adjutant &amp; the Major of our T.B. &amp; we then set to &amp; polished ourselves up for the afternoon.</p>
<p>After dinner we march 4 miles to meet the gun carriage &amp; coffin &amp; then accompanied it to the Church in Durrington to the tune &amp; time of the "Dead March in Saul" and also Chopin's "March Funebre" both of which were rendered in grand style by our full Battalion Band, and it takes a band to give the full spirit to these grand marches. Our party in all consisted of the band, led by the drum-major with his huge silver mounted baton, officers, &amp; about 200 men as mourners.</p>
<p>The service at the Durrington Church was short &amp; impressive especially because of the old hymns which we sung, viz "Rock of Ages"</p>
