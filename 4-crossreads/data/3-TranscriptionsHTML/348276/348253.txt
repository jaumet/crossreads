<p>War Diary<br/>1915  1st Dec.<br/>Rhododendron Spur &ndash; G.O.C. called and discussed thinning line of defence.  Meeting of C.Os. after and explained the method to be adopted. i.e. by holding fire trench with outpost groups, men in supports &ndash; and pushing out strong night patrols in Dere to prevent surprise.<br/>Lt. Grant to Mudros vice Capt. Kidd (invalided) to command Bde. rest details &ndash; Fired Sniper's Nest with 69th Battery &ndash; results fair &ndash; Rev. Goodman joined &ndash; C of Inquiry on stolen rum &ndash; Lt. Col Nicholas president.  F.G. Courtmartialled for drunkenness &ndash; to sit to-morrow.  Major Aldennan president.  Snow disappearing.  Lt. Mckellar reported to 10th L.H. &ndash; two working parties of one Officer and 50 others to make road near beach for mules &ndash;</p>
<p>2nd Dec.<br/>Major Todd D.S.O. returned to duty &ndash; F.G.C.M. [Field General Court Martial] sitting &ndash; usual strafing from Chunuk Bair on our mules &ndash; Sanders back from Mudros canteen stores &ndash; Capt. Bayley 9th L.H. to Imbros re canteen S.</p>
<p>3rd Dec.<br/>G.O.C. Army Corps and G.O.C. Divn. inspected Area and trenches &ndash; all very good: owing to loss of barges in late heavy weather reduction in some rations:  Informed by G.O.C. that 250 of the 9th A. Corps were drowned and some 6,000 evacuated sick, result of snow &ndash; This in about a week &ndash; Special instructions issued re precautions against cold &ndash;</p>
<p>4th Dec.<br/>Major Todd took over Comd. of 10th L.H. vice Lt. Col. Nicholas who has done most excellent work while in command.  A sound, firm and capable officer.  17th Field Siege Battery shelled "Sniper's Nest" and completely shambled it. &ndash; splendid shooting, fully a dozen direct hits &ndash; H.E. &ndash; must have been heavy casualties &ndash; nothing could have lived in it. &ndash; This had been a thorn in our side since Troops landed, as an observation and sniping point.</p>
