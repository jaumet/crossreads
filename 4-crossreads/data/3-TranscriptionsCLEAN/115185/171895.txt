It was he who later told Lieut Wrens, of the Lancashire Fusiliers - that a sister of Gibson's was here. He as you know came to see me and was full of Gibson's praises and hoped he wouldn't get a commission in the Kitchener's new army - as he'd done - but get one in the Australian or N.Z. There are I believe only 20 out of Gibson's Batt - left - It was terribly pathetic this morning to see the gaunt looking men going back.The first Brigade has gone back - and with it Geoffrey Street - whose Mother was a Miss Austin - he is a very fine boy - very clever, looks very well so full of spirits - singing and dancing round like a child - in the same way as Rosamond does. He, Captain Clive Thompson Captain Floyd, a young Englishman - who joined our troops in Sydney, and has won renown since - Military Cross, & a French Cross of War of some sort, and has been mentioned in Sir Ian's dispatches. He walked up to a machine gun put the owners to rout with his revolver, and it, out of action with the same weapon - of course no one thought he'd return alive.
