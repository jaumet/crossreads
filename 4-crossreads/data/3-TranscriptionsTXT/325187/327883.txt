 [Saturday 20 May 1916] 
 Ere this reaches you no harm done &ndash; as Mails will be scarce.  If a man is wounded here he is put in a Cacholet sort of cradle &amp; put on a Camel.  Some of our men carried a fly blown wounded Yeoman 7 miles over the sand he was so thankful &amp; [ Following paragraphs are crossed out] so scornful of his own crowd for having left him. My "Papers" re my remarks have come back from the G.O.C. with a minute on them for me to NOTE-  However I'm quite happy to be back with the Regiment   Big guns firing in the Distance today I think from the Warships.
