<p>August 3rd  37<br/>since Aug 5th 1914, &amp; in that time He has learnt German &amp; French.</p>
<p>The Comman[dant] today revealed the fact that there is a great desire for peace all over the world. There is a conference of all the Allied Powers being held in London today, &amp; tomorrow L. George is to make a Speech as to what our war aims are also our Peace terms. There is little to the further internal trouble in Russia again, at present they are retreating like a wild mob of civilians, having lost all idea of militarianism. The have made big success on the Western front, near Ypres.</p>
<p>Made a meat pudding also Jam Pancakes, both being a wonderful success especially the former.</p>
<p>Quite a number of officers paraded sick to the Dr today so as to have their names submitted, to be sent to Switzerland to recuperate for a few months, quite a num[ber] failed in their endeavours to [indecipherable] Dr.</p>
<p>I was informed that my name had already been submitted, to be exchanged to England &amp; failing that I would certainly be sent to Holland.</p>
<p>Played Bridge.</p>
<p>August 4th<br/>Slept in till 11 am. my foot very painful so I am resting it.</p>
<p>Wine barrage lifted today.</p>
<p>My wound seems to be opening more every day. Suppose it is because I am not receiving proper attention here.</p>
<p>Good concert held tonight, 50&#37; of the officers celebrated today the 3rd "anniversary of the outbreak of war in rather an intoxicating manner. There was a great hubbub about midnight &ndash;</p>
<p>Nothing startling in todays communiqu&eacute;; we are still making successes on the western front.</p>
<p>From all accounts, every body speaks in England of the war as lasting another year &ndash; rather a hopeful outlook &ndash; &amp; especially L. George [indecipherable]</p>
<p>Recd letter from George Esther of Merwood.</p>
<p>August 5th (Sunday)<br/>Raining today. Wrote letter no 11 to Mother &amp; father &ndash; had my foot dressed</p>
