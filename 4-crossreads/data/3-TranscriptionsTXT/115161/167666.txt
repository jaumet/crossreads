&#39;twas a great life.&nbsp; No swank with tables or chairs, and silver-ware.&nbsp; But now all that was changed, I should say nearly all, my rug I still retained as also did the chats show their affection for me by refusing to leave me in my altered circumstances on the contrary they seemed to cling the tighter to me.&nbsp; I needed no physical jerks to keep me &quot;fit&quot;, while they were about me.&nbsp; They kept me continuously on the move, while they on their part remained almost stationery.&nbsp; They had two or three portions of my anatomy which they considered more delicate and tender then [than?] the rest, and refused to be moved therefrom.
