<p>28 May 1916 Sun<br/>Kept going all day. German machine very low overhead seems to be observing. The guns soon opened out &amp; we received a great number of pieces of shrapnel. After tea went to town with Les Hall Keith Harris &amp; there we met Perc Connors &amp; McCaffery. Plenty of champagne &amp; a great [text continued at top of page]</p>

<p>29 May 1916 Mon<br/>Taubes were early on the scene this morning &amp; it was not long before Steenwerck guns were at them. The mail arrived to-day &amp; I received the Enmore Gazette &amp; letter from Sugar two from home &amp; one each from Mrs. Crozier, Fred Strong, Nena Adams, &amp; Thelma Smith. A photo from Sugar of Ossie's send off. One sees all the old faces again. Arthur Preston leaves</p>
