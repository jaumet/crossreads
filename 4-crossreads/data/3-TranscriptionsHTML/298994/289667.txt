<p>Judging by preparations being made by the sailors, etc, we are nearing a port.</p>
<p>Today the sea was extraordinary calm, the heat was sweltering, the sea looked like glass. A little after 2, land appeared on the horizon, <strike>and</strike> as we neared this land, we could distinguish a sort of a hilly country.</p>
<p>The speed of the ship slackened somewhat. News spread rapidly through the ship, that the land in sight was &ndash; Sierra Leone; Freetown being the capital. The coast scenery was very fine, as we neared this new port of call.</p>
<p>About 500 yards from us, a large number of black men, in a somewhat limited amount of "ship-room" was observed. By and bye, large numbers of these peculiarly shaped craft made their appearance.</p>
