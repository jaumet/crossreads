<p>most graciously on the assembled crowds.  We extracted ourselves from the mob eventually and had dinner in a quiet little Italian resturant, opposite Victoria Station. When we had satisfied our hunger we tubed to Liverpool Street and caught a train for Epping.  We had to change at Laughton and wait for &#189; an hour, but we occupied our selves by walking down the town to a refreshment shop where we had afternoon tea.  The scenery hereabouts was magnificent.  Green meadows, flower gardens &amp; well clothed trees made the country seem a most desirable place.
