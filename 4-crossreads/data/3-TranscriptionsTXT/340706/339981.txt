Thence I catch the tram for Le park & colonnade arcade de Quis Cinquantenaire - a colossal edifice erected to commemorate the 50 years of Belgian inaugeration as a nation. It is used as an exhibition building but now as a park for ASC. The view over park & up the Boulevard is a glorious setting to this magnificent monument of inspired commemoration.
[A tiny sketch of the colossal edifice inserted here].
The lofty arcade & arch are on the same immense proportions which seem to characterise this part of the world.
I the take the tram to Tervueren & it is the most glorious trip I have ever taken. Away for nearly an hour we pass by parks with rippling streams & cascades & then dive through a grand forest miles in extent whose severed aspect & stems without a branch are of a natural beauty unknown in our part of the world.
At Tervueren there is a colonial museum much patronised by visiting colonial officers & well worth the trouble of an inspection. I then return to city & running short of funds dine at a cheap cafe but it cost 9 fr for a fish & a glass of beer. Food is very scarce here & prices are out of all proportion to the ordinary.
