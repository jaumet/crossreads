<p>He was attended to by the Bn. Dr. &amp; I noticed our Chaplain, Capt. Singhe, also present.  Collins was a chap I had much taken to, a fellow you could talk to with comradeship &amp; bonhomie, &amp; we were all sorry for him.</p>
<p>We were quietly <strike>departed</strike> sent off &amp; after an hours march came to a halt once more in "Rest Gully" where we received orders to be in readiness at 6.20 next morning to relieve in the trenches.</p>
<p><strike>I had a rotten time</strike></p>
<p>Thursday 26th Aug. 1915</p>
<p>I had a restless night.  Our Snipers on the hill above were very active &amp; stray bullets from the enemy were continually plugging into the sand around us.</p>
<p>"Rest Gully" is becoming very dirty &amp; a harbour for vermin, rats,</p>
