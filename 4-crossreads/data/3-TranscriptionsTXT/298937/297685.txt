 According to lectures &amp; instructions they had received, they were expected to get, if not right across, well into the neck of the smaller peninsula.  Slowly the fleet of boats &amp; their escort steamed up the Gulf until level with the shore where the landing was to take place. This was at a spot close to Gaba Tepe headland, but the landing subsequently took place a little further north of that. No sooner had the boats slowed up as near the land as possible when the disembarkation into smaller boats took place.
