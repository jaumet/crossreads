May 14.   We were detailed into our Company &amp; Battalion this morning.   I am now in 1st. Platoon, 'A' Coy of the 19th. Battn.   We did a little drill for the rest of the day, just to pass the time away.   We saw a number of Fritz prisoners working, down here.   They seem to have a good time and most of the boys are rather envious of them.
In the cool of the evening Alan &amp; I sat at the end of our new home and there "mooched" the delightful gloaming hours away.
May 15.   After doing a few hours drill Alan &amp; I went for a walk around the camp over some of the battle-fields of last year &amp; the year before.   All the signs of the battle still remain.   The trenches, dug-outs, ammunition dumps, shell-holes etc. etc. both those of Fritz &amp; of the Allies, bear ample witness of the bitterness of the contest.
