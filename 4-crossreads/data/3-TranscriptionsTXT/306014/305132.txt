We had a good tea at 6.15, visited the post-card shop &amp; then went for a walk along the canal bank for a mile or so with two lassies who volunteered to show us round the town. The canal is used for barge transport &amp; was chiefly interesting to Alan &amp; myself on account of the large number of locks which it has. The canal rises up a fairly steep hill by means of these &amp; there must be over twenty of them within a mile or so.
