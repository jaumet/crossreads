  
 10 - I'm afraid it is going to be the Crimea over again. What a time of tribulation for these men. I'm sure many must wish they had gone before. I must send you - Mother - some of the little letters I get from the trenches. The dull dreary monotony, no water to wash in, same clothes & boots for weeks. France is not in it with this inferno. Our men are being baptized with fire - and yet how bravely they marched back - our raw untried lazy - pleasing loving boys. It's a wonderful revelation to us who have nursed the men in the ranks of both british & Australian armies. They simply aren't to be compared for self respect - reliance - good English!!! You may laugh but the english Tommie might be speaking greek most of the time for all I know or understand. I now feel I dislike the Scotch & irish accents compared with the downright speech of the Australian Tommy - of course the N.Z. working man speaks very good English.
