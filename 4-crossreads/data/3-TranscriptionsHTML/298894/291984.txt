<p>cigarettes &amp; such like delicacies, writing paper, envelopes matches also 4 blankets all of which made too big a load to carry far so the use of the car was handy. We crossed the harbour by the official boat &amp; managed to get a lift by a G.S. waggon to the camp. It was rather a strange return as I met several fellows I had not seen for some time. The first was Keith McMurtrie I had not seen for 13 years, the next was a  Lieutenant who came over with me as a Corporal &amp; then I met a chap who was one of the first wounded I met. I slept well the first in camp after listening to an impromptu concert in the big Y.M.C.A tent. this tent has done good for the men being crowded every night.<br/>Saturday 30th Shifted camp today to another site as diptheria had broken out in the 2nd Brigade and were quarantined. We worked very hard finishing about 5.30 PM &amp; had tea about 6. I thus had a heavy day for my first day in camp but enjoyed myself well. The fellows treated me well when leaving the 1st.
