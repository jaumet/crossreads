<p>July 1915<br/>shot through the head.  He was a brave man &amp; a good soldier, took risks where he wouldn't let one of his troop.  He was grazed by a bullet in the cheek in the morning.  The Turks did a lot of firing at night &amp; late in the afternoon there was a lot of artillery firing on both sides.</p>
<p>Thursday 22nd<br/>Was deepening trenches both shifts.  Things were very quiet today except for a bit of artillery fire.</p>
<p>Friday 23rd<br/>Very warm day.  Finished deepening our firing line.  The monitor put in a few heavy shells into the Turks in front of us &amp; a cruiser further south got on to a party of Turks &amp; gave them a rough time.  Got a letter from mother.</p>
<p>Saturday 24th<br/>Started sapping a tunnel out towards the Turks whose front trenches are about 40 yards away.  3 or 4 of our chaps got hit today including Lieut Robson who died about an hour later.  The Turks</p>
