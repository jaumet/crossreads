<p>done more for us. We toasted the bread &amp; enjoyed some toast &amp; butter. Oh well I, suppose this will be our last stop in safety, as the centre of the German advance &amp; fierce fighting is not more than 20 miles from here &amp; our next move will probably be up to the line. The corporal told me a lot of Australian wounded passed through here from the trenches today. I wrote up to here in my diary in their crib, &amp; then (at 9pm) went back to my hayshed  to sleep. It was raining hard &amp; I was glad we have a warm shed with a roof on. Slept well in the hay. Sunday 7 April Got up at 8. There was a parade at 9am, &amp; a sergeant asked me what sort of a clerk I was, &amp; said to report at Brigade orderly room. Everything was chaos as the troops have only just arrived, &amp; there are no headquarters or anything yet. Did nothing but make a nominal roll of  13th Bn all day &amp; there's nothing to do, so I suppose I'll go on parade instead tomorrow. Aubrey was buzzing round working as usual, &amp; was put on</p>
