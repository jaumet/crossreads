<p>My dearest Amy</p>
<p>Many thanks indeed for your chatty &amp; cheery weekly letters, it is jolly to know you write weekly but quite a lot miscarry, however I'm hoping they will eventually catch me up &amp; anyway the ones I do get are very charming &amp; it's sweet &amp; kind of you. The last I recd. yesterday enclosed Nudges with the address of Arties' boys in &amp; I'm very glad to get [indecipherable] as it's hopeless trying to find them out without full particulars.</p>
<p>My dear I'm being both <u>good</u> and careful &ndash; So don't worry: &amp; one really has to be both for theres quite an embarassment of men round:  Stacks of them over in the rest camp those who were in first landing &amp; who escaped more or less unscathed but who &ndash; the brave dear things they are &ndash; go back cheerily knowing that its a hundred chances against their ever coming back again. It's fairly heartbreaking at times. The stories one hears told so casually, in little terse sentences leave one gasping at times &amp; glad</p>
<p>[Letter continues on the next page.]<br/>[The end of this letter is written sideways in the left-hand margin of the first page:]<br/>pitiful for them not to get a few homely comforts &ndash; &amp; we could help there with things sent to us.</p>
<p>With much love to you &amp; Prince. I've written him at Foster as I suppose he's there a good deal.</p>
<p>Keep on going ahead Amy. it's good to hear of your improvement.</p>
<p>Much love<br/>From<br/>Dun</p>
