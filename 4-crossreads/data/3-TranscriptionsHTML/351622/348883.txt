<p>1 September 1916 Fri<br/>Awoke &amp; very sick. 9.30 am left Contay marching about nine miles to Beauval. On arriving went around the town. Saw Tony Matterson Clary Loten &amp; Doc Grey who's has been awarded the D.C.M. They are all well &amp; look none the worse for [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>Village Church.</p>
<p>2 September 1916 Sat<br/>[indecipherable]<br/>On water &ndash; fatigue in the morning. After dinner I went round to Jim's billet. Coming back home on the road I ran up against Clarry Walters. In the evening I went to the town-hall to a concert rendered by the 17th Batt. It was very funny many of the performers being well tanked up. Especially Spud Murphy the stretcher bearer D.C.M.</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>Drawing water 200 feet.</p>
