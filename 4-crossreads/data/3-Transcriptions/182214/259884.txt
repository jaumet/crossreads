<p>&amp; after breakfast we easily got a good supply &amp; then the French man got angry&nbsp;at taking his water &amp; we [?] told him off &amp; went into his orchard &amp; stole his apples never said a word when he new us evidently&nbsp;he thought more of his watch than his apples. It stopped raining at last so we made a big fire &amp; the boys dried their cloths &amp; we felt fit once again. It was now getting very cold at night &amp; we felt the cold very much after the heat of Egypt. We left [?]&nbsp;on October 20<sup>th.&nbsp;</sup>. We marched to the Amines road where we got on to French motor buses and went many kilos the other side of Amines. Our feet were as cold as Ice &amp; we were not sorry</p>
