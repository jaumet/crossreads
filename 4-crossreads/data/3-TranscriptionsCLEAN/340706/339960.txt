
6Helmet, codene, leather jacket & gas mask or box respirator. I have not time to collect respirator & sail without it. Train leaves 10.30 am & we take taxi leaving Codford St Marys & St Peter in grand style
The journey to Southampton is uneventful & countryside I have described before except that now owing to the rains the low lying land is a bog & the streams are slightly larger. The whole land still retains that verdant green.
We arrive at Southhampton at about 1 pm. - report to Officer & then acting on his advice dine although our movement order stated that it was impossible to get any food on the way. We dined at Princes Restaurant & then marched up & town the town. There is little to see save the old gate through which the trams pass & which congests the crowds through the other two arches.
We enter Savoy Cafe a lively place hung with flags. The number of people in the streets is enormous & they seem to have little to do as one meets the same faces over & over again. The results of the elections are due to night & perhaps this accounts for the crowd.
