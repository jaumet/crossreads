 At 11 P.M., when we were all sound asleep, the  alarm was  S.O.S. alarm was sent up from the front line &amp; we were all turned out in battle-order.   Within half-an-hour, or less, the battalion was ready for the road, with transports, cookers, ammunition, bombs, officers mounts etc. complete.   Messages to the effect that  were  we were not needed came along almost immediately &amp; we turned in again.   An unsuccessful German raid had been made against the division to the left of our Front.
