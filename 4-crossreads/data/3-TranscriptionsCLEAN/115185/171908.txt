My own beloved Mother
Just a few lines. I've been writing to Constance & Ernest tonight - I have not written them a separate letter often.I posted you or rather gave Capt Boyes R.E. letters to post to you in England. I sent you little letters from men here to me. They give you a little idea of their cheerful spirit and braveness. It's a ghastly affair this [indecipherable] the tradgedy of loosing these wonderful young lives. I was looking at Major Hershel Harris photographs today. He has a splendid collection, when he goes home & we are all home, I must get him to show them to you. He went to Anzac & Suvla Bay for a few days. He has one picture showing the landing place - the beach where the wounded lie, the hospital ship standing off in the deeper water & Imbros coast line in the distance.
