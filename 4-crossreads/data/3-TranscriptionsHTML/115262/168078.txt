<p>Wednesday 7 November 1917.</p>
<p>No stand to. What a blessing. Turned out at 6.30 a.m. &amp; <u>had a wash</u>.&nbsp;(First one since over 60 hrs ago) Something to eat, saddle up &amp; off again to the front line to relieve B Section.</p>
<p>This time I was in the group to relieve a B Sec. group with 4<sup>th</sup> Battn I.C.C. Bgde. 200 yds from our front ridge which is 400 yds from front line Turkish trenches. Continual rifle &amp; machine gun fire overhead. Stray bullets everywhere. 3 pm &quot;Jacko&quot; started shelling our ridge. Dug in under cover of rocks &amp; lay low when the shells whistled, &amp; chanced our luck.</p>
<p>When night fell firing ceased with the exception of the sniping. Turned in at 6 pm wrapped in greatcoat &amp; waterproof sheet.</p>
