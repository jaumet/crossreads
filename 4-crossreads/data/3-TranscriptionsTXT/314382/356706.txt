 Pay distributed during the afternoon by Major Mills of Strathfield, who, in conversation, asked, that when I next wrote home, his kindest regards be sent to father. As the night was a glorious one we again slept on the upper deck. We, embracing, Nick Johnston,  Bob Atwill , Reg Single &amp; myself. 
 July 25th: - Upon awakening this morning I discovered that my throat was sore and that a cold had taken possession of me with vengeance. Paraded sick at 4-30 pm, and, upon being examined by Capt Kirkwood was admitted to ship's hospital with Tonsolitis and Influenza. Temperature 104&deg; when first taken. Slept poorly throughout the night but felt better in the morning. 
 July 26th: - Examined by Capt again at 10 am. Temperature considerably lower and though I was kept in bed all day, by evening I was almost OK again. Night fearfully close though beautifully mild. 
 July 21st: - Up at 10 am, discharged. The day was as glorious as one could imagine but very hot. A concert was held on the well deck and proved very successful. The ship covered 360m, which was to-date, her record day's sailing since our departure.
