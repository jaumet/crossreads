<p>Thursday May 31. 1917.<br/>A fine day but rather windy.<br/>Yesterday, last night &amp; to day were very quiet, scarsely any bombardment &amp; no enemy planes about, but the low visibility accounts for that.<br/>It is the prelude to the coming holocast, which may come at any time. All traffic on the road from here to Nieppe will cease at 12 o'clock to night, &amp; it is said that there are 1000 guns to the mile on this front ready to bombarde the enemy at a given notice.<br/>After Sundown one of Fritz's planes made a determined attack on the observation baloon stationed here. He was almost on the baloon before he was observed coming from a great hight. Our archers opened fire &amp; also other batteries, but he was undaunted, &amp; flew around</p>
