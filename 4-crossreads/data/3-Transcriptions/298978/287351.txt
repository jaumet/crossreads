<p>Apr; 27 ?<br/>Morning. Facing our extreme left, on the ridge opposite where there is a single fir tree (not lone pine)<br/>The New Zealanders are advancing &ndash; they are nearer the enemy than they suppose &ndash; they may get cut off &ndash; from our positions we can see the enemy and their danger.<br/>From Captain Margolin &ndash; "Get a signal through to them, Silas." I get up so to do, and receive immediate attention from the Turkish snipers; some of the boys told me to take a less prominent position &ndash; if I do so the New Zealanders will not be able to see my signal &ndash; it is hardly necessary to state the course I had to take; the position is too serious, I must take my chance. God, this is awful, my sight is going; I wonder if I can get my message through or will the snipers get me first &ndash; I keep giving them "N.Z." &ndash; "N.Z." but cannot see if they are getting me &ndash; it is too awful, will they never see me?<br/>At last the N.Z. signallers see me &ndash; "The enemy are immediately in front of you, go with caution, look out for snipers, they are everywhere" &ndash; my sight is getting dimmer &ndash;(to Captain Margolin) "Send somebody</p>
