<p>The holiday spent in Capetown really gave a foretaste of the pleasure of being back again in "good old Australia".</p>
<p>So well were the Aussies' treated by all kinds &amp; classes of people that when jokingly asked if they would go back again to fight? the Aussies' replied Yes! They would re inlist if ever South Africa wanted their help.</p>
<p>A feature of Capetown that cannot be overlooked is the magnificent Pier.</p>
<p>A solid built pier wholly of concrete of considerable artistic architectural beauty stretches it would seem about half a mile into the "Bay" with a lighthouse at the extreme end a Theatre, a Restaurant &amp; a large Swimming bath with modern conveniences a Refreshment Room with all kinds of liquors &amp; drinks &amp; a Shop for the sale of all kinds of fruits &amp; sweets. Its dimensions are large, of great width &amp; from ordinary wear &amp; tear from water or from weather it appears indestructable</p>
<p>With recesses &amp; seats moulded in the solid concrete walls at frequent intervals it looks in size large enough to almost carry the whole inhabitants of the town at one time &amp; as the chairs in the Restaurant &amp; Theatre &amp; the bottoms of the seats along the Pier itself all the way on each side appear to be the only portions combustible, its destruction by fire appears impossible</p>
<p>Upon a very hot Summers evening to obtain the benefits of a fresh cooling breeze its attraction must test its carrying capacity</p>
<p>Near the entrance a work of art in sculpture portrays the States earliest history.</p>
