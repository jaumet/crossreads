  
 In France 100,000 Australians are watching another Christmas pass them by.  No gaiety here just the cheerful chatter to shut out the misery of their surroundings, no prospects for them of merry-making.  A driving sleet, snow perhaps, mud to the knee, everywhere desolation and this in contrast to the scene that meets the eye of the young men here, the sun-kissed waves of our beaches, the blue-hazed outline of the mountains after a perfect summer day and the smiles of Australia's daughters.  For these things above all our men in France are fighting while these others stay and enjoy them with never a thought for the men they once called pals.
