<p>When we got outside we were ordered to wear our <strike>lft</strike> life belts from 8 p.m. that night until 6 a.m. the following morning on account of the danger of floating mines.  We also had boat drill.</p>
<p>We had a Cruiser escorting us on account of the ships firemen.  They all got drunk and mutineed, also slashed several of the crew with knives, and it ended by five of the mutineers being taken on board the cruiser and put in irons, a about twenty Naval Marines we put on board the "Plassey" to keep order.</p>
