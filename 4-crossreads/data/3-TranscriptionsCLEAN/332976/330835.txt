  
 April 1917 8th   The weather has been gloriously fine all day ( Easter Sunday ) & about half a dozen of us went into Bap p  aume to-night. There is a "picture show" there but to-day being Easter Sunday, it was closed.  We heard to-day that we have brought out a new aeroplane, much supperior to Fritz's, & it is expected that he is in for a bad time.  A very heavy bombardment started this afternoon about 4 P.M. on our left, & lasted through the night. 9th    The offensive  Bad weather again to-day, hail & rain & a bighting cold wind. The bombardment continued this morning until about dinner time.   I have been out salvaging timber
