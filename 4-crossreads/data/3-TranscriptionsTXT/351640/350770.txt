 12 midnight:  I have been writing hard since 8 p.m., and have completed an article for one of the newspapers on school buildings in Egypt.  Should it be printed, I hope that the girls will send to you a copy. 
 Pursuing my studies in French recently, I was much impressed with some sentences in A. Madame de Chantal par [by] Saint Francois de Sales.  He writes about French birds which build nests upon the sea shore, just at the margin where the waves reach, soliloquising upon the wonderful way in which they are constructed for protection against the moving water while allowing entrance, and he builds a simile between this and the condition of a soul.
