<p>All hands were anxious to have a glimpse of the head lines &amp; when they were read out, satisfaction was expressed on all sides that the Surrender of Turkey &amp; Austria seeking an Armistice &amp; the Revolution in Vienna and Budapest was a good sign of hostilities [ceasing] very shortly. After that we continued [Page torn] our sidings &amp; left for home at  pm taking a number of loaded trucks back to Conchil to go forward over the truck lines to their destinations &amp; then back again to the depot &amp; signed off at 10 pm when I had a hot cup of coffee &amp; toast &amp; turned in &amp; read the paper.</p>
<p>As the paper is of special interest, I will write some notes from it for future reference. On the front page dated November 1st 1918, large head lines were printed &ndash;</p>
<p>Turkey Surrenders, Fleet to Sail to Constantinople</p>
<p>Austrian Army seeks Armistice</p>
<p>Revolution in Vienna and Budapest</p>
<p>Germany fighting alone</p>
<p>In smaller print &ndash;</p>
<p>Turkey has surrendered to the Allies</p>
