<p>Sunday 22 October 1916<br/>Waiting about  camp all day.  Heavy frost &amp; very cold all day.</p>
<p>Monday 23 October 1916<br/>Working about camp &ndash; heavy rain &amp; very cold.  Terribly muddy.</p>
<p>Tuesday 24 October 1916<br/>Marched to High Wood &amp; carried sand bags forward.  4 men killed, 3 wounded.</p>
<p>Wednesday 25 October 1916<br/>No. 2 &amp; 4 took up camp near "High Wood".  1 man killed No. 3.  Heavy shelling.</p>
<p>Thursday 26 October 1916<br/>Heavy shelling both sides.  Sapr. Rickard killed.  No. 2 pushing stores forward.  Very cold &amp; wet.  Things going well.</p>
<p>Friday 27 October 1916<br/>Nos. 1 &amp; 2 work &amp; pushing stores forward.  Very wet, cold &amp; muddy.  Working at high pressure.  No. 1 &amp; 4 out at trenches.  Expect to attack shortly.</p>
<p>Saturday 28 October 1916<br/>Putting stores up to front line, also other fatigues.  No. 1 &amp; 4 at Trenches.</p>
<p>Sunday 29 October 1916<br/>No. 2 proceed to trenches &amp; took over from No. 1 &amp; 4.  Working in trenches during afternoon &amp; all night &ndash; very muddy &amp; wet.</p>
<p>Monday 30 October 1916<br/>Trenches again during afternoon &amp; night.  Heavy rain, trenches waist deep in mud &amp; water.  Men very exhausted.</p>
<p>Tuesday 31 October 1916<br/>Still very wet &amp; muddy.  Cleared up at night.  Digging trenches with large Infantry party during night.  Feel very exhausted.</p>
<p>Wednesday 1 November 1916<br/>Relieved by No. 3 Sec. &amp; proceeded back to H.Q.  Expecting to attack any time.  Still wet &amp; muddy.  Large waste of men.</p>
<p>Thursday 2 November 1916<br/>Resting today.  Very tired after strenuous times in trenches.  Still wet &amp; muddy.</p>
<p>Friday 3 November 1916<br/>Nos. 1 &amp; 2 Sec. were out at "Crest Trench" forming road.  Usual bombardment going on.  Still showery &amp; muddy.</p>
<p>Saturday 4 November 1916<br/>Excepting few fatigues, all resting.  Expect to attack in front Bapaume tomorrow.  Tommies attack as "Left".</p>
