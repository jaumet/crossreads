While  we were all grouped round the O.C. getting the plan of events, Fritz was sending a few shells over, and I kept my eye on the old hands, and made up my mind not to duck my head unless they did, tho' there was a great temptation to do so when one would whistle overhead, at what seemed a few inches, but was in reality a good few feet, after a while I could hear one coming and reckoned it must have been heading straight for us, everybody ducked, for which I was very glad anyway it passed over &amp; burst about 30 yds away, I was very pleased that I overcame the wish to bolt in to a dugout, cos none of the N.C.O.'s are the least bit friendly</p>
