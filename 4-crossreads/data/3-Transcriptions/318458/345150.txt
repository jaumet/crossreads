In other cases they were spattered over by fragments of shell on face limbs body often 30 or 40 punctures being found on one man.</p>
<p>In other cases again there were extensive wounds. One man had a wound on his thigh 9 in long by about 6 in broad and 1&frac12; in deep.  Besides this his face was laid open and a piece of flesh over an inch long hanging down over his jaw.  From the latter wound we took out a piece of coal a wound about the size of a cherry.  His wounds were nearly septic and stank horribly.  This was quite general.  The wounds were done up roughly &ndash; just a piece of wadding and rag jabbed in the wound and tied up.
