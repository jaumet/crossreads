<p>Chateau Wood without incident, when a fleet of 14 Taubs &amp; Gothas came over us. The dropped their bombs vigorously a few hundred yards away &amp; peppered the roads with machine gun fire. We took refuge close beside a big tree stump &amp; escaped the machine gun fire, the bullets pelting a few yards off. Our safety was but momentary, however, for a 5.9 shell lobbed only 15 paces off &amp; showered us with mud.   A narrow squeak.</p>
<p>Chateau Wood must have been a glorious spot, with its lake on one side &amp; heavily foliaged timber. It is now so lonely &amp; desolate that one feels as if death alone dwelt there. The trees are smashed &amp; splintered &amp; only stumps; the ground is heaved up into wave like ridges with shelling &amp; here &amp; there along the lonely duckboard track lies a stricken soldier</p>
