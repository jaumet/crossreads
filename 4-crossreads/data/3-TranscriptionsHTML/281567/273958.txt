<p>rending crash of H.E. &amp; and the infernal clatter of scores of machine guns. Soon the smoke "barrage" became so thick that is was impossible to see a yard on either side, &amp; the only thing for it was to stumble forward &amp; follow the, rapidly leaping barrage. Dawn found myself   &amp; several other officers in the tangle of Grand Priel Woods. Here we gathered in some 50 Germans who wet &amp; horrified by the intensity of our barrage were quite willing to surrender in their machine guns  &amp;  mortars without firing a shot. After a struggle among the wired stumps of the wood we emerged on the flat stretch before<br/>Villeret, the trench in front of which was our objective.</p>
