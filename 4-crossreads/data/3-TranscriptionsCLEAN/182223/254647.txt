
Monday 22 March 1915Wrote Commenced letters to Girls & Mollie. Wrote to Dr Dunlop, Educational adviser, to the Egyptian Government re visiting schools. Riding between 7 & 8 a.m. Riding between 5 & 6-20 p.m. Went along the Cairo road. Writing to Girls & reading after dinner. 
Tuesday 23 March 1915Ride across dessert between 7 & 8 a.m. Reading & working till 1p.m. Two papers Table Talk & Sydney Mail War No XXIX from Mrs M. M. Knowles  of Melbourne. Riding during afternoon 5 p.m to 6-10 pm.
Wednesday 24 March 1915Went Cairo by No 15 Ambulance waggon at 10 a.m. Called Ministry for Education. Spoke with Dr Dunlop (Educational advisor) & Mr Daniels (an inspector) chatted with them for half an hour. The former a Scotchman from Glasgow, Acute & Knowledgeable. Went to a primary school under aegis of latter. Examined the classes of each year, four years in the course. Teaching in Arabic. Maps in Arabic ( British genius) Nile from origin to mouth. Mentioned to them Livingstone, Burton, Speeke, Baker, Stanley, Eunis Pash[a] & their travels Luncheon at Sheppehards Continental  with Mrs Stokes.  Went to Christian brothers school at Koronfish off the Mouski (Principal Native Street in Cairo) St Josephs. Band played &c. To call again.Sheppheards to dinner. Home 11 pm.
