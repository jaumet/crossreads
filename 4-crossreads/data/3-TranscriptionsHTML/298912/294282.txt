<p>24/1/16<br/>Next morning a roll call was taken &amp; arrangements were made amongst the men  for going ashore again. However the authorities had sent out the police to keep the bumboats away, yet there was a rope ladder hanging temptingly over the stern.</p>
<p>Things dragged on thus until just after dinner when there was a rush down the gangway by some two hundred men who got on a coal barge &amp; yelled to the bumboats to come &amp; take them ashore but the presence of the harbour police frightened them lest they lose their license. The police were then pelted with coal from the barge &amp; they scampered off in a great hurry. The barge was then cast off from the Afric &amp; gradually drifted out into the harbour. Previously the men had got some native coal wallahs on this barge &amp; now forced them to row. Another barge load followed next</p>
