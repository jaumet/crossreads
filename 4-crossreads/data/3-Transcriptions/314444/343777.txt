<p>Days are hot and windy lately, but nights always cool.</p>
<p>The first night I was in the base I (what soldiers' say) "came a flat one", which is a camouflaged way of mentioning the fact that one has had the misfortune to fall in for some work. My little job was a picquet till 4 a.m., and after a month of convalescent camp this is most certainly an example of coming a flat one.</p>
<p>However, breakfast and dinner combined next day, and am gradually becoming a soldier again.</p>
<p>I met a C.C.C. lad one day from Manly &ndash; J. D. Treharne. He is in the artillery and has been out since 1914.</p>
<p>Another humorous book I must recommend is "Shorty McCabe." (by Sewell Ford). It is similar to O. Henry's books, which are fine works of modern writings.</p>
