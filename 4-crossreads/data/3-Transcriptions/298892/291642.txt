<p>France,<br/>November 1916</p>
<p>My Dear Parents/.<br/>Since my last hurried note from our billets we have been through a weeks fighting in the trenches.<br/>[6 lines blacked out by censor]<br/>Every few minutes I was expecting to hear bullets singing overhead and was inclined to duck my head at every opening. I was immediately preceded by our Corporal and I chivied him so much - by pushing him in the back to make him run at every opening, and admonishing him for holding his head high at the low portions of the parapet - that I worked him up to a high state of nervousness and when I finally hit his steel helmet with a pebble he was certain a bullet had struck it.
