
<p>August&nbsp;&nbsp; 1918</p>
<p>11 SUN&nbsp; Fine day. very heavy firing</p>
<p>about 4 am both sides.</p>
<p>Amm. going up. Inf. overcame</p>
<p>&#39;Hun&#39; resistance,</p>
<p>&amp; went forward. Heavy firing</p>
<p>on both sides. &amp; heavy bombing</p>
<p>at night. No mail able to</p>
<p>be posted.</p>
<p>12 MON&nbsp; Fine day. fairly quiet.</p>
<p>preparing for further stunt.</p>
<p>Amm. going up. Heavy</p>
<p>bombing at night. shelling</p>
<p>Received letter from Julie</p>
<p>13 TUES&nbsp; Fine day. Artillery</p>
<p>active on both sides. Amm.</p>
<p>going up. 2<sup><u>nd</u></sup> &amp; 5<sup><u>th</u></sup> Div. guns came</p>
<p>up. Preparing for a stunt tomorrow</p>
<p>Shelled out of W. L. in aft. Tanks</p>
<p>&amp; cavalry in rear. Bombs at night.</p>
<p>14 WED&nbsp; Fine day. Warm. Carting</p>
<p>amm. for stunt. Things fairlly</p>
<p>quiet. Very hazy. Rec: letter</p>
<p>from Gladys. Railway fixed up.</p>
<p>Guns moved forward. Rec: letter</p>
<p>&amp; P.C. from Tense. Bombs at night.</p>
