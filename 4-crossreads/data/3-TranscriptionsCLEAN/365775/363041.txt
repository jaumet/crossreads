  
 our first glimpse of the enemy, big parties of them are working laying down lines ect, fine healthy-looking fellows they are, captivity evidently does not seem to affect them. A few more miles brings us to the great seaport town of "Harve", here it is that most of the machinery for carrying on of the war is landed, such as guns, horses, equipment & men, it is the receiving base for all troops coming to France. thus ends the finest trip of my life. we are quickly disentrained & are marched about four miles out to a rest camp, arriving about midnight, mud up to our boot tops. here we are to rest for a few days and
