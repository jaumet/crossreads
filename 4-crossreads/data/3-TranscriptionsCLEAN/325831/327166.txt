I am now at the Kitchener War Hospital, taken over by the No 3 A.G.H. [Australian General Hospital] & we get convoys direct from France - all kind of troops but always a fair sprinkling of Australians. We got 200 Australians on Xmas Eve & I was pleased I had gone to the trouble of ordering a crate of yellow wattle in full bloom from the South of France for they appreciated it so much when they arrived.
They get here looking very tired & worn out. Young lads with faces of old men, but the youngsters lose this look after a short spell; the older men are aged for all time I think. I am surprised at the number of men 45 to 55
