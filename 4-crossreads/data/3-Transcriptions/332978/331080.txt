<p>Apl 11th &ndash;<br/>5.30 am attack on Hindenburgs line &ndash; 6.30 wounded S M Compton dressed wound in shell hole &ndash; Stayed there for 1 hour &amp; scrambled back into our first captured trench &amp; was assisted into a deep dugout full of wounded, attended by 14th Bn AMC. Germans counter att. 10 am &amp; owing to bomb &amp; ammun. becoming exhausted we were compelled to surrender</p>
<p>Others that tried to get back were mown down <strike>like sheep</strike> by treacherous M. Guns &ndash; we were soon taken prisoners &amp; were treated rather good &ndash; Remained in dugout all day &amp; night, my wounds being dressed once. Lot of our wounded out in snow all night long, lots of dead lying all over the place. Had slice bread &amp; 3 drinks coffee &amp; was given drink kerosene once.</p>
<p>12th Apl<br/>Left dugout 10 am &amp; assisted back to Reincourt (originally intended for our 3rd objective. Remained there 1hr &amp; then transported in a springless waggon to a F. D. Station. It was an awful journey (cruel) Leg dressed again &amp; my left foot put in a splint &ndash; Half cup of soup &amp; a slice Dry Bread. Remained there till 5 pm when I left in an amb-motor car &amp; after an hours more misery arrived at another D. Stn. Had cup tea &amp; slice Bread &amp; a few particulars taken from my Pay Book &amp; after an hours stay there left again in a motor amb. &amp; arrived at Hospital well after dark &ndash; was stripped &amp; taken to oper. theatre &amp; operated upon, having little chloroform administered "came to" before operation was finished &ndash; excruciating pain &ndash; Put to Bed &amp; had fair nights sleep &ndash;</p>
<p>13th Apr<br/>Had wash for first time since 9th Apl &ndash; Bread Jam &amp; Coffee for breakfast soup for Dinner &ndash; Broth for tea &ndash; had another nights good rest Treated fairly well all day &ndash; no Dressing for leg today</p>
<p>14th Apl<br/>Bread &amp; Jam for Breakfast also coffee minus sug &amp; milk &ndash; all letters that were on me, returned this mng &ndash; also Pay Book &amp; French money which was exchanged for "Marks" &ndash; given souvenir Albert cathedral by one German attendant &ndash; More soup &ndash; this Hospital is a big marquee tent with 18 beds in it &amp; is fairly comfortable &ndash; fire &ndash; attendant real hard case &amp; very decent &ndash; visited by several German off. &amp; Sisters.</p>
<p>Left Hospital qt 1.30 in a motor amb. for another "Rest Stn" 15 yds from a Railway line &amp; arrived 3.30 pm after a most cruel journey &ndash; given soup on arrival &ndash; more particulars taken by a German clerk &ndash; a real hard case &ndash; no Dressing again today &ndash; very cold, blankets lousy &ndash; no sleep tonight &ndash;</p>
<p>15th Apl<br/>Bread, margorine &amp; coffee for Breakfast (I did not partake). Few more arrivals late last night &amp; this mng about 40 of us now including (Lt McCullum 14th Bn &amp; myself) all of the Bgde represented here &ndash; Soup for Dinner &ndash; Leg beginning to trouble me a little &ndash; no Dressing again today &ndash; Soup Bread &amp; coffee at 4 pm Soup 7 pm Left Rest Camp &amp; boarded Hosp. train at midnight</p>
<p>16th Apl<br/>very miserable journey, have only had about 3 hrs actual travelling since last evening &ndash; Train stops for hours at a time - - - Bread jam &amp; coffee for Breakfast soup for Dinner &ndash; Raining</p>
<p>Leg feeling very sore &amp; smells, also swoollen &ndash; Have not struck any of my brother officers yet &ndash; Bread for tea &ndash; Raining &amp; snowing &ndash; In great pain with my leg tonight &ndash; Had morphia pill to relieve pain &ndash; no sleep &ndash;</p>
<p>17th April<br/>at Valenciennes 8 am same place 24 hours ago &ndash; this is really a most disgusting journey &ndash; my leg easier &ndash; changed trains &amp; boarded a Hosp train for Mons 10 am arriving  8 pm &ndash; no lunch &ndash; Conveyed in motor amb. &ndash; to clearing stn &ndash; at Mons &amp; had my foot dressed at 7 pm &ndash; great pain but it is much easier now &ndash; soup for tea &ndash; struck few of 13th Bn men here room is full of 4th Bgde chap &ndash; Had shave first for 7 days &ndash;</p>
