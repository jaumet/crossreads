 Thurs. June 28th. 1917: - Left Buire &amp; the Brigade moved through Lavi3ville &amp; Senlis on to the Mailly-Maillet training area. Villages of Mailly, Forceville &amp; Auchonvillers are almost deserted though they are hardly knocked about at all. We are here for more training. 
 Sat. June. 30th: - Inspected today by "Bill" Holman &amp; Birdwood, He gave us the usual "gab" Wouldn't mind having his job. 
 Thurs. 5th. July. Had our final training "stunt" early this morning around Serre. We are moving tomorrow. 
 Fri. 6th. July: - Left Camp at Mailly-Maillet &amp; marched through Hedauville, Senlis. Hennencourt &amp; Lavieville on to our old billets at Buire. Some talk of going to St. Omer in a few days time for a train ride up north. 
 Thurs. 12th July: - They made us march out this morning full-pack up just to line the Amiens-Albert Road while the king went by in a motor car. I suppose he is over in France for the National Day (14th inst). No wonder the King gets abused after our silly "Heads" impose a full pack on our backs for hours just to line a road.
