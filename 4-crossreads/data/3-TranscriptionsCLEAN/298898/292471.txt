
road was good to Bapaume & pleasant fields met the eye. Bapaume itself was smashed to pieces. Not one house was intact & the litter & rubbish was indescribable. Many ruins were still burning having been set alight before the enemy left. The church was practically non-existant, likewise the station & all rails & even sleepers had been removed, a very thorough evacuation by the enemy. Gangs of men were employed cleaning up the streets & making some of the ruins possible as billets for the men. Rather an interesting feature was the sight of various signs in German posted at various points and the number of men billeted in the various houses was also to be seen.
