 Le Bizet again attended by Lieut. T. Graham and 1 N.C.O. Line visited at night by C.O. and Lieut. L.H. Mathieson. Between 8.30 p.m. to 2.30 a.m. 3,000 rounds were fired on the following targets, (1)  Duriez Fm. C.5.d.28.93. (2)  Laundry, C.5.C.70.53. (3)  Pineapple Emplacement, C.17.a.55.65. (4)  Soap House, C.5.C.70.28. Enemy Artillery showed slight activity.  Machine guns quit throughout day and night. A little activity shown on the part of enemy aircraft. During the bombardment on our left from 3 a.m. to 3.30 a.m. the enemy sent up a large number of light signals chiefly double green stars also double red and double gold.  These signals appeared to be repeated by stations to the rear.  Weather dull and cloudy &ndash; good visability.
