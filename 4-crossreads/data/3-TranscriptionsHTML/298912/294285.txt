<p>This follows after "Colombo "  (page 57)</p>
<p>On our way back to the B I Hotel for tea we met Frewin, J S Hopkins.<br/>&amp; we returned to have tea, which was a repetition of dinner with two lovely cups of tea extra. The native who waited on us this time however had lost his nose &amp; the absence of that feature did not by any means improve his beauty, &amp; news had also reached us that the MP's were about to form a picket to arrest those on shore so we hurried through tea &amp; got into rickshaws.</p>
<p>On the way down naturally the streets were almost deserted so we yelled to the men to "Chelow" or get a move on &amp; this ended in a great race. As we slithered down the street we suddenly remembered there were letters to post so we drew up at the GPO where the "waiting  official" licked the stamps &amp; I think added a few pence postage to a parcel, for himself.
