 "Of the whole movement we are told that the result has been to force the Germans to bring reinforcements and leave their guns here, thus preventing them adding to the forces which our army in the South would have to face.  Our men, however, are disgusted to find the operation described in brief paragraphs in the London papers as an Anglo-Australian Raid.  They think that fierce fighting for eight hours under terrific gunfire might be considered a battle."
