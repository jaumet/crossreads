 1918 Sat 2nd Nov. 
 English Theatre. "The Bosun's Mate" W.W. Jacobs. Mrs Waters.   a young widow.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;R. Alexander Geo. Benn ... a retired bosun  ..  C. Hampson. Ned. Travers &hellip;    "&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  soldier ..&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   Newble. villagers etc. 
 The mounting was perfect, worthy of any stage.   Also the stage has been enlarged &amp; the orchestra is now in a sunken pit &ndash; great improvements.   Scene-painter Cecil Tooke devised the old English Inn-parlour, with a bar &amp; wonderfully good antique settees, chairs, &amp; a grandfather clock. 
 &hellip;&hellip;. 
 Mc. ( our  Mc) gave "The Shooting of Dan McGrue" &amp; is a really good elocutionist.  (hateful word) Smith &amp; Heck in "Santa Fe" &amp; "Hello, Winter!"   The Serbo-Laundry duo in execrable ragtime. 
 &hellip;. 
 Smithy was excellent. 
 Sun. 3rd. With Tellier (the lovely Lieut.) Geo, Mc. &amp; Rodell;  we obtained permission for an afternoon walk and, glittering and gorgeous in borrowed plumes we passed down to the gate at 2 pm past the openly-jealous V.O. barrack.
