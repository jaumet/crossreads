 7.8.15 Sat. 7th.  At 4 oclock yesterday I witnessed the most impressive scene I have ever seen, after a heavy bombardment from our artillery &amp; warboats, the boys on the right charged &amp; it was a grand sight;  the country looked as though a bush fire was raging, white smoke from the shrapnel &amp; the black smoke from the high explosive shells mixed with the red soil cut up by the shells made a fine picture, &amp; our boys sprinting through it with bayonets flashing in the sunlight set it off.  At one place the Turks seemed to dispute our trespassing right but only for a few moments, then they turned &amp; fled.  It was too far from here to see the men falling, &amp; so only saw the grand side of the affair;  especially as our boys were advancing, leaping line after line of trenches, only allowing themselves a few moments occasionally to get their breath, &amp; then up again to keep the Turks on the run.
