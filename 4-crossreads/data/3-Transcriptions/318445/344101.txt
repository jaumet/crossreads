<p>When the 5th Brigade was in readiness to make the assault it's right flank extended to the banks of the Somme; and from this position it attacked due Eastwards at the face of Mount St Quentin; but numerous maneuvers had to be executed before the 5th Brigade had secured its allotted position.<br/>The Brigade, which had been resting at Corbie, moved up to the line via Coppy. On the 29th August it occupied the high ground above Fuse and Coppy whence the enemy, several days previously, had been driven from entrenched positions.
