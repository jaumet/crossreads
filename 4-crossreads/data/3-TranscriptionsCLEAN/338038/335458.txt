  
 [Printed form] Form E War Office 8 July 1918 
 Dear Sir In reply to you enquiry of 13th May 1918, I have to say that Major Garling is buried in Warloy Baillon Communal Cemetery Extension on WN West of Albert. The grave has been registered in this office, and is marked by a durable wooden cross with an inscription bearing his name, rank, regiment, and the date of death. Yours faithfully, [signature indecipherable] Captain, Staff Captain for Brigadier-General, Director of Graves Registration and Enquires. 
 .Garling Esq., Engledene, Burns Bay Road, Longueyville, Sydney, New South Wales' Australia.
