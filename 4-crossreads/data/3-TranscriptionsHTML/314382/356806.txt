<p>Ship is now travelling very quickly, though at any time she travels much faster at night than during the day.</p>
<p>November 12th: -<br/>To-day in a calm sea 321 miles were covered. Although the day was glorious at 7 pm a storm overtook us and rain fell heavily preventing us from sleeping above.</p>
<p>November 13th: -<br/>On duty as usual all day nothing unusual taking place. More rain fell at dusk but it soon cleared off and the night was pleasantly cool.</p>
<p>November 14th:<br/>Divine service on boat deck at 10 am &amp; 7 pm, Captain Patterson officiating.</p>
<p>The day was a perfect one &amp; the pleasant announcement made that leave would be given us at Colombo where we were due on the morrow.</p>
<p>November 15th: -<br/>Sighted land at 6 am, and, at 10-30 am we were anchored in the harbour alongside an English Cruiser.</p>
<p>At 11 am leave began for staff and patients I being amongst the first off. Spent a pleasant day until 7 pm in seeing the town which is very quaint &amp; interesting.</p>
