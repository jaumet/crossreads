  
 3 July 1918 Wednesday Usual artillery strafe most of the night, & not much sleep, still am fairly fit but "nerves" not as good as they were-  Our second in command gone away to get married- quite a lot of our Officers are becoming sensible in that way-  Dolly writes all well  Malarial here is different to Africa-   A man runs a high Temp: up to 104 then goes normal next day- very little SHAKES.  "Tummy" troubles are frequent, & a soreness in all limbs   I think the  weight  of air we carry so low down below sea level makes one feel off-
