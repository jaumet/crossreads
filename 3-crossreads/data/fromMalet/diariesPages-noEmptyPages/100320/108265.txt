After running through rather rocky barren country abounding in funny old castles we swung out on to flatter land with many vines. Made Marseilles at noon and got a basket from the buffet. One does not get a good view of the city. The railway now ran along the sea and the sun was hot. At Toulon handed Madame of the opposite seat over to her husband a Commodore in the French Navy. The country from now on was very lovely. We came into St Raphael in daylight. The Corniche d'or commences here and a road fringes the sea the whole way. Many beautiful bays and fine villas.
