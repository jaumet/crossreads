 Putting on our shrapnel helmets &amp; carrying our gas masks we walked across this desert, whose outskirts are already becoming green with grass, past myriad sandbagged dugouts to Lock 7, once an old canal, but now just a swampy depression. Here the awfulness of the battlefield burst on one.  The great howitzer &amp; batteries were in full operation &amp; the ear-splitting din was followed by the scream of a hail of shell which swept over our heads to the enemy lines. Things were very quiet however, &amp; our harassing fire was only lightly replied to.
