<p>26th April<br/>Our attack has blown out.  Our people seem to have a habit of putting two &amp; two together expecting to make five &amp; then find out that they only achieve three.  In short they under estimate.  The right flank which was considered the soft job &amp; was delegated to the mounted troops was unforceable &amp; thus our left could not advance for fear of being outflanked.<br/>Anyway we are stuck up here &ndash; as far as I can see &ndash; indefinitely.  Meanwhile we, H.S.L. &amp; I, have been leading a blameless peaceful (busy ?) life at the C.T.C.<br/>Cost me 10/- to get Bernard shod today. Curses on the heads of our lazy farriers.<br/>Had a miserable heat wave the last three days, the Temp. being anything up to 120&deg;.</p>
