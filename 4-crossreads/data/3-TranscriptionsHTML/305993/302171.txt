<p>War news received by wireless continuous also of floating mines &amp; their positions.  News received by wireless this day re the salvaging of the famous River Clyde at the entrance of the Dardanelles.</p>
<p>12.2.19<br/>Fair weather prevailed the temperature much higher &amp; everyone far more comfortable.</p>
<p>13.2.19<br/>7 a.m. arrived off Taranto &amp; dropped anchor [symbol of anchor on image] outer harbour awaiting a pilot.  The H.M.T. Malwa arrived from Port Said at the same time.  9 a.m. Pratque granted the Indarra &amp; Malwa proceeded into Inner harbour in charge of Pilots &amp; at 11 a.m. anchored off the British Camp, we commenced disembarking 2000 troops &amp; at 2 p.m. all troops ashore, at 9 p.m. all cargo &amp; baggage &amp; mails discharged.  The Malwa departed for Port Said at 3 p.m.  We were detained on account of being short of water.</p>
<p>14.2.19<br/>By 2 p.m. we took in 1000 tons of Fresh water &amp; departed for Salonika no cargo or troops empty ship.  Mod. Gale prevailed &amp; heavy rain throughout the night.</p>
<p>15.2.19<br/>Fresh breeze, rough sea, rolling &amp; pitching heavily as luck happens we have no troops aboard otherwise they would the majority be sea-sick.  At 10.30 p.m. off C. Matapan Light &amp; midnight Kithera Is. Greece, north of Crete the extreme southerly point on the route between Taranto &amp; Salonika.</p>
<p>16.2.19<br/>1 a.m. going through Anti Kithera Channel &amp; making for Milo Is.  Whilst at Taranto we heard that the troops were sent away in open trucks the weather severe winter time &amp; that hundreds are dying through exposure &amp; not exposed for publication, yet this mismanagement exists &amp; the truth will out later.</p>
