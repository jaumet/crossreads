
3 - and they were charmed and delighted at the link with my home - in Australia with us here in Lemnos on Christmas day - also Robyn's writing - and Aunt Lolahs and White - a Bathurst man whose mother lives near the Mount and whose Aunt was Hester the laundress at the Mount for so many years was my patient and I gave him one of Aunties testaments with a card with her writing on it in it also a game from little Stewart. Old White had tears in his eyes over them. He says he owes a lot to Mrs Stewart - and I saw one of Auntie's club boys. White's mother in law - and we all sat and talked about her dearness to us all - and I gave Roy Kinghorn a testament Auntie sent and a muffler - and all the mufflers have been much likes and given to special people from Auntie - Harold Newton and a Campbell boy in the Light Horse - Harold I had to supper & afternoon tea and passed over him. He has gone? I don't know where with his regiment and Frank Segur, Marie Segurs mother I found and mothered - a fine handsome man - Colonel Rothe & Walter
