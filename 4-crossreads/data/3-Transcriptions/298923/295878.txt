<p>31 July 1918 Wednesday (X to X crossed out)<br/>The Commander in Chief <strike>General Allenby</strike> inspected us today &amp; shook hands with all COs &amp; told the men the more he saw of us, the more he valued us as <u>fighting</u> troops X &amp; for once <u>nothing</u> was said about saluting or discipline.  I am sending Dolly's Grand-Mothers letter just to let you see type.  Also some green leaves from Richon X<br/>Love FW<br/><strike>Had a letter from Cuth congratulating me</strike></p>
