My impression at the end of the day was that I had "Gott strafed der Kaiser" about 84 times. Anyhow it kept me cheerful---and one or two of <u>les malades</u> around me.</p>
<p>Sunday, 30th:<br/>Feeling better, though still groggy. Eating well. Whales spouting. Cloudy skies; port holes closed. Church Parade---but did not risk moving from saloon deck. Brought my hammock up on deck and slept there---7 p.m.</p>
<p>Monday 31st:<br/>Read London's "Martin Eden". Holiday---Foundation of Aust. Anniversary. Rough weather crossing the Bight. Big liner (supposed to be Troopship "Wiltshire" inward bound) sighted. Bed 7.30 p.m.</p>
<p>February.<br/>Tuesday 1st:<br/>Everyone Feeling groggy. Boat pitching heavily. Do not feel too good, can scarcely write a line. I spend most of my time with Basil Urban, who was next me in hospital at Maribyrnong. He is a fine youngster---I always seem to feel older than he somehow. We seem to hit it without any effort.</p>
<p>Concert in evening. Sang "My Ain Folk". Bed 8.30 p.m.</p>
<p>Wednesday, 2nd:<br/>Land sighted starboard, 20 miles away.</p>
