 Copy H.M.N.Z. Troopship No 8 Hobart,Tasmania. 21st October. 1914 
 My dear Father We had a calm trip down the coast from Auckland to Wellington and the conditions on the troopship food, bunks, etc, were a great improvement on troopship No.12 which we were on the first time we left. We are all mounted men on this ship, about 600 of us and taking them all round a very decent lot of fellows 
 We arrived in Wellington on the Wednesday morning and left with the other troopships on the Friday. There were ten troopships altogether, and we had as an escort a battleship from the China Squadron, a Japanese Battleship and the cruisers "Pyramus", "Philomel" and "Psyche". It was a fine sight coming across, as we kept close together all the way. 
 The second day out from Wellington we got some rough weather and a large number of the men were seasick, which made things rather unpleasant. 
 We were all inoculated for typhoid fever: they stick a needle about an inch and a half in your left side &ndash; The operation is almost painless, but about 4 hours afterwards, you feel it badly, stomach-ache and swelling in the left groin.
