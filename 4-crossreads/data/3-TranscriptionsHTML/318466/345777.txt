<p>Sunday 24 March 1918<br/>Get word to hold ourselves etc. in readiness.  Guess it means the bally old Somme &amp; beaucoup stunts!!  Photos came from Rup.</p>
<p>Monday 25 March 1918<br/>Had a bath in Milne's dug out in the morng.  Sent Rup's photos back.  Hear the Boche is in Bapaume.  Wonder how long he can keep it up.  Paris shelled.</p>
<p>Tuesday 26 March 1918<br/>Moved from Belgian Barn &amp; went to Reninghelst in a toy twain!  Damned cold.  Here goes for the last battle of the war!!  Breeches from Chicken.</p>
<p>Wednesday 27 March 1918<br/>Stayed last night at Reninghelst.  Muster parade in morng.  Very cold after the lovely weather of last few weeks.</p>
<p>Thursday 28 March 1918<br/>Entrained at 4.15 in the morning for the Somme district.  Reached Mondict. in eveng. &amp; had a cold wet march to Louvencourt.</p>
<p>Friday 29 March 1918<br/>Signs of the great defensive in this village.  Hear that the Boche is in Albert but seems to be held.  Recovering from that wet march last night.</p>
<p>Saturday 30 March 1918<br/>Hope I am not getting 'flu for evermore!!  Still in Louvencourt &amp; the Boche is held!!  Browne had tea with me.<br/>May the blessing of God go with you every step of the way that ye go.</p>
