<p>Cartwright was wounded. Spent an exceptionally good night with fatty Burchall &amp; a few others. Wrestling in the billet. Plenty of language.</p>
<p>3 September 1916 Sun<br/>Raining a good deal. Being Sunday things were fairly quiet in &amp; about the village. Dick Jenning &amp; myself went down to Brigade Headquarters before dinner &amp; enjoyed some very good English beer. Met Jack Kater QMS. 17th &amp; took a stroll with him. I learnt to-day that Rolf Jolly had his arm blown off &amp; Jack [text continued at top of page]</p>
<p>[Note in margin]<br/>[lady barber]<br/>Ridge decorated. Roberts D.C.M.</p>
<p>4 September 1916 Mon<br/>Issued with P.H.S. Gas helmets. On account of it raining again to-day our section fell in <strike>again</strike> in our billet Major Aidy giving a lecture on Gas. After it was over I was sent to give Bill Rankin the bootmaker a hand to mend boots. I had mine fixed up also. When the day was finished I went with Clive Ack &amp; Jeff to a few of the estaminets.</p>
