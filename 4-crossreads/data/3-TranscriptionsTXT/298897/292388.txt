The bombardment of the front has continued all day heavily, a constant rumbling going on, &  seems to point to an attack. The taubes the other night played up with a C.R. Station about 5 miles back killing 6 & wounding about 37.& also bombed Amiens killing 9 & wounding about 23.
Tuesday 14th Nov.Bombardment still continues heavily the huns dropping a few into "Albert" occasionally.  Have had two interesting visits to a German prisoners camp.  We send an M.O. daily to attend the sick so I went with him.  An interpreter was found in a M. student who spoke English very well.  From him I learnt some very interesting things & must admit several of his views on the war coincided with my own.  He admitted a hatred of England as he considers us as cousins who had turned dog on them when fighting a common enemy the Slavs.  The Russians he considered as poor fighters, Austrians, both good & bad, Turks good when officered by Germans, French as on the good side, possessing plenty of "Elan & the British as the best, always making a fight of it.  The Italians he despised both as fighters & for turning dog on an ally & fighting against them.  He objected strongly against evil things being said of the Kaiser, looking on him as a really good man & pointed out that they never said a bad word concerning our King George.  He was intensely proud of being a German
