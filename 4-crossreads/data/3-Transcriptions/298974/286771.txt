<p>3<br/>we got out of the gates there were hundreds of women &amp; children waiting, many babes in arms. The endeavours of these poor exposed people to find their husbands &amp; lovrs were pitiful. It was very cold &amp; pitch dark too, so you can imagine their difficulty when there were 1500 of us. As we marched down Bourke St &amp; down to Woolloomoolo gases were lit &amp; people scantily clad rushed onto balconies &amp; into the street to cheer us on. It was a remarkable sight, but horribly dismal. The crowd were given &frac12; hour to say goodbye at the wharf, just</p>
