<p>B Co.  it is beautiful country here plenty of crops &amp; its OK. we had a 10 minutes halt every hour. with 40 minutes spell for tea. we marched through the village or town of Sailly in file 50 yards between platoons  I met a Leichhardt chap Bourne. cobbles stones in the streets very few civilian  people in same  plenty of soldiers we arrived at our billets after a rotten march. about 8 oclock we are housed the majority of us in wooden huts.  some in the barns trees all around us [indecipherable] hellish bombard last night she was pretty warm  Plenty of soldiers &amp; strange to say so near the firing line a good number of women &amp; children whom seem to take no notice of the gazumps</p>
<p>18.6.16<br/>Sunday to day only a muster parade no church service I went looking for Sgt Gilbert 17th Batt but gave it best &amp; walked for miles. a couple of batteries having a go at one another. observation balloons. aeroplane last mentioned are always getting a great reception from the opposite sides.  nothing much doing though last night it was dam cold we have only one blankets</p>
<p>19.6.16<br/>Big bombardment about 12 oclock last night. both sides at it we were called out for a gas alarm &amp; we were on for a couple of hours [indecipherable] night</p>
