
March 1918Monday 11
MessinesSpent a very interesting day with Mr.Kerr, at Forward O.P. came under fairly heavy shell fire during the morning. In the afternoon we went forward for three fifty yards, to obtain better observation. Major just missed being sniped.Weather warm, and very pleasant. When coming in we passed 53rd Officer and two Spooks en route to the line to partake in the big raid by 57th, 58th, and 59th.10.32 pm Big stunt commenced. Plenty of fireworks, from Fritz's way. Very clear night.Raiding party home at 11.52 pm. raid successful. 15 prisoners.
["spook" was army slang for a signaller, especially a wireless signaller]
