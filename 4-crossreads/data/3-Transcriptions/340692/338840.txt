<p>four days here, &amp; it was then our turn to go "in".</p>
<p>We marched out of Dernancourt along a congested road, each mile bringing us more &amp; more into the realization of things.  At first we began to pass our reserve lines of trenches &amp; wire entanglments (destined now never to be used)  Then such things a little wooden crosses; shell battered walls, &amp; now &amp; again demolished buildings presented themselves.  We had passed Albert well on our left, &amp; after marching all morning we halted about one o'clock at the shattered village of Fricourt, &amp; it was while quietly resting here after</p>
