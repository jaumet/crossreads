 July. 13. 
 We then returned to Invergordon in Cromarty Firth, parting with the Fleet at sea, in order to dock &amp; refit.&nbsp; At Invergordon we were docked in the floating dock, &amp; for the next 10 days defects were made good, 4 days&#39; leave given to each watch, &amp; everything made ready for sea again. 
 July. 22. 
 We then proceeded to Scapa Flow, in the Orkney Islands, a journey from Invergordon of about 80 miles.&nbsp; Here were gathered all the latest battleships under the command of Sir John Jellicoe.&nbsp; Scapa Flow is a commodious harbour about 15 miles in width, accommodating almost any number of deep draught vessels.&nbsp; It is entirely landlocked with the exception of 3 small openings, eavch guarded by boom defence lines or blocked entirely by sunken German ships.&nbsp; Existence in this Fleet had long since reduced itself to dreary monotony.&nbsp; Leave was only granted to Flotta Island, where a very primitive golf course had been laid out, &amp; which was necessarily overcrowded.&nbsp; Mails arrived thrice weekly.&nbsp; A feature of the ships congregated here was the &quot;dummy&quot; ships.&nbsp; These vessels were old merchant ships, built specially to resemble well known types of battleships.&nbsp; The turrets
