<p>The weather was good practically the whole time with bright summer days. Much milder than the bleak climate of the north.</p>
<p>The people of Orange are of the real southern type, some with dark eyes and olive skins. They speak French (with a very bad accent), Provencal &ndash; the old language of the South, and a local patois &ndash; a dialect of French, Italian Provincial &amp; slang mixed.</p>
<p>Orange is a base for Salonica and in addition to French troops I saw plenty of Servians and Greeks belonging to Venizelos' National Army as well as a few Russians. The latter, of course, are very unfavourably regarded by everyone in view of the way in which Russia is backing out of the war.</p>
<p>The town has a population of several thousand and is on the main line between Lyon (200 kiloms) and Marseille (150 kiloms). The streets are fairly narrow, plenty of well lighted shops and a busy market place. The caf&eacute;s are large and covered in with glass. The surrounding country is quite flat.
