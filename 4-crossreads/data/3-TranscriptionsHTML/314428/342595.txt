<p>and then retiring as soon as they are inspected which is as soon as they are assembled at their posts. The other two fatigues are the butchers at the canteen which do not take very long in the mornings. All other duties such as cleaning the decks (which is done by the crew) and cleaning the washhouses, latrines, etc. are done by a permanent fatigue, and they receive 1/- per day extra. The military police formed from among volunteers who are stiff, also receive 1/- per day extra. On the Persic all these duties and fatigues were done by the soldiers (except Pioneers and Transports) and without any extra pay.</p>
