  
 Jan 7th 1918 Monday Wild stormy night with Tropical deluge conditions moderated considerably during the day & only occosional showers fell. Capt Hurley & myself went out after mud pictures in which the country is at present swimming The great field in front of Esdud Station was submerged about four feet & with it valuable supplys of fodder & rations A great string of over 2000 camels had to pass over it & I pitied the wretched creatures & their destitue drivers to see these fine beasts of the burning desert wallowing in the mud of Palestine winter is pitiful indeed & their drivers with hardly any suitable clothing or protection from the fierce weather it is no wonder that they perish in larg numbers.
