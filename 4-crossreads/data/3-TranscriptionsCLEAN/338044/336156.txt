  
 15th March: To-day am sent for by my C.O. Lieut Butler who has a telegram in his hand    &  asked me if I had applied for a transfer. Of course I stated the facts    &  after receiving the permission of Mr Murphy (W.O.) he grants the transfer & I am then given my pass, Railway warrant, & letter of transfer & warned to catch the 11 am train next day for Tel el Kebir    &  formally report for my new duties & clear up with Headquarters  About 5 pm after I had made all arrangements for departure, Mr Murphy sent for me again, asked to see my papers, which he took away & tore up, then informing me that my transfer had been entirely
