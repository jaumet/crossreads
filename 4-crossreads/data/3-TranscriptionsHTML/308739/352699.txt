<p>Thursday 27 April 1916<br/>A squad of our men carried 14 miles on stretcher a wounded Tommy.  6th A.L.H. under Major White as advance party, with Col. Fuller supporting, scouring Country around Qualia.  Digging party out burying Dead &ndash; 31 Tommies &amp;amp; 10 Turks.  Party found 2 more wounded Tommys &amp;amp; 1 Turk.  This Turk had ribbons of different Battles he had been engaged in, &amp;amp; was on the Peninsular at the time we were there.  The Camel Ambulance to away some of the wounded to R.H.  Major Pelham returned to Cairo sick, &amp;amp; Major Anderson took his place as Brigade Major.  Our Major is one of the youngest in the Aus. Army &amp;amp; is rapidly coming ahead.  He joined our Regiment as 2nd Lieu. from Duntroon N.S.W.</p>
<p>Friday 28 April 1916<br/>Col. Fuller writing out reports of previous days operations.  Burying party out this morning to complete this work.  The Camel Ambulance took remainder of wounded to Rail Head.<br/>2nd A.L.H. Brigade Ambulance arrived by Camel train at 4 p.m. under Col. Croll.<br/>Col. Fuller inspecting outposts.<br/>7th Regiment formed the daily protrol. Cooks &amp;amp; other details under Sgt. Dickson arrived this Evening.<br/>Immediately on receiving the written message from the Aviator a circle is formed if contents is understood &amp;amp; if not a cross is formed of men standing near.</p>
<p>Saturday 29 April 1916<br/>Some of the H.Q. Divisional Staff arrived to inspect surrounding Country accompanied by our Brig. &amp;amp; Col. Fuller.  Major Bruxner is in charge of escorts.  Sgt. Hartley went to Railhead on Official Business &amp;amp; returned same day with Machine Gun Horses.  Ted Reed went with Col. --- to R.H. &amp;amp; returned same day.  Brig. &amp;amp; Col. Fuller returned from Kalia 6-30 p.m.<br/>Qualia as spelt on map is pronounced as Kalia.  This place is really nothing except a cement wall with good drinking water also a shallow cement trough.  The work entailed in drawing the water for both man &amp;amp; Beast is heavy as one can only use Buckets &amp;amp; rope attached &amp;amp; draw up hand over hand.  The spot has also a Date Palm grove near well, &amp;amp; a few dried up Gunyahs.  A dried up Salt Lake takes up a good deal of surrounding land, &amp;amp; like all such places is dangerous to travel over.  Camels have sunk &amp;amp; disappeared, &amp;amp; horses are often Bogged whilst attempting to cross.<br/>The Divisional H.Q. of the Anzac Mounted, is quartered near Kantara in a large stone House.  From there the whole operations are in direct touch, either by Telephone or Wireless.</p>
