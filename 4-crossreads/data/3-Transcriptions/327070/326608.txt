<p>Away to the left (the N) stretched the low-lying northerly cape of Colombo Bay, apparently covered with cocoanuts &amp; other vegetation, but fading away to blue ocean in the distance.</p>
<p>Closer in, lying stranded on the beach with a big list to starboard was a fair-sized steamer (2 masts &amp; 1 funnel) which we afterwards heard had been wrecked last monsoon. At first we thought she had the cut of a warship, but we were wrong. Close by her were 2 or 3 native craft, &amp; later several of them came close to the ship when it could be seen that they were rather roughly constructed, the neat workmanship of Fijian canoe being awanting: the oars were poles with a flat roundish blade attached to the end, but were well-wielded by the natives. In one case they paddled past they kept time to a native song or chant reminding me very much of a Fijian "meke".</p>
