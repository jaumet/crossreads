 with one of our patrols. My word he has rotten luck, he had just done two nights patrol with B Coy. whose place we took &amp; they went back in supports. One night one of their patrol of 4 men disappeared, &amp; hasn't been heard of since. Well, one of D Coy. patrol got crook or something, &amp; instead of sending out one of our men, they sent to B Coy. &amp; poor old Boo was the Jonah. It's a rotten job out on patrol in No Man's Land, walking up &amp; down, &amp; every now &amp; again Fritz sends up flares, &amp; the poor old patrol is out there standing fast while they're up, &amp; wondering if a machine gun has spotted them. Besides this, they are in an unknown land between the two sides, &amp; never know when they'll run fair into an enemy patrol. He used to bring all sorts of extra jobs on himself when in camp as a sergeant, but now he's over here as a  sergeant  private he seems to have bad luck in striking the extra things that crop up. A couple of hours later I saw them return all right, &amp; he should have had a full day's sleep today, as there is no moving about in the day time. The stew &amp; tea arrived about 10.30 p.m., after which my little party carried on with the trench digging as usual till dawn.
