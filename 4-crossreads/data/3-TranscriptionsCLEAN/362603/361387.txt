  
 Bulford Feb. 23. 1917 Dear Dad, Mum   &   George, Just a few notes about my first trip to London.  Dave   &   chap named King   &   I caught the 3.19 from Bulford   &   arrived in London at 6.30, only 8 stops   &   70 miles, English fast travelling.  We dined at a Good place in Trafalgar Square   &   set out for Lodgings.  Of course we asked a Bobby, they know everything.  He advised us to go to a small private Hotel.  He knew one only 50 yds .away so we went there.  We then set out for a theatre   &   walked into the first one we saw which happened to be "The Coliseum."  It is a magnificent Theatre   &   Vaudeville Show.  After the show was over we walked up the Strand   &   down over Waterloo Bridge. Next morning we walked round a bit   &   down through Smithfield Meat Market.   We then boarded a Cable Tram bound towards Edmonton where Cyril is in Hospital.  We spent 2 Hours with Him   &   then returned to London promising to meet Cyril on the morrow.  We toured through West End    &   Picadilly   &   again had tea at the same place in the Strand.  After Tea we went to the Adelphi to see "High Jinks."   We paid 4/- for a seat which was just as bad as 1/- seats in Sydney.
