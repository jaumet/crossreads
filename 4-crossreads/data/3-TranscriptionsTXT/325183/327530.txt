 midday and had lunch then a stroll around the town and boarded the 4.30 train for Dunkirk we believed. After a wretched journey however we pulled up at the battered town of Ardinkirk. Here we were ordered out the conductor saying that the train did not  leave  go on any further: We refused to budge however believing that he was fabricating as usual. We were soon convinced  however  that for once the Froggy was right for we found ourselves in a siding  with  about 12 midnight pouring with rain and the engine off.
