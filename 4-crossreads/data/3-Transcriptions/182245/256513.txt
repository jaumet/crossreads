
<p>1916&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 231</p>
<p>Friday 29<sup>th</sup> Dec.</p>
<p>Misty rain in morning, &amp; a cold [gale?]</p>
<p>in evening. We had reviellie at 3 AM</p>
<p>&amp; moved out of camp at 7.15 AM &amp; all</p>
<p>were very glad to get a move on at</p>
<p>last. We entrained at Amesbury</p>
<p>&amp; reached Southhampton Docks</p>
<p>about midday. Got all our battery</p>
<p>gear on board &amp; with the 25<sup>th</sup> Bat</p>
<p>&amp; 33<sup>rd</sup> (Qld) we sailed at 8 pm in</p>
<p>an old German boat the &quot;Huntscraft&quot;</p>
<p>(n&egrave;e S&uuml;dmark). It was bitterly</p>
<p>cold but not two rough.</p>
<p>Saw Spes Wharton marching</p>
<p>into camp as we marched out.</p>
<p>in the morning</p>
