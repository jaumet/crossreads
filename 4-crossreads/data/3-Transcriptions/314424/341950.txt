<p>Last night I received a "Weekly Times Dec. 22. Christmas number. It was very interesting.</p>
<p>I heard from E . artillery that the German advance was on a front of 55 miles to a depth of 20 to 25 miles. 30 miles of the front being French.</p>
<p>We had roast pork for tea also a little champagne. Many of the men were drunk last night &amp; have sore heads today.</p>
<p>Monday April 1. 1918. 1337.<br/>Easter Monday.</p>
<p>The horse lines are in a sea of mud, but this afternoon is fine, and the weather seems to have taken up a bit.</p>
<p>There was pronounced enemy aerial activity this morning , &amp; one bomb fell not far from our lines early this morning.</p>
<p>The artillery around have also been pretty active in straffing the enemy.</p>
<p>Starky Treginza was up before the major for being drunk &amp; away from parade. He was admonished and given 7 days Royal Warrant.</p>
