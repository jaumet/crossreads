<p>Dec 4<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;I went to Brulooge railhead &amp; we delivereed to the Brigade just this side of Kemmel hill. the weather was fairly cold, but no wind, it tried to snow but the sun prevailed . Fritz came over last night &amp; the night before &amp; dropped a few bombs on Hazebrouck - I did some letter writing in the evening - Jim came home well boozed -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;Last night was very cold. hard frost - to-day is cold but sunny &amp; no wind &nbsp;We did our trip for supplies, Budge &amp; I walked to the top of Kemmel hill, from which advantageous position we were able to see Lille on the right &amp; YPRES on the left &amp; the intervening country - the old cage on the top where a lion used to be kept was rather knocked about but still standing - I wrote letters in the evening &nbsp; we were given an issue of rum, which we duly appreciated as a night cap to make us sleep well &amp; warm&nbsp;&mdash;</p>
