<p>The horses one sees on the farms are generally big well-built fellows and very strong.  They are manoeuvred chiefly by word of mouth, and are very intelligent and obedient.  Roans and grays appear to be the favourite colours.</p>
<p>On the whole the people are an amicable lot and can always find time for some friendly gossip.  They listen open-mouthed to the tales told about Australia, and cannot imagine farming on such a grand scale as we do it.  They invariably ask whether you are married, or whether you have a fianc&eacute;e in Australia.  As regards the first question, I've only struck one honest man in the Australian forces yet.
