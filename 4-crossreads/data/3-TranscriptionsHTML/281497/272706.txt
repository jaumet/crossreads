<p>for the ground is so level &amp; the crops so fine especially the wheat.   There are scarcely any level crossings on the French railways they are nearly all overhead or underneath this does away with a lot of accidents.   The country we are passing through now is very pretty as far as you can see on either side is nothing but bonzer crops all turning yellow &amp;  a lot of it cut &amp; storked they have a peculiar way of drying their handreaped wheat &amp; oats they stand it up &amp; tie it round the top of the stook, &amp; there it stands like a stook of tied sheaves you should see the rows of stooks all regular &amp; straight as a gun</p>
