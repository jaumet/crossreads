<p>Thursday March 8. 1917<br/>It was very cold and windy last night.  Early this morning snow fell in thick flakes and continued until near dinner time.<br/>A patient in a bed next to me &ndash; a blacksmith in the Pioneer Battalion was told by one of the doctors who examined him this morning that he had got a Blighty.  He was hurt in the hip years ago and it is coming against him now.  The cold weather and a long march he had in England and another in the rain at La Havre is telling against him.<br/>Among the remittances I heard today was the case of a pioneer who was gradually getting worse.
