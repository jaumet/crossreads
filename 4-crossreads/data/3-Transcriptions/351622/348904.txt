<p>9 October 1916 Mon<br/>From my dugout door there lies Ypres absolutely blown to <u>pieces</u>. Shell in the old cookhouse didnt do any damage. 8.30.pm. A couple of our planes are directly over my dugout &amp; Fritz is shelling them for all he is worth. I saw one of our aeroplanes <strike>land</strike> brought down this morning on our left. Another bombardment to-night.</p>
<p>10 October 1916 Tues<br/>A very quiet day. I spent most of the day making a knife from a German shell case &amp; cartridge to send to Claude for Xmas. Mr. James came &amp; paid us late at night. Very busy at midnight some bad cases passing through one a big policeman from Melbourne a Sniper but got him [chest].</p>
