<p>Friday July 12. 1918. 1438</p>
<p>I was on 3 picquet this morning from 2 to 4.30. It was a quiet time and no shelling in this quarter by the enemy.<br/>About 7 a.m. there was a very heavy artillery activity south of us on the French sector. Today is cloudy, windy and occasional light showers.<br/>[shorthand symbols](I wrote to gunner  R. Dusting this evening)</p>
<p>Saturday July13. 1918. 1439</p>
<p>A beautiful day. Today I have been repairing Lewis Gun Carriers for the limbers</p>
<p>This evening I wrote to Kitty Martin &amp; to Cis in reply to their letters.<br/>This afternoon an enemy shell fell a few hundred yards from our camp. Just before dark I saw the enemy planes shoot down two of our baloons in quick succession and then make off admidst a hail of machine gun bullets &amp; shrapnel.</p>
<p>Sunday July 14. 1918. 1440</p>
