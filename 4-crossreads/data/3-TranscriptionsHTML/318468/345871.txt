There has been a good deal of sickness in Camp despite good sanitary precautions being taking - &amp; taken all together the place is not at all healthy &ndash; at least it does not suit the Australian    Early in the last week we were informed definitely that we were to leave at the end of the month &amp; I do not think there was a man in the Coy that was not pleased to hear the good news.   Friday &amp; Saturday there was a lot of work going preparatory to leaving &amp; Sunday the day of leaving Mena was a hummer.   The 1st F Coy &amp; their Reinforcements &ndash; The 3rd Infantry Brigade &amp; part of the 3rd Artillery were the Forces to leave.
