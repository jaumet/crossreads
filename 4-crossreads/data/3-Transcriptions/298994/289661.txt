<p>Durban the next day, we took another route, passing through a different portion of the city.</p>
<p>In company with Corporals Edgar and Harrington, I had luncheon at a fine appointed little cafe on the beach. Heavy rain fell at this period. One could obtain a fine view of the beautiful hotels on the esplanade of Durban, likewise the immense waves.</p>
<p>We had another march through Durban in the afternoon. We passed the native prison.</p>
<p>Speaking of prisons makes me think of the queer sights I saw in this city A line of native prisoners, with a large or long chain, each man having a hand shackled. The native warders, armed with a sort of waddy and a dangerous looking spear, did not look pleasing to a bystander, let alone the miserable victim!</p>
