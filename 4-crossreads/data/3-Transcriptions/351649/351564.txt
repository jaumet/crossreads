<p>However one very rapidly settled down when joined up to a new unit.</p>
<p>But again you get tired of constantly meeting and living with a new lot of fellows generally none known to you before:  my <u>age</u> tells.</p>
<p>However if the old war can only keep up the present pace, it ought to burn out in a year or two more.</p>
<p>And now lets get onto something cheery.  Of course as usual I shall entirely disregard all the questions in your letters for questions answered 4 months late are over the odds &ndash; only lawyers can hark back like that.</p>
<p>And who's this mysterious woman Jean whom you write so glowingly about?  Already you have interested me in her <u>tremendously</u> :  why, I'm almost in love.</p>
<p>But she must take off the powder, and tell her, for God's sake keep up the cooking:  I hope John approves of her as much as you do:  you know one woman's judgement of another isn't always the same as a man's.</p>
<p>But I'm afraid I'll never be able to fall in love with <strike>here</strike> her if I go on seeing you (subtle compliment in above two lines, Madam!  I really ought to scratch them out:  its against my letter writing rules to put things like that:  but as its you and your letters were so nice, I'll leave it this time &ndash; showing how old &amp; stupid I'm getting).</p>
