We return early & play tennis with the Pinches & then dine about 8 pm, egg & [rice] - liver with puree & [indecipherable] - prunes etc - beer.
23-5-19 (Frid)Harris goes to station to meet Miss Wade a pretty doll who might have been a woman if she had lived in any other country than England & had allowed her natural temperament to develop & not be warped by the bonds of conservatives & reticence.
I spent the morning making plans of the wilderness. In afternoon Coller, Ellis & another nurse turn up & play tennis. I take walk down the river to the mill rail but pass no place more beautiful than the Manor House.
Night we play cards row up the river with Mrs Rutson & Miss Wade.
24-5-19Yarn on lawn with Miss Wade - Weather is delightful warm with a cool breeze & air languid. In the afternoon some S. African friends arrive & I row them up the river to the currents that are as swift that navigation ends especially with a girl steering. The river is pretty or rather it is only a creek with a strong current.
At night we play a game of cards called [soon can] or some such name & then off to our rooms where I write till 1.2
