On the other hand there is one Mlle who has a young child of English parentage, &amp; another who had a child (who subsequently died), which was a freak, having the lower part of the face dark &amp; the upper part white. Two very smart mademoiselles whom I noticed in an adjacent cottage, are, so madam says, refugees from Amiens; very ladylike &amp; aimiable, &ndash; milliners by profession. One of them came to inquire for the paper as Madam was talking of her, &amp; I noticed the typical black dress &amp; almost blonde hair of the French modiste. She was pale faced &amp; looked rather sad, I thought, unless her dignity of bearing as an Amienois amongst provincial would account for the calm cold expression.
