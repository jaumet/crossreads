<p>&amp; until I put my hand around to feel I was not sure that I had not a hole in it, but it proved only a bit of dirt that stung my arm.  Another close touch happened at North Corner, when Fritz after landing a "Jack Jhonson" [Jack Johnson &ndash; German shell bursting with black smoke] directly on our right, decided to put one on the other side within a yd or so of us.  We had our patient on the ground at the moment &amp; were changing positions.  It burst deafening us;  we instintivly hunched our sholders, &amp; inclined our steel helmets to that side.  One fellow I remember closed his knees together, &amp; with his back to the shell, crouched over the patient's head with that natural sense of protection of the weaker.</p>
