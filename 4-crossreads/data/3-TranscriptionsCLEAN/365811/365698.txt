
2I was there during the great autumn fighting in Flanders when the Australians did so nobly & suffered so appallingly, & I came away sharing the general military view that given decent staff work the problem of the trenches had at best been solved & that the end would surely come this year.
Since then Germany has settled with Russia & pulled some of our strength into Italy & so has relatively improved her strength on the Western Front & no doubt that will to some extent delay the settlement.
But in the gain of America with her youth (all the young country peoples have excelled in the struggle : the Canadian is very closely akin to the American & is equal in fighting quality to the Australian & New Zealander) - in the gain of America we have I think been far more than compensated for the our troubles elsewhere.
At this great trial of scientific & industrial forces America should be equal to any other single power at least.
