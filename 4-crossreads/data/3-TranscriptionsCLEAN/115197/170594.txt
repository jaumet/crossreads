Resuming our journey we pass several old inns including "The Wheel Inn" & "Ye Olde Servants Inn" - and arrive at the New Forest famous for En early English Hunting - Roman Roads.  In the "forests" we see the "Rufus' Stone" on whose three sides are inscribed:
(1)  "Here stood the oak-tree on which an arrow shot by Sir Walter Tyrell at a stag, glanced and struck King William II (Rufus)
