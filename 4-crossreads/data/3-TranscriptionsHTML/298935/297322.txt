<p>late residence of Cecil Rhodes.<br/>The next morning we were not allowed leave to go ashore but no one was surprised at this.  The ship was too far away for anyone to jump down on to the quay and a rush for the gangways was stopped with fixed bayonets.  After this event the ship was anchored out in the harbour.  Just before sailing we took on board 15 men who had missed their own troopship.  Thirty five of our own men could not be found and we sailed without them.<br/>One afternoon a man fell overboard and another jumped in to save him.  The water thereabouts is infested with sharks but the men were safely picked up by one of the boats.
