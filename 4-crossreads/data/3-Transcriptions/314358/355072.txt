<p>10<br/>speaking clearly and leaving Gods air the sweeter for it another voice swells forth as though a window in heaven had opened.<br/>A woman sings<br/>And sings in English<br/>Yet the English is of little moment. Language matters not in the least, for a woman sings, and sweetly too but not so sweetly as Beatrice.</p>
<p>Where only men are they become vile. It is always so with those of ordinary capacity, yet as soon as a good woman comes amongst them they sweep up the front of their tents as it were and bury the rubbish and filth. Until she has gone.</p>
<p>A cursing Shakespeare or Bacon would be a blessed friend to have here with me. I admit.</p>
<p>I find it is really the vulgarity of men that is distasteful.</p>
<p>One could curse and be at home with his own. but the familiarity of vulgarity is what causes a man to rather be lonely than suffer it</p>
<p>I never could stand familiarity of the cheeky vulgar.</p>
<p>It is not a matter of morals. They dont count at all</p>
<p>It is not a matter of birth either but it is a matter of being naturally gentle.</p>
<p>Wordsworth and Swinbourne I think went to a war together once but they had many fine ideas shattered, and returned to nature once more were God seemed nearer to them than he was amongst men</p>
