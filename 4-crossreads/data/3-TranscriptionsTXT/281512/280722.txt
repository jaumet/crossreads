 a heavy barrage of 5.9s were coming  over at the time, so that I had a devilish tight time for an hour or so. Then the six &amp; a &frac12; miles tramp back through the slush &amp; mud to Ypres, where the car has to be left just about beat me. Soaked through &amp; mud to the thighs, I fervently thanked God when I was aboard the Ford &amp; bound back for Steenvorde. 
 12th October.  Battle of  Last night I found a letter awaiting me from Capt Bean, telling me of another battle which is to come off tomorrow. Our objective is Poelcapelle:  about 800 yards advance.
