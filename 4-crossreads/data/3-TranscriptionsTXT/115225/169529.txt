 Feb.6  th  &nbsp;&nbsp;Frank &amp; I went up to Suzanne&#39;s in the morning to learn a little bit of French. there was a family of three Mother &amp; two daughters aged twelve &amp; fifteen respectively &nbsp; Suzanne &amp; &nbsp;Lucienne &amp; Suzanne, they used to keep open house to the soldiers &amp; sell coffee at 1 d &nbsp;per time. they always had a warm fire &amp; a smile for the boys &amp; we appreciate a place to spend a few dull minutes round the home fire - Joe went to Blighty on leave. I went to Meteren in the afternoon to see Jack as it is his birthday - Coming home at night I caught up the fellows on their way home all boozed &amp; walked with them - they had been stopped by the Military police in Bailleul but had given a good account of themselves &amp; got away - 
 &nbsp; 
 Feb.7  th  &nbsp;&nbsp;The bunch are feeling a bit dopey after last night&#39;s jolification - Bud went to Blighty on special leave to be married - Frank came on with me as second driver as Jim is in hospital in Blighty &amp; expects not to be back again, thinks he can work it for Aussie. 
 &nbsp;
