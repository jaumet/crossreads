<p>1917<br/>Oct<br/>On the first occasion I had a standing up seat hanging onto a beam but on the second occasion two of us toddled in behind some officers and got a seat in the front row.<br/>We had about two hours duty every day on the phone at HQ orderly room. When first I took my shift, I found that the man on duty at Brigade HQ was Fred Fowler who was an old cobber of mine at Kings School. The whole Brigade was supposed to move to a back area for a rest but for some reason or other it was postponed.<br/>Oct 31<br/>On 31st Oct my 22nd birthday came and went. I celebrated it with Tom McCann and Heslewood by splashing through thick mud in the dark to a small canteen at the rear of the rear of a large motor lorry park. We bought some cake and chocolate and ate them on our way home.<br/>Nov 2<br/>On 2nd November McCann and I decided to take a trip to Ypres about 5 kilometres away, so we hopped onto the back of a passing motor lorry. No one ever walked around the camps</p>
