The twinkling lights drew dim in the distance and I wondered who was tending the light in my little dug-out, my home for the past five months, the light which emanated from a tobacco tin filled with fat from my weekly chop.  Here and there a rifle spat viciously and occasionally a spurt of flame preceded the rattle of a field gun and told us that that harbinger of death the 18 pounder was at work again. On our left the lights of Cape Helles come slowly into view and Anzac for the time being is but a memory, a bitter sweet memory which we shall all hold dear while life lasts.
