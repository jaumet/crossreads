<p>C. McGloughlan took a Limbre into Amiens today. The enemy was shelling the city &amp; very few civilians were to be seen. Early in the war the people paid a large indemnity to the enemy to spare the city. Now he is constantly bombing it by night &amp; shelling all the time.</p>
<p>The enemy now occupies Plougsteert wood. Neuve-Eglise, &amp; there is heavy fighting around Baileul.</p>
<p>Monday April 15. 1918. 1351.<br/>Cloudy but much finer.</p>
<p>This front is fairly quiet today except occasional artillery activity from big guns.</p>
<p>This evening I received a packet containing 7 letters. 1 from Cis dated Feb. 15. No 11. &amp; six from my wife No.114 Jan 27.  116 [few words of shorthand] Feb.5  115. Feb.4.  117. Feb.10.  110. Feb 13 and 119. Feb 17.</p>
