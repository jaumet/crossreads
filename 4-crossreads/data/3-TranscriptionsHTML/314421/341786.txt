<p>A number of enemy planes were over at mid day &amp; dropped some bombs, without any damage. One bomb from a Gotha fell near a Battalion on the march &amp; splashed dirt <strike>over</strike> over them.</p>
<p>I saw two round balloons high up float over from the enemy. They dropped some papers.</p>
<p>Tuesday Dec. 11. 1917.  1226.</p>
<p>I was on picquet this morning from 3 to 6. it was cold &amp; frosty. But I had a fire.</p>
<p>The election for the referendum on conscription was held today. I voted for conscription.</p>
<p>I was shouted a dinner of 4 eggs chips &amp; bock today by Bryan Dowden &amp; thoroughly enjoyed. It was a vast improvement on bread &amp; jam.</p>
<p>Wednesday December 12. 1917.  (1227).</p>
<p>I was on chaff cutting to day at the 38. Transport stables. While there a large number of enemy planes [shorthand transcribed as follows] (I counted 12 and there may have been more) [end of shorthand] and dropped bombs near Neuve Eglise. They flew over the stables. Later I heard that some of the bombs fell on a foot ball field and wounded 60 &ndash; killed 12, but these figures may not be correct.</p>
