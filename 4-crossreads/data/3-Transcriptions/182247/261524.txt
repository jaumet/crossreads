<td style="text-align: right; vertical-align: top;">43</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">July 31</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Wallon Capelle with Tim Woods.&nbsp; Returned to billet at about 8 and turned in on a straw bed, such a luxury after concrete, bricks, stones, galvanized iron, etc.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 1</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Wet.&nbsp; Gas Drill in billet.&nbsp; Got paid &pound;1/9/4.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 2</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Route march all morning into Hazebrouck.&nbsp; Musketry and Gas Lecture in afternoon in sun.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 3</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Route march, 12 miles, through Staples and Hazebrouck.&nbsp; Rained all way.&nbsp; Marching in coats wore most of us out.&nbsp; Wet through on our return.&nbsp; Spent rest of day in billet trying to get dry.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 4</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">War anniversary.&nbsp; Gas drill and Lewis Gun lecture in morning.&nbsp; Leave to Hazebrouck in afternoon.&nbsp; Went there per boot and stopt in to an estaminet on the way and had some champagne.&nbsp; Good stuff.&nbsp; It was wet nearly all the time we were there.&nbsp; We hunted round for a good place to eat at and, not being able to locate such a thing, came to the conclusion that the place was &quot;no bon&quot;.&nbsp; A large town but like the usual run of the Flemish towns.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 5</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Parade 9.&nbsp; Kit inspection.&nbsp; Snowy Thompson explained to Lt. Clarke that he lost his overcoat in the &quot;big battle of the Somme,&quot; much to the enjoyment of the section.&nbsp; The remark caused much amusement.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 6</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Fine day.&nbsp; Lecture and practice with Mills Bombs all morning.&nbsp; Afternoon.&nbsp; Barrel piering practice.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 7</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Fiine.&nbsp; Barrel piering all morning.&nbsp; Afternoon.&nbsp; Had to erect an aerial roadway.&nbsp; Very interesting and successful demonstration.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 8</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Fine.&nbsp; Route march in morning.&nbsp; Semaphoring all afternoon.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 9</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Fine.&nbsp; Lecture on Mills Bombs in morning.&nbsp; Semaphoring afternoon. Hazebrouck heavily shelled to-day.&nbsp; Could hear it.&nbsp; Expect leave to St Omer to-morrow.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">Aug 10</td>
<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">On leave to St Omer.&nbsp; Went to Hazebrouck to catch train.&nbsp; While waiting, Fritz shelled the town again.&nbsp; Arrived St Omer without mishap.&nbsp; Had dinner at 1.30 at Expeditionary Force Canteen.&nbsp; A Good place.&nbsp; Herb, Red and I then went over the town and had a rest in the beautiful park.&nbsp; Had tea and then went out after souvenirs.&nbsp; Bought Eagle Brooch and sent it home from there.&nbsp; Also Joan of Arc Medallion.&nbsp; Left St Omer by train at 8.45.&nbsp; Got to billet 10.35 tired and sore footed.&nbsp; Turned in pot haste.&nbsp; Learned from Bisset that John was in billet near St Omer, but had not then the time to go and see him.</td>
</tr>
