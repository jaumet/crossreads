To my mind it is fighters who are needed at present and not "Toy soldiers".

From the tone of our O.C. address everything points to us going away from here very shortly.

Large numbers of camels are used here for transport of water stores, etc across the sandy desert for the troops.

Mules and Mule Carts are in use also by the Egyptians. These carts have very large wheels.

Feb 17th. Thurs.
Building a sandbag wall around our kitchen.

Beautifully moonlight at nightime at present.

Feb 18th. Fri.
Read the "Sentimental Bloke" by Dennis, and Aust – poet. A very entertaining book.

Feb 20th. Sun.
Mumps has broken out amongst the men.

Camp inspected by Generals Murray and Ford and about a dozen other hangers-on.

Two meningitis suspects to-day.

Feb 21st.

An in rush of patients, all sick.

A mess tent has been fixed up for us, and the usual gamblers congregate here at night-time. Gambling with them seems to have an intense fascination for some men. One thing about them they seem to take their loss and gains in a sportmanlike fashion.
