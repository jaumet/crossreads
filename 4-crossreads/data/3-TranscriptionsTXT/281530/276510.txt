 Villers Brettoneux route shaded with fine trees reminiscent of the approach to the Pyramids at Cairo. The junction of the French &amp; British Armies occurs here and this road is to all intents and purposes the boundary. On the right are French batteries transport etc and the prevailing colour is blue. On the left is the Australian Corps with the familiar khaki as the prevailing tint. We rode right into the torn and battered village of Villers Brettoneux along a road that was covered with debris in such a manner as to testify to how heavy the shelling has been at times.
