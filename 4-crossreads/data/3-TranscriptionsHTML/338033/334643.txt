<p>or if in the field of a<br/>Hot day they wear a great stiff bonnet. The houses are cheaply built, often having walls of chalk. A plum bob is certainly never used, for the walls are all squiff. The main roads are always lined with trees, and run as straight as a die, for miles and miles. At approach to big towns and cities cobble stones are used for both footpath and roadway.<br/>These wear well but are very tiring to walk over and hard on sole leather.</p>
