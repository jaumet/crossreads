 of it all  is the cold settling on our stomachs during our last visit to the lines, even our Dr is down with it, you should see the latrines they are crowded night &amp; day   some of the men dare not leave them, they are so crook. While I was round there this morning a German Prisoner walks round on his own &amp; waited his turn in the queue at the latrines, some of them can be trusted to go anywhere, they are satisfied with their lot &amp; are pretty well treated, the French make them work much harder than the British. Packed up &amp; left Ribemont this afternoon we did not have far to go  about 4 miles but to the majority of us it was quite far enough.
