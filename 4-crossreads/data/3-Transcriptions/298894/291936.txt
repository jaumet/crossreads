<p>Monday 14th</p>
<p>At midday we were told to proceed to the "Vaderland" also known as the "Southland" a fine big ship which was preparing for the Dardanelles.  Here we were installed in 3rd<br/>Class Cabins useful as we can keep our kits together easily.   We moved out into the stream that night.    A fine body of men aboard were the Surrey Yeomanry.   Whilst we had a few hundred of returning wounded (recovered) both Australians and Englishmen.</p>
<p>Tuesday 15th</p>
<p>Still at anchor.   I was put on fatigue duty &amp; had to peel potatoes all day, the first time this duty having fallen to my lot, but a beautiful swim from the ships side brightened me up considerably in the evening.    About 7 P.M. we left port with everyone talking about &amp; chances of meeting a submarine.   The authorities are very strict about our lifebelts &amp; we are paraded with them 'tis a wise step to prepare for all contingencies.   Slept up on deck as I do now every night.</p>
<p>Wednesday 16th</p>
<p>For a wonder had no fatigues to do so put the day in writing home a pleasant duty when I have something to say.</p>
<p>Thursday 17th</p>
