<p>as if it were just an ordinary thing  I suppose they are quite used to it by now &amp; take no notice of it.<br/>Last night we shifted our billets again so as to be nearer the firing line &amp; also closer to our work, we finished the stake driving last night, &amp; some of it under machine gun fire its marvellous how we all escaped scot free. I would far rather be in the firing line than out on these working parties for they are far more dangerous<br/>This morning I saw Walter Fisher a chap from Walcha he enlisted the same time as I did but I never ran across him till this morning he is in the 3rd Battalion<br/>17th. Yesterday afternoon the German's opened a furious bombardment on our support &amp; communication</p>
