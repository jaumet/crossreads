<p>9-20 p.m.  The moon is at its very best tonight; sitting in my room, my head has but to be raised, and there high up in the Eastern sky, to the left of the Eastern face of Cheops is the orb of night, resplendent in all her glory. <p>30-4-15. 11-45 a.m:&ndash;   The first batch of wounded are being admitted to the hospital.  They must have come from the Dardanelles.  I have not seen them yet, but as I am acting O.C. I shall visit them all directly.  They are of no interest to me beyond just seeing them as I shall be away from here in a day or two.  It is pity just at the moment when I might be of some use in directing the energies of the junior men.  However it cannot be helped, the die is cast, and I shall be but for brief span an officer of No 2 G.H.  I fancy I have the good will of Surgeon General Ford and that he will see that I suffer no further insult or wrong.
