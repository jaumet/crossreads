 Tuesday Jan 21st. SS Takada Saturday mid day we arrived at Colomba, one does not see it untill quite close, low lying country. There was no permission granted to go ashore,  but  on account of the recent influenza epidemic, but never the less more than half the boat did go on shore. On Sunday afternoon a friend invited me to go with him. We were loading coal, &amp; the troops were going over on the empty coal barges. On landing we found our selves near the native quarters, from there we walked to the European part of the town, It was sweltering hot. There are a good many very fine buildings in Colomba, especially the Governor's house, we sat down  on  under a tree outside that building to cool down, &amp; up came a native guide, who explained that he would take us round to show us the sights for a very reasonable sum, so we engaged him.
