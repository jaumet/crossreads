<p>83 November 2nd<br/>French to return as the French were not prepared to send the German officers, <strike>and second</strike> as they were afraid the Germans would convey back important news as regards the movement of troops from France to Italy &ndash;</p>
<p>November 3rd<br/>Slept in till noon &ndash; Recd letters from Aunt Min, Father, Mrs Gowing &amp; Mr Asher &ndash; all dated about 10th August. &ndash;</p>
<p>Played Bridge again today &ndash; Walk held this mng &ndash; I did not go &ndash; Had my foot bandaged &ndash;</p>
<p>Recd my money today 160 marks, also paid for month of November 48 marks &ndash; Not feeling very well today &ndash;</p>
<p>November 4th (Sunday)<br/>No church service this mng owing to the indisposition of the German Padre &ndash; extremely cold today &ndash;</p>
<p>November 5th<br/>Slept in till noon &ndash; Played Bridge. Cinema tonight, some very good films were screened. Had another big guest evening &ndash;</p>
<p>All flying Corps officers were paraded after roll call, rumour says that they are being sent to a camp specially constructed for Flying officers &ndash;</p>
<p>Recd letter from Army &amp; Navy</p>
<p>November 6th<br/>Slept in again &ndash; <strike>God it was a sad one</strike> No debate tonight &ndash;</p>
<p>Special farewell concert tonight to say Goodbye to Flying Corps who are leaving at 3 am 7/11/17 &ndash; Commdt bade them goodbye &amp; a safe journey &amp; hoped that the time was not far distant when we could all go home to Blighty &ndash; The Concert was a very enjoyable one at the conclusion we sang Auld Lang Syne &amp; God Save the King &ndash; All hands busy saying good bye, some to their best pals but such is the life of a Kriegsgefangener</p>
