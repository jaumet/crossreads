<p>1919<br/>May 16. Shortly after 4 in the morning the Shropshire disembarked 904 men of whom 339 were Victorians, 151 Queenslanders, 358 for N.S.W. &amp; 56 for Tasmania. The men for N.S.W. were entrained for that State &amp; the remainder were driven through the City in motorcars which were in attendance as in the morning there was a large &amp; enthusiastic crowd at Port Melbourne &amp; the men received a warm welcome as they drove through the streets. The Victorians were taken to the Sturt St. depot, where the waiting crowd was exceptionally large.<br/>Argus 17/5/19</p>
