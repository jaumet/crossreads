
September 1918.Wednesday 4Attached to 15th A.1. Brdge [indecipherable]Weather fine and clear. Laid a line 5 miles in length to15th Bdge Hdqrs, where Major and Mr. Kerr were observing from.Naturally felt fairly tired when finished. Receivedinformation to the fact that we were pulling into anotherposition tonight. Packed up all kits and loaded allequipment, a pretty long job.Went across to see Lt John, Battery pulled out to newposition up to [indecipherable] and we pulled in alongside 51st and 55th Batteries.14th Bgdereceived great praise for the capture of Mont St Quentin
