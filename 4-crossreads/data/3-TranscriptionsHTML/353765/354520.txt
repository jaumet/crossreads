<p>June 1915<br/>[Note in margin:] Barrels of Wine washed up on beach<br/>15th &ndash; Two or three casks of wine washed up on beach. Possibly off "Triumph" A.S.C. fell to &amp; got dixies full etc. Our corps missed it. Somebody shook tin of tobacco from dug-out early yesty evening. Was tin I was keeping for Dave.<br/><u>Later</u> Chap passed by with about 7 or 8 gallons of wine. Several of us went down to beach &amp; saw military police wade into water &amp; smash a cask (100 gals). All wasted. About three or four on beach emptied. Another cask coming in. Waited a while but seemed too slow, so came home. Those who waited got it. Reported that 19 down at Kaba Tepe all drifted in. Number of chaps fairly full. Bob T's friend got kerosene tin full &amp; brought him a bottle &amp; Ebenezer Bateman brought jam tin full. Wine was splendid claret. Thought to have drifted or come from a Greek transport that was sunk in their attempt to land here some time ago. Rough weather appears to be breaking up vessel. One killed &amp; five injured of 7th by shrapnel. Str. Bearer killed<br/>[Note in margin:] Hoax that America declares War &amp; Advance at C Helles by <indecipherable><br/>16th &ndash; On hospital duty. Capt B. returned to dug-out. Heard that Burrell in danger of losing his eyesight. Shrapnel fairly perished all day. Reported that troops at C Helles advanced a mile, losing heavily thereby.<br/><u>Later</u> <strike>Officially</strike> The W.O posted up that America had joined the Allies &amp; that Major Charley had left for England with 3 inventions. Bomb thrower, trench drainage, &amp; camera obscura for rifles, also mention of kite &amp; bomb releaser. Officially (?) announced that English had advanced 1 &frac12; miles their side &amp; the French 1 mile on other side past Aka Baba &amp; that our cavalry were seen advancing &amp; were fired on by Turks &amp; their rifle fire &amp; cheers could be heard by our right flank. I heard cheers but think they must have been our men giving answering cheers.
