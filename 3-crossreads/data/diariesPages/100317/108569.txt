As we continued along to the right we had to duck along very fast. A shell whistling very close was only a precursor to another that landed right over our heads blinding us with flash and deafening us for the moment. Sat down to get a breather and then located old Keith Anderson and had a talk with him. He was dug in slightly close against a chalk bank. Fritz sniped us a bit as we hopped into the . All the way back we found that we were in heavily straffed ground. On its floor were many pieces of metal and now and then was a whole shell case. Did not get back to Bn H.Q. until nearly 6.30 pm being very tired and sweaty. Near our Bn HQ a man was laid out. Lifting the blanket I found he was a young chap of the 21st Bn
