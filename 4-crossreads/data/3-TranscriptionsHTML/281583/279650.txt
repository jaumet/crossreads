<p>They are of an immense size &amp; fairly give me the creeps. They seem to wait until you put the light out &amp; then they start &amp; very soon the whole floor is smothered with them, kicking &amp; squealing &amp; seeming everywhere. They always follow the battle line up &amp; live on the dead bodies. So you can imagine how ferocious they are.</p>
<p>I took a stroll round the surrounding country last night but feeling fairly tired returned to camp early &amp; tried to go to sleep but what with rats &amp; cold weather scarcely slept a wink.</p>
<p>Poperinghe was heavily shelled last night &amp; yesterday evening &amp; on account of same, all approaches to the City were guarded &amp; no one was allowed to enter. I saw Shayne for the first time for some considerable period &amp; the two of us are going to try to get to London together.</p>
<p>24.9.16  Sunday</p>
<p>Today has again been fine &amp; everything that one might desire. The front has been a little more active &amp; all night long the chit chat of machine guns &amp; the familiar hiss of travelling shells penetrated the air &amp; made sleep very hard for a long time. Situated on a hill, as our camp is, the ground.</p>
