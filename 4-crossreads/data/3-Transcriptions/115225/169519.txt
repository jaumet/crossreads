
<p>Jan 13<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We cleaned up the buss in the morning &amp; I read &quot;McClusky the reformer&quot; A.G. Hales in the afternoon - Harold just back from Paris leave spent the afternoon in the lorry with us, talking needless to say mostly about the gay city.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 14<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We had another fall of snow last night &nbsp;Nothing to do to-day - I read all the morning &amp; made an ascetylene gas lamp in the afternoon &amp; wrote letters in the evening - We are getting our rum issue every night again -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan15<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;We stayed in all day with the back down ascetylene lamp burning (works tr&egrave;s bonne) &amp; read in comfort - we also pinched a five gallon drum of &quot;metho&quot;. it rained nearly all day &amp; was a bit windy -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 16<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Very stormy night lots of rain &amp; a high wind. ditches &amp; holes were full this of water this morning. but it is&#39;nt cold. I stayed in all day &amp; read &quot;Quinneys&quot; by Vachell - Had letters also a book by Post. Joe goes into Bailleul every evening -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jan 17<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;It was snowing &amp; raining more or less all day &amp; we spent a real lazy day, we lay down all afternoon &amp; slept - Joe stayed in &amp; read in the evening for a change</p>
