<p>[This page crossed through.]<br/>They have to procure other aids to the soil.  One of these is the ruins of ancient towns &ndash; these are comprised of unbaked clay bricks &amp; the rubbish of thousands of years.  From these heaps a kind of cask is dug wh. contains sometimes as much as 12&percnt; of potash, nitrate of soda &amp; other chemicals &ndash; not even these mud heaps are becoming exhausted.  In many parts however it is not unusual to obtain four crops of lucern per ann. from this wonderfully fertile land.  There are many trees planted in the streets &amp; some wonderful gardens &ndash; the date palms flourish &amp; form a staple food.  There are 27 different kinds.</p>
