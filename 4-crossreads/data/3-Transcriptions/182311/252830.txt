<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; This was the first of many prisoners, soon the lads were bringing them along saying in high glee - &quot;Here are some more&quot; then a corporal (Percy Bone) staggering under the weight of a machine-gun, then a message &quot;would I send along a couple of men for a searchlight&quot;&nbsp; Would I?&nbsp; Why I pinched myself to find if I was dreaming for this was the first to be captured, and of great import-ance, then along came a Medical Kit which we hear since contained a much desired sample of German Anti-Gas-Gangrene-Serum.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; All the time <s>the</s> <sup>&quot;C&quot; Coy&#39;s</sup> O.C.Assault, Frank Fairweather <sub>^</sub><sup>M.C.&amp; Bar later K.I.A.</sup> who had come up with his signallers was sending a message every few minutes, every message satisfactory, and at last received the commendatory message - &quot;You are doing well.&quot;&nbsp; He sent the message down to me in the trench, where I was poking round observing the enemy trenches, defences, posts, armament, and turning over the dead Bosch collecting information.&nbsp; Meanwhile the lads were demolishing dugouts, smashing rifles and trench equipment with axes and hatchets, blowing up their emplacements and breaking the trench pumps.&nbsp; Then the time came to withdraw, &#39;Twas hard to get the boys to leave for they were enjoying the wrecking and throwing everything, bombs,helmets, rifles, sniper shields etc. into the borrow ditch.</p>
