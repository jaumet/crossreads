<p>all the way. I felt very tired when I reached the journeys end. I find not being used to their step that it is harder to march with Imperial men than our own fellows. One would in time become accustomed.</p>
<p>Before leaving next morning for the Cross Channel steamer we were informed that if we took explosives across as souvenirs we would run the risk of losing our leave. They had provision made for us here Tea, Bread, Butter.</p>
<p>Upon arrival at the boat the Australians were marched below deck. We stayed down there on the trip across.</p>
