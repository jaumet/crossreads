It was as hot as L &amp; the carriage was in a bit of a mess with rubbish but after clearing up somewhat &amp; making ourselves comfortable we began to have a feed - &amp; what a glorious banquet it was. Cakes, fruit, drinks, sandwiches, cocoa sweets in galore. Eric as usual began to think  of home .</p>
<p>At Goulburn I met Mrs Cox &amp; Ivy &amp; was given more cakes &amp; fruit. After this we doused the glim &amp; all slept except Eric who stoned the crows all night. Hop as usual spread his big plates [feet] all over the shop. At Albury at 8am we were given breakfast &amp; transferred to a Victorian train that left at 8.30 am. The accommodation here was very good  &amp; in our compartment was Serg Stewart Eric  Hop Pierce  &amp; self . The Victorian train certainly could show us points in running &amp; ran on time well.</p>
<p>We arrived at Spencer St Rwy at 2.30 &amp; met with the usual amount of chaos. Nobody knew anything apparently but at last we were marched up to the Domain camp. On the way we passed down Collins St &amp; into Swanston St over Princes Bridge. Eric was much amused with the old cable trams &amp; said that he used to play with those things once, also it was a dingdong city on account of the continual clang of bells</p>
