 1-5-1918 Warned again to move off at 5-30 p.m.  Today is showery.  250 Artillerymen marched in here this morning.  An Infantryman shot himself yesterday.  This base camp is a home for some men.  Men have been here for 12 and 18 months at a stretch, name not on the roll.  Plenty of entertainments here such as moving pictures, concerts, lectures & etc., mostly free.  The Bishop of Bathurst, C. of E. is in charge of the C. of E. hut.  He is a captain.  The Salvation Army caters for the troops in their hut.  You get a cup of tea and a packet of biscuits for 3 pence.  The Y.M.C.A. is doing the same. 
 2-5-1918 Left Havre at 3 p.m., arrived at Reoun 7 a.m. and marched to rest camp, had breakfast and dinner there.  Entrained at 4 p.m.  Moved out at 5-30 p.m.
