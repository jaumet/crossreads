<p>This is continued from P148</p>
<p>Not reported from here so that nobody knew of its presence. When our own plane was above us on its return we thought it was a Taube so horses were cut loose &amp; men ran all ways.A great mess up</p>
<p>12/6/46<br/>Expected to see Taube return as there were only machine guns (Lewis) here, but no sign of him. Go to canal for swim</p>
<p>13/6/16<br/>Go for swim again. Enemy plane reported up 7.20am going west. Horses cut off lines &amp; generally confusion everywhere. Great fun to see the kerosene tin get banged about to give warning.</p>
<p>15/6/16<br/>Payday. Taken off wireless, &amp; put on picquet. Have been crook for some day so go to sleep &amp; wake at 4am instead of 2.30.</p>
<p>16/6/16<br/>Hot as hell. No mail for 3 weeks</p>
<p>17/6/16<br/>;Hotter than hell. Flies damnable. More work  - reveille 4.30am</p>
<p>18/6/16<br/>No mail again. Cooks fatigue. Ten planes fly over 6.30am &amp; return 10am after raid on El Arish.<br/>A great sight. Sherman &amp; horses arrive &ndash; more work 10am planes return minus 1 observer &amp; 3 planes. One plane drops into sea &amp; is rescued. Two others brought down. Enemy aerodrome blown to blazes.</p>
<p>19/6/16<br/>Plane goes over &amp;shows signs of their scars from yesterday. One has big patch on one wing. Cooking all day in heat of the day</p>
<p>20/6/16<br/>Cooking. Artillery practice near camp.</p>
<p>21/6/16<br/>Get leave to go to Pt Said. Pass issued later  naturally &amp; too mean to allow us to ride a horse into Kantara to catch the train. A case of having to walk five miles which puts one  into a bad mood for a start as it is awfully hot. Catch the train by a bare few moments &amp; arrive Pt Said 12.30<br/>&amp; make for the Continental Hotel with Westwood. Arrange for a room &amp; accommodation &amp; sit down to a good meal with clean table cloth &amp; glasses etc &amp; Gippy French waiters to attend one. Feel quite happy.
