<p>was real bullocking work carrying these  sleepers, but officers &amp; all took a hand in it  When we finished we came home by the railway line which has just been newly built &amp; I got a lovely buster slipped on one of the slippery sleepers &amp; down I come fair &amp; square  I got a nasty bruise or two out of it, of course I did not say anything," oh no, we reached our miserable home about 2 oclock &amp; the cooks had hot tea waiting &amp; an issue of rum, &amp; we badly needed a hot drink for we were nearly all dead beat for we had had a strenous night.  Woke up this morning chilled to the bone &amp; shivering like a leaf one of the coldest &amp; raw winds imaginable was blowing straight in on us, &amp; I for one did not give a hang what happened I felt that miserable it is nearly impossible for us Australians to keep warm in this weather &amp; we sleep with all our clothes on, in fact we dress to go to bed &amp;</p>
