<p>1919<br/>May 7<br/>valuables, &amp; we were officially chat free.  Three blankets were given to each man, &amp; we immediately took our bags &amp; marched to another camp on the other side of the Salvation Army Hut, really part of the old Infantry Base Camp.  On going in I saw Bert Dare (Lt) so dropped out of the ranks &amp; had a short talk to him.  We were lucky enough to "click" for huts this time instead of tents.  There were two quotas to go to Engl &amp; before us, no 45 &amp; 46.<br/>May 8<br/>May 8  I was issued with a new tunic &amp; with new puttees.  Went down to Le Havre in the evening, but there was nothing of moment afoot.</p>
