Leaving Allonville on Sunday morning we marched to the town of Walloy. Here all was bustle. Men of a hundred British Regiments were quartered here resting or waiting to take their turn in the struggle ahead. Redcross ambulances wagons passes to fro; long lines of ammunition waggonslimbers food fodder vans, strung out in long queues along the white road to Albert; despatch carriers dashed through the mule trains on their way to on their way from the firing line, where the rumble of guns sounded almost unceasingly.
