<p>days of the two horses in the plough or hayrick, you will see no variation in the stamp, all practically of the same breed, &amp; on the farm they are all driven by a  single rein, a double rein as far back as the withers, from there single. they seem to manage them alright, but they are so well broke in that you scarcely need a rein at all.</p>
<p>These people drink little else but beer or coffee, you can always rely on a drink of beer in the fields. the French beer I may say is very weak, little stronger than our home made beer you used to make for us, &amp; every second house almost, is an "Estaminet", French for coffee house &amp; smoking</p>
