<p>I dug for several minutes the first day. Since then have been a general critic on trench digging, draining &amp; water-conservation.There is a homestead far away half a mile this side of the trenches. That is the point where the 7th Coy suffers many casualties which pass unnoticed. There far from grief &amp; pain one can sit on the verandah (under cover) &amp; watch the poor devils drown down under. We have a fine man C.Sgt. M "Maori" McGregor, of Walker's Ridge, who generally accompanies us on these little excursions. A great sport &amp; about the whitest in the company Mac considers that training in the art of individual foraging is far more important than acquiring rheumatic fever in a rain storm. The owners of this station, Messrs. Stanford, are in England, &amp; their manager seems to be quite fond of swaddy. We will be sorry when the trench digging stops.</p>
<p>The latest we have heard is that we leave in September for England, but it seems too good to be true.</p>
