<p>where we were met by our Company guide Sgt. Barron and conducted to billets.<br/>The French people in this district are in a pitiable condition for the most part. Many of them are living in badly battered houses, and have been literally starving. The tyrants have fled and taken with them <strike>every</strike> the few cattle horses and Poultry which the people possessed. This of course is the fortune of war but the people have suffered in many <strike>other</strike> ways other than this.<br/><strike>The</strike> During &amp; after their liberation they suffered terribly for want of food &amp; comforts. There were many sick and no medical aid of any description available.</p>
