I got a shock one time last night, as he was being fired at over head I was talking to a chum outside when a Bullet whizzed down close to me, about 3 or 4 ft away with a hiss, I did not half duck &amp; crouched in against the Hut for Cover my chum likewise, One has to be careful because the stuff falls everywhere, anyhow no harm was done &amp; all turned in.</p>
<p>Saturday Aug 10th 1918.<br/>After 10 days laying off with the Wound on my Forehead &amp; the Dr. removed the two stitches, I was reported fit for duty on Monday Aug 5th &amp; started work <strike>on</strike> the next day Tuesday on a stand by job which is to prepare an Engine &amp; remain on her for 8 hours ready for any emergency run, I continued on this work all the week until Saturday Afternoon when I received orders to work a Train of Empty Trucks from St Momolon to Lillers, St Momolon lies on a new Branch line between St Omer &amp; Watten, this line is being extended on to Esquelbecq near Bergues on</p>
