<p>Mena House<br/>The pyramids<br/>Egypt<br/>6-3-15.</p>
<p>Mollie dear:/</p>
<p>The ides of March approach and we are still at Mena House, close to the great pyramid of Cheops or Kheops.</p>
<p>For the Nurses Journal of Sydney my pen is building an article on a Days Outing in Egypt, it will likely be published, if so the girls can send to you a copy if you so desire, the matter may not be new to you but twill be arranged in different sequence and may be worth perusing.</p>
<p>Must try tomorrow to see some of the places in Cairo.</p>
<p>Enclosed you will find a newspaper cutting on which is printed an interesting &amp; instructive article upon Benedict XV as a probable peace maker. It may even when you receive it be of interest to M. M. Joseph, other of the Sisters &amp; you.</p>
<p>7.3-15. The third Sunday in lent. I missed hearing mass today because a parade of all ranks was held at 9 a.m.; the order for it was not brought to my attention till 8 a.m.</p>
<p>How is that after the preaching of the Epistle and the Gospel as set down for today, all the sins as set written about therein a very prevalent in our time? In this old land of Egypt, daily warning has to be given to the Australian warriors against giving way to temptation &amp; yeilding to the snares of the devil. It is strange, it is passing strange.</p>
<p>Yesterday morning I visited a school in a native village close by. It is called Mena village, &amp; is occupied by some 3000 people. The master presides over a small room, not as large as that which is to the left of the entrance door of the Maitland</p>
