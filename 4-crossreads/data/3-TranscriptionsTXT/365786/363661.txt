 [The following two paragraphs crossed through.] Several times I went out to the Pyramids &amp; one beautiful moonlight night I saw the Sphinx &ndash; or as our boys there used to say "Seeing the Minx by Spoonlight." It was so hot during this Egyptian summer that one could not do much sightseeing &amp; really while this dreadful war is on,  one  who feels like going out on pleasure bent. 
 Omit While in Egypt some of us Aust. women used to say to each other &ndash; how hard everybody  must  seems to be working in Australia, such bountiful supplies kept coming - &amp; while this cruel war lasts we must not slacken our efforts to help our soldiers. 
 [The following paragraph crossed through.] In the military one is constantly hearing the phrase, "carry on", it means continue your work &amp; I think it would be a good idea for all Red + Workers who may feel inclined to relax their noble  labours  efforts to say to themselves, carry on.
