<p>Coming back to Melbourne made one feel like a cat that had led eight out of its nine lives.  Bye the Bye, there are two of us now, only two. The tailor who pressed my green bow tie and glaring cheque suit stared at me with a grin of recognition. "Aren't you the bloke who drew me down the bay on the Hygea for a bob?" The old skipper was eighty four years old when I left Port Melbourne. He's still eighty four. [dash]?</p>
