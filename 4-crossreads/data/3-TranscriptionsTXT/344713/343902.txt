 Continual shelling &amp; they do "splash" in the bricks &amp; roads but we are well in our cellars when we hear them coming. 
 Fri. Sept. 1st 1916. Battalion came by train tonight &amp; "D" Company were just over 80 strong after we received about 30 refts &amp; details along I wonder where all our new reinforcements are. Some "Furphy" about us having our last go in here &amp; going to India for Winter. 
 Wed. Sept. 6th Left our cellars at 9 o'clock tonight &amp; took over line in salient from 2nd Bn. We are somewhere near Mount Sorrel. Captain Pugh &amp; Signaller West were up here yesterday on advance &amp; were killed. We are about 4 miles from Ypres &amp; 2 miles to the left of Zeeburghe &amp; the lake Hill 60 is about a mile on our right, front &amp; we were on fatigue up there every night before we left Ypres to come here. Weather is wet &amp; we are up to our knees in mud. No protection from rain &amp; nowhere to sleep if we do get a chance of a daytime.
