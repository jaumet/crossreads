Yesterday it hauled round to the South and today it is blowing very hard right in our teeth. The glass is still falling so that we expect it blow harder still. Meantime the bridge awning and other superfluous canvas has been furled in readiness for whatever may be in store.This morning we sighted the first land since leaving Rosel Island, a speck of land situated on the eastern extremity of Wreck Reef. The next land we pick up should be the South Eastern end of Queensland or the North Eastern seaboard of N.S.W.Wed 17th  : The aforesaid Southerly wind became a howling gale by Tuesday night & between midnight & 2 am a hurricane. In eight hours we made only 29 miles against the enormous sea & tremendous wind. It was a fine sight as the high bows of the "Navua" dipped into the huge green seas topped with foam.
