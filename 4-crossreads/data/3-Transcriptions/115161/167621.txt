<p>In two minutes my wife a widow and my children fatherless!&nbsp; Had I done right by coming?&nbsp; I realised now what soldiering meant.&nbsp; It is impossible to describe my thoughts during these two minutes.&nbsp; I thought of many things, of actions of mine which had caused trouble and pain to those I loved, of things I had said which were cruel and unkind.&nbsp; A lot of things passed through my mind during these two minutes.&nbsp; I prayed yes! prayed as I never prayed before, that I might come safely through to save grief and further suffering from visiting those I had left.</p>
<p>We could talk now, but very few spoke, I suppose they were all thinking as I was.</p>
<p>We move our bodies vigorously to bring warmth to our frozen limbs.&nbsp; Then through the terrific inferno came the unmistakeable and heart-thrilling order, ADVANCE.</p>
<p>CHAPTER XX11.<br /><u>THE &quot;STUNT&quot; - NORIEUL.</u></p>
<p>ADVANCE!&nbsp; Just the one word only, but what untold grief, suffering and misery does it mean.</p>
<p>At last the time has arrived for me to take part in an attack.&nbsp; If I get through I shall be able to talk of a &quot;stunt&quot; the same as I have heard (and envied) others.&nbsp; The time that I had longed for, but dreaded had arrived.&nbsp; My twelve months training has been for this.&nbsp; Many have had the same, and &quot;fallen&quot; before they have gone a yard.&nbsp; What was to be my fate?</p>
<p>To-night would complete my first twelve months as a soldier.&nbsp; Should I see this night?</p>
<p>Was the action of to-day destined to end my earthly career, or to cripple me for life, or otherwise incapacitate me from taking part in any military or civilian activity?&nbsp; Should I be debarred from assisting in or enjoying amusements and sports in the future through bodily or mental injuries resulting from to-day&#39;s experience?</p>
