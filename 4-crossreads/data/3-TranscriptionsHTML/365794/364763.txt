<p>Tuesday 25 December 1917<br/>Such a bright sunny day &amp; soft pink clouds at sunset.<br/>H.C. 6.30 a.m. in Recreation room &amp; a fair amt. of pts. present, then long busy day feeding pts., etc.  Menu &ndash; turkey, veg., plum pudding, mince pies, etc., 12.30 p.m. then 4 p.m. cakes, tinned fruit &amp; so forth.  Whist drives &amp; singing in some wards.<br/>6.40 p.m. visited No. 14 A.G.H.  Bright &amp; gay.<br/>Australian flags everywhere, aeroplanes, models of boats, mottos, even a gunyah with Aboriginal sitting before his billy &amp; little electric bulb hidden in old sticks.<br/>Pt. kiss sisters under miseltoe, etc. &amp; general air of comradeship prevails throughout.  How different to our cold &amp; sedate wards.</p>
