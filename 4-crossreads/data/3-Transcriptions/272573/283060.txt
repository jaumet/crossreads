<p>The majority of cases passing through the C.C.S. are veneral cases.</p>
<p>Some of our unit are getting a few days leave in Cairo.</p>
<p>March 6th. Mon<br/>Warrant out for P's arrest. He took French leave from hospital at Ismaelia.</p>
<p>Scottish troops passed down the Canal to-day on there way to the Persian Gulf.</p>
<p>March 7th. Tues.<br/>P, returns back to unit, gives us amusing experiences of his disappearance.</p>
<p>Kept very busy owing to being short of staff. Everything topsy turvy.</p>
<p>March 8th Wed.<br/>Major Jollie is treating the patients as if they were in a General Hospital instead of a C.C.S. which with the lack of conveniences makes the work much heavier on the depleted staff.</p>
<p>Very heavy mist this morning, the sun ultimately broke through and it became an extremely hot day.</p>
<p>The Officers put their gramophone on parade ground this evening and we were entertained to delightful music, mostly Scotch airs.</p>
<p>March 9th Thurs.<br/>A number of No 1 Stationary Hospital nurses visited the C.C.S. to-day.</p>
<p>Bands playing at H.Q.</p>
<p>March 12th. Sun.<br/>Rumours that we are going to Bombay.</p>
<p>Rumours that the German Fleet has come out into the open in the North Sea.</p>
