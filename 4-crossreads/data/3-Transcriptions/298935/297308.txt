<p>We had not time to complete our work as some snipers began potting at us and we had to bolt.<br/>Upon the 6th May the 2nd Brigade embarked on gunboats and other small craft and sailed round to Cape Helles in order to help in a big attack which was to be made upon Achi Baba &ndash; a second Spion Kop.  We disembarked alongside the now famous "River Clyde" from which the "glorious 29th" had effected a landing.  When I saw this place I felt thankful that it had not been our task to land here instead of at Anzac.  Here at Helles there was a narrow beach flanked on one side by high cliffs and on the other by the fort Seddul Bahr.  The top of the beach was defended by acres of barbed wire;  wire about a &frac14; inch diameter with a 2 inch barb</p>
