<p>O.C. of his company, he was put on light duties for a few days.  Then he got leave of absence for a few days, went to London, and then into the Hospital.  He was dead in a fortnight.  He was a young Englishman who enlisted in Australia.  All this time his Battalion doctor did not know what was wrong with him, or if there was anything wrong, although his mates could see he was wasting away.  Another case was that of a man who paraded sick and returned to duty.  At pack drill the same day he died in the lines after two hours drill.  These cases shows that some of the doctors are not fitted for their work, but most of them are capable men and are not deceived by a malingerer.</p>
