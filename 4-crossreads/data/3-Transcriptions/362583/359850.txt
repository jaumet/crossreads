<p>[Letter 2.]<br/>[Received 1 Sep '15]<br/>At Sea.</p>
<p>My dear Mother,</p>
<p>This is the first letter I have written since we embarked some weeks ago. I have left it till now partly from a natural tendency to put off today what you can do tomorrow &amp; partly in the hope of something happening worth writing home about. But up till today we have not sighted anything except sea &amp; the good ship- on which we sail; and the former of these two spectacles has not always been an exceptionally appetising sight, in fact a day or two ago saw a repetition of the first few days aboard. After we had all settled down to our new life, &amp; everything had begun to slide on evenly &amp; fairly monotonously, the first unusual occurance worth relating was the loss of our regimental wallaby. He was quietly promenading the deck after recuperating from a severe attack of mal-de-mer, when he took it into his head to test the temperature of undiluted sea water. One jump was enough. Without the slightest effort he cleared the side &amp; decided his fate. I happened to be otherwise engaged at the time &amp; so did not see the unfortunate accident, but was told that he could be seen swimming after the boat for at least half an hour after he had gone overboard.
