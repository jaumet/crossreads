<p>Apl 10 were picked up by Red Cross or Transports. Passed a fairly big town called Hazelbrook, &amp; no end of traffic on the road. Motors of all sorts, cavalry, cycle corps', pedestrians, carts sulkies &amp; buggies passed us in one long stream. Guess theres a blister on my heel &amp; the perspiration &amp; all is oozing through my boots  The weather is fine &amp; cool; Heavy artillery bombardment towards which we are apparently getting closer &amp; closer. Passed by General Walker &amp; another head.  Arrived Uttersteene at 1 pm which means a 6 hour march, full pack up with only 40 minutes spell. We were expected at 4 pm &amp; I believe there's going to be an inquiry into the matter. Did from 15 to 17 miles, &amp; there were some blistered feet (mine were sore at any rate)  Many of the boys dropped out, &amp; not a few visited the wine shops.
