<p>Feby 4th (Continued)<br/>10a 1 p.m. Spent below getting the Canteen straightened up.<br/>2.30 p.m. Soldiers now assembled for instructions re their duties for the afternoon, &amp; a few words of advice from Major O'Brien the Commanding Officer.</p>
<p>Feby 5th (Friday) One horse died early this morning &amp; was buried 11 a.m., "some splash".  Doctor performed an operation this morning on a Soldier.  Another horse died this evening, "cold feet" having set in at 8 p.m.  Very unlucky to die on a Friday too.</p>
<p>Still crossing the Great Australian Bight, weather now excellent.  Also passed two Steamers bound to Melbourne, but got no news.  Have had no war news tuesday last, &amp; I presume we will get none for another week.  We are still with the "Chilca &amp; Clan McGillivray two other Troopships going home with us &amp; we pick up another Ship (Mashobra) off Rottnest Island.</p>
<p>Feby 6th (Saturday)  Arose 6.30 a.m. &amp; attended to one or two little matters.</p>
<p>11 p.m.  Have been very busy all day &amp; had a game of "Bridge" with Doctor, Chief &amp; 2nd Engineer &amp; have arranged to play again tomorrow evening.  Another horse died during the day.</p>
<p>Feby 7th (Sunday)  Now nearing Albany where we pass 8 a.m. tomorrow.  Have had a quiet day &amp; I would not recognise it as being a Sunday in fact the only way I can tell what date or day it is, is by making it a practice of scratching the previous day's date off the calendar on</p>
