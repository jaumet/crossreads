<p>13.4.19<br/>Still awaiting orders.  Troop decks washed &amp; fumugated, decks partly caulked &amp; necessary repairs to the Engins.</p>
<p>14.4.19<br/>Orders this day to be ready for a trip to Bombay.</p>
<p>15.4.19<br/>Took in the Canal searchlight &amp; all ready for departure for Suez to embark troops.</p>
<p>16.4.19<br/>1 p.m. left Port Said in charge of a pilot for Suez.  At 11 p.m. at Ismalia we changed pilots &amp; proceeded to Suez.  Whilst passing the Australian Camp off Cantara the Australians kept shouting along the banks, where bound are you going to Ossie.  Come over here &amp; take us aboard, 4 years of misery.  Give our love to Billy Hughes and so on.</p>
<p>17.4.19<br/>At 6 a.m. arrived &amp; anchored at Suez Bay.  At 11 a.m. took in 1000 Indian troops &amp; officers a few civilians &amp; about a 100 returned Turkish offeces &amp; men who had been prisnors of war at Egypt.  The Japaneese destroyers our former escort came through the canal &amp; departed for Japan with 4 German submarines, 2 had 1 6 inch gun &amp; a 3 inch.  At 9 p.m. we departed for Bombay.</p>
<p>18.4.19<br/>Fine weather prevailed.  At 6 a.m. we passed &amp; overtook the German submarines under escort of the Japaneese destroyers.  They were steaming about 10 knots per hour.  We exchanged greetings &amp; left them well behind.  We are averaging 15 knots per hour.  At Suez a few hours before we sailed the Nestor &amp; another Blue Funnel steamer arrived from Australia also the Orient Royal mail liner Osterley</p>
