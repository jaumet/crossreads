<p>Wednesday June 21st <br/>billets: fair day: up at 9 am: cleaned up till dinner: paid 20 francs: after dinner I was innoculated again but it don't seem too bad so far: a fair bit of work is on for the boys tonight: after tea four of our "fighters" came from over Fritz's lines &amp; they did get a pasting but were not hit: when they were over head two taubes were up over their lines &amp; besides our fighters we had five 'planes up, making 9 of ours &amp; two Fritz's up besides observation balloons: wrote home &amp; sent P.C.s:</p>
<p>Thursday June 22nd <br/>billets: fair day: up at 8 am: cleaned up: after dinner went for a wash: I have been off my tucker lately &amp; only fancy dainties so tinned fruit etc is going down well. Bill &amp; I had a stroll after tea: stood by at 11.45 pm while our guns banged in a bit:</p>
<p>Friday June 23rd <br/>Billets: fair day wet after tea: up at 8 am: shelled out during morning so got up the road: women &amp; kids terrified &amp; were running around: I reckon they ought to all be sent back further: Jim came down after dinner &amp; we sat on the grass for a talk but a shell came close &amp; shifted us: I got a "P" of the billet: we went &amp; looked  F Poole up &amp; he is all right. A  Algie was wounded with shrapnel he told me. Went part of the way back with Jim: A heavy storm came up &amp; it did rain some: the thunder &amp; lightning was great: it flooded our sleeping ground out: I</p>
