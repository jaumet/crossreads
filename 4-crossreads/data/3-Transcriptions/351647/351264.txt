<p>Its so funny it being quite light till 10 p.m. &amp; seeing the fellows getting to bunk in broad daylight but I suppose the days will soon start to close in.</p>
<p>I went up to the pits the other day &amp; saw some of my old cobbers there, among them Bert Macnamara &amp; Fred Postle.  They are well but won't be sorry when things are finished.  It must be wearing to be there, you have to be ready at any odd moment &amp; you never know when the Germans will find you &amp; shell you out.  Only that morning they had dropped a few shells round, I saw the holes.  I thought it rather <strike>miraclulous</strike> miraculous.</p>
<p>On the way home I saw my pal Tansey &amp; <strike>shoul</strike> was shown where two big shells had plunged into the church.  And the was to their billet as the office to your dining room at yours.</p>
<p>That happened that morning too.  Things were quiet while I was there, they must have known.</p>
