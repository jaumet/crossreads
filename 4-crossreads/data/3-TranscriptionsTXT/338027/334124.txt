 Sunday, 24 January, 1915 In the morning before C.P. there was a large parade to headquarters where the Brigadier addressed the men, he told them that they were to move off and then gave them a severe lecture on keeping up the good name of the Australians, a Maadi resident had some fowls pinched and we are blamed. 
 My English friend called in the afternoon &amp; after showing him around the camp we went to Cairo to his house to tea.  Spent a very pleasant evening it is great to sit down to an English table with English friends, without being pestered by native waiters. 
 Monday, 25 January, 1915 All the bearers of B sect went for a fine trip to Mons well [old Roman quarry] and Napoleon's Fort &amp; the Colonel &amp; Capt Fowler started revolver practice whilst the horses were eating and they nearly all cleared out.  They were caught after a long chase. 
 The drivers went for an all day march to Helouan.  Very hot day, the worst yet.  God forbid when summer comes. 
 The army service are transporting every day to Zeitoun.  At night all on leave in town were hunted up by a picket and made to return to camp as they had marching orders for the morning.
