 swearing in the way that a gentleman shouldn't because he couldn't find out anything about the fight that he was supposed to be running.  His staff were discretly keeping out of the way &amp; waiting for something to turn up.  There was a bit of a whirr overhead, &amp; a pigeon hopped into the dovecote.  News at last!  The message was opened in silence &amp; on it as scrawled in indelible pencil "I'm [blank] well fed up with carrying this [blank] bird." 
 We had a fine time in London, &amp; the ten days went like the wind.  We saw over a big    munition factory that I can't tell you anything about, went to church once at St. Pauls &amp; to the theatre every night, saw over the Museums, or some of them, with Miss Miles, who knows them like the palm of her hand, &amp; had a jolly good time generally. 
 What do you mean by rushing about to places chock-full of earthquakes &amp; things of that sort?  Take my advice &amp; keep well away from them. 
 Your loving Son K. Ferguson
