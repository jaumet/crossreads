<p>(now at Dieran) came down to convey us &amp; our equipment from Esdud. At shelter last night &amp; at 10 am this morning we were en route. The road is frightfully cut up &amp; muddy from the recent rains through which the springless lumbers bumped &amp; ploughed a foot deep in mud, coming towards us are numerous groups of the tiniest donkeys imaginable laden with great sacks of oranges. Their Bedouin owners are dressed in wonderous colors &amp; look far more capable of conveying the loads than the donkeys. It is astounding what these donkeys will carry.
