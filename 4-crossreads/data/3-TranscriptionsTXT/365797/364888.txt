 "Mines", Sydney. 12 April, 1915 
 Dear Alan, We got postcards from Colombo, &amp; Hazel duly received your pretty present of bracelet, &amp; c, which was obtained from the P.O. without any difficulty or payment. I needn't say how much your little deed of kindness was appreciated. 
 We are waiting now for your first letter from Egypt, &amp; wondering when you &amp; Reg will meet, but I understand some of the camps are miles apart, &amp; there may not be direct tram between Gozeli &amp; Cheops. Take care of yourself &amp; &ndash; re the dust &ndash; open your mouth as little as possible &amp; breath through the nose; which also applies to the cold air at night following on a hot day. I am not sending papers now, as they are evidently obtainable on the spot.
