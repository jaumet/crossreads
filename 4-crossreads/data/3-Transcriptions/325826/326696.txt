<p>6.  failed.  However the librarian was very nice this morning &amp; promised to keep it for me.<br/>Yesterday afternoon Gwen invited Mr Leckie, another girl passenger &amp; myself to a French tea &amp; we had wedding cake &amp; sweets &amp; a novel game &amp; altogether quite a jolly little time.  Then at night we were present at a dance though needless to say I did not dance.  Gwen &amp; the doctor did though &amp; Mr Leckie &amp; I enjoyed watching them quite sufficiently.  This Mr Leckie is such a well-read young fellow and always so gentlemanly that we all think such a lot of him.<br/>There are a fair number of children on board &amp; though some of them are unbearably spoiled, some are just little dears &amp; make a pleasant change.  One little child takes fits of haunting my cabin and is by turns an angel &amp; again a rather tiresome little body. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   My knitting is going ahead, &amp; though I hardly ever seem to have it out of my hands, it doesn't get done quite so quickly as I had bargained.  Still I love doing it &amp; it forms a splendid outlet for superfluous energy.  The gentlemen have had cricket a couple of times but we ladies have had no exercise of that sort.</p>
