<p>24 Thursday<br/>Being only about 8 miles from the front now, we can hear the firing quite plain.  Marched about 2 miles to the fourth field ambulance.  I suppose we will <strike>get</strike> be put on the strength tomorrow. We are camped right in the middle of an orchard &amp; there are plenty of apples trees about.  Very badly wounded patients are brought here.</p>
<p>25 Friday<br/>Attached to "A" Section 4th Field Ambulance.  Cleaned up round camp.  Plenty of small green apples lying about under the trees, which some of the fellows knocked down &amp; never picked up.  Slept all the afternoon.  Detailed <strike>for</strike> as stretcher bearer to unload Ambulance waggons coming in from the firing line.  We weren't too busy.  A good few of the poor beggars were knocked about, there being some terrible sights.  We received orders to move out to the front in 5 minutes, but was cancelled at last minute, so we are now as a stand by move out any minute.</p>
<p>26 Saturday<br/>Kept rather busy unloading motors early this morning, some of the wounded are cut up terrible, having limbs blown off &amp; terrible wounds, it is awful.  Slept nearly all day.  <strike>We are</strike> There seems to be a good lot of fellows here, &amp; I am quite satisfied being attached to such an ambulance.  Attached to L/cpl Ryan's stretcher squad.</p>
<p>27 Sunday<br/>Orders to fall in at 11 am.  had a very early dinner. Transported in Red X waggons to Becourt Woods. Marched from Becourt wood through Casualty corner &amp; Sausage Gully to Brigade Hqrs.  Saw poor Percy Merricks [?] grave he was killed on 27/7/16.  Went up to Poziers (just behind the line.  We only had one carry &amp; it was enough as the Sap was dreadfully muddy, slippery &amp; narrow.  I barked all my knuckles on the side of the sap.
