
<p>April 15<sup><u>th</u></sup> 1918</p>
<p>France.</p>
<p>My Dear Ones,</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; It must be nearly a month since I wrote you anything like a long letter although several times I&#39;ve sat down &amp; scrawled to you the wish that all are well &amp; the fact that I am well too.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Yes it has been a very busy time for us all for we have been accomplishing quite a big task since the 23rd of March when in some haste we left our pleasant rest billets in a happy sheltered valley far from the war &amp; started out to stop the advance of Brother Boche.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; It seems the fate of our Division that a period of rest is never completed but is always curtailed &amp; the division moves off to some scene of warlike activity.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; To the lads the move came as no surprise for they were expecting a stunt, had been ever since a few days before&nbsp; they had been served with a ration of Australian Bunny.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; By some strange coincidence the Rabbit Ration quite uncommon as a soldiers food, has always been issued before a &quot;stunt&quot;, it came before Messines, before Passchendael, it came before some of our raids &amp; just as soon as we saw it while in rest, well we all knew what to expect.</p>
