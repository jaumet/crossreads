<p>Sunday 22 September 1918<br/>Was called up to see Doc. Tiery T/M.O. Div. &amp; was told I was going back to Aussie.  No mail.  Did not send across as it was raining.  O.O. today.</p>
<p>Monday 23 September 1918<br/>15th/9 came from Rup also from Ede &amp; Marjorie Wallis.  Got a packet of mail from Wing.</p>
<p>Tuesday 24 September 1918<br/>Nothing doing much.  Some trouble with the 54th Bn.  The diggers won't go over to the. new Bns.  <strike>Borrowed Courtney's horse &amp; fetched</strike>  Read "Broad Highway" by Farnol.</p>
<p>Wednesday 25 September 1918<br/>Finished "Broad Highway".  One of the best books I've ever read.  Nearly made me weep.  Rode over to Wing for mail.  Got lres. of the 17th &amp; 18th from Rup.  Damned cold.</p>
<p>Thursday 26 September 1918<br/>Held four tens this morng. at Poker!!!  Bde. sports.  Did not go.  Mehan pretty sick so looked him up.  Orders thro' re the coming stunt.  54th Bn. reformed.  No news yet as to this child.</p>
<p>Friday 27 September 1918<br/>Left the Bn. at midday for the Right Wing once more.  Bull etc. came down too.  Won 8&frac12; frogs! at Bridge.  The weather is deuced cold but the bed is warm.</p>
<p>Saturday 28 September 1918<br/>Rained nearly all day.  Lre. from Rup of the 25th &amp; also a book.  Went to a concert in the eveng, "The Australs".  Damned good.  No news yet for any of us.<br/>Leave the Bn. once more for the Div. Wing.  In [indecipherable] Doc. Tiery on Sunday.</p>
