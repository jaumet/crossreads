<p>Lemnos when we arrived seemed bleak &amp; miserable.  But it was the middle of winter when the fields were <strike>bear</strike> bare, &amp; the few fruit trees surrounding the small villages were leafless.  The island has a population of some 35,000 who are contained in Kastra [Kastron] the capital &amp; in 37 <strike>other</strike> villages.  The island is rich wheat land &amp; as one stands on one of the basalt hills &amp; looks up the valleys which mark out into farm plots like a chess board, it forms a very pleasant picture.  Small flocks of long-woolled sheep tended by a shepherd with his picturesque mountaineer's costume, feed on the hills;  donkeys, pigs &amp; oxen browse or wallow in the marshes.  The villages with their little stone houses, winding lanes, their church, market square &amp; stone wall form a pleasant contrast to the mud villages of the Egyptians.  All the wheat</p>
