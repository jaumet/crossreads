  
 (100 years or so one said), I pointed out that one cant judge a country after a week's sojourn. This morning we marched to the boat & embarked before dinner time, but being on a ration fatigue, I in common with the other two, were able to remain on shore longer & indulge in sundry cups of tea at the refreshment stall in the sheds. The boat is of a fair size & contains both English & Australians, tho' the latter predominate. Just before tea time we left the wharf & slowly pulled out of the harbour, then hugged the coast of the Isle of Wight
