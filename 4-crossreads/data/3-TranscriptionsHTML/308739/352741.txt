<p>Sunday 9 July 1916<br/>Capt. Bolingbroke of the 5th accompanied us.  In connection with the Stunt of yesterday &amp;amp; to-day, only one man Corp. Suffolk disobeying orders got out of touch &amp;amp; found Himself at Rail head tonight.  An Air o plane yesterday evening came to the ground at Oghratina &amp;amp; was dismantled &amp;amp; brought in in Sections.<br/>Capt. Stracker was out supervising the digging of fresh Wells.  The regiments arrived back to El Maler about 7 p.m. after resting most of today at Oghratina.  An Air o plane after our Operations flew into the Turks lines &amp;amp; Bombed the camp.  The distance covered by our Horses was over 60 miles &amp;amp; only those who undertake the journey have any idea of the strain on Both.</p>
<p>Monday 10 July 1916<br/>Col. Fuller at Brigade this morning.<br/>More Wells being dug &amp;amp; troughing erected.<br/>Our Kit Bags have arrived at last from Cairo.<br/>The N. Zealanders sent over to the Col. a leg &amp;amp; loin of Goat.<br/>Capt. Bolingbroke left this morning for Duedar.<br/>New Zealand Officers visiting Col. Fuller to-night.<br/>Major Bruxner camping out to-night with the outposts.</p>
<p>Tuesday 11 June 1916<br/>Col. Fuller inspecting Camp.<br/>Brigade orders are to the effect that our Regiment leaves Camp tomorrow evening for 24 hours duty around Oghratina digging wells, etc.<br/>Enemys plane crossed over this morning.  Our Plane went in pursuit.<br/>The Official War news received to-day is very satisfactory.<br/><u>Leave</u>.  One Officer per Regiment granted 3 days leave.  Lieu. Tooth left to-day &amp;amp; took &amp;pound;55 to Bank for Col. Fuller.<br/>A movement is on foot to establish a Rest Camp at Pt. Said for Troopers of the Anzac Mounted Division under Military Government.</p>
<p>Wednesday 12 June 1916<br/>Shower Buckets have been secured for the men.  These cost about 6/- each, &amp;amp; will make the present conditions much more bearable.<br/>The Brigadier &amp;amp; Col. Fuller inspecting Camp.  "Billie" the Col.'s Horse is on the sick list.  It is feared that the strain of the Desert Work is proving too severe.<br/>Leaving to-day at 4 p.m. with Regiment for Oghratina where we camp the night.<br/>Capt. Stracker accompanyies Col. Fuller.<br/>Camped  to 1&frac12; hours at Katia for Tea.<br/>Major Bruxner stays behind &amp;amp; will supervise the Camp work here.<br/>The 1st L.H. Brigade leaves to-day to scour the Country outside Oghratina.</p>
