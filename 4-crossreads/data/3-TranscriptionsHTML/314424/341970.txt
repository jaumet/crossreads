<p>Tuesday April 16. 1918. 1352.<br/>Cloudy with light misty rain.</p>
<p>[Line of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my wife today letter 124]</p>
<p>At midday the artillery severly straffed the enemy for several hours.</p>
<p>I am on picquet tonight. I drew 2 picquet at 12.30 to 3.30.<br/>[Three lines of shorthand &ndash; I wrote to my sister today and also to my father.  I also sent 3 ? service post cards the with all the letters that I wrote today]</p>
<p>One of the enemies floating baloons dropped alongside our camp. It was composed of paper (red) and was inflated with hot air. They use them for<br/>aviation purposes to ascertain the strength and direction of the wind currents as a guide to the artillery and aeroplanes.</p>
<p>Wednesday April 17. 1918. 1353<br/>Cloudy &amp; misty with a few misty showers in the afternoon.</p>
<p>There was a very heavy bombardment during last night which lasted most of the night. There was only a feeble reply from the enemy.</p>
<p>We have orders this evening to have everything packed ready to move at short notice in case of a retreat. The heads have evidently the wind up.</p>
