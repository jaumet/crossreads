<p>in charge of the mules etc, went over to the Arcadian, lying just near us &amp; told them that we were nearly out of provisions &amp; fodder &amp; suggested that we should go to Alexandria for some.  After consideration they decided to let us go &amp; at the same time to put a mail on us which amounted to 66 bags.  We wont care when we return if they keep us here 3 months, as we will be prepared.  All lights are to be out tonight as there are supposed to be submarines about.  We have on board Colonel Armstrong an Army Paymaster &amp; he has been telling us about the awful experiences of our men on shore.</p>
<p>May 21st<br/>After an uneventful trip through the Grecian Archipelago &amp; some beautiful Meditteranean scenery &amp; Islands, included among which we passed Rhodes Island, one of the oldest known in history &amp; belonging to Greece, we have arrived at Alexandria.</p>
<p>May 22nd<br/>Have found out that all our letters are up at the Dardanelles, having been sent there some days ago, consequently there is great gnashing of teeth &amp; things I wouldn't like to mention here going on amongst all hands.  I cabled home, but shortly after a late one arrived, so I cabled again.</p>
