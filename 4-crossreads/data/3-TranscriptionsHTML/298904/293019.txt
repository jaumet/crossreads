<p>all &amp; soon were aboard, the planks on Watsons pier were all covered with bags, so we got aboard pretty quietly, when the old barge was full they tried to get her started, but she would not budge an inch she was lying on the bottom, so we all had to get on one side of her to give her a good cant she got off that way she took us straight out to a ship &amp; we embarked, never no more to set our foot on Anzac" the very name seems to have a sort of charm about it, &amp; it has been made famous by the Colonials  I can tell you most of us watched the old hills disappearing from sight, the hills among &amp; on which</p>
