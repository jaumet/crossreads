<p>How I won the V.C. = continued =<br/>                     3<br/>I had 500 rounds for my rifle<br/>&amp; of hand bombs I took 41<br/>a machine gun was slung my shoulders<br/>&amp; I carried a spare periscope gun<br/>For rations  well all I took with me<br/>was a tin of Fray Bento's or two<br/>&amp; in my breast pocket I planted<br/>a nice army biscuit to chew<br/>Then I waved a farewell to my cobbers<br/>I was too much affected to speak<br/>There are times when the bravest of soldiers<br/>have feelings that render them weak<br/>One tear, then I turned to the trenches<br/>&amp; charged like a lion at bay<br/>Then I caught the words of our Colonel<br/>crying Bonzer Gott, strafem, Hooray<br/>you talk of charmed lives: I'd a thousand<br/>as I rushed up that hill like a goat<br/>I got 32 shots through my trousers<br/>&amp; 9 shrapnel balls through my coat<br/>Then a Japanese bomb burst beneath me<br/>For a moment I gave up all hope<br/>But it proved the best thing that could happen<br/>For it pushed me half way up the slope<br/>Then a 15 in shell came straight at me.<br/>I hadn't a moment to shirk<br/>But it struck on that hard army biscuit<br/>Rebounded &amp; blew up a turk.<br/>You doubt it? Well if you want something<br/>The truth of this tale to endorse<br/>Heres the biscuit &ndash; that dent in the middle<br/>is where the shell struck it of course<br/>However that isn't my story<br/>I got slightly wounded tis true:</p>
