<p>compliment by singing "God save the King".  After a calm voyage of five days, May 10, we arrived at Pt. Said &amp; stayed there the night.  The next day we caught the train at noon for Cairo.  It was a most interesting run.  First the line runs parallel to the Canal as far as Ismailia.  On the way during Sun. we passed numbers of Indian troops, with cavalry &amp; infantry encamped &amp; entrenched.  At Kantara we saw signs of the early <strike>recent</strike> fighting for it was here the Turks made their abortive attempt to cross the Canal.  We saw the trenches &amp; dugouts &amp; sandbag barricades &amp; the station protected by earthworks &amp; more sandbags.  It was our nearest approach to grim war.</p>
