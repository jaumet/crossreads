There in the murk and fume of leaden grey, while yet the higher sky is crystal clear and brilliant as with the luster and hues of new cut gems; hangs the great sun. Not as a symbol of glory, a tape of gold, or a dazzling source of life: No! for he is robbed of all splendour, and is changed, as though by a miracle, to be a sign of the curse and the tragedy of war which troubles the earth. And, maybe, a sign of sacrifice, which we may not clearly read or understand. There in the murk and fume of leaden grey he hangs like a great drop of blood
