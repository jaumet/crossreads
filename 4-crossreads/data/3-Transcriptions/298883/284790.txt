<p>after a long hot and dusty march reached the village of Caix.  I was looking forward to a good wash and a rest when the Major (Trickey) came along on his horse and informed me that I was to go to the 60th Battalion as soon as possible and report to the Commanding officer as Signal Officer for the impending attack.  Of course I was highly delighted to hear it I don't think.  I had been looking forward to going through this battle in the company under Lieut. Adams, a very gallant leader and a good comrade.  I was extremely displeased</p>
