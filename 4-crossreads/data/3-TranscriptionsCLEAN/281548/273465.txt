The distance by this narrow gauge railway between (vis from-to) the se  two broad gauge s  is about twelve miles.  In the twelve miles there are twelve tunnels the longest being nearly three miles, (about twice the length of the Hawkesbury tunnel).  The time usually taken for the journey is about two hours. The trees have been cut down and used for timbering up the tunnels and for fuel for the engines.
