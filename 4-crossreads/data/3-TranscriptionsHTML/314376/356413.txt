<p>102<br/>went to a great deal of trouble to make the dugouts strong although in many instances it was a labor spent in vain on account of the intensity of the British bombardments also the frequency of their attacks.</p>
<p>However we got some and we too started our fire. We remained here for three days and then moved further back to Fleuselles a village a good number of kilos back from the line. We put up for some nights in one of the hutments and whilst at this place I received 2 parcels from my home for Christmas.</p>
