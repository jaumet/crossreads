<p>61. France- 7.5.17. In cellar in much battered Village- fighting for 4 days over small sector in Hindenburg line- Firing on average 1000 rounds daily per Battery- on beginning of attack 37th. fired just under 4000 rounds (in one day)- [Arny?] Brown made Major &amp; gets M.C</p>
<p>62. France- 19.4.17- Been in action for 12 days. West of Bapaume- beautiful Country: this letter is very [interesting?]. Americans coming in- attempt (and failure) of [diarist has inserted an 'x' here] Germans to capture guns. [diarist has inserted another 'x' here] badly defeated. Prisoners taken included Prussian Guards. Promotion to Major Confirmed- Recommends Maurice Ferguson for decoration.</p>
<p>63. 1.5.17. Refers to Pats passing Examination  Doubts if many of the Officers serving in France could pass such a test  Writing at 7.a.m- just going into action. Comparison of tent life to Dugouts- Anzac Day. mildly celebrated  Aspinall &amp; Lloyd wounded. Rosenthal <strike>receives</strike> gets W.G's letter- Col. King dines with T.W.G- Mentions Toomey Richards, Hall, [Indecipherable], McLaughlin &amp; others in 1st Brigade</p>
