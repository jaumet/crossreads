<p>October, 1915<br/>Wednesday 20<br/>Left Alex:- at midday in company with another Transport &ndash; beautiful, calm As soon as the men were settled I went to writing room &amp; scribbled some letters [indecipherable] them on to the wharf for the People to post I hope they are delivered. <strike>Much "grog" has been drunk by the men &amp; some make [indecipherable] fools of themselves.</strike> Thank goodness I'm off to the front again, instead of <u>Light Duties</u> Egypt as so many wounded men get &ndash;<br/>Good bed &ndash; Good Bath &ndash; Good Tucker &ndash; when will we see such comfort again.</p>
