<p>[On letterhead of the Information Bureau, Red Cross Society, Sydney.]<br/>9th April, 1918.</p>
<p>No. 1879, Private J. Garbutt &ndash; 23rd Field Artillery Brigade</p>
<p>Dear Sir,</p>
<p>We send you the following copy of a report, which our Agents in London have sent to us, consisting of a statement made to them by the soldier whose name appears at the foot of the report;&ndash;</p>
<p>"Garbutt was Gunner in 110th Howitzer Battery and was wounded by a shell at Ypres Sector at the gun pit position just near Burr Cross Roads.<br/>I helped to dig him out of the pit and he was carried to the dressing station about 100 yards away and we heard he had died there."</p>
<p>Informant; &ndash; Swan (Fitter) J.H. No. 25670,<br/>110 Howitzer Battery,<br/>18 Hut, Westham.<br/>16.1.18.</p>
<p>"London, 28.1.18.</p>
<p>Yours faithfully,<br/>[Signed:] CSW Rutherford</p>
<p>E.E. Row, Esq.,<br/>Commercial Bank of Sydney,<br/>Moss Vale.</p>
