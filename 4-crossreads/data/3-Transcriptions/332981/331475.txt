<p>up some more prisoners in wagons, but had to wait a long time for some material &amp; eventually we left for the junction at Conchil where the train was to proceed past Abbeville. After waiting for a French chef, they decided to put a French engine on in place of our R.O.D. engine &amp; sent her back to the depot, &amp; as the guards required to work the chain, I went along with them riding in the leading brake van.</p>
<p>We left Conchil at 4 pm &amp; after a good run through Rue Noyelles, we arrived at Abbeville at 5.15 pm. I went along &amp; caught it, riding on the engine &amp; at 7.15 arrived back at Conchil &amp; the two guards &amp; myself walked back to the camp, taking &frac12; an hour to cover the distance of about 2 miles. After a good hot meal &amp; a smoke I turned in for the night.</p>
