<p>pile of fireworks if a shell struck them: rather an exposed post: poor old Dick very amusing the most nervous man I have struck: our guns banged a lot after tea &amp; Fritz sent over a fair few. It is amusing to hear the lads asking Fritz when is is coming over etc:</p>
<p>Tuesday May 23rd <br/>on guard in the trenches: fairly fine day: usual routine: Jim came along in morning &amp; had a yarn: plenty of bombs down th 7th Bgde way. Fritz replying with shells: plenty of shells from both sides in afternoon: a "fleet" of five German aero's passed over to the German lines but none were hit. I caught a mole after tea &amp; they are funny little things. relieved by 19th about 9 pm &amp; then billets &amp; bed about 11 pm:</p>
<p>Wednesday May 24th (Empire Day)<br/>dull day: wet afternoon: heavy firing in early morning &amp; Tom told me a "fleet" of our aero's came from the Fritzes lines in early morning &amp; all got through the fire safe: Fritz set fire to a fine old place close to our billet: we "rose" at 9 am &amp; had a fall in for kit inspection etc. Alf &amp; I went up &amp; got some fruit: all the estaminets are "out of bounds" only cafe &amp; biscuits can be had: wrote to my Kiddie" &amp; sent a P.C. to Mim, Darling &amp; one to herself: paid 15 Francs: told that I am on permanent cleaning up while in billets so missed the fatigue.</p>
<p>Thursday May 25th <br/>billets: patchy day: revellie 8.30 am: cleaned up round about: dinner: innoculated &amp; then wrote home also sent a P.C. to Aunt Myra, Jess &amp; Harold, &amp; one for mum: our guns were busy in early morning: a nice scetch has been put up on the door showing a swagman with a wooden leg &amp; hook arm</p>
