<p><span style="line-height: 1.6;">Wednesday 16 May 1917.</span><strong style="line-height: 1.6;">&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>Still at sea and still no sight of land. Spent a better day &amp; feeling almost right again.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Everyone still a little more brighter. Wrote 1 letter in afternoon and with ship rolling &nbsp;heavily upsetting plates. knives forks from shelves.</p>
<p>Wind not so strong &amp; sea not&nbsp;<u>quite</u>&nbsp;so rough.</p>
<p>We are now over&nbsp;<u>500 miles from&nbsp;<s>land&nbsp;</s>usual route</u>&nbsp;down in the &quot;Roaring Forties&quot; Very rough weather indeed. Are well out of sight of any shipping of any description along Sth coast of Australia</p>
<p>[&#39;Warrego&quot; destroyer left us yesterday Tues 15<sup>th&nbsp;</sup>inst.]</p>
<p>Only one escort with us now.</p>
