 The &quot;Gloucester Castle&quot; had been used as a hospital ship during the 
 Gallipoli campaign and was excellently fitted up and also by the way 
 had an Australian Matron.&nbsp; The crossing to Southampton occupied about 
 seven or eight hours and was made under ideal conditions, a smooth 
 sea and&nbsp; a well balanced set of engines.&nbsp; Running in past the Isle 
 of Wight we anchored for the night in Southampton water just opposite 
 Netley Hospital.&nbsp; In the morning we moored to the Quay just near 
 where the Cunard Company&#39;s &quot;Aquatania&quot; is fitting out to act as a 
 xxxxx Stationary Hospital at Salonika.&nbsp; I fancy she is the biggest 
 vessel in the world - I certainly couldn&#39;t imagine anything much 
 bigger.&nbsp; It is estimated that she will be able to accomodate 4,000 
 cases!&nbsp; At Southampton an Officer came aboard and allotted us to 
 various towns in England.&nbsp; Australians (I was the only one) went to 
 London and English, Scotch and Irish went as near their native towns 
 as hospital facilities would admit.&nbsp; Hospital trains were waiting 
 and we were soon off again.&nbsp; It was&nbsp; quite nice to see hay stacks 
 which were real and not dummy ones concealing a gun position, and 
 farms which were not ruins.
