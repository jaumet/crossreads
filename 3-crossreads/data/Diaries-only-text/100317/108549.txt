The wounded soon started to arrive at our R.A.P. and we had a great collection of groaning Huns all round our place. Some were very badly injured and were knocked about in the legs and bodies. They were not very good physical specimens. One of our chaps was shot through the stomach and crying out a lot. He became quieter ? possibly died. The front was normal at about 8 am. At 10.15 I left with Thewlis to visit 21st Bn HQ and there met the General who propounded a scheme for a raid on the morning of our attack by the 7th Bde.
