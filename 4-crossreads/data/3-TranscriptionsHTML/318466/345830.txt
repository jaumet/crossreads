<p>Sunday 29 September 1918<br/>Church parade in morng.  Hun air planes fairly active.  Parade in afternoon to hear some politicians.  Conan Doyle &amp; Sir Joseph Cook came along with kind words.</p>
<p>Monday 30 September 1918<br/>Raing. good deal.  Privett went on leave to Blighty.  Took over a sigs job on Wing.  Went down to P.O.W. cage in afrn. &amp; saw about 2000 go thro'. No mail again so 'spose its gone to Bn.</p>
<p>Tuesday 1 October 1918<br/>Hear that Bulgaria has definitely surrended.  Big mail in from Rup &amp; one from Chicken.  Bought a cap at Ordnance.  No news yet.</p>
<p>Wednesday 2 October 1918<br/>On a Board of Inquiry in the morning.  Great news from all fronts.  A bit warmer than usual.  Hear that our casualties have been heavy.</p>
<p>Thursday 3 October 1918<br/>Played Bridge in morng. &amp; won 7&frac12; good frogs!!  The nucleus crowd leave for Bns. Our truck left the rails on way up!  Slept out in the open more or less nr. Villeret.</p>
<p>Friday 4 October 1918<br/>Feelg. like nothing on earth when I turned out this morn.  Managed to get a good dugout &amp; had some Bridge in the eveng.  The D.O. looks new &amp; I have the wind up!!</p>
<p>Saturday 5 October 1918<br/>Moved back to Le Mesnil at 9.15 a.m.  Hell of a march.  My movement order was waiting me!  Cheers!!  Toddled into Peronne &amp; stayed night at R.T.O. place.<br/>My movement order comes through.</p>
