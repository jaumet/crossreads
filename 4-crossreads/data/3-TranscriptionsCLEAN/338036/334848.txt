
[28th Nov. contd]billets.Saw by an English paper that a raid was made on the Channel by German destroyers on night of 23rd inst. - the night we were transported across to France.  After tea I went for a walk around village  and succeeded in falling into a dirty trench used as a gutter.  To make things more uncomfortable water was hard to obtain.
Wednesday 29thWhen I awoke it was to learn that I was - with 19 others to go up to the lines and run a Divisional Baths at Nieppe - quite close at Armentieres.  After breakfast we set out along the muddy road and after a couple of hours arrived at Nieppe and it was interesting to see the damage done  by German guns.  The Town Hall & Church were especially suffering.  A tram line was partially closed & had not been used recently & along the road the place teemed with old trenches & barbed wire entanglements all relicts of German invasion.  quite close to us is Estaires another town which suffered.
