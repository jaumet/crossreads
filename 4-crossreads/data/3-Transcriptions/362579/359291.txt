<p>C/o Matron-in-Chief<br/>A.A.N.S.<br/>Horseferry Rd.<br/>London<br/>May 10th 1917</p>
<p>My dearest Mother   &amp;  Sisters,</p>
<p>I was delighted to get Alice's note   &amp;  Ethels' (103) yesterday. The mail closes tomorrow, so I must write this time if I'm up till mid-night.</p>
<p>My, we have had a furious time. It is the busiest time any C.C.C. has had. Anyhow we all stuck to it   &amp;  did our best but nearly broken-hearted &ndash; Oh, I must not tell you the horrors of this dreadful war. It seems as if this was would never end. What a dreadful   <u>cost</u> this little victory means &ndash; you could not imagine &ndash; we all hope   &amp;  pray that the Huns will soon give in. The noise of the guns is awful   &amp;  yet we are some distance away   &amp;  they are not firing in one direction. Our big guns must be wonderful. I shall be able to tell you all about it when</p>
