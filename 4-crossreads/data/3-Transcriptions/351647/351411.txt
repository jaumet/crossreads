<p>However I got to London only to find Lon had departed 36 hours before.  It was pretty disappointing &amp; disheartening &amp; I nearly just went straight back to France.  However he had left things for me to square &amp; I had a bit of biz of my own &amp; other chaps to do so I stayed but I can't say I enjoyed it terribly &amp; the coming back was awful.</p>
<p>The weather was unspeakable, cold, wet &amp; snowy.  Oh friggish.  I saw two or three theatres, Zig Zag with George Robey, Shirley Kellogg &amp; a little girl, an Australian, named Daphne Pollard <strike>where</strike> were the chief stars &amp; were very good.  The first &amp; last being very funny.  Then I saw the Maid of the Mountains, a simply splendid thing, gorgeously mounted, the colouring was wonderful &amp; the music good.  I loved Jose Collins &amp; Thorpe Bates' singing was absolutely A.1.</p>
<p>Also saw Oscar Ashe &amp; Lily Brayton in Chu Chin Chow.  Just the old "Forty Thieves" but wonderfully well done.  The dressing &amp; mounting was simply gorgeous &amp; quite Eastern.
