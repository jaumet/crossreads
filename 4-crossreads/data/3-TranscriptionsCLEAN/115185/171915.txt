  
 4 - McCullum - looking so well & bonny came to tea with me and Colonel Roth was here last night at our New Year Eve party. But I left the mess tent at 11.30 pm and spent the last of 1915 with Sister Dowe reading Scotch hymns out of Auntie's book - & thinking of all our dear ones. There is much to be thankful for in the wonderful care that was spread over our troops in the evacuation of Gallipoli - and when Geoffry Sheet & Major Coltman of the 1st Batn they last with 19 men to retire from the jaw of death arrived safe and sound. I felt well - I don't know how I felt - but we gave them tinned peas & cheese & bread & we were a grave little party Sister Dowe Major Coultman Geoffrey & I - they left us at 10pm and long before we were up had moved off again. Where to? Major King the Brig Maj of the 1st - I like very much & so do all the men. I saw him for a few minutes. I hope we follow them soon. We are nearly all packed up to go.
