
December 247.Charleroi in the night, an estasy of purple great lamps, and a hundred great glowing lights, exquisitively hazed like frozen hung flares in the thin thread haze mist of rain:  and the cobbles each individually gleaming and black masses silvered by the lights filling the lorry crowded square.
Today Arthur C. in after presents for Madame and Mdslle - two lace collars and scent from 'Maps' and the C.C. office people.
No Mail:  and all day cancelling and re-arranging, grouting and gutzing:  But the promise is goods!
December 268.A night that ended in a candlelight procession through Presles and to the Chateau courtyard, carolling Hark The Herald Angles Sing! - closely followed, sung well with tears, "How Dry We Are".  Then to a "Resuscitation" at the A.D.M.S. and to our Maisonette to bed in the small hours, and the night still and a high sparsely starr'd sky.
Yesterday morning with Maurie through the wood for Ivy and Miseletoe:  the afternoon decorating and arranging the Estaminet:  vast excitement in the villiage and a hundred [indecipherable] sightseers.
