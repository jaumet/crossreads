
<p>26</p>
<p><u>At Sea</u>.</p>
<p>31-7-1916.</p>
<p>Transport &quot;Runic&quot;</p>
<p>My Dear Ones,</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; &#39;Tis Sunday, the day of good deeds and mine shall be a letter home, I hope it will not bore, will try and make it very &quot;nutty.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; We are now in the tropics, days very hot, but the trade wind fortunately keeps us alive, but oh! the interior of the ship is stifling, in fact it is a Turkish bath.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; The nights are glorious, cool with a salt cool-ness that makes me think of St.Kilda on a hot night, I sit at the stern, smoking and watching the wake of the vessel milky with the light of millions of tiny phosphorescent organisms, the rest of the sea inky black, lighted occas-ionally with a glowing ball drifting past.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Have I told you yet of our pets, a wallaby and a marmoset, both are very tractable and great favourites with the boys.&nbsp; The wallaby is a silent little dear, but the chattering and screaming of the monkey may be heard at all times.&nbsp; I must go down now and feed him some nuts I saved from dinner last evening.</p>
