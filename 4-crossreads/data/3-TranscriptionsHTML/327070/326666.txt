<p>The heavy fall of rain last Sunday did a fair amount of damage, pools of water everywhere showing that no provision whatever is made for surface run-off. Such a place must be a real hot-bed for disease as I hear it is also for crime.</p>
<p>At one spot I came upon a square room where school was going on, the teacher sitting cross-legged on a high chair &amp; having round him a group of perhaps 20 boys (no girls) more or less dressed in the usual style of loin cloth, jacket &amp; red fez &amp; having tin sheets from which they were being taught to read in Arabic. Some of them had such bright eyes &amp; merry faces &amp; one could not help wishing that their environment could be improved.</p>
<p>Here &amp; there groups of women sitting down gossiping, all talking together in shrill tones. The men kept markedly to themselves, sometimes in groups of twos &amp; threes, at others sitting in solitary state outside their hovels smoking their "hookah".</p>
<p>In spite of all the offending odours it was an interesting morning. During the afternoon was on duty in the same low locality, but did not have any very exciting experiences.</p>
<p>After tea at night developed the photos. A mail of 9 letters was brought to me from Heliopolis, letters mainly from C South dated up to 7th Feb.</p>
