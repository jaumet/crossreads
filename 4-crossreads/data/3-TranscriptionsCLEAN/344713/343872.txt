April Fool's Day 1916.Supposed to have been our closest to Paris this morning at 3 A.M. when we branched off at Juvigny.Said to be only 20 minutes train journey away. A case of "So near & yet so far".Passed through Versailles, St, Cyr & St Germain.Crossed the Seine  & passed through Precy, Cannes, Breteuil, La Falaise, Longueau, Amiens, Dreuil, Longpre, Fontaine  &  Etaples (a lot of Australians there).Went on through Boulogne & Calais.Getting near the end of our train journey.
Sunday. April 2nd.disentrained at Steenbecque at about 4 A.M.. & marched though Hazelbrouck to Staples a little village where we billeted in barns. Doing the pigs out of their "Doss".Not a bad little place - Food & beer is cheap.Can hear the guns in the distance, think we will see the firing line in about 10 days time  & a big move is to come off.
Tues. April 4th.Aust Army attached to the 2nd British Army.
Sat. April. 8thOut first pay-day in France. Paid 40 Francs (-8-8) by Lt Matthews.
