<p>to a road, where we waited for the motor buses to come. A great lot came along one after the other, 25 got on each 'bus &amp; the 4th Brigade (13, 14, 15, &amp; 16th Btns.) travelled on them, so there were a few. Passed through several villages, &amp; cultivated fields were on each side of the road. A long line of new trench was dug, &amp; barbed wire ready, a new position to fall back on. We eventually came to a village full of French troops, &amp; some wore loose hanging caps &amp; different colored uniform to the usual light blue, they were fine styles of men, very solid &amp; big, one told me they were chasseurs, &amp; they have just come from the Italian front. Every French soldier I have seen is a big chap, much different to the weedy Tommies about. I thought we would be billeted in this town, but we straightway marched out of it, &amp; wondered where we were off to, &amp; why the motors couldn't have taken us right there. We went 2 or 3 miles &amp; halted in the midst of big trees &amp; long <strike>trees</strike> grass, &amp; it looked very cheerless with rain threatening. It was 6.30 p.m., &amp; to our delight tents arrived, &amp; were soon put up, &amp; when we got them up, it began to rain, so we were O.K.
