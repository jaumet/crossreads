<p>The Russians now appear to be giving the Germans a little of what they gave Napoleon's Army in its great retreat from Moscow.  In many respects the recent advance of the Germans into Russia &amp; their new equable retreat is a striking parallel to the fate of Napoleon's great invasion (See John Bull article).</p>
<p>I have it from a reliable source that 2 Divisions of French &amp; 1 Greek Division are in local waters, north of Suvla, waiting the decision of Bulgaria.</p>
<p>Gen. Godley returns from Mudros.</p>
<p>Friday Oct. 8 1915</p>
<p>Wrote Mum letter &ndash; characteristic re cook etc.</p>
<p>Gen. Godley visited by Sir Ian Hamilton &amp; Gen. Birdwood.  Godley with his characteristic blandness met them &frac12; way down the track and halted each briefly and shook hands.</p>
<p>A Telegram from Petrograd announces that diplomatic relations between Russia &amp; Bulgaria have been broken off.</p>
<p>There are eruptions in the Greek Cabinet.  Owing to a difference of opinion with the Crown, Venizelos resigned office on Tuesday night.  The King who accepted the resignation conferred with the leaders of the Political Parties yesterday morning &amp; as a result of their deliberations it was decided to form a coalition Cabinet under the Presidency of Mr. Zaimis, the Governor of the National Bank, Mr. Venizelos being excluded.  Mr. Zaimis is an experienced statesman, a former High Commissioner of Crete &amp; a friend of the Quadruple Entente.</p>
<p>Rain has threatened all day &ndash; the sky has been overcast with filmy lead colored clouds &amp; occasional drops of rain have fell.  It seems determined to hang off.  I have not seen an hour's rain since I have been on the peninsula.  Whether it's the weather, diet, or long hours I cannot say, but my whole self is pregnated with a feeling of lassitude &amp; weakness.  I've had severe spasms of diarrhoea, especially in the early morning, &amp; I have little sleep.</p>
