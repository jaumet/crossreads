<p>October 1915<br/>Sunday 10th<br/>There was a fair bit of artillery fire especially about dusk.  The Turks scored a win with one of the shells getting 12 of the 7th LH.  They put them in at irregular intervals throughout the day.  Wrote a letter to Doug.</p>
<p>Monday 11th<br/>We were relieved at 10 oclock.  It came on to rain at noon &amp; for an hour things were unpleasant.  Was corporal of the tunnel guard at night.</p>
<p>Tuesday 12th<br/>Australian mail today.  Recd letters from Nellie Ferguson, Frank also some English papers.  Had a busy afternoon so didn't get a chance to answer the letters.</p>
<p>Wednesday 13th<br/>More mail today.  The parcel from mother with the socks &amp; chocolate came so am alright for sox now.  The artillery were very busy for a couple of hours this afternoon.  The weather is very cool now &amp; it is hard to keep warm.  Did some washing after dark.</p>
