 Have just had a chat with the German interpreter of the company of prisoners attached to this place for labour. He is educated; speaking Latin, French, English, and can give some really clear ideas of modern German writers. He paints quite a good picture of the treatment the civilians are having behind the line in Belgium &amp; parts of France. These civilians have, he says, have got quite used to them &amp; (naively) "often come to the canteens to endeavour to  buy  eggs   things to eat". which after all is quite natural.
