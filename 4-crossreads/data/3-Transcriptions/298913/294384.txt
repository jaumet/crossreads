<p>After a while we get half suffocated but the chaffeur, assures us that we are out of sight. At last we moved out &amp; we crawled out on top. Away we tore down the main street to the Nile along which there is a fine asphalt road. The leading transport has an Australian flag flying from a pole &amp; the sight is great. As we get on the road speed increased &amp; the dust began to fly in clouds. Through the streets of Old Cairo we tore scattering animals &amp; human beings all ways. There is no stopping.Time, Tide &amp; Transports wait for no man, and so it was because all traffic has to make way for us.</p>
<p>As we entered Cairo, proper people tore out to see what the excitement was. Trams stopped &amp; all traffic &amp; pedestrians seemed to fade away for us. Again the street widened out into asphalt wide streets &amp; as we tore up here numbers of motor cars passed us carrying officers &amp; red cross  cars  tore past at great speed. Despatch riders on motor cycles at a greater speed still. These last mentioned have practically control of roads  They have the privilege of using the centre of the road &amp; can travel at any speed The dull heavy throbbing of the transports &amp; the patter pitter patter of the lighter cars form a great contrast.</p>
<p>On arrival at Zeitoun we emptied the lorry &amp;again left for Ma'adi for another load. Ma'adi was practically empty but another load was gathered &amp; we left Ma'adi LH Camp bare &amp; empty. Not a tent stood where scores had been.Only the white outlines of stones showed where our camp had been once.
