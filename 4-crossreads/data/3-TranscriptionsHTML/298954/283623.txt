<p>Although Karlsruhe is mainly a distributing camp &amp; few remain longer than a few days at present when so many prisoners are coming from the Western front there are quite a number of old stagers here who are actually in receipt of Red Cross Parcels!</p>
<p>They usually invite some of the new arrivals to little feeds, the N.A's, of course, being expected earn their grub with amusing anecdotes and interesting small-talk.   Rundle has almost adopted me, &amp; teas &amp; suppers me regularly.</p>
<p>To again resume:-</p>
<p>"Dinner" (!) is at 6 p.m. &amp; is somewhat similar to lunch.</p>
<p>(For these two scanty meals &amp; our "accommodation" we are charged about 2 marks a day.   The British Govt. allow Mercantile Marine Captains &amp; C.O.'s. 100 marks monthly &amp; junior officers (including me) 60 marks for these expenses.)</p>
