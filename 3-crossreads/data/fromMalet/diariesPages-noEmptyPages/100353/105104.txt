On awaking in the morning the hollow resembled an ant bed. Heads were bobbing out of dugouts; tired ?cornstalks" were stretching themselves in the mounds some were busying themselves in boiling their billies.
By daylight one can see the difficulties encountered by the landing party, appreciate their great work. The hills covered with a short prickly undergrowth, slope in almost precipice-like manner to the sea valleys. Now everywhere at every point, the hills are terraced, dugouts show out resembling vast warrens. Water carriers stream to fro to the firing line; Indian A.S.C. pass by with their mules laden with ammunition at intervals the big guns round Shrapnel Gully boom, force the new-comers to bob instinctively.
