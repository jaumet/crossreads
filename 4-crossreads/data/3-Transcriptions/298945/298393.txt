<p>constructed to give a clear waterway. The result is several pieces of water &amp; one fairway under a roof of trees. The whole valley is well wooded while the <strike>surrounding</strike> bordering hills are mostly bare.</p>
<p>Roy St George knew of a hill which gave a beautiful view, so he, Dick Barlow, Ob. Grey (Long Grey of Caestre days) &amp; I started out along the canal. We crossed it by a pontoon bridge, met the artist who was with us in the Caestre Camp (coincidence), went through the village of Vaux, along a dusty road, up a hill &amp; arrived at some trenches that gave us food for thought. They may have been used in the March retreat, but in any case they were ours &amp; were on a bluff which gave a wonderfully commanding position.</p>
