<p>A gap again &ndash; Jan 29 &ndash; Feb 25</p>
<p>Feb 26<br/>We have shifted camp from Irish House to Voormezelle, and are in tents in a field  near the ruins of the Convent.  At the gate of this convent the Germans crucified a Highlander, and, I think, a Canadian.  The men object strongly to being in the tents; and other men, who are billeted in the vicinity are annoyed: they say the tents will be observed and will draw fire.</p>
<p>My notes have been suspended for a while.  I had a painful accident with some cordite &ndash; sixteen bags of 4.5 <strike>cordite</strike> charges which we are in the habit of bringing back with us to throw in handfuls on the fire (it burns with intense heat, &amp; dries the wood) were lying in one of the corrugations of our hut, &amp; I was sitting cross legged beside it before the fire, when a spark must have caught it, &amp; the whole lot went off.</p>
<p>Fortunately cordite needs to be confined to explode, &amp; this simply gave off an intense flame which filled the hut, but mainly swept round the iron towards the roof.  I [it?] woke the other two, who were sleeping, &amp; we shot into the air, myself singed like a cinder.  The blanket over the doorway was also burnt to a cinder, but the fire did not damage much that was not near it.
