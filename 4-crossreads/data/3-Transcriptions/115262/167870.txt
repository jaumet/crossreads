<p>Friday 13 April 1917.</p>
<p>&quot;<u>The day</u>&quot;. Reville 6.30. Roll call 6.45</p>
<p>Breakfast 7.30. Moved off 8.15 a.m, Trawool bridge at 9.30 a.m.</p>
<p>Turned on to Tallarook road &amp; after another hours march were invited to a farmhouse for &quot;morning tea&quot; which we had in about&nbsp;&frac12; hr. or over.</p>
<p>Tea very acceptable &ndash; new life in us. Pushed on &amp; reached Tallarook at 12.30 pm.</p>
<p>&frac14; hrs spell.&nbsp;then on again.</p>
<p>Dinner by the road at 12.45</p>
<p>On again at 1.30. Reached Seymour at 3.30. SPell of 20 min.</p>
<p>Straight through to camp &amp; reached there at 4.30.</p>
<p>On special leave from tonight till Monday morning. 8.15 train from Seyr. Expect&nbsp;<u>now</u>&nbsp;to move out on 23rd inst</p>
<p>Won competition for sketch of country etc.</p>
