<p>Monday 6th [Aug]<br/>Weather clearing today &amp; prospects much brighter.</p>
<p>Tuesday 7th  Wednesday 8th  Thursday 9th<br/>Weather still against operations though improving.</p>
<p>Friday 10th<br/>Letters to home &amp; M.B. &amp; Ern.  Balloons in galore.</p>
<p>Saturday 11th<br/>Showery.  Fritz over last night dropping the usual quantity of bombs.  The searchlights put the wind up him a bit &amp; he 'about turned'  Lecture by Archdeacon Ward on venereal Diseases etc.  Very forceful lecture..</p>
<p>Sunday 12th  Monday 13th<br/>N. Broughton one of our Reinforcements fractured his tibia by a water cart running over it.</p>
<p>MOUNT KEMMEL<br/>At night Fred Cooke &amp;amp I went to Pierrots &amp; had a hot bath also.</p>
<p>[Frederick Rupert Cooke. 11931]<br/>Tuesday 14  Wednesday 15<br/>A. MacRay (mock) broke his collarbone falling.  Our Division marching out for a rest.  All other Australian soldiers &amp; divisions are now close to us.  Met Rupe (?) Thompson from 1st Fld Ambulance (Board man)</p>
<p>Thursday 16th<br/>Went up to Mount Kemmel tonight to watch the artillery &amp; saw a beautiful sight.  Our guns were going their hardest &amp; in spite of light haze we could [see] the bursts in enemy country.  One big gun in particular near Dickbusch</p>
