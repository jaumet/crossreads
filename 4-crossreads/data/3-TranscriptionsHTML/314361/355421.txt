On the 12th I &amp; another was detailed to lay a line from [indecipherable] to front line. It was hard work, we arrived at [indecipherable] soon after the evening [indecipherable] had finished &amp; got to work at once. The line went along sunken road where mwd is, turned up sap, the sap is about 600 yds &amp; ends[?] in another sunken road, this we followed for a little over 200 yds, to cross roads &amp; about 70 yds up the cross road we were very fortunate to have it so quiet, owing to men working in sap<strike>it has</strike> the lines had to be placed on top. In the morning the section hopped over &amp; took Bullecourt the consequent bombardment cut our line to pieces, so out we went &amp; repaired break after break, we then laid line at bottom of sap. The sunken road at [indecipherable] is a little like a part of hell, shells mostly 5.9 bursting all round all day long, men were constantly being bombarded. Yesterday afternoon fritz sent over a lot of pineapples, some would land right in</p>
