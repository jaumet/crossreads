<p>30 &ndash; E deM<br/>pleasure grounds, at the bottom is the town, not at the foot of a hill like Capetown but built on a gradually rising hill from he water, it is one of the prettiest &amp; safest harbours I have ever been in. You can imagine with what longing we all gazed at the shore but we are doomed to disappointment  &amp; remain out in the harbour fastened to a buoy &amp; here we coal &amp; water remaining 3 days.<br/>As early as it is there is a young lady named Mrs Campbell on the wharf to welcome us &amp; sends per semaphore a message of welcome  &amp; regret at our inablility of getting ashore. She is wonderfully kind &amp; has fruit, cigarettes, books etc sent off for us, she does this to every Australian boat coming from &amp; going to Australia. Every soldier who has called here know her &amp; has a warm spot in their heart for this lady whose sole object in life seems to be in furthering the comforts of the Australian troops &amp; trying to make their lot somewhat softer when coming to this beautiful city &amp; she does it too, &amp; as we finally glide past the end of the breakwater a cheer is raised, for there at the extreme end is this sweet lady sending us a final farewell by means</p>
