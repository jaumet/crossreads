<p>Monday Aug. 20. 1917(1113)<br/>A beautiful day.  This afternoon 7 of us went to a village several miles away to cut some chaff for our horses.<br/>I have got a very stiff shoulder and neck this afternoon, &amp; if it is no better in the morning I will parade sick.  [Some shorthand here]<br/>I sent the parcel to my wife today, &amp; registered it.  It cost 1 franc and a half to register it.<br/>A big battle is raging in Flanders and Langemarck has been taken.</p>
<p>Tuesday August 21. 1917. (1114)<br/>A fine &amp; beautiful day.  I was on sick parade today &amp; got my shoulder rubbed with some embrocation &amp; took it easy the rest of the day &amp; had a good sleep.<br/>Today I finished reading an Australian Book.  "Ginger Mick" a tale in rhyme by C.J. Dennis of a larrikin who enlisted and done his bit at Gallipoli</p>
<p>Wednesday August 22. 1917. (1115)<br/>A beautiful day   We were on the usual routine work this morning cleaning harness.  There is to be a route march to morrow &amp; the Transport is to take part in it.  We were paid this morning.  I received 20 francs. 14/8.<br/>There is a half holiday this evening, but there is no place within easy walking to go to.<br/>[1 line of shorthand]<br/>[I have finished reading a book "Ginger Mick" by C.J. Dennis in rhyme.] X</p>
<p>Thursday August 23. 1917. (1116)<br/>We are 9 months in France today.  It is showery.  We are at the usual routine work. Only the coopers &amp; water carts with their drivers had to go on the start today.<br/>Bill Grey went on the blighty leave this morning.  The leave train near Bologne colided with an ammunition train &amp; it is reported here that 40 were killed &amp; injured.</p>
