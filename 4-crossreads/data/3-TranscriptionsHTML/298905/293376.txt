<p>every man was either given sand bags or picks &amp; shovels, for they are just as necessary as you rifle &amp; bayonet in a charge when you have to dig yourselves in as we had to do<br/>At last the fateful hour came &amp; A. Coy. sneaked quietly out &amp; we followed them, when we were about 100 yards out our gun's opened up the bombardment it lasted exactly 2 minutes on the first trench &amp; we all advanced under cover of it, so good was the timing that when we were about 40 yards off their barb wire the guns lifted &amp; A. Coy. rushed the trench supported by C. the instant our guns lifted, the German batteries &amp; machine guns which had been waiting opened up on us &amp; let Hell lose for a torrent of high explosive shrapnel &amp; bullets were let loose on us men fell in all directions, but nothing could stop the boy's now their blood was up into the trench they dashed &amp; bayonetted &amp; shot</p>
