<p>Friday 17th January<br/>In office all day. Dealt with a huge amount of repatriation and educational matters.</p>
<p>Saturday 18th January<br/>In office all day. We now have verily a paper war. Dined with M &amp; Madame Lamotte. Spent a pleasant evening. I again regret my inability to converse in French</p>
<p>Sunday 19th January<br/>Writing letters in morning. In afternoon attended artillery Race meeting at Thuin. A very successful show indeed. With Lt Pope dined at No 20 C.C.S. Gen McNicoll arrived in connection with Education matters.</p>
<p>Monday 20th January<br/>Gen McNicoll visiting unit and lecturing. My electrical masseur ill with influenza and cannot attend to me for a week or more.</p>
<p>Tuesday 21st January<br/>Several callers in morning. After lunch attended Divisional Racemeeting at Bomeree. Quite a good show. A few accidents but none serious. Capt OHara Wood's horse won one race. Late in afternoon visited M &amp; Madame Gobbe, Charleroi. Many visitors present. Dealing with "Peace" Honour list.</p>
<p>Wednesday 22nd January<br/>Visited 5th, 6th &amp;7th Brigades, Artillery and Engineers re Honours list. Platoon competitions being held by 7th Brigade. In afternoon called on M &amp; Madame Luyier at Charleroi. At night dined with our host and hostess here. After dinner visited ballroom at Marchienne- au -Pont, where 26th Battalion had a very fine dance for officers. Hall beautifully decorated and music excellent. I only stayed 20 minutes and did not dance.</p>
