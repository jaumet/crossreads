<p>379 Sig. H.W.Pryce<br/>16th M.Gun Coy, 16th Bde AIF<br/>Abroad</p>
<p>23rd June 1917</p>
<p>[The following words are written across the top of the page:]<br/>Moving from here shortly but hope for leave (school) first. I may see a little of the south, hope so as this place in summer is just as deadly as it was in <u>early winter</u>  Love from Harry</p>
<p>My dear Mother</p>
<p>As you see I have had another move along, am a sig. fully fledged, bristling all over with the impedimenta of my profesh. and looking more than ever like a banshee from the back blocks; what with telescopes compasses, watches, helios &amp; phones etc, I hardly know 'which is me' when I go out walking. All this miscellaneous tangle of gear and shooting irons gets very perplexing at times &ndash; not only does it have the devilish intelligence that causes it to crawl all over &amp; round the wearer until he is finally reduced to a state of imbecility of the most profane type &ndash; but it also has the additional vice, at times, which leads to short sighted people saluting</p>
