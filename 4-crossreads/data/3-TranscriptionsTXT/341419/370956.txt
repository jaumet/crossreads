 there when up steps a gentleman &amp; enquires if it would be possible to get a message to a friend of his on board ship.  I was going back there so of course I offered to take the message.  He thanked me, wrote a message &amp; had bad taste enough to put a shilling on it for me.  It took the wind out of my sails for a bit but I did not like to refuse it &amp; so I sent it back by the sailor who accompanied me.  But ye Gods, I must have developed into a very low down looking individual!  But then, Australians have eyes to see &amp; see not. 
 Tomorrow we disembark into the train &amp; reach Sydney on Boxing morning.
