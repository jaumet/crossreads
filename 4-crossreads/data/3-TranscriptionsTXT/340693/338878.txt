For miles along the road are avenues of the camps. Shops of all descriptions are in each batch of Camps and picture halls and theatres are most modern, one theatre giving two performances nightly. Laundries grocer and mercery shops are all there, in fact quite a town is springing up on each side of the road for a long way on. The motor garages and attendant taxis are almost as numerous as in London and all do remarkably well &ndash; in fact they are most independent now but now the Petrol shortage is so acute I daresay they will be very much affected. Some of the homes and retreats for soldier's amusement, run by several of the County ladies are models of luxury and most beautifully kept. One must see these places to realise the extent the people of England have given up their time, money and talent
