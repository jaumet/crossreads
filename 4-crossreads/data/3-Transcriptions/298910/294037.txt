<p>quite a lot who had managed to scramble in with broken arms, legs, etc. Then there was another phase to interest us to get the wounded off no mans land &ndash; last night a dozen volunteers went over &amp; got 8 cases &ndash; but the Snipers made it too hot so we had to give it up &ndash; All day today we can see on to mans land by periscope at least 5 cases of wounded trying to move to give us a signal they are still alive, for these poor devils we are volunteering to go to night &amp; do our utmost &ndash; During today we got three fellows by rope etc &ndash; first we throw them a water bottle &amp; then drag them in - <u>My mate Harry Ramsden this evening stood up on the parapet &amp; looked over</u> - just below him was a huge shell hole &amp; a fellow partly buried, in answer he said he was unhurt, we soon got him over. Also got Cpl Jacobs 32nd shot in the breast, a brave lad too. <u>On Wednesday</u> evening at 6 pm</p>
