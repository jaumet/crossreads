By nightfall the huge camp had disappeared and about 20,000 troops with baggage had marched into Cairo.  On this march we discovered that our feet had become very tender after manoeuvring on nothing but yielding sand for so long a time.We entrained and arrived at Alexandria the following day about noon.  The 6th and 7th Battalions embarked on "A24" the "Galeka" and we sailed shortly afterwards. The ship was rather small and we were uncomfortably crammed.
