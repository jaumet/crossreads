<p>from the nearest railway station, viz Amesbury, and about 2 miles from Shrewton, the nearest village. Stonehenge is about 4 miles away &amp; there are two or three other villages a few miles away. Such is a brief outline of our new home.</p>
<p>To return to the days doings;  Alan &amp; I went to Shrewton this afternoon. It is a real old-fashioned township about half-a-mile in length &amp; lies along a beautiful little running stream. It has no design, &amp; its streets wander wither they wish, here there &amp; everywhere. It has nothing modern in it but still possesses the old English style of small houses &amp; cottages many of them with mud walls and all of them with tiny casement-like windows, some of which look quite romantic. In fact, as we were passing one house, I mentioned this to Alan &amp; suggested that one of the windows in it made me think of the days when sweet maidens smiled down upon warriors brave, &amp; strange to say, no sooner had we gone</p>
