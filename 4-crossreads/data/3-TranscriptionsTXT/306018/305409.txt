 March 1917 Shifted camp again into a more sheltered position.  There was a Brigade muster parade in the afternoon. &amp; the troops were lectured about taking the natives fowls, crops etc.  Offenders to be strictly dealt with etc. 
 Tuesday 13th Took the camels to water.  Each camel takes &frac12; an hr.  It is a long job &amp; takes fully &frac12; a day.  Drew the rations in the afternoon &amp; afterwards took some camels to the 22nd Bgde First line 
 Wednesday 14th Had an easy day.  2 Turkish planes were about nearly all the morning &amp; were heavily fired on.  It is getting quiet common now that hardly any notice is take of them at all. 
 Thursday 15th Went round the regiments &amp; collected all the waste horse feed.  In the afternoon drew the rations  An Australian mail arrived.  I got 3 letters.  Got paid today 
 Friday 16th orderly to day. 
 Saturday 17th Went out with the camels grazing.  They are getting very little work now &amp; are making up condition
