8th Dec 1918
To-day is Sunday and the weather has been simply glorious, a great contrast to the week days.  The sun shone warmly and the mud seemed a little less troublesome, although the puddles &amp; holes were still full of soft mud.
During the day I continued to arrange matters in readiness for Charleroi, &amp; this evening a meeting was held to elect a new secretary for the Central committee, &amp; to discuss the arrangements.  Harry McNeil, who was secretary of our concert committee, was elected secretary. The attendance at the meeting was not good, &amp; the sporting demand &ndash; mostly of the transport was conspicuous in their apparently intentional absence.
