<p>The chateau had not been shelled so far, but there was a feeling that soon, something would be doing in the line.</p>
<p>An observation baloon [balloon?] of the enemy was a very little way away, straight down from the chateau, the occupant could easily see that was taking place.&nbsp; Felt that something was going to happen at any time, and that wretched balloon gazing at us the whole time was most uncanny.</p>
<p>April 1st. arrived and all was going on much the same as usual, when suddenly a shell flies over the roof of the chateau, and makes a mess of the church.&nbsp; I am working with others in the entrance hall, which faces this wretched balloon, and the front door looks awfully wide and large.&nbsp; I don&#39;t like it a bit.</p>
<p>Another shell bursts just outside on the lawn, then another hits the right wing of the building.&nbsp; Enough!&nbsp; he has started, so we start too and all get down to the cellars as quickly as possible, general and all the lot.&nbsp; Rank doesn&#39;t count much now, the main thing is comparative safety.&nbsp; He slows down for a time, which gives us a chance to run to a wood about half a mile away, where there are some dugouts.&nbsp; We stay there for the night.</p>
<p>The following day we have to load the lorries with the stuff from the chateau.&nbsp; This gives him his que [cue?].&nbsp; He starts slamming in the shells &quot;thick and heavy&quot;, it&#39;s a case of move quick or stop altogether, most of us do the former.&nbsp; I am just going for all I&#39;m worth down the drive to jump on a lorry when I hear another coming, just had time to fall flat and missed the burst, but an officer got some of it.&nbsp; I jumped up again and had just got my leg on to the lorry when one hit the ground only a few yards away.&nbsp; The lorry driver waited no longer, the fellows &quot;lugged&quot; me on board, and I guess that lorry had never travelled quicker.&nbsp; None of us spoke for a time, until we were safely away from</p>
