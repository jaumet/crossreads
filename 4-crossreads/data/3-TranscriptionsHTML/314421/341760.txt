<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows]<br/>I received a letter from my wife today 86, dated September 9, also one from Griffiths Brothers telling me they were forwarding a hamper sent by the people of Bridgewater.  [End of shorthand]<br/>There is a very heavy bombardment this evening which grew to great intensity to night.</p>
<p>[Shorthand transcribed as follows]<br/>Tonight I received a letter and a parcel of tobacco from my sister dated September 14, No. 6.  [End of shorthand]<br/>Wednesday Nov. 14. 1917.  1199<br/>It is foggy &amp; cloudy today, and a sharp frost last night. This afternoon we went for a bath to a Bath run by a scotch Battalion. It was first class, hot showers etc. We were issued with new underpants, shirt &amp; socks in exchange for our dirty one. We took up a collection for them.</p>
