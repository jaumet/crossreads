<p>Wednesday June 21. 1916.<br/>A nice day with the wind from the South West. The course is 4 points North of West. One of the Pioneers "Darkey" Silver was sent to the Hospital yesterday evening and this morning I was told he was suffering from measles. The Sports are to be held this afternoon.</p>
<p>The Sports were held this afternoon &amp; were a very tame affair. They consisted chiefly of Rope Quoits, Deck Quoits, and Deck Billiards.</p>
<p>They will be continued to morrow. I wrote 2 letters today.</p>
<p>Thursday June 22. 1916.<br/>A nice day, with a fairly strong head wind on the starboard bow. The course is 2 points North of West.</p>
<p>Short arm inspection this afternoon.</p>
<p>Friday June 23. 1916<br/>Another nice day with a head wind. The course has not changed. I have finished reading a great lady detective tale entitled "Joan Mar" by Marie Connor</p>
