<p>Monday 25 October 1915&nbsp;<br />Steaming South in the first class Steam Ship Demosthenes, of the Aberdeen line.&nbsp;11. A.M.&nbsp;A submarine scare, ship canted by the sudden putting over of the helm.&nbsp;11-30am. A water spout on the port&nbsp;bow, reaching from the clouds to the water.<br />Rest of the day uneventful.<br />Passed close by &amp; in the distance many islands. Each with forbidding precipitous sides rising &nbsp;from the sea. Villages.&nbsp;Cultivated areas.<br />Sea Calm.</p>
<p>Tuesday 26&nbsp;October 1915&nbsp;<br />Night without incident. Morning none more perfect for atmosphere &amp; Sea.&nbsp;<br />Steaming in S.S. Demosthenes a zig zag course down the Aegean sea.<br />Absence of Seabirds remarked. A few species of different kinds of land birds came on to the ship.</p>
<p>Wednesday 27 October 1915&nbsp;<br />Morning perfect atmospherically &amp; Marinely.&nbsp;<br />In Alexandria <s>a</s> Harbour at 9-30 a.m. At wharf at 12-45 p.m. Landed on wharf at 1-45 p.m. At Majestic Hotel 2-30 p.m. Reported Head Quarters Mutapha [Mustapha]&nbsp;D.A.A.S. &amp; A.D.M.S. at 5-30 p.m. &ndash;&nbsp;Took Jerrom to Variety show <s>at</s>&nbsp;6 to 7-45 p.m.<br />Dinner Hotel Majestic 8-15 p.m. Saw Lieut. Col. Christian in his bed-room somewhat sick.&nbsp; Bed. 10 p.m.</p>
