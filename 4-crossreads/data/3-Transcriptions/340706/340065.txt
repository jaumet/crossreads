<p>he used to ride in the Bois de Boulogne &amp; after presenting me we have a drink though more than one the girls will not take. One is as lively as a cricket &amp; much attracts me. We afterwards make a party of 4 &amp; decide to dine at Maxim much against the girls wishes who declare that it is too dear &amp; not nice. However being men we are obstinate &amp; enter the door &amp; red vestibule of this place so famous [by] Merry Widow</p>
<p>The walls contain painting for most part of nude women &amp; mirror &amp; glass are everywhere, seats along the side are cushioned &amp; the remainder is chair. If you wish to sit near the wall a table is pulled out for you. Your hats &amp; sticks are pounced upon &amp; everywhere flunkies surround you in the hope of a tip. The girls choose a meal that is not too expensive &amp; dispute one of the items yet cost is 144 fr. &amp; we have had scarcely any wine (2 carafes).</p>
<p>From here we wander off without any definite plans. One girl wants to go dancing at Montmartre &amp; this appeals to me but McColl cannot dance so we go in Folies Bergeres, &amp; if a bergere entered into this spacious building she would be overwhelmed.
