<p>February 1917<br/>daughters are in England &amp; one is engaged to an Australian.  Next day we called on Mr Briggs, Chief Electrical Engineer, Egyptian Rlways &amp; had a good yarn with him.  Afterwards went out to the 14th Genl Hospital Abbassia &amp; saw several of our boys there.  They find it very lonely &amp; they are glad to see anyone they know.  Listened to the band in the gardens in the afternoon &amp; had dinner there.  Cairo has changed very much.  Nearly all the old places of amusement are closed as there are so few troops to patronise them.</p>
<p>3rd Febry<br/>We left Cairo by the 7 am train &amp; got to Kantara after 11 am.  Managed to get room on the desert train for the goods, so got them over to East Kantara &amp; loaded on the 10 to 6 pm train.  Kantara is an immense  place now, the most important on the canal  At Gilban we saw one of the new "tanks".  Several of them are here &amp; we are wondering how Johnny Turk will like this.
