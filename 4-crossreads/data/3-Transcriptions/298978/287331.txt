It is hinted that we may be in a scrap pretty soon; somehow don't feel much worried about it.<br/>The wife of the Captain of the ship visits him &ndash; a most unusual spectacle, at least to us, to behold once more a woman. It is only when one has been cooped up for a considerable period with nothing but men that one can understand what this means. It made me long for the refinement of society.</p>
<p>Jan: 28<br/>Leave the Port for the Canal. We are now going to have an interesting time &ndash; our first view of the land of the Pharoahs &ndash; on either side of the Canal are trenches and camps; all the stations are protected with sandbags; this looks like the real thing. There are a large number of Indian troops all along the Canal. We remain a night, in the Salt Lake, prepared to land at any moment for a scrap.</p>
<p>Jan: 29<br/>We hear there is something doing. Our first view of an aeroplane.</p>
<p>Jan: 30<br/>I make a sketch of an Arab woman. Through a telescope it looks as if there is going to be some interesting work for my brush.
