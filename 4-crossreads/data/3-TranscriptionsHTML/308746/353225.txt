<p>1917<br/>Nov. 8<br/>Relieved at 1400 &amp; took up position in trench near Routers Railway.  Ration fatigue to Anzac Ridge but no rations arrived.  Raining.</p>
<p>Nov. 9<br/>Two trips to Hooge for rations &amp; again for dixies a.m.  Duckboard fatigue p.m.  Gas guard 0500 to 0600.</p>
<p>Nov. 10<br/>Duckboard track laying in pouring rain a.m.  Fritz was blowing the track out almost as soon as we laid it down.</p>
<p>Nov. 11<br/>Relieved at 0900 by Lancashire fusiliers.  Hell for leather back to Ypres &amp; then on to Vancouver Camp nr. Ouderdam.  Free beer on issue!  Pay available.</p>
<p>Nov. 12<br/>Bath p.m.  Clean shirts, pants &amp; socks &ndash; no singlets.</p>
<p>Nov. 13<br/>Left per boat for Mont des Cats.  Camped in tents nr. Berthen.  Nail in boat harmed me.</p>
<p>Nov. 14<br/>Left for Zuytpeene per motor lorry as foot sore.</p>
<p>Nov. 15<br/>Bn. left a.m. for Wardrecque.  I remained behind guarding ammunition &amp; packs with 3 other men.  Lorry did not arrive &amp; we had no rations.</p>
