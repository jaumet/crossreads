 The anguish of the suffering and the weakness of the strong And the thousand things that make the soldier's name. 
 You cannot know the bravery that leads the soldier forth Defying shells and guns that sweep the space And mow as with Time's sickle from the pride of Nature's youth As toward the foe he boldly turns his face 
 And the tiny little dug-outs where the weary men are crammed For the moment just to stay Exhaustion's grip And the shells discordant screeching drown the rifle's noisy din The ghastly roar, then moans from someone's lips
