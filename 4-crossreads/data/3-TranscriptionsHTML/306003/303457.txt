<p>4-8-16<br/>at times, &amp; then the Turkish artillery got the range of our led horses &amp; played havoc with them but very few of the men leading them were hit  Brigades of Turkish infantry could be seen coming up to reinforce them &amp; at 7 oclock we received the order to retire the whole of Head Quarters were in the firing line, &amp; Nelson &amp; I were the two last to retire from our end of the line.</p>
<p>The machine guns &amp; shrapnel fire was very heavy as we retired &amp; we were under a heavy fire right back to the horses (3/4 of a mile)   Nelson had retired 100 yards &amp; I was about to follow when he fell hit by a machine gun bullet in the foot I ran over to him &amp; pulled his legging &amp; boot off &amp; dressed the wound &amp; called to Rich (H.Q.) &amp; we started to help Nelson back, we were on our own as the rest had all retired &amp; the Turks</p>
