<p>early &amp; to my surprise saw Charleys divinity waiting with Ma presumably for early door rush. Charley became wildly excited, smiled &amp; bowed &amp; made himself a general nuisance to others nearly landing us both in a fight. He squeezed his way up near them &amp; shrewd boy engaged Ma in conversation. He suggested he should procure tickets as it was probably his last night in Melbourne and he felt very happy. He asked would he secure a box  &amp; save waiting. My mind began to wobble a box &amp; supper for 4/8<br/>Ma said certainly not &amp; I breathed freely. I saw Gus. L. passing, I rushed over to him &amp; asked for loan of a pound or two. He could only manage 2/-. Ma told Charley off to get the tickets for her &amp; daughter the borrowed 2/- bought Choc's for Charley We arrived</p>
