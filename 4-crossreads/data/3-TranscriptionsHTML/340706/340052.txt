<p>98<br/>as Paris is much the same as Sydney I seem to know every street &amp; house as if I had lived here for years. I wonder if I ever did &ndash; At anyrate I strolled along to Avenue to Champs Elysees flanked on either side with guns &amp; on the seats many a lover <strike>of</strike> from this gay city a strange contrast of love &amp; war.</p>
<p>But Paris is essentially a city of contrasts in which a dead Past is ever thrusting its <strike>skull</strike> hollow eyes on a gay &amp; living Present which smiles to hide the Past. Arrived at [indecipherable] I get a room 7 fr &amp; breakfast 3 fr &amp; retire for night. There are two others in the room one of whom is an Australian perhaps I shall see him to morrow. His name is Baker.</p>
<p>8-4-19 (Tues)<br/>Morn &amp; morn such as Paris loves to clothe herself bright warm sunshine &amp; a sky of blue. I join forces with Baker who agrees to follow my lead &ndash; in breakfast room I meet Coghill &amp; am afraid as he is my battalion commander &amp; I am really here without permission. He recognises me &amp; is no longer in command of battalion &amp; I arrange to meet him at 6 pm in order to go to the Opera.</p>
