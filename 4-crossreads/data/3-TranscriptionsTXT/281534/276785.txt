
15/1/19 GourdinneAn unpleasant cold day. The whole of the 1915 draft paraded at 10.30 looking very well with their new ribbons. All the village was turned out to see them off. I shook hands with each one of them and the Band then struck up "Auld Lang Syne". Under the R.S.M the Band then swung round followed by our standard bearers and the column swung out of the square followed by the cooker. Many of the men felt the parting keenly and I was not unmoved myself. Mounted my horse and rode across country to Bde HQ where I saw the General and had a talk to him about Honours & Rewards among other things. Afterwards went with Brown BM to the corner of the square and saw our draft swing through under K Anderson. The General
