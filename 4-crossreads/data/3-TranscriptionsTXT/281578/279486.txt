It put the wind up him, as he had a searchlight playing about the sky for hours afterwards, but no more 'planes came. Fritz was sending up some very pretty lights spiral green ones like a snake, a signal of some sort. The usual bright flares were also plentiful. The listening post party went out, &amp; a Lewis gun team took up a position in front. A party was also put on wiring round the walls of the brick house. Fritz spotted them, turned on a machine gun, &amp; a corporal came in with that rotten question, that means so much "Are the stretcher bearers there ?" These brave chaps went out twice &amp; carried in two badly wounded men. Later Cpl, Oakes struggled in groaning &amp; said "Is there an officer there, he's coming up the bridge, &amp; we'll lose all our men soon. "Oakes was wounded by a grenade &ndash; there were only three chaps in our part of the trench, &amp; Cpl Brandt &amp; I were sent out, the dozen or so chaps lined a bank away from that cursed house, &amp; strained their eyes, but no Germans were coming.
