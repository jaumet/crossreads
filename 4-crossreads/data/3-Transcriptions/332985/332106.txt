<p>Glorious day, St. Patrick day.  Half holiday.  First pay in new pay books.  General Birdwood here.  Bapaume has fallen 4-30 a.m.</p>
<p>18-3-1917, Sunday<br/>Weather improving.  Football match.  1st Bty. v 2nd Bty.  Men granted half holiday.  Sgt. McNamara gone to D.A. to get his Military Medal.</p>
<p>19-3-1917<br/>Raining.  Good progress made on our front.  32 Kilometres in all.  The town of Bapaume dates back 864 A.D.  It was set on fire by the Germans.</p>
<p>20-3-1917<br/>Snowing, sleet and rain, very disagreeable day.  Struck a snag in the remounts, had to rope him.</p>
<p>21-3-1917<br/>Colder than usual.  A move rumoured on the 26 inst.</p>
<p>22-3-1917<br/>Supposed to be going North, nothing doing.</p>
