<p>8.<br/>with a sheaf of papers in his hand and a look of gentle purpose on his face.<br/>Rest seems to be the need of the majority of the men here, especially of the Australians who have had a long spell in a bone rattling train and have seen all they care to see for the day. As a rule they potter about and dig up whatever interest a place may be capable of producing but now hardly a man of even the wakeful amongst them is interested in anything. Scarcely a man of them lifts his head to gaze at the spectacle of two German officers being brought along by an armed escort from a more forward part of the dock, or at the batch of German Red Cross prisoners following in their wake.<br/>There is one Australian, however, who looks on and who in spite of all that he has previously seen of the German soldier mentally notes new impressions.
