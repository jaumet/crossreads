<p>damage.  At 9 o'clock on a beautiful morning a train took us round the pretty Bay of Naples to Pompei.  The country passed through was being worked by women, children &amp; old men &ndash; all of a marked southern type.  Plenty of small shipbuilding was going on round the bay.  Making up a party of 4 with a Canadian officer and American Red Cross Commissioner we had a good look over the old city of Pompei and after dinner left to climb Vesuvius.  After a journey of two hours in stages, first in a light carriage, then on a mountain pony and finally a short stiff climb on foot, we were looking down on a sulphurous mass into the crater of the famous mountain.
