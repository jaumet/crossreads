<p>1st South African Gen. Hosp.                                                                                                 France.                                                                                                                                  September 24th 1916.<br/>My dearest Mother,<br/>My last letter was not very cheerful I'm afraid but the weather &amp; conditions were unusual for us &ndash; after the heat of Egypt. The last few days have been perfect &amp; the country is very beautiful when seen in the sun-shine, - We are about one mile from a little country village. The district is under cultivation &amp; the crops are ripe &amp; gathered in some fields &amp; quite green in the next. The fields or paddocks are separated by a hedge &ndash; no fences, even along the roadside, not even a hedge in some places.<br/>Had a great walk yesterday for about one hour, gathered Bluebells, Poppies, Cornflowers    &amp;  Daisies and the grass is so green, makes one very</p>
