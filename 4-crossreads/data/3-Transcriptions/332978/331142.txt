<p>63 Sept 19th<br/>intention of doing so until the Germans submit theirs, so both nations are at loggerheads once again &ndash;</p>
<p>Septr 20th<br/>Had shower (cold) Wrote letter to Uncle Arthur Mother &amp; a PC to Capt Davis, enclosing Photos &ndash; Played Bridge &ndash; Discussion tonight, as to what sort of an army shall we have after the war, opened by Lt Col Tollemache.</p>
<p>Septr 21st<br/>Saw "life" today for first time since being at Freiburg &ndash; The adjutant has been untiring in his efforts to secure we invalid officers bath chairs so as to enable us to go out on walks &ndash;</p>
<p>He has at last been successful &amp; my first outing this mng was simply magnificent &ndash; I had numerous offers from officers to push me. The scenery is really splendid, best I have seen, we have nothing to equal it in Australia &ndash;</p>
<p>Needless to say, we were looked upon as novelties by the populas &ndash; we left camp at 8 am &ndash; had a bath in the public baths &amp; then proceeded on our walk returning to camp at noon &ndash;</p>
<p>Was weighed today &ndash; weighing 13.7 stone &ndash; no parcels yesterday or today &ndash;</p>
<p>September 22nd<br/>Played my first game billiards with Leckler today scoring 4.</p>
