It was a job to throw it out from the bottom, you couldn't hoist it off the shovel, it was too sticky, &amp; if you put it near the edge at your head, some of the muck fell in on you. The engineers brought the cable, 3 ropes of it, laid it along the bottom, &amp; we shovelled 6 feet of earth in again. The poor old infantry do all the dirty work &amp; fighting too. Got back about 12, when I was sad indeed to hear Freedman was killed by a shell last night, &amp; 5 others wounded. I went across to A Coy's dugouts, &amp; it was only too true, his body was on a stretcher, &amp; 4 of them carried it off, the chaplain with them. No language could describe the horror of this cursed war, &amp; if God would only end this awful slaughter of good men! A man hasn't a chance, here was Freedman back here at night, &amp; suddenly struck down like a leaf. Only a few days ago, I was talking to him, &amp; he looked a picture of health &amp; vigour, he said he had a whiff of gas, &amp; was hoping he might get worse &amp; get a Blighty out of it.
