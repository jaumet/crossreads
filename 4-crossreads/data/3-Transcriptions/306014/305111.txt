<p>&amp; "Abide with me". We then had the procession to the cemetery in slow-time &amp; after the burial service fired our three volleys of blank-cartridge as a salute, &amp; presented-arms whilst the buglers sounded the "Last Post".</p>
<p>We then left the cemetery, formed up on the road &amp; marches back to camp in great style to the tunes "Keep your eye on Germany", Australia will be there" etc &amp; arrived back in time for 7 o'clock tea &amp; a hot bath at 8 and supper with "bluey" Cavanagh at 9.</p>
<p>Such a parade as this is of the pure ceremonial type &amp; more than almost anything else inspires one with that military &amp; regimental pride, commonly termed "spirit de corps". This sentiment was particularly evident as we marched home post the other camps and "swanked it" for all we knew.</p>
