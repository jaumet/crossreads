<p>built a blockade about 100 yards in length with the snow &amp; also got ready piles of ammunition, in the way of snow-balls, squeezed as hard as possible. We then lay down behind the parapet &amp; waited the attack &amp; when the enemy came near we let fly &amp; for a few minutes there was something doing. Almost everyone of us, officers included, were soaked through with thawed snow &amp; it was a cold-footed column that wended its way back to camp.</p>
<p>Needless to say the boys got plenty of amusement out of the snow. They had snow-fights among themselves &amp; at times it was not safe to put one's head outside the hut door for fear of "stopping one". They also made huge snowballs.
