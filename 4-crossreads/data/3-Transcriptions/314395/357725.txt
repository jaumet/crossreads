<p>6.  Any delay on the journey should be vouched for by the following who will endorse the pass:-</p>
<p>From Unit to Home.<br/>(a)  Delay between Unit and Embarkation by M.L.O. or O.C. Rest Camps.<br/>(b)  Delay on Sea Voyage by Embarkation Officer at Port of Arrival.</p>
<p>From Home to Unit.<br/>(a)  Delay on railways in Great Britain by R.T.O. at the station at which that part of the journey is completed where the delay occurred.<br/>(b)  Delay on Sea Voyage by M.L.O. or O.C. Rest Camps at Port of Disembarkation.<br/>(c)  Delay at Rest Camps by O.C. Rest Camp who will stamp the Return Tickets to this effect.<br/>(d)  Delay during journey from Port of Disembarkation to Unit by D.A.D.R.T. [Deputy Assistant Director of Railway Transport] or R.T.O.'s.</p>
<p>No one else is authorised to grant extensions of leave on account of delay.</p>
<p>7.  Officers and men are reminded that it is their duty to give no information of a military nature to anyone whilst they are on leave in England.  Information which is widely known in England soon reaches the enemy, and the success of Military operations may be endangered thereby.  Non-observance of the above will lead to cancellation of any present leave granted to the offender, and the stoppage of all future leave.</p>
<p>8.  A copy of these rules will be given to each man with his leave form.</p>
