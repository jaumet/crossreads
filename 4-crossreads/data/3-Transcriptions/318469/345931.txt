<p>24th<br/>Everything going well.  Usual exchange Artly rifle fire.</p>
<p>25th<br/>Usual work in tunnels &amp; Fire trench &amp; same old exchange of lead.</p>
<p>26th<br/>Work in new firing line going well as is everything with us.  Nothing fresh &ndash;  usual exchange shells &amp; bullets.</p>
<p>27th<br/>Nothing out of the ordinary today.  Attended Church Service about 7.30 p.m.  A goodly crowd gathers to these Services on the hillside &amp; all appear to  look forward to having them.  As at last service that I attend here there was a beautiful sunset &amp; instead of the loud gun that was firing on that occasion there was a Destroyer firing in the same position work still going on in new trenches.</p>
<p>28th<br/>There was tremendous bombardment of Enemys position by our boats &amp; land Guns near Cape Helles today.  Along the hillside the smoke from bursting Naval shells resembled a bushfire.  In the afternoon we made an attack on some Turk trenches in front of our Right Flank &amp; some of the Light horse with part 11th Battln advanced about 800 yds &amp; found a great many dead in some of the trenches that the Turks had abandoned about 8.30 p.m. we opened fire along the line again &amp; under cover of this fire our boys abandoned the trenches captured earlier in the afternoon &amp; returned again to our lines.  Our casualties were about 250.  During the night some troops were landed down the coast also British French &amp; Indian advanced about 1,000 yds near Krithia &amp; firmly hold position gained.</p>
