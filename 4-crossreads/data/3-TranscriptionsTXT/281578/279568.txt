The Yanks, new at the game & under the guidance of Australians in their first experience of war, never said a word in protest. We began to wonder if our guide would ever find his company, & as the line is not connected up, but only isolated posts, there was a danger of going into the Hun lines in the dark. Finally he struck A Coy., & they showed him where B Coy. were. We dropped the tucker, came back & had our own stew, then set off for the ration dump to bring the rations (bread, jam, cheese) & bacon for breakfast. We got to B Coy. without any trouble this time, & then had to take the empty thermos back. Fritz sent gas over, as we came back, making the air white, but it was hard enough to see as it was, & we walked through it without putting the gas masks on; it made us sneeze & our eyes water, but wasn't strong, just tear gas. Had another weary tramp back to the ration dump with the empty thermos & back to our lines, & thank goodness, the night's labors are over.
