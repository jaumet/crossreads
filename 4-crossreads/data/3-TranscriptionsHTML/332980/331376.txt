<p>of my sojourn in Glasgow, after a stroll &amp; making a few purchases of Soveniers to send out to Ausie I went back for Dinner &amp; at 2.20 joined a Party of Boys for a trip to Cathcart. After a few minutes Tram ride we arrived at the Munition Works of C &amp; J Weir &amp; after a few minutes wait along came 3 guides &amp; we formed up into 3 Parties &amp; started on a Tour of Inspection, one large place was full of Girls working on Aeroplane Engines others were working the Lathes, others with the Hammer &amp; Chisel chipping &amp; so on, they appeared to be a happy lot &amp; well dressed, wearing Blue Overalls over their clothes, needless to say the Boys received the Glad Eye everywhere &amp; some of the boys was that interested in the Fair Maid's that they dawdled along &amp; the Guide got to far ahead of them &amp; they wandered about on their own chatting here &amp; there with the Fair Scotch Lasses, I was informed that there are about 5000 hands, a large percentage being Girls, I saw the Shells in their rough state &amp; through different Machines to the Finished Article, after wandering about for 1 hour we took our Departure &amp; as it was 4.30 Pm.
