  
 Saturday 23 June 1917. 
 Usual routine & extra work this morning with extra work again during afternoon out on the desert in the scorching sun. 2 more aeronauts killed (accidents to aeroplanes.) Aeroplanes still continue to fly above us all the day & some practice at shooting from them. More wounded  arrive  pass through here from Gaza. (Minor cases.) 
 A hospital train goes to Suez, and a train load of Camels and another of supplies seen passing our camp. Cases of mumps & measles break out. 
 We are beginning to see that our soldiering in Vic.  is  was play to what we get here. 
 I am out of tobacco & cigarettes. 
 Working late till 6.30 pm.
