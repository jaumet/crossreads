<p>DATE &ndash; TIME &ndash; PLACE &ndash; REMARKS</p>
<p>31 &ndash; Lemnos &ndash; Big Australian Mail.</p>
<p>April 7 &ndash; Lemnos</p>
<p>10 &ndash; Lemnos &ndash; 3rd 10 reinforcements arrived.</p>
<p>14 &ndash; Lemnos &ndash; About this time came to know of landing on Gallipoli.  Battalion issued with colours purple and blue.</p>
<p>24 &ndash; Lemnos &ndash; B. and C. Companies to H.M.A.S. prince of Wales, A &amp; D Coy to torpedo destroyer.</p>
<p>25 &ndash; About 3 A.M. &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; (Sunday) landed on Gallipoli (3rd Brigade) 29 officers 921 other ranks.</p>
<p>28 &ndash; Night &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; Battalion relieved by Marine Division.  Men thoroughly <strike>exauhsted</strike> exhausted.</p>
<p>29 &ndash; A.M. &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; Bivouacked in shell Green &amp; Roll call 13 officers <strike>580</strike> 380 other ranks present.  Casualties up to 30/4/15, 13 officers 453 other ranks.</p>
<p>May 1 &ndash; A.M. &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; Relieved 9th Batt. on extreme right flank.</p>
<p>7 &ndash; A.M. &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; 100 reinforcements and Lt. Cornish and Cullen.</p>
<p>19 &ndash; A.M. &ndash; Anzac Cove &ndash; Water getting scarce, Heavy Turk attack.</p>
