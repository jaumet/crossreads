<p>August 1916<br/>from the noise of battle for nearly everybody was  <strike>worn</strike> nerve strung on account of  concussion. On the morning of August the 9th we had to pack our gear &amp;  started to wend our way back to the firing line. Our first march was as far as La. Vicogne which was a good distance &amp; where we rested till next morning &amp;  then marched to the village of Herissart &amp;  remained there training for a couple of days. It was the morning of the 14th that we made our next move &amp; then went as far as Vadencourt where we went into huts situated in the centre of a big forest but our stay was sort for next day we marched to the hills at the rear of Albert were we bivouaced for a few hours &amp; prepared for the firing line. It was at dusk of the same day when we moved off as platoons at long intervals &amp;  marched to a reserve trench behind the lines of our big guns &amp; we remained there  till 1am next morning. The rain began to fall &amp;  when we were awakened at 1am it was fairly heavy &amp;  then our orders were to move. "A" Coy was the last to go &amp;  we marched through mud over the tops of our boots to the support trench on the far side of Poziers &amp;  we took up our position about 5am &amp;  the other companies took over the firing line.
