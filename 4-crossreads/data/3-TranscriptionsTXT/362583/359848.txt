 slowly improving &amp; we are settling down very comfortably. With the exception of attending a church parade we have done no hard work yet. The food is good all round that is both for officers &amp; men. I'm afraid this will have to be the end, not only because of want of news, but because all letters have to be handed in  to  within one &amp; a half minutes. I'll write again at the next port of call. 
 Your loving son Arthur. 
 If you don't get this letter you'll know it has been censored. This is not my own.
