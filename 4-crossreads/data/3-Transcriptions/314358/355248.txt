<p>4<br/>And you are reading the closed leaves which are kept in the secret covers of sorrow.</p>
<p>Here we are at last away from the sound of guns.</p>
<p>The peace is so profound that the younger men are, even now, almost wearly of it.</p>
<p>Yes but when you remind them of what still prevails back yonder they remember and are content.</p>
<p>Aye and would be in a desert for the matter of that; for life is ever sweet.</p>
<p>Here we dont even have to blind our sights</p>
<p>The sky is no longer a dome of terror.</p>
<p>The town is old fashioned and quaint, with cobble stone streets and grand place or square.</p>
<p>Today was market day.</p>
<p>The simple peasant people brought in their pink pigs, burnt brown cows, fruit, vegetables, rabbits, fowls, calves eggs, and butter etc. Also fish</p>
<p>All these things and the life of them were crowded in the square; and there were many temporary stalls erected at which cloths, stationery, sweets, pork, cottons and laces, and nic nacks were sold.</p>
