<p>afternoon tea &amp; a stroll round looking into the Belgian hospital return to town. Accompany Belge to Sotteville station on some work &amp; have tea at Mrs Davis' as usual. Spend half an hour with a friend in Rue Lessard &amp; straight home, soon to bed   Paid to day:- 7 francs 50 cents to send parcels, 1 f 50 for aft. tea &amp; 3 fr. for sundries. (12 fr.).</p>
<p>24th September:- Sunday<br/>Heavy fog. Up early to <strike>Bonsecours</strike> St Sever Church at 8 am. Fog lifts &amp; a beautiful mildly warm day. Not much work, as few casualties are coming in. More success on the British front at Flery. Serbs still pressing on &amp; Monastir will soon fall. Further developments in regard to my transfer. Capt. Mackay, who <u>is</u> a <u>gentleman</u>, talks to me for a full half hour on the subject, &amp; tells me to wait until he talks to <u>Staff Capt</u> Parker. At 11-45 am</p>
