He only rejoined this battalion today. We marched a good way, &amp; our feet were fairly sore when we reached the deserted village of Daours. There are some nice buildings in the town, &amp; it is a great pity these poor French people have to leave their homes. The town is full of   Australian   Australian soldiers. Our company are quartered in a big building which looks as if it was once workshops, we are up on the top storey. We took off our boots &amp; socks for foot inspections, &amp; as the river Somme was right alongside I washed my feet there. It was a perfect treat, as for a month now we haven't had a change of clothes or an opportunity of a bath. I hope they 'll let us go in &amp; wash our bodies tomorrow. It is a narrow river, but runs swiftly, &amp; the water is nice &amp; clear. After tea, I went up the village looking for B Coy. &amp; Boo, found him in a barn, he is a terrible old stick-in-the-mud.
