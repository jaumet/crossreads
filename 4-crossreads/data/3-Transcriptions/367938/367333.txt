<p>The Lock Esk anchored 200 yds from shore &amp; I stayed there till 11.15 pm without any luck &ndash; bailed lots of pinnaces but they wld. not take off anyone &ndash; till at that hour one condescended. So it took a continual fight &amp; ten 10 boats to get ashore. The bullets were all the time hitting the Lock Esk while at anchor &ndash; these trawlers live in this state. Full of experiences I returned to camp about 1.a.m.</p>
<p>Otherwise there's nothing to say. We move soon &amp; its just as well as this life had fed us all up. The NSW papers are funny with their accts of our "Still [indecipherable] to advance slightly" etc 20 ft we've done in 2 months. When we do move there'll be lots of casualties you know but I daresay you'll know before this reaches you.</p>
<p>I got yr prized parcel of tobacco &ndash; handerkerchief &ndash; socks soap &amp; paper &ndash; very useful articles indeed &ndash; The soap however will keep. A crowd gathered to have a look at it &amp; smell it! &ndash; &ndash; &ndash; &ndash; &ndash;</p>
<p>Can't write more as have dysentry &amp; a cold &amp; indigestion still &ndash; though [indecipherable] if I do talk to the flies &ndash; Man hit by schrapnel in the water bathing near me yesterday &ndash; put his head out of the water &amp; said "the beggars have &ndash; &ndash; torpedoed me"</p>
<p>Gwilliam hit on the shoulder &amp; gone &ndash; Clark is O.K &amp; all the officers &ndash; good luck.</p>
<p>V.</p>
