<p>very little damage done by shells or bombs, but no shops open yet.  That is because the civilians were on rations still, which is not remarkable remembering that the Germans have only been gone a fortnight.  It can easily be seen that things are rapidly returning to normal.</p>
<p>We arrived in the town by a by road, but just [indecipherable] and  the barn which was allotted to B Section as a billet I found a main road.  Where it leads I do not know except that it is probably north &amp; south &amp; therefore perhaps between Mons &amp; south.  Along this road I found a little cottage where madam was good enough to give me a room, although she already had refugees billeted on her.</p>
