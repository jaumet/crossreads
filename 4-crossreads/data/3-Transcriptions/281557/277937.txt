<p>villages &amp; bivouaked in a lovely shady orchard about 4 pm: it was a solid march (about 14 miles): plenty of planes about as an aerodrome is near by: got a couple of books from J.B.J: read accounts of our "stunts" they read well on paper &amp; give little indication of what a Hell it is to go through: a heavy bombardment was on as we left Warloy:</p>
<p>Wednesday Aug 9th <br/>shady orchard: good day: marched off about 10 am &amp; rested for dinner in a nice shady woods: passed the first Bgde &amp; seen A Amecy in the 2nd Batt: passed through a large town &amp; across the railway: the view from the top of the hills was glorious: arrived at Halloy about 3 pm: We made a camp out in the grass &amp; the sounds of the trains not far off sounded nice: this is a louzy place &amp; we can't buy anything at all: had a good wash &amp; then sleep</p>
<p>Thursday Aug 10th <br/>Halloy : woke by rain (drizzly) at 6 am: went at 10 am for a "wash" &amp; also got a change of underclothes there &amp; a pair of "slacks": got a letter from Harry Simpson, two from J Garbutt &amp; a lot of nice P.Cs &amp; a letter from J.B.J. Wrote in afternoon:.</p>
<p>Friday Aug 11th <br/>Halloy: heavy mistin morning: physical jerks 6.30 &ndash; 7.30 am: parade at AMC 10 &ndash; 12.30 am for lecture</p>
