Smith was sent to the Transports this morning to fill Darkey Tregenza's place. Today I posted to my wife a large piece of German shell that fell near my dug out when I was in Blangy Tronville. 1/3 1/2d. To day W. M. Hughes Prime Minister of Australia & Joe Cook visited this area, and inspected our Brigade, part of the 37. Battalion forming a guard of honour. They were accompanied by Sir John Monash. 
 Wednesday July 3. 1918. 1429. 
 A beautiful day but much cooler. More men were sent from the Battalion to assist in the preparations for the competition on Friday. At 2 o'clock we were all lined up and accompanied by Mr Boyland and Mr Chapman were marched to brigade Headquarters where our respirators were inspected and we were all put through a gas room with the respirators on.
