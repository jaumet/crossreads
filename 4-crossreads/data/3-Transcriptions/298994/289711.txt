<p>We reached Ypres an hour after. We disentrained here and made our way among the ruined buildings of Ypres again. We stayed here till nightfall.</p>
<p>Two or three of (B Coy) 50th Battalion had a narrow shave, a bomb buried in the ground, near which a fire was burning set the thing flying into fragments These chaps got a piece as a souvenir of the Great War (1914-1918)!</p>
<p>We left Ypres for the line again and after chequered experience with the mud etc, we did get there eventually. The distance must have been 8 miles. Luckily duckboards helped us a bit also Fritz's shells in the way of light!</p>
<p>We spent a hell of a night amidst the mud of our new homes, the trenches, What with shells of every calibre thrust upon us, gas and the discomfort of the eternal "chats", oh it was a life! Still here I am today, well out of it!</p>
