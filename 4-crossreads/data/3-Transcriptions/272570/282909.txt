<p>the attack came up tonight; we had a good night's sleep. I wrote up this diary, &amp; read the "Daily Mail" till 11 p.m., then we nt to sleep. Had to blow out the candle I was reading by at different times, when Hun's 'planes could be heard droning overhead.<br/>Monday 12 Aug.<br/>Had a nice easy day resting, &amp; we are all feeling much better. We are paid today, &amp; the canteen did a good biz. At night Hun 'planes came over &amp; dropped bombs around. I wrote a long letter home today.<br/>Tuesday 13 Aug.<br/>Was orderly corporal today. We went down for a hot bath &amp; change of clothes this afternoon, &amp; then got the rotten news we are to do another stunt in a day or two on the Harbonnieres sector. We have to capture a wood. By Jove, they are making it hot, we'll have hardly any men left soon. Oh well, I shall hope &amp; pray for the best, as usual. One can only trust in Providence. Well these heads ought to be goaled the way they treat men. It's enough to put a man off fighting for his country. We fell in at 5.30 p.m., thinking we'd have a nice easy march this evening, &amp; a good rest before going in the line tomorrow night for the stunt. We walked along a road thick with dust &amp; covered with military traffic. On we went through a village where three dead horses were lying on the road. Lor' talk about hum, they nearly turned us up.
