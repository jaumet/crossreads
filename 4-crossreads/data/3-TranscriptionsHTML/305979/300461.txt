<p>by the  aid of the wind scattered itself, &amp; us all over the deck . There was a hurried awakening, a grabbing  for blankets, shirts etc in the dark &amp; an ignominous descent into our more sheltered but less comfortable quarters in the fore-hold; and on the floor of that latter place, in a spare corner, yours truly dossed till 5.30 as though nothing had happened.</p>
<p>Last night at 7.30 we lowered a second chap into a watery grave. He had been coughing the night before &amp; broke a blood-vessel and died within about 16 hours. Once more we were assembled in our quarters and those who wished were marched right aft to witness the short ceremony; and "his day's work was done".</p>
