<p>April 17</p>
<p>Reveille blew at 4 this morning and at 4.15 "the troops" breakfasted and a little later received their lunch, which consisted of a (1) sandwich of bread &amp; margarine &amp; bloater-paste and two 'dog' biscuits. At 6.30 every Battn moved out of camp, and I can assure you there was 'some khaki' on the road within half-an-hour or so. The tents which we had put up were occupied by visiting troops &amp; these all joined us. 'Rollestone' alone must have contributed four or five thousand Australians to the column. That means that our line was just about a mile</p>
