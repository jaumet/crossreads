  
 Y.M.C.A. gave each man a little wallet and tonight The Salvation army came around to each ward and sang a little Christmas carol led by an Australian who represents the S.A. here. 
 Thursday Dec. 26. 1918. 46. Boxing Day. A fine day but cloudy. After dinner I got up and sat at the fire in my overcoat reading for about 2 hours. President Wilson comes from Paris today & will be the guest of the King at Buckingham Palace. 
 Friday Dec. 27. 1918. 47. A fine day. 
 I was up for a little while this afternoon. The papers today are full of the visit of P. Wilson to London a million people welcomed him & he got the greatest reception ever given to any visitor to London. One of the patients in this ward had a fit and there were several doctors attending on him for a long while. 
 Saturday Dec. 28. 1918. 48. A dull cloudy day. The M.O. Captain Black was back again this morning and resumed his duties in this ward No. 44. He examined me this morning.
