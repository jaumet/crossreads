<p>Sunday 15 December 1918<br/>Got the first breath of Australia in the air.  Donned our light clothes.  Tropic sea and the boat still rolls!!</p>
<p>Monday 16 December 1918<br/>Weather remains bon but I have seen much smoother tropic seas.  Played Bridge in eveng.  Getting quite keen on deck tennis.</p>
<p>Tuesday 17 December 1918<br/>Arrd. Honolulu at dawn.  Went ashore with the Sturdees &amp; Nevatina's.  Lovely place.  Windy.  The fish were wonderful.  Sailed again at 5 p.m. with regrets.</p>
<p>Wednesday 18 December 1918<br/>Rup was not too good.  R'matism I believe.  Played Tennis a lot.  Feelg. in good form.  Major Morgan called on Rup.</p>
<p>Thursday 19 December 1918<br/>Three years ago today we evacuated Gallipoli.  Rup in bed &amp; pretty crook.  Weather much warmer.</p>
<p>Friday 20 December 1918<br/>Tennis Tournaments in full swing &amp; other games.  Sports in afternoon.  Major Morgan called in anor. Dr. for Rup.</p>
<p>Saturday 21 December 1918<br/>Richardson licked me in the Tennis final.  Very Hot &ndash; passed Equator.  Best crossing so far as a breeze was about.<br/>Crossed the Equator once more.</p>
