The bearers told off for the work were doing  that  the last, so I started on dressings. There were plenty of A Section to do that however, so I transferred my help in the direction of those outside, helping to put blankets under cases, giving drinks &amp; seeing that no serious cases were overlooked. 
 After I had been going for some time, finding plenty to do, &amp; only too glad to be taking so active a part, Mr Garland the W.O. told me I had better get some more sleep, because there was likely to be a rush through the night; so I accordingly went back to the dug out &amp; slept for an hour or so.
