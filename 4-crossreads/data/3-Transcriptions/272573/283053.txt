<p>Feb 9th. Wed.<br/>Sand storm to-day again.</p>
<p>Feb 10th. Thurs.<br/>Weather very good to-day.</p>
<p>Building an incinerator. Some of the "big nuts with plenty of Red Tape" about them inspected our camp to-day.</p>
<p>Compulsory swimming parade to-day.</p>
<p>Feb 11th. Frid.<br/>Heard the sound of heavy guns firing to-day. In all probability they are the warships having gun practice down on the lake at Ismaelia.</p>
<p>The rumour is that there is a scrap on with the Turks as D.W. the bugler got orders not to blow his bugler to-night.</p>
<p>An aeroplane was seen over head to-day.</p>
<p>The people on board the ships that passed down the canal throw tobacco and cigarettes to the men on the banks.</p>
<p>Feb 12th. Sat.<br/>Football match, Australian Rules Officers, Non Com O, against Privates. Privates won 25-16.</p>
<p>Read "Scottish Regiments at the Front". Short accounts of the origins of the various Scottish Regiments.</p>
<p>Feb 13th. Sun.<br/>Church Parade at Headquarters. You never saw such a conglomeration of uniform for a unit. Everyone seemed to be dressed differently, this was, as we were where we came</p>
