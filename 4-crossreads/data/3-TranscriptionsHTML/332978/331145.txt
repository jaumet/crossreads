<p>66 Sept 25th<br/>this evening &ndash; Played Bridge &ndash;</p>
<p>Sept 26th<br/>out for walk again this mng &ndash; The back wheel of my chair broke, but it was soon repaired by several volunteers The new censor had charge of the walk &ndash; we cripples rested half way &amp; played Bridge in the open &ndash; It was quite a change &ndash; The population here do not seem to look upon we prisoners with much scorn &ndash; some even smile whist others bid us good mng &ndash; despite the fact that nearly every German male &amp; female, wears a very worried &amp; stern expression &ndash;</p>
<p>Recd letters no 71 from Mother dated 17th Apl Ruth Horsfield, Father, Victor, Miss Tonks, Doris &amp; Mrs Melen &ndash; all these had been sent on from Bn &amp; after a lot of careful censorization I received them some in a very dilapidated state &ndash; I was very pleased indeed to receive same, there must be several more for me &ndash;</p>
<p>Played cards &ndash;</p>
<p>Sept 27th<br/>Capt Robinson sent to another Camp &ndash;</p>
<p>Read in the garden most of the day enjoying the glorious sun &ndash;</p>
<p>Germany reply to the popes Paper note in todays paper. There is nothing startling in the reply excepting that one can see, reading between the lines, that she is anxious for Peace &ndash;</p>
