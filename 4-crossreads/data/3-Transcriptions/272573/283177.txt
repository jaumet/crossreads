<p>In this wood there were numerous trenches which must have been made early in the campaign as they were all overgrown with foliage. In the distance we could see Messines ridge.</p>
<p>The village of Neuve Eglise is terribly smashed. There is scarcely a whole house in the place.</p>
<p>Were fortunate in getting a lift in Motor Transport both ways.</p>
<p>Fritz had apparently been trying to get on to Bailleul as just outside the town there were marks of recently dropt shells.</p>
<p>July 2nd. Mon.<br/>A bomb was dropped in the "Rue Musee" just by the side of the C.C.S.</p>
<p>I thought our building was going to collapse. The vibration was something terrific</p>
<p>The pit, pit, pit, of the machine guns could be heard overhead. Our aeroplanes were chasing the bomber in the semi darkness  He was flying very low.</p>
<p>An English Officer, a chap about twenty years of age, was brought into the Operating Theatre with his arm blown off from the socket as a result of the bomb which was dropped.</p>
