 Photoes of the "Shropshire" as she was about to leave the docks at Avenmouth are now on sale at 1/- each. 
 Wednesday April 9.1919.   (15) [Shorthand transcribed as follows]. 9th on the voyage.  [end of shorthand] The weather continues perfect. The sea is fairly smooth & the wind is moderate. We are very close to the line and many are sleeping on deck. 
 There was a parade this morning at 9 a.m. at the mess tables & the roll called as it was feared some men were missing. It has been very hot today. It is said that we crossed the line at 4.30 today. We are steaming much quicker than was  expected.   At the present rate of speed we will reach Cape Town on next Monday week. 21/4/19 
 There was a sports meeting on the upper deck this afternoon. The programme consisted in various sporting events. But the space was so confined & very few could see them. Contributions for the ships paper will close on the 15th inst. I am sending in several items.
