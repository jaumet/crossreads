I am afraid that I was no exception, &amp;  when the colonel informed the assembled officers that reveille, on the following morning, would be blown at 4.30, owing to a strange desire of the town medical authorities to have the ship thoroughly fumigated, I almost frothed at the mouth.</p>
<p>Reveille at 4.30! We crawled out of bed &amp;   prepared for a tiring day. By 7.30 all troops were off the ship &amp;   an hour later were in sight of the Capetown recreation grounds. A strong picket was told off to enclose an area of somewhere about 50 acres, &amp;   the men were then dismissed within these boundaries. They had brought out a few footballs with them, &amp;    these were soon flying round the grounds &amp;   games started all over the place. All day we spent there with nothing to do except eat our midday meal to which everyone did ample justice.</p>
<p>On our arrival back at the wharf at 4.30, the fumes were still too thick to allow of the troops returning to their decks. For two &amp;   a half dismal hours we sat twiddling our thumbs watching</p>
