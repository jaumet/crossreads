<p>War Diary<br/>1915  23rd Nov.<br/>they are only comparatively lightly held &ndash; Shelled 2 M.G. Positions but results poor &ndash; selected position Table Top for an 18 Pr. which should easily be able to knock Snipers Nest out &ndash;<br/>CRA Gen. Johnston called and we conferred re-Artillery co-operation &ndash; Phone connections rotten &ndash; same all through &ndash; No wire, bad, obsolete Phones and commutators &ndash; cannot get Mark III. &ndash; and we are using 'phones condemned in Australia before the war &ndash; we cannot get others &ndash; This means disaster in an attack, but no one appears to bother &ndash; Requisions have been in and renewed for 9 months -  New Regulations out, by which Officers and N.C.Os. absent 3 months &ndash; wounded or sick, may be placed on supernumerary list and their places permanently filled -  On return, liable to be posted to any unit of [or] arm of service to which he belongs &ndash; a good and much needed regulation &ndash;</p>
<p>[In right margin]  A.O. 40. 41  22nd Nov/15</p>
<p>24th Nov.<br/>Instructions issued for 48 hours general silence &ndash; from 8 p.m. &ndash; No fire rifle or artillery to try and induce enemy to attack.</p>
<p>25th Nov.<br/> Nothing doing &ndash; Enemy for first time shelled Sazli Dere getting mule convoys carrying water and rations &ndash; Several casualties &ndash; These are visible from Chunuk Bair and Snipers Nest.  General Russell called re Divisl. Canteen &ndash; This Brigade concurred &ndash; scheme is to get a good firm to establish canteen (dry) at Anzac &ndash; we to guarantee half losses by sea &ndash; Bond of &pound;500 by Contractor &ndash; In the event of sudden move we to take over stock to value of &pound;1000 landed:  Stores to be carried free of cost to Contractor &ndash;</p>
<p>26th Nov.<br/>I and the four other Brigadiers (Russell, Monash, Meredith and Braithwaite) N.Z.M.R., 4th Infy. Bde., 1st L.H. Bde., N.Z.M.I. Bde. sent for &ndash; Gen. Godley informed that he had been appointed to command Army</p>
