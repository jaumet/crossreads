 Broad Road. I called in and made myself known, and was very warmly welcomed by all hands, notably Mr Samuck Rowe, who undertook the job of directing me round London. I was shown in to Mr George Briscoe who congratulated me on coming so far to fight when I was so young. He then asked me did I wish to see the cashier to obtain an advance, but I declined, as I could see they intended paying my expenses, and I didn't care to abuse a good thing.
