<p>26th Infantry<br/>Hawkesbury River<br/>16th August 1914</p>
<p>My dear mother,</p>
<p>I was very sorry to hear yesterday, that you haven't been too well, &amp; hope you are not worrying about Arthur &amp; I. Two letters came up yesterday from father, one addressed to Middle Head and the other to Hawkesbury River. He tells me that you are staying up the mountains &amp; I think that a change and a rest will do you the world of good.</p>
<p>On the day I came away, <strike>I</strike> father sent me out a wrist watch; it was a little beauty &amp; kept perfect time, but after a week it started carrying me into the middle of next week, the pace it managed to get up was tremendous, &amp; it used to go about ten minutes fast every half hour. I sent it into Flavelles to get regulated the day before I left Middle Head, &amp; I suppose it has been sent back to the old camp.
