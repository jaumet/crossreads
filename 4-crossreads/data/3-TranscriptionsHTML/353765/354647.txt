<p>April 1917<br/>7/13th &ndash; Report says two killed. Tried to drop them on hangars. No material damage done. Sent to Koday for 1A K Jr camera Shirras to bring it with him Very long winded in returning A few Jackos brought in, including a cavalry man fully equipped. Very poor turn out. Lecture to all of Bde N.C.O.s at B.H.Q. on Gas etc. Sgt also lecturing 3 p.m. Home mail in. Letter from Aunt Amy. 2nd I.M.D. joining up, Dave's old Regt also in vicinity.<br/>14th &ndash; Jacko started dropping howitzer stuff on railhead, presumably 6" do not think any larger. Tried to get Rly dump &amp; failed. Managed to put five in Ambs. &amp; C.C. Stn killing 14 &amp; wounded 49. Apparently very deliberate. Shirras returned from Cairo.<br/>15th &ndash; Jacko at it again after guns this time. Do not know what casualties no guns<br/>16th &ndash; Prep to move out. Given horse several times &amp; finally horse given to another. About 7.30 pm Grant slyring Standring &amp; self in carts. Travelling off &amp; on all night &amp; at daylight a Taube spotted us &amp; made off apparently to get a load of bombs. We watered at Shelal about 6-7 am &amp; had halted when advice that Taube flying low &amp; in our direction had been sighted. Bde moved out but not quite so soon enough as our tail end &amp; Q Ms who were warned to remain &amp; A.S.C. &amp; B.H.Q. details were bombed (three bombs) Poor old Bob Thomson as game as ever was, got badly hit, half head blown off, thigh smashed &amp; doubled under him also abd. wounds. <strike>badly</strike> killed instantly. "Bobby" his horse had 4 legs blown off &amp; other injuries. Four others killed, Usher A.S.C. Thorpe &amp; Austin &amp; interpreter &amp; one Twist 2nd Sig Tr died at C.C.S. About 15 wounded &amp; 11 horses 8 of ours done for. Our Sand Carts had filed out but sledges &amp; one limber not clear</p>
