<p>Dec 6<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Still very cold, but bright &amp; no wind. frozen under foot all day - we made our trip for suppllies as usual - I wrote several letters in the evening. The Primus was&#39;nt going too well, I had no prickers -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 7<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Weather quite mild today &nbsp;we got home from supplies at 2.30 P.M. as usual &amp; worked on the lorry till 4.P.M. - Fred (came over with me on the &quot;Medic&quot;) from 5<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;D.S.C. came in &amp; spent the evening with me &amp; we partook of some delicasies that I had received that day in a parcel from my Aunt in Kingston -</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 8<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Very warm to-day &amp; a little wet &amp; muddy underfoot. Yank got back from leave (8 days late) &amp; was put on 38316 lorry - We made our trip for supplies as usual.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 9<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;Sunday - It rained all last night &amp; all day to-day - We did our usual trip for suppllies</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Dec 10<sup><u>th</u></sup>&nbsp;&nbsp;The weather cleared &amp; remained fair but it was very raw cold - Joe &amp; Jim came home together well inked &amp; I had to put them both to bed &amp; give them&nbsp;something to eat - Usual trip on supplies</p>
