<p>the usher said he would get us a seat &amp; took us in the side door : we were taken aback when he led us right up to the choir seats &amp; put us in with the girls (me with my baldy head) but we enjoyed it after the shyness wore off : The service was nice though rather (high). The choir wore blue &amp; white gowns &amp; looked well. The majority of the congregation were soldiers. The altar was of marble with beautiful carvings : came home &amp; had a sing song in the tram : then bed.</p>
<p>Monday Feb 21st<br/>clear day : Revellie 6 am : on Cairo piquet. breakfast : fell in 7.30 am marched to Kasr-el-nil barracks : marched with full kit &amp; rifle : very solid : about 10 miles : told off for our different piquets : I missed "our mob" &amp; was put on B.2 piquet to fall in 8 am at our beat tomorrow morning : our room was on the third story &amp; about 40 of us got in : the barracks are very big buidings about 200 by 100 yards &amp; supposed to have been built by Napoleon &amp; they certainly looked it . Great big rooms &amp; dreary corridors : no lights in them : rather inconvenient going downstairs for everything : passed away the day &amp; then bed. Barracks on the bank of the Nile : three bridges within view</p>
<p>Tuesday Feb 22nd<br/>clear day : no revellie sounded here : slept on a sack filled with straw &amp; had a good sleep : breakfast : fell in &amp; marched to our beat, the native bazaars : A gold job : marched through the bazaars &amp; then had a sit down till time was up : our hours are from 8 am to 1 pm : but we fall in &frac14; hour earlier to allow time for walking : came home : dinner : got a pass &amp; went to look at Cairo main station : had a talk to a couple of guards : hydraulic buffers in use : tea : after tea : Bill Adshead  : Blue Nimmo &amp; myself went to the pyramids : got there just on dusk &amp;</p>
