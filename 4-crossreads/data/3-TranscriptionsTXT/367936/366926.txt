 Monday 31 January 1916 Long Bay for final time.  A most beautiful day.  Very heavy dew at nights, blankets always sopping wet if one sleeps outside.  Paid to end of Jan., sum of &pound;4.  Musketry has been very easy job.  Today only fired 5 shots &amp; slept rest of day.  Hard work tomorrow. 
 Tuesday 1 February 1916 Received issue of 2nd pair boots, kit bag, under clothing, etc.  Fatigue duty in morning cleaning up lines, rotten job. All hands enoculated for Thypoid, and sent to watch riding test to keep quiet.  Saw Cousing Jocelyn and Alan &amp; went to theatre with them.
