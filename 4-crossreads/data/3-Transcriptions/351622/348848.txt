<p>Another gas alarm last night.</p>
<p>23 June 1916 Fri<br/>Bricklaying occupied most of my work to-day. Very hot in fact. I got a sweat up. Received a letter from Aviator Whitfeld telling me he is coming over to France next month. Sports meeting &amp; issue of comforts 7.30. Poor old Ryan Bellemey brought in having been gassed at the</p>
<p>[Text continued in margin]<br/>gas school at Croix-du-bac</p>
<p>24 June 1916 Sat<br/>Work up to find it a very wet day. The first thing I did was to see Bell. He does not look to good although they say it is not very serious. Had the afternoon off. Keith goes into hospital. Guthrie also comes in. Retired to bed early. The boys come home merry as usual.</p>
