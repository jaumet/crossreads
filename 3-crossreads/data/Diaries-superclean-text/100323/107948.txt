31/7/19 Cape Town
and demanded leave or they would take possession of the ship boats. We called down the Captain and got his assurance that we leave tomorrow at 11 but the troops were not satisfied. Accordingly I reported their demands and intentions to Shore Authorities and asked for a launch to come and take off Smythe and a couple of men. During this conference angry faces surrounded the cabin windows and crowds were gathered on the decks to be subsequently harangued by their delegates. Up until a late hour no reply was forthcoming to our request for a launch and nearly everyone turned in for a sleep. The balance of the night was quiet and slept until 7 am.
