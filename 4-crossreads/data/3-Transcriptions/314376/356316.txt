<p>5.<br/>right to the end of my stay in Camp. I used frequently to pay but it required some moral courage in front of the other men.</p>
<p>I decided too I would always say my prayers on my knees and this continued throughout the campaign. Be it said of the men that they always respected my resolution also the fact of saying Grace before meal.</p>
<p>I became acquainted with a chap by the name of Harold Davis here a man from Hornsby &ndash; this friendship still continues.</p>
<p>My first tentmates alright with the exception of a wild sort of man who had done time in gaol. He would of continued abuse me and make my existence miserable.</p>
