Today I bought in the Y.M.C.A. a book entitled "Australias Part in the Great War" a pictoral record from official potographs price 4/- I am on canteen picquet tonight from 6 to 9.30 
 Thursday March 27. 1919   137 Cloudy & rather windy. Yesterday I paraded to the dentist to get a new tooth on my plate & today to the doctor to get a pair of light boots. I have finished making security handles for my two kit bags. I have only the locks to  buy. 
 I finished reading today a very interesting & instructive book by C.W.Salelby M.D. F.R.S. on "The progress of Eugenics.  Eugenics is the new science which deals with nature and nurture or the betterement of the human races. 
 [Shorthand transcribed as follows]  I sent a post card to Mrs. [indecipherable] Gibson telling her that I am leaving here by the Shropshire early in April. [end of shorthand] 
 Friday March 28. 1919.   138 Fine day. I had a warm bath early this morning. At the Q.M. store I changed my service boots for a pair of very light tan boots, on the doctors recommendation. Cpt. R.J. Vercoe
