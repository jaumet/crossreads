<p>leading to this Mosque are some painful sights of maimed, blind &amp; poor begging for alms. Cairo is a terrible place for these sights. It is stated that many children have purposely been maimed so that they must beg for alms. In the streets on the way back to Cairo, to which place we were obliged to walk, on account of our financial position we saw several pitiful sights. One of these was a blind man turning a big coffee grinding machine, throwing himself round with the handle. Many also are stricken so that they simply sit down &amp; sway their heads from side to side all day &amp; night. Such sights are very common &amp; are too pitiful to describe.
