 Wednesday July 19th   Rubempre: fine day: reveille 6.30 am: no physical jerks: parade 10 am for instruction on bones &amp; fractures: after dinner instructed how to apply splints for fractures: paid twenty francs but could not buy anything as the shops were bought out: slept cold again: the boys were pretty merry: a few 'planes kicking around: plenty of gunfire at night lately: 
 Thursday July 20th   Rubempre: fine day: reveille 6.30 am: packed up &amp; fell in to move off at 8.50 am: moved off &amp; marched through the village where the 19th &amp; 20th  were: got to our billets at about noon after a solid march the pace being pretty hot: this is a large village (Warloy Baillon) got a top "perch" &amp; then went to a canteen &amp; got fruit, condensed milk &amp; lemon squash &amp; had a good eat up to the great amusement of some Tommies billeted over the road: they seem to wonder where we put it all: plenty of transports &amp; amulances with wounded in kicking about: this place is about 5 miles from Albert &amp; there are all sorts of "latrines" about: Jim Marsland looked me up &amp; we met J. Shulstad &amp; F Poole so had a talk: Jack told me that old Mrs Mabbott had died: a fair few 'planes &amp; obs balloonsup: wrote to Mum &amp; Ethel as this may be out last chance for a while: Jim told me that 50 boys from Lithgow were all congregated at one corner
