
Thursday 17 May 1917.
Still at sea and we are of the opinion that we are just now in "bight"  judging by the very rough weather we have run into
The roughest day yet of the voyage, since leaving Melbourne.
1st .Cl . Bgde  Fd Amb. does guard. & police duties on board.
Medical inspection in afternoon
A very rough night. Sat on upper deck till 9 p.m. & then down below.
Have veered slightly in a Northerly direction but are stll about 500 miles from land.
