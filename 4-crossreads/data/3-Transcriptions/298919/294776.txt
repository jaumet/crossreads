<p>the Atlantic ocean the sea hereabouts is not the usual azure blue that characterises the Eastern and Central Mediterranean but a dirty blackish colour, similar to cabbage water.<br/><u>Sunday 27th June.</u>   Very strong wind blowing from early morning, sea rather rough, but the sun shining brightly.  The strong wind retards our progress somewhat &amp; will delay our arrival in port. <strike>somewhat</strike>  At 4pm hills on the coast of Spain can be seen on the Port Side &amp; Morocco on the S.B.  When I retired at 10pm the light outside Gibraltar was expected to be seen at any minute<br/><u>Monday 28th June 1915.</u>  Woke about 4am and saw lights of a town extending apparently to the sky.  Water quite still &amp; ship static indicates our arrival outside Gib.</p>
