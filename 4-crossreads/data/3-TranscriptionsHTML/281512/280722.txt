<p>a heavy barrage of 5.9s were coming  over at the time, so that I had a devilish tight time for an hour or so. Then the six &amp; a &frac12; miles tramp back through the slush &amp; mud to Ypres, where the car has to be left just about beat me. Soaked through &amp; mud to the thighs, I fervently thanked God when I was aboard the Ford &amp; bound back for Steenvorde.</p>
<p>12th October.  Battle of <br/>Last night I found a letter awaiting me from Capt Bean, telling me of another battle which is to come off tomorrow. Our objective is Poelcapelle:  about 800 yards advance. God knows how those red tabbed blighters at headquarters (60 miles from the front) expect our men to gain such a strong position when they have to drag them-</p>
