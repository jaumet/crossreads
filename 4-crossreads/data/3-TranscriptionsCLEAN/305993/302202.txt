Concerts & games are held amongst the troops on various parts of the ship.  All the Soldiers having been in India & Mesepotamia for 3 to 4 years are over joyful at going home to Blighty.  We are due at Malta tomorrow at 3 p.m.  We passed one of the Australian ships today, the Australfield bound from Australia to England. 
 24.5.19 1.30 p.m. arrived at Malta, 4 p.m. commenced coaling & 1000 tons of bunker taken in by 8 p.m.  Went ashore & had a pleasant drive around the Island & supper with some of the passengers & returned aboard at 12 p.m.  The Passengers bought 100 worth of cakes, sweets & cigarettes & cigars for the  passengers  troops. 
 25.4.19 8 a.m. we departed from Malta for Plymouth, games, concerts & religious meetings every day, having 6 parsons & 6 Salvation Army people among the passengers. 
 26.4.19 The Passengers complaining bitterly re not having the comforts of pre war terms which is impossible on account of having 1800 troops & 660 passengers aboard.  Complaints of no avail. 
 27.4.19 Fine weather prevailed.  News by wireless re floating mines on our track, a sharp lookout kept & troops & passengers unused to have their life belts with them where ever they go on the transport.
