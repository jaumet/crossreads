Here was a problem &ndash; to send the message up to the bridge &amp; to stay at the instruments. However I left them &amp; called Captain Jackson giving him the message. I was astonished to see him with all his bed clothes on &amp; a kind of coat over him, as if it were cold. "Dammit what do they want to fall overboard at this time of the day for" he asked. I could not explain, &amp; left him to reason it out. As I took up the phones again I heard H.M.S. Hampshire calling the Orvieto &amp; telling the flagship to lead the first division away. The tones were so sharp &amp; precise that I thought the cruiser was about to "blaze away" at someone. The Orvieto thereupon repeated the direction to be taken but several of the sub-flagships "jambed" in &amp; caused me to miss part of it. I was in a fix as we were liable to run the next transport down or be run down, &amp; they all depended on my receiving the message.
