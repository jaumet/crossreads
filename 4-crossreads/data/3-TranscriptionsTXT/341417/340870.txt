 I shall  do  not bore you with an account of our landing &amp; the ensuing three wild &amp; wobbly days &amp; nights. 
 I had the honor of being part &amp; parcel (with the other stretcher bearers of our Ambulance) of the landing troops that morning. We have since learned a terrible lot from the Sydney papers of various things that were supposed to have happened such as the Light Horse man who through Turks over his head. We saw his photo &amp; emoluments in the last mails we received &amp; you should have heard the roar of derision.
