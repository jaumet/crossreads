  
 1919 Apl 8 "The House of All Nations" one of the swankiest things of its kind in Paris.  Tapestries on the walls Electric Pianos, heavy furnishings, thick carpets - & women.  I dont know how many were there, but I saw about twenty.  This place cost about 20 francs to enter in the first place (if not with a guide as we were) & the man after a gay time who got out under 70 or 80 francs could think himself lucky.  We had champagne there & then went home.  I must say that the whole show disgusted me more than anything, but it most certainly was an eyeopener to the sort of depravity that existed under an ordinary exterior.
