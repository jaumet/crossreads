They heeded not their general's advice who rode out to them on horseback with words of friendly advice.  But in retribution, they upended the general's motor car in front of his own eyes at the very doors of Shepherds Hotel and    burnt  set it aflame utterly destroying its further use.  These were disgraceful acts and greatly to be regretted.  The casualties  were  amounted to about 35 in all, &amp; the destruction cost the Australian government  &pound;20,000.
