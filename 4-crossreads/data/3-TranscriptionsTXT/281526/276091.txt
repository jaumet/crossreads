
In Lone Pine in an angle of trench the withered wizened dead hand of a corpse protruded itself from its grave. To this hand some of the boys affixed a placard "Give it backsheese" and put a few piastres in the rotting palm.
After Bullecourt. "Coming out". Our horses were waiting and we cantered back to camp. Later poor tired exhausted men just out of hell, started to come in one by one. Then one called out to others plodding in. "There's hot tea". The pathos of it!
